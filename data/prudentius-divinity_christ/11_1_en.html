Man’s nature does not admit—provided he is not a grovelling beast but looks at the heavenly bodies with visage erect—I say, does not admit of denying the might of a supreme governor. Of this even Numa’s soothsayer was conscious in his heart, and so is the half-bestial Scot, who is worse than a dog in its wars.b But we, who have now twice seen the Lord, in the scriptures and in the body, first by faith and then in flesh and blood with us and who, when the books of the prophets were proved true by the witness of the cross, plunged searching fingers into the raw wounds in his side and, because our eyes doubted, explored the holes in his hands and recognised the everlasting king Jesus, believe it sin to deny the title and name of Father to God who brought forth our king from himself—not king of the nation of the Parthians nor of the sons of Romulus, but king of the highest and of the middle and of the lowest realm, and therefore Lord of creation and king over all things. He holds the middle domain, which is that of the flesh, the highest, which is that of the Father, and the lowest, which is that of hell. By these degrees He passes down and again returns to himself.
