He calls him oratora and adds the attribute of sweetness of speech, a thing you do not find nowadays in most of them—more barking in some than speaking; but what follows is certainly the greatest title to praise in eloquence:

He used to be called by his fellows of that time, The men who then lived and passed their restless days, The choice flower of the people—

and well said indeed; for as reason is the glory of 59 man, so the lamp of reason is eloquence, for preeminence in which the men of that time did well to call such a man the flower of the people,
