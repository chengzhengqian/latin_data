  • aL. Cornelius Lentulus, prosecutor in 61 b.c. of P. Clodius, the later enemy of Cicero; follower of Pompey, consul 49 b.c. The vehemence of his attacks upon the proposals conciliatory to Caesar are alluded to at the beginning of Caesar’s Civil War. They confirm the characterization given here—plena terroris oratio.
