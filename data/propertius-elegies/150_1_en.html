Book IV

did Tarpeia lament the love-pangs that were abhorrent to nearby Jupiter:

‘O ye campfires and command tent of Tatius’ squadron, and Sabine arms so handsome to my eyes, would that I might sit as a captive before your hearths, if as a captive I might only gaze on the face of the Tatius I love! Farewell, ye hills of Rome, and Rome that crowns those hills, and farewell, Vesta, whom my sin must put to shame! That horse, whose mane Tatius himself smoothes to the right, that horse will carry back to camp with him my heart!

‘Why marvel that Scylla cut her father’s hair and had her white loins transformed into savage dogs? Why marvel that the horns of a monster were betrayed by his sister, 43 when the twisted path was revealed by the gathering of her thread? What a reproach I shall bring upon the maidens of Italy, a sinful girl chosen to be the servant of the virgin hearth! If any wonder that the fires of Pallas 44 have gone out, let him pardon: her altar is flooded with my tears.

‘Tomorrow, says rumour, the whole city will be off its guard: it is then you must climb the dewy ridge of this thorn-covered hill. The whole route is slippery and treacherous, for a deceptive track of grass covers up the waters hidden beneath. O that I were acquainted with the spells of magic incantations! This tongue of mine also would have aided my handsome hero. To you is suited the royal robe,

  • 43Ariadne, half-sister of the Minotaur.
  • 44The sacred fire belongs to Pallas as well as Vesta, since her image was also brought from Troy and kept in the temple.
