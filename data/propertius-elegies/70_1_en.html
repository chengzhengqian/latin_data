Yet no abuse by a mistress can wear down that love which remains steadfast and listens with blameless ears to her threatening words, a love which pleads at once, when scorned; when injured, confesses itself in the wrong, and returns of its own accord with lagging feet.

You, too, credulous one, who put on airs because your love is at the full, no woman can be relied on for long. Does any man fulfil his vows in mid-tempest, when many a ship floats shattered even in port? Does any man claim the prize with the race unfinished, before his chariot’s axle has grazed the turning-post a seventh time? Fair winds in love deceive and mock us: the fall that comes late is a mighty fall.

Meanwhile, however much she love you, keep none the less your bliss locked up in a silent breast. For when a man has possession of his love, by some strange law it is always his own boastful words that do him harm. Although she calls you often, be sure to go but once: what invites envy is not apt to last for long. But if the times now were which girls of old liked well, I should now be in your shoes: I am undone by the age. Still these bad times will not alter my ways: let everybody know how to go his own road.
