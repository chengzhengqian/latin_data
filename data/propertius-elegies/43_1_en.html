What guards, then, what barred threshold shall I set for you, past which no enemy foot may tread? A stern guard set over an unwilling woman is a futile precaution: she who is ashamed to sin, Cynthia, is guarded well enough.

2.7 A threat removed

Never shall wife, never shall mistress part us: you shall ever be mistress, ever be wife to me. How you must have rejoiced, Cynthia, at the repeal of that law, 19 whose erstwhile issuance caused us to weep for many an hour in case it parted us! Still, not even Jove himself can part two lovers against their will. ‘Yet Caesar is mighty.’ True, but mighty in warfare: in love the defeat of nations counts for naught.
