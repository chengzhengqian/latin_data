Is this the husband’s loyalty and are these the bridal gifts you pledged when all innocent I surrendered to your embraces? The torch which gave omen as it headed my wedding procession drew its murky light from a dying funeral pyre; I was sprinkled with water from the Styx, and the wreath was set awry on my hair: Hymen was absent when I wed.

On every gate hang my offerings that do naught, alas, but harm: this is now the fourth 33 cloak I am weaving for your warfare. Perish he who from a guiltless tree plucked the soldier’s stake and fashioned the trumpet to sound its mournful notes through screeching bone: he rather than Ocnus deserved to sit sideways twisting the rope and feed for ever the donkey’s hunger!

Tell me, does not the breastplate blister your delicate shoulders, and the heavy spear chafe your unwarlike hands? Rather may this hurt you than that some girl should implant on your neck love-bites for me to lament. I hear too 34 that your face is thin and wan: but I hope that this pallor of yours comes from missing me.

  • 32Britain makes an odd appearance in this couplet, for Augustus’ intended campaign never got beyond the Channel; but the text seems assured by 2.1.76.
  • 33Suggesting four years of absence.
  • 34Perhaps indirectly from a fellow-soldier whose letters have reached home.
