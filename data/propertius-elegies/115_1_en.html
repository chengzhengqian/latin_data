Book III

3.13 Noble savagery and corrupt civilization

You all ask why the greed of girls makes their nights so costly and why our coffers, drained by Love, complain of their losses. The cause of such ruin is certain and obvious: the path of high living has become too free. The Indian ant sends gold from the caves of her mines, 39 and from the Red Sea comes the shell of Venus; Cadmean Tyre purveys her crimson tints, and cinnamon, the Arab distiller of rich scents.

These are the weapons that storm even the cloistered chaste and women who wear the disdain of Penelope. Matrons step forth arrayed in the fortunes of spendthrifts and flaunt the spoils of dishonour before our eyes. There is no shame in asking, no shame in giving in return, or if there be some reluctance, even that is got rid of for a price.

How felicitous is that funeral custom enjoyed by Eastern husbands, whose complexion the red Dawn darkens when rising with her steeds! But ours is a faithless race of brides, no girl of ours will play faithful Evadne or loyal Penelope.
