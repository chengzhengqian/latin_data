There are some whom the prize in the Olympic chariot-race runs to meet, some for whose swiftness of foot glory is destined; one is bred for peace, another effective with the weapons of war: each man follows the elements of his own nature.

But I have adopted your rule of life, Maecenas, and am constrained to surpass you by your own example. Though as a magistrate of Rome you might establish your imperial axes and dispense justice in the middle of the Forum; though you might pass through the fierce Parthians’ spears and adorn your house with trophies nailed on the walls; though Caesar gives you strength to achieve your will, and at all times wealth comes so readily flowing in: yet you hold back and humbly withdraw to a modest background: of your own choice you furl the full canvas of your sails. This wisdom of yours, believe me, will match you with any great Camillus of old, and your name too will live on the lips of men. 28
