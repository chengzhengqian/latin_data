2.17 A pledged night denied

Either say, no, if you are unkind; or, if not unkind, then come! Oh, what’s the good of uttering words that have no value? This is the bitterest of all a lover’s agonies, to be suddenly told at the peak of hope that she will not meet him. What deep sighs keep him tossing all over his bed, when, as if he were a stranger, she refuses him admittance! And he exhausts the messenger by asking him again and again questions that he has already heard answered, commanding him to divulge the infidelities he dreads to know.

To deny a pledged night, to lead on a lover with promises, this is as bad as having murder on one’s hands! Now, wicked girl, I feel like throwing myself from a rugged cliff or taking a drug compounded to bring about my death. Such is my song of woe as often as I spend bitter nights 51 forlorn, bruising my limbs on either side of the bed. 52
