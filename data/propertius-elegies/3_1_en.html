Believe me, there is no improving your appearance: Love is naked, 3 and loves not beauty gained by artifice.

See what coloured flowers the earth untilled sends forth 4 ; see how ivy grows better wild, the arbute arises lovelier in lonely glens and water has wit to flow in untaught channels. Shores are happiest when painted with natural pebbles, and birds sing more sweetly from their lack of art. 5

Not thus did Phoebe, daughter of Leucippus, set Castor afire, or Hilaïra, her sister, Pollux with her ornaments 6 ; not thus on her father’s banks did the daughter of Evenus once set Idas and ardent Phoebus at strife; nor by false glamour did Hippodamia win a Phrygian husband, she who was borne away on a stranger’s wheels: these had beauty which owed nothing to jewellery, pure as the hues in paintings by Apelles. These had no eagerness to gain lovers with cosmetics: for these chastity was beauty fair enough.
