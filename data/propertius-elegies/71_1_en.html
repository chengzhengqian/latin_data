2.26a A dream of Cynthia

In a dream, 56 my darling, I saw you shipwrecked and sweeping weary arms through the Ionian surge, confessing all your deceit towards me and no longer able to lift your tresses weighed down with water, like Helle tossed on the purple waves, whom the golden ram carried on his fleecy back.

How I feared lest the sea perchance should take your name and mariners sailing your waters should weep for you. What vows did I then make to Neptune, to Castor and his brother, and to you, Leucothoë, a goddess now!
