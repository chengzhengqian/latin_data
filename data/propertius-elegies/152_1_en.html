Tarpeia, judging this her moment, approaches the foe: she seals the bargain, herself to be part and parcel of the bargain. The sleeping citadel left everything open to attack: Jupiter decreed that her wakefulness should incur a penalty. Now had the hill been scaled, unguarded through feasting and revelry: in a moment she silences with a sword the watchdogs that might have given sound. Now she had betrayed the trusty gate and her defenceless country, and asks him to name the wedding-day of his choice.

But Tatius (for the foe allowed no honour to treachery) answered: ‘Wed, and thus mount my royal bed.’ So saying he crushed her beneath the massed shields of his com-pany. 47 This, maiden, was a meet dowry for your services. And could a single executioner have been enough for the wicked girl who presumed to betray the sacred fire of Vesta?

From the guide Jove’s hill took its shameful name 48 : O wakeful one, you do not deserve such a reward for your death. 49
