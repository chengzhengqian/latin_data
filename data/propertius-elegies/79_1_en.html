30a Fragment: Love the inescapable

Hard-hearted man, are you now planning to sail the Phrygian waves and seek the wild shores of the Caspian sea, to stain common house-gods with each otherâ€™s blood and bring back dread trophies to ancestral hearths?

Whither do you flee, madman? There is no escape: though you flee to the Don, Love will follow you all the way. Not though you were to fly through the air on the back of Pegasus, or the pinions of Perseus were to move your feet; nor, if the cloven breezes were to sweep you along on winged sandals, would the lofty path of Mercury profit you at all.

Love ever looms above your head, looms above the lover, and sits, a heavy burden, even on a neck once free. He keeps vigil as a keen sentry and will never let you raise your eyes, once captured, from the ground . . .

And though you should offend, he is not a god deaf to prayers, if only he sees that they are prompt to follow. 73
