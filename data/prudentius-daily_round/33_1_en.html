For it is a noble form of virtue too, to clothe the naked, feed the needy, give kindly aid to them that beg for it, hold that rich and poor share one and the same humanity. Blest enough the man who holds out a right hand that grasps at merit but is lavish of money, whose left hand knows not the kind deed: him everlasting wealth straightway satisfies, and profit an hundredfold on his lending makes him rich.

VIIIA Hymn After Fasting
