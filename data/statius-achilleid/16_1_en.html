Sacred shall you be to wind and wave, a quiet home for the Nereids among the shallows of the Cyclades when Aegean tempests shatter the rocks, an island for sailors’ prayers: only grant not access to Danaan keels, grant it not, I pray. “Here are only dances, nothing for warriors’ use”—so teach Rumour to tell; and while Doric arms are making ready and Mavors rages between two worlds 52 (let him for all I care), be Achilles good Lycomedes’ maiden.’

Meanwhile avenging Europe sets righteous wrath astir, aflame with sweet fury of arms and suppliant complaint of kings. For Atreus’ son, he the more whose wife is still at home, 53 makes canvass, honing Ilium’s crime in the telling. Heaven’s progeny, he says, nursling of mighty Sparta, is taken prisoner without war or weapons; laws, good faith, and the High Ones trampled in a single larceny. Such is Phrygian compact, such the commerce of two continents! What should the peoples expect, when so foul an outrage has descended on their leaders? All races, all ages flock together.
