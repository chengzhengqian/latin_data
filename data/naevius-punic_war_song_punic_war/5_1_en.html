Lactantius: Varro . . . says . . . that the fourth Sibyl is the ‘Cimmerian’ in Italy, who is mentioned by name by Naevius in his books of The Punic War.

Servius supplemented, on ‘trembles high Prochyta’ in Virgil: Naevius says that this island (Procida) took its name b from a kinswoman of Aeneas.
