Not unimpressive is the saying of a certain Spartan. Eminent in noble birth and purity of life but defeated in his candidature for a magistracy, he said it was a great joy to him that the country had some better men than himself.14 By that speech he made a rejection tantamount to an honour.

5. Of Justice

It is time also to approach the holy sanctuary of Justice, where always dwells respect religiously observed for fair and honest dealing and where partiality yields to modesty and desire to reason, where nothing is judged expedient that could seem less than honourable. The chief and surest example of it among all nations is our community.

When Consul Camillus was laying siege to Falerii, a schoolmaster led a large number of boys of the highest birth as though for a walk out of the town to the Roman camp. If they were taken, there was no doubt that the Falisci would put aside their obstinate war-waging and surrender to our commander. In that situation the senate1 decreed that the schoolmaster be bound and the boys sent back to their town flogging him with rods. By such justice the hearts of those whose walls could not be stormed were taken captive. Vanquished by benefaction rather than by arms, the Falisci opened their gates to the Romans.2
