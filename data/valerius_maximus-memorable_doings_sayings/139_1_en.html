Book III

Whom Euripides had for a father or Demosthenes for a mother was unknown even in their own period. But the writings of almost all the learned speak of the mother of the one as selling vegetables11 and the father of the other as selling knives.12 But what is more famous than the power of the first in tragedy and the second in oratory?

5. Of Those Who Degenerated From Famous Parents

The part of my twofold promise that follows must be delivered after the masks of illustrious men have been veiled, since I have to tell of those who degenerated from their splendour, noble monsters steeped in the foulest filth of sloth and rascality.
