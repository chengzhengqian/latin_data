L. Crassus too was as great a man in the courts as was Aemilius Scaurus in the senate, for he ruled their votes with his stout and successful campaigns of eloquence and was leader of the Forum as Scaurus was of the House; but when he launched a very violent thunderbolt of testimony against the defendant M. Marcellus, he turned out to be heavy in the onslaught but flimsy in the outcome.3

And again: Q. Metellus Pius, L. and M. Lucullus, Q. Hortensius, and M.4 Lepidus not only as witnesses sought the downfall of C. Cornelius, charged with lèse-majesté, but demanded him, asserting that the commonwealth could not survive if he remained a member of it. What ornaments of the community (I am ashamed to relate it) were rebuffed by the judicial shield!5
