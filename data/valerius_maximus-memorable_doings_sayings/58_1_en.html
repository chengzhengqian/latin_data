Since we have touched upon these items transcending normal reason, let mention be made of a snake recorded with no less particularity than eloquence by T. Livius.48 He says that in Africa at the river Bagrada there was a snake of such great size that it forbade the use of the river to the army of Atilius Regulus. Many a soldier was seized by its vast mouth, a number were crushed by the coils of its tail. When it could not be pierced by the casting of spears, it was finally attacked from all sides with catapult machines and fell forward from the frequent weighty blows of stones. All the cohorts and legions feared the sight of it more than Carthage itself and moved the Roman camp away, because the neighbouring district was polluted by the waters infected with the creatureâ€™s blood and the pestilential vapour from its prostrate body. He adds that the skin of the beast measuring one hundred and twenty feet was sent to Rome.
