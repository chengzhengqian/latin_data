At first the enemy revered their aspect, impressed by the novelty, the magnificent attire, and the very audacity. But who could doubt that Gauls and victors would presently turn their amazement into ridicule and every sort of insult? So C. Atilius11 did not wait for such ripeness of outrage but brought his staff with a vigorous blow down on the head of a Gaul who was stroking his beard, and as the pain made the man rush to kill him, eagerly offered his body. So valour knows no captivity, ignores dishonourable patience, thinks submission to Fortune worse than any fate, invents new and spectacular ways to perish, if any man perish who is extinguished thus.
