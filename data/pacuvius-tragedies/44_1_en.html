Plays

218–19

Iliona desires revenge on Polymestor:

Nonius:

‘Danunt,’ the same as ‘dant’ . . .—

Iliona

Though Gods destroy me, yet they wish that I Receive their help, since they before I perish Do grant me time for vengeance.

220–1

Polydorus (Deiphilus) has promised his help:

Festus:

‘Perpes’ is a term which the poets used for ‘perpetuus’ . . .—

Iliona

See that, as you have now begun this service, You everlastingly perform it for me—

May I gash out his eyes! a

222

Polymestor is lured by Iliona into the palace?

Nonius:

‘Sonunt.’ This form is also derived from that verb (sc. ‘sonere’). Ennius. . . . Pacuvius in Iliona—

Polymestor?

I’ll go to her to inquire what is their wish, [knocks]

Iliona? (rvithin?)

There’s a noise at the doors.

  • aThe reading here is uncertain. If oculis is right, perhaps a line has dropped out.
