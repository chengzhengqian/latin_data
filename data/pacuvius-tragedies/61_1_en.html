305–7

Diomedes has thought of a plan:

Nonius:

‘Consternari’ again means to be uplifted . . .— a

Diomedes Dismayed, b my soul? Be brave, my mind, to unfold Out of the breast the plan which was just now Born on a sudden, by what means you may Bring death to foes, life to this man. 308–9

Alcmaeon or Sthenelus c promises help:

Nonius:

‘Perbitere’ to perish . . .—

. . . For that I Should perish, they be aided by my death, No matter how I die—that is my wish, If only I can help.
