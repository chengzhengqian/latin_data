to a prophetic mistress c with a lackwit consort. d

Medus

was Medus (without knowing this was true) sent by Medea to kill Perses. Could she therefore kill him? Medus, when led out to the tender mercies of Medea, was recognised by her; she asked to converse with him, gave him a sword, and told him to avenge his grandfather. Medes kills Perses, obtains the kingdom, and names it Media (Hygin., Fab., 27). Cf. R., 318 ff.

Scene, Colchis. Chorus of companions of Medus? Cicero (de Nat. Deor., Ill, 19, 48) says that in Pacuvius Medea’s brother was called Aegialeus.
