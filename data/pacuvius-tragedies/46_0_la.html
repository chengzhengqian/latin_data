Medus

Medus, son of Medea and Aegeus king of Athens, was stranded on the coast of Colchis by a storm while seeking his mother, and pretended to be Hippotes, son of Creon. Perses, son of the sun-god and brother of Aeetes, fearing an oracle which warned him to dread the vengeance of Aeetes’ descendants, imprisoned Medus. The land was seized by famine; Medea came and pretended to be a priestess of Diana and able to expiate the dearth.
