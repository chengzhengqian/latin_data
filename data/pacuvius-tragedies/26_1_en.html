Prologue. Clytaemnestra has betrothed Electra:

Nonius:

‘Dies.’ They would have it that, when we use it in the feminine gender, we should mean time; when in the masculine, day simply. I have found cases where the opposite holds good. . . . Pacuvius in Thraldorestes—

Her daughter she betrothed; this is the day Which she appointed for the marriage.
