Medea excuses herself to Aeetes for her desertion of him when she gave herself to Jason:

Cicero:

What says the renowned leader of the Argonauts in tragedy? . . . (see Ennius, Med., Remains of Old Latin, Vol. I, pp. 322–3). . . . What then? That love of Medea—what a blaze of miseries it roused! And yet in the work of another a poet she dares to tell her father that she had for a husband—
