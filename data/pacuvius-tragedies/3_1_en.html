Plays

7–10
Nonius:

‘Saeptuose,’ obscurely. Pacuvius in Antiopa—

Chorus Enclosed in such a barricade of words Is this your riddle made; and even a sage Wise in his guessing scarcely can perceive it.

Cicero continues: When his words were thus too obscure, then the townsmen a reply—

We do not understand, unless you speak Your meaning openly.

Then Amphion in one word—

Amphion

Shellback. b

Well then, my dear harpist, couldn’t you have said that in the beginning?

Controversy continued:

Cicero:

Amphion in a play of Euripides [and also of Pacuvius] . . . after disparagement of music praises wisdom. c

Cicero:

I wonder why it was that like the famous Zethus in Pacuvius you declared war, one might almost say, on philosophy; and elsewhere: And he also . . . used to say that your Zethus in Pacuvius was more unfriendly towards learning than a man should be.

  • bPun on testudo, a tortoise, and testudo, a harp.
  • cCp. Rhet., ad Herenn., II, 27, 43, and Hor., Epp., I, 18, 39 ff.
