Plays

57–8

She asks to see the bracelet which she had given to Parthenopaeus:

Nonius:

‘Cette’ means ‘tell’ or ‘give,’ from the word ‘cĕdŏ’ . . .—

Of you two which is he to whom was given The token? Tell me!

59

A little later (change of metre); Parthenopaeus shows the token:

Festus:

‘Ungulus’ in the Oscan tongue means a ring . . .—

Parthenopaeus

The ring I show, a there! hung on my left arm.

60

Parthenopaeus to Atalanta, who shrinks from telling how he became her son by Meleager (?) and was exposed:

Nonius:

‘Tristitas’ for ‘tristitia’. . .—

Parthenopaeus

What trouble’s there? What sadness knits your brows?

61

Atalanta is ashamed:

Nonius:

‘Porcet’ (holds back) means prevents . . .—

Atalanta

My son, shame holds me back so that I dread To speak it out.

  • aWe might read ostende and give the line to Atalanta.
