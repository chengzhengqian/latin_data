Description of Polyphemus?;

Gellius:

Pacuvius, in the tragedy which is entitled The Washing, used ‘facies’ for the tallness of a man’s body. He says—

. . . A man in life’s full prime, And fierce in disposition, tall in feature.

277–8

How b Ulysses built a raft when he was on Ogygia with Calypso:

Festus:

‘Serilia.’ Verrius thinks that this is a name given to Istrian and Liburnian ships whoso grain is thickened with flax and broom; and that the name is derived from ‘consero’ and ‘contexo.’ His reason for believing this is that Pacuvius says in The Washing—

and no tenon held fast the framework of the hull, but it was sewn with flax and plaitage of broom;

whereas it was a coined word which Pacuvius used, as a periphrastic turn, for ropes which are entwined, ‘conseruntur,’ out of broom.
