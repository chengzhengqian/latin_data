266–8

Euryclea washes Ulysses’ feet:

Gellius:

Pacuvius called water ‘flava’ (yellow), and dust ‘fulvus’ (tawny); I am glad to mention his verses, since they are very charming—

Euryclea

Give me your foot, that in yellow water I may wash away the tawny dust with these same hands with which I often stroked Ulysses’; and with the softness of my hands let me soothe your weariness.
