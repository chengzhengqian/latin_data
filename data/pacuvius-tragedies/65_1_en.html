Protesilaus

first to leap ashore at Troy and meet his death at the hands of Hector or some other hero; and how he was allowed to come to life again for a short time for the sake of his loving wife Laodameia.

Antonius Vulscus, on the thirteenth of Ovid’s Heroines: Pacuvius and Titius each brought out a tragedy called Protesilaus; Ovid has transferred much out of it to this letter.

Teucer

had not avenged the death of Ajax his step-brother or had not brought Ajax’s remains to Salamis. Teucer left Salamis and found a new home in Cyprus.
