250

Medea unknowingly tells Perses the truth—that it is Medus he has arrested and Hippotes is dead. Perses asks:

Festus:

‘Super’ . . . is even put for ‘de,’ by a usage of the Greeks, just as they employ ὑπέρ . . .

Perses

You said that Hippotes was killed; on what reason this?

251

Festus:

‘Ostentum.’ That this word is used not only for a portent but also as a participle . . . Pacuvius is a witness in Medus—

Medea Why, see him! There in the very nick of time The old man is disclosed. 252
