Plays

155

Clytaemestra seeks Aegisihus:

Nonius:

‘Eliminare,’ to cast outside the ‘limen’ (threshold) . . .—

Clytaemnestra Where is that man? Curse my bad fortune! Whither Casts he himself outdoors so secretly? 156

A call for help:

Nonius:

‘Adiutatur’ for ‘adiutat’ . . .—

Him I seek who is an accomplice.

157–8

A remonstrance:

Nonius:

‘Amiciter’ for ‘amice’ . . .—

Now surely do not wait for him. For when Did you for him your friend a friendly act?

159
Nonius:

‘Autumare’ means to hope . . .—

I think he’s here, or will be here forthwith.

160–1

Orestes thanks his supporters:

Nonius:

‘Fatiscere’ (to fall open; faint, flag) means to be opened, and also to fail . . .—

Orestes But if your merits are as great as this, I fear I cannot counterbalance them, Unless it be I’ll never flag in doing What good I shall be able to perform.
