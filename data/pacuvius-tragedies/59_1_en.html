Pentheus, when he was appointing a heavier penalty for him, ordered him in the meantime to be kept fettered in prison. And when the doors of the jail had fallen open of their own accord, and Acoetes’ bonds had fallen from him, Pentheus was astonished and went to Cithaeron in order to be a spectator of the rites of Father Liber. When the bacchanal women saw him they tore him to pieces; and Agave his mother, thinking he was a wild beast, is said to have begun the business by cutting off his head.
