Plays

32

Ajax refuses the conditions and scorns Ulysses as a competitor: a

Nonius:

‘Certatur’ for ‘certat’ . . .—

Ajax

And is there any one could deem you worthy To be a man with whom he might compete?

33

Ajax claims that he has already earned the arms in warfare:

Festus:

‘Reapse’ means ‘reipsa’ . . .—

if the service I have rendered Is not in very fact a thankless one. b

34–5

He jeers at Ulysses and how he shirked his duty by feigned madness: c

Festus:

‘Reses’ and ‘residuus’ are terms applied to a dastard, because ‘residet,’ ‘he sits and waits.’ Accius * * * * * Pacuvius in Award of the Arms—

. . . For your part, you Preferred to be a laggard and a lounger Though we were here . . .

36

Agamemnon promises to follow Athena’s d advice:

Nonius:

‘Moneris’ for ‘monueris’ . . .—

Agamemnon

Tell me what I should do; I’ll give effect To that which you advise.

  • aCp. Ov., Met., XIII, 5 ff. R., 218–19.
  • bOr, ‘if it is not a thankless thing in the very fact that I have done well,’ Cp. Ov., Met., XII, 13–14.
  • cOv., Met., XIII, 7, 9, 36 ff.
  • dSee next note. Athena’s answer was apparently an order to set up a special court.
