Pacuvius

Paulus

The only known fabula praetexta of Pacuvius. It probably dealt with the victory of the consul Lucius Aemilius Paulus over Perseus, king of Macedon, in 168 b.c., at Pydna.

1

Priscianus, ap. G.L., II, 196, 3: Antiquissimi hic alienigenus et haec alienigena et hoc alienigenum dicebant . . . nec non cetera similiter a genere composita proferebant . . . Pacuvius in Paulo—

qua vix caprigeno generi gradilis gressio est.

Cp. Macrob., S., VI, 5, 14.

2

Gellius, IX, 14, 9: Sic ‘dies dii’ a veteribus declinatum est, ut . . . ‘progenies progenii’ . . . —

Paulus

Pater supreme nostrae progenii patris,

Cp. Non., 490, 5.
