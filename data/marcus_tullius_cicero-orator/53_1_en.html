  • oPraemunitio, προπαρασκευή: the term here paraphrased by Cicero is used by the rhetoricians with different meanings: Quintilian (9. 2, 17) says, qualis Ciceronis contra Q. Caecilium quod ad accusandum descendat qui semper defenderit; i.e. excuse or self-defence. Julius Rufinianus (p. 46 Halm) cites as example: Cicero pro Milone ante praemunit licere hominem occidere, et tum subicit occisum P. Clodium iure. I have adopted the latter sense in the translation.
