LXIII. But who does not know what my return was like?b how the people of Brundisium held out to me on my way home the right hand, as it were, of all Italy, and of my country herself. For that very fifth of August was the birthdayc of my arrival and return. It was also the birthday of my beloved daughter, whom I then saw for the first time after cruel longing and sorrow, the birthday too of that colony of Brundisium itself,d and the birthday, as you know, of the Temple of Saluse; and it was the day when the self-same house of those excellent and most learned men, Marcus Laenius Flaccus and his father and brother, gave me most joyful welcome, which the year before had offered me refuge in grief, and protected me at its own peril.f Everywhere, during my journey, all the cities of Italy seemed to be keeping the day of my arrival a holiday; the roads were crowded with deputations sent from all parts to meet me; my approach to the city was a triumphal progress
