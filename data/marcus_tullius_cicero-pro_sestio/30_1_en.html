XXI. “The disloyal would have been conquered.”b 47 But they were citizens, but it would have been by arms, but it would have been by a private citizen, who even as consul had saved the State without arms! And if the loyalists had been conquered, who would be left now? Do you not see that the State would have fallen into the power of slaves? Ought I myself, as some think, to have been resigned to death? What if I was? Was I avoiding death? Was there anything which I thought more desirable? Or when I was doing all I did amidst so great a multitude of traitors, was not death, was not exile always before my eyes?
