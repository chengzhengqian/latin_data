But he has two excuses. 135 “In the first place,” he says, “I exhibit beast-fighters, and the law refers to gladiators only.” A humorous distinction! Now listen to something still cleverer. He is going to say, “I am not showing gladiators, but one gladiator only; I have transferred all the cost of my aedileshipe to his exhibition.” What a splendid aedileship! one Lion,f two hundred beast-fighters! But let him make use of this excuse; I wish him to have confidence in his cause; for, when he lacks confidence, he is in the habit of appealing to tribunes of the commons and of breaking up a court by violence.g I am not surprised so much at his defying my law,h the law of an enemy, but at his having made it his principle not to recognize any consular law as law.
