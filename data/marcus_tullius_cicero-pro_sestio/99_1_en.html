Lastly, let us remember, that if the body of a brave and great man is mortal, yet the impulses of the mind and the glory of virtue are eternal; and, when we see this belief consecrated in the person of Hercules, that most venerable of heroes, whose courageous life is said to have passed into immortality after his body had been reduced to ashes, let us none the less believe that those who have enlarged, defended, or preserved this mighty State by their counsels or labours have obtained immortal glory.
