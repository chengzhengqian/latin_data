Crassus, 41 however, said that my cause ought to be taken up by the consuls, and Pompey also appealed to them, declaring that he, though a private individual, would not desert a cause officially taken up by them; but devoted as he was to me and most eager for the salvation of the State, he was warned to be more careful by certain persons, placed in my house for that purpose,b who declared that a plot against his life was being planned by me at my house. And they kept this suspicion awake in him, some by sending letters, some by messengers, others in person, so that at last, although he certainly had nothing to fear from me, he thought that he ought to be on his guard against these people, for fear they might attempt something in my name. Moreover, Caesar himself, who by certain persons ignorant of the truth was thought to be especially offended with me, was at the gates of the city, with military authority; his army was in Italy, and in that army he had given a command to a brotherc of that very tribune of the commons who was my enemy.
