“So then,” said Aper, “you have not had enough of those tragedies of yours? Otherwise you would not turn your back on your profession of speaker and pleader, and spend your whole time on plays. The other day it was ‘Medea,’ and now it is ‘Thyestes’; and all the while you are being summoned to the forum by the long list of your friends’ cases, and the equally long list of colonies and country towns for which you ought to act. Why, you could hardly meet all those calls even if you had not so gratuitously shouldered this new occupation of lumping in with Greekling legends a Domitius1 and a Cato, that is to say, stories from our own annals too, with Roman names.”

4. “I should be greatly put out by your harsh words,” said Maternus, “had not frequent and constant disputation become for us by now almost second nature. You on your part are never done assailing the poets with your invective, and I, whom you charge with neglect of professional duty, am daily retained to defend the art of poetry against you.
