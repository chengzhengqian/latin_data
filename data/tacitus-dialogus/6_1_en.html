The standard plain text is the Teubner of E. Koestermann (1964), with bibliography. H. Furneaux’s Oxford Classical Text (Cornelii Taciti, Opera Minora, 1900) is now out of date. In the Budé series we have the text edited by H. Goelzer with translation by H. Bornecque, edn. 3, Paris, 1947.

A Note on Manuscripts

We owe the preservation of the Dialogus, as of the other minor works of Tacitus, to the lost1 Codex Hersfeldensis; nothing is known of what happened to the part of the codex that contained the Dialogus, but as in the Germania we can reconstruct two lost copies of the codex from fifteenth-century manuscripts:

X = the consensus of Vaticanus lat. 1862,andLeidensis Periz. Y = the consensus of Vaticanus lat. 1518,andNeapolitanus IV C21.

X and Y are both indispensable to the editor, but the only direct evidence we have of the readings of the Hersfeld MS. (Decembrio’s at 36) suggests that Y is superior.
