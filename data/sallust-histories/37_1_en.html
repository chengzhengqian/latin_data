Pompey deprived Lepidus of his province of Cisalpine Gaul by bringing about the surrender of Lepidus’ legate M. Brutus (father of the future tyrannicide, M. Brutus, pr. 44) at Mutina (Plut. Pomp. 16.4–6).

at Mutina

70 80M, 68Mc, 107D, 115K

After returning from Cisalpine Gaul (see fr. 69), Pompey joined Q. Catulus (cos. 78) in defeating Lepidus in a battle near the Mulvian Bridge, putting him to flight (Flor. 2.11.6; cf. Exsuper. 6 [39Z]; App B Civ. 1.107).

he hastened his flight

71* 82M, 71Mc, 51D, 54K

After his defeat outside Rome (fr. 70), Lepidus withdrew to the north and embarked his forces from Etruria by way of the costal town Cosa (Rut. Namat. 1.297), fleeing to Sardinia (Liv. Per. 90).

COSAS: a Tuscan city whose name is singular [Cosa] according to Sallust.
