52* 63M, 54Mc, inc. 85D, inc. 53K

After Pompey was wounded in the battle at the river Sucro in 75, he managed to escape by abandoning his horse, whose rich trappings diverted the attention of the enemy from pursuit when they sought to claim a share of the spoils (Plut. Pomp. 19.5). A verb of the sort potiti sunt (they gained possession of) may have formed the predicate (so Maurenbr.).

his horse and distinctive arms
