Schol. Veron. ad Aen. 4.178 (Ac per . . . contenderent) “in primo Historiarum”: to illustrate S.’s use of the pl. of fama (cf. fr. 78), in contrast with the sing. in Virgil.

Cod. P. Vindob. L 117 recto column 1, lines 1–5 (accitas . . . a Domitio . . . m . . . ). This fragment and fr. 98 are preserved on a part of a bifolium that once belonged to a fourth-century parchment codex written in rustic capitals, having two columns of twenty-six lines each to a page; column 2 of recto and column 1 of verso are missing. The symbol “|” marks the divisions of the lines in the manuscript, while italics signifies that transcription is uncertain.
