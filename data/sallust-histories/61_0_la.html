Sardinia quoque, quam apud Timaeum Sandaliotin legimus, Ichnusam apud Crispum, in quo mari sita sit, quos incolarum auctores habeat, satis celebre est.

Solinus 4.1.

4 13M, 5Mc, 82D, 80K

Nam procul et divorsis ex regionibus

Charis. GL 1.215.5–6 = p. 278B.5–6: informs us that the second-century AD commentator Asper interpreted the adv. procul in this context as referring to place of origin, glossing procul e loco.

5* 6M, 8Mc, 4–5D, 7–8K

Aristaeum . . . Apollinis et Cyrenes filium . . . hic, ut etiam Sallustius docet, post laniatum a canibus Actaeonem filium matris instinctu Thebas reliquit et Ceam insulam tenuit primo, adhuc hominibus vacuam; postea, ea relicta, cum Daedalo ad Sardiniam transitum fecit.

Serv. ad G. 1.14: background information on Aristaeus.

{Prob. GL 4.7.27 (Apollinis filia [sic] et Cyrenes): to illustrate gen. sing. in –es of a f. noun having nom. in –e; Schol. Bern. ad G. 4.283 (Aristaeus filius Apollinis et Cyrenes, without mention of S.); Serv. ad Aen. 6.14 states that according to S., Daedalus made his way first to Sardinia and afterward to Cumae; Gloss. 5, p. 176.30 states that according to S., Aristaeus first settled on Cea after leaving his native land.}
