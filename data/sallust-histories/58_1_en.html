137 151M, 137Mc, 116D, 119K

Charisius cites Book 2 of the Elder Pliny’s Sermo dubius in defense of the abl. sing. in –e when the adj. agrestis describes an animal, as opposed to something inanimate. Possibly S. used this word to describe some fauna or tribe in a region of Mauretania or Spain in connection with the Sertorian War.
