In the last phase of the war, after Crassus had defeated the Gallic-Germanic detachment (referred to in frr. 27–29), he made great haste to crush the remaining force under Spartacus in order to forestall Pompey from robbing him of the credit for ending the war (Plut. Crass. 11.8; Nic.-Crass. 3(36).2)—haste characterized as reckless (Plut. Pomp. 21.2).
