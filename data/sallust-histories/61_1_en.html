For from far off and separate regions

5* 6M, 8Mc, 4–5D, 7–8K

Same context as fr. 1: Aristaeus is said to have been the second settler to arrive in Sardinia, after immigrants were brought from Libya under the leadership of Sardus, from whom the island took its name (Paus. 10.17.3, rejecting, for reasons of chronology, the tradition that Daedalus accompanied Aristaeus).

Aristaeus . . . the son of Apollo and Cyrene . . . , as S. too states, left Thebes at the prompting of his mother after his son Actaeon had been torn apart by his dogs, and at first possessed the island of Cea [Ceos], up until then devoid of people; afterward, he left that island behind and with Daedalus made the crossing1 to Sardinia.

  • 1From the Greek ἴχνος, meaning “footprint.”
