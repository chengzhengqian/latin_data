Appian (1.116) and Florus (2.8.6) credit Spartacus’ followers with arming themselves by fashioning makeshift weapons during the winter of 73/72 (hence this fragment belongs in Book 3, not 4, as cited), and Florus (l.c.) and Frontinus (Str. 1.7.6) include among those weapons shields made of wickerwork, covered with hides, of the type referred to in this and the following fragment. The men described in this fragment may be Lucanians, shepherds and herdsmen in southern Italy who flocked to join Spartacus (cf. Plut. Crass. 9.4), since according to Servius (ad Aen. 7.632), S. mentioned the manufacture of such hide-covered, wicker shields by Lucanians.
