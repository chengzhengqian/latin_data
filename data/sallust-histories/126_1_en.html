they hurled [against the siege engines?] grappling irons and other devices suitable for forming an attachment

From high ground, the besieged people of Cyzicus rolled heavy objects down the slope to smash Mithridates’ engines of war (cf. App. Mithr. 74).

Huge boulders and wheels joined with an axle were set rolling down the slope, and from the axles projected thin darts, each two feet long, arranged after the fashion of a military hedgehog.1
