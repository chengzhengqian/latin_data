The banquet given by Perperna at the town of Osca (Vell. 2.30.1), in the course of which Sertorius was assassinated. Diod. (37.22a) reports a different placement of the guests, having Sertorius recline between Tarquitius and Antonius, the latter of whom, according to Plut. (Sert. 26.11) struck the first blow.

And so they reclined at table: Sertorius in the lower position on the middle couch, above him Lucius Fabius Hispaniensis, one of the proscribed senators; on the top couch, Antonius, and below him Sertorius’s scribe Versius; and the other scribe, Maecenas, in the middle position on the bottom couch, between Tarquitius and the host Perperna.1

  • 1Couches were placed along three sides of the table, each having room for as many as three diners to recline. The diagram shows the arrangement. Servius, who seems to have mistakenly concluded that Sertorius was the host, quoted Sallust in support of the view that Virgil’s “mediamque locavit” described the usual position of the host. Sertorius, in fact, was the guest of honor, and as such he occupied the place reserved for that person, the locus consularis, the lowest (imus) position on the middle couch. Fabius, who shared the couch with Sertorius, was a former quaestor under C. Annius, governor of Spain in 81 (MRR 3.86).
