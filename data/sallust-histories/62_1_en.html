8 7M (Prisc.), 9Mc, 3D (Prisc.) , 5K (Prisc.)

Same context as fr. 1: descendants of Hercules were led by Hercules’ nephew Iolaus to Sardinia, where they settled (Strabo 5.2.7, p. 225; cf. Paus. 10.17.4). During a period when Sardinia was largely, but not exclusively, under Carthaginian control, Libyan or Iberian deserters from the Carthaginian army joined settlers from abroad (Corsica?) in the mountains of Sardinia; they were known as “Balari,” meaning “fugitives” in the Corsican language (Paus. 10.17.5).
