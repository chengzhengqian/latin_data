Criu Metopon (Ramsbrow: Κριοῦ Μέτοπον), the promontory at the tip of the Tauric Chersonesus (Crimea), lay across the Black Sea from the promontory of Carambis on the coast of Paphlagonia (Strabo 7.4.3, p. 309) and another, on which Sinope stood, ca. eighty miles to the east.

In his geographical description of the Pontus, S. employed “proximus” [= “next”] concerning the promontories of the Paphlagonians and the one he called Criu Metopon.
