16 18M, 43Mc, 33D, 38K

Et relatus inconditae olim vitae mos, ut omne ius in viribus esset.1

Adnot. super Luc. 1.175 to illustrate the statement that “might (vis) became the standard (mensura) of right (ius).”

{Fronto p. 157.14–15, omne ius in validioribus esse is most likely a paraphrase (see fr. dub. 3).}

17* 19M, 16Mc, 14D, 15K

Tantum antiquitatis curaeque maioribus1 pro Italica gente fuit.

Serv. ad G. 2.209: to support the view that antiquus approaches the meaning of carus (dear); Fronto p. 159.18, commenting on a meaning of antiquitas (OLD 5) coined by S.
