Hieron. Ep. 70.6.2: an often repeated pseudonym coined by Jerome for his adversary Rufinus (cf. Ep. 102.3.1, Calpurnius cognomento Lanarius; adv. Rufin. 1.30, Sallustianus Calpurnius).

84* 96M, 84Mc, deest in D et K

Salinator in agmine occiditur.

Comm. Bern. ad Luc. 1.478: defining the expression “in agmine esse” as meaning one who is making a journey/march.
