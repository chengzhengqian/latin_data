Because the holding of a public meeting during the annual festival in honor of Apollo (July 6–13), especially on the day of the chariot races in the circus (July 13), would have been rare and seems to point to a political crisis, Maurenbr. speculated that the occasion was a struggle over passage of the lex Aurelia in 75 (see fr. 44). Dietsch, by contrast, assigned this fragment to the clash between the tribune Sicinius and consul C. Curio in 76. Certainty is impossible.
