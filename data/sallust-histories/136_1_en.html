Part of S.’s description of Crete. It is not clear whether Servius is quoting or paraphrasing.

Crete is more elevated in the region where it faces east.1

48* 12M, 70Mc, 30D, 34K

If, as Maurenbr. speculated, this fragment formed part of S.’s description of Crete (cf. frr. 46–47), modica (moderate) is best interpreted as a reference to Crete’s climate—between the extremes of Scythia in the north and Cyrenica in the south—thus complementing the mention of a variety of crops (especially vines and trees: Solin. 11.12; Isid. Etym. 14.6.16). Less apt is “of moderate size” (so McGushin, in agreement with Frassinetti), since Crete ranks fifth in size among islands of the Mediterranean (after Sicily, Sardinia, Cyprus, and Corsica).

Moreover, the whole island is temperate and subject to various forms of cultivation.
