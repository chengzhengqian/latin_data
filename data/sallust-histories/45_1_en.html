A mountain * * * ,1 which had been occupied in advance by the Lusitanians, received [as a place of refuge] all who had crossed.

94 106M, 97Mc, 87D, K

Possibly referring to the seven hundred Libyan followers of Sertorius (Plut. Sert. 12.2).

a people who had seldom left their own territory

95 108M, 95Mc, 68D, K

Shortly after Sertorius crossed over from Mauretania in 80, as reported in frr. 92–93, Fufidius, the governor of Farther Spain (see fr. 49.21n.), was defeated by Sertorius on the banks of the river Baetis (Plut. Sert. 12.4).

Fufidius, soon arriving with his legions, after he saw the terribly steep banks,1 the one ford by no means easy for combatants, all conditions more advantageous for the enemy than for his own forces,
