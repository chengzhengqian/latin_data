76 54M, 37Mc, 24D, 25K

In the spring of 72, soon after Mithridates entered the Black Sea on his retreat to Sinope in Pontus, a massive storm destroyed a large portion of his fleet and left wreckage floating for days (Plut. Luc. 13.2; App. Mithr. 78).

Indeed, those who had tried to swim away, after having been often struck by the debris of the ships, or dashed against the hulls1 by the violence of the waves, still perished at last with horribly battered bodies.
