Asia was taken possession of by them; lastly, on the death of Nicomedes,9 they snatched away Bithynia, although a son had unquestionably been born of Nysa, whom Nicomedes had addressed as queen.

  • 8So called because in it Attalus III (d. 133), son of Eumenes II (d. 158), bequeathed his kingdom to the Romans.   • 9In 75 (see fr. 2.57); like Attalus, Nicomedes IV left his kingdom by will to the Roman people.
  • 10In 89, Nicomedes IV was pressured by Rome’s legate M.’ Aquillius (cos. 101), who had restored him to the Bithynian throne, to raid Mithridates’ neighboring kingdom, and this invasion precipitated the First Mithridatic War (89–85).
