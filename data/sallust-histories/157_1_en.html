“That man” (ille) may possibly be Lucullus, since dreams play a prominent role in Plutarch’s account of Lucullus’ campaigns against Mithridates—e.g., before the naval battle of Lemnos/Chryse (Luc. 12.1; cf. fr. 74) and before the capture of Sinope (op. cit. 23.3)—and Lucullus had been advised by Sulla in his Memoirs to put faith in dreams (op. cit. 23.6).

That man, on the contrary, thinking that he was being misled, inquired by means of entrails whether a treasure was being foretold in his dream.
