Cicero in his prosecution of Verres in 70 scoffed at the claim of the ex-governor that he deserved credit for preventing the war with Spartacus from spreading across the Strait of Messina to Sicily (2 Verr. 5.5). Yet contrary to Cicero’s portrayal, it may have been Verres’ defensive measures that caused the Cilician pirates to break their agreement to transport Spartacus and his men to the coast of Sicily (Plut. Crass. 10.7).

Gaius Verres fortified the shores near Italy.
