In 82, Pompey was sent to Sicily by Sulla (Plut. Pomp. 10.2), with imperium granted by the senate (Liv. Per. 89), to crush the “Marian” forces. At Lilybaeum, Pompey executed Cn. In 81, after Pompey crushed the “Marian” forces under Cn. Domitius (son-in-law of Cinna, cos. 87–84) in Africa, he invaded Numidia, captured Domitius’ ally King Iarbas, and installed Hiempsal as monarch (Plut. Pomp. 12.6–7; App. B Civ. 1.80).
