20* 29M, 25Mc, 24D, 39K

Same context as frr. 18–19. There was a tradition that Hannibal put his pilot Pelorus to death when, on a voyage, the general could not make out in the distance the narrow Strait of Messina (between Cape Pelorus and the “toe” of Italy) and so mistakenly believed that Pelorus had betrayed him by steering him into a trap (Serv. loc. cit.; Mela 2.116; Val. Max. 9.8 ext. 1).

PELORUS: it is a promontory of Sicily named, according to Sallust, after Hannibal’s helmsman buried there

21 30M, 26Mc, 8D, 29K

When the fugitive slaves failed to secure passage to Sicily on pirate vessels belonging to Cilicians, who had struck a bargain with them but then sailed away, leaving them stranded (Plut. Crass. 10.6–7), Spartacus and his men tried to cross the Strait of Messina on makeshift rafts (Flor. 2.8.13; cf. Cic. 2 Verr. 5.5), setting out, no doubt, from their base of operations on Cape Scyllaeum.

large earthenware vessels which they placed underneath planks and secured with vines or strips of leather
