80 59M, 41Mc, 37D, 39K

The proquaestor P. Oppius was dismissed by his commander M. Cotta (cos. 74) and charged with bribery and conspiracy when the two were serving in Bithynia (Dio 36.40.3). The incident may have taken place at the siege of Heraclea in 72.

But Oppius, after he accomplished nothing by pleading, was restrained by Cotta and Vulcius when he tried timidly to unsheathe a dagger hidden in his clothing.1

  • 1In his speech in defense of Oppius in 69, Cicero argued that it was unclear whether the dagger was drawn by Oppius to commit suicide or to injure Cotta (Quint. 5.10.69).
