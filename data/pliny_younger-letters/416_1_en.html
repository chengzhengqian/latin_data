LXXXV

Pliny to the Emperor Trajan

Your freedman and procurator Maximus,2 throughout the time we have been associated, has always proved honest, hard-working, and conscientious, as devoted to your interests, Sir, as he is a strict maintainer of discipline. I gladly give him this testimonial in all good faith, as demanded by my duty to you.

LXXXVI A

Pliny to the Emperor Trajan

Gavius Bassus,3 Sir, the prefect of the Pontic Shore, has always proved high-principled, honest, and hard-working in his official duties, and has shown me every respect. I give him my full support and recommendation, in all good faith, as demanded by my duty to you.
