Book X

LXXVI

Trajan to Pliny

Julius Largus chose you for your sense of duty as if he had known you personally. Consider what will suit the conditions of both places, and also what will best perpetuate his memory, and make your own decision; you can adopt which ever plan you think best.

LXXVII

Pliny to the Emperor Trajan

It was a very wise move, Sir, to direct the distinguished senator Calpurnius Macer to send a legionary centurion to Byzantium.1 Would you now consider giving the same assistance to Juliopolis? Being such a small city it feels its burden heavy, and finds its wrongs the harder to bear as it is unable to prevent them. Any relief you grant to Juliopolis would benefit the whole province, for it is a frontier town of Bithynia with a great deal of traffic passing through it.

LXXVIII

Trajan to Pliny

Byzantium is in an exceptional position, with crowds of travellers pouring into it from all sides.

  • 1From the Danube armies, as Bithynia had only auxiliary troops, a small force of which would serve under the centurion to control traffic.
