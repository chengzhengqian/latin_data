The same sort of reception and applause has greeted my policy of saying nothing for Varenus. The consuls have granted Polyaenus’s request and left the whole question open for the Emperor to decide. I am anxiously awaiting the result of the inquiry; for that day will determine whether we are to have some peace and respite from worry on Varenus’s account or to renew our efforts with fresh anxiety.

VII

To Pompeius Saturninus

I thanked our friend Priscus only the other day, but I was very glad to do so again at your bidding. It is a great pleasure to me that splendid men like yourselves, both friends of mine, should be so devoted and conscious of your mutual attachment. Priscus also
