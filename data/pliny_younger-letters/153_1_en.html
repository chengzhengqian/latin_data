XXVI

To Maecilius Nepos

You want me to re-read and correct the copies of my speeches which you have assembled with such care.2 Of course I will, for there is nothing which I ought to do so gladly, especially at your request. When a man of your judgement, scholarship, and eloquence (and moreover as busy as yourself and the future governor of an important province) thinks my writings worth carrying around with him, I should surely do my utmost to see that this item of luggage is not a useless encumbrance. My first care then shall be to make your present travelling companions as congenial as possible; and my second to provide you with more which you may like to add to them on your return. The fact that you are one of my readers is no small encouragement to new work.
