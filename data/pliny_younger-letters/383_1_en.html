XXXIV

Trajan to Pliny

You may very well have had the idea that it should be possible to form a company of firemen at Nicomedia on the model of those existing elsewhere, but we must remember that it is societies like these which have been responsible for the political disturbances in your province, particularly in its towns. If people assemble for a common purpose, whatever name we give them and for whatever reason, they soon turn into a political club. It is a better policy then to provide the equipment necessary for dealing with fires, and to instruct property owners to make use of it, calling on the help of the crowds which collect if they find it necessary.

XXXV

Pliny to the Emperor Trajan

We have made our annual vows,2 Sir, to ensure your safety and thereby that of the State, and discharged our vows for the past year, with prayers to the gods to grant that those vows may be always thus discharged and confirmed.
