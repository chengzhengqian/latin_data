However, some cycles are short and thus noticeable, others are longer but no less fixed. And what is remarkable3 about this, when you see that in nature the sequence of events proceeds in accordance with established laws? Winter never misses, summer becomes hot at the proper time, the changes of autumn and spring come from habitual causes, the solstice and the equinox alike return on their proper dates.

There are also laws of nature under the earth, less4 known to us but no less fixed. Believe me that there exists below whatever you see above.1 There, too, vast caverns exist, and great recesses, and vacant spaces with mountains overhanging here and there. There are gulfs gaping into infinity which have frequently swallowed up cities that fell into them and buried the mighty ruins in the depths. These places5 are filled with air—for no void exists anywhere—and there are marshes enveloped in darkness, and great lakes. Also, living creatures are born there, but they are slow and deformed since they were conceived in dark, heavy air, and in water made torpid by its inactivity. Many of these creatures are blind, like moles and subterranean rats who have no vision since none is needed. According to Theophrastus, fish are dug up from such depths in certain places.2

  • 1Lucretius 6.536–542: the earth below as above is everywhere full of windy caverns, bearing many lakes and many pools with rocks and steep cliffs.
  • 2Pliny (9.176) reports that Theophrastus says there are kinds of fish that make themselves caves in the ground and are dug up when their movement shows that they are alive.
