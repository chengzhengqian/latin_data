The oil in the lamp sputters And a crumbling excrescence Grows on the wick,2

or in the same way that it is a forecast of rough sea if

The sea-coots play on dry land, And the heron deserts her familiar swamp And flies above the high cloud,3

but in the way that the equinox is a sign of the year turning to hot or to cold, or as the things the Chaldaeans predict, the sorrow or joy that is established at people’s birth by a star.

In order that you may understand that this is true:2 the rising of a comet does not immediately threaten wind or rain, as Aristotle says it does, but makes the entire year suspect.4 From which it is clear that the comet has not drawn from its close neighbourhood the signs which it gives for the immediate future but that it has them stored up and linked to the laws of the universe.
