4. Thus, atmosphere is a part of the universe and,1 in fact, an essential part. This is what connects heaven and earth and separates the lowest from the highest in such a way that it none the less joins them. It separates because it intervenes midway; it joins because through it there is a communication between the two. It transmits to the upper region whatever it receives from the earth. On the other hand, it transfuses to earthly objects the influences of the stars.

I call such things as animals and trees a quasipart2 of the universe. Now, the class of animals and trees is a part of the universe because it is considered in the sum of the whole and because there is no universe without such a class; but a single animal or single tree is a quasi-part because even when it is lost nevertheless the whole from which it is lost is still intact. But the atmosphere, as I was saying, is connected both to sky and to earth; it is innate to both. Moreover, whatever is an inborn part of anything has unity. Nothing is born without unity.
