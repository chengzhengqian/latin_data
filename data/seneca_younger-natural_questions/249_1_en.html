23. This must be the cause of the motion that1 makes the ground quake from beneath. Now I pass on to the second type.3 The earth is naturally porous and has many voids. Air passes through these openings. When air in large quantities flows in and is not emitted it causes the earth to tilt.

This theory is accepted by others, as I mentioned2 a little above—if a crowd of authorities will impress you—but is also approved by Callisthenes, and he is a man not to be looked down upon. For he had outstanding intelligence and did not submit to the rage of his king. The murder of Callisthenes4 is the everlasting crime of Alexander, which no virtue, no success in war, will redeem.
