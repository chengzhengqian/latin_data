But the 8 explanation for some of these is difficult, especially when the time of the occurrence—a subject of inquiry—has not been unwitnessed but is still uncertain, and so the immediate and related cause cannot be discovered. However, there is a common cause: all standing and enclosed water naturally purges itself. For, in water that has a current, impurities are not able to settle; the force of the current sweeps them along and carries them away. Water which does not emit whatever settles in it more or less boils. As for the sea, it drags from the depths dead bodies, litter, and similar debris of shipwrecks, and purges itself of them, not only in storms and waves but also when the sea is tranquil and calm.

But this subject reminds me to wonder how a1 great part of the earth will be covered over by water when the fated day of the deluge comes.2 Will it be by the force of the ocean and the rising of the outer sea against us or will heavy rains fall without ceasing and persistent winter eliminate summer and hurl the full force of water down from burst clouds?
