These causes2 give water its different taste, its medicinal power, its disagreeable exhalation and pestilential odour, as well as its unwholesomeness, heat or excessive cold. It makes a difference whether it passes through places full of sulphur, nitre,1 or bitumen. When water is polluted this way it is a risk of life to drink it. Accordingly, this is what Ovid means when he says:3

The Cicones have a river Which when drunk Turns the viscera to stone And puts a layer of marble On everything it touches.2

The water is adulterated and has mud of such a nature that it cements and hardens objects. Just as the dust of Puteoli becomes rock if it touches water so, on the contrary, if this water touches a solid object it clings and attaches itself to it. This is why4 objects thrown into a lake of that type3 are subsequently extracted as rock-like substances. This happens in certain localities in Italy.
