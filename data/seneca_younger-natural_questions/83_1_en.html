Whatever is burned up is, of course, also burnt, but what is burnt is not completely burned up. Likewise, whatever is ignited (for something5 can be burned by the mere passing of fire)—well, everyone knows that something can be burnt and yet not glow but that nothing glows which is not also burnt. I will add this one point: something can be burned up but not set on fire and something can be set on fire but not burned up.

Now I pass on to the type of lightning which blackens6 the objects it strikes. This lightning either colours objects or discolours them. To each process I will grant the difference due to it. Something is discoloured when its colour is blemished, not changed. Something is coloured when its appearance becomes other than it was, blue, for example, or black, or whitish.
