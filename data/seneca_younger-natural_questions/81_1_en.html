“Well, then,” you say, “what benefit is a soothsayer 4 to me? In any case it is necessary for me to offer expiation even though he does not advise me to.” He provides this benefit: the fact that he is a minister of fate. Thus, although the recovery of good health is owed to fate it is also owed to the doctor because the benefit of fate came to us through his hands.

39. Caecina3 says that there are three kinds of1 lightning, the advising, the confirming, and that which is called the conditional. The advising one happens before the event but after a thought has been conceived, when people who are planning something in their minds are either persuaded or dissuaded by a stroke of lightning. The confirming lightning comes after action has been done, indicating whether it will be either for bad or for good. The2 conditional lightning comes to people who are quiet, doing nothing, not even thinking, and it either threatens, promises, or warns.
