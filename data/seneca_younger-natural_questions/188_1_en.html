Do you consider this thirst? It is a fever, and all11 the more severe because it is not detected by the pulse or by heat suffused through the skin; indeed it burns the heart itself. Luxury is an incurable malady and from being soft and weak it hardens to endure anything. Do you not realize that all things lose their force because of familiarity? And so, that snow, in which you are even now swimming,1 has come to such a pitch, by constant use, and daily slavery of the stomach, that it takes the place of water. Search now for something still colder than snow, because cold that is familiar is nothing.
