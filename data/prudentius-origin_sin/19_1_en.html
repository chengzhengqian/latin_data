Is it not known beyond doubt that the souls of the seed of Jacob suffer exile among the Gentiles, held in captivity by the realms of Persia and compelled to join with them? There they forget the way of life to which they were born, and shedding their native manners, debase themselves to obey heathen laws, adopt new speech and dress, learn to befoul themselves with unhallowed worship, and efface from their froward heart all thought of Zion, their nurse. They care not any longer to remember their own country, they break their holy instruments of music, and speak well of the sacrifices of a foreign kingdom.
