One of the sacred psalms tells us: “Come and behold the works of the Lord, what marvels he has placed upon the earth.”1 Elsewhere,2 I shall set forth, as best I can with God’s aid, the reason why woman was made out of the side of man and what this first marvel, as we may call it, foreshadowed.

Now, since I must bring this book to an end, let us imagine that with this first man who was created in the beginning there had arisen, not as yet indeed in plain sight, but already in the foreknowledge of God, two societies or cities among the human race. For it was from him that mankind was destined to arise, of which one part was to be joined in fellowship with evil angels for punishment and the other with good angels for reward. Such was God’s decree, which, though hidden, was yet righteous. For, since we read in Scripture: “All the paths of the Lord are mercy and truth,”3 we know that neither can his grace be unjust nor his justice cruel.
