‘Temple of God’ or the ‘City of God,’ it is all the same and not at variance with idiomatic Latin usage. For Virgil too refers to the supreme imperial city as the ‘house of Assaracus,’1 by which he means the Romans, who derive their origin from Assaracus through the Trojans. And he refers to these same Romans as the ‘house of Aeneas’2 because Rome was founded by the Trojans after they had come to Italy under the leadership of Aeneas. In so doing, that celebrated poet followed the practice of holy Scripture, where the Hebrew people is called the ‘house of Jacob’ even after it has grown enormous.
