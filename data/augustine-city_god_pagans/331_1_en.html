XXIII

Of the war in which Radagaisus, king of the Goths and worshipper of demons, was defeated in one day, along with his vast army.

There is an event of very recent date,1 within my own memory, in which God acted miraculously and mercifully. But the pagans do not remember the event with thanksgiving; rather, they attempt, so far as they can, to cause all men to forget it. If I should fail to mention it, I should be equally unthankful.
