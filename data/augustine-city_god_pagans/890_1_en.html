Hence, even if souls (to make a completely baseless supposition) actually underwent, as they come and go, such endless alternation of purification and defilement, we deduce that it cannot have been truthfully said that all blameworthy and unwholesome emotions of souls spring up in them from their earthly bodies. For, according to the men themselves, that direful lust, as their famous spokesman calls it, is so far from having its source in the body that it of itself drives the soul to exist in a body after it has been cleansed of all bodily plague and situated outside all bodily substance. Thus, even by their own admission, the soul is not only so affected by the flesh that it feels desire and fear, joy and grief, but it can also through itself be stirred by these emotions.
