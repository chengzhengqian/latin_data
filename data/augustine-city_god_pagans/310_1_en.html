These apostles were followed by martyrs who surpassed men like Scaevola, Curtius and the Decii both in true courage, because they had true religion, and in their vast number. Their tortures were not self-inflicted, but they bore tortures that were inflicted by others. Those Roman heroes were citizens of an earthly city, and the goal of all their loyal service to it was its security and a kingdom not in heaven but on earth. Since there was no eternal life for them, but merely the passing away of the dying, who were succeeded by others soon to die, what else were they to love apart from glory, whereby they chose to find even after death a sort of life on the lips of those who sang their praises?
