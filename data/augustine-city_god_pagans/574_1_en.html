This was evidence that would be more clearly visible to the angelic senses even of malign spirits than to the weak powers of men. Consequently, when he judged that such signs should be to some extent suppressed, and when he hid himself a good deal deeper, the prince of the demons became uncertain of him and tempted him, trying to discover whether he was Christ, but only so far as he himself allowed himself to be tempted. His purpose was to shape the human person in which he played his part into a model that we might copy. After that temptation, however, when the angels, as it is written, ministered to him,1 that is, the good and holy angels, who were therefore bound to cause fear and trembling in unclean spirits, his greatness became gradually better known to the demons, so that when he gave commands, however contemptible the infirmity of the flesh might seem to be in him, none dared to resist him.
