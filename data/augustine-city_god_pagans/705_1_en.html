XVIII

The beauty of the universe which, as God has arranged it, becomes even more brilliant by the contrast of opposites.

Now God would never create any man, much less any angel, if he already knew that he was destined to be evil, were he not equally aware how he was to turn them to account in the interest of the good and thereby add lustre to the succession of the ages as if it were an exquisite poem enhanced by what might be called antitheses.2 Antitheses, as they are termed, are among the most elegant ornaments of style. In Latin they might be called opposita or, more accurately, contraposita. We are not in the habit of using this term, although Latin and indeed the languages of all nations employ the same ornaments of style.
