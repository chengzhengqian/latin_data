For the first, which is called the old covenant, was the law delivered from Mount Sinai through Moses. Of this, however, which was to be given through Christ it was foretold: “Out of Sion shall go forth the law, and the word of the Lord out of Jerusalem.”2 Wherefore he himself said that repentance must be preached in his name among all peoples, but beginning from Jerusalem.3 So that is where the worship of this name began, if we mean that men believed in the Jesus Christ who had been crucified and had risen again. That is where this faith blazed up with such marked initial success that some thousands of men were converted to the name of Christ at an amazing rate, and sold their goods for distribution among the needy, so that by holy resolution and most ardent love they might achieve a voluntary poverty, and among raging and bloodthirsty Jews they might make themselves ready to do battle for the truth even unto death, not by force of arms but by the even stronger force of patience. If this came to pass through no magical arts, why do they hesitate to believe that the like could have come to pass throughout the whole world by the same divine power?
