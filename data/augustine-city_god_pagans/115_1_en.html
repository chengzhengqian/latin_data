Book II

But such accidental evils, evils physical rather than spiritual, that are inflicted by enemies or some other calamity, are not for the moment my theme. My present concern is with the decay of morality, those morals that first dwindled by degrees, but later dashed headlong like a cascade till, though the houses and fortifications still stood intact, the republic was so far gone that their great writers frankly report it as a lost cause. Moreover, the gods had rightly departed to permit its loss, abandoning shrines and altars, if so be the city had ignored their precepts of right living and justice. As it is, though, what value must we set on gods who declined to live with a nation that worshipped them, when, though it led an evil life, they did not instruct it how to lead a good life?
