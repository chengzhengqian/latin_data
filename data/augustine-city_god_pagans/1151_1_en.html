That is why I have designated the prophecies as divided into three classes rather than two, for such is my firm conviction. Yet I do not censure those who have been able to carve out a spiritual application for every event recorded there, provided that they first take care to maintain historical veracity. But as to sayings of such a nature that they do not yield any clue to past or future events, whether effected by human or by divine agency, what believer can doubt that these were not uttered vainly? Who would not interpret them according to their spiritual meaning, if he can, or else admit that they should be so interpreted by the man who has this capacity?
