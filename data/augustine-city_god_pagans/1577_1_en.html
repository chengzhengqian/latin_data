XX

Of those who promise indulgence, not to all, but only to those reborn in the Catholic Church, even if later they fall into many sins and errors.

Likewise there are some who promise this escape, not to all who have the sacrament of Christ’s baptism and his body, but only to Catholics, however wickedly they live. For they have eaten the body of Christ, not only in a sacrament, but also in reality, and are established in his very body, of which the Apostle says: “We who are many are one bread, one body.”2 Hence, even if they have later lapsed into some heresy or even into heathen idolatry, for the mere reason that in the body of Christ (that is, in the Catholic Church) they have received Christ’s baptism and eaten his body they will not die to remain for ever dead, but will at some time attain eternal life. And so all their impiety, however great, cannot lead to eternal punishment, but only to a punishment of greater duration and severity than that of others.
