Now although the thickly shaded mountain might be interpreted in many ways, I would prefer to accept it as referring to the profundity of the divine Scriptures by which Christ was prophesied. For surely in these there are many obscure and thickly shaded sayings that tax the mind of him who searches them. But Christ comes from there when he is found there by the man who understands. “His glory covers the heavens and the earth is full of his praise”; what is this save what is also indicated in the psalm: “Be exalted over the heavens, O God, and thy glory over all the earth”?1 “His splendour shall be as the light”; what does this mean but “his fame shall enlighten those who believe on him”? “Horns are in his hands”; what is this but the trophy of the cross? “And he has implanted an abiding love for his strength” needs no explanation.
