XXVII

On the dates of the prophets whose oracles are preserved in books and who made many prophecies about the calling of the gentiles at the time when the Roman empire began and the Assyrian empire fell.

In order, therefore, to focus attention on their era let us go back to a slightly earlier time. At the beginning of the book of Hosea the prophet, who has first place among the twelve, these words are written: “The word of the Lord came to Hosea in the days of Uzziah and Jotham and Ahaz and Hezekiah the kings of Judah.”1 Amos also writes that he prophesied in the days of king Uzziah, and adds the name of Jeroboam, king of Israel, who lived at the same time. Isaiah also, the son of Amos, whether his father was the prophet just mentioned or, as is more widely held, another man called by the same name who was not a prophet, lists at the beginning of his book the same four kings that Hosea named, in whose days he says that he prophesied.2 Micah also indicates this same period as the time of his prophecy, after the days of Uzziah.
