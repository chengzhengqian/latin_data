That was the time indeed when wars were so frequent everywhere that to meet the scarcity of soldiers they enrolled for service the proletarians, who had received that appellation because, being too poor to serve as soldiers, they had leisure to beget offspring.4 Then again Pyrrhus, King of Greece, at that time at the height of his fame, was invited by the Tarentines and became an enemy of the Romans.
