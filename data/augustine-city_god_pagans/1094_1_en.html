Finally, any who boast that they have calculated and listed the whole number of stars, as do Aratus, Eudoxus and any others there may be,1 the authority of this book holds them in disdain.

At this point, to be sure, a statement occurs which the Apostle quotes to commend God’s grace: “Abraham believed in God, and this was accounted to him for righteousness.”2 He would not have the circumcised boast and refuse to admit uncircumcised peoples to faith in Christ. For at the time when it came to pass that Abraham’s faith was accounted to him for righteousness, he had not yet been circumcised.
