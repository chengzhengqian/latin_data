Book XXII

God does these things in person, in the marvellous way in which the Eternal brings about events in time, or does them through his servants. Nor in the case of deeds done through his servants does it matter whether he does some deeds through the spirits of martyrs, just as he does some through men still living in the body, or does all these things through angels, to whom he gives commands in a manner invisible, incorporeal and immutable, so that the things said to be done through martyrs are done through their prayers and intercessions alone, and not by any action on their part. Or he may do some things in the ways I have stated while other things are wrought in such ways as cannot be understood at all by mortals; still these things that are done bear witness to that faith in which the resurrection of the dead for eternity is proclaimed.
