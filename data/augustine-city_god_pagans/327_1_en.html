For though Glory herself is not a dainty woman, she is puffed up and swollen with vanity. Hence it is not appropriate for her to be served by any firm and solid virtues, so that Prudence should foresee nothing, Justice bestow nothing, Fortitude endure nothing, Temperance regulate nothing, except with the aim of pleasing men and serving a glory that is inflated with wind.

Nor will those men escape from this sorry picture who scorn the judgements of others as men who despise glory, but seem wise to themselves and are self-satisfied. For their virtue, if they have any, is made subject to human praise in another way, for the man who is satisfied with himself is no other than a man. But the truly religious man who puts his faith and hope in God, and loves him, gives more attention to the matters in which he is dissatisfied with himself than to those, if such there be, with which he, or rather, the truth is satisfied. Nor does he ascribe that which already gives satisfaction to anything but the mercy of Him whom he fears to displease. To him he gives thanks for the curing of some of his faults, and pours out prayers for the curing of the rest.
