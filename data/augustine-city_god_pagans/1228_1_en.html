Marvelling at these new inventions of his, men who were still uneducated either believed or decreed that at his death he had become a god. For Io also is said to have been a daughter of Inachus, and she, being later named Isis, was worshipped in Egypt as a great goddess. But other writers say that she came to Egypt from Ethiopia as queen, and that because she ruled widely and with justice and instituted many useful customs for them, including the art of writing, divine honours were granted her after she died in that country, and so great was her honour that a man was held guilty of a capital crime if he said that she was mortal.

IV

On the times of Jacob and his son Joseph.

When Baleus the tenth king of the Assyrians was reigning, and Messapus, the ninth king of the Sicyonians, who is also called Cephisus by certain writers—if indeed both names belong to one man, and those who used the second name did not rather confuse one man with another—and when Apis was the third king of the Argives, Isaac died at the age of a hundred and eighty, and left his twin sons a hundred and twenty years old.
