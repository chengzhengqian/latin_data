VIII

Apuleius’ classification of gods dwelling in heaven, demons in the air and men on earth.

Well then, are we to take little or no account of his own definition of the demons, which certainly includes all of them in one category? He says that demons are animal in genus, affected by emotions, rational in intelligence, airy in body and eternal in time.2 In this list of five characteristics he has mentioned absolutely nothing that demons appear to have in common with good men in particular that is not found also in those who are bad. For when he broadened his description somewhat to include men themselves, he described them in their turn as at the bottom and earthly.
