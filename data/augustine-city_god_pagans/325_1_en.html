But if those who are endowed with true religion and live good lives know the art of ruling the nations, there is no greater blessing for mankind than for them, by the mercy of God, to have the power. Such men, whether the virtues that they have in this life be great or small, ascribe them only to the grace of God, because he has granted virtue to them according to their good will, their faith and their prayers. At the same time they understand how far they fall short of perfection in righteousness, such as exists in the company of the holy angels, for which they strive to fit themselves. However much we praise and proclaim the sort of virtue that, in the absence of true religion, is subordinate to human glory, it cannot even be compared with the first faint steps of the saints who have put their hope in the grace and mercy of the true God.
