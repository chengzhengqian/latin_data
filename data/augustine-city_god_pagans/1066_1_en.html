X

On the generations of Shem, by way of whose descendants the course of the City of God is directed towards Abraham.

The series of generations must therefore be traced from Shem himself, to show us the city of God after the flood, just as the line of descent from the man named Seth showed it in the period before the flood. On this account the holy Scripture, after describing the earthly city in Babylon, that is, in ‘Confusion’, returns for a new start to the patriarch Shem, and begins with him the list of generations to Abraham, recording also the number of years that had elapsed before each individual begot a son belonging to this line, and how long each lived. We must surely recognize in this a reference to a point that I promised to explain earlier,1 in order to show why it was said of the sons of Heber: “The name of one was Peleg, for in his days was the earth divided.”2 For how else can the statement that the earth was divided be understood except as referring to the diversity of languages?
