I do not know whether the demons themselves, if called upon to testify, would admit as much as does Hermes. He says: “Since our ancestors erred gravely in their concept of the gods through their unbelief and because they did not give any heed to worship and divine religion, they invented the art of making gods.” Did he say that at least they erred in a moderate sort of way and so were led to invent the art of making gods, or was he content to say: “They erred” without adding “gravely”? No, it was this grave error and unbelief on the part of men who did not pay due attention to worship and divine religion that invented the art of making gods. And yet it is the loss of what was invented by grave error, unbelief and an aversion from worship and divine religion, namely the art whereby man could make gods, that the sage deplores, as if he mourned the loss, at a definite time to come, of a religion of divine origin.
