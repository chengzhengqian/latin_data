Book XX

XXIX

The coming of Elijah before the judgement, by whose preaching and revelation of the secrets of the Scriptures the Jews are to be converted to Christ.

After admonishing them to remember the law of Moses (for he foresaw that for a long time to come they would not take it, as they should have, in a spiritual sense), the prophet continued: “And behold, I will send you Elijah the Tishbite, before the great and shining day of the Lord shall come, and he shall turn the heart of the father toward the son and the heart of a man toward his neighbour, lest perchance in my coming I utterly shake the earth.”1 That in the last days before the judgement, by means of this great and wonderful prophet Elijah and his explanation to them of the law, the Jews are to believe in the true Christ, that is, in our Christ, is a frequent topic on the lips and in the thought of believers. For not without reason is it expected that he will come before the Saviour when He comes as judge, since there is reason to believe that he is living even now; for, as holy Scripture most clearly bears witness, he was carried up from the world of men in a fiery chariot.2 When, then, he shall come, by explaining in a spiritual sense the law which the Jews now understand in a carnal sense, “he shall turn the heart of the father toward his son,” that is, the hearts of the fathers toward their children (for the Septuagint translators have used the singular for the plural number).
