They took these deeds to be pleasing to the gods, and hence not only believed them to be suitable for exhibition in their honour but also for imitation by themselves. As for whatever supposed good or honourable instruction there may have been, this was given, if at all, to so few and so secretly as to indicate greater fear on their part that it might be disclosed than that it might not be put into practice.
