XI

Where Plato was able to acquire the understanding by which he came near to Christian knowledge.

Moreover some of those who are our fellows in the grace of Christ are astonished when they hear or read that Plato had views about God which they can see are in close agreement with the truth of our religion. For this reason some have thought that, when he went to Egypt, he heard the prophet Jeremiah speak or read the books of the prophets in the course of this same journey; and I have recorded their opinion in some of my books.1 But a careful calculation of dates according to chronology shows that Plato was born about one hundred years after the time when Jeremiah uttered his prophecies.2 Plato lived eighty-one years; and from the year of his death until the time when Ptolemy, king of Egypt, sent for the prophetic Scriptures of the Hebrew people from Judea and had them translated and safeguarded by seventy Hebrew scholars who also knew Greek, we find that about sixty years elapsed.
