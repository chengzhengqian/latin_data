XLII

The dispensation of God’s providence whereby the sacred Scriptures of the Old Testament were translated from Hebrew into Greek, that they might become known to the Gentiles.

Moreover, one of the Ptolemies, kings of Egypt, was eager to know and possess these sacred books. For after the forcible rule of Alexander of Macedon,1 surnamed “the Great,” which, though amazing in the highest degree, was not lasting in the least, whereby he had subdued all Asia and indeed almost the whole world, partly by force of arms, partly by terror, and among other parts of the east had entered and won Judea also, his generals did not peaceably divide that vast empire after his death so as to hold possession of it, but rather dissipated it by wars so as to lay everything waste. Egypt then went on to have Ptolemies as her kings, the first of whom, the son of Lagus, carried off many captives from Judea into Egypt.
