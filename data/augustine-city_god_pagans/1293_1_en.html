But many of them have not recognized that he whom they sought and desired has really come, for their hearts are blinded by their preceding merits.3

As for the covenant mentioned previously, where he says: “My covenant was with him”, or in this passage, where he called him “the angel of the covenant,” we must undoubtedly take it to be the new covenant, where eternal rewards are promised, not the old one, which promised mere temporal goods. Most men, being weak and serving the true God only for the sake of these temporal rewards, value such goods greatly, and are consequently distressed when they see how abundantly the irreligious possess them.
