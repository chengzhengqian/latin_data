VII

Whether it was reasonable for Janus and Terminus to be separated into two divinities.

Who then, I ask, is Janus, with whom Varro begins his list?2 The answer is, “He is the world.” This is certainly a short and clear answer. Why then is it said that the beginnings of things belong to him, while the ends belong to another whom they call Terminus?
