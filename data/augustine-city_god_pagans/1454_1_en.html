Therefore those who are free from these evils, whether they are still living in this mortal flesh, or are dead, are even now reigning with Christ in some manner that befits the period during all the intervening time that is symbolized by the number of years, one thousand.

“The rest of them,” he says, “did not come to life.”1 For “the hour now is when the dead shall hear the voice of the Son of God, and they that hear shall live”;2 the rest of them therefore shall not live. By the words that follow, “until the thousand years are ended,” we must understand that they did not become alive at the time when they should have, that is, by passing from death to life. And therefore when the day shall come on which the resurrection of the body as well shall come to pass, they shall come forth from their tombs, not to life but to judgement, that is, to the condemnation which is called the second death. For whoever has not lived until the thousand years are ended, that is, has not during that whole time in which the first resurrection is in operation heard the voice of the Son of God and passed from death into life, he surely shall in the second resurrection, that of the flesh, pass, flesh and all, into the second death. For he goes on to say: “This is the first resurrection. Blessed and holy is he who has a part in this first resurrection,”3 that is, who participates in it.
