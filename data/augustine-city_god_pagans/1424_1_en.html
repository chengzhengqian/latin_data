And who can gather or count the many other examples of this kind?

Now if in these matters we found some consistency even in being illogical during this life, in which, as the sacred psalmist says, “Man is like to vanity, and his days pass like a shadow,”1 so that only the wicked won the transitory good things of this world, and only the good suffered the same kind of ill, this could be referred to the just or even benign judgement of God.
