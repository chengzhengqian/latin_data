If, on the one hand, the gods are angered by the keeping of faith, let them look for faithless men to worship them; if, on the other, men and states can perish visited by many and terrible torments, though the gods be propitious to them, their worship does not yield a return in this sort of happiness. Let those who think that their misfortunes are due to the cessation of rites in honour of their gods, cease to be angry, for it is possible that even if the gods were not only still here, but were also well disposed, they might be, not as now merely whining that they are wretched, but, like Regulus and the Saguntines in the past, even perishing to extinction after ghastly tortures.
