Now, when we say that blindness is a defect of the eyes, we indicate that sight belongs to the nature of eyes, and when we say that deafness is a defect of the ears, we prove that hearing belongs to their nature. So, in like manner, when we say that it is a defect in one created an angel hot to cling to God, this is very clearly a statement that it is essential for such a nature to cling to God. Moreover, no one can adequately imagine or express in words how great a theme for praise there is in so clinging to God as to live for him, derive wisdom from him, find gladness in him and enjoy this great good without death, deviation or trouble. Therefore, even the defect which prevents bad angels from clinging to God serves to make it quite clear, since every defect violates nature, that God created so good a nature in them that separation from God is harmful to it.
