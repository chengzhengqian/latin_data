Being a devotee of deceptive oracles, and relying on their assurance of victory, he burned the ships in which the necessary provisions were transported. Then he pressed on hotly with unrestrained daring and soon was slain to pay for his rashness. His army was left in enemy territory without supplies, so that they could never have escaped from there unless, contrary to the omen of the god Terminus mentioned in the preceding book,1 the boundaries (termini) of the Roman empire had been changed. So the god Terminus yielded to necessity, though he had not yielded to Jupiter. Clearly all these things are ruled and guided by the good pleasure of the one true God. Though the causes be hidden, are they unjust?

XXII

The time and the outcome of wars depend on the will of God.

Likewise the periods of warfare also are fixed in accordance with his will and just judgement and mercy, either to chasten or to comfort the human race; thus some wars are finished more quickly, others more slowly. The war with the pirates was ended by Pompey with incredible speed in a very short time, as well as the Third Punic War by Scipio.
