XVIII

On the third, fortieth, fifteenth and sixty-seventh psalms, in which the death and resurrection of the Lord are prophesied.2

Nor are the oracles of the psalms by any means silent concerning his resurrection. For what other interpretation is there for the song that is put in his mouth in the third psalm: “I fell asleep and slumbered; I awoke from sleep, for the Lord sustained me”?3 Unless there happens to be someone so witless as to believe that the prophet meant to inform us, as if it were a great thing, that he fell asleep and rose from his sleep! The sleep he mentions must mean death, and the awaking a rising from the dead; and that is just the way for him to speak prophetically of Christ.
