XVII

The ark of the testimony and the miraculous signs wrought by God to support the authority of his laws and his promise.

Hence the law of God, as delivered in pronouncements of angels,1 the law in which it is laid down that the one God of gods is to be worshipped by religious rites, but no other gods whatever, was deposited in a chest that has received the name of ark of the testimony. This name clearly indicates, not that our God, who was worshipped in all these rites, made a practice of being boxed up and shut into one place, though his answers and certain signs intelligible to human senses were vouchsafed from the enclosed space of the ark,2 but rather that testimonies to his will were noised abroad from it. For the law itself was also engraved on tablets of stone and deposited, as I have said, in the ark. During the wandering in the wilderness it was carried with due reverence by the priests along with a tabernacle likewise called the tabernacle of the testimony.
