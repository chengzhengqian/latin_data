XXVIII

What Plato, Labeo and even Varro might have contributed to produce a true faith in the resurrection, if their views had coalesced to form a single expression of opinion.

There are some Christians who, because they love Plato for his distinguished literary style of speaking and for some true judgements of his, declare that he adopted a view similar to ours concerning the resurrection of the dead. Cicero, in his treatment of this point in his Republic,1 declares that Plato was rather in a playful mood than in earnest when he said that the doctrine was true.
