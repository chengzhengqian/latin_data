The question whether the holy city ran a continuous course after the flood, or was so interrupted by recurrent periods of irreligion that not a single man worshipped the one true God, is one for which it is difficult to discover any clear answer in the words of Scripture. For from Noah, who with his wife and three sons and their three wives won the privilege of escaping by means of the ark from the all-engulfing flood, to Abraham, we find not a single man mentioned in the canonical books whose devotion to God is clearly attested by any statement in the divine word, apart from the fact that Noah, by a prophetic blessing, commends his sons Shem and Japheth, since he knew by intuition and foresight what was to occur in the distant future.
