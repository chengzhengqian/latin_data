Now his settling in the land of Canaan does not imply that he left Haran after his father’s death, for his father was already dead when Abraham bought the land of which he then first became owner in his own right. Furthermore, God’s words to him when he was already settled in Mesopotamia, that is, when he had already left the land of the Chaldeans: “Depart from your land and from your kindred and from the house of your father,”1 were not said to make him remove his body from that place, which he had already done, but to detach his mind from it. For he had not departed thence in spirit if he was bound by the hope and desire of returning, which hope and desire had to be cut off by the command and help of God and by his own obedience. Surely there is no reason to disbelieve that it was at the time when Nahor followed after his father that Abraham fulfilled the Lord’s command to go out of Haran with Sarah his wife, and Lot, his brother’s son.
