XXVI

About the sacrifices to be offered to God by the saints, which will be pleasing to him as in the days of old and in former years.

Now it was because God wished to show that his city will not then continue in that custom that he said that the sons of Levi would offer sacrifices in righteousness; not, therefore, in sin, and accordingly not for sin. So we may understand that the words that follow: “And the sacrifice of Judah and Jerusalem shall be pleasing to the Lord, as in the days of old, and as in former years,”2 are vainly invoked by the Jews when they promise themselves a return of the past times of sacrifices according to the law of the old covenant.
