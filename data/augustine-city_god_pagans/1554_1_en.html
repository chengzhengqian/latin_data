Behold, the Creator of every nature altered its nature by a miraculous change into something disgustingly different, and the change that came after so long a lapse of time maintains itself still so long after.

Therefore, just as it was possible for God to make such natural kinds as He wished, so it is possible for him to change those natural kinds into whatever he wishes. From this power comes the wild profusion of those marvels which are called omens, signs, portents, prodigies. If I should try to recall and enumerate these, where would this treatise end? The various names monstra, ostenta, portenta, prodigia come from the verbs monstrare ‘show,’ because they show something by a sign, ostendere ‘display,’ portendere ‘spread in front,’ that is, display beforehand, and porro dicere ‘say aforetime,’ that is, predict the future.2 Let the interpreters of these signs observe how at times they are deceived by them, and at times they even speak the truth by the prompting of spirits who aim to involve men worthy of such punishment in the snares of harmful curiosity, and at times they merely stumble on some true prediction in the course of much random talk.
