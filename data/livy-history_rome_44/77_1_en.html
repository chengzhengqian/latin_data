Book XLIV

During the days in camp at Pella, numerous embassies b.c. 168 were received which came, especially from Thessaly, to offer congratulations. Then on receiving the news that Perseus had crossed to Samothrace, the consul set out from Pella and arrived at Amphipolis on the fourth day’s march. The pouring out of the entire populace to meet him showed to any one that Paulus had not robbed them of a just and good king . . .1
