Let those Symplegades, which narrow the Scythian Pontus, hold my hands fastened on this side and that and stretch them apart over the deep; and when the rocks drive together on the recoil, and force up to heaven the sea caught between the closing crags, let me lie as an unresting buffer between the mountains. Why not collect timber, mound it up into a pyre and burn this body spattered with unnatural bloodshed? This is what must be done. I shall return Hercules to the underworld.
