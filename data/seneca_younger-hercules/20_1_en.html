HERCULES

lycus

Did Phoebus face monsters or savage beasts?

amphitryon

The first to stain Phoebus’ arrows was a dragon.

lycus

Do you not know what serious trials he bore while small?

amphitryon

A boy expelled by the lightning bolt from his mother’s womb soon stood at the side of his lightning-wielding father.29 What? Did he who governs the stars and shakes the clouds not lie concealed as an infant in a cave of a hollow cliff? Disquiet is the price of such high births, and there is always a great cost to being born a god.

lycus

If you see someone wretched, be sure that he is mortal.

amphitryon

If you see someone resolute, you cannot call him wretched.

lycus

Can we call him resolute, when the lionskin and club fell from his shoulders and became a present for a girl,30 and when his body shimmered in brightly-colored Sidonian clothes? Can we call him resolute, when his shaggy hair was soaked in nard, and when he moved those renowned hands to the unmanly sound of the tambourine, encircling his fierce brow with an oriental turban?

  • 29The boy was Bacchus, his mother Semele, his father Jupiter.
  • 30Omphale.
