HERCULES

Hard Toil arises and bestirs every care and opens every home. A herdsman, turning loose his flock, gathers them fodder white with frost; a calf, its brow not yet broken by horns, plays freely on the open meadow; dams replenish their empty udders; a boisterous kid wanders lightly on an unsteady course in the soft grass. A sailor, risking life, entrusts his canvas to the winds, as a breeze fills the hanging folds. One fellow, perched on eroded rocks, either prepares his concealed hooks or in suspense watches the prize with his hand kept firm; the line senses the quivering fish. ​ Such are the guiltless lives of those who have quiet peace and a home that delights in its own small means. But in cities giant ambitions roam and trembling fears. One man forgoes his sleep to court the proud portals and hard doorways of the mighty. Another endlessly hoards rich resources, gaping at his treasures and poor amid his piled-up gold. One is dazed by the people’s acclaim; the mob, more shifting than waves at sea, hoists him as he swells with an empty breeze.
