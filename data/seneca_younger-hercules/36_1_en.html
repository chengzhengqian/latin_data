For these alone, to reduce their fear, torches may be carried to relieve the darkness; the rest go sadly through the gloom. What is your state of mind once the light is lost, and each has a sorrowful sense of being buried beneath the whole earth? Inert, that oppressive void—grim darkness, the sullen color of night, the lethargy of a silent world, and empty clouds. Late may old age carry us there! No one comes too late to that from which, once come, he never can return. What is the good of hurrying harsh fate? This crowd that wanders the great earth will all join the shades and set sail on lifeless Cocytus. For you grows all that the rising and setting sun beholds; be lenient, Death, we will come; we are groomed for you. You can be slow, we hasten of ourselves. The hour that first gives life, erodes it.48 ​ A joyful day for Thebes!
