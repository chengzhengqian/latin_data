HERCULES

Touch the altars in thanksgiving, sacrifice fattened animals. Let young women joined with men break into festive dances; let the tillers of the fertile soil set down the yoke and rest. By the hand of Hercules there is peace from the Dawn to the Evening Star, and where the sun at its zenith withholds shadows from bodies. Every tract that is washed by the long circuit of Tethys has been tamed by Alcides’ toil. He crossed the waters of Tartarus, pacified the underworld, and returned. Now no fear remains: nothing lies beyond the underworld. ​ (to Hercules as he enters) To sacrifice, crown your rough hair with your beloved poplar.

ACT FOUR
hercules

Slain by my avenging right hand, Lycus fell sprawling facedown on the ground. Then all who had been the tyrant’s associates died as associates of his punishment too. Now I shall make offerings for my victory to my father and the gods, and honor their altars as they deserve with sacrificed victims.
