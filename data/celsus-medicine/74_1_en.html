If again there is disease in the smaller intestine, vomiting, hiccough, spasm, delirium are bad signs.

In jaundice again it is most perniciousb for the liver to become hard.

If dysentery has seized upon those with disease of the spleen which has then turned into dropsy or into leientery,c scarcely any medical treatment can save them from danger.

The disease of the smaller intestine,d unless resolved, kills within seven days.
