ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

*103 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

temere [rashly] also means “easily”: Plautus [Bacch. 85] . . . It also means “suddenly.” Ennius:

where do you go so suddenly

​

*104 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“that the men had come together and decide by the sword”: This is a correct and ancient usage, for he followed Ennius, who says:

they were determining in discussing momentous affairs

​

*105 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“places pliant legs”: Ennius about cranes:

they pick their way through the bean field and place pliant legs

​

**106 Priscian

iubar [the morning star] they also used to put in both the masculine and the neuter.1 Ennius in the Annals:

meanwhile the pale morning star flees Hyperion’s course

  • 1iubar is properly the morning star (e.g., Varro, Ling. 6.6; Serv. ad Verg. Aen. 4.130); its description here parallels the retreat of the sol albus at 1 Ann. F 43.84.
