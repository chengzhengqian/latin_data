ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

87 Festus

People with plenty of sharp cunning are called sagaces. Afranius in The Girl from Brindisi, “who with such a shrewd heart and rare talent” [Tog. 15 R2–3]. Lucretius in Book 2 “that the keen mind is no less able to recognize these things” [840]. <Ennius also calls a keen hunting> dog

an invincible hound, keen of nose and buoyed by strength1

​

*88 Paul the Deacon, Epitome of Festus

A trifax is a missile three cubits long, which is shot from a catapult. Ennius:

or the wall1 pelted by the trifax might crumble

​

**89 Gellius, Attic Nights

Varro . . . discusses . . . and distinguishes most acutely the difference between “a half” and “halved,” and says that Ennius said this knowledgeably in the Annals:

as if someone were to bring a halved cup of wine

just as the part missing from this cup should be spoken of not as “halved” but “a half.”

  • 1Scansion suggests an early hexameter poet, thus most likely Ennius. Cf. the simile at 10 Ann. F 7. The supplements are supported by related definitions (minus the quotations) at Cic. Div. 1.54; Paul. Fest., pp. 303.2–4, 427.1–2 L.
  • 1Strictly speaking, a house wall and not a fortification. Contrast Inc. Ann. F 90.
