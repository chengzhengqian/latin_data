Ennius drew on these lines:

and then, just like a horse, stuffed full at the manger, that in high spirits snaps his tether and then takes himself through the green and fertile pastures of the field with chest high: he often shakes his mane loftily, his breath sends white foam from his heated soul

Virgil: “as when . . . from his tether” and so on [Aen. 11.492–97].1
