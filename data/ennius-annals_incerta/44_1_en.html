ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

*135 Nonius

armenta [cattle] mostly in the neuter gender, Ennius has it in the feminine:

he himself to the cattle, the same men1

​

*136 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“whom, not lacking in courage, black day took away and plunged in bitter death”: It is a verse of Ennius:

(whom?)1 not lacking in courage

​

*137 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“and light from uplifted nostrils they breathe out”: It is an Ennian verse with the order reversed. For he says:

and they pour out from uplifted nostrils light

​

*138 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“and with a spear stout as a beam”: Ennius:

and with a spear stout as a beam

​

*139 A Glossary

aplustra: the fan [lit., “wing”] of a ship, as Ennius:

the sternpost1

  • 1That is, the fan-shaped sternpost, curving back toward the bow, its ribs both recalling the feathers of a wing and explaining the plural form for what is in fact a single object. The term is borrowed from Greek, ἄφλαστον (pl. at Hdt. 6.114).
  • 1Sense and syntax are obscure, and no proposed emendation has won wide acceptance.
  • 1Whether Virgil’s relative pronoun was part of the phrase he borrowed is uncertain, nor is it clear from Servius’ comment that the Ennian echo ended with egentem.
