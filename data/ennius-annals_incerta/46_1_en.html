*144 Diomedes

Some ancients nevertheless also conjugate possum [I can] with the passive forms potestur and possuntur; we also find quitur and quitus sum among some ancients . . . Accius uses quitus sum [Trag. 662 R.2–3] . . . Caecilius “if it cannot [quitur] be fixed” [Com. 279 R.2–3]. Similarly, we find potestur in Ennius:

nor is he enabled . . . to be drawn back by commands1

laeva [on the left]: favorable. “Favorable powers” he said in accord with divination that the left is favorable, as in the second book “thundered on the left” [Aen. 2.693], since our left is his right and our right is his left, as Ennius says:1
