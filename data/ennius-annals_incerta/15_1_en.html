*44 Porphyrio, Commentary on Horace

“It is worth your while, you who wish no straightforward progress for adulterers, to hear how they struggle on all sides”: He wittily twists lines of Ennius:1

it is worth your while to listen, you who wish straightforward progress for Roman affairs and Latium to increase

but that is wittier because, when Ennius said “you wish,” he [Horace] introduced “you do not wish.”
