ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

*99 Nonius

metus [fear] in the masculine. In the feminine Naevius: “an upsurge of great fear possesses his breast” [BP, F 53 Strzelecki]. Ennius:

nor does any fear hold them; they rest secure in their courage

​

*100 Donatus, Commentary on Terence

“with that sort of spirit” for “of this spirit.” Ennius:

the best Roman youth with fine spirits

​

*101 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“with point reversed he struck the mountain’s hollow in the side”: the order is, “with point reversed into the hollow mountainside he struck,” and elsewhere “into the side . . . he twisted” [2.51]. Others take “into the side” as “the side.” Ennius:

for the heavy onslaught of Death strikes into my side

​

*102 Nonius

lapides [stones] can also be used in the feminine gender, as in Ennius:

in such a mass were the stones then raised up

on the model of Homer, who used “stones” in the feminine gender [Il. 12.287; Od. 19.494].
