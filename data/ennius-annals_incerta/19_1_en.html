ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

**57 Charisius

Some say that in the first declension old writers were accustomed to produce nouns with a dative in -i . . . and further that poets also had this habit in the dative case, as Virgil’s aulai medio [“in the middle of the hall,” Aen. 3.354], and Ennius’

of the fruit-bearing earth

in the Annals.1

​

*58 Gellius, Attic Nights

So too Ennius said rectos cupressos, contrary to the accepted gender of the word, in this verse:

pines with nodding heads and upright cypresses1

The sound of the expression seemed stronger to him, I believe, and more vigorous by saying rectos cupressos rather than rectas.
