ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

*63 Isidore, Origins

tonsilla, a hook of iron or wood to which, when fixed on the shore, a ship’s hawsers are tied. On this Ennius:

they pierce the beach, they tie up to the hooked poles

​

*64 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“I will fill out the number”: as we said above,1 explebo is “I will diminish,” for Ennius says:

they unfilled themselves from the ships and refilled the land

​

*65 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“armed with iron”: either well equipped with weapons or, as Asper says,1 having hearts of iron, i.e., bent on harsh and bloody deeds; as if he followed Ennius, who says:

girt at their breasts with swords

​
