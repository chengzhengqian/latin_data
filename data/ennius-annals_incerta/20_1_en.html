ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

*60 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“and we wage [war] with unconquered men”: and yet above we dealt with “Phrygians captured twice” [9.599]. But he says “unconquered” because of what follows, “not conquered . . . by the sword.” Moreover, he says wonderfully “they are able to abstain” as if he were saying: even if they wanted to, their nature does not permit them to abandon war. Ennius:

he who conquers is not a conqueror unless the conquered acknowledges it

Varro and others say the Trojans were unconquered because they were overcome by ambush. They maintain that those are conquered who surrender themselves to the enemy.

*62 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

The word imber is used of all types of moisture, as Lucretius [1.715]: . . . For old writers used to say imber for all water. Ennius uses imber for sea water:

and Neptune’s water rumbled with ships
