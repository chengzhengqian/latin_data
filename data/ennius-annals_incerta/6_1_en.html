ANNALS: UNPLACED FRAGMENTS

​

**13 Charisius

euax! [hurrah!1] Plautus in Bacchides [247] . . . Ennius, too, in a book of the Annals:

the Latins were refreshed2

​

14 Cicero, On the Republic

From such a [public] life the greatest men win honor, as for example Manius Curius,1

whom none could overcome by sword or by gold

​

**15 Gellius, Attic Nights

Furthermore, why does he [Hyginus] not also fault Ennius, who in the Annals calls not the wings of Daedalus praepes [favorable], as Virgil does, but something quite different:

Brundisium [he says] belted by a handsome, hospitable harbor

​

*16 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“to bellow in the elegant Carinae district”: carinae are buildings made in the form of ships’ keels [carinae]: . . .

  • 1The exclamation of Charisius’ lemma appears in Plautus but is difficult to interpret in the Ennian line as quoted.
  • 2Lit., “water was splashed upon the Latins.” The metaphorical sense is common and also appears in the Plautine line Charisius cites.
  • 1Manius Curius Dentatus (cos. 290, 275, 274 BC), victor over Pyrrhus, for which reason the fragment is often assigned to Book 6.
