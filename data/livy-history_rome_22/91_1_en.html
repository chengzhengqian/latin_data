Even so, you will find both in them and in us good and valiant soldiers, and us you will also find even more ready to fight for our country because we shall have, through your kindness, been ransomed and restored to our fatherland.

“You are recruiting troops of all ages and from every social class—I am told that eight thousand slaves are being put under arms. Our number is no smaller than that, and we can be ransomed for no more than the cost of buying them. But if I compare ourselves with those people I would be insulting the Roman name!187

“When you are debating such a question, senators, I would think you need to consider this point, too: if you lean toward harshness (though we have done nothing to deserve your doing so), think of the enemy to whom you will be abandoning us. To a Pyrrhus, perhaps, who treated his prisoners like guests? Or to a barbarian, and a Carthaginian to boot—and whether the man’s greed or his callousness is the greater is hard to say. Could you but see the chains on your fellow citizens and their squalor and filthy state, that sight would certainly move you no less than if, instead, you saw your legions strewn over the fields of Cannae. You can certainly observe the anxiety and tears of our relatives who are standing at the entrance to the senate house awaiting your response. When these feel such anguish and anxiety for us and for those who are not here, what do you think are the feelings of the men themselves whose lives and freedom now hang in the balance?
