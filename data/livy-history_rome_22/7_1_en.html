“Why, yes, let us just sit before the walls of Arretium!” he said, “This is where our homes and our hearths23 are! Let Hannibal slip through our fingers and make a thorough job of pillaging Italy! Let him reach the walls of Rome, looting and burning everything on his way, and let us not move from here until the senators summon Gaius Flaminius from Arretium, as once they did Camillus from Veii.”24

With this indignant remark he ordered the standards to be quickly pulled from the ground and leaped onto his horse, but the horse suddenly took a stumble and threw the consul over its head. All the bystanders were terrified at this apparently dreadful omen at the start of the campaign, and in addition word was brought that, despite all the standard-bearer’s efforts, a standard could not be pulled out of the ground.

Turning to the messenger, Flaminius said “You are not also bringing me a dispatch from the Senate forbidding me to engage, are you? If their hands are too numb with fear to pull it up, go back and tell them to dig up the standard.”

The column then began to move forward, though the officers, apart from disagreeing with Flaminius’ strategy, were also frightened by the twofold portent; but the rank and file in general were delighted with their commander’s bravado—they had high hopes, without considering on what the hopes were based.
