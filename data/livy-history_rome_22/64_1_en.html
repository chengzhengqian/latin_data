I prefer to have a discerning enemy fearing you than foolish citizens praising you. Hannibal will despise a man who takes all kinds of risks but fear one who makes no foolhardy move. I am not encouraging you to take no action but rather to let reason, not chance, direct the action. Always keep yourself and your every move under control; be armed and on the alert; do not let slip your own opportunity and do not give the enemy his. Everything is clear and in focus for the man not in a rush; haste is improvident and blind.”

40. The consul’s response was not very positive; while admitting that what Fabius said was true, he added that it was not easy to put it into effect. The dictator had been unable to cope with his master of horse, so what power or authority would a consul have over an unruly and reckless colleague? He had in his previous consulship come away badly burned from the fiery wrath of the people,143 he said, and while he wanted everything to turn out well he would rather face the spears of the enemy than the votes of the angry citizens.

They say that Paullus left after this conversation, with the leading senators at his side. The plebeian consul was attended by his plebeian adherents, a group impressive in numbers but lacking men of distinction.

On their arrival in camp, the new and the old forces were combined and the camp split in two sections, so that the newer and smaller was closer to Hannibal but the older one contained the greater part of the troops and all the best and strongest.
