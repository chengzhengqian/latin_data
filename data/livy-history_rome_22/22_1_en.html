These informed him that if he brought his army into Campania he would have an opportunity to capture Capua, but as this was too great a step to take just on the word of his informants, Hannibal hesitated, fluctuating between trust and distrust; but they did convince him to make for Campania from Samnium.

These men he sent off with a warning to strengthen their repeated promises with action and under orders to return to him with more of their people, including some prominent citizens. He then instructed his guide to take him into the territory of Casinum—he had been told by people who knew the area that if he seized control of thepass there he could prevent the Romans from getting through to bring aid to their allies. But a Carthaginian speaker’s difficulty in pronouncing Latin names64 made the man hear “Casilinum” rather than “Casinum,”65 and so he branched off from hiscorrect route and went down through the territory ofAllifae, Caiatia and Cales into the plain of Stellas.66 WhenHannibal, looking around, saw the area enclosed bymountains and rivers, he called the guide and asked where on earth he was. It was when the guide declared that Hannibal would lodge that day at Casilinum that the mistake finally became clear and Hannibal realized that Casinum was far off in another direction. He had the guide flogged and crucified to intimidate the others, established a fortified camp, and sent Maharbal and his cavalry to raid the territory of Falernum.67 The raid extended all the way to the Baths of Sinuessa.68 The Numidians
