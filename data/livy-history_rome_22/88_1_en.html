After that Marcus Junius, who had been named dictator by the authority of the senate, and his master of horse, Tiberius Sempronius, proclaimed a troop levy and recruited younger men who were seventeen or over and some who were still wearing the praetexta.184 From these were constituted a force of four legions and a thousand cavalry. The two men also sent word to the allies and those of Latin status to have troops provided for them, as prescribed by the treaty. They issued orders for the preparation of armor, weaponry and other equipment and took down old enemy spoils that had been hanging in the temples and porticoes. There was also a novel form of recruitment forced on them by the scarcity of free men and the crisis: they bought and armed at state expense eight thousand sturdy young men from the slave population, first asking them one by one if they were willing to serve. Such soldiers were preferred even though they had the chance of ransoming captives at a lower price.

58. For after his enormously successful engagement at Cannae Hannibal had busied himself with affairs more properly the concern of a victor than a soldier still at war. He had brought forth his prisoners, separated them into groups and, as earlier at the Trebia and Lake Trasimene, he addressed the allies kind words and once again released them without ransom. But he also—something he had never done before—summoned the Romans and addressed them in quite gentle terms.
