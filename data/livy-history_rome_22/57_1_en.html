BOOK XXII

There were two additional praetors, Marcus Claudius Marcellus for Sicily and Lucius Postumius Albinus for Gaul. These magistrates were all elected in absentia, and apart from Terentius (the consul elect) none was given an office that he had not previously held. A number of intrepid and active men were passed over because it was felt that under the circumstances no one should be assigned an office that was new to him.

There was also an increase in military personnel, but such are the discrepancies in the sources regarding numbers and kinds of troops involved that I would not venture to give precise information on the size of the additions to infantry and cavalry forces. Some record a supplementary mobilization of ten thousand men, others of four new legions (making a total of eight for the campaign). Certain authors claim that the legionary complement was also increased in infantry and cavalry, with one thousand infantry and one hundred cavalry added to each legion, bringing the strength of each up to five thousand foot and three hundred horse, with the allies contributing double that number of infantry and the same number of cavalry. There were, they claim, 87,200 men under arms in the Roman camp when the battle was fought at Cannae. On one point there is no disagreement: the effort and vigor devoted to the campaign was greater than in previous years because the dictator had raised hopes that the enemy could be defeated.
