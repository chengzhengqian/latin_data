As those who had escaped to Canusium were afforded only the protection of the city walls and shelter by the Canusians, an Apulian woman called Busa, who was known for her noble birth and wealth, also assisted them with gifts of grain, clothing and money for traveling.165 Later, on the conclusion of the war, she was honored by the senate for such generosity.

There were four military tribunes present there: from the first legion Fabius Maximus, whose father had been dictator the previous year; from the second legion Lucius Publicius Bibulus and Publius Cornelius Scipio;166 and from the third Appius Claudius Pulcher, who had very recently been an aedile.167 By general agreement supreme command was vested in Publius Scipio, who was just a young man, and in Appius Claudius. These were discussing the situation of the state with a few others when Publius Furius Philus, son of a former consul, declared that they were nourishing idle hopes, that the republic was completely finished and as good as dead. Some young noblemen, with Lucius Caecilius Metellus at their head, were contemplating taking to the sea in ships to leave Italy and seek asylum with one of the kings, he said.
