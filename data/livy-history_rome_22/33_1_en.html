They now set sail for Onussa83 and disembarked there. They took the city by storm, pillaged it after capturing it, and then headed for New Carthage. Here they raided all the farmlands around the city and eventually set fire to the buildings adjoining the wall and gates. From there, well laden with plunder, the fleet came to Longuntica,84 where large quantities of esparto grass85 had been laid up by Hasdrubal for the use of his fleet. The Romans removed what they could use and all the remainder they burned. They then not only skirted the coastline of the mainland but also sailed over to the island of Ebusus.86 There they spent two days on an intensive but fruitless assault on the island’s capital city, and when they realized time was being wasted on a futile enterprise they turned to pillaging the countryside. After looting and burning a number of villages, they returned to their ships with more plunder than they had taken from the mainland, and delegates from the Balearic islands came to Scipio suing for peace.

The fleet then turned back and returned to the more northerly parts87 of the province, where they were met by numerous ambassadors from all the peoples living north of the Ebro as well as many from the furthest reaches of Spain.
