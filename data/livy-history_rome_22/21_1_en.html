There was a unit of cavalry and light infantry drawn up and ready to meet sudden attacks, and while this provided complete security for his men it was also a threat to scattered enemy foragers. There was no risking everything on an overall engagement; and slight, insignificant brushes with the enemy, conducted from points of safety and with shelter close by, accustomed the men to be less uneasy, after the fright of the earlier defeats, about their courage and their chances.

Fabius, however, found that with such sound strategy he was vexing Hannibal no more than he was his master of horse, whose subordinate authority alone was holding him back from destroying the republic. He was arrogant and impulsive in judgment, and too quick with his tongue. Among a few men at first, and then openly before the rank and file, he would call Fabius’ hesitation idleness and his caution cowardice, imputing to him faults somewhat akin to his virtues; and he would try to exalt himself by maligning his superior, a loathsome practice that has increased because many have found it all too successful.

13. Hannibal crossed into Samnium from the territory of the Hirpini, laid waste the agricultural land of Beneventum, and took the city of Telesia;63 and he was by this also deliberately provoking the Roman commander, hoping to infuriate him with all the humiliation he inflicted on the allies and bring him down to a battle on level ground.
