When these things had been announced as reported, with witnesses ushered into the senate house, the consul sought the senators’ opinions on appropriate rites. It was decided that some prodigies should be expiated with full-grown animals and others with suckling animals,8 and that there be a three-day period of supplication9 at all the couches of the gods. Beyond that, the decemvirs were to study the books,10 and such measures were to be taken as they interpreted from the holy11 verses to be pleasing to the gods.

On the advice of the decemvirs, it was decreed that in the first instance a gift of a fifty-pound thunderbolt of gold should be made to Jupiter and gifts in silver to Juno and Minerva. Sacrifices of full-grown victims were to be made to Queen Juno on the Aventine and to Juno Sospita at Lanuvium.12 In addition, the married women were to contribute as much money as each could afford and bear it as a gift to Queen Juno on the Aventine, where a lectisternium13 was also to be held. Even freedwomen were required to contribute money, according to their means, for a gift to be given to Feronia.14
