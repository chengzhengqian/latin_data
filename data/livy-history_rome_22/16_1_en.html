He convinced them to have the decemvirs consult the Sibylline books,43 a decision rarely taken other than when dire prodigies have been reported. When the decemvirs consulted the Books of Fate, they reported to the senators that there had been some irregularity in the formulation of the vow made to Mars for that war44 and that it should be formulated again and on a more generous scale, with Great Games vowed to Jupiter and temples to Venus of Eryx and Mens.45 A supplication46 and a lectisternium should also be held, and a vow taken to hold a Sacred Spring47 if the campaign turned out successfully and the republic remained in the same condition in which it had been before the war. As conduct of the war would keep Fabius busy, the senate, on the advice of the College of Pontiffs,48 instructed the praetor Marcus Aemilius to see that all these measures were quickly implemented.
