The Gauls and Spaniards had shields almost identical in shape, but swords differing in size and appearance. Those of the Gauls were very long and without points whereas those of the Spaniard, whose practice was to lunge at his enemy rather than to slash, were short, easy to wield, and pointed. These tribes provided a more terrifying spectacle than others because of their large physique and also their general appearance. The Gauls were bare above the navel; the Spaniards had taken up their position dressed in purple-fringed tunics that shone with an amazing whiteness. The number of infantry in the battle line that day totaled forty thousand, the cavalry ten thousand. The commanders on the wings were Hasdrubal on the left and Maharbal on the right; Hannibal himself held the center with his brother Mago.

Whether they owed their positions to choice or pure chance, both sides were fortunate enough to have the sun at an angle, the Romans facing south and the Carthaginians north; but the wind, locally known as the Volturnus,153 now rose in the direction of the Romans, rolling large clouds of dust right into their faces and obstructing their vision.

47. When the battle cry went up there was a charge bythe auxiliary forces, and the fighting between the light infantry began. Then the Carthaginian left wing, composed of Gallic and Spanish cavalry, clashed with the Roman right; but it was no typical cavalry engagement. Because there was no room left for encircling maneuvers since the river hemmed them in on one side and the lines of infantry on the other, they were forced to charge head-on.
