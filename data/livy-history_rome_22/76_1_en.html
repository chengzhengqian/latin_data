As for me, let me breathe my last amid this massacre of my men, so I can avoid once more standing trial157 after my consulship or coming forward to accuse my colleague in order to defend my innocence by blaming another.”

As they so conversed, they were overtaken first by a crowd of their fellow citizens in flight and then by the enemy. The enemy, not knowing who the consul was, showered him with projectiles, and in the confusion Lentulus was whisked away to safety by his horse.

There followed disordered flight everywhere. Seven thousand men made good their escape to the smaller camp, ten thousand to the larger. Some two thousand escaped to the actual village of Cannae but were immediately surrounded by Carthalo and his cavalry since the town had no fortifications protecting it. Whether by accident or design, the other consul had joined none of these particular groups of fugitives but slipped away to Venusia with about fifty cavalrymen.
