Speeches in this vein having no effect, he saw to the election to the consulship of Marcus Atilius Regulus and, to avoid being involved in the dispute over the rights of command, slipped away to his army after dark on the day before the motion was to be brought forward.

When the plebeian assembly met at dawn, there was unspoken animosity toward the dictator and approval for his master of horse rather than men having the boldness to step forward to speak on behalf of a measure that was widely favored; and so, despite overwhelming enthusiasm for it, the motion lacked substantive support. One man alone was found to speak in favor of the bill: Gaius Terentius Varro, who had been praetor the previous year and whose family background was not just lowly but downright sordid. They say his father had been a butcher, one who peddled his meat himself102 and had employed this very son in the degrading activities of his trade.

26. When he was a young man, the money from that sort of trade that was left to him by his father gave him the confidence to hope for a more respectable position. Public life and the courts103 appealed to him and, using a blustering rhetoric on behalf of disreputable individuals and causes and against the property and reputations of decent people, he first achieved notoriety with the masses and then civil offices. He gained the quaestorship, two aedileships (plebeian and curule), and eventually the praetorship, at which point he presumed to entertain hopes of a consulship.
