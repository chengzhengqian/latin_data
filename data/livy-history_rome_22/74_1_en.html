48. By now battle had also been joined on the Roman left, where the allied cavalry had been facing the Numidians. The fighting was sluggish at first and had commenced with a Carthaginian trick.155 Some five hundred Numidians kept swords hidden under their corselets apart from their regular weapons, and posing as deserters they rode over from their side with their shields behind their backs. Swiftly dismounting, and throwing down their shields and spears at their enemies’ feet, they were welcomed into the center of the Roman ranks, taken to the rear of the lines and told to wait there at the back. While the engagement was under way at all points they quietly remained there; but when the battle had everyone’s attention and eyes preoccupied, they seized shields that lay scattered here and there among the piles of corpses and attacked the Roman line from behind. Hacking at soldiers’ backs and severing their hamstrings, they caused enormous loss of life and a lot more panic and confusion. At that point, when there was terrified flight in one place and obstinate but quite hopeless fighting in another, Hasdrubal, the commander in that quarter, withdrew the Numidians from the center of the line since they were showing little spirit in fighting their opponents and sent them in pursuit of the scattered fugitives. He then brought in the Spaniards and the Gauls to support the African infantrymen, who were by now almost exhausted, more from massacring their foe than fighting them.
