Those who had broken ranks and scattered far and wide ran back from all parts to join the new line of battle;106 others who had turned and run off in large numbers together now swung round to face the enemy. They formed a circle, and at one moment slowed their retreat and at the next bunched together to stand their ground. By now almost a single line had been formed from the defeated and the fresh army, and they were advancing on the enemy, when the Carthaginian commander sounded the retreat. This was Hannibal openly declaring that, while Minucius had been beaten by him, he had been beaten by Fabius.

When this day of wavering fortunes was mostly over and they had returned to camp, Minucius summoned his men and said: “Men, I have often been told that the best man is one who himself gives helpful advice and that after him it is the man who listens to good advice; but the most inadequate is the one who can neither give advice nor accept another’s. In our case, since we have been denied the top level of talent and ability, let us grasp the second ranking—the intermediate—and while we learn to command let us resolve to be guided by a man with insight. Let us join our camp with Fabius’. When we have borne the standards to his headquarters—where I shall address him as ‘father,’ as the benefit he conferred on us and his exalted position requires—you, men, are to salute as ‘patrons’107 those who just now gave you protection with their weapons and right hands; and, if nothing else, this day will at least have brought us honor for having grateful hearts.”
