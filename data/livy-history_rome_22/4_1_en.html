When this was done, the decemvirs held a sacrifice of full-grown victims in the forum at Ardea.15 Finally, when the month of December came round, a sacrifice was offered at the temple of Saturn in Rome, and orders were issued for a lectisternium—and the senators laid out the couch for it—and a public feast. Then, throughout the city, the cry “Saturnalia”16 went up for a day and a night, and the people were instructed to keep that day as a holiday and observe it ever after.

2. While the consul17 was busy at Rome propitiating the gods and mobilizing troops, Hannibal had left his winter quarters as there was a report that the consul Flaminius had already arrived in Arretium. Thus, although shown an easier but longer route, he took the shorter path through the marshes, where the River Arno had at that time flooded them more than usual. Hannibal directed the Spaniards and Africans—the very best of his veteran troops—to march in front, taking their baggage within their ranks so they would not lack vital supplies if compelled to halt at any stage. He ordered the Gauls to follow and form the center of the column, while the cavalry were to be at the end. Mago,18 along with the light-armed Numidians, was to close up the column, having the special charge of keeping the Gauls in check, preventing them from slipping away or stopping if they tired of the effort of the grueling journey (the race having little tolerance for such hardships19).
