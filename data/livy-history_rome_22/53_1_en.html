To such an extent did the Romans refuse to overlook their interests anywhere in the world, even those far distant, despite the burden of this massive war on their backs.

Religious concern also arose over a temple of Concord that the praetor Lucius Manlius had vowed two years earlier during a mutiny of the troops in Gaul—its building had to that day not been put up for contract. Accordingly, duumvirs were expressly appointed for that purpose by the urban praetor Marcus Aemilius: Gaius Pupius and Caeso Quinctius Flamininus, who contracted out the building of the temple on the citadel.125
