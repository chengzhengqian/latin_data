For searching out and retrieving such information, said Fabius, active young men should be used; and the senators themselves, since there was a shortage of magistrates, should suppress any disturbance or panic, keep women away from public places and compel them all to remain within their homes, and place restrictions on family mourning. They should impose citywide silence and see that bearers of any news were brought before the praetors—people should wait at home for news touching themselves. In addition, they must post sentinels at the gates to prevent anyone from leaving the city, and thus force people to see that their only hope lay in preserving the city and its defenses. The senators should be recalled to the house when the unrest died down and defensive measures for the city should then be brought up for discussion.

56. As there was unanimous support for this proposal without debate,172 the crowds were removed from the forum by the magistrates, and the senators went off in different directions to quell the disturbance. Then, finally, a letter arrived from the consul Gaius Terentius reporting that the consul Lucius Aemilius and his army had been destroyed, and that he himself was at Canusium bringing together the wreckage from that catastrophic defeat. He had some ten thousand men with him, he said, disordered and unassigned to companies, and the Carthaginian—with neither the spirit of a victor nor the comportment of a great leader—was ensconced at Cannae haggling over prisoners’ ransoms and other plunder.
