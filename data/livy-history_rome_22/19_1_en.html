Fabius then set off along the Flaminian Way to meet the consul and his army; and when, near Ocriculum on the banks of the Tiber,55 he caught sight of the column and the consul proceeding toward him with his cavalry, he †sent an attendant†56 to instruct the consul to come to the dictator without his lictors. Since Servilius followed the order, the meeting of the two men did much to impress on citizens and allies the grandeur of the dictator’s office—with the passage of time they had almost forgotten the authority it carried.57 At this point a letter was brought from the city with news that transport vessels carrying supplies from Ostia to the army in Spain had been captured off the port of Cosa58 by a Carthaginian fleet. The consul was therefore ordered to leave for Ostia immediately, to man with marines and crews59 the ships lying at anchor at Rome or Ostia, and with them to give chase to the enemy fleet and patrol the coasts of Italy. Large numbers of men were conscripted in Rome; even freedmen who had children and were of military age60 had sworn the oath of allegiance. Members of this urban force who were under thirty five were assigned to the ships and the others were left to guard the city.
