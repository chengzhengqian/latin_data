Captives released without payment by Pyrrhus once returned to Rome; but they returned with ambassadors, leading men of the state sent to ransom them. Am I to return to my fatherland a citizen not worth the payment of three hundred coins? Everyone has his own thoughts on this, senators. I know that my life and personal safety are at stake; but the risk to my reputation concerns me more, in case we leave here condemned and rejected by you. For people will not believe that you were penny-pinching about the ransom.”

60. When he finished speaking, a tearful wail immediately went up from the crowd in the comitium, and they stretched out their hands toward the senate house, begging the senators to restore to them their children, their brothers and their relatives. Fear and the dire situation had now also brought women to join the crowd of men in the Forum.

The Senate was cleared of unauthorized persons and the debate began. A variety of opinions was put forward there, some supporting the ransom of the prisoners at state expense and others wanting no public expenditure but saying that ransoms paid from private sources should not be disallowed (and any lacking ready money being given treasury loans, but with the state indemnified though guarantors or mortgages).
