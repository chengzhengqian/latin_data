It was by the nobles, he said, who had been thirsting for war for many a year, that Hannibal had been brought into Italy, and by them, too, that the war was being fraudulently prolonged when it could be finished off. That operations could be successfully conducted by four united legions had been demonstrated by Marcus Minucius’ successful engagement during Fabius’ absence, but now two legions had been thrown before the enemy to be slaughtered and then had been pulled back from the very brink of slaughter—just so that the words “father” and “patron” could be applied to the man who had prevented the Romans from conquering before he prevented them from being conquered. After this, the consuls had drawn out the war by Fabius’ tactics when they could have been finishing it off. This was a pact made between all the nobles, and the Romans would see no end to the war until they had elected as consul a real plebeian, that is a “new man.”130 For the plebeians who had been made nobles were now initiates in the same rites as them, and they had started looking down on the plebs ever since they themselves hadstopped being looked down upon by the senators. To whom was it not obvious that the aim of all this activity and maneuvering for an interregnum was to ensure that the elections remained under the control of the senate?
