Hannibal had become hopeful that the consuls would afford him the chance to do battle since the area was naturally suited for a cavalry engagement, the branch of his forces in which he was unbeatable. He therefore deployed his battle line and provoked his enemy with a sudden charge by his Numidians. At this there was upheaval once more in the Roman camps, with the men insubordinate and the consuls in disagreement. Paullus taunted Varro with the recklessness of Sempronius and Flaminius, while Varro taunted Paullus with Fabius as an attractive model for cowardly and indolent leaders, and he also called on gods and men to witness that no blame lay with him for Hannibal’s occupation of Italy virtually through right of possession.152 His hands remained tied because of his colleague, he said, and soldiers angry and eager to fight were having their swords and weapons taken from them. Paullus replied that if the legions were betrayed and sacrificed to a hasty and ill-advised engagement, he would be blameless for any misfortune befalling them but would none the less have to share all the consequences. So Varro must see to it that those men with glib and impetuous tongues had hands just as vigorous in battle.

45. While time was being wasted on squabbling rather than planning, Hannibal had kept his line in formation until late in the day. He then withdrew his Numidians from it and, while he was taking all the other troops back to camp, he sent them over the river to attack the water detail from the smaller of the Roman camps.
