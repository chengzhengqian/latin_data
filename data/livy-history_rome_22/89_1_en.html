He was therefore giving the prisoners the opportunity to ransom themselves: the price would be five hundred quadrigati per cavalryman, three hundred per infantryman and one hundred per slave.

Although this was for the cavalrymen something of an increase in the price that they had negotiated on their surrender, they gladly accepted any condition for a settlement. It was decided that ten spokesmen elected by them should go to the senate in Rome, and the only guarantee taken from them was a vow to return. A Carthaginian nobleman, Carthalo, was sent with them to bring the terms in the event of the Romans inclining toward a peace.

After they left the camp one of the spokesmen—a man of truly un-Roman character—pretended he had forgotten something, returned to the camp simply for the purpose of discharging the oath, and caught up with his companions before nightfall. When news of their coming was brought to Rome, a lictor was sent to meet Carthalo and inform him in the name of the dictator that he was to leave Roman territory before dark.
