Fabius kept marching along the heights half way between the enemy column and the city of Rome, neither losing contact with Hannibal nor engaging him. On leaving Paelignian territory the Carthaginian changed course and, heading back to Apulia, reached Gereonium,80 a town abandoned by its inhabitants, who were fearful because a section of its walls had collapsed. The dictator then established a fortified camp in the region of Larinum.81

When recalled to Rome from there for religious duties, he not only pressed his authority with his master of horse but also offered him personal advice and almost pleaded with him to trust more to strategy than luck and to follow his lead rather than that of Sempronius and Flaminius. Minucius should not think that almost a whole summerspent playing cat and mouse with the enemy was timewasted, he said—physicians, too, sometimes find rest more beneficial than exercise and activity. It was no small achievement to have ended defeats by an enemy so often victorious, and to have recovered their breath after their interminable disasters, he said; and after offering such advice—all in vain—to his master of horse, he set off for Rome.

19. At the start of the summer in which this was taking place, land and sea operations also commenced in Spain. To a number of fully manned and fully equipped vessels that he had taken over from his brother Hasdrubal added a further ten. He then passed on command of a fleet of forty to Himilco and set off from New Carthage.
