31. While this was going on in Italy, the consul Gnaeus Servilius Geminus sailed around the coastline of Sardinia and Corsica with a fleet of a hundred and twenty ships112 and, after taking hostages from both islands, crossed over to Africa. Before landing on the mainland he laid waste the island of Meninx, took ten talents of silver from the inhabitants of Cercina113 as the price for their land not to be burned and pillaged, and then proceeded to the coast of Africa and disembarked his troops. From there soldiers and ships’ crews were led off on marauding expeditions, and they spread out as widely as if they were plundering uninhabited islands. Their lack of caution thus soon brought them into an ambush when stragglers were surrounded by bands of natives—men ignorant of the land by men who knew it—and they were driven back to the ships in ignominious flight and with heavy casualties.114 Some one thousand men were lost, including the quaestor Tiberius Sempronius Blaesus.
