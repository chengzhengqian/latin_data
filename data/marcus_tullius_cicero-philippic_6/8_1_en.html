But then there is the statue that 15takes the prize, about which I would not be able to speak without laughing if the times were better. “To Lucius Antonius, our patron, from the Exchange.”19 Really? Is the Exchange now numbered among Lucius Antonius’ clientele? Who was ever found in that Exchange to lend Lucius Antonius a thousand sesterces?

[6] But I have dwelt too long on trifles: let us get back to our cause and our war. All the same, it was not irrelevant for you to pass in review certain personalities, so that in your own minds you could think about the kind of people against whom war is being waged.

Now I urge you, Men of Rome, to wait calmly for the return of the envoys, even though a different course would have been better. The cause has lost something in speed, but the cause has gained something too. For when the envoys 16report, as report they surely will, that Antonius is not in your control, nor in the senate’s, who will be so wicked a citizen as to think that this man should be considered a citizen?
