For being led by her maids to the monastery (being hard by adjoining), where she declared she had full belief to be healed, she was brought unto the burial-ground: and as she prayed there for a space upon her knees she was thought worthy to have her request heard forthwith. For rising up from her prayer, before she went out of the place she recovered the benefit of the sight that she sought: and she that had been led thither by the hands of waiting-maids returned home joyfully, walking freely on her feet by herself; so that it might seem that she lost the light of this world only for this end, that she might shew by her healing how great the light and what grace of mighty working is that Christ’s saints have in heaven.
