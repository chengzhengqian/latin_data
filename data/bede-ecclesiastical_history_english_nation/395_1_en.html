And in the winter season, when half broken pieces of ice dropped down all about him, which too he had sometimes broken himself, to have space of standing or plunging in the river, divers men seeing him, said: “It is a strange ease, brother Drythelm,” for so the man was called, “that you can possibly suffer such bitter cold.” He answered simply, for he was but a man of simple nature and sober-spirited: “I have seen greater cold.” And when they said: “It is marvel that you will live so continent and austere a life,” he answered: “I have seen more austerity.” And so until the day of his being called hence, for the unwearied desire he had of heavenly felicity, he would subdue his aged body with daily fastings, and by his words and conversation brought salvation to many.
