But he died the 15th day of February, and left Egfrid his son inheritor of his realm; in the third year of whose reign Theodore gathered a council of bishops1 along with many teachers of the Church, such as loved and understood the canonical statutes of the fathers. Who being assembled together, he began with that mind that became a bishop to teach such things to be observed as were convenient for the unity and peace of the Church. Of which synodical proceeding the record is as follows:
