Chapter XVII

What also he mentioned touching the place of the Lord’s ascension, and the tombs of the patriarchs.

The author above mentioned speaketh also in this wise touching the place of the Lord’s ascension:

“Mount Olivet is as high as Mount Sion but excels it in breadth and length: there groweth few trees but vines and olives; wheat and barley too it bringeth forth in good store. For it is not scrubby1 either, but the nature of the soil is good for grass and flowers: upon the topmost height thereof, where the Lord ascended to the heavens, standeth a great round church, having 3 porches round in a circuit, vaulted and covered over.
