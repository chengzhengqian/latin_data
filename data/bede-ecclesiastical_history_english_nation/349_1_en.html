all Cuthbert was chosen to be bishop of the church of Lindisfarne. And when he could in no wise be drawn out of his hermitage for all the messengers and letters that were sent him, at last the foresaid king himself, having the most holy bishop Trumwine with him, and also other religious persons and men of authority, did pass over into the island. Many too of the brethren of the isle of Lindisfarne itself came with them for this very purpose, all which on their knees earnestly desired him for the Lord’s sake, and with weeping tears besought him; until they made his own eyes full of tears and gat him out of his sweet retreat and brought him to the synod. And when he came thither, though much against his will, he was overcome by the one assent and will of all the rest, and was made to submit his neck to bear the yoke and office of a bishop: being forced thereto most of all by the word of Boisil the servant of the Lord, who, when with the prophetical spirit which he had he did declare all the things that should befall Cuthbert, had also foretold that he should be bishop. And yet his ordination was not appointed to be straightway, but after the winter had passed, which was then at hand, it was finished at York in the solemnization of the feast of Easter itself in the presence of the foresaid king Egfrid, and there came to his consecration seven bishops, among whom Theodore of blessed memory held the chief place.
