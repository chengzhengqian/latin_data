But that we should not think the same 7 days to be counted from the 14th to the 20th, He added straightways: ‘The first day there shall be no leaven in your houses. Whosoever eateth leavened bread from the first day until the seventh, that soul shall be cut off from Israel,’ and so forth, till He says: ‘For in this self-same day will I bring your army out of the land of Egypt.’

“He calleth then that the first day of unleavened bread in the which he was to bring their army out of Egypt. But it is manifest that they were not brought out the 14th day, in the evening whereof the lamb was slain, and which is properly called the Passover or Phase; but in the 15th day they were brought out of Egypt, as it is evidently written in the book of Numbers2: ‘They departed therefore from Rameses in the 15th day of the first month; on the morrow after the Phase the children of Israel went out with an high hand.’ Therefore the seven days of unleavened bread, in the first of which the people of the Lord was brought out of Egypt,
