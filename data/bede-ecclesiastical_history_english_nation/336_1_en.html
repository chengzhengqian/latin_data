Chapter XXV

What manner of vision appeared to a certain man of God, before that the monastery of the town of Coludi was consumed with fire.

About this time the monastery of virgins which they call Coldingham, of which too we have made mention above, was through fault of negligence destroyed with fire. Which thing notwithstanding happened by reason of the wickedness of them that dwelt therein, and especially of them which seemed to be the elders thereof, as all that knew it could very easily see.
