I personally, if the vintage is wet, usually mix a triens of the preservative in two amphorae; if it is dry, a quadrans, so that the quantity of must is four urnae, an urna being twenty-four sextarii. I am aware that some husbandmen have put a quadrans of the preservative in each amphora, but that they were obliged to do so owing to the excessive weakness of the wine, which scarcely kept sound for thirty days. However, if there is plenty of wood,8 it is better to boil the must and clear off all the scum with the dregs; if this is done a tenth part will be lost, but the rest keeps good forever. But if there is a scarcity of wood, it will be necessary to mix an ounce of what is called the “flower” of marble or plaster and also two sexiarii of must boiled down to a third of its original quantity with each amphora. This, though it does not make the flavour of the wine last forever, yet at any rate generally preserves it until another vintage.
