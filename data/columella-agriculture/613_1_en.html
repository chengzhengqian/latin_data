At night, however, the vessel must be covered so that the dew may not fall upon it. When we have prepared the pitch in this manner and wish to preserve wines when they have finished fermenting for the second time, we shall add two cyathi of the above-mentioned pitch to forty-eight sextarii of must in the following manner: we shall have to take two sextarii of must from the3 quantity which we are about to preserve, and then gradually pour must from these two sextarii into a sextarius of pitch and turn it over and over with the hand, as one does honey-water, to make it amalgamate more easily, and when the whole of the two sextarii have amalgamated with the pitch and formed, as it were, a single substance, it will then be proper to pour them into the vessel from which we had taken them and to stir them with a wooden ladle so that the preservative may be thoroughly mixed in.
