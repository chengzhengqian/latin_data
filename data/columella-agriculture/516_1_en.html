Therefore, as I have7 said, your future bailiff must be taught and must be hardened from boyhood to the operations of husbandry and must be first tested by many trials to see not only whether he has thoroughly learned the science of farming but also whether he shows fidelity and attachment to his master, for, without these qualities, the most perfect knowledge possessed by a bailiff is of no use. The most important thing in this kind of superintendence is to know and estimate what duties and what tasks should be enjoined on each person; for the strongest man could not carry out an order, unless he knows what he has to do, nor the most skilful, if he lacks the strength. The nature therefore of each operation must be8 taken into consideration; for some tasks require strength only, such as the moving and carrying of heavy loads, others require a combination of strength and skill, as in digging and ploughing and mowing field crops and meadows; to some less strength and more skill is applied as in the pruning and grafting of a vineyard, and for some knowledge is of the chief importance, for instance, in the feeding and doctoring of cattle.
