I think that it is not out of place, Publius Silvinus, as a conclusion to my task which is now finished, to declare to my future readers, if there be any who deign to take cognizance of such things, that I have never doubted that the number of subjects which might have found a place in the material of my treatise is almost infinite, but I have deemed it proper to set forth only such things as seemed most essential. However, nature has not bestowed even upon the grey-headed a practical knowledge of all things; for even those who are held to be the wisest of mortals are said to have known many things, but not all.
