Book X

Now is the time, if pickles cheap you seek, To plant the caper and harsh elecampane And threateninga fennel; creeping roots of mint 120And fragrant flowers of dill are spaced now And rue, which the Palladian berry’sb taste Improves, and mustard which will make him weep, Whoe’er provokes it; now the roots are set Of alexandersc dark, the weepy onion, Likewise the herb which seasons draughts of milkd And will remove the brand, signal of flight, From brows of runagates, and, by its name In the Greek tongue, its virtue demonstrates.e Then too is sown the plant,f which o’er the world To common folk alike and haughty King Its stalks in winter and in spring its sprouts In plenty yields, grown on the turf-clad shore 130Of ancient Cumae,g in Marrucine lands,h At Signiai on the Lepine mountain’s side, On Capua’sj rich plains, in garden plots Nigh to the Caudine Forks,k at Stabiael Famed for its fountains, in Vesuvian fields, At learned Naplesm by Sebethis’n stream Bedewed, and where the sweet Pompeian marsh To Herculean salt-pits neighbour lies,o
  • aBecause used for caning schoolboys.
  • bThe olive which was sacred to Pallas Athena.
  • dSee Book XII. 8. 3, where Lepidium silvestre is said to be used for curdling milk.
  • eColumella connects the name of this herb (λεπίδιον) with the verb λεπίζειν, which means to peal off the husk or skin.
  • fI.e. the cabbage.
  • gA former Greek colony on the coast of Calabria.
  • hThe district round Pescara in the Abruzzi.
  • iThe modern Sagni about forty miles S.E. of Rome.
  • jIn Campania.
  • kSixteen miles E. of Capua.
  • lThe modern Castellamare, 17 miles S.E. of Naples. The ancient town was destroyed in a.d. 79 at the same time as Pompeii.
  • mParthenope, a poetical name for Naples.
  • nA small river which flows into the Gulf of Naples.
  • oPompeii and Herculaneum on the Gulf of Naples.
