  • aAccording to tradition, Cincinnatus was called from the plough to the dictatorship in 458 b.c., to save the Roman army besieged by the Aequians on Mt. Algidus. He delivered the consul Minucius and his army, resigned the dictatorship, and returned to his little farm after holding the office only sixteen days. Cf. Livy, III. 26–29.
  • bOne iugerum = about three-fifths of an acre.
  • cConsul in 282 and 278 b.c., his noble conduct toward Pyrrhus, king of Epirus, led to the evacuation of Italy by that king.
  • dConsul in 290 and 275 b.c. Famous for his frugality and his conquests over the Samnites, Sabines, Lucanians, and Pyrrhus, he retired to his farm, refusing all share in the booty.
