You then put them into a vessel and fill the jar with must boiled down to half or a third of its original volume, putting on the top a plug of dry fennel, so as to press the olives down. Most people, however, mix three3 parts, and some two parts, of boiled-down must or honey with one of vinegar and preserve the olives with this liquid.
