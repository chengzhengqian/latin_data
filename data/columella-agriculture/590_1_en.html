And if you wish to make more brine, you will pour the brine already made into vessels well daubed with pitch and keep it covered up in the sun; for the action of the sun takes away all mustiness and causes a pleasant odour. There is also another method of proving that the brine is ripe; for, when you plunge a piece of fresh cheese into it, if it sinks to the bottom, you will know that it is still crude, but, if it floats on the surface, you will know that it has reached maturity.

All these are2 conveniently preserved by one method of pickling, that is to say, a mixture of two-thirds of vinegar and one-third of hard brine.
