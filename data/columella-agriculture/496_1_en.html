Book X

Spare not with mattocks broad her inmost parts To scrape and mingle with the topmost turf Yet warm, that they may lie for frosts to sear Exposed to Caurus’a wrath and chilling scourge, That savage Boreasb may bind and Eurusc loose them. But when bright Zephyrd with his sun-warmed breeze Thaws the Riphaeane winter’s numbing frost, And Orpheus’ Lyref deserts the starry pole And dives into the deep, and swallows hail 80Spring’s advent at their nests, the gardener Should with rich mould or asses’ solid dung Or other ordure glut the starving earth Bearing full baskets straining with the weight, Nor should he hesitate to bring as food For new-ploughed fallow-ground whatever stuff The privy vomits from its filthy sewers. Now let him with the hoe’s well-sharpened edge Again attack earth’s surface packed with rain And hard with frost; then with the tooth of rake Or broken mattock mix the living turf 90With clods of earth and all the crumbling wealth Of the ripe field set free; then let him take The shining hoe, worn by the soil, and trace Straight, narrow ridges from the opposing bounds And these across with narrow paths divide.
