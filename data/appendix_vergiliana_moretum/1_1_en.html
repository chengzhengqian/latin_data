In a burned-out log there still remained some tiny kindling, while ashes concealed the glow of live coal beneath. Bending low his head, he brings his lamp forward to the embers, draws out with a needle the dried-up wick, and with many a puff wakes up the sluggish fire. Rousing at last a flame, though hard the task, he draws back, and with sheltering hand guards the light from the draught, while his key, peeping through, unlocks the closet door. On the ground was poured a poor heap of corn: from this he helps himself to as much as the measure, which runs to twice eight pounds in weight, would hold.
