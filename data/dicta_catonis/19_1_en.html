Cato

60None him arraigns who wants truth said to him. ’Tis a poor win to seek to beat your own. Calm looks do sometimes cloak a loathsome mind. Another’s woe consoles all wretched folk. Speak the truth freely, though the truth be hard. 65The steadfast strive to end a task begun. Unfair the man who approves his own acts only. Long lapse of time consumes all handiwork. The mind’s pretences will not long endure. Let man by skill make good unwelcome chance. 70Hard work grows easy to the practised hand. Long leisure saps the strength which work upbuilds. That worth may shine, let rest be free from rust. Sweet task it is to face a task and win. a The great aim high; plain folk ply humbler tasks. 75Whate’er may be recovered think not lost. Sin not in the hour when you may safely sin. Sad reaping comes, if joyful pleasure wanes. Champion the absent’gainst backbiting tongues.
