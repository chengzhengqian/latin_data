Cato

5. Test but the life and ways of them who blame Their fellows; all, you’ll find, have faults the same. 6. Gear that may harm forgo, however dear: Wealth yields to usefulness in time of fear. 7. Be mild or firm as circumstances claim: A sage may change his outlook free from blame. 8. A wife’s complaints about the slaves mistrust: Her husband’s favourite wakens her disgust. 9. In warning one who fain would not attend, Drop not the endeavour, should he be your friend. 10. To fight the wordy you must words eschew: Speech is bestowed on all, sound sense on few. 11. Love other men; yet be your own true friend: Do good to good men so no loss attend. 12. Shun tattling, and the newest thing to say Seek not: closed lips hurt no one—speaking may. 13. Think not hopes built on promises are sure: Much said by many seldom proves secure. 14. When someone praises you, be judge alone: Trust not men’s judgement of you, but your own. 15. Let others’ kindness frankly be revealed; Your own good turns to others keep concealed. 16. When you, grown old, blame what folk do or say, Think what you did in your own youthful day. 17. Reck not of what the whispering lip lets fall: Self-conscious men think they’re the talk of all. 18. In happy hours beware the hapless lot: What the start promises, the end is not.
