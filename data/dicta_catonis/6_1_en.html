33. Since our frail life through dangers sure must run, Count every day that comes as something won. 34. Yield to your mate some points you well might score: Compliance keeps your friends attached the more. 35. In mighty aims small cost you must not spare; For those whom trifles cramp high fame is rare. 36. Beware of strife with one close linked to thee: Anger breeds hate, love feeds on harmony. 37. If, stung by slaves’ misdeeds, you’ve angry grown, Control yourself and so hurt not your own. 38. Sometimes put up with him you might beat down; Of human virtues patience is the crown. 39. What you have won conserve at cost of pains: Want must increase, when labour brings no gains. 40. Though sometimes on your friends you lavish gear, In giving always to yourself keep near. a
