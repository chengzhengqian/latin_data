Cato

12. Do not for dowry’s sake espouse a wife, Nor wish to keep her, if she causes strife. 13. From men’s behaviour learn what to pursue Or shun. The life of others gives the cue. 14. Try what you can, lest by hard task foredone You fail and drop what you’ve in vain begun. 15. Do not conceal ill deeds within your ken, Lest silence look like aping wicked men. 16. If sued unfairly, ask the judge for aid: The very laws would fain be justly swayed. 17. What you deserve to bear, with patience bear: And, when you’re judge of self, you must not spare. 18. Read much, but, having read, with much dispense; Bards’ themes are wonders, but revolt the sense. 19. Upon your talk, at dinners, set a bit, Lest you’re dubbed “rattle,” when you’d fain be “wit.” 20. Fear not the words your angry wife may say: A weeping woman plots but to waylay. 21. Use your estate, yet shun extravagance: Want follows waste and begs for maintenance. 22. Be this thy motto—“I do not dread death”: Death, if no boon, our troubles finisheth. 23. A thrifty wife may talk and talk: endure: Lost patience and loud brawling are no cure. 24. Love both your parents, one as much as other: To please your father never wound your mother.
