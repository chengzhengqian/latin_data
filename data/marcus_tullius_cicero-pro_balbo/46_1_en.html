Then also remember that Cornelius lives and has lived such a life, that although there are Courts of Inquiry for all offences, he is summoned to trial, not for the punishment of his sins, but for the reward of virtue. Remember also, that by your verdict in this case you are to decide whether you prefer that for the future the friendship of illustrious personagesa shall be a calamity or a distinction for their fellow-men. Last of all, gentlemen, keep this fixed in your minds, that in this case you are about to judge, not whether Lucius Cornelius has committed an offence, but whether Gnaeus Pompeius has rendered a service.
