Therefore,19 gentlemen, the request above all which I ought to make to you, I do not make for fear of appearing to doubt your wisdom and your human feelings. But I must ask you not to hate talent, not to show yourselves enemies of industry, not to think that human feelings should be crushed and that virtue should be penalized. And I do ask that, if you see that my client’s case is in itself sound and unshakable, you should prefer his own personal distinctions to be a help rather than a hindrance to his case.

VIII. What has given rise to the case of Cornelius, gentlemen, is a law which Lucius Gellius and Gnaeus Corneliusa carried, in accordance with a resolution of the Senate; and we see that by this law it was enactedb that those on whom Gnaeus Pompeius, with the concurrence of his advisers,c conferred Roman citizenship individually, should be Roman citizens.
