What shall10 I say of his influence, which is as great as it is bound to be in view of his virtues and his renown? And this man, upon whom the Senate and People of Rome bestowed rewards of the highest distinction though he never claimed them, but even refused great commandsa—is it not disgraceful for the Roman People and for you, that an act of such a man should be so discussed, gentlemen, that the point at issue is whether it was lawful for him to do what he did, or whether indeed it was—I will not say unlawful, but even impious—since it is alleged that he acted contrary to a treaty, that is, against the sacred obligations and good faith of the Roman People?
