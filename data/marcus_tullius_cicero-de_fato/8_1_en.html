Therefore the proposition ‘Fabius will die at sea’ belongs to the class of impossibilities. Therefore every false proposition about the future is an impossibility.

13VII. But this is a view that you, Chrysippus,Chrysippus denies Diodorus’s identification of ‘possible’ with ‘necessary’; will not allow at all, and this is the very point about which you are specially at issue with Diodorus. He says that only what either is true or will be true is a possibility, and whatever will be, he says, must necessarily happen, and whatever will not be, according to him cannot possibly happen. You say that things which will not be are also ‘possible’—for instance it is possible for this jewel to be broken even if it never will be—, and that the reign of Cypselus at Corinth was not necessary although it had been announced by the oracle of Apollo a thousand years before.
