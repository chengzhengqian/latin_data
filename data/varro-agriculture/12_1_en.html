The pain go in the ground, and may my feet be sound.’2 He bids you chant this thrice nine times, touch the ground, spit on it, and be fasting while you chant.” “You will find many other marvels in the books of the Sasernas,” said I, “which are all just as far away from agriculture and therefore to be disregarded.” “Just as if,” said he, “such things are not found in other writers also.
