Still, he should not be so much older that his age will prevent him from being as able to stand hard work; for neither old men nor boys can easily endure the hardships of the trail and the steepness and roughness of the mountainsâ€”all of which must be encountered by those who follow the herd, and especially herds of cattle and goats, which like cliffs and woods for pasturage. The men chosen for this work should be of a sturdy sort, swift, nimble, with supple limbs; men who can not only follow the herd but can also protect it from beasts and robbers, who can lift loads to the backs of pack animals, who can dash out, and who can hurl the javelin. It is not every people that is fitted for herding; thus neither a Bastulan nor a Turdulan1 is suited, while Gauls are admirably adapted, especially for draught cattle. In the matter of purchase there are some six methods of acquiring a legitimate title: by legal inheritance; by receiving, in due form, through mancipation2 from one who had a legal right to transfer; by legal cession,3 from one who had the right to cede, and that at the proper time; by right of possession;4 by purchase at auction from war-booty; and lastly by official sale among other property or in confiscated5 property.
