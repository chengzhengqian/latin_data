For those gods follow them, but are called the Twins.1 And they were not content to have one-sixth of the twelve signs bear the names of domestic animals, but added Capricornus, so that one-fourth might have them. And besides this, they added of the domestic animals the she-goat, the kid, and the dog. Or are not tracts on land and sea also known by the names of animals? For instance, they named a sea Aegean from the word for goats,2 a mountain on the border of Syria, Taurus,3 a mountain in the Sabine country, Cantherius,4 and two straits Bosphorus (ox-ford)5—the Thracian and the Cimmerian. Did they not give such names to many places on land, as, for instance, the city in Greece called ‘Hippion (horserearing) Argos’? And, finally, is not Italy named from vituli (bullocks), as Piso states?6 Further, does not everyone agree that the Roman people is sprung from shepherds? Is there anyone who does not know that Faustulus, the foster-father who reared Romulus and Remus, was a shepherd? Will not the fact that they chose exactly the Parilia7 as the time to found a city demonstrate that they were themselves shepherds?
