Lex Reperta Bantiae.

A Law, 3 containing, it is generally held, part of the concluding portion or ‘sanctions’ of a treaty concerning Bantia on the borders of Lucania and Apulia. But it may be the Lex Appuleia of Saturninus, about treason, passed in 103 or 100 b.c. Fragment of a bronze plate; the following Latin inscription is on one side, an entirely different Oscan inscription being on the other. The Oscan law deals with local affairs of Bantia, the Latin (which is a Roman Law—all the magistrates mentioned are Roman) with either some relations between Bantia and Rome, or offenders against the proposed law of treason.
