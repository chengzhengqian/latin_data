  • 1 appareo is used in the special sense of ‘serve as attendant.’ decuria meant a division composed of ten, a tithing or decade, but used of numbers more than ten. The decuriae concerned in this inscription were the decuriae viatoriae (a viator, in the official sense, being a ‘travelling messenger’ or ‘summoner’ attendant on certain Roman magistrates, in the latter Republic generally those with potestas and not attended by lictors), which we know from other epigraphic evidence, and the decuriae of praecones (‘criers’), which decuriae were probably called decuriae praeconiae. The precise meaning of the regulations laid down by this law for the composition of the decuriae is not certain.
