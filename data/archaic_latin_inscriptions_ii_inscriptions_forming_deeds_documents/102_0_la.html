Decree of the Roman Senate concerning Asclepiades, Polystratus, and Meniscus, 78 b.c. (For other senatorial decrees known in Greek translation only, see Bruns, Fontes, ed. VII, 166 ff.) Cf. L. Gallet, ‘Rev. Hist. de Droit,’ XVI, 242 ff., 387 ff.

Tablet of bronze found at Rome; now at Naples. Bilingual. Of the Latin only parts are preserved, but the rest is supplied from the Greek translation, which is almost complete. This inscription is to be classed with Senatus consulta like that given on pp. 252–5, but I have put it near the end of this book because not much of the actual Latin is preserved, and because the date is at the very end of the archaic period, if not beyond it.
