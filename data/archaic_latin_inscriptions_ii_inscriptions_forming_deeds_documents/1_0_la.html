Sacred law (or laws?), of about 500 b.c. or earlier, inscribed on the four faces of a four-sided and tapering block of tufa of which the top is broken off. Found between the Forum and the comitium at Rome under a black marble pavement. The lines of the inscription, of which only the lower part is preserved, run vertical to the base, the letters lying on their sides when the pillar is upright.
