On Instituting a Trial Against a Person Who Shall be Deceased or Shall Have Gone into Exile

In the case of any person who shall have been summoned under this law, if he shall be deceased . . . or shall have gone into exile before the said case shall have been adjudicated, the praetor, before whom he shall have been summoned, shall conduct the inquiry into the said case through those persons who under this law shall form the court, in the same manner as if the person who shall have been summoned under this law were alive or present in this30 State. . . .

On the Holding of an Investigation for Evidence

Their duties lay in civil cases, but, where there were no local magistrates available, they had duties of government also.
