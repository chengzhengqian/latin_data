imposed by the Lex Caelia tabellaria on 107 b.c. One of the two laws was perhaps annulled before use of the plate for the other; I give here all that is found on the pars postica, because it deals with allotment of jurymen in setting up a court, and it is of some interest to compare it with certain parts of the Lex Acilia Repetundarum given below, pp. 316 ff.; cf. A. Rudorff, ad Leg. Acil. de pec. repet. (1862). 485–7. C. Klenze, Fragm. Leg. Serv. (1825), prol., IV. The clauses indicated below describe the action to be taken in regard to pilae. These were small ‘balls’ or ballots which in this law are applied to the selection of jurymen before a trial. The word aequare means here to make the number of pilae provided equal
