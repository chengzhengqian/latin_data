Instrumenta

The temples to Diana and Juno here meant were vowed by Aemilius Lepidus in 187 and dedicated in 179. A temple to the goddesses of weather was vowed by L. Scipio son of Barbatus during a storm on his return from Sardinia in 259 b.c. See the inscription on his tomb, pp. 4–5. The temple was built by the Via Appia outside the porta Capena. Intercalary month. This is placed after December in this calendar; but when this month was actually inserted into the year, it was put between February and March—see p. 108. So little of it has survived in this calendar that we cannot tell whether the alleged name Mercedonius, ‘pay-month,’ supposed to rest on a mistake in Plutarch, is true or not, because the part giving the month’s name is lost.
