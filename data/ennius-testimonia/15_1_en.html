T 36 (cf. T 2) Varro, Menippean Satires

A poema [poem] is a piece in rhythmic language, that is, a considerable number of words brought into some form according to rules; therefore they call even an epigrammatic distich a poema [poem]. poesis [poetry] is a continuous narrative in meter, such as Homer’s Iliad and Ennius’ Annals. poeticē [i.e., technē; poetics] is the art of these things.

T 38 (cf. T 91, 97) Cornelius Nepos, Cato

As praetor [in 198 BC], he1 governed the province of Sardinia, from which, when returning from Africa as quaestor [in 204 BC], he had previously brought back the poet Ennius, which deed we value no less than any grand Sardinian triumph.

  • 1Tragedies by Accius, Pacuvius, Ennius, respectively (cf. Aiax: Trag. F 9–12; Melanippa: Trag. F 101–6).
  • 1A Roman name for a group of twelve deities, six male and six female (cf. 7 Ann. F 24).
  • 1M. Porcius Cato (234–149 BC, cos. 195 BC).
