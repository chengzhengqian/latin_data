T 40

a Horace, Satires

This genre, attempted in vain by Varro Atacinus [a late-Republican poet] and some others, was there for me to be able to improve upon, though a lesser poet than its inventor [Lucilius]; nor would I dare drag from him the crown clinging with great acclaim to his head.

b Porphyrio, Commentary on Horace

“this genre, attempted in vain” and so on: while others whom he has mentioned were writing other genres of poetry very well, Terentius Varro Narbonensis, by contrast, who was called Atacinus from the river Atax, had attempted the genre of satire in vain, so too Ennius, who left four books of satires, and Pacuvius did not manage this genre of versification successfully. He [Horace] says that he writes this in a way to be better than the others but less good than Lucilius, whom he rightly called the inventor of this genre, since Lucilius was the first to write poems of this kind.
