The conclusion is, not that pleasure is not pleasure but that pleasure is not the Chief Good. The famous Laelius, who had been a pupil of Diogenes the Stoic in his youth and later of Panaetius,Yet really self-indulgence is not happiness; was not called ‘the Wise’ because he was no judge of good eating (for a wise mind is not necessarily incompatible with a nice palate), but because he set little store by it.

Dinner of herbs, how all the earth Derides thee and ignores thy worth! Tho’ Laelius, our old Roman sage, Shouted thy praises to the age, Our gourmands one by one arraigning.

Bravo, Laelius, ‘sage’ indeed. How true too the lines:

‘O bottomless gulf of gluttony, Publius Gallonius,’ cried he, ‘You’re a poor devil, truth to tell, Who never in your life dined well, No, never once, although you pay A fortune for a fish away, Lobster or sturgeon Brobdingnagian.’a
