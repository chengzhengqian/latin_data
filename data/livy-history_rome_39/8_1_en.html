Also in the procession were some twenty-seven generals, either Aetolian and Cephallanian or officers of the king left behind by Antiochus. Before riding into the city, Fulvius that day presented military decorations in the Circus Flaminius23 to a large number of tribunes, prefects, cavalrymen and centurions, both Romans and allies. He distributed twenty-five denarii from the plunder to each of the infantrymen, twice that amount to a centurion and three times to a cavalryman.

6. The time for the consular elections was now drawing near. As Marcus Aemilius, who had been allotted responsibility for them, could not attend, Gaius Flaminius came to Rome. Under his supervision Spurius Postumius Albinus and Quintus Marcius Philippus24 were elected as consuls, and then the following became praetors: Titus Maenius, Publius Cornelius Sulla, Gaius Calpurnius Piso, Marcus Licinius Lucullus, Gaius Aurelius Scaurus and Lucius Quinctius Crispinus.

On March 5th—at the year’s end and after the election of the magistrates—Gnaeus Manlius Vulso celebrated his triumph over the Gauls living in Asia.
