35. The commissioners departed from their meeting with Philip giving the distinct impression that they were satisfied with none of the proceedings, and Philip was also left in no doubt that he must go to war again. Because his strength was not yet up to this, however, he decided, in order to gain time, to send his younger son Demetrius to Rome both to clear him of the charges against him and also to allay the anger of the senate. He was confident that Demetrius, despite his youth, would make quite an impression because he had given an indication of having the character of a king while he was a hostage at Rome.132 Meanwhile, Philip set off, ostensibly to assist the people of Byzantium, but really to strike fear into the Thracian chieftains. Defeating them in a single battle, and taking the chieftain Amadocus prisoner, he returned to Macedonia, having first sent men to incite the barbarians living along the River Hister to invade Italy.133
