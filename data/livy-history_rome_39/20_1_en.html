The priests of the cult, whether male or female, were to be tracked down, not just in Rome but throughout the country towns and regional centers,43 so that they should be at the disposition of the consuls. A proclamation was also to be made in the city of Rome, with edicts sent out the length of Italy, forbidding anyone who had been initiated in the Bacchic rites to hold religious gatherings or assemblies or to perform any divine ceremony of this kind. Above all there was to be an investigation conducted into those people who had gathered or conspired to perpetrate an immoral or shameful act. Such were the decisions of the senate.

The consuls instructed the curule aediles to hunt down all the priests of the cult, apprehend them and keep them under house arrest44 for the investigation; and the plebeian aediles were to ensure that there be no clandestine celebration of the rites. The triumviri capitales45 were charged with the task of posting of sentries through the city, seeing that there were no gatherings at night, and taking precaution against arson. The triumviri were to be assisted by the quinquevirs who, on both sides of the Tiber, were each to keep watch on the buildings in his district.46
