Philip had with no great effort driven Amynander from Athamania and recovered a number of cities; and, in addition, he brought under his sway Demetrias84—a strong city well positioned in every respect—and the people of Magnesia. After this he also acquired a number of cities in Thrace that were in chaos because of feuding among their leading citizens—for which their recently acquired independence, to which they were unaccustomed, was to blame—and he did so by allying himself with whatever factions were losing in the internal struggles.

24. The king’s rancor toward the Romans was for the moment appeased by these acquisitions. However, he never took his mind off amassing in peacetime resources that he could use in time of war whenever given the chance. He increased the revenues of his kingdom not only by taxes on agricultural produce and by harbor duties but also by reopening old, abandoned mines and starting new ones in many places. In order to restore earlier population levels after the losses caused by military defeats, he proceeded to establish a new generation of his people not only by forcing everyone to produce children and bring them up85 but he had also transplanted in Macedonia large numbers of Thracians. He had thus, while he had a period of respite from war, focused all his attention on augmenting the resources of his realm.
