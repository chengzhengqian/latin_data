That same year some Transalpine Gauls crossed into Venetia without looting or hostile action and occupied a spot for founding a fortified settlement not far from where Aquileia77 stands today. When Roman envoys were sent across the Alps to investigate the matter, they were given the answer that the migrants had set out without the authorization of their people, who did not know what they were doing in Italy.

At this time Lucius Scipio put on, over a period of ten days, the games that he said he had promised in a vow during the war with Antiochus, and for them used money that had been contributed for the purpose by kings and city-states.78 According to Valerius Antias, Scipio had been sent as an ambassador to Asia in the period following his conviction and the sale of his property in order to settle disputes between King Antiochus and King Eumenes, and it was at this time that the funds were awarded to him and performers also enlisted throughout Asia. Scipio, he claims, had omitted to mention the games in the period following the war in which he said they were vowed, and only after the embassy was discussion of them finally raised in the senate.
