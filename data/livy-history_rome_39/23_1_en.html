The mischief is growing and infiltrating society each day—it is already too large for the resources of an individual to keep under control and it seeks to engulf the whole state. Unless you take precautions, my fellow citizens, their nocturnal assembly will soon be able to rival this daylight meeting legitimately convened by a consul. At present, they as individuals stand in fear of your unity; soon, when you have slipped away to your homes and farms and they have assembled, they will be discussing saving themselves and destroying you at the same time—and then it is them assembled that you as individuals will have to fear. Each and every one of us, then, must pray that all his relatives have shown good sense. If lechery or folly has swept any of them into that maelstrom, we must each consider that that person is not with him but with those people with whom he conspired to commit all manner of crime and villainy.

“Citizens, I am not confident that none of you will falter and go astray. Nothing is more deceptively alluring than a depraved cult. When the will of the gods is used as a screen for criminal activities, fear comes into the mind that in punishing human wrongdoings we may be violating some divine law that is fused together with them. From such religious scruples you are freed by countless decrees of the priests, resolutions of the senate and, indeed, responses from our soothsayers. In the days of our fathers and grandfathers, how many were the occasions on which the magistrates were charged with the responsibility of outlawing the practice of foreign rites!
