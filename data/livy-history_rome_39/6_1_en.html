Furthermore, what were these slanderous criticisms that he brought before the pontiffs about the sacking of the temples of the immortal gods in the captured city? Or perhaps it was morally acceptable for Rome to be embellished with artwork from Syracuse and other captured cities—only in captured Ambracia did the rules of war not apply! He was appealing to the senate, he said, and asking the tribune, not to let him be made an object of ridicule by a supercilious opponent.

5. The senators all assailed the tribune from every side, some with entreaties, some with abuse; but the address of his colleague Tiberius Gracchus moved him most of all. Even to indulge one’s own vendettas through one’s office did not set a good precedent, said Gracchus; but for a tribune of the plebs to be a spokesman for another person’s vendettas was a disgrace and an affront to the authority and sacred laws of that college.18 One should follow one’s own judgment in disliking or liking people and in approving or not approving measures; and a tribune of the people should not be hanging on another’s expression or nod of the head or be led around by the prompting of someone else’s sentiments or be supporting a consul’s anger.
