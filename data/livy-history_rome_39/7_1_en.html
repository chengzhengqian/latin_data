Overawed by such censure, the tribune left the temple; and on the proposal of the praetor Servius Sulpicius a triumph was formally awarded to Marcus Fulvius. When he had expressed his gratitude to the senators, Fulvius added that he had vowed great games to Jupiter Optimus Maximus on the day that he had captured Ambracia20 and that 100 pounds of gold had been contributed to him by the cities for that purpose, and he now requested that the senate authorize this gold to be separated from the money that he would deposit in the treasury after it was carried in his triumphal procession. The senate ordered that the college of pontiffs should be consulted on whether the entire quantity of gold needed to be spent on the games. When the pontiffs stated that the amount spent on the games had no religious significance the senate left to Fulvius the decision on how much to spend, provided that he not exceed the sum of 80,000 sesterces.

Fulvius had decided to hold the triumph in the month of January. However, when he heard that the consul Marcus Aemilius had received a letter from the plebeian tribune Marcus Aburius about the withdrawal of his veto and that Aemilius was himself coming to Rome to block the triumph but had stopped on the way because of an illness, he advanced the date so as not to face more battles over the triumph than he had in the war.
