There was also a delegation from King Eumenes that included his brother Athenaeus,191 which came to complain that the garrisons were not being withdrawn from Thrace and also that reinforcements for Prusias’ campaign against Eumenes had been sent to Bithynia by Philip.

47. All these complaints had to be addressed by Demetrius who was then quite young.192 It was not easy for him to remember either all the charges that were brought or the responses needing to be made to them. Not only were they many in number but several were also utterly trivial: border disputes, the abduction of individuals and the theft of farm animals, court decisions made arbitrarily or not made at all, and judicial verdicts reached under duress or through corruption. The senate could see that Demetrius was unable to offer any clarification on these items and that they could not obtain explicit information from him; but they were at the same time moved by the young man’s inexperience and unease. They accordingly had the question put to him whether he had been given any written brief on these items by his father. When Demetrius replied that he had, it was decided that nothing should have priority or precedence over hearing the king’s replies to each of the charges. They forthwith demanded the scroll be produced and then allowed Demetrius to read it out.

It contained, however, only summary arguments against the individual charges, with Philip claiming that in some instances he had followed the commissioners’ directives and that in others responsibility for his not doing so lay not with himself but with the very people accusing him.
