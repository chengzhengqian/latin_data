42. There was peace in Farther Spain following the crushing defeat of the Lusitanians in the most recent hostilities.165 In Hither Spain, Aulus Terentius, operating among the Suessetani, took the town of Corbio166 using siege sheds and earthworks, and he sold off the prisoners. After that the closer province also enjoyed a tranquil winter. The erstwhile praetors Gaius Calpurnius Piso and Lucius Quinctius returned to Rome. Both were accorded a triumph with the enthusiastic approval of the senate. Gaius Calpurnius celebrated his triumph over the Lusitanians and the Celtiberians first; and in his procession he carried on display 83 golden crowns167 and 12,000 pounds of silver. A few days later Lucius Quinctius Crispinus also triumphed over the Lusitanians and Celtiberians; and the same quantity of gold and silver was transported in that triumphal procession.

The censors Marcus Porcius and Lucius Valerius revised the senate membership in an atmosphere of suspense mingled with apprehension. They removed seven men from the senate, including one well-known both because of his noble birth and the offices he had held: the ex-consul Lucius Quinctius Flamininus.168 It is said that the practice of the censors affixing the mark of infamy to the names of those removed from the senate dates to a period within the memory of those senators.
