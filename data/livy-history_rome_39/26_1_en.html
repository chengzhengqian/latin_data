Some of the prisoners had merely been initiated and had, following ritual formulae dictated by a priest, made prayers in which were found the unholy conspiracy to commit all the criminal and shameful acts;53 they had not actually perpetrated against themselves or others any of the acts to which they had been bound by their oath. These the consuls left in prison.54 Those who had dishonored themselves by degenerate behavior or by murder and those who had degraded themselves by giving false testimony, counterfeiting seals, making fraudulent insertions in wills and other sharp practices, they condemned to capital punishment. More were executed than imprisoned. There was a large number of men and women in both categories. Condemned women they delivered to their relatives or legal guardians55 for these to inflict the punishment on them in private; if there was no suitable person to administer the punishment, the penalty was applied in public custody.
