After a heated argument with the praetor, the Cenomani achieved success with their case. The praetor was instructed to restore their arms to them and leave the province.

An audience with the senate was then granted to ambassadors from the allies and those with Latin rights10 who had gathered there in great numbers from all over Latium. They complained that a large portion of their citizens had moved to Rome and been registered for the census there. The praetor Quintus Terentius Culleo was assigned the duty of tracing these and forcing anyone whom the allies proved had been enrolled with them or had had a father enrolled with them, in or after the censorship of Gaius Claudius and Marcus Livius,11 to return to the original place of census. Following this investigation 12,000 Latins went back to their homes—even by then the large number of foreigners was proving to be a burden on the city.
