Also under discussion195 was whether the Spartans should remain in the Achaean League or be the only state in the Peloponnese to have a separate constitution, as before. The decision reached was that the men should be repatriated and the sentences set aside, and Sparta was to remain in the Achaean League. This enactment was to be written down and signed by both the Spartans and the Achaeans.

Quintus Marcius was sent as a commissioner to Macedonia and was also given orders to examine the situation of the allies in the Peloponnese. There, too, unrest still lingered after the quarrels of the past and, in addition, Messene had seceded from the Achaean League. But should I decide to recount the causes of this war and how it developed, I would be forgetting my decision not to touch upon foreign affairs except where they were connected with Roman history.196

49. A noteworthy event in the campaign was that, although the Achaeans had the upper hand in the war, their general Philopoemen was taken prisoner. He had entered a dangerous valley in order to gain Corone197 ahead of the enemy, who were making for the town, and he was taken by surprise along with a few cavalrymen.
