After Appius said this, Lycortas made the following reply, partly in his capacity as praetor but also because he belonged to the party of Philopoemen, who had been responsible for all that had happened in Sparta:

“Speaking before you, Appius Claudius, is more difficult for us than was speaking before the senate in Rome recently.139 For then it was a case of responding to the Spartans’ accusations; now we have been accused by you, the men before whom we must present our case. We accept this unfair situation in the hope that you will hear us from the viewpoint of an arbitrator, setting aside the impassioned rhetoric with which you spoke a moment ago. Anyway, although you a little while back repeated the complaints that the Lacedaemonians made here at an earlier date before Quintus Caecilius and subsequently in Rome, I continued to believe that my response was not being made to you, but to them before you.

“You charge us with the murder of the men who were killed after being summoned to plead their case by the praetor Philopoemen. This is an accusation, Romans, which I feel should not even have been made against us before you and much less made by you. Why so?
