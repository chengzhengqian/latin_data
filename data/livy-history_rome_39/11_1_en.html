8. The following year saw the consuls Spurius Postumius Albinus and Quintus Marcius Philippus turn their attention from the army and military and provincial affairs to punitive measures against an internal conspiracy. In the praetorian sortition of provinces Titus Maenius received the urban jurisdiction and Marcus Licinius Lucullus the jurisdiction between citizens and foreigners; Gaius Aurelius Scaurus received Sardinia and Publius Cornelius Sulla Sicily; and Lucius Quinctius Crispinus received Hither Spain and Gaius Calpurnius Piso Farther Spain.

Both consuls were assigned the task of investigating clandestine seditious activity.33 It started when an obscure Greek arrived in Etruria, a man blessed with none of those many accomplishments that this most erudite of peoples has brought to us for our intellectual and physical betterment—he was a sacrificial hierophant and oracle monger. Nor was he the type to delude people’s minds while openly practicing his religion and overtly proclaiming his business and his precepts; rather, he was the high priest of a secret cult that operated at night. There were rites of initiation that were at first limited to a few but which subsequently spread more widely among men and women.
