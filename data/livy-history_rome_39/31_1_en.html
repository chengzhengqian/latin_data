22. Roughly in the period that this news arrived from Spain, the Taurian Games71 were held over a two day period for religious reasons. Then, for ten days and with great ceremony,72 Marcus Fulvius put on the games that he had vowed during the Aetolian War.73 A large number of performers came from Greece to honor him. This was also the very first time that competitive athletics were put on as a spectacle for the Romans; and a lion and panther hunt was also staged, and the show held on a scale and with a variety of events almost matching today’s. A nine-day supplication followed because there had been showers of stones for three days in Picenum, and because it was reported that fires had broken out in the sky in numerous places and, with a light breeze, had in particular singed the clothes of several people.74 In accordance with a decree of the pontiffs, a single day supplication was also added because the temple of Ops75 on the capitol had been struck by lightning. The consuls used full-grown victims for expiation and ritual purification of the city.
