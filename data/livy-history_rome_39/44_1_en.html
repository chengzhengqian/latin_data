Such were the considerations that particularly set Philip against the Romans; so one can see that war was not fomented by his son Perseus from a new set of motives but was for the reasons given handed down from the father to the son.

At Rome there was no idea of war with Macedon. The proconsul Lucius Manlius had returned from Spain; in the temple of Bellona108 he requested a triumph of the senate and the extent of his achievements lent weight to his petition. What stood in the way was precedent: ancestral custom had established that nobody should triumph who had not brought his army home, unless he had passed on to his successor a province that had been totally subdued and pacified. Manlius was nonetheless granted the compromise honor of entering the city in ovation.109 His procession included 52 golden crowns110 as well as 132 lbs of gold and 16,300 lbs of silver. He also announced in the senate that his quaestor Quintus Fabius111 was bringing 10,000 lbs of silver and 80 lbs of gold, and that he would also deposit this in the treasury.

There was a dangerous slave revolt in Apulia that year. Lucius Postumius had Tarentum as his province, and he conducted a rigorous inquiry into a conspiracy of shepherds whose banditry had made roads and public pasture lands unsafe. He convicted some 7,000 men; and many of them fled from there and many were put to death. After being long delayed in the city conducting troop levies, the consuls eventually left for their provinces.112
