Lycortas was heard with applause by most of his audience and everyone thought his speech in keeping with the dignity of his office, so that it was readily apparent that the Romans could not maintain their dignity by treading softly. At this point Appius declared that he strongly advised the Achaeans to try to enter the Romans’ good graces while they could still do so of their own free will; later they might be doing this involuntarily and under duress. While this remark was received with a groan from all present, it also filled them with fear of refusing to carry out his orders. Their one request was that the Romans undertake such modifications as they thought appropriate vis-à-vis the Spartans and not trammel the Achaeans with a religious difficulty of invalidating what they had sanctioned by oath. The condemnation recently passed on Areus and Alcibiades was the only measure repealed.

38. When the allocation of consular and praetorian provinces was discussed in Rome at the beginning of that year, the consuls were assigned the Ligurians because there were no hostilities elsewhere. In the praetorian sortition Gaius Decimius Flavus received the urban jurisdiction and Publius Cornelius Cethegus the citizens/foreigners jurisdiction; Gaius Sempronius Blaesus received Sicily, and Quintus Naevius Matho Sardinia (and also an investigation into poisonings); Aulus Terentius Varro received Hither Spain and Publius Sempronius Longus Farther Spain.
