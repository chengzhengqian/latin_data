A public sacrificial feast187 was held for Publius Licinius’ funeral, and there was a contest featuring 120 gladiators, funeral games lasting three days and a banquet following the games. During the banquet, dining furniture had been set out throughout the Forum but a storm that arose with high winds obliged a large number of people to pitch tents in the Forum. Shortly afterward, with the return of fine weather everywhere, they were taken down. The word then went around that the people had fulfilled the prophecy that soothsayers had pronounced as one of the decrees of fate, namely that tents had to be pitched in the Forum.188 Relieved of this religious scruple, another immediately faced them—it had rained blood for two days in the precinct of the temple of Vulcan.189 To expiate this prodigy a period of supplication was enjoined by the decemvirs.190

Before they left for their provinces, the consuls brought delegations from overseas before the senate. Never before had there been in Rome so many people from that part of the world. Word had spread among the peoples living close to Macedonia that serious consideration was being given by the Romans to charges and grievances lodged against Philip and that many had found it worth their while to make a complaint.
