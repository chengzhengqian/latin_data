THYESTES

(to Fury) Why do you menace my face with your whip and threaten me fiercely with entwined snakes? Why do you rouse the hunger set in my bones’ marrow? My heart is fired and ablaze with thirst, and flames dart through my burned flesh. I follow!

fury

Distribute this very frenzy throughout the house! They must be driven just like this, and in enmity thirst for blood of their blood by turns. The house feels you entering, it shuddered throughout at this accursed contact. It is done, and amply! Go to the infernal caverns and the river you know. Already the sad earth is oppressed by your step: do you see how water deserts the springs, forced back within? How rivers are empty, and a fiery wind carries off the sparse clouds? Every tree wilts, the branches stand bare as the fruit vanishes, and the Isthmus, which booms to waves close by on each side, as it divides neighboring gulfs with its strip of land, grows wide and catches their sounds far off. Now Lerna has shrunk back, the Phoronean streams have disappeared, holy Alpheus does not bring forth its waters; Cithaeron’s ridges stand nowhere white, their snow sloughed off; and renowned Argos fears its ancient thirst.8 See, even the Titan hesitates whether to bid the daylight follow him, and to force it with the reins to come forth to its ruin.
