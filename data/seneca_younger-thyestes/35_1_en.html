Not yet is the herald of eventide, Vesper, summoning the lights of night; not yet does the turning of the western wheel bid you loose your steeds, their task completed; not yet, at the sinking of day toward night, has the third trumpet voiced its signal;35 the plowman, his oxen not yet weary, stands in amazement: suddenly suppertime! What has driven you out of your heavenly course? What cause has forced your horses down from their fixed path? Can it be that the prison of Dis is open and the conquered Giants are venturing war? Can it be that wounded Tityos renews his ancient rage in his weary breast? Can Typhon have thrown the mountain off and stretched his limbs? Can it be that a soaring path is built by Phlegraean foes,36 and that Thessaly’s Pelion is burdened with the weight of Thracian Ossa?
