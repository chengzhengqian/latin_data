Stand by me, drive my hand. Let the father rend his children avidly, gleefully, and eat his own flesh. This is good, this is ample. This measure of revenge pleases me—for the present.

But where in the world is it? Why has Atreus remained innocent so long? Now the whole picture of the carnage hovers before my eyes—childlessness stuffed down the father’s throat!17 Why take fright again, my spirit, and slacken before the event? It must be dared; do it! The principal outrage in this crime—he will commit it himself.

atreus

He could not be caught, unless he wanted to catch others. But as it is, he desires my kingdom. In this desire he will confront Jove’s threat of the thunderbolt; in this desire he will face the threats of the swelling flood, or enter the treacherous straits of the Libyan Syrtes; in this desire he will do what he thinks the greatest evil: see his brother.
