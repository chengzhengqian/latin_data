In his own eyes, he is a paragon of Roman virtue (his name suggests that he is the “ghost” or “shade” of Romanness), fleeing an un-Roman Rome and longing for a return of the good old days (312–14). But Juvenal hints that he is, instead, a jealous failure (his name can also be interpreted as “Mr Shady”). He has tried to succeed (e.g. 92–3) but cannot compete and is, consequently, envious of those who can. That perhaps puts his departure in a different light.
