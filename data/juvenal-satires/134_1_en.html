Yet, to actually give you something to ask for and some reason to offer the guts and little sacred sausages of a shining white piglet at the little shrines, you should pray for a sound mind in a sound body. Ask for a heart that is courageous, with no fear of death, that reckons long life among the least of Nature’s gifts, that can put up with any anguish, that is unfamiliar with anger, that longs for nothing, that prefers the troubles and gruelling Labours of Hercules to the sex and feasts and downy cushions of Sardanapallus. 67 I’m showing you something you can give yourself. There is no doubt that the only path to a peaceful life lies through goodness. Fortune, you’d have no power, if we were sensible: it’s we who make you a goddess, it’s we who give you a place in the sky.

  • 67The last king of Assyria, associated with luxury, the antithesis of the hero Hercules, whose Twelve Labours made him a saint-like figure.
