Satire 3

Although I’m distressed at the departure of my old friend, all the same I approve of his decision to establish his home at empty Cumae 1 and to donate a single fellow-citizen to the Sibyl. 2 It’s the gateway to Baiae, a lovely coast, delightfully secluded. Personally, I would prefer even Prochyta 3 to the Subura. 4 After all, have you seen any place so dismal and lonely that you wouldn’t consider it worse to live in dread of fires, and buildings collapsing continually, and the thousand other dangers of savage Rome—and poets reciting in the month of August? *** But while his entire house was being loaded onto a single waggon, he halted under the ancient arch of dripping Capena. 5 We walk down into the vale of Egeria with its artificial grottoes. How much more real the spirit of the spring would be were the waters enclosed by a green edge of grass and if marble didn’t profane the native tufa stone.
