XVI

I’ll bugger you and stuff you, you catamite Aurelius and you pervert Furius, who have supposed me to be immodest, on account of my verses, because these are rather naughty. For the sacred poet ought to be chaste himself, though his poems need not be so. Why, they only acquire wit and spice if they are rather naughty and immodest, and can rouse with their ticklings, I don’t mean boys, but those hairy old ‘uns unable to stir their arthritic loins. Because you’ve read of my many thousand kisses, do you think I’m less virile on that account? Yes, I’ll bugger you and stuff you all right!

XVII
