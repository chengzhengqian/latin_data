The Poems Of Catullus

Fragments

1. But you shall not escape my iambics.1

2. This inclosure I dedicate and consecrate to thee, O Priapus, at Lampsacus, where is thy house and sacred grove, O Priapus. For thee specially in its cites the Hellespontian coast worships, more abundant in oysters than all other coasts.

3. <But do you, Priapus, scare off those villains who> have2 the urge to take a taste of what is mine.3
