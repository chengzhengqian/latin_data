LXXXIX

Gellius is thin, and well he may be; with a mother so kind and so lusty and lively, and a sister so charming, and so kind an uncle, and so many girls of his acquaintance all over the place, why should he cease to be lean? Even if he touch nothing but what none may touch, you will find any number of reasons why he should be lean.

XC

From the unholy commerce of Gellius and his mother let a wizard be born, and learn the Persian art of soothsaying; for a wizard must be the offspring of mother and son, if the unnatural religion of the Persians is true, so that with acceptable incantations he may offer pleasing worship to the gods, whilst melting the fat caul in the altar flame.
