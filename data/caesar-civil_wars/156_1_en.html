After having arranged his provisioning and put heart into his soldiers Caesar thought that he should find out what Pompey’s intention or desire for a fight was, now that enough time had elapsed since the Dyrrachium battles for him to feel that he had sufficiently observed the soldiers’ morale. 2So he led the army out and drew up his line, at first in a spot he controlled, some distance from Pompey’s camp, but on the following days advancing further from his own camp and setting his line at the foot of the hills controlled by Pompey.
