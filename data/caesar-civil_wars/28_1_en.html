He put Decimus Brutus in charge of the ships and left his officer Gaius Trebonius for the assault on Marseilles.

While making these preparations and arrangements he sent his officer Gaius Fabius ahead to Spain with the three legions he had stationed for the winter at Narbo and nearby.58 He ordered the immediate occupation of the Pyrenees passes, which at that time were garrisoned by Pompey’s officer Lucius Afranius. 2Caesar ordered the rest of the legions, whose winter quarters were further away, to follow. 3Fabius, as ordered, acted swiftly to dislodge the garrison from the pass59 and hastened toward Afranius’ army by forced marches.
