Learning of Caesar’s approach he left that location and within a few days came to Mytilene. 5After being delayed two days by weather and acquiring some additional fast vessels he went on to Cilicia and thence to Cyprus. 6There he learned that the people of Antioch and all of the Roman citizens in business there had by common consent seized the citadel117 in order to keep him out, and had sent word to the Pompeians who, people said, had gone to nearby communities after their escape: “Do not come to Antioch. If you do, your lives will be in great danger.” 7The same thing had happened to Lucius Lentulus, the previous year’s consul, at Rhodes, and to the ex-consul Publius Lentulus, and some others. These men were following Pompey after their escape, and when they reached Rhodes they were not received into the city or harbor. After messengers were sent to tell them to leave the area, they reluctantly set sail. 8Communities were already getting word of Caesar’s approach.
