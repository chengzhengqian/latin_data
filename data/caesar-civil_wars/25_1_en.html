If he approved, why has he prevented me from using a favor granted by the Roman people?” 4Caesar brought up his own forbearance in initiating a request that both armies be dismissed, a matter in which he stood to lose dignity and prestige, 5and showed the harshness of his enemies, who were refusing to do what they asked him to do and preferred utter confusion to giving up power and armies. 6He emphasized the injury they did in depriving him of his legions, and their brutality and highhandedness in obstructing the tribunes. He recounted the terms he had proposed, the negotiations requested and refused. 7For all these reasons he exhorted the senators and asked them to take charge of the state and administer it with him. “But if fear makes you shirk the task, I will not be a burden to you but will administer the state myself.”53 8He said that representatives ought to be sent to Pompey for a settlement, that he did not feel the anxiety expressed by Pompey in the senate a short while before, namely, that sending representatives increased the authority of those to whom they were sent and showed the fear of those who sent them. 9“This bears the stamp of a petty and feeble character. As for me, just as I have worked to get ahead in resources,54 so I desire to outdo others in justice and equity.”
