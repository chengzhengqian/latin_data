19. The two camps of Pompey and Caesar were separated by just one river, the Apsus, and the soldiers frequently held talks among themselves, and no weapons were thrown for the duration by the speakers’ agreement. 2So Caesar sent his officer Publius Vatinius right up to the riverbank to do the things that seemed most conducive to peace and to repeat this message in a loud voice: “Are citizens permitted to send a delegation to fellow citizens in safety—something permitted even to deserters in the wilderness of the Pyrenees, and to pirates33—especially when their aim is to prevent armed conflict between fellow citizens?” 3Speaking at length and like a suppliant, as he was right to do concerning his own and everyone’s survival, he was heard in silence by soldiers on both sides. 4A response came from the other side, that Aulus Varro
