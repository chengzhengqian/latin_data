CIVIL WAR, BOOK III

33. Furthermore, at Ephesus Scipio ordered long-standing deposits to be removed from the temple of Diana, fixing a date for it. Upon Scipio’s arrival50 in the temple accompanied by some men of senatorial rank whom he had summoned, a letter from Pompey was given to him: “Caesar has crossed the Adriatic with his legions. Make haste to come to me with your army and treat this as the highest priority.”51 2Upon receiving this letter Scipio dismissed the men he had summoned and began to make preparations for his own march to Macedonia, setting out within a few days. This development saved the money at Ephesus.
