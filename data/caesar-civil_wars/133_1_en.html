For the moment, it had an abundance of every kind of provisions except grain, and the men saw that thanks to these the time was going by better70 each day, and that a greater hope lay ahead in the ripeness of the grain crops.

50. This being a new type of warfare, new combat methods were devised by each side. The enemy, when they noticed from the fires at night that our cohorts were doing guard duty beside the fortifications, would approach noiselessly and shoot a volley of arrows71 into their midst, then return immediately to their own lines. 2Learning from experience, our men devised the following remedies for this: they would build their fires in one place . . .72
