91. There was a reenlisted man, Crastinus, in Caesar’s army, who had been his chief centurion in the tenth legion the year before, a man of remarkable courage. 2After the signal was given, Crastinus said “Follow me, you who were men of my unit, and give your commander the effort you have resolved on.110 This one battle remains. Once it is over, he will have his dignity again and we our liberty.” 3At the same time, looking at Caesar, he said, “My actions today, general, will make you thank me either alive or dead.” 4After saying this he was the first to run forward from the right wing, and about a hundred and twenty picked soldiers, volunteers, followed him.
