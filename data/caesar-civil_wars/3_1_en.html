But the majority were stripped of their capacity for free decision. 6The censor Lucius Piso and likewise the praetor Lucius Roscius volunteered to go to Caesar to inform him about these matters; they requested a six-day period to accomplish the business. 7A few senators even proposed that a delegation be sent to Caesar to lay out for him the senate’s decision.
