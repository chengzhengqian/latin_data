At this point Caesar ordered the third line to charge; it had not seen action and had held its position until now. 2Therefore, since fresh, sound troops were relieving tired ones, and others were attacking from behind, the Pompeians all turned and ran, finding resistance impossible. 3Nor was Caesar wrong in thinking that the first stage of victory would come from the cohorts stationed in the fourth line facing the cavalry, as he himself had predicted in exhorting the men. 4It was by these that the Pompeian cavalry was routed, first, and these same units slaughtered the archers and slingers, and likewise outflanked Pompey’s line on the left and started the flight. 5As for Pompey, when he saw his cavalry routed and realized that the unit he trusted most was terrified, having no confidence in the rest he left the line and rode immediately to his camp.
