3. Meanwhile Lucius Nasidius, who had been sent by Gnaeus Pompey as support for Lucius Domitius and Marseilles with a fleet of sixteen ships, a few equipped with rams, sailed through the Sicilian strait without Curio being aware of or anticipating the fact.4 2When he had put in at Messana, and the town’s leaders and councilors had evacuated the place because of the sudden panic, he took a ship from their dockyards. 3After adding this to the rest he finished his course toward Marseilles. Sending ahead a boat in secret he informed Domitius and the people of Marseilles about his approach and urged them strongly to add his forces to their own and engage with Brutus’ fleet again.
