21. When the consul Servilius and the rest of the magistrates resisted, and he himself was achieving less than he expected by way of rousing people’s enthusiasm, Caelius withdrew the first law and put forward two laws, one in which he remitted tenants’ rent payments for a year, 2another a cancellation of debts. After a crowd attacked Gaius Trebonius, and some people were injured, Caelius ejected Trebonius from his official seat. 3The consul Servilius referred these matters to the senate and the senate voted to remove Caelius from his public duties. With this decree in hand the consul barred Caelius from the senate and pulled him down off the rostra when he was trying to address a public meeting. 4Humiliated and hurt, Caelius pretended in public that he was on his way to Caesar. In secret he sent messengers to Milo, who had been convicted of murder after the murder of Clodius, and summoned him to Italy, for Milo had the remnants of a band of gladiators from the large-scale shows he had given. Caelius joined Milo to his cause and sent him ahead to rouse the herdsmen. 5He himself went to Casilinum.
