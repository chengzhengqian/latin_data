Thus at news of the enemy’s approach they moved their ships out of the harbor and fought the Massilians. 3Both sides fought with the utmost courage and vehemence, and the Albici were not much inferior to our men in prowess, being hardy mountaineers with experience in warfare; 4having just left Marseilles, they had the people’s recent promises firmly in mind. Domitius’ herdsmen, stimulated by the prospect of freedom, were eager to prove their effectiveness under their master’s eye.
