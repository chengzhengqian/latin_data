They began their march in the hope that our cavalry’s departure would cause delay. 4Upon seeing this, Caesar followed with unencumbered93 legions, leaving a few cohorts to guard the baggage. The cavalry returned quickly to their regular role on the march. 5At the enemy’s rearguard the combat was intense, such that they nearly ran away, and a number of soldiers, indeed some centurions, were killed.
