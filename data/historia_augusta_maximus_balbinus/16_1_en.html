the same as Pupienus, although Herodian, who wrote of his own lifetime, speaks of Maximum, not of Pupienus, and Dexippus, the Greek author, says that Maximum and Balbinus were made emperors against Maximinus after the two Gordians, and that Maximinus was conquered by Maximum, not by Pupienus. In addition to this, they show their ignorance by saying that the child Gordian was prefect of the guard,1 not knowing that he was often carried on a man’s neck to be displayed to the soldiers.2

Maximum and Balbinus reigned for one year,3 after Maximinus and his son had reigned for two years, according to some, for three according to others.4

XVI. Balbinus’ house is shown in Rome to this day in the Carinae,5 large and impressive and still in the possession of his family. Maximum, who many think was Pupienus, was of slender substance, though of the most ample courage.
