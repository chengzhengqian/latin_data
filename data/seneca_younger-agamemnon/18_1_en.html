AGAMEMNON

clytemnestra

Happy the news that has reached my ears. Where then is he delaying, this husband I have sought for ten years? Is his presence felt on sea or on land?

eurybates

Unharmed, enhanced in glory, filled with renown, he has set his returning feet on the long-desired shore.

clytemnestra

We must honor with sacrifices this day of success at last, and the gods’ favor, however tardy. Tell me, does my husband’s brother live? And tell me, where is my sister dwelling?

eurybates

In my prayers I beg and beseech the gods for better things, for the sea’s hazards preclude my speaking with certainty. Once the scattered fleet endured swollen seas, one ship of the group could not see another ship. Even Atreus’ son himself, while wandering on the vast expanse, took heavier losses from the sea than from war; he returns, though a conqueror, like one conquered, with a few battered ships in his wake out of that great fleet.

clytemnestra

Tell us what chance engulfed my ships, what misfortune at sea scattered the leaders.

eurybates

You request what is bitter in the telling; you bid me mix ill-omened news with joyful. My mind balks at speech, sickened and shuddering at such disasters.
