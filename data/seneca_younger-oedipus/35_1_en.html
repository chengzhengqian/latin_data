OEDIPUS

oedipus

I have the guilty man: the number and place match. But what of the time?

jocasta

Now is the tenth harvest.

(old man from Corinth enters)
old man

The people of Corinth call you to your father’s throne: Polybus has reached his eternal rest.

oedipus

How cruel Fortune attacks me on every side! Come now, explain how the old man met his fate.

old man

A gentle sleep released his aged spirit.

oedipus

My father lies dead without a hint of bloodshed! I testify that now in good conscience I can lift my hands to heaven, hands that are pure and fear no crimes. Yet the more fearful portion of my destiny remains.

old man

Your father’s throne will dispel any fears.

oedipus

I would claim my father’s throne, but I am in dread of my mother.

old man

You fear your mother, who is longing for your return in anxious suspense?
