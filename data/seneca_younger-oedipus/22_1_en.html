OEDIPUS

the earth drank deep of the sweet fluids, white springs of snowy milk and Lesbian wine blended with scented thyme. The new bride was escorted to the vast heavens; Phoebus, with hair flowing down to his shoulders, sang the ceremonial hymn, while twin Cupids swung their torches. Jove laid aside his fiery weapon, and abhorred the thunderbolt at Bacchus’ coming.31

As long as the shining stars of the age-old heavens run on, as long as Ocean circles the encompassed earth with its waves and as long as the Moon at the full recovers its fires that waned, as long as the Daystar heralds the dawn each early morning and as long as the lofty Bear knows nothing of sea-blue Nereus, so long we shall worship the radiant face of comely Lyaeus.
