OEDIPUS

oedipus

If the collapse of Thebes is not enough to move you, the fallen scepter of your kinsman’s house should move you.

creon

You will wish you had not learned what you so demand to know.

oedipus

Ignorance is a feeble remedy for troubles.32 Will you really bury information about the people’s wellbeing?

creon

When the cure is foul, being healed is repugnant.

oedipus

Tell us what you have heard, or you will learn, broken by suffering, what an angry king’s might can perform.

creon

Kings hate the very words they bid be spoken.

oedipus

You will be sent to Erebus as a scapegoat for all, unless your words reveal the secrets from the ritual.

creon

Allow me silence. Can any smaller freedom be requested from a king?

oedipus

Often the freedom of silence is more dangerous than speech to king and kingdom.

  • 32This dialogue from 511 is adapted in Chapman, Byron’s Conspiracy 3.3.55–69, beginning so:
    La BrosseYou bid me speak what fear bids me conceal.
    ByronYou have no cause for fear, and therefore speak.
    La BrosseYou’ll rather wish you had been ignorant
    Than be instructed in a thing so ill.
    ByronIgnorance is an idle salve for ill.
