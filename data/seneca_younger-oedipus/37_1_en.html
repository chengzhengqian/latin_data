OEDIPUS

old man

I was herding flocks of horned animals on that mountain.

oedipus

Now tell me also the unmistakable marks on my body.

old man

Your soles had been pierced with iron, and you took your name from the misshapen swelling of your feet.46

oedipus

I need to know who handed over my body.

old man

He tended the royal flocks: under him was a subordinate group of herdsmen.

oedipus

Tell me his name.

old man

Old men’s first weakness is their memory, tired and ebbing away in slow decay.

oedipus

Could you recognize the man by the features of his face?

old man

Perhaps I could: a small token can often recall a faint memory buried by time.

oedipus

All the flocks were driven to the rites at the altar, and their leaders are with them: go, servants, quickly summon the man who has chief control of the herds.

  • 46Oedipus supposedly means “swollen-footed” (Οἰδί-πους).
