OEDIPUS

jocasta

What shall I call you? Son? You object? You are my son: it is my son that feels shame.52 Speak, my reluctant son! Why turn away your head and empty eyes?

oedipus

Who prevents me from enjoying even the darkness? Who gives me eyes again? It is my mother’s voice, my mother’s. I have wasted my efforts. It is not right for us to come together any more in such corruption. The vast sea should separate us, a remote land sunder us, and that strange world that lies opposite this one, facing other stars and a distant sun, should bear one of us two.

jocasta

This fault is fate’s; no one becomes guilty by fate.

oedipus

Spare your words now, mother, spare my ears: I beg you by what remains of my mutilated body, by the inauspicious bond of my blood,53 by all that is holy and unholy in our names.

jocasta
