OEDIPUS

Always a new group files toward Death, a long, sad column hastening in sequence to the shades; but the gloomy line is stopped, since the seven gates do not open wide enough for the crowd seeking graves; the heavy carnage is clogged, a funeral is crowded by a funeral close by. The scourge first touched the slow sacrificial sheep, which hardly grazed the lush grass. A priest stood poised to smite a bull, but while his raised hand aimed the stroke, the beast with his gleaming gold-red horns5 sank down limply. His drooping neck was opened under the weighty blow: no blood, but a foul gore poured from the dark gash and stained the blade. A steed slowed in his running and fell on the very track, throwing his master over his sinking shoulder. Livestock lie abandoned in the fields; the bull weakens as the cattle perish; the herdsman fails, his herd diminished, dying among the wasting bullocks.
