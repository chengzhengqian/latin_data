45Again, if one with a fine fortune rears a sickly son whom he has taken up,b then for fear lest open devotion to a childless man betray you, by your attentions worm your way to the hope that you may be named as second heir,c and if some chance send the child to his grave, you may pass into his place. Seldom does this game fail.

57Suppose someone gives you his will to read, be sure to decline and push the tablets from you; yet in such a way that with a side glance you may catch the substance of the second line on the first page.d Swiftly run your eye across to see whether you are sole heir or share with others. Quite often a constable, new-boiled into a clerk, will dupe the gaping raven, and Nasica the fortune-hunter will make sport for Coranus.e
