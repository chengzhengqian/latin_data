Satires I

Satire VII

How the mongrela Persius took vengeance on the foul and venomous Rupilius Rex (“king”), an outlawed man, is a tale well known, methinks, to every blear-eyed man and barber.b This Persius, a rich man, had a very large business at Clazomenae, also a troublesome lawsuit with Rex. A rough man he was, the sort that in offensiveness could outdo Rex, bold and blustering and so bitter of speech as to outstrip a Sisenna or a Barrus with the speed of white coursers.c

9To return to Rex.
