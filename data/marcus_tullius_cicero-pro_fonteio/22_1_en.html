Wherefore, gentlemen, if you need, too, any42 reminder from me (as indeed you do not), I think that I may, in a modest way, as far as I carry any weight, enjoin upon you to keep carefully in your service men of this description, whose valour, energy, and good fortune in warfare have been weighed and not found wanting. In time past there was a greater abundance of such men in our commonwealth than to-day; yet though this was so, not only their safety but also their honour was regarded. And to-day how should you act, now that the profession of arms has fallen out of fashion among our youth; when our best men and our greatest generals have been wasted either by age or by civil dissension and public calamity; when wars so numerous are either unavoidably undertaken by us, or sprung upon us with unforeseen suddenness?
