Can you then bring yourselves to prefer men of whom you know nothing to men whom you know, the prejudiced to the dispassionate, the foreigner to your own countrymen, the interested to the judicious, the hireling to the unrewarded, the unscrupulous to the conscientious, and the bitterest foes of our empire and our name to true and loyal allies and citizens?

XV. Can you hesitate to believe, gentlemen, that it33 is a blood-feud which is cherished and which is waged by all those tribes against the name of the Roman people? Do you think that as they stand here, cloaked and breeched,a theirs is the meek submissive mood customary to the victims of outrage who, as humble lieges, appeal for aid to a jury?
