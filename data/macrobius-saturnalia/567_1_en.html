26. “All water,” he says,

contains a certain amount of very rarefied air that makes it healthy, but it also contains earthy impurities that give it body, though less body than the earth. When it is compacted by cold air and frigid weather, then, and congeals, the very rarefied air is inevitably expelled, as it were, by evaporation, and with that air’s departure it freezes solid, left with only its earthy nature. This is apparent from the fact that when the same water is melted by the sun’s heat, less of it is left than there was before it froze, while it lacks the only healthy element in the water, which the evaporation removed.

27. Snow, which is merely water that has condensed in the atmosphere, loses its rarefied element by being condensed and therefore plants in our innards the seeds of a wide range of diseases when it is melted and drunk.’
