43. They gave Apollo the surname Nomios, not from his service as a shepherd434—according to the story in which he pastured king Admetus’ flocks—but because the sun provides sustenance for all the things the earth generates.435

44. That’s why he is celebrated as the shepherd, not of a single kind of flock, but of all flocks, as when Neptune says in Homer (Il. 21.448)

Phoebus, you were herding the cattle with their twisted horns and shambling gait;

and the same poet represents him as pasturing mares, saying (Il. 2.766–67),

. . . the ones that Apollo of the silver bow nurtured in Phêriê, both of them mares, bearing with them the terror of Ares.
