9. Let no one form a low opinion of the ancient poets because their verses strike us as rough. That’s the only style that pleased Ennius’ contemporaries, and succeeding generations labored long to acclimatize themselves to the more finely spun style we know. But I don’t want to keep Caecina waiting any longer to present his own recollections of Maro’s borrowings from antiquity.’

Then Caecina said, ‘Rufius has shown, with the knowledge4 of the full range of ancient and more recent texts, how much Maro profited from antiquity both in writing specific lines and in treating whole topics. I will try to show that this most scholarly bard formed very apt judgments about individual words the ancient authors used and inserted in his work some choice elements of diction that our own disregard for antiquity causes us to think his own devising. 31
