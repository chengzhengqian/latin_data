Like a famished lion that often haunts the lofty pens (insane hunger drives him on), should he happen to spy a roe-deer in flight or a stag towering high with his rack, his monstrous maw gapes gleefully, his mane bristles, and he clings to the innards as he reclines at his meal: gross gore washes his relentless jaws— so Mezentius rushes eagerly upon his foes’ dense ranks. (A. 10.723–79) 8. Like a lion that rejoices in coming upon a greatbodied prey, when he has found a horned stag or wild goat in his hunger: he devours it greedily, should swift hounds or sturdy youths come rushing down upon him. So Menelaus rejoiced when he spied with his eyes godlike Alexandros: he had in mind to punish the sinner. (Il. 3.23–28)
