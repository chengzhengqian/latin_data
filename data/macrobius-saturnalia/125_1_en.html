11. Similarly, we learn that in Thrace the sun and Liber are considered the same: they call him Sebazius and worship him in a splendid ritual, as Alexander writes (no. 273 fr. 103 FGrH),481 and on the hill Zilmissus482 they dedicate to him a round temple, its center open to the sky. The temple’s round shape points to the sun’s shape, and light is let in through the roof to show that the sun purifies all things when it shines down from on high, and because the whole world opens up when the sun rises.
