6. The shadows of tall groves, the gently rippling meadows cannot lift the spirit, nor the stream, more Lucid than amber, that tumbles over rocks and seeks the plain. (G. 3.520–22)

Lucretius in Book 2 (361–63):

Neither tender willows and grasses green and dewy nor rivers gliding past the tops of their banks can beguile the mind and turn aside a sudden anxious thought.
