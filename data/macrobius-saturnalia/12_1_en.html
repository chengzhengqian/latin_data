7. The customary ritual for taking auspices also shows that the reckoning is the same: since magistrates must both take the auspices and perform the action to which the auspices were a prelude all on a single day,30 they take the auspices after midnight and perform the action after sunrise, and are thereby said to have taken the auspices and to have acted on the same day.

8. Furthermore, though tribunes of the plebs are not permitted to be absent from Rome for a full day,31 when they leave the city after midnight and return after sunset, but before the next midnight, they are not regarded as having been absent for a day: by returning before midnight, they spend some part of the day in the city.
