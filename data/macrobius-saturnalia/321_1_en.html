(A. 1.326–29) I beseech you, my lady: are you a god or a mortal? O goddess, should I start at the beginning and go on from there, and had you time to hear our labors’ chronicles, the evening star would sooner put the day to rest, the doors of Olympus close. (A. 1.372–74) Who among mortals could speak of all those things? Not even should you abide five years, yes, and six, could you tell all the woes the brilliant Achaians suffered there. (Od. 3.113–16)
