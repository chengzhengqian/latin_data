Note also the following from Naevius’ play The Soothsayer (21–24 SRPF3 2:10): —Who visited you yesterday?—Guests from Praeneste and Lanuvinum. —’Twas fitting to entertain them with their native food, the one set given empty sow’s womb, boiled, the other, nuts poured out down a hill.158 The Greeks call this the “Pontic” nut, and each race gives it a name from the region where it grows with exceptional abundance.159
