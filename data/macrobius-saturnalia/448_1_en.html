Book VI

32. What race of men is this, what barbarous [land permits] such behavior . . .? (A. 1.539)

Furius in Book 6 (fr. 11 FPL3):

What race of men is this, holy offspring of Saturn?

33. . . . and sows the seeds of varied rumor and speaks as follows . . . (A. 12.228)

Furius in Book 11 (fr. 12 FPL3):

They sow the seeds of varied rumors and ask many questions.

34. . . . calling on each by name, and refreshes them for battle after their repulse. (A. 11.731)

Furius in Book 11 (fr. 13 FPL3):

He calls on each by name, tells them the time for what was said is at hand,

then further on (fr. 14 FPL3):

He bucks them up with his words, at the same time rousing their keen spirits for war and readying their thoughts again for battle.

35. Speak, maidens of Pieria: we cannot all do all things (E. 8.63)

Lucilius in Book 5 (224):

He was the elder: we cannot all do all things.16

  • 16The tag “we cannot all do all things” seems to be treated as a Virgilian original at 5.16.7.
