11. ‘Though the names are different, the same religious expression occurs among the Egyptians when Isis mourns Osiris. It is obvious that Osiris is nothing other than the sun, Isis nothing other than the earth (as I’ve said)541 or the natural order, and the same interpretation that is applicable to Adonis and Attis can be applied to the Egyptian rites: grief and joy alternate with the changing conduct of the year.

12. To make plain that this Osiris is the sun, the Egyptians carve a scepter whenever they want to represent him in hieroglyphics: on the scepter they set the image of an eye and with this symbol signify Osiris, showing that this god is the sun and that he looks down on all things with kingly power from on high, as the ancients called the sun the eye of Jupiter.542

13. The Egyptians also call Apollo—that is, the sun—Horus,543 who gave his name to both the twenty-four hours [horae] that a day and night comprise and the four seasons [Gk. hôrai] that complete the year’s cycle.544
