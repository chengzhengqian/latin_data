‘But mention of the Licinii prompts me to move from15 the ancients’ dancing to their extravagant indulgence in the sea’s bounty—specifically, the Licinii with the surname Murena [“moray”], earned (it’s quite well known) from the exorbitant delight they took in that fish.

2. Marcus Varro agrees, saying that the Licinii gained the surname Murena the way that Sergius Orata got his, from his great fondness for the fish called “gilt-heads” [auratae].127

3. This is the Sergius Orata who was the first to have heated baths, the first to install his own oyster-beds at his estate in Baiae, the first to decide that oysters from the Lucrine Lake have the best taste.128 He was a contemporary of the famous orator Lucius Crassus,129 whose serious and stern character Cicero also describes for us.130

4. Although Crassus, a former censor—he was the colleague of Gnaius Domitius [92 BCE]—was considered the most eloquent of all and had pride of place among the men of greatest distinction, he nonetheless put on mourning when a moray of his died in a fishpond at his home and grieved for it like a daughter.
