Book VI

[The cavalry] follows, and the hoof shakes the earth with a din,

again in Book 17 (431 Sk.):

The cavalry passes, the hollow hoof shakes the earth with its beat.

23. The one man who restored our state by his delay. (A. 6.846)12

Ennius in Book 12 (363 Sk.):

One man restored our state by his delay.

24. He fell forward onto his wound, his arms clattered atop him. (A. 10.488)13

Ennius in Book 16 (411 Sk.):

He collapsed and at once his arms clattered atop him.

25. And [Aurora] was starting to dapple the lands with a new day’s light. (A. 4.584)14

Lucretius in Book 2 (144):

. . . when first the dawn dapples the earth with light.15

26. . . . coiling long trailers of flame from behind. (G. 1.367)

Lucretius in Book 2 (207):

Don’t you see [them] drawing long trailers of flame?
