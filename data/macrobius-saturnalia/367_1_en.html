“Die now, and let the father of gods and king of men see about me . . .” (A. 10.739–44)

11. I will speak another word to you, and you store it away in your mind: you yourself will not live long, surely not, but already death and a mighty doom stand at your side: you will be broken by the hands of blameless Akhilleus, grandson of Aiakos. (Il. 16.851–54)

and elsewhere:

Brilliant Akhilleus addressed him as he lay dead: “Die: I shall accept my doom at whatever time Zeus and the other immortal gods wish to achieve it.” (Il. 22.364–66)
