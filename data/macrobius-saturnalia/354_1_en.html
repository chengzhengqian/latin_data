Book V

9. He went striding like a lion raised in the mountains, who too long has been without meat, and his valiant spirit urges him on to go and make an attempt on a crowded fold of sheep: if he finds there the men who tend them, keeping watch on the sheep with dogs and spears, he has no wish to leave the farmstead untested but either pounces and makes his kill or himself is struck at once by a swift hand’s spear-cast. So then did his spirit drive godlike Sarpedon on to dash at the wall and hack a way through the battlements. (Il. 12.299–308) 10. Tears are scattered upon the earth, weapons scattered too. (A. 11.191) The sands grow wet, warriors’ arms grow wet also. (Il. 23.15) 11. In a frenzy, Turnus girds himself hastily for battle, already dressed now in his cuirass, glowing warmly, bristling with brazen scales, his calves encased in gold, head still bare, his sword hitched to his side as he runs, a golden thunderbolt, down from the lofty citadel.
