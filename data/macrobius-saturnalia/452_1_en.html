Book VI

49. . . . his face looming, uncanny pale. (A. 1.354)

Lucretius in Book 1 (123):

. . . but certain images, uncanny pale . . .

50. Then a clammy sweat trickles over his entire body. (A. 3.175)

Ennius in Book 16 (417 Sk.):

Then in his fear sweat trickles from his entire body.18

51. The greased keel glides over the shallows . . . (A. 8.91)

Ennius in Book 14 (376 Sk.):

The greased keel glides, its onrush flies over the waves.19

52. . . . and an iron rain comes rushing down. (A. 12.284)

Ennius in Book 8 (266 Sk.):

The spearmen send their spears broadcast, an iron rain arises.

53. Still, the speeding spear carried off the topmost peak of his helmet. (A. 12.492–93)

Ennius in Book 6 (173–74 Sk.):

. . . Still, the spear as it fled carried off the emblem.
