21. For all that, you still see that Virgil preferred to follow Euripides’ authority, not Aristotle’s—for I can scarcely believe that a man of such finicky learning was unaware of all this. And he was right to prefer Euripides. That he had a vast knowledge of the Greek tragedians can be inferred either from what I’ve just said or from what I’m about to say.
