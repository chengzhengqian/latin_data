8. [Father Anchises] finished speaking and vanished like smoke in the light breeze. “Where are you hurrying now?,” Aeneas said, “Where are you rushing? Whom are you trying to avoid? Who keeps you from my embrace?” (A. 5.740–42) Three times he had tried29 to enfold him in his arms, three times the image, embraced in vain, fled from his grasp. (A. 6.700–1) So she spoke, but in my anxiety I wanted to take in my arms my dead mother’s soul. Three times I darted forward, my spirit bidding me to hold her, three times she flew from my grasp like a shadow or a dream: heartily indeed did I feel sharp grief. (Od. 11.204–8)

the latter (Il. 23.114),
