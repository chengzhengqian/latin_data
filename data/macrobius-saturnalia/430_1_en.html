Still, I think that Hercules was carried across the seas, not in a cup, but in a ship that was called a scyphus,102 just like the cantharus above, and the carchesium, and the cymbia that are named after the cymba, all of which I have shown to be terms for sailing vessels.

‘Virgil sometimes borrows proper names, too, from ancient22 Greek history. As you know, he calls one of Diana’s companions “Opis,” a name people commonly think Virgil perhaps used at random or even made up: they don’t know that the sly poet chose to give the name to the companion because ancient Greek authors had given it to Diana herself.

2. But here is what Virgil says (A. 11.532†–35):

Meanwhile in their resting place on high Latonia was addressing swift Opis, one of her maiden allies, one of her holy troupe, and with her lips she spoke these sad words;

and further on (A. 11.836†):

but for a long while now in the mountain Trivia’s guardian, Opis. . . .

3. He says that Opis is Diana’s companion and ally. But now hear how the name came to be used by Virgil, who (as I said) gave it to the goddess’ companion because he had read that it was an epithet of the goddess herself.
