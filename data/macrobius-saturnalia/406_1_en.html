‘But enough about such things, most of which all18 Romans know, while others are known to at least some. I come now to things fetched from the inner sanctum of Greek literature, things no Roman knows, save those who have earnestly drunk a full draught of Greek learning. For this poet here was as evasive and secretive in his learning as he was diligent and finicky: as a result, the sources of many of his borrowings are difficult to recognize.
