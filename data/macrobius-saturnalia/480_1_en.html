Book VI

Furthermore, as we previously called a bed’s feet and Lamps clinopodes and lychni, to strike a certain tone . . .

19. There’s also this turn of phrase (A. 3.585–86),

. . . nor the vault of heaven alight with the aethra [brilliance] of stars . . .,

which Ennius previously used in Book 16 (415–16 Sk.),

Meanwhile the torch of heaven set and a reddening aethra slowly covered the surface of Ocean,

and Julius in Teuthras (3 SRPF3 1:263):

Through the fiery aethra the blazing torch is borne abroad.

20. daedala [skillful] Circe (A. 7.282),

after Lucretius’ phrase (1.7, 228): daedala earth.

21. And the woods and distant Olympus reboant [reecho] (G. 3.223),

after Lucretius’ phrae (2.28), nor do the golden coffered ceilings reboant with the lyre.

22. But the ancients used this license more extensively, Maro more sparingly, seeing that they used the words pausa [pause], and machaera [short sword] and asôtia
