3. . . . spews a huge wave of morning callers from every nook and cranny (G. 2.462).

“Spews [vomit] a wave” is a nice touch—and ancient, for Ennius says (453 Sk.),

and the Tiber spews its stream into the salty sea.

That’s the source of the so-called vomitoria we have in venues for shows, which allow people entering in a mass to disperse into their seats.

4. Using “column” [agmen] to mean “movement” and a kind of “directed motion” is not an indelicate touch (A. 2.782),

the Thybris flows with its gentle column— no indeed, but it’s also ancient: so Ennius’ phrasing in Book 5 (163 Sk.), the river that flows with its gentle column through the charming city.
