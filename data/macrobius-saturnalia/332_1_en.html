7. O, the only likeness of my Astyanax left to me: such were his eyes, such his hands, such his face. Three times the crags produced a bellow from within the hollowed rocks, three times we saw the foam sprayed out, the stars drenched. (A. 3.566–67) Beneath it awful Kharybdis sucks down the black water. Three times a day she sends it forth, three times she sucks it down . . .
