28. It is said, moreover, that among the prophecies of the soothsayer Marcius, whose two book-rolls were brought into the senate, the following was found (cf. FPL3 pp. 14–15):

If, Romans, you wish to expel the enemy from your land—the pus of nations come from afar—I hold that you must vow to Apollo a set of games that will graciously be mounted for the god each year. Let that praetor be in charge of these games who will have supreme judicial authority over the people and plebs.417 Let the Board of Ten offer sacrifice with victims in the Greek manner.418 If you do this correctly, you will always know joy and the commonwealth will become ever better: for that divinity will destroy your enemies, who graze placidly on your fields.
