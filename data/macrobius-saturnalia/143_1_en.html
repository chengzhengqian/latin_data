19. For that reason they also represent Ammon, the god the Libyans regard as the setting sun, with ram’s horns,550 which are the heart of that animal’s power as the rays are of the sun’s; note too that the Greeks call the ram krios from the word for “head” [kara].551

20. ‘Egyptian cult practice shows that the Bull [Taurus] has a manifold relation to the sun: because in Heliupolis they especially worship a bull called Mnevis, which is consecrated to the sun;552 or because the Apis bull in the city of Memphis553 is welcomed as the likeness of the sun; or because in the town of Hermunthis, in a splendid temple of Apollo, they pay cult to a bull they call Bouchis, which is consecrated to the sun and distinguished by miraculous qualities appropriate to the sun’s nature.554
