3. ‘Virtually everyone agrees that the “profane” is that which lies beyond the concerns of a holy precinct, that is to say, “set farther (porro) apart from a holy precinct (fanum) and religious scruple.”19 Virgil succeeded in providing an example of this meaning in speaking about a grove and the entrance to the underworld, each of which was “sacred” (A. 6.258–59):

“. . . Away, profane ones, be ye far away,” the seer cries out, “and stand apart wholly from this grove.”

4.
