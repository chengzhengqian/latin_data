12. Yet how richly, how colorfully he produces the same idea when it pleases him! (A. 11.768–73, 777)

Choreus, by chance, a devotee of Cybele and once her priest was conspicuous from afar in his gleaming Phrygian arms, riding a foaming horse that was covered in mail of Bronze scales fastened with gold in a feather pattern. He himself, a brilliant sight of outlandish red and purple, was shooting Cretan arrows from a Lycian bow . . . his tunic and barbarous5 leggings were embroidered.
