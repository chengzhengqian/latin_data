27. . . . the clouds explode, the fires redouble. (A. 3.199)

Lucretius in Book 2 (214):

Now on this side, now on that the clouds explode, the fires . . .

28. . . . was summoning up images of war. (A. 5.674)

Lucretius in Book 2 (324):

. . . arrange and fill: images of war are summoned up.

29. and images of those who’ve lost the light of life. (G. 4.472)

Lucretius in Book 4 (38–39):

. . . when often we spy uncanny shapes and likenesses of those who’ve lost the light of life.

30. Fierce, with bitter glances, he retreats. . . . (A. 9.794)

Lucretius in Book 5(33):

Fierce, with bitter glances, the snake of monstrous bulk . . .

31. Aurora was leaving Tithonus’ saffron-hued bed. (A. 4.585)

Furius in Book 1 of his Annals (fr. 7 FPL3):

Meanwhile Aurora was leaving Ocean’s bed.
