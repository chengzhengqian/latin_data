13. Where could there have been greater scope for denouncing luxury when a pontiffs’ meal was stuffed with such things! How disgusting just to list the sorts of food! Indeed, Titius, in his speech supporting the law of Fannius (fr. 3 ORF2),107 reproaches his contemporaries for serving Trojan pig, so-called because it is “pregnant” with other animals enclosed within, just as the famous Trojan horse was “pregnant with armed men.”108

14. ‘That sort of unrestrained gluttony also drove them to force-feed even hares, as Varro reports when he speaks about hares in his third book on agriculture (3.12.5): “The practice of force-feeding, too, has recently been established: they take the hares from the hutch, shut them up in cages, and make them fat in the confined space.”

15. If anyone’s surprised at Varro’s report that they used to force-feed hares in those days, let him take in something that merits even greater surprise: snails were force-fed, as Varro also reports in the same book (3.14.5).109 I’ve provided the reference, should anyone want to read Varro’s own words.
