Book VI

15. Still, you now will pay your debt to me with your hot blood. . . . (A. 9.422†–23)

Ennius in Book 1 (94–95 Sk.):

No man alive, by god, will do this and go unpunished, nor will you: you pay your debt to me with your hot blood.

16. From all sides the undaunted yeoman come charging with their lances . . . . (A. 7.520–21)

Ennius Book 3 (143–44 Sk.):

After they become weary . . . of showering one another with their thonged spears, they come charging from all sides with their lances.

17. They strive with all their might and main. (A. 12.552*)

Ennius in Book 4 (151 Sk.):

On the ladders the Romans strive with all their might and main,

and Book 16 (404–5 Sk.):

By their rule kings seek statues, and memorials after death, they build up their fame, they strive with all their might and main.

18. And unscroll with me the vast reaches of war. (A. 9.528†)
