21. After the divinity of the site has been invoked, the party swearing the oath testifies to the matter that the oath concerns: if he has acted in good faith, he departs unharmed, but if the oath rests upon bad faith, the perjurer soon loses his life in the pool. This fact so confirmed the religious awe attaching to the brothers that the Mixing Bowls came to be called “those beyond conciliation,” the Palici “those easily reconciled.”

22. The precinct of the Palici is also used for divination: when a barren year had left Sicily parched, the inhabitants were advised by an oracle of the Palici to offer a specific sacrifice to a certain hero, and when they did, the island’s fertility returned. In thanks, the Sicilians heaped every sort of produce on the altar of the Palici, which came to be called the “rich altar” because of that abundance.
