13. But the person entirely bereft of praise in every circumstance and at every moment is someone whom none can praise, the very worst and basest of all; and just as being free of every fault makes one a person whom none can fault—and since being such a one is the image of perfect virtue—being one whom none can praise is therefore the ultimate definition of wickedness.

14. That’s why Homer usually expresses the full measure of praise not by naming virtues but by noting the absence of vices: hence we find (Il. 5.366 et al.)
