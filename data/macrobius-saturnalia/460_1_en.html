11. Lucretius says (1182–89):

Then they gave many signs of death besides: mind disturbed, thoughts sunk in grief and fear, brow distraught, looks wild and fierce, ears beset by constant roaring to boot, breathing rapid or an occasional great sigh, neck clammy with a glistening slick of sweat, spittle thin and sparse, bright yellow and salty, coughing that scarcely escaped their rasping throats.

12. Virgil says (509–11):

It did some good to pour liquid Bacchus in through a funnel: that seemed the only help for the dying. Soon this proved deadly itself.

Lucretius says (1226–29):

Nor was there at hand any generally reliable cure: the Thing that had allowed some to feel on their faces the sky’s life-giving breeze and to see the precincts of heaven proved deadly for others and sealed their doom.
