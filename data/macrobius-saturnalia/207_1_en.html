8. ‘It remains to take counsel with Virgil on the meaning of “filled with religious scruple.” Servius Sulpicius has recorded (fr. 3 IAH 1:241 = fr. 14 IAR6) that “religious scruple” [religio] is the term attached to something that a certain holiness makes remote and apart from us, as though the term were derived from “to leave behind” [relinquere], as “reverence” [caerimonia] is derived from “to be in want of” [career].21
