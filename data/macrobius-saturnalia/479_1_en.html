16. . . . and to overturn the seasonable pine in the woods. (G. 1.256)

He took this application of the word “seasonable” [tempestiva] to the pine from Cato, who says (Agr. 31.2), “When you uproot pine, walnut, and any other wood, do it after noon at the time of a waning moon, when there is no south wind; then, indeed, it will be seasonable [tempestiva], when its seed will be ripe.”
