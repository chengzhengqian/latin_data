6. So Quadrigarius wrote in Book 3 of his Annals (fr. 43): “There a thousand of people [mille hominum] is slain”; and Lucilius in Book 3 of his Satires (fr. 125):

From gate to gate there is a thousand [mille . . . est], then six to Salernum.

7. Elsewhere he even declined this noun: so in Book 15 he says (fr. 511–13),

Beat this horse though it might at a thousand of paces, and two thousand, no jolting galloper from Campania will stay in the race over a greater distance—in fact, it will seem to go backwards.

similarly in Book 9 (fr. 331),

With one thousand of sesterces you can make a hundred thousand.
