13. But these usages are derived from what Pomponius wrote in his Atellan farce34 titled The First of March (57–60 SRPF3 2:280–81):

You should make your tone fine-spun, so they seem a woman’s words.—Just order that my gift35 be brought, and I’ll make my tone slight and tinkling, and further on, Even now shall I make my tone fine-spun.

14. . . . and we scrape by the jutting [proiecta] rocks of Pachynus. (A. 3.699†–700)

proiecta means “downcast” according to current usage, but “extended” [porrecta]—“cast straight ahead” [porro iacta]—according to the ancients’ usage, as he says elsewhere (A. 10.587*–88),

while with left foot extended [proiecto] he readies himself for battle.
