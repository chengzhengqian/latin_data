Accordingly, a phenomenon that alternates with the seasons everywhere else is always the same in Egypt, where the air is always hot: as it seeks the depths, the cold becomes wrapped up in the roots of the vines and so gives that property to the juice produced there. That’s why the wines of a hot region are free of heat.’

12. ‘Having once entered on the topic of heat,’ Albinus said, ‘our line of inquiry is not finding it easy to head in a different direction.72 Please tell me why a person who enters a hot bath is burned less if he doesn’t move but feels the heat more if he stirs the water with his own motion, as the water burns more intensely every time it is moved.’

13. Dysarius said, ‘Hot water that adheres to our body soon feels milder, either because the skin has become acclimated to it or because it is cooled by contact with us. But motion constantly brings new hot water into contact with the body, the acclimatization I just referred to fails to occurs, and the constant supply of new water increases the sensation of heat.’

14. ‘Why, then,’ Albinus said, ‘when the hot air is moved by a breeze in the summer, does it feel not hotter but cooler? By the same principle motion should increase the burning sensation in this case too.’
