5. Yet he did not overlook the view concerning open space that Varro cited first: for example (A. 4.56–57†),

First they approach the shrines and seek the gods’ peace among the altars. . . .

to which he soon adds (A. 4.62),

. . . or before the eyes of the gods she paces about near their rich altars.

For does not “pace about” mean simply “walk about over the expanse of a broad thoroughfare”? And by adding “near their altars” he shows that the space was taken over for the gods’ use. Thus Virgil in his usual manner develops a point about cult by the way, as it were.
