Then after a few years Antius Restio brought another law before the people152—an excellent law, yet rendered null and void, not because it was formally repealed, but because luxury was firmly planted and the vices were united in an unshakable alliance. Still, one memorable thing is recalled about Restio, the law’s mover: afterwards, and for as long as he lived, he never dined out, lest he witness the disdain heaped on the law that he himself had carried for the common good.
