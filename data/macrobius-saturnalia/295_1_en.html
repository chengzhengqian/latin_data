6. ‘Orators also use the source of emotion that is called “contrary to expectation.” Virgil often put it to work (A. 1.250):

We, your offspring, to whom you grant heaven’s citadel . . .

and the rest. And Dido (A. 4.419–20):

If I was able to anticipate this great grief, I will be able to endure it, too, my sister.

7. Aeneas, speaking of Evander (A. 11.49–50),

And now perhaps, very much a captive of empty hope, he is even making vows,

and this (E. 9.2–4*):

. . . that a newcomer— a thing I never feared—should say, as the new tenant of my small plot: this is mine, you old dwellers clear out.
