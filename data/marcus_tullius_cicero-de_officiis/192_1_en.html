For if physical deformity excites a certain amount of aversion, how offensive ought the deformity and hideousness of a demoralized soul to seem! 106 Therefore, thosea who discuss these problems with more rigour make bold to say that moral wrong is the only evil, while thoseb who treat them with more laxity do not hesitate to call it the supreme evil. Once more, they quote the sentiment:

“None have I given, none give I ever to the faithless.”

It was proper for the poet to say that, because, when he was working out his Atreus, he had to make the words fit the character. But if they mean to adopt it as a principle, that a pledge given to the faithless is no pledge, let them look to it that it be not a mere loophole for perjury that they seek.
