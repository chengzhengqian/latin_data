Later, the Phoenicians, some for the sake of reducing the large population at home, others out of a desire for power, having roused the commons and others who were eager for radical change to join them, founded Hippo,52 Hadrumetum, Leptis,53 and other cities on the seacoast.54 These soon became greatly enlarged and were in some cases a defense and in others a glory to the mother cities. As to Carthage, I think it better to be silent rather than to say too little, since time warns me to hasten on to other topics.

In the neighborhood, then, of the Catabathmos, the region which separates Egypt from Africa, the first city down the coast55 is Cyrene, a colony of the people of Thera, and then come the two Syrtes with Leptis56 between them. Next is a site called the Altars of the Philaeni, the point which the Carthaginians regarded as marking the boundary of their empire in the direction of Egypt;57 then other Punic cities.58 The rest of the region as far as Mauritania is held by the Numidians, while the people nearest Spain are the Moors. South of Numidia, so we are told, are the Gaetulians, some of whom live in huts, while others lead a less civilized, nomadic life. Still farther to the south are the Ethiopians; then the regions scorched by the sun’s heat.
