17. My subject appears to require that I set out a brief account of Africa’s geography and touch upon those nations with which we Romans have had wars and alliances. However, as to regions and tribes that are less frequented because of the heat or rough terrain, as well as deserts, concerning them I would not easily relate any factual information. The rest I shall cover as briefly as possible.

In marking off the earth’s surface, most have placed Africa in a third division, while a few assert that there is only Asia and Europe but that Africa is part of Europe. It has as its boundary on the west the strait between our sea38 and the Ocean; on the east, a broad sloping track, a district called Catabathmos39 by the inhabitants. The sea is rough, lacking harbors; the soil fertile in grain, good for herding but unproductive of trees; there is a lack of water from rainfall and the ground. The race of men here are physically fit, fleet of foot, and capable of strenuous exertion. They commonly pass away from old age, except for those who have perished from weapons or wild beasts; disease seldom gets the better of any of them. In addition, there are a great many animals of a harmful sort.
