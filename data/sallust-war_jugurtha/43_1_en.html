37. In Rome at that time the nation was being fiercely disturbed by dissensions among the tribunes. The plebeian tribunes Publius Lucullus and Lucius Annius,127 were striving to prolong their term of office in spite of the opposition of their colleagues,128 and this wrangling blocked the elections of the whole year.129 Because of the delay, Aulus, who, as I stated above, had been left as commander in the camp in place of the consul, came to hope for either finishing the war or obtaining a bribe from the king because of the fear the army inspired in him. He, therefore, called up his soldiers in the month of January130 from their winter quarters for active duty in the field, and by forced marches, in spite of the severity of the winter, reached the town of Suthul,131 where the king’s treasury was located.
