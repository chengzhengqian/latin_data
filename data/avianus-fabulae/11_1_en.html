XII

The Peasant and the Treasure

On breaking up the earth by the impact of his plough a peasant noticed a treasure-hoard leap into view from the furrows. Presently with quickened heart he abandoned the plough, now disesteemed, and drove his oxen to better pastures. At once with vows he raised altars in honour of Earth, since she unasked had given him the wealth entrusted to her. As he rejoiced in his new estate, Fortune with an eye to the future gave him a warning; for she was piqued that he did not think her also deserving of incense. “For the moment you neglect to hand over your treasure-trove to any temple of mine, and prefer to share it with other gods; but when the gold is stolen and you are in the depths of grief, I shall be the first whom you will tearfully entreat in your beggary.”
