II

The Tortoise and the Eagle

Once a tortoise said to the feathered birds that if one of the swift fliers could carry her away and set her safe on the ground b she would at once from the sands of the Erythraean Sea produce shells c on which their bright-crusted pearl conferred a value. She felt it an outrage that, despite her diligence, her slow pace prevented her doing anything or making any progress the whole day. She loaded an eagle with false promises, but her untruthful tongue found a broken troth to match her own.
