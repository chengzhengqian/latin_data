VII

The Dog that would not Bark

[Not readily is it the nature of evil dispositions to believe themselves deserving of stripes and punishment.]

It happened once there was a dog with no gruff bark, that did not open its mouth in a wide gape as a first sign of mischief, but put its soft-wagging tail in fear beneath it, and then would fly into a fury and snap recklessly with its teeth. To prevent anyone being taken unawares by its pretended good character, its master had made it wear a bell a round its savage throat. He fastened its neck and tied the tinkling brass underneath to give signals of warning by its ready motion. The dog, however, believed this was worn by it as a reward, and triumphantly began to look down on the crowd of dogs like itself.
