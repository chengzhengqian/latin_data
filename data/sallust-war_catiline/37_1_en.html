Amid these preparations and arrangements, Cethegus constantly complained about the inaction of his accomplices: that they were wasting great opportunities by indecision and delay; that in such a perilous situation there was need for action, not deliberation, and that if a few would give aid, he would storm the senate house,90 even though the rest were fainthearted. By nature he was aggressive, violent, and prompt to act; he set the highest value upon speed.

44. Meanwhile, the Allobroges, in keeping with Cicero’s instructions, approached the other conspirators through Gabinius as intermediary. They demanded of Lentulus, Cethegus, Statilius, and likewise of Cassius a sealed oath to take back to their countrymen, saying that otherwise their countrymen could not readily be induced to embark upon so serious an enterprise. The others complied without the least suspicion; Cassius promised to come in person to Gaul shortly and left the City a little before the envoys.
