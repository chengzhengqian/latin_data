But if the mental excellence of kings and commanders were just as vigorous in peace as in war, human affairs would maintain a more even and steady course; you would not behold power shifting to and fro and everything in turmoil and confusion. For sovereignty is easily preserved by those practices which brought it into being in the first place. But when hard work is replaced by laziness, self-restraint and evenhandedness by willfulness and insolence, there is a change in fortune accompanying the change in character. Thus sovereignty is always being transferred to the best man from the hands of his inferior.
