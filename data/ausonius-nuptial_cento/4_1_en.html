I.—The Preface

Give heed to these my words and hither turn gladsome minds, ye twain for courage, ye twain for prowess in arms renowned, ye twain who prosper—a breed invincible in war. And thou especially—for there is clear assurance that under high omens thou passest o’er the deep—than whom none ever was more strict in reverence of the gods, none greater in war and deeds of arms; thou and thy son, the second hope of mighty Rome, the flower and excellence of heroes of old time and my especial charge—he who in name is his grandfather’s double, but in spirit and in might his father’s. I sing as I am bidden. To each his own essay shall bring toil and event: for me ’tis lawful to perform a task enjoined.
