He encamped there for three days, and with a further three days’ march from Apamea reached Pamphylia, where he had ordered the king’s representatives to take the money and the grain. Here 2,500 talents of silver were taken in and transported to Apamea; the grain was distributed among the troops. Manlius then marched to Perga,158 the only place in the region occupied by a garrison of the king. As he drew near, the commander of the garrison met him with a request for a moratorium of thirty days in which to consult King Antiochus about surrendering the city. Granted the time, the commander left the garrison on the specified date. The consul sent his brother Lucius Manlius from Perga to Oroanda with 4,000 men to collect the remainder of the money that the people of Oroanda had agreed to pay,159 and since he had been told that King Eumenes and the ten commissioners had arrived in Ephesus from Rome Manlius himself led the army back to Apamea, ordering Eumenes’ representatives to follow him.

38. There a treaty with Antiochus was drawn up on the recommendation of the ten commissioners, worded much as follows:160

There shall be peace between King Antiochus and the Roman people on the following terms and conditions:

The king shall not permit to pass through his own lands, or those of peoples who will be subject to him, any army intending to make war on the people of Rome or their allies, or help it with provisions or give it any other form of assistance. The Romans and their allies shall make the same commitment to Antiochus and those who will be subject to his rule.
