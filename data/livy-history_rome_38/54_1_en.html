34. The Spartans, terrified by this episode, were first of all ordered to tear down their walls. Next they were told that all foreign auxiliary troops who had been mercenaries under the tyrants should quit Laconian territory, and then that slaves that the tyrants had freed—and there were large numbers of them—should leave before a certain date; and that the Achaeans would have the right to seize, remove and sell any who remained. They were also ordered to repeal the legislation and system of Lycurgus131 and adapt to Achaean laws and institutions. In this way, they were told, they would become a united body and find it easier to reach agreement on everything.

No condition did the Spartans fulfill with more readiness than the demolition of their walls, and none more reluctantly than the recall of the exiles.132 The decree authorizing their restoration was passed in a full council of the Achaeans in Tegea.133 It was also noted at the meeting that the foreign auxiliaries had been sent off and that the “newly enfranchised Spartans,” as they called the men who had been freed by the tyrants, had left the city, scattering into the countryside.
