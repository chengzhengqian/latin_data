King Antiochus shall not have the right to hire mercenaries from races that are in the power of the Roman people or even accept volunteers from them.

Houses and buildings belonging to Rhodians or Rhodian allies that are within the bounds of Antiochus’ kingdom shall remain the property of those Rhodians and allies on the same legal basis as before the war. In the case of financial debts, they shall be called in; and the right to seek out, identify, and recover any property that has been removed shall likewise obtain.

Antiochus shall also remove his garrisons from any cities due to be surrendered that are held by people to whom he gave them, and ensure that they are properly handed over.

He shall pay 12,000 Attic talents165 of unadulterated silver in equal installments within a period of twelve years—with a talent to weigh not less than eighty Roman pounds—and 540,000 measures of wheat. He shall pay King Eumenes 350 talents within a five-year period, as well as 127 talents in lieu of grain, as has been assessed.

He shall give the Romans twenty hostages, to be changed within three years, none being younger than eighteen years of age or older than forty five.
