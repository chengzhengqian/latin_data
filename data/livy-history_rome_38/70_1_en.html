BOOK XXXVIII

At the conclusion of the elections, the consul brought before the senate the question of the provinces that they wanted assigned to the praetors. The senators decided that two should be at Rome for legal administration; two, Sicily and Sardinia, should be outside Italy; and two, Tarentum and Gaul, within Italy. The praetors designate were also instructed to draw lots for them at once before entering office. That year, on the order of the urban praetor Marcus Claudius, Lucius Minucius Myrtilus and Lucius Manlius were handed over to ambassadors by the fetiales and taken off to Carthage because they had allegedly beaten some Carthaginian envoys.187

There was talk of a major war in Liguria that was escalating every day. Accordingly, on the day on which the new consuls raised the matter of the provinces and the state of the republic, the senators formally assigned Liguria to both as their province.
