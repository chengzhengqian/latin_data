On the first day they reached the River Aladrus,87 and on the next a village they call Tyscos. When ambassadors from the Oroandans88 came there seeking a treaty, a price of 200 talents was demanded of them and they were granted their request to take the demand home.

The consul next led the army to Plitendum,89 and then encamped at Alyatti. There the men who had been sent to Eposognatus returned to him and with them were representatives of the chieftain, who begged him not to attack the Tectosagi. Eposognatus would visit that tribe in person, they said, and would persuade them to follow Manlius’ instructions. The chieftain was granted his request and the army moved on from there through the land called Axylon.90 Its name is based on fact: not only does bear no wood, but it does not even have thorn bushes or anything else to fuel a fire—the inhabitants use cow dung instead of wood.

The Romans were encamped at Cuballum, a fortress of Galatia, when enemy cavalry appeared creating a great uproar. With their sudden attack they not only threw the Roman outposts into disarray but also killed a number of men.
