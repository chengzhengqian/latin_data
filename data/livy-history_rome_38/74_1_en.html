After this, by decree of the decemvirs, a three-day supplication195 was offered for the health of the people because a deadly plague had been taking a heavy toll in the city and the countryside. After that the Latin Festival took place. When the consuls were freed from these religious duties and had completed the muster of the troops—both preferred to employ new recruits—they set off for their province and demobilized all the veterans.

After the departure of the consuls, the proconsul Gnaeus Manlius came to Rome. When granted an audience with the senate at the temple of Bellona196 by the praetor Servius Sulpicius, Manlius gave an account of his achievements and demanded that, in the light of them, the immortal gods be appropriately honored and that he be permitted to ride in triumph into the city. However, a majority of the ten commissioners who had been with him opposed this, especially Lucius Furius Purpureo and Lucius Aemilius Paullus.197
