30. After settling affairs in Cephallania, and installing a garrison at Same, the consul crossed to the Peloponnese since the people of Aegium especially, but also those of Sparta, had long been pressing him to come. Since the beginning of the Achaean League, the plenary meetings of the nation had invariably been convened at Aegium, which was due either to the city’s importance or its convenient location. This year for the first time Philopoemen attempted to undermine the tradition and was preparing to propose a law that the meetings be held in turn in all the member states of the Achaean League.123 Just before the arrival of the consul, the damiurgi (that is the chief magistrates) of the member states called a meeting at Aegium but Philopoemen, who was then praetor,124 announced Argos as the venue. When it became clear that nearly all members would make an appearance there, the consul also came to Argos, despite his support for the cause of the people of Aegium. A discussion of the issue arose here and the consul, seeing that the case of the people of Aegium was lost, abandoned his initial support for them.
