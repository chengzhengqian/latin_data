Fighting all who resisted them and imposing tribute payments on those who sued for peace, they reached Byzantium and for a time settled on the coast of the Propontis, where they levied taxes on the cities of the region. Then a desire to cross to Asia overtook them since, being now close to it, they were hearing reports of the great fertility of its soil. They therefore took Lysimachia74 by subterfuge, occupied the whole of the Chersonese by military force, and went down to the Hellespont. When they saw that it was by only a narrow strait that Asia was separated from them, their appetite for crossing was greatly whetted and they sent messengers to Antipater, the governor of that coast, to arrange a passage. But when negotiations were more drawn out than they had anticipated, a fresh quarrel erupted among the chieftains. Lonnorius retraced his steps to Byzantium, whence he had come, with most of the men.
