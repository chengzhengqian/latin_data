To this Gracchus, it is generally agreed, the younger of Scipio’s two daughters251 was married (the elder was certainly betrothed by her father to Publius Cornelius Nasica252). What is not so certain is whether the girl was both betrothed and married to him after her father’s death,253 or whether the anecdotal version is true. This has it that when L. Scipio was being taken to prison and none of Gracchus’ colleagues came to his aid, Gracchus swore that his earlier feud with the Scipios was still alive, that his actions were not designed to curry favor with them, but that he would not have the brother of Africanus taken to that prison into which he had seen Publius Africanus take enemy potentates and commanders.
