Claudius states that the Numidian force consisted of 400 cavalry and a few elephants and that Muttines’ son broke through the enemy center with 100 elite cavalry. Setting his elephants in the center and deploying his cavalry on the wings, Muttines engaged the enemy and the son soon caused panic among them with an attack from the rear. Thrown off balance by this furious cavalry charge, the Thracians failed to reach the infantry column.

Gnaeus Manlius led the army through Macedonia and into Thessaly. He thereafter continued through Epirus to Apollonia and, since he did not have so little regard for the sea in winter as to risk a crossing, spent the winter in Apollonia.

42. Toward the end of the year the consul Marcus Valerius came to Rome from Liguria to oversee the appointment of the new magistrates, though he had achieved nothing noteworthy in his province to provide a reasonable excuse for his arriving later than usual for the elections. The consular elections took place on February 18th; and at them Marcus Aemilius Lepidus and Gaius Flaminius were chosen.184 The next day the following were designated praetors: Appius Claudius Pulcher,185 Servius Sulpicius Galba, Quintus Terentius Culleo, Lucius Terentius Massiliota, Quintus Fulvius Flaccus, and Marcus Furius Crassipes.
