In addition, the territory of Belbina, which the tyrants of Sparta had unlawfully seized,135 was returned to that state under an old statute of the Achaeans passed during the reign of Philip son of Amyntas.136 The state of Sparta was virtually drained of her strength by these measures and was long subject to the Achaeans, but nothing was so detrimental to them as the removal of the discipline of Lycurgus, to which they had become accustomed over eight hundred years.

35. Marcus Fulvius left the council in which the debate between the Achaeans and Spartans had taken place in the consul’s presence and came to Rome for the elections, because the year was now at an end. There he oversaw the election of Marcus Valerius Messalla and Gaius Livius Salinator137 as consuls, after engineering the defeat of his enemy Marcus Aemilius Lepidus,138 who was also seeking election that year. The following were then elected as praetors: Quintus Marcius Philippus, Marcus Claudius Marcellus, Gaius Stertinius, Gaius Atinius, Publius Claudius Pulcher,139 and Lucius Manlius Acidinus.140 The elections finished, it was decided that the consul Marcus Fulvius should return to his province and his army, and both he and his colleague Gnaeus Manlius had their commands prorogued for a year.
