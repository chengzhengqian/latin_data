The two parties shall settle their differences by legal and judicial procedure or, if both agree, by war.166

There was also a clause included in the treaty about surrendering the Carthaginian Hannibal, the Aetolian Thoas, the Acarnanian Mnasilochus and the Chalcidians Eubulidas and Philo,167 and another stating that additions, removals or modifications subsequently negotiated could be made without voiding the treaty.

39. The consul took an oath to observe the treaty, and Quintus Minucius Thermus and Lucius Manlius,168 who happened to return from Oroanda at that time, left to demand an oath of compliance from the king. The consul also wrote to Quintus Fabius Labeo, commander of the fleet, ordering him to leave for Patara immediately and demolish and burn any of the king’s ships that were docked there. Leaving Ephesus, Labeo demolished or set fire to fifty decked ships. On the same expedition he accepted the surrender of Telmessus,169 whose inhabitants were terror-stricken by the sudden approach of the fleet. Ordering the troops left at Ephesus to follow, he immediately crossed from Lycia to Greece by way of the islands. After spending a few days in Athens to allow the ships to reach Piraeus from Ephesus, he brought the whole fleet back from there to Italy.
