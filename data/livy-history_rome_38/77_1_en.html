But my word! We were the same force that was cut down, put to flight and robbed of its baggage when we fell among Thracian bandits on the return journey, as fortune reminded us of what would have happened had we had a real enemy facing us. Along with many other brave men Quintus Minucius Thermus met his end there,202 a loss much more serious than if Gnaeus Manlius had perished, the man through whose recklessness that debacle had occurred. The army bringing back the spoils of King Antiochus was broken into three parts, the vanguard in one place, the rear in another and the baggage in a third; and it spent a night hiding in the bushes in the lairs of wild animals.

“And it is for this that a triumph is being requested? Even if no defeat or ignominy had been incurred in Thrace, who are the enemies over whom you would be requesting your triumph? Those, I suppose, whom the senate or Roman people had assigned to you as enemies. This was how a triumph came to be awarded over King Antiochus to Lucius Scipio here and to Manius Acilius over there; and over King Philip a little earlier to Titus Quinctius; and over Hannibal, the Carthaginians and Syphax to Publius Africanus.203 And even then, when the senate had already voted for war, the most minor details were still investigated. To whom was the declaration of war to be made? Was it to be declared to the kings themselves, or was an announcement to some outpost enough?
