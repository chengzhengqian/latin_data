If those provinces needed to be secured by armies, then just as the consul Lucius Scipio had replaced Manius Acilius, and then Marcus Fulvius Lucius and Gnaeus Manlius had as consuls replaced Lucius Scipio, so the consuls Gaius Livius and Marcus Valerius should have replaced Fulvius and Manlius. The Aetolian war was over, he continued, Asia had been recovered from Antiochus, and the Gauls had been defeated. Now surely either the consuls should be sent to the consular armies or the legions there should be brought home and finally restored to the state. After listening to these arguments, the senate remained firm in its judgment that Liguria should be the province of both consuls but decided that Manlius and Fulvius should quit their provinces, withdraw the armies from them and return to Rome.

43. There was bad blood between Marcus Fulvius and the consul Marcus Aemilius and in particular Aemilius supposed that it was through Marcus Fulvius’ devices that he had been held back from the consulship for two years.190 Accordingly, to provoke antipathy toward Fulvius, Aemilius brought before the senate some Ambracian envoys whom he had primed with accusations. They protested that they had been subject to aggression although they had been at peace with Rome,191 had done the bidding of the former consuls and had been ready to show the same compliance to Marcus Fulvius. First of all their lands had been plundered, they said, and their city faced with the dreadful prospect of pillage and massacre, so that they were driven by fear of that to shut their gates.
