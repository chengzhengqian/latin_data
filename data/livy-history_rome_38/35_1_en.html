The action commenced with missiles hurled at long range and at first the fighting was even, the Gauls advantaged by their position, the Romans by the diversity and quantity of their weapons. As the contest developed, there was no longer such equality. The shields of the Gauls, long but not wide enough for their large physique,104 and flat, as well, gave them inadequate protection. Furthermore, they had no weapons other than swords, and these were of no use since the enemy was not engaging with them hand to hand. They did make use of stones, though not the smaller ones (since they had not stockpiled them), but only what came into each man’s hands as he frantically groped for them; and also, being inexperienced in this mode of fighting, they had neither the skill nor the strength to assist the throw. Lacking protection, they were struck by arrows, sling projectiles, and javelins coming from all directions; and, blinded by rage and panic, they could not see what they were doing and were caught up in a kind of fighting for which they had very little aptitude. In hand-to-hand combat the Gauls receive and inflict wounds by turns and anger fires their courage; but receiving wounds from light weapons coming from unseen points far away, and having no object at which to charge in their blind fury, they charge wildly into their own comrades like speared animals.
