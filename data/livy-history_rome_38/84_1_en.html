50. On that day the accusations would have carried more weight than the defense had not the senate drawn out the debate into the evening. The house broke up leaving the impression that it would refuse the triumph. But the following day the relatives and friends of Gnaeus Manlius pressed their case as hard as they could, and the authority of the senior members also carried weight when they claimed that there was no known precedent for a commander entering the city without the triumphal carriage and laurel wreath, as a private citizen denied his meed of honor, after crushing an enemy, fulfilling his allotted assignment and bringing home his army. Embarrassment prevailed over spite and a large majority authorized the triumph.
