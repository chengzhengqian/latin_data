From the rostra he climbed to the Capitol. At the same time the whole gathering turned and followed Scipio, so that finally even the clerks and messengers left the tribunes and nobody now remained with them apart from their retinue of slaves and the herald whose function was, from the rostra, to summon the defendant.225 Scipio, along with the people of Rome, did the rounds of all the temples of the gods, not just on the Capitol but throughout the city. Thanks to the public acclamation and the true appreciation of his greatness, that day was almost more festive than the one on which he rode into the city in triumph over King Syphax and the Carthaginians.226
