“Since the charge of starting the war has been disposed of, I must now account for its conduct. Here, I would feel confidence in my case even if it were not before the Roman senate that I was appearing but the senate of Carthage where, they say, commanders are crucified for poor strategy in a campaign,211 even if it is successful. However, I am appearing in a state that brings the gods into the commencement and execution of all its enterprises for the very reason that it subjects to no man’s vilification actions that have received divine approval; and in a state which, in its formulaic language, when it decrees supplication212 or a triumph, includes the expression: ‘Inasmuch as he has well and successfully performed public business.’ Suppose I were reluctant to vaunt my military prowess and thought it overweening and presumptuous to do so but I still requested that due honor be paid to the immortal gods for my own good fortune and that of my army, in that we defeated such a great tribe with no loss of soldiers; and suppose that I also asked that I myself should climb in triumph to the Capitol, the place from which I set off after the formal declaration of my vows. Would you deny this to me and to the immortal gods, as well?
