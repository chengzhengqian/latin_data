“As for the war itself—the war for which you are requesting a triumph—how did you conduct it? Did you have favorable ground for the battle and a time of your choosing? You are quite correct in demanding that the immortal gods be shown due honor—first because they did not want the army to pay for the recklessness of its commander, whose campaign was in flagrant contravention of international law, and secondly because they set before us wild beasts, not soldiers.

46. “Do not think that it is just the name of the Gallogrecians that is a mongrel. Their physique and character showed the effects of interbreeding and contamination much earlier.201 If they really were the Gauls with whom we had a thousand encounters in Italy with mixed success, do you think that, if all depended on your commander, even a messenger would have returned from there? Twice he fought with them, twice he advanced on them over unfavorable terrain; and he set his battle line in a valley below the enemy, almost beneath their feet. They could have crushed us even without hurling weapons from the higher ground—just by throwing their naked bodies at us! So what was the result? Great is the fortune of the Roman people, great and awesome its reputation! The Gauls were left almost stupefied by the recent downfall of Hannibal, Philip and Antiochus.
