BOOK XXXVIII

24. There still remained the war with the Tectosagi, which had not yet begun. Setting off against them, the consul reached Ancyra,112 a city famous in that area, on the third day of his march, and from there the enemy was little more than ten miles off. When the army was encamped here, a remarkable exploit was brought off by a female captive.

She was the wife of the Gallic chieftain Ortiago,113 a very attractive woman being held with a number of other prisoners. Charged with guarding her was a centurion with a soldier’s sexual appetite and greed. The man at first attempted to seduce her but, seeing that consensual sex was abhorrent to her, he assaulted her person, which fortune had made his slave. Later, to mitigate the humiliation of the assault, he gave the woman hope of returning to her people but even that he did not offer free of charge as a lover would. He negotiated the payment of a certain amount of gold and, not to have any of his men aware of his actions, allowed the woman to send any fellow prisoner of her choosing as a messenger to her people. He picked a spot near the river to which no more than two of the prisoner’s kinsmen were to come with the gold to fetch her the following night. It so happened that a slave actually belonging to the woman was among the prisoners in custody with her. This was the messenger the centurion took out at dusk beyond the sentry posts.
