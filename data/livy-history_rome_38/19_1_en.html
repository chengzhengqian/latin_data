13. There is there a revered temple and oracle of Apollo; its priests are said make prophetic utterances in verse not lacking in elegance. From here, after two days march, they reached the River Harpasus, where ambassadors from Alabanda46 came to Manlius requesting that by his authority or by armed force he bring back into its former allegiance a stronghold that had recently defected from them. There, too, Athenaeus, brother of Eumenes and Attalus, came to Manlius, together with the Cretan Lensus and Corragus the Macedonian. They brought with them 1,000 infantry of various races and 300 horse. The consul took the stronghold by force, sending a military tribune with a modest contingent against it, and after its capture he returned it to the people of Alabanda. He did not himself depart from his route, and he pitched camp at Antioch on the River Maeander.
