“We have put them to the test not only fighting legion against legion but also engaging them man to man. Titus Manlius and Marcus Valerius81 have demonstrated how superior Roman valor is to Gallic rage. Marcus Manlius on his own flung down the Gauls as they climbed the capitol in a body.82

“Besides, those ancestors of ours had genuine Gauls to deal with, men born in their own land. These are now a lesser breed, mongrels and truly ‘Gallograeci,’ as they are called.83 It is the same in the vegetable and animal world: seeds have not the power to preserve the strain to the extent that the natural qualities of the soil and the climate in which they grow change it. Macedonians who inhabit Alexandria in Egypt, or Seleucia and Babylonia, and other colonies scattered throughout the world, have degenerated into Syrians, Parthians and Egyptians. Massilia, situated among the Gauls, has derived some of its character from its neighbors.84 What do the people of Tarentum85 have left of their tough and rugged Spartan discipline? Whatever grows in its own natural surroundings is of superior quality. Planted in foreign soil its nature changes and it declines to the level of the environment by which it is fed.
