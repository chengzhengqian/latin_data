After that the consul, aware now that he had reached the enemy, went ahead only after reconnoitering and with his column carefully arranged. He now came to the River Sangarius91 by uninterrupted marches, and because the river was nowhere fordable he proceeded to construct a bridge.

The Sangarius flows from Mt. Adoreus through Phrygia and joins the River Tymbris92 at the Bithynian border. Flowing with greater volume, its waters now doubled in size, it runs through Bithynia and empties into the Propontis, remarkable less for its size than for the enormous quantities of fish it provides for people in the area. On the completion of the bridge the Romans crossed the river, and while they proceeded along the bank they were met by Galli of the Magna Mater from Pessinus93 wearing their ceremonial insignia. They uttered prophesies in frenzied chanting, saying that the goddess was opening up a road to the war for the Romans and granting them victory and mastery over the region. The consul declared that he accepted the omen, and encamped on that very spot.

The next day he reached Gordium.94 While not a large town, this is a more populous and busy commercial center than is usual for the interior.
