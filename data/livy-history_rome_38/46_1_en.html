They put out for contract the building of a support structure on the Capitol above the Aequimelium,117 and the paving with flint of the road between the Porta Capena and the temple of Mars. The people of Campania consulted the senate on where they should be registered for the census and it was decided it should be in Rome. There were huge floods that year and the Tiber twelve times inundated the Campus Martius and the level areas of the city.

When the war with the Gauls in Asia had been finished off by the consul Gnaeus Manlius, the other consul Marcus Fulvius, who had now defeated the Aetolians, crossed to Cephallania. He sent deputies around the states on the island to ask whether they wanted to surrender to the Romans or else take their chances in war. In all cases fear managed to disincline them from rejecting surrender. They delivered the hostages demanded of them, the poor peoples according to their strength . . . the Pronnaeans. . . ,118 but the Cranians, Palensians and Samaeans each gave twenty.   • 116The princeps senatus had his name entered first on the senate list compiled by the censors, and he was the first to give his opinion on any item under discussion. He held the position for life. He invariably came from one of the great families (maiores gentes), and all known holders of the position were from one of six gentes: the Aemilii, Claudii, Cornelii, Fabii, Manlii, and Valerii. It was often the senior living ex-censor who was chosen for the position. Cf. OCD s.v. princeps senatus.
  • 117Or, possibly, Aequimaelium: an open space on the slope of the Capitoline, supposedly named after Spurius Maelius (a wealthy grain dealer executed for aiming at monarchy [cf. 4.15.5– 16.1]), whose house had stood there. By Cicero’s time it was a market for sacrificial lambs (Cic. Div. 2.39). Cf. Richardson 3.
  • 118The text is corrupt here (see textual note). Thucydides (2.30.2) mentions four city-states of Cephallania: Same, Pale, Crane, and Proni. As the people of the first three are mentioned, mention of the fourth (and the number of hostages) has probably dropped out of the text. “Pronnaeans” is a supplement.
