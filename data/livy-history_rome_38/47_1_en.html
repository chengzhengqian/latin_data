When the response indicated no peaceful resolution, the siege of the city got under way. Fulvius had with him the whole battery of slings and siege engines that had been shipped over from the siege of Ambracia, and his men energetically completed the works that needed to be built. Battering rams were therefore moved up at two points and began to pound the walls.

29. Nothing by which the siege works or the enemy could be obstructed was overlooked by the people of Same. They had, however, two principal means of resistance: first, where the wall was demolished they would invariably block the gap with a strong, new one close behind it; secondly, they made sudden counterattacks either on the enemy siege works or their outposts, and in these encounters they usually came off better.
