And was it on ‘favorable ground’ that Manius Acilius fought with King Antiochus at Thermopylae?213 And was not Philip in a similar position, occupying the heights overlooking the River Aous, when Titus Quinctius dislodged him?214 In fact, I have so far been unable to work out what conception my opponents have of this enemy or how they wish you to imagine him. If it is as one who was degenerate and corrupted by the delights of Asia, what danger was there in facing him even on ‘unfavorable ground’? If it is as an enemy fearsome both for his barbarous character and physical strength, do you then refuse a triumph for a victory as great as this? That is blind jealousy, members of the senate, and it knows only how to disparage acts of courage and sully the honors and rewards that accrue from them. So I ask for your indulgence, members of the senate, if my address to you has been rather protracted—made so not by any desire to sing my own praises but by the need to defend myself against these charges.

“Furthermore, when passing through Thrace, could I have made narrow passes broad or made plains out of heights and cultivated land out of wild forests? Could I have ensured that Thracian bandits were nowhere lurking in lairs known only to them, that no piece of baggage would be filched and no pack animal taken from a column of such length? That no one would be wounded, and that the valiant and stalwart Quintus Minucius would not die of his wound? They focus on this incident in which we unhappily lost a citizen of such qualities.
