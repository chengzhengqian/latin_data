And what about his position as legate in Asia, made insignificant by ill health and sullied by his son’s mishap,233 as well as by the necessity, after his return, of facing trial or else quitting both the trial and his native region at one and the same time. But no war fought by the Romans was greater and more fraught with danger than the Punic war, and he alone earned the paramount glory of bringing it to an end.

54. With the death of Africanus the morale of his opponents improved, and chief among these was Marcus Porcius Cato234 who, even during Scipio’s lifetime, made a habit of carping at the man’s eminence. It is thought that it was at Cato’s instigation that the Petillii commenced their suit against Africanus while he was alive and introduced their bill after his death. The bill was worded essentially as follows: “Citizens, with regard to the portion of the moneys appropriated, removed and exacted from King Antiochus, and those under his command, but not delivered to the public purse,235 is it your wish and command that the urban praetor, Servius Sulpicius, put to the senate the question of which of those who are now praetors it wishes to investigate the matter?”

At first, Quintus and Lucius Mummius tried to veto the bill; they claimed that it was appropriate for the senate to investigate any case of moneys not paid into the public purse, as had invariably been done in the past.236 The Petillii proceeded with an attack on the nobility and the regal comportment of the Scipios in the senate.
