Moving on from there to the Rhotrine Springs, Manlius pitched camp at a village they call Aporidos Come.66 The next day Seleucus came there from Apamea. Manlius sent the sick, and the baggage for which he had no use, to Apamea and, setting off with guides that he received from Seleucus, he advanced to the plain of Metropolis67 that same day, and to Dyniai, in Phrygia, the next. From Dyniai he came to Synnada,68 where all the surrounding towns were deserted from fear. He was after that trailing along a column weighed down with loot from these towns, and when he had barely covered a distance of five miles in an entire day he reached Beudos (the so-called “Old Beudos”).69 He next bivouacked at Anabura, the following day at the Springs of Alexander and the third day at Abbasium.70 There he maintained a camp for a number of days because they had reached the territory of the Tolostobogii.71
