21To the same type of figure belongs that which occurs when the same word is used first in one function, and then in another,c as follows: “Why do you so zealously concern yourself with this matter, which will cause you much concern?” Again: “To be dear to you would bring me joy—if only I take care it shall not in anguish cost me dear.”d Again: “I would leave this place, should the Senate give me leave.”e

In the four kinds of figures which I have thus far set forth,f the frequent recourse to the same word is not dictated by verbal poverty; rather there inheres in the repetition an elegance which the ear can distinguish more easily than words can explain.
