Now I see: You do not wish to contend with me in a trial at law, where failure brings shame, and success glory. You wish to kill me. True, I shall be killed, but I will die unconquered.’ ‘Sententiousa even at the point of death! You do not wish to beg your life of me when you see I have you in my power?’ Then the woman: ‘Nay, truly he begs and implores you. I plead with you, be moved to pity. And do you, in heaven’s name, clasp his knees. He has you in his power. He has prevailed over you, and do you now prevail over your spirit.’ ‘Why do you not cease, my wife,’ says he, ‘to utter words unworthy of me? Be silent, and attend to your tasks.b And you, why do you not, once for all, rob me of life, and yourself, by my death, of every hope of enjoying life?’ The intruder thrust the weeping woman from him, and as the master began to say something or other, worthy, I am sure, of his manliness, buried the sword in his side.”c
