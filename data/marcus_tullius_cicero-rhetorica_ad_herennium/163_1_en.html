In order to make this figure, which is hard to invent, quite clear, I have subjoined several examples—so that, well understood, it may be easier for the speaker to invent.

XXIX. Surrenderd is used when we indicate in speaking that we yield and submit the whole matter to another’s will, as follows: “Since only soul and body remain to me, now that I am deprived of everything else, even these, which alone of many goods are left me, I deliver up to you and to your power. You may use and even abuse mee in your own way as you think best; with impunity make your decision upon me, whatever it may be; speak and give a sign
