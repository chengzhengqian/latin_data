Therefore, because no one has written carefully on this subjecta—all have thought it scarcely possible for voice, mien, and gesture to be lucidly described, as appertaining to our sense-experience—and because the mastery of delivery is a very important requisite for speaking, the whole subject, as I believe, deserves serious consideration.

Delivery, then, includes Voice Quality and Physical Movement.b Voice Qualityc has a certain character 20of its own, acquired by method and application. It has three aspects: Volume, Stability, and Flexibility. Vocal volume is primarily the gift of nature; cultivationd augments it somewhat, but chiefly conserves it.
