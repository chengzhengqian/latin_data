Ad Herennium, II

Caelius, sitting in judgement, acquitteda of the charge of injuryb the man who had by name attacked the poet Lucilius on the stage, while Publius Mucius condemnedc the man who had specifically named the 20poet Lucius Accius. Therefore, because different past judgements can be offered for a like case, we shall, when this comes to pass, compare the judges, the circumstances, and the number of decisions. The Law rests on Equityd when it seems to agree with truth and the general welfare; for example, a man who is more than sixty years old, and pleads illness, shall substitute an attorney for himself.e Thus according to circumstances and a person’s status virtually a new kind of Law may well be established.

It is Law founded on Agreementf if the parties have made some contract between themselves—if there is some covenant between parties.
