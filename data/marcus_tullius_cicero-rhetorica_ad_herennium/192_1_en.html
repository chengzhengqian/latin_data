It renders a thought more brilliant when used for no other purpose than beauty; clearer, when throwing more light upon what was somewhat obscure; more plausible, when giving the thought greater verisimilitude; more vivid, when expressing everything so lucidly that the matter can, I may almost say, be touched by the hand. I would have added individual specimens of each type had I not under Refining demonstrated the nature of Exemplification,a and, under Comparison, made clear the motives for its use.b Therefore I have been unwilling to make my discussion of it either too brief for it to be understood, or too long once it is understood.

Similec is the comparison of one figure with another, implying a certain resemblance between them. This is used either for praise or censure.
