From these letters one can see how the young man had been improved and bettered by his father-in-law’s counsel. Some say that Timesitheus’ letter was written in Greek but in any case to the above effect. So great was the power, moreover, of his strength of character and righteousness, that he rose from great obscurity to make the Emperor Gordian illustrious not only for his noble birth but also for his deeds.

XXVI. There was a severe earthquake in Gordian’s reign—so severe that whole cities with all their inhabitants disappeared in the opening of the ground. Vast sacrifices were offered through the entire city and the entire world because of this. And Cordus says that the Sibylline Books were consulted, and everything that seemed ordered therein done; whereupon this world-wide evil was stayed.
