Comets also presage civil discord and strife between kin. At no other time did heaven experience more conflagrations than when the sworn forces of the blood-stained conspirators c filled with their ranks the plains of Philippi, and on sand scarcely yet dry of blood d the Roman legionary took his stand on the bones of warriors and limbs mangled in former fighting; the empire’s armed might engaged in conflict with itself, and Augustus, father of his country, marching in his father’s steps, prevailed. Nor was this all: war at Actium was still to come, waged by an army pledged in dowry, when the destiny of the world was again at stake and the ruler of heaven was determined on the sea; the fate of Rome, threatened with a female yoke, hung in the balance, and the very thunderbolt clashed with the sistrum of Isis. e Still to come f were battles against slaves
