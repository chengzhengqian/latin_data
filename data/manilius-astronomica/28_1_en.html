This circle is held by the Crab at the top, at the bottom by Capricorn, and is twice crossed by the circle which balances day and night, b whose line it cuts in the signs of the Ram and the Balance. Thus the curve of the circle is drawn through three circles c and conceals by its downward slope the straightness of its path. Nor does it elude the sight of the eye, as if it were a circle to be comprehended by the mind alone, even as the previous circles are perceived by the mind: nay, throughout its mighty circuit it shines like a baldric studded with stars and gives brilliance to heaven with its broad outline standing out in sharp relief. d
