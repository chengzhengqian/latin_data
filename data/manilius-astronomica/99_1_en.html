For the Ram deducts as many hours from the nights as the Fishes had previously taken away on their own account c ; an hour is given to the Bull, and the Twins add a further half so as to amass the debits of the preceding signs. d Thus the last sign corresponds to the first e ; and likewise assessed with equal wealth are the signs that shine next to these, f and so also the two middle ones. g This is the sequence in which the nights diminish and the days increase from the constellation of midwinter, h and in which the year’s cycle revolves until the solstice takes place at the sign of the lingering i Crab; then the night matches the winter’s day, then the long day the space of winter’s night, and returns over a course similar to that over which it had increased.
