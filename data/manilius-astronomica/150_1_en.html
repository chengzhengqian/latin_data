32Lord of the flock and conqueror of the sea, to which, a horn lost and robbed even of its fleece, it gave its burden c and a name, the Ram, which bade the magic arts of Colchian Medea journey to Iolcos and spread her poisons throughout the world, even now draws Argo by the poop to its side, as though still on the seas, through the stars on its right. d But the foremost part of the poop emerges to show its fires only when the Ram has brought four degrees of his countenance above the horizon.
