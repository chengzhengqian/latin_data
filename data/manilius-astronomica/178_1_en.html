30Such are the special powers and times of influence which in ages past the creator of the mighty firmament appointed for the planets.

Finally, that I may bring my account of heaven to a close, let me tell you of the different degrees of luminosity with which the stars shine. b Brilliants of the first order enable you to identify easily the constellations of the Bull, the Lion, and the Virgin; Bootes, the Lyre, and the Charioteer; Orion, the Dogs, the Ship, and the Southern Fish. Twinkling with a lesser radiance, although still passing bright, are stars which mark the Twins, the Balance, the Scorpion, and the Archer; Helice, the Crown, the Swan, the Horse, and Perseus and Andromeda. 710The third magnitude has provided the dowry of the Pleiad sisters, c spreading over their maiden faces a blush of bronze.
