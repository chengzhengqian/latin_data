Two sorts of objections can be raised to this undertaking of mine. The first is: “It is better in the original Greek.” One might ask this critic whether they themselves can produce anything better in Latin. The second is: “Why should I read this translation of yours, rather than the Greek original? “But at the same time they accept the Andria, the Synephebi and likewise the Andromache or the Antiope or the Epigoni in Latin.b Why their aversion to speeches translated from the Greek when they have none to translations of poetry?
