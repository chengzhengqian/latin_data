Unassigned Fragments

20

Priscianus: ‘Plico, plicas,’ perfect ‘plicui’ or ‘plicavi,’ . . . Still it is found to form the preterite in -avi even when a prefix is added; but this use is rare. Pacuvius— a

Where brought you in your ship of cares, Ο woman So sadly overwhelmed with troubles?

21

Festus: ‘Solari.’ That archaic writers used this verb without a prefix is witnessed by Pacuvius when he says—

He solaces aids and cheers me.

22

Nonius: ‘Frons’ in the feminine gender. . . . Pacuvius— b with smothered voice and beetling brow and bloated face.

23–4

Paulus: ‘Incicorem’ (untamed, ungentle), untamed and avage. Pacuvius—

Restrain your ungentle wrath.

Now and then ‘cicur’ (tamed, gentle) is put for wise; for example, Pacuvius again—

a gentle counsel.

25–6

Varro: In a passage of Pacuvius—

Nothing can make him gentle, nothing hale, Nothing remodel him;

‘cicurare’ means to make tame.

27

Paulus: ‘Mulcere’ . . . means to soften or soothe. Pacuvius—

Why, do you try to scare me with a look, And fondle me with praises?
