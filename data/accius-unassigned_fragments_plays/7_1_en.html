Unassigned Fragments of Plays

38

Varro: In a passage of Accius—

By calling on thy name and nod divine With many a prayer

they say ‘numen’ means authority.

39

Nonius: ‘Frenos’ . . . in the neuter . . . Accius—

Some fitted bit and bridle to the carriage, And to the horses’ mouths.

40

Varro: ‘Iugula’ (Collar-Bone), a constellation which Accius calls Orion in the phrase—

More swiftly opes Orion.

41

Priscianus: ‘Experta,’ passively. Accius—

Many a blessing has been found to rise From this, and has been proved by trial.

42

Nonius: ‘Ferox’ is used with the verb ‘to be’ and an ablative case like ‘fretus’ or ‘arrogans’ . . . Accius—

No man is either proud with violence So great as that, or overflows so much With fortune’s wealth.

43

Cicero: That was the difficulty from which arose, in the words of Accius, those—

tricky-tongued rogueries
