  • 1A ravine near Sparta, called καιάδας.
  • 2The passage is obscure and perhaps corrupt. Since Thucydides says that Pausanias was first buried near the καιάδας, procul may mean “hard by,” as in Horace, Sat. ii. 6. 105 and Epist. i. 7. 32, and quo erat mortuus may be a gloss. The death of Pausanias took place soon after the condemnation of Themistocles; see ii. 8. 2, and note 3.
