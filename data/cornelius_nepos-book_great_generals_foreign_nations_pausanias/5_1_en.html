5. Upon getting this evidence the ephors thought it would be better to arrest him in Sparta.2 When they had left the place, and Pausanias, having won over the Argilian, as he thought, was on his way to Lacedaemon, in the course of the journey, just as he was on the point of being arrested, from the expression of one of the ephors, who wished to warn him, he perceived that they had designs upon him. Accordingly, he took refuge in the temple of Minerva, surnamed Chalcioikos,3 outstripping his pursuers by only a few steps. To prevent his leaving the place, the ephors at once blocked up the doors of the temple and destroyed its roof,4 in order that he might the sooner die from exposure to the open heavens. It is said that Pausanias’ mother was living at the time, and that having learned of her son’s guilt, in spite of her great age she was among the first to bring a stone to the entrance of the temple, to immure her own child.
