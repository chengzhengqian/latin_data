Pescennius Niger

“Glorious Niger stands here, the dread of the soldiers of Egypt, Faithful ally of Thebes, willing a golden age. Loved by the kings and the nations of earth, and by Rome the all golden, Dear to the Antonines, aye, dear to the Empire too. Black is the surname he bears, and black is the statue we’ve fashioned, Thus do surname and hue, hero and marble, agree.”

As for these verses, Severus refused to erase them when this was proposed by his prefects and masters of ceremonies, and said, besides: “If indeed he was such a man, let all men learn how great was the man we vanquished; if such he was not, let all men deem that such was the man we vanquished; no, leave it as it is, for such he really was”.
