19. On the whole I rather think that from chaos came choum and then cavuma ‘hollow,’ and from this caelum ‘sky,’ since, as I have said, “this around and above, which holds in its embrace the earth,” is the cavum caelum ‘hollow sky.’ And so Andromeda says to Night,b

You who traverse the hollows of sky With your chariot marked by the stars.

And Agamemnon says,c

In the shield of the sky, that soundeth on high,

for a shield is a hollow thing. And Ennius likewise, with reference to a cavern,d

Enormous arches of the sky.

20. Wherefore as from cavum ‘hollow’ come caveaa ‘cavity,’ and caullaeb ‘hole or passage,’ and convallisc ‘enclosed valley’ as being a cavata vallis ‘hollowed valley,’ and cavernae ‘caverns’ from the cavatio ‘hollowing,’ as a cavum ‘hollow thing,’d so developed caelum ‘sky’ from cavum, which itself was from chaos, from which, in Hesiod,e come all things.
