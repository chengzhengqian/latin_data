VII. 51. Narroa ‘I narrate,’ when I make a second person narus ‘acquainted with’ something; from which comes narratio ‘narration,’ by which we make acquaintance with an occurrence. This part of acting is in the section of saying,b and the words are united with time-ideas or are from them: those of this sort seem to be radicals.

52.a That man fatur ‘speaks’ who first emits from his mouth an utterance which may convey a meaning. From this, before they can do so, children are called infantes ‘non-speakers, infants’; when they do this, they are said now fari ‘to speak’; not only this word, but also, from likeness to the utterance of a child, fariolusb ‘soothsayer’ and fatuus’ prophetic speaker’ are said.
