This is wrong; for the tree-names ought to be spoken with E and I,b fici like nummic ‘sesterces,’ because the ablative is ficis like nummis, and the genitive is ficorum like nummorum. If the plural were ficus, then it would be like manusd ‘hand’; we should say ablative ficibus like manibus, and genitive ficuum like manuum, and we should not say accusative ficos, but ficus, just as we do not say accusative manos but manus; nor would usage speak the oblique cases of the singular genitive fici and ablative fico, just as it does not say genitive mani but manus, nor ablative mano but manu.

XLIX. 81. Moreover, they think that there is proof of the non-existence of Regularity, in the fact that Lucilius writesa:

Priced a ten-as, or else we may say at ten-asses.b

They are in error, because Lucilius should not have been uncertain as to the form, since both are right.
