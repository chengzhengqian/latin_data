Therefore they advehebantur ‘were conveyed’ thither by rafts; and traces of this survive, in that the way by which they were then transported is now called Velabrum ‘ferry,’ and the place from which they landed at the bottom of New Street is a chapel of the Velabra.d

44. Velabruma is from vehere ‘to convey.’ Even now, those persons are said to do velatura ‘ferrying,’ who do this for pay. The mercesb ‘pay’ (so called from merere ‘to earn’ and aes ‘copper money’) for this ferrying of those who crossed by rafts was a farthing. From this Lucilius wrotec:
