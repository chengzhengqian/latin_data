In front of this is the Rostra ‘Speaker’s Stand’d: of which this is the name—the rostra ‘beaks’ taken from the enemy’s ships have been fastened to it. A little to the right of it, in the direction of the Comitium, is a lower platform, where the envoys of the nations who had been sent to the senate were to wait; this, like many things, was called from a part of its use, being named the Graecostasis ‘Stand of the Greeks.’e

Above the Graecostasis was the Senaculuma ‘Senate-Stand,’ where the Temple of Concord and the Basilica Opimia are; it was called Senaculum as a place where the senate or the senioresb ‘elders’ were to take their places, named like γερουσία ‘assembly of elders’ among the Greeks. Lautolae ‘baths,’ from lavare ‘to wash,’ because there near the Double Janusc there once wered hot springs. From these there wasd a pool in the Lesser Velabrum, from which fact it was called velabrum because there they vehebantur ‘were conveyed’ by skiffs, like that greater Velabrum of which mention has been made above.e
