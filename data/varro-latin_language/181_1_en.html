According to Festus, 322 a 24 and 323. 6 M., sardare means intel-legere, perhaps ‘to understand like a Sardinian,’ that is, very poorly, for the Sardinians had in antiquity a bad reputation in various lines. The verse of Naevius runs: Quod bruti nee satis sardare queunt.
