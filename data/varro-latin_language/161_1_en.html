On the Latin Language, VII

61. In the Pagona:

Respect for hash is gone,b for haunch of ham, for chops.

Syncerastum ‘hash’ is all kinds of food mixed together, under an old Greek name.

62. In The Lazy Hanger-ona:

I started to go home by a side-way to the right.

Tramesb ‘side-way’ is said from transversum ‘turned across.’

63. In The Runawaysa:

Then come and look, and see what welts.—I’ve looked now; well, what next?

Vibices ‘welts,’ the flesh of the body raised high by lashes.

64. In The Story of the Trinket-Boxa:

As if they aren’t here now, the dark and dirty slugs.

Limaxb ‘slug’ from limits ‘slimy mud,’ because it lives there.

Diobolous women, rush-perfumed, quite wonder-foul.c Diobolares ‘diobolous,’ from two obolsd apiece. Schoenicolae ‘rush-perfumed,’ from schoenus ‘aromatic rush,’ an unpleasant perfumed ointment. Miraculaee ‘wonder-foul,’ from mira ‘wonderful things,’ that is, monstrosities; from which Accius saysf:

Plautus, Frag. 108 Ritschl.

  • bThat is, the speaker has lost his appetite.
  • cPlautus, Cist. 407.
  • dOne third of a drachma, or franc of the pre-war standard; now somewhat over five pence British, or ten cents U.S.A.
