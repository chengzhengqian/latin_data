The remaining officials, because they are inferior to these magistri ‘masters,’ are called magistratus ‘magistrates,’ derived just as albatus ‘whitened, white-clad’ is derived from albus ‘white.’b

XV. 83. The sacerdotes ‘priests’ collectively were named from the sacra ‘sacred rites.’ The pontifices ‘high-priests,’ Quintus Scaevolaa the Pontifex Maximus said, were named from posse ‘to be able’ and facere ‘to do,’ as though potentifices. For my part I think that the name comes from pons ‘bridge’b; for by them the Bridge-on-Pilesc was made in the first place, and it was likewise repeatedly repaired by them, since in that connexion rites are performed on both sides of the Tiber with no small ceremony. The curiones were named from the curiae; they are created for conducting sacred rites in the curiae.d

84. The flaminesa ‘flamens,’ because in Latium they always kept their heads covered and had their hair girt with a woollen filum ‘band,’ were originally called filamines.
