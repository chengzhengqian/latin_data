106. In the Casinaa:

Let him go and make love, let him do what he will, As long as at home you have nothing amiss.

Nihil delicuumb ‘nothing amiss’ is said from this, that things are not ad deliquandum ‘in need of straining out’ the admixtures, as those which are turbid are strained, that they may become liquida ‘clear.’ Aureliusc writes that delicuum is from liquidum ‘clear’; Claudius,d that it is from eliquatum ‘strained.’ Anyone who prefers to follow either of them will have an authority to back him up.

In Atiliuse:

With joy his mind is melted.

Liquitur ‘is melted’ is formed from liquare ‘to melt.’
