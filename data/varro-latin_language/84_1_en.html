181. Tributum ‘tribute’ was said from the tribus ‘tribes,’a because that money which was levied on the people, was exacted tributim ‘tribe by tribe’ individually, in proportion to their financial rating in the census. From this, that money which was allotted was attributum ‘assigned’; from this also, those to whom the money was assigned, that they may pay it to the soldiery, were called tribuni aerarii ‘treasury tribunes’; that which was assigned, was the aes militare ‘soldier’s pay-fund’; this is what Plautus meansb:

Comes the soldier, asks for cash.

And from this comes the term milites aerarii ‘paid soldiers,’ from the aes ‘cash-pay,’ because they earned stipends.c

182.
