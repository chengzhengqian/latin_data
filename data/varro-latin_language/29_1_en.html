On the Latin Language, V

Inasmuch as the separation of life and body is the exitus ‘way out’ for all creatures born, from that comes exitium ‘destruction,’ just as when they ineunt ‘go into’ unity, it is their initia ‘beginnings.’

61. From this fact, every body, when there is excessive heat or excessive moisture, perishes, or if it survives, is barren. Summer and winter are witnesses to this: in the one the air is blazing hot and the wheat-ears dry up; in the other, nature has no wish to struggle with rain and cold for purposes of birth, and rather waits for spring. Therefore the conditions of procreation are two: fire and water. Thus these are used at the threshold in weddings,a because there is union here, and fire is male, which the semen is in the other case, and the water is the female, because the embryo develops from her moisture, and the force that brings their vinctio ‘binding’ is Venusb ‘Love.’

62. Hence the comic poet says,a

Venus is his victress, do you see it?
