65. These same gods Sky and Earth are Jupiter and Juno, because, as Ennius says,a

That one is the Jupiter of whom I speak, whom Grecians call Air; who is the windy blast and cloud, and after wards the rain; After rain, the cold; he then becomes again the wind and air. This is why those things of which I speak to you are Jupiter: Help he givesb to men, to fields and cities, and to beasties all.

Because all come from him and are under him, he addresses him with the wordsc:

O father and king of the gods and the mortals.

Pater ‘father’ because he patefacitd ‘makes evident’ the seed; for then it patet ‘is evident’ that conception has taken place, when that which is born comes out from it.
