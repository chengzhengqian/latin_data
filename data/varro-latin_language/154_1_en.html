42. In Ennius is thisa:

Olli answered Egeria’s voice, speaking softly and sweetly.

Olli ‘to him’ is the same as illi, dative to feminine olla and to masculine ollus. The one of these is said by the herald when he announces at the elections “Olla ‘that’ century,” and not illa. The other is heard in the case of funerals of which announcement is made, wherein is said

Ollusb ‘that man’ has been given to letumc ‘death,’

which the Greek calls λήθη, that is, oblivion.

43. In Ennius this verse is founda:

Banquetsb he first did establish, and likewise the shieldsc that are holy

The ancilia ‘shields’ were named from their ambecisus ‘incision on both sides,’ because these arms were incised at right and left like those of the Thracians.
