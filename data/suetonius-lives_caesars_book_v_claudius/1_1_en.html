The Deified Claudius

I. The father of Claudius Caesar, Drusus, who at first had the forename Decimus and later that of Nero, was born of Livia1 within three months after her marriage to Augustus2 (for she was with child at the time) and there was a suspicion that he was begotten by his stepfather in adulterous intercourse. Certain it is that this verse at once became current:

Three-month children are born to the blessed.

This Drusus, while holding the offices of quaestor and praetor, was in charge of the war in Raetia and later of that in Germany.3 He was the first of Roman generals to sail the northern Ocean, and beyond the Rhine with prodigious labour he constructed the huge canal4 which to this very day is called by his name.5 Even after he had defeated the enemy in many battles and driven them far into the wilds of the interior, he did not cease his pursuit until the apparition of a barbarian woman of greater than human size, speaking in the Latin tongue, forbade him to push his victory further.
