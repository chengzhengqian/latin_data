boy in every bedroom when you were young and an adulterer later on, you are a disgrace to every order and a reminder of the Civil War. What worse could we undergo than to see you as a citizen in this order? Give it up: the truculent attacks on good men, the indulgence in your pathological insolence, the judging of others by your own character—give it up! You can’t make a friend by such behaviour. You seem to want to have an enemy.

I shall bring my words to a close, Conscript Fathers. For I have often seen that those who speak candidly of the enormities of others offend the ears of their audience more gravely than those who perpetrate the same. I have to put my mind not to what Sallustius deserves to hear but to saying what can properly be said by me.
