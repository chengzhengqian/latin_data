VI.—To a graceful Boy

While Nature was in doubt whether to make a boy or girl, thou didst become almost a girl, my handsome boy.

VII.—On the Mother of an Emperor

Yet for this cause1 I shall be called happy above all others, whether I, a woman, have borne a man or hero.

VIII.—To Dido

Ah! luckless Dido, unhappy in both husbands: this, dying, caused thy flight; that, fleeing, caused thy death.
