(i) Found 4 at Venusia, now lost. c. 225 b.c. Inscribed with a decree of the local Senate.

The quaestors Quintus Ravellius, son of . . .; Publius Cominius son of Publius; Lucius Mallius son of Gaius, consulted the local 5 Senate. The Senators resolved that the place should be either sacred or else public state-property. 6

  • 1Others of a similar type: C.I.L., I, 2, 647–651.
  • 2miles from Ephesus.
  • 3Rarely does a Roman milestone give the miles to a place. At one time it was claimed that figures could be read on the stone after Pisas. But fresh examination seems to show that there were never any figures at all after Pisas.
  • 4The first three lines are illegible. Cf. also C.I.L., I, 2, 403.
  • 5This is uncertain, but something like senatuom decuriatom or decuriatim or decurionom is probable. The quaestors are here local officials.
  • 6public meant much the same as sacred, since it meant belonging to the government.
