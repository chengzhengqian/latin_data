Menaechmi

Prologue

Enter the SPEAKER OF THE PROLOGUE.

spea

First and foremost I announce that Salus is well-disposed toward myself and you, spectators.1 I’m bringing you Plautus . . . with my tongue, not my hand. I ask you to receive him with benevolent ears. Now receive the plot5 summary and pay attention. I’ll put it in as few words as I can. This is what writers do in comedies: they claim that everything took place in Athens, intending that it should seem more Greek to you. I shall say what happened10 nowhere except where it is said to have happened. This72 city is Epidamnus as long as this play is being staged. When another is staged it’ll become another town, just as households too always change. At one time a pimp lives75 here, at another a young man, at yet another an old one, a76 pauper, a beggar, a king, a hanger-on, a soothsayer.
