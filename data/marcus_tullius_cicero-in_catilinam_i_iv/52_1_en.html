In Catilinam

The Fourth Speech Against Lucius Sergius Catilina Delivered in the Senate

I see, gentlemen, that the faces and eyes of you all 1 are turned upon me; I see that you are concerned not only at your own danger and that of the Republic but also, if that is averted, at my own. Your goodwill towards me comforts me in my troubles and gratifies me in my anxiety, but in the name of the immortal gods lay it aside, forget my safety, and think of yourselves and your children. For myself, if these were the terms on which I was given the consulship, that I should endure all the suffering, all the anguish and all the afflictions, I shall bear them bravely and even gladly, provided that my efforts secure for you and for the Roman people your authority and your salvation.
