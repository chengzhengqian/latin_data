Have no doubt, 29 citizens, that I shall ensure that upon my return to private life I shall uphold and consolidate the work of my consulship and see that any hatred incurred in saving the Republic injures those who hate me and redounds to my own glory. In short, my policy will be to remain true to the memory of my achievements and ensure that men realize that they were the work of meritorious service and not of chance.

Do you, citizens, since it is now night, offer up your prayers to Jupiter, the guardian of this city and of yourselves, and then depart to your homes and defend them, although the danger is now averted, as you did last night with your pickets and sentries. I shall see to it that you do not have to do this for too long and that you can dwell, citizens, in peace for evermore.
