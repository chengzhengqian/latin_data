No violence of flame or swelling wind, no fearful violence of a whirling spear, matches a wife bereft of her marriage, burning and hating:

not when the south wind carries the mists and rains of winter, and the Hister rushes in spate and forbids the joining of bridges and strays from its course;

not when the Rhone drives into the deep, or when, with its snows melting into streams under the strength of the midspring sun, Mt. Haemus thaws.

Blind is the fire whipped up by anger, careless of control, impatient of curbs, fearless of death, longing to attack straight against swords.

Mercy, you gods! We pray for pardon; he who tamed the sea, let him live in safety. But the lord of the deep rages at the conquest of the second realm.
