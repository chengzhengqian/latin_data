Fortune can take away my wealth, but not my spirit. But who pounds the palace doors, creaking on their hinges? It is himself, swollen with Pelasgian power: Creon.

creon

Medea, that noxious child of Colchian Aeetes—is she not yet withdrawing from my realm? She is contriving something: her cunning is well known, so is her handiwork. Whom will she spare, whom will she leave untroubled? For my part I was planning to eliminate this evil infection quickly with the sword, but my son-in-law’s prayers prevailed. She has been granted life: let her free the land of fear and depart in safety.

She advances fiercely against me, and with a threatening air seeks closer speech. Keep her away, servants, well away from contact or access; bid her be silent. She must finally learn to endure a king’s authority. (to Medea) Depart with haste, and remove at long last a savage and fearful horror!
