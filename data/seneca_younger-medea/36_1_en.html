MEDEA

I shall strike my arms with the holy knife. My blood must flow onto the altar. Get used, my hands, to drawing steel and enduring the feel of your own dear blood. With a blow I have offered the flowing sacrament. But if you protest at too frequent a summons from my entreaties, forgive me, I pray: my reason for summoning your arc too often, daughter of Perses, is always one and the same: Jason. ​ (to Nurse) You must now tincture the clothes for Creusa, so the moment she wears them, crawling flame may burn its way deep into her bones. Enclosed and lurking in the tawny gold is shrouded fire: the one who pays for his theft from heaven with the tissue that grows in him gave it me and taught me to store its power by craft: Prometheus. Mulciber too gave me fire concealed in fine-grained sulfur, and I gathered the flashes of living flame from the kindred body of Phaethon. I possess gifts from the belly of Chimera,42 I possess flames caught from the scorched throat of the bull,

  • 42Literally, from her “middle”: of her three parts (lion’s front, goat’s middle, serpent’s tail), the midmost was thought to produce fire.
