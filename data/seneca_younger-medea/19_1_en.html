Did he fear Creon, and war with the Thessalian leader? True love is incapable of fearing anyone. But grant that he was forced to give way and surrender: surely he could have approached his wife and comforted her in a last conversation—yet even this the fierce hero feared to do. Surely he had scope as son-in-law to postpone the moment of bitter exile; yet for two children a single day is granted. I do not complain about the short time: it will go far. Today—today will achieve what no tomorrow will fail to speak of. I shall attack the gods, and shake the world.
