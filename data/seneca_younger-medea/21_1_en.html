MEDEA

Symplegades? Should I head for little Iolcos, or Thessalian Tempe? Each path I opened for you, I closed for myself. Where are you sending me back? You order the exile into exile, but do not provide it.

But go I must; the royal son-in-law has ordered it; I make no protest. Heap dreadful punishments on me: I have deserved them. Let the king’s anger crush your mistress with bloody tortures, burden my hands with chains and bury me in a stony prison of unending night: my sufferings will be less than I deserve. Ungrateful creature! Wind your thoughts back to the fiery breath of the bull, the flame-darting beast of Aeetes encountered, amid the savage threats of that untamed nation, on the field that bore arms, and the spears of the sudden foe, when at my bidding the earthborn soldiers fell in mutual slaughter.23 Think too of the long-sought spoil from Phrixus’ ram, of the unsleeping monster bidden to close his eyes in unfamiliar sleep, of the brother put to death (a single crime involving many acts of crime), of the daughters that dared through my trickery to dismember a father who would never revive.24 By your hopes in your children, by your well-established house, by the monsters I overcame, by these hands which I never spared in your cause, by our bygone fears, by sky and sea, witnesses of my marriage: pity me; in your good fortune make recompense to your suppliant.
