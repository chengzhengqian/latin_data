The Father Saved by his Son

One who has been saved shall have no right of action against his saver.1

A man who has been saved by his son disinherits him. The son brings an objection.2

For the father: Would that I had been allowed to die, if I am not allowed to speak.—Do you think I was saved? I was taken captive.—Send me back to the enemy—prisoners are allowed to speak.—Since he boasts so loudly of the value of life, hear who was the first of us to bestow it.3—Anyone who gives me back to the enemy I shall call my saviour.—Do you regard disinheriting as an “action”?4 Even if it is, the law when it speaks of people saved refers only to non-relations; it applies to father and son no more than to master and slave, freedman and patron.—Leave the power exercised by a father and come to a
