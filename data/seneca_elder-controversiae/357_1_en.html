Misfortune dooms itself, and quite the worst thing about the human lot is that those whom Fortune has made miserable it makes superstitious as well. The gods are worshipped with more care when they show anger.—Who then did the deed? How can I know? I was looking after my mother.—I am pleading in the name of the laws, for fear they may bring to bear on the wretched the executioner with which they threaten the sacrilegious.—She worshipped the gods, as you expect of one who had so many reasons4 to fear them.
