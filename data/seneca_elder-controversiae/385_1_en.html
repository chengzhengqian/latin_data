Pompeius Silo. It would have been shameful for a whore to make a fine nobleman—one who had held such high office—lenient: she made him cruel.—“I have never seen . . .” Go on to say, please: “and I shan’t be able to see it when someone else is praetor.”

Albucius Silus. But if anyone, judges, wants me6 to tell of the praetor’s cruelty, how many besides this man he slaughtered, how many innocent men he condemned, how many he shut up in prison, I promise that I shall satisfy such an enquirer: in one party I shall unroll the story of the accused and his praetor-ship.—In his province he organises a banquet. The feast is lavishly arranged with splendid appointments; silver cups are set off by gold ones. What more need I say, judges? The province felt the preparation of that banquet, to its cost.—A man is hauled from the prison and taken to the praetor’s party; the whore smiles on the poor dazed wretch. Rods are produced, and the victim of sadism is slaughtered before the table and before the gods. Alas, you have made a game out of the terror inspired by the empire of Rome. In sadistic practices you have surpassed7 every tyrant!
