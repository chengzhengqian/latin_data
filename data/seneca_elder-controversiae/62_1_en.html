Arellius Fuscus Senior. Let rivers reverse their course,2 let the sun trace his orbit in the opposite direction, let the sacrilegious flee to the altar: a girl who has been ravished is trying to save her ravisher.

The other side

Pompeius Silo. The day after, when he had his3 error of the night before explained to him—he thought he’d only encountered one girl—he lowered his hands in supplication to this girl first; she was the first he implored and won over—hence, I suppose, the other’s greater anger with her ravisher.—One of the girls wants the ravisher to die, the other wants him saved; one judge condemns the defendant, the other acquits him. The votes are equal—let the gentler prevail.3—I shall say what I feel: she would be more angry if he’d only raped one; she’d say: “So am I the only one he thought it worth wronging?”
