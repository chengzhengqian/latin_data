I ask you: wouldn’t you imagine a child had done it—and not even a five-year-old?”

Vibius Gallus produced an outrageous epigram14 when describing the murder: “He killed the father, he wounded the step-mother, he spared the boy: he already thought of the boy as his own.”2 Cestius, indeed, said the boy should be spared at all costs; this is why, when praising his evidence, he said: “You were born while I was agent.”3 Hermagoras put this idea more appropriately: “According to his brother—or not?”4

Blandus’ epigram was praised; when he had described the child pointing out the agent, he said: “What an informative finger!”
