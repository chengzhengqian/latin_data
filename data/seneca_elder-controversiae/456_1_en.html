Albucius said nothing against the rich man. His11 declamation had the following colour: “‘To accuse someone without having prosecuted him is to commit an injury. Why do you follow me?’ he says. So that you should at last take pity on me, should cease to persecute a prostrate household, should realise that I cannot in this plight accuse you, should covet the glory of avenging a death. You alone, if you will, can find the man who killed him, you alone can accuse him. ‘But some people regard me as suspect because of this.’ You can dispel that suspicion: look for the man responsible.’ If you want to see that you are causing me unpopularity, remember that when I said: Accuse me, you didn’t say you wouldn’t, but instead you replied: I will accuse when I can.’ Forgive me, I can no more accuse anyone yet than acquit him: I am looking for the man responsible. Mine are feeble proofs—but the ones that weigh against other people are empty. You are my enemy, he was found unrobbed; I have here no reason to accuse you—but I do have reason to suspect.”

Vibius Rufus used this as a colour: “I am in12 mourning—I grieve. I follow you so as to be safer. I am afraid of whoever it was who killed my father; I know that I cannot perish so long as I am with you.”

Following this colour, Murredius said, very foolishly:1 “Why do I follow you? My father was killed because he walked the streets alone.”
