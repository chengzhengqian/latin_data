Fuscus said: “Son, I beg you, show me that I have11 not lost my hands so long as you are whole. The adulterer disputes you with me; come and show which of us is your father.”

Publius Vinicius said nicely, and also originally (usually he stole everyone else’s witty sayings): “I burst into the adulterers’ bedroom. Why should I lie, alas? The room was unlocked—they were waiting for me.”3

Cestius said: “I called my son. The adulterer laughed, as though to say: He’s mine.”
