The Tyrannicide the Pirates Let Go

Children must support their parents, or be imprisoned.3

A man killed one of his brothers, a tyrant.4 The other brother he caught in adultery and killed despite the pleas of his father. Captured by pirates, he wrote to his father about a ransom. The father wrote a letter to the pirates, saying that he would give double if they cut off his hands. The pirates let him go. The father is in need; the son is not supporting him.5

For the son

Porcius Latro. Give me the letter of this hungry1 father. He says: “Let his hands be cut off” May
