Gavius Silo. Whom do you regard as the victor? I have merely my prize, you have both the prize and a hero.2

Division

**’s division was as follows: Can a son be disinhertited8 for something he did in accordance with the law? “No-one, in one and the same matter, has the law on his side and also fears the law.” On the other side he said: “If he does something that is not allowed, the law will punish him; if he does something that is allowed but that should not be done, his father will punish him. The enquiry concerns not a sin done by the son, but his duties.” Then: “Let each make use of the law that favours him. You were allowed that, I am allowed this. ‘One may disinherit children.’ And there is a law that puts the father above the son.”3 If he can be disinherited even for a legal action, can he be disinherited even for an action for which he has received a prize? “He cannot be punished privately for an action that brings him public honour.
