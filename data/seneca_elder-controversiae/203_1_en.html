It is not easy to kill a man; meanwhile I suppress my groans, and drive within the tears that burst forth.—I know what will happen. You have been deceived by my countenance.3 Fool, do you suppose that anyone would be prepared to delay his daughter’s marriage?4 You are running on to the axe, summoning the executioner of your own accord.—You should be imploring—yet you insult.—No-one can be forced to avenge himself.

The other side: Nothing is more wretched than to be left uncertain whether one will live or die. By now, it will be a boon even if he chooses death.—I fell in love with the girl. You say I should have asked her father for her hand. I did, but you see how slow he is even where the law insists that he should choose.—A ravisher’s life is in another’s power, but his freedom is in his own.5—That law has something for both of us.
