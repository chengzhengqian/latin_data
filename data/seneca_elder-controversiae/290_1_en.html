Popillius, Killer of Cicero

An action may lie for misconduct.1

Cicero defended Popillius on a charge of parricide; he was acquitted. When Cicero was proscribed, Popillius was sent by Antony to kill him, and he brought back his head to Antony. He is accused of misconduct.2

Against Popillius
