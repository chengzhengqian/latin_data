The Cut Noose

An action may lie for an offence not specified in the law.1

A man who had been shipwrecked, and had lost three children and his wife in a fire at his house, hung himself. A passer-by cut the noose. He is accused of an offence by the man he saved.

For the passer-by: “I have lost three children.” Would that I could have saved them too!—Live: human happiness has its vicissitudes: the proscribed have sometimes themselves proscribed.2—The defeated fly, the proscribed lie hidden, the shipwrecked swim to shore.—“I have lost my wife, children, estate.” Do you think that you received them on terms that precluded your losing them?
