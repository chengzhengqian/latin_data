Controversiae 4

Book 4

Preface

Seneca to his sons Novatus, Seneca and Mela greetings

I am doing what gladiator-producers often do1 when, in order to maintain the suspense of the populace, they distribute new pairs over each day of the games, so that there is always something to please the spectators and bring them back—I am not bringing all my declaimers on at once; let a book always have something new, to keep you on your toes by means of the novelty of the speakers as well as of the epigrams.1 The desire to get to know the unknown is keener than the desire to go back to the known. We see this everywhere—in connection with actors, gladiators and orators, at least where reputation has promised something beforehand: men flock to the new, avoid the old. But I won’t keep you on tenter-hooks2 by bringing them on only one at a time; today I shall be liberal and open-handed.
