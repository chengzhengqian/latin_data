The Heroic Son who would not Yield to his Heroic Father

A hero may choose the reward he wishes; if there are more heroes than one, they must dispute the point at law.1

A father and his son acted heroically. The father asked the son to give way to him. He was not prepared to. He disputed the point at law and defeated his father. As his reward he asked for statues to be erected to his father. His father disinherits him.2

For the son

Junius Gallio.
