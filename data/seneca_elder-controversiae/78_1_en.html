Cornelius Hispanus. I don’t know what my father begged me:3 I plunged a sword bloody with a public punishment into a private tyrant.—When I was captured, the pirates merely bound me.—“I shall give twice the money.” What? You promise more than is asked? Where do you get such vast hoards of wealth? Can it be that you speak as though you still controlled a tyrant’s coffers?4—My brother violated my wife—whom even the tyrant had not violated.—When the pirates fixed the price, I rejoiced: “How rich my father is!5 He could have given it even if they’d asked double.”—They sent me back to the commonwealth with my hands—and to my father with his letter.

Pompeius Silo. I made a bargain about the5 ransom, and wrote to my father. I am sure that all those of you who were in suspense for your tyrant-killer prayed anxiously that the letter should reach my father.—“I am in need,” he said. So suddenly?—What he shouldn’t buy at all, he is ready to buy at twice the cost.
