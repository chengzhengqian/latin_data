The Man Slandered in Connection with His Daughter-in-law

The father of two youths provided one of them with a wife. The son went abroad, and the father began to be slandered in connection with his daughter-in-law. The husband returned, took one of his wife’s servants and put her to the torture, under which she died. The husband, without revealing what he had been looking for, hung himself. The father orders the second son to marry the same woman; he refuses, and is disinherited.

For the son: “Marry your brother’s wife.” If that is possible, my brother has found the adulterer he was looking for.1—The reason2 for my disinheritance is the reason for my brother’s death.—“Marry your brother’s wife.” If you will believe me, I thought I was being put to the test.—Woman, if you are capable of marrying a man weighed down by grief, you make it possible to believe anything of you.—I am forced
