The other side: He2 burst insultingly into the house—as though it belonged to an exile.—He went to my father: he did not spare his shame.3—But my complaint was that he did him an injury while he was away.—I cannot be disinherited for doing something legal.4—You could not, father, be the judge of an injury that you did not know about.5—We can be made to write much that we do not want to.—Fortune had made you vulnerable to injury; he was a man capable of doing an injury—even in his own country.—Because he knew his case was weak, he pleaded it before someone who was ignorant of the facts.—He returned all high and mighty, told us to choose marriage, tried to force us to. It looked as if he had used similar force over there,6 too.—However, he got one thing out of the letter: no-one ever took longer to die.7—He collected a gang of desperadoes, stormed the house, violated the girl. This is what the ravisher had not told you.
