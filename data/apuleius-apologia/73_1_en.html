65. I have said enough about the name for the present. Beyond that, I too am aware that certain bystanders wish to hear why I especially wanted the statuette made of wood, and not of silver or gold, 2and as I believe, they desire it less to excuse than to understand it, 3and to be relieved of this further uncertainty by seeing every suggestion of criminality completely refuted. 4So anyone who cares to understand may listen, but must listen with a mind as alert and attentive as possible, since you are about to hear the very words of the aged Plato from the last book of the Laws: 5“Dedications to the gods that the moderate man presents to them should be moderate.
