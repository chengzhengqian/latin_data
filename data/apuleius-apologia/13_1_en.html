You once shone among the living as the eastern Star; Now dead, you shine as the Evening Star among the shades.

9And again the lines of Plato uniting the boys Alexis and Phaedrus in one poem:26

Alexis I only said was fair, And now he’s looked at everywhere, For everyone to see. My heart, you’ll rue it if you show Bones to dogs. Was it not so Phaedrus was lost to me?

10Not to recall more lines, I will quote one last one by him about Dion of Syracuse before I finish:

Ah, Dion, who maddened my heart with love.

11. But am I not a fool to mention even these things in court? Or rather are you not a slanderer to include even these things in your charge, as if trifling in verse was any index of character! 2Have you never read how Catullus made this reply to malicious critics,27

“A virtuous poet should be chaste Himself: his poems needn’t be”?

3When the deified Hadrian adorned his friend Voconius’ tomb with a poem, here is what he wrote:

Playful in verse, but pure in heart were you28
