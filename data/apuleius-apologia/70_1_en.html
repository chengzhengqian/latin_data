4You say that the statue was made in secret, but how can that be? So far are you from not knowing who made it that you demanded his presence in court. 5Here indeed is the maker, Cornelius Saturninus, a man whom his acquaintances both praise for his skill and commend for his character. You questioned him closely not long ago, Maximus, and he went through the whole history of the affair with the utmost reliability and honesty. 6I had seen many geometrical figures in his shop, precisely and ingeniously made of boxwood. His skill was an inducement to ask him to make me certain mechanical devices, and at the same time to carve an image of any god he wished for me to worship in my usual way, using any material provided it was of wood. 7At first, therefore, he tried one in boxwood. In the meanwhile, I was in the country when my stepson Sicinius Pontianus, who wanted to do me a kindness, begged some ebony boxes from that excellent woman Capitolina, brought them to him, and urged him to make it from that wood instead, being more rare and more durable; that would make a particularly welcome present. 8Saturninus followed his instructions as best he could with the boxes available, and by fitting together bits and pieces of the panels, he was able to turn out a small Mercury in the round.
