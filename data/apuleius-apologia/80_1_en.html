72. So matters stood between the mother’s marital prospects and her son’s fears, when by chance or by destiny I arrived on my way to Alexandria. So help me, I would have said, “if only that had never happened,” if consideration for my wife did not restrain me. 2It was the winter season. I had arrived after an exhausting journey, and was laid up for a good number of days at the house of my friends here, the Appii, whom I name to show my esteem and affection. 3There Pontianus came to see me, since not long before he had made my acquaintance at Athens through certain mutual friends, and thereafter we had shared lodgings together and become closely attached. 4In all he did he showed deference to my position, concern for my health, shrewdness with regard to my affections; for, you see, he thought he had found the perfect husband for his mother, one in whose hands he could without any risk place the family’s whole property. 5At first, indeed, he tested my inclination by speaking indirectly,
