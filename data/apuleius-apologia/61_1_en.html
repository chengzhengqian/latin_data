Indeed, I give you leave to imagine, invent, suppose anything that might seem to be magical, and even so I would dispute what you said about it. 2I would say either that it was a replacement, or that I had been given it as a medicine, or received it in a rite, or commanded in a dream. There are countless other charges that common practice and the most ordinary of everyday customs would allow me to refute with truth. 3As it is, this is something that would not harm me before an upright judge, even were it detected and proved, and yet you expect it to incriminate me when it is an empty suspicion, vague and unverified.
