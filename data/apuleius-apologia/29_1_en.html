The philosopher Anacharsis was born among the doltish Scythians, the buffoon Meletides among the clever Athenians.57 7I do not say this because I am ashamed of my native place, even were it still the home town of Syphax. 8Still, after his defeat we passed by gift of the Roman people to king Massinissa, and thereafter, newly refounded with veteran soldiers, we became a most distinguished colony, in which colony my father had the position of mayor in the emperor’s place, when he had held every office.58 9I have maintained his position in that city from when I first began to be a member of the city council, not at all unworthily of him and, I hope, with equal honor and repute. 10Why then have I brought all this up?
