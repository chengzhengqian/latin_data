73. By pressing all these points so insistently he convinced me, and entrusted his mother and the boy here, his brother, to me. I helped them considerably in our joint studies, and our intimacy greatly increased. 2Meanwhile I recovered, and gave a public speech at the request of my friends. The audience filled the town hall, where the lecture took place, in large numbers, and among other compliments gave a unanimous shout of “Bravo,” begging me to stay and to become a citizen of Oea.130 3Later, when the audience had left, Pontianus made this his cue to influence me, interpreting the unanimous public opinion as a sign from heaven, and revealed that he had a scheme, if I had no objection, to marry me to his mother, for whom many men were lying in wait; since, so he said, I was the only person whom he could trust and believe in every respect. 4If I did not take on this burden, since the prospect was not a pretty young ward but a homely mother of children—if with that consideration in mind I was readying myself
