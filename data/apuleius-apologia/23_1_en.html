Or is that you think Claudius Maximus the right person to hear poverty mocked, just because he himself is blessed with a rich and plentiful fortune? 2You are wrong, Aemilianus, you are entirely mistaken about his views if you judge him by the blessings of fortune and not by the standards of philosophy, and if you do not suppose that a man of such strict beliefs and such long military service is more inclined to disciplined moderation than to pampered opulence, and that he esteems wealth as he does a tunic, for its propriety not for its excess. 3Wealth too, if it is trailed rather than worn, impedes and trips one up no less than a garment hanging down in front.49
