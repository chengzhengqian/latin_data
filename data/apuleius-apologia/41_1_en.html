5And yet maybe you thought you had found a very clever ruse—alleging that I had tried to get these two sea creatures, a cockle and a cunner, for their magical powers of attraction80 (for let me tell you the creatures’ Latin names, which I have mentioned separately, so that you have learned something before you accuse me again). 6But remember, it would be just as absurd to argue that obscenely-named sea creatures are desired for sexual purposes as to say that one wanted a comb fish to comb one’s hair, a hawk fish to catch birds, a boar fish to hunt boar, sea skulls to raise the dead.81 7My reply to this passage of your speech, a fabrication as tasteless as it is absurd, is that I did not try to get this sea trash or shore refuse either for a price or for nothing.
