85. But why do I deplore the past, when the present is just as painful? To think that you have corrupted this poor boy so much that he reads out his mother’s letter, thinking it a love letter, 2doing so before the proconsul’s bench and before the most honorable Claudius Maximus—that beneath these statues of the emperor Pius he charges his mother with shameful sexual exploits, and reproaches her for having affairs.144 3Who is so forgiving as not to feel fury? You monster, do you pry into your mother’s thoughts in these matters, watch her eyes, count her sighs, investigate her feelings, intercept her papers, condemn her love? 4Do you investigate what she does in her bedroom? In your eyes is your mother, I will not say “oversexed,”145 not even a woman? Do you only think of her as owing the duty of a mother? 5How unlucky was your womb, Pudentilla!
