Once relieved of that concern, and because men of the upper class were eager to marry her, she determined to remain a widow no longer, for though she could endure the tedium of loneliness, she could not bear her physical illness. 2She was a woman of impeccable chastity, so many years a widow without fault or rumor, but she was lethargic from lack of conjugal intercourse and ill from her womb’s long inactivity, so that her inner uterus became infected, and the resulting pains often exhausted her till her life was in danger.127 3The doctors and the midwives agreed that she had contracted her illness from deprivation of conjugal life, that the danger was increasing with every day and her pains getting more acute; while life was still left to her, she should counteract her illness by marriage. 4Everyone approved the plan and especially Aemilianus
