Well, would you then deny that Solon was a respectable citizen and a philosopher, even though he wrote that very risqué line:

“Of lovely thighs and honeyed mouth”?18

10Do all the lines I have written include anything so naughty as that one by itself? 11Not to mention the writings of Diogenes the Cynic and Zeno, the founder of the Stoic school, many of which are like that. I will read mine out again so that those people know that I am not ashamed of them.19

12Critias is my beloved, yet for you, Dearest Charinus, half my love’s reserved. Fear not: this double fire may burn me as it will, I will endure these two flames just to win you.
