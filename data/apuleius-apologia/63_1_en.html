I could indeed deny that I had put any napkin in Pontianus’ book chest, 4and even if I were fully to concede that I had, I would could well say that I had nothing wrapped in it 5(and if I did say so, no testimony or argument could refute me, since no one touched it and, so you claim, just one freedman saw it); 6even so, I repeat, for all I care it was chock full. Yes, imagine that, if you want, as once Ulysses’ companions imagined they had found a treasure when in fact they were rifling through a bag full of wind.108 7Do you want me to tell you what sort of things wrapped in a napkin I entrusted to Pontianus’ house shrine? I will oblige you.

8I have been initiated into many mysteries in Greece, and the priests entrusted me with certain symbols and tokens of them, which I store carefully. What I say is nothing strange or secret.
