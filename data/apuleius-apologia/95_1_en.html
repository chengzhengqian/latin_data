Though a son, you read what you call your mother’s love letters in this assembly, and yet if you were told to read some rather risqué poet before it, you would certainly not dare: some sense of decency would restrain you even yet. 3Or rather, you would never have read your mother’s letters if you had had any other reading.147

4But how wicked it was of you to give your very own letter to be read! How very disrespectful, insulting and shameful was that letter you wrote about your own mother! She was still raising you under her protection when you secretly sent it to Pontianus, no doubt so that you would not do wrong only once, or let such a kind deed be buried in oblivion. 5Poor fool, don’t you see that your uncle allowed all this to happen—to excuse himself to the world when it became known from your letter that even before you moved in with him, even while you were fawning on your mother, you were already deceitful and unfilial.
