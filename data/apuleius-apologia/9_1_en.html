That colossal monster, the crocodile born in the Nile—he too, I find, offers his teeth to be cleaned by harmlessly opening his jaws. 7Since his mouth is huge but tongueless, and is usually open under water, many leeches get caught in his teeth; so that when he emerges and opens his mouth on the riverbank, a friendly waterbird pokes its beak in and cleans it with no risk of harm to itself.16
