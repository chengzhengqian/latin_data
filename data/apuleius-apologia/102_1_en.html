5And yet is there anyone, even someone with only slight experience of life, who would dare to find fault if a widowed woman, of middling looks but not middling age, and inclined to marry, had attracted a grown man of no slight attractiveness, character, or fortune, thanks to her ample dowry and easy terms? 6A beautiful virgin may be very poor, but still is amply endowed, since she brings to her new husband the cast of her thoughts, the charm of her beauty, the freshness of her bloom. Virginity is the one thing that cannot be returned once it has been received; of all the dowry goods, it alone remains with the husband. 8A widow, however, is the same woman entering marriage and leaving it by divorce.
