Ex Ponto, III

79

Nor am I unworthy, and if thou art willing to confess the truth, some return is owed to my services. That return thou dost indeed make to me with usury, nor could rumour, even if she should wish, injure thee. But none the less add this one thing to thy previous deeds: be the canvasser for my misfortunes. Toil that I may rest in a less hostile region and no part of thy duty will halt. Great is my request, yet not one that brings odium on the petitioner; shouldst thou not attain it, thy defeat involves no danger. And be not wroth with me if so many times in my song I ask thee to do what thou art already doing and to imitate thyself. The brave have often been helped by the trumpeter, and the general urges on with his own lips men who are fighting well. Thy probity is known and witnessed for all time; let thy courage too be not inferior to thy probity. Thou hast not to take up in my behalf the Amazon’s battle-axe nor bear with thy frail hand the indented1 target. Thou has to implore a deity, not to become friendly to me, but less angry than heretofore. If grace thou findest not, tears shall win thee grace; by this or by no means canst thou move the gods. That they will not fail thee is well assured by my misfortunes; with me as husband of tears thou hast rich store; and as things are with me thou wilt weep, I think, at all times—these are the means that my fortune renders to thee. If thou hadst to redeem my death at the price of thine own—away with the thought!—Admetus’ wife2 would be a model to follow.
