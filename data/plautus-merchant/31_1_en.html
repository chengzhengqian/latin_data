Mercator

pas

So old, my dear old man?

lys

It’s of the Greek19 type; if you look after it, it’s very good, it can be fleeced really nicely.

pas

In order to show you my appreciation I’ll be grateful for anything I’m given.

lys

Now, my woman, don’t be mistaken, you aren’t mine, so don’t think you are.

pas

Then tell me please, whose am I?

lys

You’ve been bought back again for your master; I’ve530 bought you back, he asked me to.

pas

Life has returned to me, if good faith is kept with me.

lys

Take heart, he’ll free you: he’s madly in love, and yet he’s only seen you today.

pas

Heavens! It’s already two years since he began to be involved with me. I’ll tell you now because I know you’re his friend.

lys

What do you say? He’s already been involved with you for535 two years?

pas

Certainly; and we swore an oath together, I with him and he with me: neither of us would rub heads with another20 for unchastity, I with a man and he with a woman, except I with him and he with me.

lys

Immortal gods! He shouldn’t even lie with his wife?

pas

Please, is he married? He isn’t and he won’t be.

lys

I wish he weren’t. He’s perjured himself.

pas

There’s no young man I love more.540

lys

He’s actually a little boy, silly: he lost his teeth not long ago.

  • 19Pliny (Nat. Hist. 8.190) also praises the breed of sheep called “Greek.” There is a pun here: Demipho is Greek.
  • 20I.e., kiss someone else.
