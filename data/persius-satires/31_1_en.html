Do a deal, swear on it.” “But God will hear.” “For heaven’s sake, you fool: if life on good terms with God is your aim, you will have to be content with scraping away with your finger at the saltcellar so often tasted.” Now, ready for action, you are saddling the slaves with baggage and bottle. Quick, all aboard! There’s nothing stopping you from racing over the Aegean in your enormous bark, unless sly Luxury takes you to one side first to lecture you: “And where are you rushing off to now, you lunatic, where? What are you up to? Even a jug of hemlock couldn’t quench the macho bile which has swollen beneath your fevered breast. You?—Leaping over the sea? You?—Having your dinner on the rowers’ bench, reclining on a coiled rope, with a pot-bellied mug reeking of reddish Veientan, 33 spoiled by stale pitch? What for? Do you want the coins which you’ve nurtured here at a modest five per cent 34 to go on to sweat out a greedy eleven? Enjoy yourself, 35 let’s grab our pleasures, life is all we have, you’ll soon be ashes, a ghost, a tale. Live with death in mind. The hour races by and the time I spend talking is subtracted from it.” So what do you do? You are torn apart by a double hook. Do you pursue this, or that?
