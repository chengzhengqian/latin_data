“And so, Antonius, I grant both your points, first the great and frequent utility of witticisms in oratory, secondly the absolute impossibility of learning these from art. One thing certainly surprises me, and that is your ascribing so much success in this sphere to myself, instead of awarding the prize for this, as for all else, to Crassus.”

228

“I should certainly have done so,” answered Antonius, “were I not now and then a little envious of Crassus in this connexion; for merely to be as witty and shrewd as you please need not excite unmeasured envy, but that the most attractive and polished of all speakers should at the same time be obviously the most impressive and austere, as has been the lot of our friend alone,—this did seem rather more than I could bear.”

Even Crassus smiled at this, and Antonius went on, “However, Julius, though you denied the existence of any art of pleasantry, you did just start something that seemed worth teaching.
