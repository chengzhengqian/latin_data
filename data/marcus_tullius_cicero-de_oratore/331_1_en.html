“I have given the best exposition I could, not as I End of the debate. should have liked but as compelled by the limited time available; for it is a wise plan to cut one’s coat according to one’s cloth, when one cannot add more even if one wishes.”

“O but as for you,” said Catulus, “so far as I can judge, you have gathered together the whole of the points with such genius, that you appear not to have learnt from the Greeks but to be competent to instruct the Greeks themselves in these subjects. For myself at all events it is a great satisfaction to have been admitted to share in your discourse; and I only wish my son-in-law, your friend Hortensius, had been here. He, I am convinced, will come to the top in all the accomplishments that you have included in oratory.”
