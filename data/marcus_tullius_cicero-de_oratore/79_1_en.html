On these questions I admit that the teaching is abundant and manifold, and the theories numerous, copious and varied; but we, Crassus, are looking for something 223different, and widely different. We require a man of sharpness, ingenious by nature and experience alike, who with keen scent will track down the thoughts, feelings, beliefs and hopes of his fellow-citizens and of any men whom on any issue he would fain win over by his word.
