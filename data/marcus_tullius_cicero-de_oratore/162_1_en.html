XLII. “I am running over these things in a hurry, Favour of audience must be secured. and like a half-trained man who is facing experts, in order that we may come at last to those more essential matters. Now nothing in oratory, Catulus, is more important than to win for the orator the favour of his hearer, and to have the latter so affected as to be swayed by something resembling a mental impulse or emotion, rather than by judgement or deliberation. For men decide far more problems by hate, or love, or lust, or rage, or sorrow, or joy, or hope, or fear, or illusion, or some other inward emotion, than by reality, or authority, or any legal standard, or judicial 179precedent, or statute. And so, unless you think differently, let us proceed to the things I spoke of.”
