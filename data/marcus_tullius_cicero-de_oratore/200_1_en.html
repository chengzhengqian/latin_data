“Then again there are those intentional understatements under statement, or overstatements which are exaggerated to a degree of the astonishing that passes belief, such as your own assertion, Crassus, made in a speech before a public assembly, that Memmius thought himself so exalted an individual that, on his way down into the Market Place, he lowered his head in order to pass under the Arch of Fabius.b To this category also belongs the taunt said to have been uttered by Scipio at Numantia, when he was in a rage with Gaius Metellus, that ‘if the mother of Metellus should bear a fifth time, she would be found to have borne an ass.’c
