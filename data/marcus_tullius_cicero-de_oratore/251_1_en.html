There is a single art and method of painting, and nevertheless there is an extreme dissimilarity between Zeuxis, Aglaophon and Apelles, while at the same time there is not one among them who can be thought to lack any factor in his art. And if this be surprising and nevertheless true in the case of what may be called the silent arts, how much more remarkable it is in oratory and in language! This art is occupied with the same supply of ideas and expressions, and yet it comprises extreme dissimilarities—not in the sense that some speakers deserve praise and others blame, but that the ones admittedly deserving of praise nevertheless achieve it in a variety of styles.
