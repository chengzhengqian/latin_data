XXIV. “At last,” said Catulus, “at last I take your meaning, Crassus; and I vow I agree with you. It is clear enough to me that you as an extremely keen and thorough student have had enough time to get to know the things that you are saying.”

“Do you persist in thinking,” said Crassus, “that what I am saying refers to myself and not to the fact of the matter? But now if you please let us go back to our subject.”

“Why certainly,” said Catulus.
