“There is a fairly long lecture for you from a person Conclusion: Crassus complimented and requested to speak on ornaments of style. Debate adjourned to afternoon. whom I hope you will not think conceited! Though not over-modest I am sure you must think me, for having discoursed at such length on the theory of rhetoric before an audience including not only you. Catulus, but also Lucius Crassus—for no doubt I was right in not troubling so much about hearers of the age of our friends here. But I am sure you will forgive me if only I explain to you the motive that has urged me on to a talkativeness for me unusual.”
