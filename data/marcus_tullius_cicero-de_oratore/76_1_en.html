Then too, if a man is capable in both ways, such as the originator of national policy who is also a good senator, he is not just for that reason an orator; nor did the accomplished orator, who happens also to be outstanding in public administration, attain that special knowledge through his fluency in speaking. There is a vast difference between these gifts, and far apart are they sundered; nor was it by any uniform theory and method that Marcus Cato, Publius Africanus, Quintus Metellus and Gaius Laelius, orators all, gave brilliance to their own style and to the reputation of their community.
