The things we say when exasperated are altogether more persuasive than those we say in our first attack, as greater quickness of device is shown in retort, and to retort is human. For we give the impression that we should have remained quiet, had we not been exasperated, just as, in that identical harangue, our friend here said scarcely anything we thought particularly witty, which was not said by way of retort to a challenge.
