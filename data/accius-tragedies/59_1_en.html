Plays

337

Nonius: ‘Enoda’ (unknot) means explain . . .—

But do you unknot what I ask of you And who you are unfold.

338–9

Nonius: ‘Censere’ means to believe, suppose . . .—

For that’s the hope which cheers a stranger lost In hardship—while belief that his sad plight Is hidden from his fellows.

340

Nonius: We are faulty in our diction when, in stating that we have been ‘in the forum,’ we say we were ‘apud’ or ‘ad forum,’ since ‘apud’ means near by . . .—

Hard by himself you stand.

341

Nonius: ‘Locus,’ birth, nobility, dignity . . .—

And from estate not slender, as you see.

342

Nonius: The accusative for the genitive . . .—

Ah! Wretched me! When I recall all this, Remember too those bygone days,

343–4

Nonius: ‘Vulgus’ . . . in the masculine . . .—

. . . a He routs the commoners, unlifes them all, . . .

Dashes the walls to pieces.

  • aThe fr. is very corrupt at the beginning and in the middle, and no restorations have been convincing.
