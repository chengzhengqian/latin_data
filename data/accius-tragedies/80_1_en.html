469 An effort to mollify Achilles?: a

Nonius: ‘Confidentia’ again means rashness, boldness . . .—

Bridle your wrath, stand up and face your passions, Restrain your self-assurance.

471 The ambassadors state their case; Neoptolemus should don a helmet?: b

Festus: ‘Taeniae.’ A Greek word which Verrius explains by saying that it is a woollen adornment for an honoured head . . .—

It’s better thus to decorate his head Than with the hallowed foliage and ribbons.
