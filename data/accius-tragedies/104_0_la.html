Accius

Prometheus

I have included under Prometheus two fragments, but whether the first one (lines 606–7) really belongs to Accius is doubtful. Cicero (Tusc. Disp., II, 10, 23) having made his own translation into verse of a long passage from Sophocles’ Trachiniae, goes on to give two more passages, the second being long, and informs us that in both it is Aeschylus’ Prometheus speaking (sc. in Prometheus Unbound). Now the first of these Aeschylean passages is quoted a little more fully by Varro (L.L., VII, 11) apparently as from Accius’ Philoctetes on Lemnos (see pp. 506–7), while one line and a half of Cicero’s second
