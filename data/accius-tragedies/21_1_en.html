Plays

120–1

He thinks of his child Eurysaces?:

Nonius: ‘Anxitudo’ . . .—

Where dwells anxiety, there lies sharp misgiving; There all a man’s destiny, all his reasoned plans Are in a deadlock.

122

He approves of Tecmessa’s act in removing Eurysaces out of his reach, and feels a trace of his frenzied wrath:

Nonius: ‘Subiti’ used for ‘sudden’ . . .—

Well done! But now what sudden malady Is this a fever has aroused in me?

123

He speaks to Eurysaces:

Macrobius, quoting Virgil: ‘Learn you, boy, bravery and true labour from me, fortune from others.’ Accius in The Award of the Arms—

Ajax . . . In valour be you like your father, In fortune be unlike him. a 124

The chorus? on Ajax:

Nonius: ‘Exerciti’ or ‘exercituis’ for ‘exercitus’ . . .—

In whom all the army rests its highest hopes Of safety.

  • a literally, ‘Be like your father’s valour, unlike his fortunes.’
