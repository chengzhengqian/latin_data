Achilles is overcome by grief:

Nonius: ‘Senium’ itself occurs thus . . .—

Achilles Death of a friend subdues me; that’s to me By far the keenest sadness. 305–6

The wonderful deeds of Achilles in battle:

Nonius: The accusative of the singular number put for the genitive plural . . .—

Messenger City wards from the fleet they bent their course; Nor then could any man, himself in arms, Gaze at the glow of glaring arms. a 307

Charisius: ‘Acris.’ Accius in The Battle at the Ships—

So fierce was the onrush.

308–9

Priscianus: The form ‘Iovis’ is also found in the nominative case . . . Accius in The Battle at the Ships—

Jove may burn the fir with gleaming glow.

310–11

Hector’s challenge:

Nonius: ‘Cernere’ again means to fight or strive . . .—

The foremost of the leading chiefs by name He challenged, if there should be any one Who might desire to strive with him in arms.
