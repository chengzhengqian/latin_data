Plays

64

Macrobius: ‘Fero’ and ‘tollo’ both have ‘tuli’ in the perfect. . . . But Accius, in Andromeda, inflects from a form ‘tulo’ . . .—

Unless your power could bring us any help, Then I should perish.

65

Phineus’ reply:

Nonius: ‘Pudet’ and ‘piget.’ . . .—

Phineus Nor can I find a way whereby to help you; To encourage—that disgusts me; not to aid— But that disgraces a me. 66–7

Cepheus to Phineus:

Nonius: ‘Segnitas’ for ‘segnitia’ . . .—

Cepheus For, that through dread or sloth you hesitate To give your help—is not for me to say. b 68–9

Exhortation to Cassiepeia or Andromeda:

Nonius: ‘Fors’ and ‘Fortuna’ differ in this: ‘fors’ is an occurrence of the moment, ‘Fortuna’ is the goddess herself . . .—

Woman, Many a man in his unrighteous heart In mischiefs mischief has for himself enlarged; Then has his crooked nature done more hurt Than chance or Lady Fortune.

  • aThis rendering reproduces the jingle of piget . . . pudet.
  • bCp. Ον., Met., V, 22; R., 562.
