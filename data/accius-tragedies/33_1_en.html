194–5

Thyestes bewails his fate:

Nonius: ‘Dignatus’ means held to be ‘dignus’ . . .—

Thyestes

Could I—could I so much as lay a hand On sway imperial o’er the Argives, be Held worthy of the house of Pelops? Where May I reveal myself? What shrine may I Approach? Whom may my mournful mouth address?
