Single combat with Hector:

Charisius: ‘Duo,’ accusative masculine plural . . .—

You would believe a Mars with Mars had joined In armed fight.

313–14

Achilles rejoices:

Nonius: ‘Amnis’ . . . in the feminine . . .—

Achilles Scamander’s sacred stream I overspread With brackish blood, and in the river deep Piled plenteous heaps of enemy carcasses. 315

He jeers at Hector: a

Nonius: ‘Terriculae’ . . . In a neuter form . . .—

Achilles And now where are your scare-babes? 316

Priam begs Achilles to return Hector’s body:

Nonius: ‘Fatiscuntur’ for ‘fatiscunt’ . . .—

Priam Still I will not faint to implore your protection.
