637–8

The Myrmidons lie idle:

Priscianus: ‘Seneo.’ This form is found in use. Accius in Telephus—

Chorus By now the hearts of the Thessalians droop And dither in sottish sleep.

Tereus

presents to the queen, sent Procne a robe on which she wove an account of her wrongs. Procne read the message, found her sister, slaughtered her own son Itys and served up his limbs as a delicacy for Tereus. Tereus pursued Procne and Philomela, but all three were changed into birds.

The model was, it seems, chiefly Sophocles. Scene: Daulis at the time of the triennial festival of Dionysus when the matrons haunted the mountains by night. Cf. R., 577 ff. In Cicero’s time this play was very famous on the stage: Cic, ad Att., XVI, 2, 3; XVI, 5, 1; Phil., I, 15, 36.
