Plays

500 All is ready for a race. Oenomaus to Pelops:

Nonius: ‘Usus’. . . in a feminine form . . .—

Oenomaus

And take this last enjoyment of the sun!

501–4 Oenomaus makes ready according to his custom:

Nonius: ‘Obscenum’ means also evil-spoken . . .—

Now go you all at once; with great dispatch Proclaim throughout the town that all its people, Who dwell near to the stronghold and the Alpheus, Must bring a solemn and propitious silence To attend propitious omens, and dispart Words of ill-boding import from their tongues.

505–6 Pelops has won the race. He addresses Oenomaus:

Festus: ‘Numero’ means very swiftly, very quickly . . .—

Pelops

To be your kinsman, not your murderer— That’s why I sought your daughter; in too full number Dread takes your heart by storm.
