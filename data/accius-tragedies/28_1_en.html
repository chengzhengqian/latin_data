161

Athamas thanks Mercury for the return of Phrixus and Helle:

Nonius: ‘Grave’ (weighty), solid and firm . . .—

Athamas

By thy benefits thou hast begotten weighty and grateful requital.

Atreus

model was probably Sophocles. Ribb., 447 ff. But two fragments point to Euripides. I. Lana, in A.A.T., XXIII, 1958–9, 293 ff.; P. J. Enk, in Eos, LII, 1, 1962, 105.

A scholiast: Thyestes lay with the wife of his brother Atreus and by her begat sons whom Atreus gave to Thyestes himself to eat. The sun, when he saw this villainy, turned back and fled. This is according to Accius a in his tragedies, and Ovid in his Metamorphoses.

From the prologue:

Servius (supplemented): Others relate as follows: that Sterope and Atlas had children Oenomaus and Maia; Oenomaus had a daughter Hippodamia, of whom Atreus was born; while Maia had a son Mercury, from whom sprang Arcadians; from them sprang Evander. Accius gives fuller details about this in Atreus.
