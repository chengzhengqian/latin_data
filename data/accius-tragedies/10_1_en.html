  • aThere is no point in Festus’ explanation (setius he takes to mean serius) unless setius = sequius is here used in its original sense of ‘later’ (cp. sequor). But setius normally means ‘worse’ or ‘otherwise.’ Did Verrius, Festus’ source, have before him a text where serius was misspelt setius?
