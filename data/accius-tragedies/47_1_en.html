Unplaced fragments:

266

Nonius: ‘Adtendere’ means ‘intendere’ . . .—

At the same time I bent an ear to him, So that I could hear more.

267–8

Nonius: ‘Adtendere’ . . .—

If ever I should be embarrassed, son, You must not strive, dear boy, to beg of me What it would be a wrong to grant to you.

269

Thanks to Jupiter?:

Nonius: ‘Largi’ for ‘largire’ . . .—

Let your bestowal be most plentiful, Kindly, and of a piece with kindness rendered.

270

Sloth condemned:

Nonius: ‘Tarditudo’ and ‘tardities’ for ‘tarditas’ . . .—

Many things men let go through tardiness And witlessness.

271

flocks:

Nonius: ‘Pecua’ and ‘pecuda’ are terms used by the old writers in the same way as ‘pecora.’ . . .—

They all about the fields were walling a in The flocks that were the spoil.
