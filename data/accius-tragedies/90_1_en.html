522–6

Opening of the play. Chorus to Ulysses on his landing on Lemnos:

Apuleius: Accius praised Ulysses in his Philoctetes, in the beginning of that tragedy—

Chorus

O man renowned, brought forth by a little land, master of a famous name and strong in honoured heart, to the Achaean fleets a supporter, b to the clans of Dardanus a stern punisher, son of Laertes;

He mentions the father last.

  • aPossibly to be identified with the play Amphitryo (see pp. 340 ff.); R., 557.
  • bI retain auctor. Ulysses was famed for his knowledge of seamanship and brought twelve ships to Troy. But classibus may mean ‘army,’ ‘hosts.’
