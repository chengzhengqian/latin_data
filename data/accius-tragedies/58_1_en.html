327–30

Nonius: ‘Extorris’ is a term used for one ‘extra terram’ or ‘extra terminos.’ Accius in Eurysaces—

Outlander now, out of my kingdom thrust, A wanderer over lands, on sea . . .

Nonius: ‘Anfractum’ . . . In the neuter . . . Accius in Eurysaces—

Back over deep pools of father Ocean will I see again the windings and turnings of the land. a

331–2

Nonius: ‘Fragescere,’ to be broken . . .—

Never will he be so savage That he’ll not break when he is made aware That this man by my help was not destroyed.

333–4

Nonius: ‘Squalor’ . . . Of the feminine gender . . .—

Ah! ye Immortal gods! Come you b and see A human shape, so eminent, unworthy Of outrage and of squalor!

  • aHardly ‘the windings of my father’s lands’; anfractum is, in classical Latin anfractus (gen. -ūs), a bending.
  • bOr read invisitatam (with a lacuna after humanam)—‘A human shape! How strange it is . . .’ If invisita is right here, it is the only example of a verb invisito.
