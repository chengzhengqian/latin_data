Plays

494 Oenomaus on the oracle that he must beware of a son-in-law:

Nonius: ‘Auguro.’ Accius in Oenomaus—

Oenomaus

And that is what by surmise I foretell.

495–6 Coming of Pelops?:

Nonius: ‘Iteris’ put for ‘itineris’ . . .—

(A) Here even now am I at hand. (B) Out with Your message; for I see that you do come After a long journey.

497 Pelops explains his ambition:

Nonius: ‘Itiner’ used for ‘iter’. . .—

Pelops

Marriage to seek at Pisa, and to you Direct my journey.

498 He describes his journey?:

Nonius: ‘Grummus’ is a word used for mound . . .—

Whatever hillock or sheer mountain-chain I had set foot on,

499 Pelops(?) is aghast as he sees the rotting heads:

Nonius: ‘Honestitudo’ for ‘honestas’. . .—

The fearful fealty of Europe’s chieftains a From foremost rank

  • aOr possibly ‘The bristling row of valiant heads of Europe’s chieftains . . . But principium may be right, and the fr. would then refer to Europa who was carried off by Zeus—‘Europa’s rough chastity, the first cause in the first place,’ whatever that may mean.
