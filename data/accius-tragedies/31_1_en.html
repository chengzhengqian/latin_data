179–80

Cicero also: ‘Many are the ambushes laid for the good’ (178) is a most true saying. But he states—

. . . To demand is foolishness What many men do envy, many covet, Unless you carry out the troublesome task With greatest diligence.

I could wish he had not said in another passage . . . ‘Let them hate’ (168) . . .

181–2

None but Thyestes may sit with Atreus at the feast:

Nonius: ‘Vesci’ means to eat . . .—

Let none take seat at table with the king For feasting’s sake, or eat of the same banquet As he.

183–5

The chorus hears thunder which accompanied the turning away of the sun in horror:

Nonius: ‘Sonere’ . . .—

Chorus But why perceived we suddenly heaven’s plains Grown stormy, rocking, roaring with grim thunder? 186

A messenger describes the dreadful preparations:

Messenger Maker of a feast. Outblotter of a brother’s crimes.
