Plays

440 He tells Althaea’s brothers what manliness is?:

Nonius: ‘Facul’ for ‘faciliter’. . .—

That was a manly thing—to bear bad fortune easily.

441–2 Bloody battle between Meleager and his uncles Plexippus and Toxeus:

Nonius: ‘Eliminare,’ to cast outside the ‘limen,’ threshold . . .—

Althaea

Affrightedly I put myself outdoors Out of the clamour once the voice I knew Did hit upon my ears.

443 She is gripped by a Fury; she will slay her son:

Nonius: ‘Fervit’ for ‘fervet’. . .—

Oh! My blind senses seethe with anger! By madness am I borne and hurried on!

444–5 She remembers the prophecy a of the Fates about Meleager:

Nonius: ‘Torrus’ is a term for torch . . .—

that, when the flaming torch Should die out—that alone would be the end Of life and destiny for Meleager, His slaughter.

446–7 She resolves to put the brand into afire and so destroy Meleager:
