Alcmeo

This play has been thought to be identical with Alphesiboea, but apart from the fact that Nonius quotes from both as separate plays, there were separate myths concerning Alphesiboea (see notice to that play, pp. 336–337 and R., 497 ff.). G. Krókowski, in Tragica II, Trav. de la Soc. des Sci. et des Lettres de Wroclaw, Sér. A., LIV, thinks Accius’ model was Euripides’ Ἀλκμαίων διὰ Κορίνθου. I follow the story told in Apollodorus, III, 7, 5:—

Alcmaeon, made mad after the murder of his mother Eriphyle, went to Arcadia, first of all to king Oïcles or Oicleus.
