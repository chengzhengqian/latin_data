541 Ulysses asks where Philoctetes may be found:

Nonius: ‘Habere’ (have, keep) again means to inhabit . . .—

Ulysses

Where keeps he? In the city or afield?

542–3

A shepherd a replies:

Cicero: The force of nature shows itself most clearly when . . . men . . . endure what we see Philoctetes endure on the stage; when he was tortured by unbearable pains, he none the less prolonged his life by catching birds—

Shepherd

He standing, slow, swift flying birds transpierces;

as we find in a passage of Accius: ‘and made covering for his body with braided feathers.’ Cicero here paraphrases a line which Censorinus quotes thus: The tragic trimeter—

With braided feathers for a dress he veils His limbs.

544 Philoctetes is unapproachable:

Macrobius, quoting Virgil: ‘Neither easy for any man to look on, nor easy to accost.’ Accius in Philoctetes—

Whom you could neither look in the face nor speak to.
