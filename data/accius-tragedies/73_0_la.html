Meleager

Accius’ model may have been Euripides’ Μελέαγρος, but the subject was a popular one amongst ancient playwrights. Accius adopted the following form of the story; Oeneus, king of Calydon in Aetolia offended Diana, who therefore caused a huge boar to plague the realm. No one was brave enough to face the beast, until Meleager the invulnerable, one of Oeneus’ sons, hunted and killed it with the help of other heroes and also of Atalanta (daughter of Iasus or Iasius), who, according to one version, inflicted the first wound.
