Achilles

the fragments of both plays and their titles to Nonius alone. Several pairs of plays in Accius have been thought, as will be seen, to be one play under two titles, but it is hardly likely that in these several instances Nonius has at random used sometimes one title, sometimes another, of the same play.

1

Achilles is contemptuous of Agamemnon’s difficulties:

Nonius: ‘Flaccet,‘droops, fails. . . . Accius in Achilles—

Achilles Or else droops now his sceptre? Let him bear it! a 2

Patroclus pleads with Achilles after the unavailing embassy:

Nonius: ‘Delica’ (strain, make clear), explain, indicate. . . .—

Patroclus Make clear in what way else you can escape From the indictments of unfriendly men. 3

Nonius: ‘Fervat’ for ‘ferveat’ . . .—

Yes, then—when seethes the breast with anger full. b

  • aBut ferat is doubtful. Perhaps we ought to read fera with Quicherat.
  • bEither take iracundiae as a genitive after fervat or pectus, which seems impossible, or read iracundia.
