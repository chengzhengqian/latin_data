Plays

462

Nonius: ‘Sufferre’ (undergo) means to sustain . . .—

Myself will plainly show I did no wrong, Or else I’ll undergo the penalty.

463–4 His desire to go home:

Nonius: ‘Anima,’ wind . . .—

Into the brine a to launch the fleets and let The sails into the winds’ breath.

465 His resentment:

Nonius: The accusative for the genitive . . .—

Forgetting the deeds I did in battle-line.

466–7 He regrets that Ajax does not side with him:

Nonius: ‘Maestaret’ for ‘maerentem faceret’. . .—

But if, as was becoming, you had stood On my side, or if sorrow shown by me Had saddened you, then long ago the sons Of Atreus would have seen their ships ablaze.

468 Agamemnon in face of a revolt:

Nonius: ‘Cernere’ again means succedere,’ to succeed. Whence sons were said ‘cernere’ an inheritance, that is to succeed their fathers . . .—

Agamemnon Prefer they to entrust command to you? Accept the inheritance; will I surrender The armies?
  • aThe Romans derived solum from sal, wrongly.
