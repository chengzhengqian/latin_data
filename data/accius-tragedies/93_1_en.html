545–6

Nonius: ‘Cupienter’ (greedily), with great cupidity. . . .-

Who, grant him but the power, would greedily Rend with his jaws your limbs.

547 Where Philoctetes can be found:

Macrobius, quoting Virgil: ‘And the goat-born herd among the grasses with none to tend them’ . . .—

worn away by the hoofs of the goat-born.

548 Ulysses will face Philoctetes:

Nonius: ‘Cautim’ for ‘caute’ . . .—

Ulysses

Warily must I Attack him, face to face, and try to catch him.

549–53 Philoctetes on his wound:

Cicero: Surely you cannot apply the term brave man . . . (because he scorned human fortune) either to the famous Philoctetes or—well, I would rather not take you as an ex ample; but at any rate no brave man is he who lies—

Philoctetes

In shelter damp, Which, dumb thing though it is, repeats my cries, Tearful, re-echoing with wails and plaints, With moans and groans,
