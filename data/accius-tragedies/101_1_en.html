Plays

590–2

Danger of strife between Eteocles and Polyneices. a

Nonius: ‘Dividae,’ used like ‘dissensiones’. . .—

That discords and divisions of these brothers May not disturb and dissipate the riches, So great and so abundant, of the townsmen.

593

Eteocles, the first-born, ruled first; when Polyneices returned from his year’s absence, there was civil strife. b

Polyneices to Jocasta on his marriage with Adrastus’ daughter: c

Nonius: ‘Castitudinem’ for ‘castitatem’. . .—

There righteousness, there all time-honoured chastity

594

A parley between the brothers; Polyneices to Eteocles (?):

Nonius: ‘Vesci’ even means to have the use of . . .—

Polyneices Surely you think not That I enjoy on equal terms with you, Rewards our father made? d 595

Eteocles bids Polyneices leave the city:

Nonius: ‘Eliminari’ (be put out of doors) means to go out. . .—

Eteocles Go forth! Go out! Get you gone! Outdoors with you from the city!
  • aContext uncertain; the metre precludes attribution of these lines to the prologue.
  • bAccius perhaps developed this part of the plot at greater length than Euripides did.
  • c sc. Argeia. So I take this fr. Contrast R., 479.
  • dIn Accius Polyneices naturally speaks of his father’s orders or reward (see n.b of p. 525). According to Euripides, however, it was the brothers themselves who agreed to rule by turns (Eur., Phoen., 69 ff.).
