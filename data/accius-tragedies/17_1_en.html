91–2

The sentry rouses the guard:

Nonius: ‘Expergo’ for ‘expcrgefacio’ . . .—

Ho! Men of the watch, hurry there! Wake up your senses slow from sleep. Get up!

93–4

Antigone is arrested, is examined by Creon, and faces death:

Macrobius, quoting Virgil: ‘Now neither almighty Juno, nor Father Jupiter Saturn’s son, looks with kind eyes on these our affairs.’ Accius in Antigone—

Antigone Now the gods rule not, now the all-highest king Of gods cares not at all for men’s concerns. 95

Chorus begs Creon not to dismiss Teiresias?:

Nonius: ‘Numero’ (forthwith) means quickly . . .—

Dismiss not him forthwith who suddenly Was brought into your presence. a
