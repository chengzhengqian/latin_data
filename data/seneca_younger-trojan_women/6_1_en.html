TROJAN WOMEN

hecuba

For you my right hand strikes my arms, it strikes my shoulders for you till they bleed; for you my right hand pounds my head; for you a mother’s fingers have made her breasts lie torn. Every scar I made at your funeral must burst open, flow and stream with copious blood. Pillar of our country, delayer of doom, you were a defense for weary Phrygians, you were a wall, and by your shoulders she stood buttressed for ten years; with you she fell, and Hector’s last day was his country’s also. Turn your mourning: pour out your tears for Priam now; this suffices for Hector.
