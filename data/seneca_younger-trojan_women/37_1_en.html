TROJAN WOMEN

Pleuron detested by the virgin goddess, or Troezen curving the broad sea’s edge? Pelion, proud kingdom of Prothous, third step to heaven? Reclining here expansively in a cave of the fissured mountain, teacher of a boy already savage,47 Chiron, as his pick struck jangling strings, sharpened even then his mighty wrath by singing of wars. Crete roomy enough for a hundred cities, or Carystos rich in marbled stone, or Chalcis thrust into the edge of the restless sea on the ever hurrying Euripus? The Calydnae, easy ports in any wind, or Gonoessa never lacking wind, and Enispe which fears the norther? Peparethos clinging to Attica’s shores, or Eleusis exulting in its holy Mysteries? * * * 48 not to Salamis home of the true Ajax, or Calydon famed for its savage beast, and those lands the Titaressos waters with its sluggish stream before passing under the sea? Bessa and Scarphe, or the old man’s Pylos? Pharis, or Pisa and Elis famed for Jove’s victory wreaths? Let the gloomy storm carry our misery anywhere, make a gift of us to any land,
