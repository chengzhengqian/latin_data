TROJAN WOMEN

chorus

It is no untrained crowd, new to tears, that you bid lament: this is what we have done for years unbroken, from the moment when the Phrygian guest touched at Greek Amyclae,10 and the pine sacred to Mother Cybele cut through the deep. Ten times Mt. Ida has grown white with snows, ten times denuded for our pyres, and in the fields of Sigeum reapers have cut ten harvests in fear, while no day has been empty of grief, but some new reason has furnished laments. Give yourselves to blows of sorrow. O queen, raise your unhappy hand: the common crowd, we shall follow our lady; we are not unschooled in mourning.

hecuba

Faithful companions of my fall, unbind your hair; let your locks flow over your necks in mourning, soiled with the dust of Troy, still warm. Fill your hands: this much we may take from Troy. Let our group make ready, its shoulders stripped: drop your clothes, tuck in their folds, bare your bodies down to the womb.

  • 10Paris was a guest of Menelaus at Sparta, near Amyclae, when he seduced his wife, Helen.
