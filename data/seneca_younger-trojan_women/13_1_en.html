a cruel act of bloodshed “marriage,” I will not permit. The guilt of all comes back to me: one who does not forbid wrongdoing, when he has the power, commands it.

pyrrhus

Shall Achilles’ ghost gain no reward?

agamemnon

It shall, and all shall hymn him with praises, and lands unknown shall hear his great name. But if the dust is assuaged by the pouring-on of blood, cut the necks of prime cattle from a Phrygian herd, and let the blood that flows not bring grief to any mother. What kind of custom is that? When was human life expended on tomb offerings for a human? Lift the burden of odium and hatred from your father: you want him revered through reprisals!

pyrrhus

You are proud-hearted while good fortune exalts your spirits, but fainthearted at the clatter of danger, you tyrant of kings! Now is your heart inflamed with the fire of love, of a new passion? Are you to take spoils so often from us alone?23 With this hand I shall pay Achilles the victim that is rightfully his. If you refuse and withhold her, I shall give a greater victim, a worthy one for Pyrrhus to give; indeed, my hand has rested too long from slaughtering kings, and Priam claims his peer.

  • 23The previous “spoil” was Briseis. The preceding line suggests lust as Agamemnon’s motive in “stealing” both Briseis and Polyxena from Achilles.
