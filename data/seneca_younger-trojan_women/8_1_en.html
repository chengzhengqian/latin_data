talthybius

My mind feels fear, a shuddering tremor shakes my body. Things too unnatural to be true—they scarcely command belief—I saw with my own eyes, I saw them. The Titan was just grazing the mountain ridges as he rose, day had defeated night, when suddenly the earth shook with a muffled roar and heaved all of its inner recesses from the lowest depths. The treetops swayed; lofty woodland and sacred grove thundered with an awesome sound of breaking. On Ida rocks fell from the shattered ridges. Not only the earth trembled: the sea too sensed its own Achilles near,14 and made its waters roll.
