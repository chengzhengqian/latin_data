V. All his boyhood, then, Albinus spent in Africa, where he got a fair education in Greek and Latin letters. And even at that time he showed signs of a haughty and warlike spirit, for at school, it is said, he used often to recite to the children:

“Madly I seized my arms, though in arms there lay little reason”.1

And he repeated again and again the words, “Madly I seized my arms”.

It is said that his rule was predicted by a number of omens that occurred at the time of his birth. For instance, a snow-white bull was born, whose horns were of a deep purple hue. And he is said to have placed these, when tribune of the soldiers, in the temple of Apollo at Cumae, and when he made inquiry of the oracle there concerning his fate, he received a response, it is said, in the following lines:

“He shall establish the power of Rome though tumult beset her, Riding his horse he shall smite both Poeni and Galli rebellious.”2
