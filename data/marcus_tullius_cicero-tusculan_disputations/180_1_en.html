And so strength of soul resembling the strength and sinews and effectiveness of the body is also described by similar terms. Agility of body again is termed quickness, and the same term is held to imply praise when applied to the intellect as well, because of the soul’s rapid survey of a number of things in a short space of time.

XIV. There is this dissimilarity between soul and body, that the strong soul cannot be attacked by disease as bodies can, but physical aversions can occur without blame,1 while it is not so with aversions of the soul in which all diseases and disorders are the result of contempt of reason. Consequently they are only found in human beings; for while animals act in a way that is comparable, yet they are not attacked by disorders of the soul. There is, however, this difference between quick-witted and dull-witted men, that gifted men resemble Corinthian bronze which is slow to be attacked by rust, and similarly they are both slower to be attacked by disease and quicker in recovery, while with the dull-witted it is not so.
