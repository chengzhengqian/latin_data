There are some too in favour of concentrating all these ways of administering comfort (for one man is influenced in one way, one in another) pretty nearly as in my Consolation I threw them all into one attempt at consolation; for my soul was in a feverish state and I attempted every means of curing its condition.

But it is necessary in dealing with diseases of the soul, just as much as in dealing with bodily diseases, to choose the proper time, as was shown by the famous Prometheus, who after the words

And yet, Prometheus, this I think you know, That speech physician is to wrathful heart,

replied:

Yes, if a man applies a timely cure, And crushes not the wound with heavy hand.1

XXXII. The first remedial step therefore in giving comfort will be to show that either there is no evil or very little; the second will be to discuss the common lot of life and any special feature that needs discussion in the lot of the individual mourner; the third will be to show that it is utter folly to be uselessly overcome by sorrow when one realizes that there is no possible advantage.
