Yet none the less they wish the phantoms to speak and this cannot take place without tongue and palate, or without a formed throat and chest and lungs in active working. It was because they could frame no mental vision; everything was brought to the test of eyesight: and indeed it requires a powerful intellect to abstract the mind from the senses and separate thought from the force of habit. There must in my belief have been other thinkers in the long succession of the centuries, but so far as literature tells us, Pherecydes of Syros5 was the first who pronounced the souls of men to be eternal, and he was decidedly venerable, for he lived when my clansman6 was upon the throne.
