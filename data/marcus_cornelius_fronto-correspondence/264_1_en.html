In either Parthian war a man of consul arrank, in either case commanding an army, was putto the sword: Severianus2 while Lucius had at thetime not even left the city; Appius,3 however, while Trajan was present in the East making morestringent the ferry dues for camels and horses on theEuphrates and Tigris, was slain by Arbaces4 in rearof the Emperor.

17. This is also brought as a charge against both equally, that they sent for actors5 from Rome into Syria.
