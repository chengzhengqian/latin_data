Ad Antoninum Imp. ii. 7 (Naber, p. 111).

Magistro meo.

Orationes desiderat sibi Dominus frater a me vel a te quam primum mitti. Sed ego malo, mi magister, tu mittas; easque ut in promptu haberes, exemplaria quae apud nos erant misi tibi. Moreover the words, given by Naber, p. 107, at the beginning of Ad Anton. ii. 6 (Ambr. 143, col. 2), do not appear to belong to that letter, and I give them here as read by Brakman Vel a <te> visum quanta sollicitudinem <mihi adferant> ..... ita deo .... id ago .... explora diligeitius. They are from a letter of Fronto’s and refer, perhaps, to his grief.
