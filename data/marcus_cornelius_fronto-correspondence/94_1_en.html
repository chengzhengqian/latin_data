Marcus Aurelius to Fronto

To my master, greeting. ? 144–145 a.d.

These things at present . . . . Farewell, my dearest Fronto, my mother greets you. Greet our consul1 and our lady.

Fronto to Marcus Aurelius as Caesar

To my Lord. 145–147 a.d.

If you have any love at all for me, sleep those nights that you may come into the Senate2 with a good colour and read with a strong voice.

M. Aurelius to Fronto

To my master. 145–147 a.d.

I can never love you enough: I will sleep.

Fronto to Marcus as Caesar

To my Lord. 145–147 a.d.

Pardon me, cancel one word from your speech and, I entreat you, never use it—dictio for oratio. Farewell, my Lord, my everlasting glory. Greet my Lady your mother.

  • 1It is not known who is referred to.
  • 2For his speech of thanks as consul (145 a.d.) or as invested with Trib. Pot. (147).
