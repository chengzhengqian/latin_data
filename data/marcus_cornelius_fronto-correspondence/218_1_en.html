Fronto to Marcus

To my Lord Antoninus Augustus. 163 a.d.

1. First done, then entered,1 say they who keep their books carefully. The same saying is applicable to this letter, which now at last answers your recent one to me. The reason of the delay has been that, when I made up my mind to write, some tilings came into my mind, which could not be written down beak in air, as the saying is. Then intervened the sitting of the Senate, and the labour it entailed was felt the more heavily in that, being simultaneous with my joy, it had taken deeper hold of me, just as the wind when combined with the sun.2 Now this letter, as it was not forthcoming at its due time, asks the indulgence usual in postponements, that it be without prejudice.
