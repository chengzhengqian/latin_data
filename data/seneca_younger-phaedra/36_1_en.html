PHAEDRA

nurse

She tells no one; in her grief she hides the secret, and is determined to carry with her the trouble causing her death. Come now, come, I beg you: you must be quick.

theseus

(to slaves) Unbar the closed doors of the royal house. (the interior scene is revealed, with Phaedra holding Hippolytus’ sword) O consort of my marriage bed, is this how you respond to your man’s arrival, the face of your long-missed spouse? Why not uncouple your hand from the sword, restore my spirit to me, and explain whatever it is that drives you from life?

phaedra

Alas! By your sceptered power, great-souled Theseus, by the promise of our sons, by your own return, and by my ashes soon to be, allow me to die.

theseus

What cause compels your death?

phaedra

If the cause is told, the profit of my death is lost.

theseus

No one else will hear it but me.

phaedra

A chaste woman fears only her husband’s ears.

theseus

Speak out: I shall hide your secret in my faithful heart.

phaedra

If you want another to keep a secret, first keep it yourself.
