It was there that the greatest slaughter of the enemy occurred, that is to say, about eight thousand men and five elephants. And for the Romans also it was not a bloodless victory. About one thousand seven hundred from the two legions, and of the allies more than one thousand three hundred, were killed; wounded were a large number of citizens and allies. Hannibal moved his camp that night; Marcellus was eager to pursue, but the numbers of the wounded prevented him.

XV. Scouts sent to pursue the column reported next day that Hannibal was on his way to the land of the Bruttii.1
