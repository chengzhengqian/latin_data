What I didn’t dream of, think of, or believe in, that I would have this opportunity, this has now, as it were, fallen down from heaven: my master has sent me to Eretria22 to purchase plow oxen for him; he gave me the money, because he said there260 would be a market there a week from now. It was stupid of him to give me this money since he knew my ways; I’ll use this money up elsewhere: “There were no oxen for me to buy.” Now I’ll support my friend and do myself many good turns. In a single day I’ll use up what will make me happy for a long time. It’ll be swish-swash for my back. I don’t care. Now I’ll present my friend265 with these plow animals from my wallet: this is lovely indeed, to fleece overstingy, elderly, greedy, tightfisted men properly, who seal up the salt in the saltcellar to stop the slaves getting it.23 It’s a virtue to see clearly when the occasion reminds you to. What will he do to me? He’ll have me beaten with blows and have shackles put onto me. Let him get a beating, so he won’t believe I’m going270 to entreat him: bad luck to him! He can’t bring me anything new any longer, anything I haven’t experienced. But who is coming out from here? Look, it’s Paegnium, Toxilus’ slave boy.

Enter PAEGNIUM from the pimp’s house, running past SAGARISTIO.
