I am thinking of putting a marvellous episode into Canto II of my Vicissitudes: Apollo in the assembly of the gods foretelling the return of the two generals, one of them after losing his army, the other after selling it.

Caesar dispatched a letter to me from Britain on the Kalends of September which I received on 27 September, giving a good enough account of the British campaign. He says that, in case I might wonder why I got no letter from you, you were not with him when he got to the coast. I have not replied, not even to congratulate him, because of his bereavement.

Once again, my dear fellow, I beg you take care of your health.

22 (III.2) Rome, 11 October 54 From Marcus to his brother Quintus greetings.
