Bribery is rampant again, dreadful, worse than ever before. On the Ides the rate of interest rose from 4 to 8%2 because of a coalition between Memmius and the Consuls with Domitius. Scaurus on his own can hardly defeat this. Messalla is a tired horse.3 I do not speak in hyperbole: they are settling to distribute ten million sesterces among the first Century. It is a flaming scandal. The candidates for the Tribunate have agreed among themselves to deposit HS 500,000 each with M. Cato and make him referee of the elections, anyone contravening his rulings to be condemned by him. If these elections go through without corruption, as is thought likely, Cato will have achieved more on his own than all the laws and all the juries.
