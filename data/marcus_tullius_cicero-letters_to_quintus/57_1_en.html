You got an honourable mention from the men of Magnesia by Mt Sipylus; they said you were the only one to stand up to L. Lucretius’ poetry is as you say—sparkling with natural genius, but plenty of technical skill.5 But of that more when you come. If you read Sallustius’ From Empedocles,6 I’ll rate you both more and less than ordinary humanity.7

15 (II.11) Rome, ca. 13 February 54 From Marcus to his brother Quintus greetings.

I am glad my letters give you pleasure, though just now I should not have had any subject to write about if I had not had a letter from you.
