[Tibullus] III

Now wilt thou take heart, now fan my flames more boldly. This, alas! is the mischief brought me by my chattering tongue. Now, do what thou wilt, I will remain thine always, nor flee from bondage to a mistress that I know, but will sit in my chains at the altar of holy Venus. She brands law-breakers and befriends the suppliant.

XXUnkind Rumour

Rumour says that my girl is oft unfaithful. Now could I wish my ears were deaf. These charges are not made without suffering for me. Why dost thou torture thus thy victim, bitter Rumour? Peace!

DOMITIUS MARSUSOn the Death of Tibullus

Thee too, Tibullus, ere thy time hath Death’s unfeeling hand

Despatched to fare by Vergil’s side to dim Elysium’s land,

That none should be to plain of love in elegy’s soft lay

Or in heroic numbers sweep with princes to the fray.
