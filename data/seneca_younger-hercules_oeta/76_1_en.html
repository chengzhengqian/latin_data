HERCULES ON OETA

Why are you anxious, my spirit? You hold the ashes of Hercules! Clasp his bones; his remains will give aid, they will be protection enough; even your shade will frighten kings.

philoctetes

Mother of glorious Alcides; hold back your tears, deserved as they are by your son. One who by his valor has denied doom access is not to be mourned nor weighed down with the heaviness of death. His deathless valor forbids weeping for Hercules; the brave forbid mourning, the ignoble require it.

alcmene

Shall I quiet my laments as a mother, after losing the one who protected land and sea, where the gleaming sunlight looks on both oceans110 from its bright chariot? How many sons have I, his wretched mother, laid to rest in this one son? I held no throne, but had power to grant thrones. Alone of all mothers on earth I refrained from prayers, asked nothing of the gods, while my son lived. Could not Alcides’ ardor grant me anything that any god could deny me? The answer to my prayers lay in this hand; whatever Jove would deny me, Hercules would grant me. Has any mortal mother ever given birth to anything like him?
