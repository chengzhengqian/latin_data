When, Titan, where under heaven will you have another Hercules to escort on earth? Whose hands will the hapless world invoke if some prolific scourge near Lerna deploys its rage in a hundred snakes, or if some boar disquiets the forests of Arcadians, that olden folk, or a scion of Rhodope in Thrace, harsher than Helice’s snowy land, spatters his stables with human blood?93 Who will bring peace to fearful folk if amid their cities the gods in anger command some birth? He whom Earth produced as the Thunderer’s equal, lies everyone’s equal. Through countless cities let blows of grief resound: with hair fully unfastened let women bare and strike their arms. Let the doors of the gods be closed, and only his carefree stepmother’s shrines be open.
