HERCULES ON OETA

—I am wrong, wrong, crazy, insane! The shades and ghosts do not fear you now. No more does the pelt you stripped from the Argive lion, with its mantle of a tawny mane, make a fearsome shield for your left arm, nor its savage fangs fence round your brows. Your quiver now has gone as a gift, and a lesser hand will shoot your arrows. You walk unarmed among the ghosts, my son, where you will stay forever.

hercules

(appearing above, unseen at first by Alcmene) Now I have reached the realms of the starry sky and have finally been granted my place in heaven, why do you force me by your mourning to taste death? Refrain! My valor has paved a way for me now to the stars and the very gods.

alcmene

Whence is that sound that strikes my affrighted ears—that shout that forbids my weeping? Now I recognize: he has conquered chaos! Son, you return from the Styx once more to me, crushing grim death for a second time; again you have conquered the region of darkness and the gloomy waters of the underworld vessel.
