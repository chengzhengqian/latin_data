Another wants treasure to fill his hunger; but all the tract of jeweled Hister cannot suffice not the whole of Lydia slake his thirst,48 nor the land facing the western winds that is dazzled by radiance from bright Tagus’ golden river— not even if the whole of Hebrus served him and rich Hydaspes added its lands, and he saw the whole of Ganges’ river flowing within his own domains. The greedy, the greedy find Nature too little. One man courts kings and kingly abodes, not so his plowman shall hunch forever down on his plow and never rest, or a thousand tenants till his fields: he craves wealth only to put it by. Another courts kings to trample on all, destroying some and supporting none: he covets power for harm’s sake only. How few of them die at the fated time! Those seen in fortune by the Cynthian are seen in misery by the budding day. To be old and fortunate is rare. The greensward, softer than Tyrian sheets, brings slumbers that are free from fear;
