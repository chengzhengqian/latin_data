13. Sometimes at their banquets the scales are even called for, in order to weigh the fish, birds, and dormice1 that are served, whose great size they commend again and again, as hitherto unexampled, often repeating it to the weariness of those present, especially when thirty secretaries stand near by, with pen-cases and small tablets, recording these same items, so that the only thing lacking seems to be a schoolmaster.2

14. Some of them hate learning as they do poison, and read with attentive care only Juvenal and Marius Maximus,3 in their boundless idleness handling no other books than these, for what reason it is not for my humble mind to judge.4
