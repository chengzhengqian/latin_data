44. But the river Danube, rising near Augst,1 and the mountains near the Raetian frontier, extends over a wide tract, and after receiving sixty tributaries, nearly all of which are navigable, breaks through this Scythian shore into the sea through seven mouths.2

45. The first of these, as their names are interpreted in the Greek tongue, is the aforesaid island of Peuce,3 the second Naracustoma, the third Calonstoma, the fourth Pseudostoma; but the Borionstoma and Stenostoma are far smaller than the others; the seventh is muddy and black like a swamp.

46. Now the entire Pontus throughout its whole circuit is misty,4 has sweeter5 waters than the other seas,6 and is full of shoals, since the air is often thickened and condensed from the evaporation of moisture, and is tempered by the great masses of water that flow into it; and, because the many rivers that pour into it from every side bring in mud and clods, it rises in shoals that are full of ridges.
