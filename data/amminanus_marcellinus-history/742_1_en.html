And, for savages, they had a just cause of complaint.

2. For Valentinian from the very beginning of his reign burned with a desire of protecting his frontiers, which was indeed praiseworthy, but carried too far; for he ordered the building of a garrison-camp across the Danube in the very territories of the Quadi, as if they were already claimed for Roman rule. The natives, being indignant at this and cautious for their own interests, tried to prevent them for a time merely by a deputation and by whispered complaints.

3. But Maximinus,4 being prone to every kind of wickedness and unable to control his native arrogance, which was swollen still more by his prefecture, upbraided Aequitius, who was at the time commander of the cavalry in Illyricum, as rebellious and slothful in hot yet having finished the work the earlier construction of which had been arranged; and he added, as if having regard for the general welfare, that if the rank of general5 in Valeria6 were given to his own son7 Marcellianus,8 the fortification would rise without any excuses.
