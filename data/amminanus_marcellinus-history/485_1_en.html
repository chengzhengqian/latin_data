3. Julianus Augustus promises the soldiers a hundred denarii each, as a reward for their good services, and when they express contempt for so small a gift, he recalls them to their senses in a temperate address.

1. The day after these events the serious news came to the emperor, while he was quietly at table, that the Persian leader called the Surena1 had unexpectedly attacked three squadrons of our scouting cavalry, had killed a very few of them, including one of their tribunes, and carried off a standard.

2. At once roused to furious anger, Julian hurried forth with an armed force,—his safest course lay in his very speed—and routed the marauders in shameful confusion; he cashiered the two surviving tribunes as inefficient and cowardly, and following the ancient laws, discharged and put to death ten2 of the soldiers who had fled from the field.
