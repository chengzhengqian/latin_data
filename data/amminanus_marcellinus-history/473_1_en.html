Book XXIV

1. Julian with his army invades Assyria; he receives the fortress of Anathas on the Euphrates into surrender, and destroys it by fire.3

1. After thus testing the spirit of the soldiers,4 who with unanimous eagerness and the usual acclaim called God to witness that so successful a prince could not be vanquished, Julian, believing that their main purpose must speedily be accomplished, cut short the night’s rest5 and ordered the trumpets to give the signal for the march.
