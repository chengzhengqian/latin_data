3. From there, with back-flowing current,5 it laves the temple of Apollo Sminthius,6 the Troad, and Ilium, famed for the death of heroes, and forms the bay of Melas,7 facing the west wind, at the entrance of which is seen Abdera, the home of Protagoras and Democritus, and the bloodstained dwelling of the Thracian Diomedes,8 and the vales through which the Hebrus9 flows into it, and Maronea and Aenos,10 a city which Aeneas began under unfavourable auspices, but presently abandoned it and hastened on to ancient Ausonia under the guidance of the gods.
