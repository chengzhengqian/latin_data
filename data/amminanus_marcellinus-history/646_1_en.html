5. But let us come to our subject.

Maximinus, who formerly held the office of vice-prefect363 at Rome, was born at Sopianae, a town of Valeria,3 of very humble parents, his father being an accountant in the governor’s office4 and sprung from ancestors who were Carpi, a people whom Diocletian drove from its ancient abode5 and transferred to Pannonia.

6. Maximinus, after some slight study of the liberal arts, and after acting as a pleader without acquiring distinction, became governor of Corsica, also of Sardinia, and finally of Tuscia.6 Then, because his successor lingered too long on the369-70 way, although transferred to the charge of the city’s grain supply, he retained also the rule of Tuscia, and at the beginning acted with moderation, for a three-fold reason.
