27. Egypt also breeds innumerable serpents, surpassing all their destructive kind in fierceness: basilisks, amphisbaenae, scytalae, acontiae, dipsades, vipers, and many others,2 all of which are easily surpassed in size and beauty by the asp, which never of its own accord leaves the bed of the Nile.3

28. Many and great things there are in that land which it is worth while to see; of these it will be in place to describe a few. Everywhere temples of vast size have been erected. The Pyramids have been enrolled among the seven wonders of the world,4 and of their slow and difficult construction the historian Herodotus tells us.5 These are towers higher than any others which can be erected by human hands, extremely broad at the base and tapering to very pointed summits.
