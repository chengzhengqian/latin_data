Gratianus Valentinianus II

3. An unsuccessful plot of the Persians.

1. When affairs had reached this stage in Isauria, the king of Persia,1 involved in war with his neighbours, was driving back from his frontiers a number of very wild tribes which, with inconsistent policy, often make hostile raids upon his territories and sometimes aid him when he makes war upon us. One of his grandees, Nohodares by name, having received orders to invade Mesopotamia whenever occasion offered, was carefully reconnoitring our territory, intending a sudden incursion in case he found any opening.

2.
