XXVII.—On a Wild Beast slain by Caesar

The beast which knows not how to yield when pierced with the broad steel, but hurls itself upon the gory spear of a full-armed man, how marvellous the death it suffers from a tiny wound, showing that on the hand alone death’s might depends. Men wonder at swift disasters and sudden downfalls . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . and not content to drive its deadly course through the stricken limbs, a single arrow deals two deaths at once. If full many deaths come from one lightning stroke, these wounds also thou mayest deem sent from heaven.
