As herald to the Euxine Sea even now I long to speed, that Valens, who is Heaven’s next care, may learn of this—that with slaughter, flight, and fire the Swabians2 are hurled to destruction, and Rhine no longer is accounted the frontier of Gaul. But if at the sea’s behest my stream should flow backwards may I hither bring from there news that the Goths are vanquished.

XXIX.—To Valentinian the Younger. For a marble Statue

Now we have made thee of marble, as our means afford: but when thine Emperor-brother is returned, be thou of gold.3

XXX.—Lines inscribed under a Picture showing a Lion slain by Gratian with a single Arrow

The death which the lion suffers through so frail a reed is due, not to the weapon’s power, but to the wielder’s.
