Letters to Friends

286 (XIII.20) CICERO TO SERVIUS SULPICIUS RUFUS

Rome, 46 or 45

From Cicero to Servius greetings.

Asclapo, a physician of Patrae, is on familiar terms with me. I liked his company, and had favourable experience of his professional skill when members of my household1 were taken sick. He gave me satisfaction in this connection as a knowledgeable doctor, and also a conscientious and kindly one. Accordingly I am recommending him to you, and would request you to let him understand that I have written about him with some particularity and that my recommendation has been to his no small advantage. I shall be truly obliged.

287 (XIII.21) CICERO TO SERVIUS SULPICIUS RUFUS

Rome, 46 or 45

From Cicero to Servius greetings.

M. Aemilius Avianianus has cultivated my acquaintance from his early youth and always held me in regard. He is an honourable man and a very amiable one, most conscientious in serving his friends by every means in his power.
