364 (X.28) CICERO TO TREBONIUS

Rome, 2 February or shortly after, 43

From Cicero to Trebonius greetings.

How I wish you had invited me to that splendid feast on the Ides of March! We should then have had no leftovers! As it is, we are having so much trouble with these that the immortal service which you and your friends rendered the commonwealth leaves room for some criticism. And when I think that it was your excellent self who drew that noxious creature1 aside and that he has you to thank that he is still alive, I sometimes grow half angry with you, which for me is almost sinful.2 For you have left me with more trouble on my hands than everybody else put together.
