89 (II.14) CICERO TO CAELIUS RUFUS

Laodicea, mid March (?) 50

From M. Cicero, Imperator, to M. Caelius, Curule Aedile, greetings.

M. Fabius is a man of fine character and culture, and on very close terms with me. I have a quite exceptional regard for him, for his brilliant intellect and scholarly attainments go along with unusual modesty. I want you to take up his case1 as though it was an affair of my own. I know you great advocates—a man must commit murder if he wants your services. But in the present instance I take no excuse. If you care for me, you will put everything aside when Fabius wants your services.

I am eagerly and longingly awaiting news of Rome, and especially want to know how you are. Because of the severity of the winter it is a long time since any news reached us.
