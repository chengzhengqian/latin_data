Presumably something will be decided, or there will be a scandalous veto. For in the course of the discussion Pompey threw out the remark that everyone ought to obey the Senate. For my own part though, I am looking forward to nothing in the world so much as to Consul-Elect Paullus making the first speech.6

I am reminding you about Sittius’ bond, not for the first time, because I am anxious to make you realize my strong personal concern in that matter. Likewise about panthers—please send for some from Cibyra and have them shipped to me. Another thing, we have had word of the death of the king of Egypt,7 and it is now taken as certain. Please write to me at length, advising me how to act and telling me of the state of the kingdom and who is in charge of it.
