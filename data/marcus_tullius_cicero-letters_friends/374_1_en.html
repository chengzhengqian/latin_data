214 (XV.17) CICERO TO CASSIUS

Rome, January (beginning) 45

M. Cicero to C. Cassius greetings.

Your couriers are a queer set—not that I mind; but when they go away they demand a letter, while when they arrive they bring none. Even so, it would be more convenient if they gave me a little time to write, but they arrive with their travelling caps on and say their party is waiting for them at the city gate. So you must forgive me. You are going to get a second short letter. But you may look forward to full amends. Though I can’t think why I am apologizing to you, when your people come to my house empty-handed and go back to you with letters.

Here in Rome (just to write you something after all) we have a death to talk about—P. Sulla senior. Some say it was bandits, others overeating. The public doesn’t care which, as there’s no doubt that he’s ashes. You will bear the news like the philosopher you are. Still, the town has lost a personnage.
