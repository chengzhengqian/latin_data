159 (XIV.12) CICERO TO TERENTIA

Brundisium, 4 November 48

From Tullius to his dear Terentia greetings.

I hope your joy at our safe arrival in Italy may be a lasting one. But distracted as I was by my unhappiness and the cruel ill treatment of my family, I am afraid I may have taken a road with no easy outcome. So help me as far as you can—though what you can do I cannot imagine. There is no reason for you to leave home at present. It is a long, unsafe journey, and I do not see what good you can do if you come.

Good-bye.

Dispatched 4 November from Brundisium.

  • 1Dolabella, to whom money was due for Tullia’s dowry; cf. Letters to Atticus 212 (XI.2).2.
