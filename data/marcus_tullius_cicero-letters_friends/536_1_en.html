331 (VII.22) CICERO TO TREBATIUS

Rome, 46 (?)

From Cicero to Trebatius greetings.

You made game of me yesterday over our cups for saying that it was a moot point whether an heir can properly take action for theft in respect of a theft previously committed.1 So when I got home, though late and well in tipple, I noted the relevant section2 and send you a transcript. You will find that the view which, according to you, has never been held by anybody was in fact held by Sex. Aelius, Manius Manilius, and M. Brutus.3 However, for my part I agree with Scaevola and Testa.
