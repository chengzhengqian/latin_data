221 (VI.22) CICERO TO CN. DOMITIUS AHENOBARBUS

Rome, May (?) 46

From Cicero to Domitius.

I was not deterred from sending you a letter after your return to Italy by the fact that you had sent none to me. But I knew neither how to make you any promise, being myself in need of everything, nor how to advise you, when I was short of counsel myself, nor how to comfort you in the midst of such afflictions. The present situation in these respects is no better, it is far more hopeless even. However, I prefer to write an empty letter than not to write at all.
