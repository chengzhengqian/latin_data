134 (XIII.65) CICERO TO MINUCIUS THERMUS

Province of Cilicia, 51 or 50

From Cicero to Thermus,1 Propraetor, greetings.

With P. Terentius Hispo, who works for the Grazing Rents Company2 as their local manager, I have a great deal of familiar contact, and each of us is indebted to the other for important services, equal and mutual. His reputation very largely depends on his concluding tax agreements with the communes still outstanding. I do not forget that I tried my own hand at the business in Ephesus and was quite unable to get the Ephesians to oblige. However, according to the universal persuasion and my own observation, your complete integrity combined with your remarkable civility and gentleness have earned you the heartiest compliance of the natives with your every nod. I would therefore particularly request you as a compliment to me to make it your wish that Hispo should get this credit.
