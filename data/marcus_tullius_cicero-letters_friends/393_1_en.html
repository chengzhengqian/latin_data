226 (VI.12) CICERO TO AMPIUS BALBUS

Rome, beginning of October (?) 46

From Cicero to Ampius best greetings.

I congratulate you, my dear Balbus, and in all sincerity. For I am not so foolish as to wish you temporary enjoyment of an illusory happiness, only to be dashed of a sudden and to fall so low that nothing later on could lift you into equanimity. I have pleaded your cause more openly than my present circumstances warranted, for I thought more of my fondness for you and the uninterrupted affection which you have been so sedulous to foster than of the debility which has overtaken my personal influence. All that concerns your return and restoration has been promised and confirmed, positively and definitely. I was present, observing and taking note.
