411 (XI.21) CICERO TO D. BRUTUS

Rome, 4 June 43

From M. Cicero to D. Brutus, Imperator, Consul-Elect, greetings.

Confound that Segulius,1 the most arrant scoundrel alive or dead or yet to be! Do you really suppose that he talks only to you or to Caesar? He has told the same tale to every person he could find to listen to him. But I am properly grateful to you, my dear Brutus, for wanting me to know of this piece of tittle-tattle, such as it is—a notable mark of your affection.

As for this same Segulius’ story that the veterans are grumbling because you and Caesar are not on the Commission of Ten, I only wish I was not on it! A most tiresome business! However, I did move that a vote should be taken on generals in command of armies, but was met by an outcry from the usual quarters.
