But there is a great deal to be said, particularly in times like these, for having a refuge: a town full of people who care for you and a house and land of your own, that too in a retired, healthy, and pleasant locality. And I feel that I myself have some stake in the matter, my dear Trebatius. However, you must keep well and look after my affairs and expect me back, Gods willing, before midwinter.

I have taken away a book, from Nico’s6 pupil Sex. Fadius: ‘Nico on Heavy Eating.’ What a charming physician, and how docile a patient he would find me! But our friend Bassus told me nothing about that book—though he seems to have told you!7

The wind is getting brisker. Take care of yourself.

20 July, Velia

334 (VII.19) CICERO TO TREBATIUS

Regium, 28 July 44

From Cicero to Trebatius greetings.

This will show how highly I regard you—as indeed I ought, for your affection is no less warm than my own; but anyhow, that request of yours to which to your face I said something a little like no, and at any rate did not say yes—in your absence I could not let it go unanswered.
