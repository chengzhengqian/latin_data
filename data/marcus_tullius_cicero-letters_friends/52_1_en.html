I was not dealing with a nation in its dotage, nor did I have a free choice whether or not to engage in public life, being inextricably involved. But I congratulated myself on having a cause to champion both expedient to myself personally and commendable to any honest man.28 An added incentive was Caesar’s quite remarkable, in fact amazing, generosity towards my brother and myself. He would have deserved my support whatever his accomplishments, but in so brilliant a career of success and victory I should think him worthy of honour even if he were not the good friend to us that he is. For I would have you believe that, apart from yourself and your fellow architects of my restoration, there is no man to whose good offices I acknowledge myself so deeply beholden—and am glad to do so.
