140 (XIII.58) CICERO TO C. TITIUS RUFUS

Laodicea, February 50 (?)

From M. Cicero to C. Titius Rufus, son of Lucius, Praetor Urbanus, greetings.

L. Custidius is a member of my Tribe, a native of my town, and my good friend. He has a case, which he will be submitting to your judgement. Allow me to recommend him to you within the limits imposed by conscience on your side and propriety on mine, merely asking that he find access to you easy and obtain his fair demands with your good will; and that he have reason to feel that my friendship, even at so great a distance, is advantageous to him, especially where you are concerned.
