Did I leave anything undone in the way of warning or protest in advocating the most inequitable peace as preferable to the most righteous of wars? My advice was overborne, not so much by Pompey, on whom it made an impression, as by persons who in reliance on Pompey’s generalship conceived that victory in such a war would be highly opportune for their personal affairs and ambitions. When hostilities were joined I remained passive, and I stayed in Italy as long as I was able after they had been shifted elsewhere. But sensibility prevailed with me over fear. I was ashamed to fail Pompey in his hour of need, when he in time gone by had not failed me. Duty then, or my reputation with the honest men, or sensibility carried the day.
