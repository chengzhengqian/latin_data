If the tokens of affection and regard count, has he not made me the confidant of all his secrets and projects? Has he not chosen me of all others to represent his interests in the Senate during his absences? Has he not desired for me all manner of high distinctions? Can I forget how readily and graciously he accepted my efforts on Milo’s behalf, which sometimes ran counter to his own policies, how anxious he was to ensure that I was not touched by the public feeling aroused at that time? How he protected me by his dispositions, his authority, even his armed forces?9 His firm and lofty mind in those days would not let him lend credence to ill-natured talk about me even from the highest quarters, let alone some denizen of Phrygia or Lycaonia, as you did in the matter of the delegates. Well, Pompey’s son10 is your son-in-law; and apart from that connection I am well aware how dear and delightful a friend you are to him. What then should be my feelings towards you? Add that he has sent me a letter, which, even if I had been your enemy, as I am so sincerely your friend, would have disarmed my resentment and rendered me obedient to the wishes, the very nod, of the man to whom I owe so much.
