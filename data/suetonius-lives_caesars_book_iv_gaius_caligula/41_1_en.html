He reduced the amount of armour of the murmillones.107 When one Columbus had won a victory, but had suffered a slight wound, he had the place rubbed with a poison which he henceforth called “Columbinum”; at least that name was found included in his list of poisons. He was so passionately devoted to the green faction108 that he constantly dined and spent the night in their stable,109 and in one of his revels with them he gave the driver Eutychus two million sesterces in gifts.110 He used to send his soldiers on the day before the games and order silence in the neighbourhood, to prevent the horse Incitatus111 from being disturbed. Besides a stall of marble, a manger of ivory, purple blankets and a collar of precious stones, he even gave this horse a house, a troop of slaves and furniture, for the more elegant entertainment of the guests invited in his name; and it is also said that he planned to make him consul.
