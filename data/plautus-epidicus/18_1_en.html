Then one of them said to the woman she was walking with—

per

(interrupting) What?

epi

Be quiet then so that you hear it—after they saw the woman your son is madly in love with, she says: “Please, how easily and happily things work out for a woman her lover wants to free!” “Who on earth is he?” the other one245 asks her. Then the first names Stratippocles, the son of Periphanes.

per

I’m dead! What must I hear from you?

epi

The facts. After I heard them talk about this, I began to approach them backward, gradually, as if the crowd were pushing me back against my will.

per

I understand.

epi

Then the second asks the other: “How do you know?250 Who told you this?” “Well, today she was brought a letter from Stratippocles, saying that he’d taken money at interest from a moneylender in Thebes, that it was ready, and that he was bringing it for that purpose.”

per

I’m certainly dead!

epi

This was what she said; she said she’d heard this from the woman herself and the letter.
