Epidicus

chae

But I myself am being subjected to loud harassment and demands.

stra

I’d prefer friends of your sort being baked up to being bankrupt. Well, I’d like to buy the services of Epidicus120 now for a precious price. I’ll irrigate him with blows and hand him over to the miller unless he gets me forty minas today before I’ve pronounced the last syllable of the sum.

epi

(with sarcasm) The matter’s safe: he’s making good promises, I hope he’ll keep his word. Without any expense125 on my part contributions11 have already been prepared for my shoulder blades. I’ll approach him. (loudly) The slave Epidicus bestows his greetings on his master Stratippocles upon his arrival from abroad.

stra

Where is he?

epi

(stepping up) He’s present. That you have arrived safely—

stra

(interrupting) I believe you in this as much as myself.

epi

Have you been well throughout?

stra

Physically, yes, mentally, no.

epi

I’ve taken care of my duty. What you told me to do has130 been achieved. The slave girl’s been bought, which was why you kept sending me letters.

stra

You’ve wasted all your efforts.

epi

How come I’ve wasted them?

stra

She isn’t dear to my heart and I don’t love her.

epi

What was the point of commanding me so much and sending letters to me?

  • 11A symbola “contribution” is a share each person participating in a joint banquet pays.
