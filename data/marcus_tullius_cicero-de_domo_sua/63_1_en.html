What a situation, calculated to arouse the unquenchable laughter of the frivolous, but which the serious cannot hear of without the deepest mortification! Has Publius Clodius, who robbed the house of the supreme pontiff of its sanctity, conferred sanctity upon mine? Is this the man whom you, who are the overseers of ritual and sacrifice, have as the guide and director of the state religion? Ye immortal gods (for I desire that what I say should reach your ears), is it indeed Publius Clodius who makes your sacrifices his care, who quakes before your power, and who thinks that all human affairs are swayed by the observance paid to you? Surely the fellow does but mock at the authority of all the great men here present; surely he does but do outrage, gentlemen, to your august presence. What word of religion can fall or slip inadvertently from his lips, those same lips wherewith he has so abominably and outrageously polluted religion, in that he accused the senate of being too austere in its decrees 105 upon religion?
