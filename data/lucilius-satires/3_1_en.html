Book I

10

The evil ways of Rome of Lupus’ time:

Nonius: ‘Meret,’ gets a very lowly and sordid profit . . . Lucilius in bk. I has ‘mercedimerae’—

“and wage-earning brigades.” a

11

The good old times:

Nonius: ‘Popinones’ are those whom we now call barloungers, from ‘popinae,’ or they are extravagant people who devote themselves to vittling-houses . . . —

“and to hate the infamous and shameful vittling- house.”

12

But now costly foreign wares bring luxury:

Nonius: ‘Tunica’ is a garment without sleeves . . . —

“Bordered cloaks and underdress, all dirty mean workmanship of Lydians,” b

13

Nonius: ‘Amphitapoe’ is the name given to coverings having nap-tufts on both sides . . . —

“single-napped and soft double-napped coverlets with huge tufts.”
