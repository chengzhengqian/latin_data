Sat. 11. 172–5

A fight between gladiators (L. Kaiser, Class. Journ., XLV, 187):

Nonius: ‘Spurcum’ (nasty, filthy, low), cruel or bloodthirsty b . . . —

In the public show given by the Flacci was a certain Aeserninus, a Samnite, a nasty c fellow, worthy of that life and station. He was matched with Pacideianus, who was by far the best of all the gladiators since the creation of man.

  • aThe right readings and meaning are not clear. Tenero (sc. corpore) maneat may be right; and lacerto may mean ‘arm’ or ‘strength.’
  • bNonius is doubtless wrong.
  • c spurcus in the sense of base, common, low. Aeserninus may mean ‘a man of Aesernia.’ ‘Samnis’ as on p. 59.
