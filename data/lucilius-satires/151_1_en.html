Book XXIX

938–9

Preparations for attack:

Nonius: ‘Reddere,’ to make . . . —

He will hammer out pent-houses and roofs, he will make shield-shelters.

940

Charisius: Again the archaic writers often put ‘nemo’ for ‘nullus,’ and therefore when using the term ‘nemo,’ as though they expressed their meaning too vaguely, they used to add ‘homo’ also; for example. . . . Lucilius in book XXIX . . .—

“What man are you?” “No man am I.” a

941

Nonius: ‘Anceps,’ double . . .—

“No man b may break open these double hinges with an axe.”

942

Nonius: ‘Anceps,’ sharp on either side . . . —

“With crowbar and with axe of double head c these hinges I’ll break open.”

943–4

The defence:

Nonius: ‘Deicere’ (throw down) is a term used for to send . . . —

I’ll throw from the windows a pot down on the heads of any who have strained their way hither close by the door.

  • aAristoph., Wasps, 184 τίς εἶ ποτ᾽ ὤνθρωπ᾽ ἐτεόν;—Οὔτις νὴ Δία. Hom., Od., IX, 355, 366.
  • bPerhaps ‘No man’ of line 940.
  • cI take it that the attacker replies to the preceding fragment in elevated style, turning ancipites into a different sense.
