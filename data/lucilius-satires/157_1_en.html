Book XXIX

973

Nonius: ‘Efferre’ (raise up, puff up), to lift up . . . —

If I may cut a long story short, all the rest of it— I mean the things in which we are puffed up.

From Book XXVIII or BOOK XXIX 974–5

On women:

Priscianus: Lucilius writes—

Rather let him who may wish, embrace her. I won’t let him embrace me.

976–7

Feeble health:

Nonius: ‘Tollere’ (carry off), to kill. Lucilius in book XXVI a —

A man whom one fever, one attack of indigestion did choke,—one ladle of wine, I tell you, was enough to have carried him off. b

978

A grammarian: ‘Nasum’ of the neuter gender, for example Lucilius—

This nose here and this body are a shield.

979–80

The following is doubtful:

Probus: ‘Nasus’ masculine or ‘nasum’ neuter? The archaic writers used to employ the neuter form. Thus Lucretius—

if I wanted my nose to be a little more hooked c than crooked.

  • aWhich did not contain senarii such as are the following.
  • bI suggest anxit or angit, but the fragment is doubtful. Potuit here means probably ‘could have.’
  • cor, if we retain diductius, more cleft. And pandiue may mean wider, flatter. Lucretius (in whom the fragment does not occur) is a common mistake for Lucilius.
