II. On Lucius Aurelius Cotta, c consul in 144 b.c.?

Nonius: ‘Tricones’ (tricksters) capricious (or ‘mŏrosi,’ dilatory) persons and hard to get a return from . . . —

The old man Lucius Cotta, my dear Pacenius, the father of this fat fellow, d was a great trickster, a taker of bribes, pliant for paying no one; e

‘lentus,’ that is, easy.

Nonius: ‘Tricae’ (trifles, trifling, trickeries) are hindrances and entanglements . . . —

I’ve no use for this lecher, no use for this bail- bound trickster.
