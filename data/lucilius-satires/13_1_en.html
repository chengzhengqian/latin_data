Book II

75

Nonius: ‘Circumferre’ properly means to cleanse . . .

(S.d.)“Then all was done aright, I was purified. a

76

Nonius: ‘Lustrare’ . . . —

(S.d.) “cleansed, made pure.”

77

The charges of thieving?:

Nonius (utterly mistaking the meaning of ‘coicere’): ‘Coicere,’ to steal, take away . . . —

(S.d.) “What say you? Why comes it that you make that contention of yours?”

78–9

Questioning of witnesses by Albucius.

He questions Aemilius, witness in favour of Scaevola:

Nonius: ‘Excantare’ (to charm out by sons) means ‘excludere’ . . . —

(A.n.) “which I now fore-chant to Aemilius here, which I force out and chant out.

80–1

Albucius questions the witnesses of L. Atilius Nomentanus? accused with Scaevola?:

Nonius: ‘Exculpere’ means to twist out . . . —

(A.n.) “And now you will hear from me all that I can gouge out of Nomentanus’ witnesses by ques- tioning him myself.” b
