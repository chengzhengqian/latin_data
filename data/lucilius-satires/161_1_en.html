(vi) Simile of a funeral:

Nonius: ‘Praeficae’ is a term which the ancients used to apply to women who are wont to be hired on pay to be present at a funeral. . . . Lucilius in book XXII—

keeners, who, hired on pay, weep in another’s funeral-crowd, tear their hair and cry out much more than others do. a

997–8

(vii) The human body:

Nonius: ‘Coxendices,’ hip-bones. b Lucilius—

The head is upheld by the neck, but the trunk by the hips.

999

(viii) Doubtful:

Nonius: ‘Conducere,’ to be useful * * * —

but he alone (will tell) . . . him alone what is to the advantage of his stock and profit. c

  • a sc. because they are paid to do it. The fragment probably belongs to book XXVII or XXVI. Cf. Marx; also Fiske, 168, 463; Cichor., 118–9.
  • b Coxendices, hips: coxae, hip-bones. But in Pliny X, 168, XXVIII, 179, coxendices are hip-bones; so perhaps here.
  • cThe fragment looks like Lucilian poetry, but attribution to Lucilius is guesswork. Perhaps quaesti . . . scio are right readings.
