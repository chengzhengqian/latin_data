Invective:

Festus: ‘Pipatio’ b is the noise of one bewailing in a shrill voice. Lucilius—

“Do you ask it, do you cheep? Out with it!” ‘With pleasure,” said he.

That is, “do you ask it, do you cry? Out with it.” “That suits me” said he.

Donatus: ‘Senex’ is used with reference to age, ‘senium’ with reference to a sneer; thus Lucilius—

Says someone, “you old dotard, you fool of a quibbler.”
