Sat. III. ‘The Brothel.’ On the subject of choosing a woman; addressed to a friend of rank and promise.

Title:

Arnobius: You hold stamped on your memories The Brothel b of Lucilius, and Marsyas of Pomponius.

910–1

Lucilius begs a hearing:

Nonius: ‘Adtendere’ means to strain towards . . . —

moreover that you may be pleased to give and apply your mind to my words.

912

Because of his friend’s future?

Nonius: ‘Rogare,’ to constitute . . . —

a rising proposer of his country’s counsels and laws.

913–5

A. Behaviour towards married women:

Nonius: ‘Permities,’ danger, mischief . . . —

Now do you come forward with an objection? You indeed when you deny that you have lusted after married women, and not without mischief either?
