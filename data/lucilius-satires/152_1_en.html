Book XXIX

945

Set-back of the attackers:

Nonius: ‘Depilati’ (‘with the hairs pulled out’) is a term used for thinned out . . . —

“Gnatho, what’s happened?” “We’re all fleeced.” a

946

Nonius: ‘Caedere,’ to break . . . —

“Smash the door, Gnatho, shove!” b “They won’t shift; we’re done for!”

947

Nonius: ‘Offendere’ means to strike hard . . . —

“Your leg against a stone? c It’s nothing.” “I’ll believe it, if he strikes you hard!”

948

Nonius: ‘Filum,’ the feature of the face . . . —

Up with you, woman; get a move on; it’s not a bad phiz. d

949

(iii) Rome and Hannibal. e

Battle of Cannae(216 b.c.):

Nonius: ‘Deletio’ . . . —

the destruction of our army to a man.

  • aNonius is vague; scalded smooth by the hot water thrown on them thinks Marx; surely it simply means ‘we’re done for,’ ‘cheated.’
  • b sc. against the defenders; or the door-posts?
  • cIt seems to me that some well known saying is applied here: but the fragment is obscure.
  • dMarx takes this as an incomplete septenarius; the reading is certainly doubtful. We might read ducis filum . . . ‘you spin a good thread,’ with a pun on filum,‘face.’
  • eThe events alluded to date from 216 to 202 b.c.
