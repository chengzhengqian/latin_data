Book XXX

1034

Progress of the meal:

Nonius: ‘Deblaterare,’ to talk at, make up talk(?) . . .

They babble away, and a dear old blockhead yokel chimes in with them. a

1035–6

Nonius.: ‘Delica’ (make clear) means lay open and explain . . . —

“No one will poke through yonder belly.” “Let her make clear if there is any way in, and you’ll see.” b

1037

Nonius: ‘Excutere,’ to shut out . . . —

“Let’s kick out the whole crowd, host and all!”

Sat. III. On social life in Rome. 1038

Take my advice:

Nonius: ‘Mittere,’ the same as ‘omittere’ . . . —

The one you will send packing, the other you will use with my good will. c

  • aWe might take deblaterare as: strike up or babble a silly tune; obloqui: join in singing; confingere: improvise; concinit: sings in harmony, in tune.
  • bI adopt the reading of Housman, C.Q., I, 156–7 and refer the fragment to one of the two guests mentioned in lines 1028–9.
  • cHe seems to mean: you must have done with the evils I describe and profit from my advice about them.
