Book XXII

623

Pacilius (epitaph):

A Glossary: ‘Abzet’ a means ‘put out’ or ‘dead.’ Lucilius in book XXII—

First my treasurer Pacilius, a very father, a ’s a cauld corp.

624–5

Metrophanes (epitaph):

Donatus on a passage in Terence: Or does ‘columen’ here mean ‘columna’? From this word comes ‘columellae’ applied in archaic writers to the more important slaves of a household . . .—

Here lies a slave who was both faithful and in all ways useful to his master, a little pillar of Lucilius’ house, by name Metrophanes.

626

Zopyrion:

Nonius: . . . ‘Labea’ . . . of the feminine gender. . . . Lucilius in the twenty-second book of the Satires—

Zopyrion b buffets his lips right and left.

  • aAn Oscan or a Paelignian word; cp. Paelignian afded. Pacilius is the Oscan Paakul.
  • bThis slave is probably the same as he who is alluded to in lines 362–3.
