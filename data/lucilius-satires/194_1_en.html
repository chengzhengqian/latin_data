Unassigned Fragments

1188

Bad times:

Nonius: The old writers have established with strong authority that ‘interfici’ and ‘occidi’ can be used even of lifeless things. . . . Lucilius—

Meanwhile everything passes away and goes slipping backwards again.

1189–90

Stoic ideas:

Porphyrio: But further the Stoics believe that a man of perfect wisdom has all things. Lucilius also, dealing with the same idea, speaks as follows—

But not even he who has all this will alone be called a handsome fellow, a rich one, a gentleman, a king amongst us. a

Still that poet does not state this simply as a fact, but in derision of the Stoics.

1191

Repentance:

A commentator, on Virgil: ‘Sub’ is put for ‘parum.’ Lucilius—

He feels rather ashamed for what he did . . .

1192

Disgust of Lucilius:

Varro: In a passage of Lucilius—

Why should I try to write you roundabout ramblings?

‘ambages’ comes from the word ‘ambe,’ which is contained in ‘ambitus’ and ‘ambitiosus.’
