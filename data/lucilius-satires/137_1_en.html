848

Nonius: ‘Tollere’ means to lift up . . . —

Mountains so vast will raise themselves out of the darkness to the open sky. a

849–50

Nonius: ‘Vatax’ and ‘varicosus,’ having deformed feet . . . —

as if . . . an ancient lineage, from which have sprung Quintus Maximus b and the swell-veined splay-footed. . . .

851

Nonius: ‘Spur cum’ (nasty) even means smelly . . . —

and things which are nasty to sight and smell.
