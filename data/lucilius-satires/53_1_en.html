Sat. I. 347

The Salii, flamines, and pontifices:

Priscianus: We find even nom. feminine ‘capis,’ gen. ‘capidis’ (one-handled bowl), of which the diminutive form is ‘capidula’ . . . Lucilius in book IX—

Hence arose the Shields, hence the invention of the Cone-Caps b and the Bowls.

  • a i.e. with the wind right aft, at full speed (cp. Cic, ad Att., XVI, 6; Ov., F., Ill, 565). This passage is desperately corrupt. The cercurus was really a light ship used particularly by the Cyprians. Greek κέρκυρος. Nonius spells it cercyrus.
  • bPlut.’, Num., 13; Livy, VI, 41, 9; X, 7, 10; Cic, Par., I, 11. The ancilia belonged particularly to the Salii, the apices to the flamines, and the capides to the pontifices.
