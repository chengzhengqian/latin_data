1138–41

Tubulus and others:

Cicero: What shall we say about the sacrilegious, what about the impious and the oath-breakers?—

If ever Lucius Tubulus, if Lupus or Carbo—or Neptune’s son, a

as Lucilius says—

had thought that there are gods, would he have been such an oath-breaker,

or—

a man so foul?

Vettius Philocomus?: b

Quintilian: I say nothing of Tuscan and Sabine people, nor of Praenestine; . . . Lucilius attacks Vettius as one who used words of their dialects.

1142–3

One Decimus Sura?:

A scholiast on a passage in Persius: This period is to be found in Lucilius—

  • a sc. L. Tubulus (praetor in 142 b.c.; received bribes as a judge), L. Cornelius Lentulus Lupus (consul in 146, censor in 127; see pp. 4ff.), C. Papirius Carbo (alleged murderer of Scipio; or Marcus an extortioner—Cic, ad Fam., IX, 21, 3), and the Cyclops who scorned the gods (Homer, Od., IX, 273 ff.). Whether the words putasset e. q. s. are likewise from Lucilius is not certain.
  • bA friend of Lucilius—Suet., de Gramm., 2. Cp. Introd., xvii. But Vettius was a common name among the Marsi and Vestini. Cichor. suggests Vettius who was a friend of C. Gracchus (Plut., C. Gr., 1).
