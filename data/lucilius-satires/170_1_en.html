1049–51

(B) Household economy.

Great men and small estates:

Nonius: ‘Sescentum’ for ‘sescentorum’ . . . —

if Maximus has bequeathed a six hundred and a thousand <great sesterces> a in money.

The same poet . . . —

But what does it matter if you possess one or two hundred thousand <great sesterces>? b

1052

Estimating one’s resources:

Nonius: ‘Sumpti’ for ‘sumptus’ . . . —

what he would be able to give, and how much he could afford to offer and spend.

  • a1,600,000 sesterces. Sescentum, accusative. Maximus is Q. Fab. Max. Aemilianus (consul in 145 b.c.), brother of Scipio, unless we read maximus.
  • b100,000,000 or 200,000,000 sesterces Dūcentum as in fr. 581.
