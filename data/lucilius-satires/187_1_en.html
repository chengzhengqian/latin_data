Unassigned Fragments

1153

Charisius: ‘Satis’ is taken in different ways. Lucilius has it in place of ‘par’ in . . . of the Satires * * *—

for whom the whole people, if it should unite on oath, would hardly be a match. a

1154

Public rejoicing:

A scholiast on ‘Lead a mighty chalked bull to the Capitol’ in Juvenal: (Cretatum) white, as for example Lucilius—

and lead a chalked bull to the mighty Capitol. b

1155–6

Roman manners. Luxury of women:

Porphyrio: Lucilius used the term ‘echinus’ as though it were a leathern bottle, for example when he says—

a toilet-flask stained with dragon’s-blood. c

1157

Paulus: ‘Sicyonia,’ a certain kind of footwear. Lucilius—

and with her left hand she takes the fine d Sicyonian shoes off her feet.

  • aThis is after Charisius’ own explanation; the man is so powerful that not all the people acting together could be equal to resisting him: there is an allusion probably to the political clubs which were important in the first century at any rate. I would suggest that cui caused the number ot the book to drop out. It may have been XVI or XII, but the quotation suggests an anecdote in book XI.
  • bThis was done on occasions of public rejoicing.
  • cSo I take cinnabari in the meaning of oriental dragon’s-blood (obtained from a species of Dracaena growing in Socotra and Somaliland) which was used as a medicine, as a dye, and as a paint. But the staining of the flask might be due to its containing this, or the mineral red-lead, or sulphide of mercury (these were also called cinnabari).
  • dcp. Lucret., IV, 1125 (pulchra in pedibus Sicyonia); but here perhaps it is her hand that is fine or pretty.
