On the fate of Lupus:

Varro: In a passage of Lucilhis . . . —

“O Bass, juices of the shabar-fish and the Nile- perch are the death of you!” b

Sat. III

I put these fragments after the concilium deorum because such an order is suggested by the order of two citations in Nonius, namely of lines 8 and 50–1.

47

Diomedes: Others think that the term (satura) is derived from a tacked law, which includes many things at once in one bill, for to be sure, in verse too called medley, many poems at once are included. Of this tacked law Lucilius makes mention in his first book—

who may absolve from the laws an aedile elected by tacked procedure. c

  • aHousman, C.Q., I, 148. The Latin gives only the initial letter of each of the last five words. Emathia was a part of Macedonia.
  • bI take this as a forecast, by a god, of Lupus’ death and suggest two puns:—(a) on Lupus, lupus, a greedy kind of fish (a bass) in contrast with the shabar(?) and the Nileperch; (b) on ius, iura, ‘sauce’ or ‘soup’ and ius, iura, ‘justice’ (cp. Cic, Verr., II, 1, 46, 121) which the smaller fry in the end exercise on the giant.
  • cThe words of this line can be connected in various ways, and we do not know what Lucilius meant. For various views see Cichor, 234–6; Leo, H., XXIV, 69, n. 3; Ullman, CP. VIII, 178 ff.; Kappelmacher, in Pauly-Wissowa, s.v. Lucilius (4), 1035; Terzaghi, ed. Luc. Sat., p. 3.
