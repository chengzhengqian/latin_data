A neglected estate:

Donatus: ‘Stetisse’ . . . Lucilius—

The farm stands massed with thornbushes. a

1159

A grasping landlord:

Iulius Capitolinus: Pertinax as a private citizen was not unsuspected of being miserly, when, his tenants being hard pressed by accumulated interest on debts, he pushed forward his boundaries along the waters of the lake of Sabate. In fact he was dubbed—

a diving-bird b of the fields

from a line of Lucilius.

1160

Business:

Charisius: ‘Plure’ . . . Lucilius—

They go and sell out of doors at a greater price that which they bought at a lesser . . .

said in archaic fashion.
