Book XXIX

892

Nonius: ‘Putare,’ to appraise . . . —

“Please go on, and if you can, bring it about that I can count myself a woman worthy of you men.

893–5

Nonius: ‘Destinare’ (set up, put up, destine), to buy (only by implication) . . . —

“I do it; I come to the pimp. I am put up for sale into freedom at three thousand.” a

896

Nonius: ‘Eugium,’ a part midway within the private parts of a woman. Lucilius in [epodes]—

Hymnis without maidenhead b . . .

897

Nonius: ‘Obducere’ (draw over, draw across), to open. . .—

Meanwhile, you people, away with the light, and draw the curtains across.

Varro: ‘Spondere’ is to say ‘spondeo’ (I promise) from ‘sponte’ . . . and thus it is that Lucilius writes about Cretaea, c that when she came to his house to lie with him, she was led ‘sponte’ of her own free will to cast aside her tunic and the rest.

  • asc. drachmarum; cf. also Cichor., 172; Baehrens, H., L1V, 79.
  • btext probably corrupt; destina cannot be a ‘support’ here, as in Arnob., II, 92; Vitr., V, 12, 3, because of the lemma in Nonius. The attribution to book XXIX is mine.
  • cCretaea: ‘Cretan woman,’ used as a proper name.
