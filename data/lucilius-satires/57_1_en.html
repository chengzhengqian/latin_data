368–72

Vowels, a: c

Terentius Scaurus: Lucilius, laying down the rules of good spelling in the ninth book of the Satires, says—

‘a’ comes first; I will begin with this and then the letter-names which come after it. . . .

and then—

First ‘aa’ for the long, ‘a’ for the short syllable. d But we will spell both with one letter, and as we say now will write in one and the same way pācem Păcideianum, āridum ăcetum, e just as the Greeks do with ῏Αρες Ἄρες.
