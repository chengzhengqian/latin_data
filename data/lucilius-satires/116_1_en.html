Book XXVI

Sat. VI. Literary controversy. a Methods of certain writers are rejected.

720–1

Nonius: ‘Capere,’ to delight . . . —

Now likewise . . . the people together with such writers as these; I wanted to take their fancy.

722

Nonius: ‘Vescum’ (foodless; thin), minute, obscure . . . —

than to pass through life as a squeamish and a thin feeble fellow. b

723

Imitations of the tragic style:

Nonius: ‘Angues’ in the masculine gender . . . —

P c unless you all write about portents and flying winged snakes.

724–5

Nonius: The dative d instead of the accusative . . . —

P? If these persons actually show self-pity, look to it lest their case, being the better one, has put itself in that position.

  • aWith special allusions and references in parody to the tragic style of Pacuvius (Cichor., 127 ff.), marked here P.
  • bMarx attributes this line to a speech of Antiopa (of Pacuvius). But she would have used feminine adjectives.
  • callusion to Pacuvius, Medus, cf. Cichor., 136 ff.; Fiske, T.A.P., XL, 528, and Remains of Old Latin, Vol. II, pp. 254–5.
  • dNonius mistook ipsi for a dative. Cp. Fiske, 110, and id., in T.A.P., XL, 128. illorum causā (abl.) would suit the metre better. Krenkel suggests: superior(e) | e loco <tum>contioneris. I translate eo loco.
