Book VIII

Book VIII a

Sat. I. On women and men’s relations with them 324–5

The best woman for a man?

Nonius: ‘Gracila est’ for ‘gracilis est.’ Lucilius in the eighth book of the Satires—

because she is slender and nimble, because she has a pure heart, because she looks like a boy.

326

A simple house:

Nonius: ‘Postica’ . . . in the neuter gender . . . —

Built on to it is a pounding-mill, a backhouse, a store-room, and a kitchen.

327–7a

The good wife’s work:

Priscianus: ‘Panus’ . . . Lucilius in book VIII—

provided that, inside, the spindle stands upright and, outside, b a bobbin-full of weft.

328–9

Her pride?:

Nonius: ‘Priores’ and ‘primores’ . . . —

when a poultry-cock, winner in a fight, raises itself finely on its toes at full stretch and on its front claws.

  • aThis book certainly dealt with matters of sex, and probably with trades which ministered to table-luxury.
  • bthat is, all round, covering the spindle.
