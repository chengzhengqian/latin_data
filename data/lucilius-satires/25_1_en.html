an ill-favoured horse?:

Festus: ‘Petimina,’ a sores on the shoulders of beasts of burden. . . . Lucilius mentions them when he says—

so that the rider touches with his nose the horse’s sore or with his nape the buttocks.

But that this word was habitually applied also to the breast or the space that lies between the two fore-quarters of a pig is testified by Naevius.

  • aThis passage of Festus is our only source for the word petimen. In spite of his statement one might translate according to his second explanation. For Naevius’ passage cf. Remains of Old Latin, II, pp. 146–7.
