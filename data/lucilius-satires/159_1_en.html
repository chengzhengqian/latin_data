Book XXIX

985

(ii) Friendship:

Priscianus: Lucilius writes—

he may be helped by me.

‘auxiliatus’ passively, in Greek βοηθηθείς.

986

(iii) Eating and drinking:

Nonius: ‘Cima’ in the neuter, for example Lucilius—

soft asparaguses and green cabbage-sprouts.

987

Priscianus: Lucilius . . . in—

oil of Casinum pressed from green olives

used ‘Casinas’ (nom. sing, neut.) instead of ‘Casinātĕ.

988

Nonius: ‘Mandones,’ gluttons . . . Lucilius—

and the crunchers’ gullets a one and all.

989

Probus (on the forms austerus, austeris): When Lucilius says-

He was squeezing out the juices of the sourest herbs, b

then undoubtedly it is shown c that he uses the form ‘austeris’ nom. sing. masc. or fern.

  • aor, ‘all the gluttonies of the crunchers.’
  • bThis describes perhaps the preparation of a drug.
  • cno, it is not.
