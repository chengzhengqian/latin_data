507–8

(A) On horses (especially the breeds of Spain?)

What is useful?:

Nonius: ‘Paenula’ (overcloak) is a garment which we put on over the tunic. . . . Lucilius in bk. XV of the Satires—

If you ask me, an overcloak, a gelding, a slave, a straw-coat—I have more use for any one of these than I have for a wiseacre.
