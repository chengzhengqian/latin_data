Liber VI

This book, written probably about 115 b.c., contained, it seems, at least two satires. One of them has been taken (Fiske, Lucilius and Horace, 330 ff.), rightly I think, as the

Sat. I. 252–3

Porphyrio, ad Hor., S., I, 5, 87: ‘Oppidulo quod versu dicere non est.’ Aequum Tuticum significat . . . Hoc autem sub exemplo Lucili posuit; nam ille in sexto Saturarum sic ait—

Servorum est festus dies hic quem plane hexametro versu non dicere possis.
