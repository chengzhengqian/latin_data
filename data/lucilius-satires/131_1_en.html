814

The following belongs to this satire: fear of the satirist’s invective?:

Nonius: ‘Inritare’ is a term properly used for to provoke . . . —

. . . of Lucilius, if he should vex him in his affairs of love.

Sat. II. A Greek banquet (at Athens?); philosophy discussed. a

815

Nonius: ‘Ire,’ to recline at table . . . —

Chremes had gone to the middle, Demaenetus to the head couch of the feast. b

816

Nonius: ‘Pareutactoi’ . . . —

a certain stripling of the kind they call ‘in train- ing.’ c

817–8

The meal:

Nonius: ‘Interficere’ (kill), to consume, make an end of . . . —

“You have done ruin to a great mass of fish and fattened fowls.” “But I deny it.”

819

Discussions begin:

Nonius: ‘Interpellare’ tell to, inform . . . —

“But what are you up to? Come put in a word with me, so that I may know.”
