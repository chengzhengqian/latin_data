Book XXVI

726

Lycus and Dirce?:

Nonius: ‘Monstrificabile’ . . . —

P a Well now, to these men low birth is a wonder, a monstrifical thing.

727–8

Antiopa imprisoned:

Nonius: ‘Inluvies,’ dirt. . . . —

P b Here she is racked by hunger, and by cold Uncleanliness, bathlessness, washlessness, Uncaredforness.

729–30

Nonius: ‘Invidiosum’ (really enviable), the sort of thing which ought to be avoided as regards the eyesight . . . —

P c . . . a woman overwhelmed With deepest dirtiness and scabbiness, In direst want, not envied by the unfriendly, Nor to be longed for by a friend.

731

Antiopa? Ajax?:

Nonius: The accusative instead of the dative. . . . —

P d whether she should hang herself or fall upon a sword, lest she still drink the air.

  • auncertain, but it mocks at Pacuvius’ (?) ‘luctificabile’; cp. Cichor., 130; Fiske, T.A.P., XL, 129; Remains, Vol. II, pp.164–5.
  • b Remains, Vol. II, pp. 164–5; Pacuv., Antiopa.
  • csee preceding note.
  • dThis fr. may refer not to Antiopa but to Ajax, in Pacuvius’ Armorum Iudicium, Remains, II, pp. 178–9.
