Liber XXIX

That there were five satires in this book has been rightly argued by Marx (see his proleg., CVII-CXI), but we must reverse his order; the satires were written as follows:— I iambics, II mixed, III hexameters, IV iambics, V trochaics. (Nonius, 36, 5; 36, 10; 36, 18; 36, 25; 36, 27; cp. 245, 18; 248, 8). The book shows clearly how deeply the poet had imbibed the hellenistic influences in the Roman society of his day.
