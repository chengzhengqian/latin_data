Trinummus

cal I did and I don’t regret the deed.

meg To what faithlessness the young fellow was entrusted! Haven’t you given him a sword with which to kill himself? What else is it, what difference is there, when you130 give a lovesick young man without self-restraint money into his hands, with which he can complete building the ruin he has begun?

cal Shouldn’t I have paid him?

meg No, you shouldn’t have, and you shouldn’t have bought or sold anything from him or given him the means to be135 even more depraved. Haven’t you got the man who was entrusted to you into trouble and haven’t you thrown the man who entrusted him to you out of his house? Goodness, the job was placed into your care beautifully and carried out properly! Trust this chap with the guardianship: he’ll look after his own interests better.

cal Megaronides, with your harsh words you’re forcing me,140 and forcing me unexpectedly, so that what was secretly entrusted to my silence, my faith and reliability, so that I shouldn’t tell anyone or make it known—so that I can’t avoid entrusting it to you now.

meg Whatever you entrust me with you’ll be able to recover145 from where you placed it.

cal Just look around you to make sure that we don’t have any witness present, and please, look around again and again.

meg (doing so) I’m listening, if you’re saying anything. cal If you’re quiet, I’ll speak. When Charmides was about to go abroad, he showed me a treasure in this house, in a150 certain room here; but do look around.
