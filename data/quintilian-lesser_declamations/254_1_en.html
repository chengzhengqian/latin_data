317

A general challenged by his son

Whoso being challenged by the enemy does not fight, let him be capitally punished. A commander’s son deserted to the enemy. He challenged his father. The latter did not come down to fight alone but joined battle and defeated the enemy; in which battle his son too was killed. He is prosecuted because being challenged by the enemy he did not fight.

discussion

Ought whoever being challenged by the enemy does not fight to be punished? Does this law apply to a commander? Was he challenged by the enemy? Did he fight?
