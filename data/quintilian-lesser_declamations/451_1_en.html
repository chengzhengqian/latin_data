declamation

“The first heir,” she says, “was buried.” Who is this then? Where from? What makes him confident? “His age is the same.” Well, doesn’t this argument usually carry weight with you?27 But why does no one else among his coevals assert the same hope?28 I pass over this argument. “His figure is in his favor.”29 This too does not impress me: you too say as much. But let points like these carry weight when the face is battered: where we have the features, the particular eye color, the character of the hair, they are trivial. “But it was the resemblance that emboldened him.” Come, the slave girl recognizes him, doesn’t she?30 A humble persona to be sure, and her fortune detracts from her credibility.
