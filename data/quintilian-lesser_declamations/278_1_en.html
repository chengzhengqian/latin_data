declamation

I call upon the immortal gods, gentlemen of the jury, but especially the deity most familiar to me and, as we have lately seen, most present:5 first of all that, if I was led to do what I am charged with only by respect for sacred things, by piety, and by religion, they may will my priestly duty to go unpunished; next (and this sometimes makes me anxious) that they may be pleased to regard the spectacle of this trial, in which the dedication of a temple is expiated by mortal danger, without bitterness and wrath.
