So much as to law, now as to equity.

declamation

You want to opt twice, when even once is much. The law gave you power of life and death; that this license should last long is beyond all monarchy, beyond all tyranny. Even thunderbolts fall quickly. The executioner’s axe has a limit to expectancy. If you had opted for death and were wanting to opt for marriage, you would appear to be acting against the law, and somebody would say: “You should have thought about it before, looked into it before; the word has gone out, you have yourself terminated your power.” But now, having opted for marriage, you want to opt for death. What is this repentance into cruelty? She realized herself3 how savage, how cruel it is. Did I offend again afterwards? You forgave me for having raped you, you forgave me for taking away your virginity. You priced these things at a marriage, do you price bashfulness at death? And when I denied the rape, I was not refusing marriage with you; I wanted to wed you not as a rapist, since I thought I should be dearer to you that way, if I had not done you an injury. Or I wanted to deceive your feelings. You should not have been angry.
