I tax you in that you brought a third party to the dinner. If this underhand proceeding1 stems from family affection, it should have been secret. I tax you in that you brought a parasite of all people: did the unfortunate man, the exile, have time for dining with a parasite?

As to other matters, I realize that they are irrelevant to the present disowning. There are charges of earlier date, deserving of their own laws; but you should get away with them because they did <not> fall in your father’s way.2 I tax you with a plan to murder your brother. If I were prosecuting, if I were asking the sack3 for you, this is what I should have said: “The persona fits the crime. You have a parasite.” And the motive for the murder is clear; you are coheir with your brother. Opportunity aided the plan: he can be killed inside the borders. An agent was not lacking: the parasite is in your power. As for other things, who cannot see them? Would the parasite have dared insult your brother if you had not willed him? A man born to flatter, whose starvation only your godlike favor deferred, would never have done anything he thought might offend you. You’re asleep (let’s believe you on that for the present): did he think it could not be reported to you? And yet it’s unbelievable that you were asleep at that time, in that place: it was a dinner. And what sort of a dinner? Your brother was a guest.Would he have dared such abuse if he had seen you an affectionate brother?
