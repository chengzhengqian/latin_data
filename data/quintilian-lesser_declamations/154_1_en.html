285

General, exile’s son

Let a victorious general receive a reward. A general whose father was in five-year exile won a victory. He asked for another1 reward. The father returns after five years and disowns.

discussion

This father is both in good repute and in distress; <an exile,> he has a general for a son. Question: is the son accountable for the five years in which his father was in exile? Use the color: “By way of defense he argues that I was not an exile.2 But he was afraid that if I had been recalled, the command would be transferred.”
