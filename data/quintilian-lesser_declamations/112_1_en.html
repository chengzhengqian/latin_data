So children are not bound to do everything their father orders. There are many things that cannot be done; and judicial proceedings against disowners were constituted just because nature allowed that even fathers sometimes either erred or ordered unjustly.

discussion

This question can be supported by the following arguments: if you were to order your son <as a juror> to give a verdict contrary to his opinion or command that he bear witness about something of which he is ignorant or give his opinion in the senate <contrary to his own judgment>. These examples are more from public life, there for the taking. If <those others> derived from our freedom are to be recalled,3 if you were to order me to set fire to the Capitol, seize the castle, it is permissible to say: these are things that ought not to be done.

In the second place we ask whether he can be disowned for something he can do legally.
