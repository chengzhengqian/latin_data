In itself it was a cruel thing for your brother to die, but compared to me, it was tolerable. What then? Would I have died? “I saved my brother,” he says. So now your father disowns you.5 He owes you gratitude for a boon, I penalty for a wrong. But what if you were not doing it for family feeling but to kill me? As far as you were concerned, I was found guilty. “I appeared for you,” he says. If you had not, would you merely have been disowned6?

Exposed, on oath1 to marry his foster father’s daughter

A man on his death-bed promised a young man whom he had brought up as a son that he would tell him who his true parents were if he swore to marry the daughter whom he was leaving. The young man swore, the other told him. After the death of the foster father he was taken in by his natural father and is disowned because he refused to take a rich orphan as his wife.
