declamation

We had the same father; he left us the same liabilities, the same assets. Why are you rich? You have all the land, for which you have spent nothing, paid nothing down. I am bare and needy. That could be borne when I appeared to be on a level with you. <As it is,> you take the benefaction of the community away from me and leave me as I would have been if our father had left us nothing except debt.3 Even4 at the time I did it unwillingly; I had regard to the infirmity of my age. If that land had come to you by inheritance, you would not in human decency have failed to share it with your brother. Not only are we united by the ties of blood; necessity5 itself is something, partnership itself is something.
