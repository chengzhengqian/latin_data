Yet in these constraints, in these distresses, did she show the approaches by which they could get into the city, did she lay open our plans, the hidden intentions of the community? Under torture she threatened.7

“But the help sent by our allies was crushed.” If time serves here to bewail our losses in the war, gentlemen, we have lost more than that; so many of our bravest citizens also fell in the ranks that lie around that unfortunate woman’s son. But if that is the law of war and no victory was ever so bloodless but that it held some recollection of sadness, we have balanced all that with the victory that followed,8 so that her capture may seem to have occurred under favorable omens and a providence of the immortal gods on our behalf. The enemy would have got inside the community; the whole hostile army would have suddenly appeared in the vitals of the city itself as we slept deep, free of any thought of wars, and would not have left before they had burned and overturned and plundered.

Here, if you will, find fault with the woman’s courage and the strength she put into her love of country. She shook off her bonds; the bonds that the enemy had laid upon her (to use the mildest of words), she broke them, a woman, an old woman, tortured. Do you ask how courageously?
