But if anyone shall also think my meaning dark, you will defend me against him in this way: first, that such tours de force1 will go for nothing unless they are dark; secondly, that numbers are not like bulrushes, without knots;2 lastly, if you also find my meaning dark—you who have left nothing unconned, nothing unconquered—then indeed I shall be happy in attaining what I have sought after, to make you want me, long for me, think of me. Thrice drink or else as many times three cups: thus stands the mystic law—whether three draughts thou drinkest or three thrice multipliest, with nine times three uneven form the cube!3
