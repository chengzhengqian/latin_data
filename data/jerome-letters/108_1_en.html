Why do we admire that which Christ calls the Mammon of unrighteousness?2 Why do we respect and love that which Peter proclaims he does not possess?3 Moreover, if we follow only the letter and find pleasure in the bare lists of riches and gold, let us keep to everything else together with the gold: let Christ’s priests take virgins as wives; let a man be deprived of his priesthood, however honest he be, if he is scarred or disfigured in any way;4 let bodily leprosy be counted worse than spiritual faults; let us increase and multiply and replenish the earth; let us slay no lamb and celebrate no mystic passover, for the law forbids these things where there is no temple; let us pitch a tent in the seventh month and with a trumpet noise abroad the solemn fast. But if all these things are spiritual, and we compare them with things spiritual, and know with Paul that the Law is spiritual, and chant David’s words: ‘Open thou mine eyes that I may behold wondrous things out of thy law,’5 understanding them as Our Lord understood them when He thus explained the Sabbath; then we should reject the gold together with the rest of Jewish superstition, or, if we approve of the gold, we should approve of the Jews as well. The Jews must go with the gold whether we approve or condemn.
