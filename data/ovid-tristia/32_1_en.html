But even if our house be small in wealth and in origin, at least my genius does not suffer it to be obscure. This I may have employed in too youthful exuberance, yet my name is great throughout the world; a throng of the cultured are well acquainted with Naso and venture to count him with those whom they do not despise.

121 Fallen then is my house, though pleasing to the Muses, beneath one charge albeit no small one—yet so fallen that it can rise again, if only time shall mellow the wrath of injured Caesar whose leniency in the choice of my penalty is such that the penalty is milder than I feared. Life was granted me; thy wrath halted ere it achieved my death: O sire, with what restraint hast thou used thy power! Then too there is added—for thou takest it not away—my inherited wealth, as if life were too small a gift. Thou didst not condemn my deeds through a degree of the senate nor was my exile ordered by a special court. With words of stern invective—worthy of a prince—thou didst thyself, as is fitting, avenge thine own injury. And thy command, though severe and threatening, was yet mild in naming my punishment, for it calls me relegatus, not exile, and thou dost use therein language especially adapted to my fate.1
