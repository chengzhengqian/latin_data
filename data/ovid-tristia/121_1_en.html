IX. In Gratitude

O hadst thou but allowed thy name to be set in my verse, how oft wouldst thou have been named! Of thee alone would I have sung in memory of thy service; in my books no page would have been completed without thee. My debt to thee would be known throughout the city—if I, an exile, am still read in the city I have lost. Thy kindness the present, thy kindness later time would know, if only my writings endure age, nor would the accomplished reader cease to bless thee; this honour would abide with thee for having preserved a poet. Caesar’s gift—that I draw breath—comes first; after the mighty gods it is to thee that I must render thanks.
