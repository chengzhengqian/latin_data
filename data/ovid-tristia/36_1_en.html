In fine, though the body of the empire is vaster than has ever existed, no part is weak. The city also wearies thee, and the guardianship of thy laws and of the morals which thou dost desire to be like thine own, nor to thy lot falls that repose thou bestowest upon the nations, for thou art waging unremitting war against vice.

237 Can I wonder, then, that under this weight of great affairs thou hast never unrolled the volume of my jests? Yet if, as I could wish, thou hadst changed to have the leisure, thou wouldst have read no crimes in my “Art.” That poem, I admit, has no serious mien, it is not worthy to be read by so great a prince; but not for that reason is it opposed to the commandments of the law, nor does it offer teaching to the daughters of Rome. And that thou may’st not doubt for whom I write the books, one of the three has these four verses:2 “Far from me! ye narrow fillets, badge of modesty! and thou, long ruffle3 covering half the feet! I shall sing of naught but what is lawful, of loves which men allow. There shall be in my song no sin.” Have I not strictly excluded from this “Art” all women whom the assumption of the robe and fillet of wedlock protect?
