Epitaphs

IX.—For Pyrrhus

At the world’s centre1 I am buried, greater in prowess than my father, seeing that while yet a boy I, Pyrrhus, won a king’s2 own spoils.

3

Orestes slew me before the altar, adding sacrilege to treachery—what wonder, when he was raving from his mother’s murder?

X.—For Euryalus3

I, too, am not unworthy of an epitaph; so Pleuronia holds, which was the common home of Euryalus and Sthenelus with Diomede. I was the third who held that realm, wherein a grave alone contents me now.

XI.—For Gunes4

The sea holds Gunes; this tomb, his name but not his body. His fame dwells amongst men; his spirit is returned above. All elements unite to form one tomb for so great a leader. Which? Heaven, earth, and sea, and the breath of men.
