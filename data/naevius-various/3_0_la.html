Naevius

Additional notes on the fragments of Naevius

I. Important work has been done by S. Mariotti, Contributi al testo dei frammenti scenici di Nevio. Studi Urbinati di Storia, Filos., e Lett., N.S. XXIV, B 1–2, 1950, 174–190.

II. The following may belong to Naevius. Varro, L.L., VII, 7: Caelum qua attuimur dictum templum . . . id est ut ait Naevius—

hemisphaerium ubi conca <vo> caerulo septum stat.

concavo K. O. Mueller conca cdd.

Translate:

The sky, where we look at it, came to be called ‘templum’ . . . that is, as Naevius says—

where (land’s) semicircle stands fenced by the hollow vault of blue.

III. On Naevius’ quarrel with the Metelli (pp. xv–xvi), see Laura Robinson, Freedom of Speech in the Roman Republic, 1940.
