Equity is also said to have three parts: one pertains to the gods in heaven, the second to the spirits of the departed, the third to men. The first is called piety, the second respect, the third justice or equity.

There are three kinds of speeches on special subjects: the judicial, the deliberative, and the encomiastic; and the “ends “of these three show what topics are to be used. The end of the judicial speech is justice, from which it also derives its name. But the parts of justice were enumerated when we discussed equity.a The end of a deliberative speech is advantage, and the divisions of this subject have just now been enumerated.b The end of an encomiastic speech is honour, and this, 92too, was discussed above.c But particular inquiries are built up of topics which are the peculiar property, as it were, of each one.
