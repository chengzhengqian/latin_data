“Yet after all this is enough,” he said, “I have lived and I die in thy friendship, Caesar; and, as I die, it is enough. From thy kindly eyes some drop will fall, when thou art told the sudden news that I am gone. This be my lot, to lie ’neath the impartial earth: nor yet would I have thee longer grieve for this. But I would wish for remembrance: there in thy talk would I live; for I shall always exist, if thou wilt always remember me. ’Tis fitting so, and I shall surely live for thee in affection ever; thy dying friend ceases not to be thine own. Myself, whatever I shall be among the ashes and the embers, e’en then I shall not be able to forget Caesar. ’Tis thanks to thee I have lived the luxurious pattern of bliss, thanks to thee that I was the one Maecenas of the day. I was my own controller: I willed to be what fell to my lot: a I was truly the heart of thine own heart.
