And do you dare to attack any man’s character, you who are forced to admit that you were so blinded by passion and avarice that, although you did not know what was going to happen afterwards—and many things might have happened—you founded your hopes of gain from a present crime on the uncertain issues of the future? XXVII. All these facts, Gaius Aquilius, are of such84 a kind that anyone can clearly see that in this cause rascality and influence are contending against helplessness and integrity. How did the praetor order you to take possession? According to his edict, I suppose. In what terms was the “wager” or “engagement” drawn up? If the goods of Publius Quinctius have not been taken possession of according to the praetor’s edict. To return to the edict. In what manner does it order possession to be taken? If Naevius took possession in quite a different way from what the praetor ordered, can it be disputed that he did not take possession according to the edict, and that I have won the wager? Certainly not, I imagine. Let us examine the edict. Those who have entered into possession according to my edict.
