Pro Publio Quinctio, xix.

He who has not been legally defended in his absence. Nor even that.a When or how, Naevius, do you think that Quinctius ought to have been defended in his absence? At the time when you made application to the praetor to take possession of the property? There was certainly no one there then, for no one could foresee that you would make such a request, nor was it anyone’s business to object to what the praetor ordered, not to be done, but to be done in accordance with his edict.b What61 then was the first opportunity the agent had of defending the absent man? When you advertised the sale of the property. Then Sextus Alfenus came forward; he did not allow this; he tore down the placards. His first duty as an agent was most carefully discharged by him.

Let us see what followed next. You seized a slave belonging to Quinctius in the street and attempted to carry him off. Alfenus refused to allow it, he took him away from you by force and had him taken back to Quinctius’s house. In this also the duty of a zealous agent is shown to have been admirably performed. You assert that Quinctius is in your debt, his agent denies it; you wish to bind him over to appear in court, he promises to appear; you summon him before the magistrate,c he follows you; you demand a trial, he does not refuse it.
