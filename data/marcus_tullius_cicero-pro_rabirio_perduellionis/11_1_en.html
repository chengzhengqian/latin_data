Once again, I ask you personally, Labienus:21 seeing that it was the consuls who, acting on a decree of the Senate, had issued the call to arms; that Marcus Aemilius,d the president of the Senate, had taken his stand in the Assembly with arms in his hand (for though he could hardly set foot to the ground, he thought that his lameness would be no hindrance to him in pursuit but only in flight) and that Quintus Scaevola,e hampered though he was by old age, incurably ill, disabled, crippled, and infirm in every limb, was displaying as he leaned upon his spear at once his mental vigour and his bodily weakness:

  • aIt is probable that private citizens were not allowed to keep arms.
  • bHis real name was Equitius; see Introduction, para. 2.
  • cThe Order of Knights was given this privilege by Gaius Gracchus in 123 b.c. and deprived of it by Sulla in 81 b.c.
  • dMarcus Aemilius Scaurus, a leader of the senatorial party much admired by Cicero, is said to have supplanted Saturninus in some government appointment and thereby to have thrown him into the arms of the extremists.
  • eQuintus Mucius Scaevola, surnamed the augur, to distinguish him from his kinsman the pontifex maximus. Both were great jurists.
