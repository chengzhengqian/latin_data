Could I know that and not proclaim it? I would be ungrateful. 44On the contrary, I express and I feel the warmest possible thanks to your whole legislature for its enormous kindnesses to me. You have honored me by most handsome acclamations in a council house where the mere mention of one’s name is a high honor.

45And so, hard as it was to do, difficult in reality and not merely in appearance—to please the populace, satisfy the legislature, gain the approval of the magistrates and the dignitaries—in this (if I may say so without boasting) I have now in a way succeeded. 46What therefore is left to complete the honor of my statue, other than determining the price of the bronze and the service of the sculptor? Things that have never been refused me even in lesser cities can hardly be denied me in Carthage, where even in connection with weightier issues the most distinguished senate is more used to making decisions than calculations.52 47But on that subject my speech will be more polished when your plan is more advanced. Yes, to you, noble senators, distinguished citizens, honorable friends, soon, when my statue is dedicated I will also sing a hymn of thanks in a written work, and I will give that work a mission: 48to travel through every province, and thereafter through the whole world, and for all future time, to renew my praise of your kindness in every land and down all the years.
