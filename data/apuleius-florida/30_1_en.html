FLORIDA

17. Let that be the concern of those who have a way of thrusting themselves on busy governors, so that by their unwillingness to keep silent they may earn praise for their talent, and may pride themselves on a false appearance of having your friendship; since both practices are not my way at all, Scipio Orfitus.53 2For my talent, however slight, has long been too well known to the world, as far as my abilities allow, to need further commendation; 3and I would rather possess than flaunt the favor of you and those like you, and I prefer to aspire to such a lofty friendship rather than to boast about it, since no one can aspire without sincerity, but anyone may boast without truth.

4Furthermore, I have always from my earliest youth devoted myself tirelessly to liberal studies; and both in our province and among your friends at Rome, I have earned such a reputation for character and learning, as you yourself are more qualified than anyone to testify, that all of you must covet my friendship no less than I must wish for yours. 5For being reluctant to excuse someone for the rarity of his visits is a sign that you miss his attentions, and the highest proof of affection is to be pleased by regular visitors and to be vexed by infrequent ones, to praise the faithful friend and to miss the one who no longer comes; since inevitably one who gives pleasure by his presence also pains by his absence.

6But still, a voice locked in perpetual silence will be no more useful than nostrils filled with mucus, ears blocked with dirt, eyes dimmed by cataract. 7What happens when hands are pinioned with handcuffs, feet are locked together
