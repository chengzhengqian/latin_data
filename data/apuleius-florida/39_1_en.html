FLORIDA

19. The famous Asclepiades, the greatest of physicians with the sole exception of Hippocrates, and superior to all the rest, was also the first to find how to treat the sick with wine, of course only by giving it at the right time, a juncture he was highly skilled at observing, since he could detect an uneven or an irregular pulse very precisely. 2Well, he happened to be returning to the city from his suburban estate when he noticed a large funeral prepared outside the city boundary, and a huge crowd of people standing around to attend the last rites, all of them very downcast and in very shabby clothing. 3He came closer, either from a human curiosity to learn who it might be, since he had inquired and got no reply, or else to observe something in the man for himself by his expertise. Actually, though the man was laid out and almost buried, he canceled his doom. 4For after surveying all the poor man’s body when it was already sprinkled with perfumes, his face already daubed with fragrant ointment, his body already washed, already all but prepared for the flames, 5and after carefully observing certain signs, he ran his hands several times over the man’s body and found hidden life in it. 6Immediately he shouted, “The man is alive, so throw away the torches, scatter the fire, dismantle the pyre, move the funeral banquet from the grave to the table.” 7Meanwhile mutterings arose: some said that a medical man deserved belief, some even mocked the art of medicine.
