No, gentlemen, this is certainly not the case; it is121 improbable that Chrysogonus took a fancy to their culture and good manners or that he appreciated their diligence and fidelity in his household affairs. There is something behind this, which, the greater the efforts made by them to suppress and conceal it, the more evident and conspicuous it becomes.
