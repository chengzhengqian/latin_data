XXXVIII. Capito came to the camp as one of the109 deputies among the ten heads of the decurions.a Now learn thoroughly the manner of life, the nature and character of the man from his behaviour on the deputation alone. If you are not convinced, gentlemen, that there is no duty, no right so sacred and inviolable that his wickedness and perfidy have not violated and trampled under foot, why then you may judge him to be a most honourable man.
