XIX. But you yourself are aware that such arguments are worthless. Now let us consider what we began to speak of, which is the strongest proof of hatred that can possibly be found: “the father intended 53 to disinherit the son.” I do not ask for what reason, I ask how you know it. Certainly you ought to have stated and enumerated all the reasons, and it would have been the duty of a conscientious accuser, whose object it was to convict anyone of such a crime, to set forth all the vices and transgressions of the son, by which the father could have been so enraged as to bring himself to overcome his natural feelings, to drive out of his mind that love so deeply rooted in it, and, lastly, to forget that he was a father, which it seems to me could never have happened without the gravest transgressions on the part of my client.

However, I give you permission to pass over these54 faults, which by your silence you admit are nonexistent; as for your affirmation that he intended to disinherit his son, you certainly ought to prove it. What then can you bring forward to convince us that such was his intention?
