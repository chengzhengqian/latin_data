Consolation III

VI

All human kind on earth arises from the same origin; There is one Father of all things, one who looks after all. He gave the sun his rays, the moon her horns, He peopled too the earth with men as the sky with constellations; He locked into limbs spirits brought down from their high abode. So did a noble seed produce all mortals. Why shout about your lineage or your forbears? If you consider Your beginnings and God your Author, no man is now degenerate Save who embracing baser things in vice forsakes his proper origin.

VII

What shall I say about the pleasures of the body, the longing for which is full of anxiety, the satisfac-tion of which full of regret? What dreadful diseases, what unbearable pains they generally cause in the bodies of those enjoying them, as a kind of fruit of their wickedness! What pleasure there is in stirring them up, I do not know; but that these pleasures have a bitter end, anyone will understand who is willing to recall his own lusts.
