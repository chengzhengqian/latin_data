Verses I made once glowing with content; Tearful, alas, sad songs must I begin. See how the Muses grieftorn bid me write, And with unfeigned tears these elegies drench my face. For age has come unlooked for, hastened by ills, And anguish sternly adds its years to mine; My head is white before its time, my skin hangs loose About my tremulous frame: I am worn out. Death, if he come Not in the years of sweetness But often called to those who want to end their misery Is welcome. My cries he does not hear; Cruel he will not close my weeping eyes.
