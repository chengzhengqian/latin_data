Fish of no less size also occur in certain rivers, the catfish in the Nile,a the salmon in the Rhine, the sturgeon in the Po, a fish that grows so fat from sloth that it sometimes reaches a thousand pounds; it is caught with a hook on a chain and only drawn out of the water by teams of oxen. And this monster is killed by the bite of a very small fish called the lamprey which goes for a particular vein in its throat with remarkable voracity. The wels ranges about and goes for every living creature wherever it is, often dragging down horses when swimming.
