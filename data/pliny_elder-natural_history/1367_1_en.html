LXI. Now we ought also to speak of the difference ofJuices of herbs. the juices and flavours of herbs, this being even greater in their case than in fruits.c The juice of savory, wild marjoram, cress and mustard has an acrid taste; the juice of wormwood and centaury is bitter, that of cucumbers, gourds and lettuces watery; that of thyme and cunilago pungent; that of parsley, dill and fennel pungent and scented. The only flavour not found in plants is the taste of salt, though occasionally it is present as a sort of external layer, like a dust, and this only in the case of the chickling vetch.
