VII. The globules growing on the hard-wood oakHard-wood oak globules. mixed withb bear’s grease restore hair lost through mange.c The leaves, bark and acorns of the TurkeyTurkey Oak. oak dry up gatherings and suppurations, and check fluxes. Paralysed parts of limbs are strengthened by fomenting with a decoction of it, which as a sitz bath is useful for drying and bracing these parts. The root of this tree counteracts the poison of scorpions.

VIII. The bark of the cork-tree, pounded and takenCork. in hot water, arrests haemorrhage from either partd of the body, and the ashes of the same taken in heated wine are highly praised as a cure for spitting of blood.
