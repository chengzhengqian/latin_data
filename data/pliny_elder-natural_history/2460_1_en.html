But such statements only make it easier to pardon their ignorance of amber when their ignorance of geography is so great. More cautious but equally misguided writers have described how on inaccessible rocks at the head of the Adriatic there stand trees which at the rising of the Dog Star shed this gum. Theophrastus states that amber is dug up in Liguria,ade Lap. 29. while Chares states that Phaethon died in Ethiopia on an island the Greek name of which is the Isle of Amnion, and that here is his shrine and oracle, and here the source of amber. Philemon declares that it is a mineral which is dug up in two regions of Scythia, in one of which it is of a white, waxy colour and is called ‘electrum,’ while in the other it is tawny and known as ‘sualiternicum.’b Demonstratusc calls amber ‘lyncurium,’ or ‘lynx-urine,’ and§ 52. alleges that it is formed of the urine of the wild beasts known as lyxnes, the males producing the kind that is tawny and fiery in colour, and the females, that which is fainter and light in colour. According to him, others call it ‘langurium’ and state that the beasts, which live in Italy, are ‘languri.’ Zenothemisd calls the same beasts ‘langes’ and assigns them a habitat on the banks of the Po, while SudinesXXXVI. 59. writes that a tree which produces amber in Liguria is called ‘lynx.’ Metrodoruse also holds the same opinion.
