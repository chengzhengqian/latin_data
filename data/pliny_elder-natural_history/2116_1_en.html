XXVI. They say that a camel’s brain, dried andRemedies from the camel. taken in vinegar, cures epilepsy, as does the gall taken with honey, this being also a remedy for quinsy; that the tail when dried is laxative, and that the ash of the burnt dung makes the hair curl.a This ash applied with oil is also good for dysentery, as is a three-finger pinch taken in drink, and also for epilepsy. They say that the urine is very useful to the fullers, and for running ulcers—it is a fact that foreigners keep it for five years, and use heminadoses as a purgative—and that the tail hairs plaited into an amulet for the left arm cure quartan fevers.
