Close attention to the facts will make this clear; for first of all blight is never found to occur except at night and before the sun gives any heat, and it depends entirely on the phases of the moon, since damage of this sort only takes place at the moon’s conjunction or at full moon, that is, when the moon’s influence is powerful—for the moon is at the full at both phases, as we have often said, but at the point of its conjunctionII. 46. it reflects hack to the sky all the light it has received from the sun. The difference between the two phases is great, but it is obvious: the moon is hottest in summer and cold in winter at the conjunction, whereas on the contrary when full it makes the nights cold in summer and warm in winter.
