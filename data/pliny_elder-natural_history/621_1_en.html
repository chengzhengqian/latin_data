XL. There is a second class of flatfish that hasBoneless varieties of flatfish. gristle instead of a backbone, for instance rays, prickly rays, angel-sharks, the electric ray, and those the Greek names for which mean ‘ox,’ ‘sorceress,’ ‘eagle’ and ‘frog.’ This group includes the squalus also, although that is not a flatfish. These Aristotle designated in Greek by the common name of selachians, giving them that name for the first time; but we cannot distinguish them as a class unless we like to call them the cartilaginea. But all such fish are carnivorous, and they feed lying on their backs, as we said in the case of dolphins; and whereas all other fish are oviparous, this kind alone with the exception of the species called the fishing-frog is viviparous, like the creatures termed cetaceans.
