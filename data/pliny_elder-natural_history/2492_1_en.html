Our most recent authorities assert that the stone is found also near Alabastrum, a town in the Thebaid, and divide it into two varieties, the ‘prasoides,’ or ‘leek-like,’ and the ‘chrysopteros,’ or ‘golden-feathered,’b of which the latter resembles the ‘chrysoprasus.’ In general, the colour tends to resemble§ 113. the tints of the leek. Incidentally, the peridot is the largest of gemstones. Also, it is the only precious stone that is affected by an iron file, whereas all others have to be smoothed with Naxian stonec and emery. Moreover, peridot is worn away by use.
