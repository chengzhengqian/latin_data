In old days the vines were never thought to beDates of vintage and use of wine press. ripe for the vintage before the equinox, but nowadays I notice they are commonly pulled at any time; consequently we must also specify the times for this by their signs and indications. The rulesc are as follows: ‘Do not pick a bunch of grapes when they are warm’—that is during unbroken dry weather, with no rain in between; ‘Do not pick a bunch of grapes if wet with dew’, that is if there has been dew in the night, and not before it has been dispelled by the sun. ‘Begin the vintage when the grape-shoot begins to droop down to the stem, or when after a grape has been removed from a cluster it has been clearly noticed that the gap does not fill up and that the grapes are no longer getting bigger.’ It is a very great advantage for the vintage to coincide with a crescent moon. One pressingd ought to fill twenty wine-skinse: that is a fair basis. A single wine press is enough for twenty wine-skins and vats to serve twenty acres of vineyard. Some press the grapes with a single press-beam, but it pays better to use a pair, however large the single beams may be.
