Book XXXVI

The largest pyramid is made of stone from stoneThe pyramid of Cheops. from the Arabian quarries.a It is said that 360,000 men took 20 years to build it. The time taken to build all three was 88 years and 4 months.b The authors who have written about them, namely Herodotus,Hdt. ii. 124. Euhemerus, Duris of Samos, Aristagoras, Dionysius, Artemidorus, Alexander Polyhistor, Butoridas, Antisthenes, Demetrius, Demoteles and Apion, are not all agreed as to which kings were responsible for their construction, since chance, with the greatest justice, has caused those who inspired such a mighty display of vanity to be forgotten. Some of the writers mentioned record that 1600 talents were spent on radishes, garlic and onions alone. The largest pyramid covers an area of nearly 5 acres. Each of the four sides has an equal measurement from corner to corner of 783 feet; the height from ground-level to the pinnacle amounts to 725 feet,c while the circumference of the pinnacle is 16 1/2 feet. As for the second pyramid, each of its sidesThe pyramid of Chephren. The pyramid of Mycerinus. from corner to corner totals 757½ feet. The third is smaller than those already mentioned, but on the other hand is far more splendid, with its Ethiopian stoned towering to a height of 363 feet along its sloping sides between the corners.   • aPliny means limestone from ‘the Arabian Hills’ (see p. 46, n. d) on the E. side of the Nile. Only the casing was made of this limestone.
  • bOr possibly ‘all three were built within a period of 88 years and 4 months.’
  • cThis figure is meant to apply to the height along the sloping sides, as with the pyramid of Mycerinus. The original perpendicular height was 481 feet.
  • dThe lower part of this pyramid was faced with Aswan granite.
  • eThe ‘lentils’ are nummulites, small fossils from the disintegrated limestone casing of the pyramids (Stanley Smith).
