XLVII. It is now high time to pass on to stonesWhetstones. used in industry, and first of all to whetstones intended for sharpening iron. Of these there are many varieties. Cretan whetstones for long enjoyed the highest reputation, the second place being held by the Laconian from Mount Taygetus. Both kinds need to be lubricated with oil. Among those used with water the Naxian came first in merit, and then the Armenian,d both of which were mentioned§ 54. earlier. Cilician whetstones are effective if used with oil and water mixed, and those of Arsinoee if used with water alone.
