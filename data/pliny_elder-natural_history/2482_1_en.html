In the case of men, it is an individual’s caprice that sets a value upon an individual stone, and, above all, the rivalry that ensues. A case in point is that of the Emperor Claudius, when he took to wearing a ‘smaragdus’ or a sardonyx. But according to Demostratus, the first Roman to adopt a sardonyx§ 34. was the elder Africanus, and hence arose the esteem236–181 b.c. which this gemstone enjoys at Rome. And so it is to this stone that I shall award the next place after the opal.

Formerly, as is clear from the very name, sardonyxaSardonyx. meant a stone with a layer of carnelian resting on a layer of white, that is, like flesh superimposed on a human finger-nail, both parts of the stone being translucent. Such is the character of the Indian sardonyx according to Ismenias, Demostratus, Zenothemis and Sotacus.
