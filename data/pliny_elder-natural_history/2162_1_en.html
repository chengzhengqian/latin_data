Consumptives are benefited by wolf’s liver in thin wine, by the lard of a sow fed on herbs, and by ass’s flesh taken in its gravy. This treatment for the complaint is very popular in Achaia. The smoke also from dried dung of an ox fed on green fodder, inhaled through a reed, is said to be beneficial, or the burnt tip of the horn of an ox, the dose being two spoonfuls, with the addition of honey, swallowed in pills. It is held by not a few authorities that by she-goat’s suet in groat porridge consumption and cough are cured, or by fresh suet melted with honey wine, an ounce of suet added to a cyathus of wine and stirred with a spray of rue. An authoritativea writer assures us that a despaired-of consumptive has recovered by being treated with a cyathus of mountain-goat suet and the same amount of the milk. Some have written that pig’s dung reduced to ash, taken in raisin wine, has proved of value, or the lung of a stag, especially a subulo,b dried in smoke and pounded in wine.
