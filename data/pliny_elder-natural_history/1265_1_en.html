Book XVIII

Nature, under the head of which we will register the opinion of Ciceroa in his own words:

The mastich, ever green and ever teeming, Is wont to swell with thrice-repeated produce: Thrice bearing fruit, she marks three ploughing seasons.

One of these seasons, this last one, is the same also for sowing flax and poppy. For poppy Cato givesR. R. XXVIII. 4. the following rule: ‘On land used for corn burn any twigs and brushwood left over from your utilization of them. Sow poppy in the place where you have burnt them’. Wild poppy boiled in honey is wonderfully serviceable for making throat-cures, and also cultivated poppy is a powerful soporific. So far as to winter sowing.
