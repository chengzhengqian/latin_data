Next in esteem to the Cyprian ‘smaragdi’ comeEthiopian (Egyptian) emeralds. the Ethiopian, which, according to Juba, are found at a distance of twenty-five days’ journeying from Coptos, and are bright green, although they are rarely flawless or uniform in tint.b Democritus includesOther varieties of ‘smaragdus.’ in this class the Thermiaean and Persian stones.c He states that the former are massived and convex, while the Persian stones, although they are not transparent, satisfy the eye with their agreeably uniform colour without allowing it to see within. He compares them to the eyes of cats and leopards, which likewise shine without being transparent, and mentions, moreover, that the stones are dimmed in sunlight, glisten in shadow and shine farther than other stones.
