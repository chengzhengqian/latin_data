XXV. There is also pissasphaltos, that is pitchPissasphaltos combined with bitumen, found in a natural state in the territory of Apollonia;a it is sometimes made artificially. It is a specific for itch-scab in cattle and for the sores caused by the young on the teats of their mothers. The best partb of it is that which floats on the surface when it is boiled.

XXVI. Zopissa, as I have said,c is scraped off ships,Zopissa. wax being soaked in sea brine. The best is taken from ships after their maiden voyage. It is also added to poultices to disperse gatherings.

XXVII. A decoction in vinegar of pitch pined makes an efficacious wash for aching teeth.

XXVIII. Of the lentisk tree the seed, bark andThe lentisk gum-drops are diuretic, and astringent to the bowels. A decoction of them is a useful fomentation for creeping sores. It makes a liniment for moist sores and also for erysipelas, and it rinses the gums. The leaves are rubbed on the teeth whene they ache; loose teeth are rinsed with the decoction, which also dyes the hair.
