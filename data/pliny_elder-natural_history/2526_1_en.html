The ‘anancitis,’ or ‘compulsive stone,’ it is said, is used in divination by water to conjure up divine apparitions, while the ‘synochitis,’ or ‘holding stone,’ so we are told, holds the shades of the dead when they have been summoned from below. As for the white ‘dendritis,’ or ‘tree stone,’ it is said that if it is buried beneath a tree that is being felled the edges of the axes will not be blunted. There are many more stones that are even more magical; and these have received foreign names from men who have thus betrayed the fact that they are ordinary, worthless stones, and not precious stones at all.c But I shall here remain content with having exposed the abominable falsehoods of the Magi.
