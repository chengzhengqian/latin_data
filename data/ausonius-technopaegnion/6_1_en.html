IX.—On Articles of Food

I will tell also of our articles of food, as fixed by ancient use, to which for relish we add sea-born salt. Of old food for beast and man alike was furnished by the oak,5 ere that in fields there was store of wheaten ears. Next came spelt, and from spelt pottage-making meal, that ’mid the sons of Romulus furnishes the tables of the common folk. Thereafter, food and drink both (when mixed with water), pulse.
