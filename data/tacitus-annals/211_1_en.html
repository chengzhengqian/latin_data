The Spains, finally subdued not long before,2 were kept by three. Mauretania, by the national gift, had been transferred to King Juba.3 Two legions4 held down the remainder of Africa; a similar number, Egypt: then, from the Syrian marches right up to the Euphrates, four sufficed for the territories enclosed in that enormous reach of ground; while, on the borders, the Iberian, the Albanian,5 and other monarchs, were secured against alien power by the might of Rome.
