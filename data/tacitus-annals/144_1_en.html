The bier had been surrounded with the family effigies of the Claudian and Livian2 houses; the dead had been mourned in the Forum,3 eulogized upon the Rostra; every distinction which our ancestors had discovered, or their posterity invented, was showered upon him. But to Germanicus had fallen not even the honours due to every and any noble! Granted that the length of the journey was a reason for cremating his body, no matter how, on foreign soil, it would only have been justice that he should have been accorded all the more distinctions later, because chance had denied them at the outset. His brother4 had gone no more than one day’s journey to meet him; his uncle not even to the gate. Where were those usages of the ancients—the image placed at the head of the couch, the set poems to the memory of departed virtue, the panegyrics, the tears, the imitations (if no more) of sorrow?”
