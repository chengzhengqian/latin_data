His easy accessibility, on the other hand, and his unreserved courtesy—virtues unknown to Parthia—were construed as exotic vices; and the good and ill in him, as they were equally strange to the national character, were impartially abhorred.

III. Consequently Artabanus,4 an Arsacian of the blood, who had grown to manhood among the Dahae,5 was brought into the lists, and, though routed in the first engagement, rallied his forces and seized the kingdom.
