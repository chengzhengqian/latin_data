Book XIII

LVIII. In the same year, the tree in the Comitium, known as the Ruminalis,1 which eight hundred and thirty years earlier had sheltered the infancy of Remus and Romulus, through the death of its boughs and the withering of its stem, reached a stage of decrepitude which was regarded as a portent, until it renewed its verdure in fresh shoots.

  • 1The fig-tree under which the wolf suckled the twins. It migrated spontaneously—augurante Atto Navio—from the Lupercal on the Palatine to the Comitium, opposite the senate-house (Plin. H. N. XV. 18, 77).
