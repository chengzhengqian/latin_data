I. In the consulate of Rubellius and Fufius, botha.v.c. 782 = a.d. 29 surnamed Geminus, Julia Augusta departed this life in extreme old age;1 by membership of the Claudian family and by adoption into the Livian and Julian houses,2 associated with the proudest nobility of Rome. Her first marriage and only children were to Tiberius Nero;3 who, exiled in the Perusian War,4 returned to the capital on the conclusion of peace5 between Sextus Pompeius and the triumvirate. In the sequel, Augustus, smitten by her beauty, took her from her husband. Her regrets are doubtful, and his haste was such that, without even allowing an interval for her confinement, he introduced her to his hearth while pregnant. After this, she had no issue; but the union of Agrippina and Germanicus created a blood connection between herself and Augustus, so that her great-grandchildren were shared with the prince.6 In domestic virtue she was of the old school, though her affability went further than was approved by women of the elder world.
