Accordingly in his reply, he first explained141 the requirements of the two occasions to show that his speech had been designed to suit the facts of either case; next, in order to let Brutus see what manner of man he had provoked, and how gifted not only with eloquence but with wit and humour, he in his turn produced three readers each carrying one of the treatises on law left by M.
