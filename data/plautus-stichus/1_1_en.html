The Stichus by Titus Maccius Plautus. Greek play: Menander’s Adelphoe. Staged at the Plebeian Games when Gnaeus Baebius and Gaius Terentius were plebeian aediles. 1 5Put on stage by Titus Publilius Pellio. Set to music by Marcipor, slave of Oppius, in its entirety for the Sarranian flute.2 10∗ ∗ ∗ when Gaius Sulpicius and Gaius Aurelius were consuls.
  • 1Aediles were Roman officials in charge of public games and markets; the plebeian aediles, as opposed to the curule aediles, were the ones chosen by the common people.
  • 2The Sarranian flute, named after the Phoenician city Sarra (Sor), better known as Tyre, has two pipes of equal length.
