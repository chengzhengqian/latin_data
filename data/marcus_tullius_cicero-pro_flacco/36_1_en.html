A responsible, wealthy and distinguished city is complaining because it does not hold on to something that does not belong to it; it says that it has been robbed because it does not have what it does not own. Can you imagine a more outrageous statement than this? A town was selected which was to be the sole repository of the money collected from the whole of Asia for the celebrations in honour of Lucius Flaccus. The whole of this money was converted from these celebrations to lending at interest for their own profit and it was only recovered many years later. What injury has the city suffered? 57 But the city is annoyed. I accept that, for the profit was unexpectedly lost after it had already been swallowed up in anticipation. But the city complains. It does so quite unjustifiably, because we cannot reasonably complain about everything that we do not like. But it accuses Flaccus in the strongest terms. Not the city, but the ignorant rabble stirred up by Maeandrius. And I here repeatedly urge you to remember the irresponsibility of the crowd, the instability characteristic of Greeks and the force that a seditious speech has in a public meeting.
