If, gentlemen, I 60 were saying what the facts require me to say, I should be dealing more harshly than I have hitherto with the degree of trust that you should place in Asiatic witnesses; I should be taking your minds back to recall the Mithridatic War, to the horror of that barbarous massacre inflicted simultaneously upon all Roman citizens in every city, to the surrender of our praetors, the imprisonment of their officers and the almost total obliteration of all memory of the name of Rome and of every trace of our rule from the Greek settlements and from their very records. They called Mithridates Lord, Father, Saviour of Asia, Euhius, Nysius, Bacchus, Liber.a That was the very time at 61 which all Asia was closing its gates to the consul Lucius Flaccus, but was not only receiving the Cappadocianb in its cities but even inviting him spontaneously. Allow us, then, if we cannot forget these events, at least to be silent about them; and allow me to complain more about the unreliability of Greeks than about their savagery. Are they to carry weight with men whom they wanted to exterminate? For they killed all the Roman civilians they could and did away with the words “Roman citizen” so far as was in their power. They are throwing their weight about in this city which they hate, among men whose sight they dislike, and in a state for whose destruction they lacked not the will but the strength.
