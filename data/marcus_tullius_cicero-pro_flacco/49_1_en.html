The unanimous opinion was that if Decianus was able to make other people’s slaves his own by declaring them, he would have the largest household of any of us. Amyntas was given the opinion that the acts of Decianus were not legal. Subsequently Flaccus took the same view when he heard the case, and gave his ruling on this basis.

Now you have the cause of the enmity which fired 81 Decianus to place this profitablea accusation before Laelius. Laelius complained when speaking of the treachery of Decianus: “the man who was my informant, who laid the information before me, whose guidance I followed, was bribed by Flaccus and deserted and betrayed by me.” Were you really responsible then for endangering the whole fortune of Flaccus on whose council you had sat and in whose company you had held all your public offices, a man of the highest integrity and of aristocratic birth, and one who had served the State devotedly? Assuredly, I shall defend Decianus who has fallen under suspicion with you although he has done nothing wrong. He was not bribed, believe me. What could be got 82 from him in return? That he should prolong the trial?
