This type of Proof may be met in three ways, that is to say with reference to any of its three parts; the Major Premiss, the Minor Premiss, and the Conclusion (conclusio) may all be attacked, and sometimes all three.

(1) Major Premiss attacked: “I was justified in killing him, because he lay in wait for me.” The very first Question in Milo’s defence is “whether it is right for a man who confesses to having killed a man to look upon the light of day.”
