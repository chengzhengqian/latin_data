For my part, however—holding as I do that nothing is foreign to the art of oratory which must be admitted to be essential for the making of an orator, and that one cannot reach the top in any subject without going through the elementary stages—I shall certainly not refuse to stoop to those matters which, though minor, cannot be neglected without blocking the way to greater things. I shall proceed exactly as if a child were put into my hands to be educated as an orator, and shall plan his studies from his infancy.

I dedicate this work to you, Marcus Vitorius. You are a very good friend of mine, and you have a burning enthusiasm for literature; but these are not the only reasons (strong as they are) why I regard you as particularly worthy of this pledge of our mutual affection. It is also because I think that these books will be useful for the education of your son Geta, whose early years already show such clear promise of talent.
