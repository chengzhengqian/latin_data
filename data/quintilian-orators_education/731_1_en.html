Chapter 3

Figures of Speech

Figures of Speech on the other hand have always been subject to change and are continually changing according to prevailing usage. So if we compare the older language with our own, almost everything we say now is a Figure. We say hac re invidere (“to grudge a thing”) instead of hanc rem, as the ancients, and particularly Cicero, said;1 likewise incumbere illi (for in illum),2 plenum vino (for vini),3 huic adulari (for hunc),4 and countless others. Let us hope the worse usages do not prevail!
