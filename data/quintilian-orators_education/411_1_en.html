Authority

Authority is a further type of external proof which may be adduced to support a Cause. Following the Greeks, who call these arguments kriseis,44 our people call them “judgements” or “adjudications”; this does not mean verdicts given in legal proceedings (these come under the head of “Examples”) but opinions which can be attributed to nations, peoples, wise men, distinguished citizens, or famous poets. Even common sayings and popular beliefs may be useful. All these are in a sense testimonies, but they are actually all the more effective because they are not given to suit particular Causes, but spoken or given by minds free of prejudice and favour for the simple reason that they seemed either very honourable or very true.
