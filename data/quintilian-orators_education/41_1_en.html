Barbarisms in speaking

Faults which arise in speaking need a finer appreciation, because examples cannot be given in writing, unless they occur in verse, like the divided diphthong in Europai and Asiai,21 and the opposite fault, which the Greeks call synairesis or episynaloiphē (let us call it complexio) and which is seen in Publius Varro’s tum te flagranti deiectum fulmine, Phaethon.22 If this were prose, one could pronounce the letters with the proper syllable division.
