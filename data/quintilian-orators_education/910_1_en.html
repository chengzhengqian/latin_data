A great banquet was held to honour the victory and Simonides was invited, but he was called out of the room by a message that two young men on horseback were said to be asking for him urgently. There were no young men to be found, but he realized from what happened next that the gods were grateful to him. For scarcely had he left the building, when the dining hall collapsed on to the heads of the diners, and so crushed them that the relatives who looked for the bodies for burial could not identify their faces or even their limbs by any marks. Then, it is said, Simonides, who remembered the order in which they had all been sitting, restored the bodies to their respective families.
