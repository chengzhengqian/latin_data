This was Cimber,31 whose murder of his brother is remarked on by Cicero in the words “Cimber has killed his brother german.” Sallust is the target of an equally well-known epigram:

You, Crispus, plunderer of old Cato’s words, and author of Jugurtha’s history.32

This is sheer pedantry. Anyone can practise it, and it is particularly bad because the enthusiast for it will not choose words to fit his facts but bring in irrelevant facts for which these words will be suitable equivalents.

(2) Coining words (as I said in Book One)33 is more a privilege of the Greeks, who have not hesitated to fit words even to certain sounds and emotions with the same freedom with which primitive man gave things their names. The few ventures that our countrymen have made in compounds or derived words have scarcely met with acceptance.
