Thus Menenius Agrippa is said to have reconciled plebs and patricians by means of the famous story of the quarrel of the limbs against the belly.25 Horace too did not regard the use of this type as undignified even in poetry: witness the lines, “As the shrewd fox said to the ailing lion . . .”26 The Greeks call this an ainos (“tale”), and speak of Aesopic fables (as I said) and “Libyan fables”;27 some Roman writers use the word apologatio,28 though the name has not been accepted in common use.
