Prefixes are also often modified by this joining: thus we have abstulit, aufugit, amisit,88 though the preposition is only ab; coit,89 though the preposition is con; and so ignavi and erepublica90 and the like. But all this suits the Greeks better. It is not very successful with us—not I think because of any innate weakness, but we favour foreign imports, and so admire kurtauchen,91 but can hardly protect incurvicervicum from ridicule.

“Proper” and metaphorical words

Words are “proper” when they signify that which they were first designed to name; metaphorical, when they have one meaning by nature and another in the context.

Words in current use or made up
