In 7.10, Quintilian seeks to define the differences between these various Issues (1–4), but his emphasis in concluding the book is once again to point out that theory cannot do everything; good sense and a right judgement of the individual case are vital. There is a lot to learn, mainly by practice and experience. At 7.10.17 he introduces the analogy between Disposition and arrangement of words; this is a contrived transition to elocutio, which begins with the next book.
