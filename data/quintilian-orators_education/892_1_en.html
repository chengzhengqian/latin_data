One of the poets says he does not much care “whether Caesar is a black man or a white”:34 sheer madness, but turn it round, so that Caesar has said this of the poet, and it is arrogance. Tragic and comic poets pay greater attention to characters, because they use many different ones. The same was true of those who wrote speeches for others,35 and is true of declaimers today, since in declamation we do not always speak as advocates, but very commonly as litigants.

However, even in Causes in which we do appear as advocates, the same differentiation must be carefully observed. This is because we use imaginary persons and speak as it were with other men’s lips, and so have to provide the appropriate personalities for those to whom we lend our voice.
