Thanks to an exceptionally long life (he lived to 109),6 he had many contemporaries; he was the rival of those I have just mentioned, and yet he went on to outlive Socrates. His contemporaries include Thrasymachus of Calchedon,7 Prodicus of Ceos,8 Protagoras of Abdera9 (for whose teaching Euathlus10 is supposed to have paid 10,000 denarii, and subsequently to have published it), Hippias of Elis11 and Alcidamas of Elaea,12 whom Plato13 calls Palamedes.
