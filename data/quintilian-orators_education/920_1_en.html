How much aptitude and application can do for memory is proved by Themistocles, who is known to have spoken excellent Persian within one year;21 by Mithridates22 who is said to have known twenty-two languages, as many as the nations over whom he ruled; by Crassus the Rich, who, as governor of Asia, had such mastery of the five dialects of Greek that he would give judgement in whatever language the case had been put forward;23 and by Cyrus, who is believed to have known the names of all his soldiers.24 Theodectes25 too is said to have repeated off the cuff any number of verses which he had heard once. There were said to be people in our own time who could do this, but I never had the good fortune to witness it. We ought to believe it, however, simply because believing it gives us hope.
