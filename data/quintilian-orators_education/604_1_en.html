Importance and difficulty of Elocution

What follows needs more effort and care. I have next to treat the theory of Elocution, which all orators agree is the most difficult part of the whole work. On the one hand, Marcus Antonius,1 whom I mentioned above, says that he has seen many competent speakers, but no eloquent one; he thus holds the view that saying the right things suffices for the merely competent, but the eloquent speaker’s special part is to say them in style. If this virtue was to be found in no orator up to his day, and not even in him or in Lucius Crassus, it is obvious that the lack of it in them and in their predecessors was due to its great difficulty.
