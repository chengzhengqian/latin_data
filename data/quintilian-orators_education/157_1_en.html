But some of his dialogues were composed to refute opponents, and these are called “elenctic” dialogues, while others are for teaching, and are called “dogmatic.”30 Now Socrates (or Plato) applies this description to rhetoric as practised in those days, for he speaks explicitly of “this way in which you conduct public affairs,”31 but he has an understanding of true and honourable rhetoric.
