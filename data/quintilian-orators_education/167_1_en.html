Let us not allow building to be an art either: primitive men constructed their huts without art. Music too: singing and dancing of some sort exist in all peoples. So if any speech whatever is to be called “rhetoric,” then I must agree that rhetoric existed before there was an art; but if it is not true that everyone who speaks is an orator, and people did not speak like orators in those days, then they must admit that the orator is produced by art, and did not exist before art.

This also rules out their argument that anything done by a man who has not learned it cannot be an achievement of art, and yet there are men who can make speeches without having learned. In support of this view they adduce the fact that Demades9 the rower and Aeschines10 the actor were both orators.
