What Cicero102 calls the “shorter” form of Digression can be effected in many ways. The following examples will suffice: (1) “Then Gaius Varenus, the man who was killed by Ancharius’ slaves—observe this point, members of the jury, carefully . . .”;103 (2) in Pro Milone:104 “He looked at me with the look he used to have in the days when he was threatening everybody with everything.”

There is another Figure, not indeed an Aposiopesis (which would involve an unfinished sentence) but a cutting short, as it were, of a passage before its proper end: “I press too hard, the young man seems to be upset”105 or “What more? You have heard the young man himself say . . .”106
