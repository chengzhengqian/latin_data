(3) Agreement: for example, in the same speech, the admission that the jury was bribed.94

(4) This Figure is more striking when we agree to something which is going to help us; but this can only occur through some failing on our opponent’s part.

(5) We may also make a show of praising something, as Cicero does in In Verrem in connection with the charge concerning Apollonius of Drepanum: “It is a real pleasure to me to think that you took something from him, and I say that you never did anything more justifiably.”95

(6) Sometimes, we exaggerate charges which we could easily refute or deny. This is too common to need illustration.

(7) Sometimes, we detract from the credibility of charges simply on the ground that they are so grave: so Cicero in Pro Roscio exaggerates the horror of parricide, obvious as it is, by the force of his eloquence.
