I mention these because they too are called Issues by many writers, though some prefer to regard them as Legal Questions.

Four-Issue Theories

Four Issues are found in Athenaeus.45 They are:

(1) protreptikē or parormētikē stasis, that is to say “hortatory,” an Issue peculiar to the deliberative speech;

(2) syntelikē,46 which the context reveals as equivalent to “conjectural,” though the name does not make this clear;

(3) hypallaktikē,47 Definition (because this results from a change of name);

(4) juridical, for which he uses the same Greek term48 as the others.

As I said, there is a lot of variety in nomenclature. Some think hypallaktikē is equivalent to Transference, as involving the sense of “change.”

Others give this same number of Issues, but different ones: Does it exist? What is it? What is it like? How big is it? (so Caecilius49 and Theon50).
