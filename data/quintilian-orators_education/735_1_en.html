It has even gone as far as non paeniturum (“not about to repent”) for “not intending to repent” and visuros (“about to see”) for “sent to see” in the same author.21 These may indeed have been Figures when Sallust created them, but one must ask whether they can be called so, now that they have become accepted. For once a usage is accepted, we are content even with popular parlance as Authority: rebus agentibus, which Pollio22 condemns in Labienus, and contumeliam fecit, which is well-known to have been stigmatized by Cicero23 (in his day they said adfici contumelia), have come to be current.
