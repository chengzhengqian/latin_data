III.—On the Good Man: a Pythagorean Sentence

The upright man and wise—Apollo, when invoked,1 could scarce find one such amongst all the thousands of mankind—sits in judgment on himself and searches out his whole self to a hair’s breadth. What the great think, or what the fickle opinion of the empty-headed mob, . . . he cares not, but, after the fashion of the globe, keeps himself rounded and compact, too smooth for any blemish from without to settle upon him.
