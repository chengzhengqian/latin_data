Thou, Mars, lovest the beardless,1 and thou, Moon, the bald: these do not forbid hair and cheeks to be trimmed. The Sun and Saturn have no scruples as to nails: therefore cancel that line of which gods disapprove.

  • 1i.e. youths in their prime and fit for war. A persistent tradition denies the soldier a beard. Why the moon loves the bald is not clear, unless it be that the moon itself resembles a bald head.
