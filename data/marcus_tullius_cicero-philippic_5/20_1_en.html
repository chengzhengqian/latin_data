Assuredly they ought not to have suffered for being good sons. If only Marcus Lepidus could have saved all of them! That he would have done so he made plain by doing what he could, in restoring Sextus Pompeius to the community, a shining ornament of the Republic and a most notable memorial of his own clemency. Grievous was that stroke of fortune to the Roman people, a grievous destiny! In Pompeius the father the light of the Roman people’s empire was put out; and then his son, who resembled him so closely, was slain. But by the judgment of the 40gods, the preservation of Sextus Pompeius to the Republic, has, as I see it, made amends for all.
