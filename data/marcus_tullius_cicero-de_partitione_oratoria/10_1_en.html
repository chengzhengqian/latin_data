It will also give the style charm to employ some unusual or original or novel expression. For anything that causes surprise gives pleasure, and the most effective style is one that stirs up some emotion in the mind, and that indicates amiability of character in the speaker himself; and amiability of character is expressed either by his indicating his own judgement and humanity and liberality of mind, or by the modification of the style when it appears that the speaker for the sake of magnifying a second party or disparaging himself is saying something different from what he actually thinks, and that he is doing this more out of good nature than insincerity. But there are many rules for charm that render the style either less lucid or less convincing; consequently in this department also we have to use our own judgement as to what the case requires.
