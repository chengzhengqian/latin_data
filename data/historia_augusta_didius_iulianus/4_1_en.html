The people, meanwhile, detested Julianus because it had been their belief that the abuses of Commodus’ régime were to be reformed by the influence of Pertinax, and he was considered to have been killed with Julianus’ connivance. And now, those who had begun to hate Julianus were the first, to spread it abroad that on the very first day of his reign, to show his contempt for Pertinax’ board, he had served an extravagant banquet embellished with such dainties as oysters and fatted birds and fish.
