Unassigned Fragments

11–12

Varro: ‘Praefica.’ According to Aurelius, a term applied to a woman hired for the purpose of mourning. . . . Naevius bears witness to this view in the words—

That woman, by god, is a leader of keeners, I think; she sings high praise of a man who’s dead.

13–14

Various fragments:

Festus: Archaic writers used ‘tam’ even in the sense of ‘tamen,’ for example, Naevius—

What if he says nothing? So long as he sees, let him still know what has been written.

15

Festus: ‘Tintinnire’ in a passage of Naevius occurs in the following form, that is, ‘tintinnare’—

So great was the clattering of millstones there, and the jingling of fetters.

16

I don’t know whether shoulder-blades or neck Have harder skin. 17
