Another takes me by her movement, swaying her arms in rhythm and curving her tender side with supple art—to say naught of myself, who take fire from every cause, put Hippolytus in my place, and he will be Priapus!a You, because you are so tall, are not second to the ancient daughters of heroes, and can lie the whole couch’s length. Another I find apt because she is short. I am undone by both; tall and short are after the wish of my heart. She is not well dressed—I dream what dress would add; she is well arrayed—she herself shows off her dower of charms. A fair white skin will make prey of me, I am prey to the golden-haired, and even a love of dusky hue will please. Do dark locks hang on a neck of snow—Leda was fair to look upon for her black locks; are they of golden hue—Aurora pleased with saffron locks. To all the old tales my love can fit itself. Fresh youth steals away my heart, I am smitten with later years; the one has more worth, the other wins me with charm of person.
