The Amores

III

Go, believe there are gods—she swore and has failed her oath, and still her face is fair, as ’twas before! She has hair as long since she insulted the gods, as she had still unforsworn. Before, she was dazzling fair, and her fairness was mingled with rosy red—the rosy red still glows in her snowy cheeks. Her foot was small—her foot is still of daintiest form. She was tall and handsome —tall and handsome she remains. She had sparkling eyes—like stars still beam the eyes by which she has often falsely lied to me. Surely, even the immortal gods indulge the fair in swearing false, and beauty has its privilege divine. By her own eyes not long ago she swore, I mind me, and by mine—and lo, mine have been the ones to smart!

15

Say, O ye gods, if she deceived you and has gone unpunished, why have I borne the pains for another’s desert? Or is Cepheus’ daughter no reproach to you—she whom you bade to die for her mother’s ill-starred beauty?a Is it not enough that I have found your witness without weight, and that unpunished she makes the gods her mirth as well as me? That she may redeem her perjuries, am I to suffer, and, though deceived, be victim to my deceiver?
