Common topics: in favour of reasoning by analogy, that it is proper to proceed by inference from what is written to what is not written, and that no one can include every case in one statute but that he makes the most suitable law who takes care that some things may be understood from certain others. 153Against reasoning by analogy as follows: that inference is no better than divination, and that it is a stupid lawmaker who cannot provide for every case that he desires.
