De Inventione, I

But when the emotions have been aroused it will be advisable not to linger over the conquestio. For as the rhetorician Apollonius said, “Nothing dries more quickly than tears.”a

Now since I have said enough, I think, about all the divisions of the speech, and this volume has grown too long, what follows I shall include in the second book.

  • aOn this proverb compare G. D. Kellogg in Amer. Journal of Phil, xxviii (1907), pp. 301–310. Apollonius is probably the rhetorician surnamed Molon who was later Cicero’s teacher.
