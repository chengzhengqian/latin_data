A thing is brought on the stage if in the enumeration the words are given to something of this sort, a law, a place, a city, or a monument; for example, “What if the laws could speak? Would they not make this complaint to you:’ What more do you desire, gentlemen of the jury, when this and this has been made plain to you?’” In this kind of summing up as well, one may use all the same methods. As a general principle for summing up, it is laid down that since the whole of any argument cannot be given a second time, the most important point of each be selected, and that every argument be touched on as briefly as possible, so that it may appear to be a refreshing of the memory of the audience, rather than a repetition of the speech.

In discussing this topic we wish it to be understood at the beginning that indignatio is used in connexion with all the topics which we laid out when giving rules for confirmation.
