Then he will demand that the defendant produce the law under which he acted. Then, just as we suggested in the case of comparison that the prosecutor should disparage as much as possible the deed which is cited by way of comparison, so in this case he should compare the fault of the person to whom the blame is transferred with the crime of him who says that he was justified in committing it; afterward it should be pointed out that the one act is not of such a nature that because of it the other should have been done. Finally, as in case of comparison, the point for decision is taken up and dilated upon in accordance with the rules for a deliberative speech.a

XXVIII.
