IV

Copy of a letter of Alexander to his mother Olympias; and Olympias’ witty reply.

In many of the records of Alexander’s deeds, and not long ago in the book of Marcus Varro entitled Orestes or On Madness, I have read2 that Olympias, the wife of Philip, wrote a very witty reply to her son Alexander. For he had addressed his mother as follows: “King Alexander, son of Jupiter Hammon, greets his mother Olympias.” Olympias replied to this effect: “Pray, my son,” said she, “be silent, and do not slander me or accuse me before Juno; undoubtedly she will take cruel vengeance on me, if you admit in your letters that I am her husband’s paramour.” This courteous reply of a wise and prudent woman to her arrogant son seemed to warn him in a mild and polite fashion to give up the foolish idea which he had formed from his great victories, from the flattery of his courtiers, and from his incredible success—that he was the son of Jupiter.
