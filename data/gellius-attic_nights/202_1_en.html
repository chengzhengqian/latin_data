Book IV. xvii.

XVII

A discussion of the natural quantity of certain particles, the long pronunciation of which, when prefixed to verbs, seems to be barbarous and ignorant; with several examples and explanations.

In the eleventh book of Lucilius are these lines:1

Thus base Asellus did great Scipio taunt: Unlucky was his censorship and bad.

I hear that many read obiciebat with a long o, and they say that they do this in order to preserve the metre.2 Again farther on he says:3

I’d versify the words the herald Granius spoke.

In this passage also they lengthen the prefix of the first word for the same reason. Again in the fifteenth book:4

Subicit huic humilem et suffercitus posteriorem,5

they read subicit with a long u, because it is not proper for the first syllable to be short in heroic verse. Likewise in the Epidicus of Plautus6 they lengthen the syllable con in

Haste now, Epidicus, prepare yourself, And throw (conice) your mantle round about your neck.
