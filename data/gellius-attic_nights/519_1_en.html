XXXI

The meaning of caninum prandium in Marcus Varro’s satire.

Lately a foolish, boastful fellow, sitting in a bookseller’s shop, was praising and advertising himself, asserting that he was the only one under all heaven who could interpret the Satires of Marcus Varro, which by some are called Cynical, by others Menippean. And then he displayed some passages of no great difficulty, which he said no one could presume to explain.
