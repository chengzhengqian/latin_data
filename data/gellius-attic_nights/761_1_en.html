Accordingly Ennius, wishing to describe such action, said that restitution was demanded, not by legal processes, such as are carried on before a praetor, nor by a laying on of hands according to law, but by war and the sword, and by genuine and resistless violence; and he seems to have expressed this by comparing that civil and symbolic1 power which is exercised in name only and not actually, with warlike and sanguinary violence.

XI

The meaning of the word sculna, used by Marcus Varro.

Publius Lavinius is the author of a carefully written book, entitled On Vulgar Words. In it he wrote that sculna was a colloquial form for seculna, “for which,” says he, “more elegant speakers use sequester, or ‘arbiter.’” Each of these words is derived from sequor, because both parties “follow” the decision of the arbiter who is chosen.
