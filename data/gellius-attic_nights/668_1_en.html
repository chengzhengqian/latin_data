Book XVII. xix.

XVIII

The statement of Marcus Varro that Gaius Sallustius, the writer of history, was taken in adultery by Annius Milo and was let go only after he had been beaten with thongs and had paid a sum of money.

Marcus Varro, a man of great trustworthiness and authority in his writings and in his life, in the work which he entitled Pius, or On Peace,1 says that Gaius Sallustius, the author of those austere and dignified works, whom we see in his history writing and acting like a censor, was taken in adultery by Annius Milo, soundly beaten with thongs, and allowed to escape only after paying a sum of money.2
