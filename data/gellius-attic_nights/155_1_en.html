The two smallest of these, which touch the ends of the axis, he says are called πόλοι, or “poles”; but that because of their small diameter they cannot be represented on what is termed an armillary sphere.2 And the zodiac itself is not uninfluenced by the number seven; for the summer solstice occurs in the seventh sign from the winter solstice, and the winter solstice in the seventh after the summer, and one equinox in the seventh sign after the other. Then too those winter days during which the kingfishers nest on the water he says are seven in number.3 Besides this, he writes that the course of the moon is completed in four times seven complete days; “for on the twenty-eighth day,” he says, “the moon returns to the same point from which it started,” and he quotes Aristides4 of Samos as his authority for this opinion.
