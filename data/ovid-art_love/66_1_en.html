Perhaps too my name will be joined to theirs, nor will my writings be given to Lethe’s waters; and someone will say, “Read the elegant poems of our master, wherein he instructs the rival parties;1 or from the three books marked by the title of ‘Loves’ choose out what you may softly read with docile voice; or let some Letter2 be read by you with practised utterance; he first invented this art, unknown to others.” So grant it, O Phoebus! so grant it, ye blessed souls of poets, and thou, O horned Bacchus, and ye goddesses nine!

Who would doubt that I would have a woman know how to dance,3 that when the wine is set she may, when bidden, move her arms. Artists whom the stage displays win favour as they move their sides; so great a charm has this easy movement. I am ashamed to advise in little things, that she should know the throws of the dice, and thy powers, O flung counter.4 Now let her throw three dice, and now reflect which side she may fitly join in her cunning, and which challenge.
