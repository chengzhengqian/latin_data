What a house he had, what crowds of visitors every day, what distinction from his close associates, what devotion from his friends, what a throng from every class in society! All this, won by long and arduous toil, a single hour snatched from him. Publius Sulla received, gentlemen, a deep and deadly wound, but after all the sort of wound to which men thought that one of his life and character was exposed. He was held to have been over-ambitious for office and distinction; if no other consular candidate nursed a similar ambition, Sulla was held to have been more ambitious than the rest; but if there were others who shared his desire for the consulship, fortune was perhaps harder in his case than upon the rest.
