Then he proclaimed, in the Greek language and with Greek ritual, the opening of the navigation season.29 The crowd’s acclamation which followed confirmed that his words had been auspicious to all. Then, steeped in joy, the people brought forward boughs and branches and garlands and kissed the feet of the goddess, who stood on the steps, fashioned of silver. They then dispersed to their own homes. For my part, my heart would not let me go a nail’s breadth away from that spot, but I continued to concentrate on the goddess’s image as I pondered my former misfortunes.

Swift Rumour, however, had not held back through any18 slowness of wing,30 but speedily in my own country she had told the story everywhere of the provident goddess’s praiseworthy blessing and my own noteworthy fortune. At once my friends, my household slaves, and those who were related to me by the closest ties of blood dropped the mourning they had taken up at the false report of my death, and in their delight at this unexpected joy, they all brought varous gifts and straightway hurried to see me restored to the daylight from the dead. I too was renewed by seeing those whom I had given up hope of seeing, and accepted their kind offerings gratefully, since my friends had thoughtfully taken care to bring me enough to relieve my living expenses in a generous manner.

And so I dutifully spoke to each of them and rapidly19 described both my former sufferings and my present joys; and then I returned again to the contemplation of the goddess, which was my greatest delight.
