Metamorphoses II

The battle was over. Photis had been awakened by the uproar and had opened the door. I crept inside panting and bathed in sweat, and immediately—as befitted a man exhausted in battle against three thieves in the manner of the slaughter of Geryon37—I surrendered simultaneously to bed and sleep.

  • 37A three-bodied giant, slain by Hercules in one of his twelve labors.
