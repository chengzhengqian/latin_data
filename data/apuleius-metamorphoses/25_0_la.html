Apuleius

cuncta circumibam tamen.2 Dum in luxum3 nepotalem similis ostiatim singula pererro, repente me nescius forum cuppedinis4 intuli. Et ecce mulierem quampiam frequenti stipatam famulitione ibidem gradientem accelerato vestigio comprehendo. Aurum in gemmis et in tunicis, ibi inflexum, hic intextum, matronam profecto confitebatur. Huius adhaerebat lateri senex iam gravis in annis, qui, ut primum me conspexit, “Est,” inquit “hercules, est5 Lucius,” et offert osculum, et statim incertum quidnam in aurem mulieris obganniit. “Quin” inquit “etiam ipse parentem tuam accedis et salutas?” “Vereor” inquam “ignotae mihi feminae,” et statim rubore suffusus deiecto6 capite restiti.

At illa, optutum in me conversa, “En” inquit “sanctissimae Salviae matris generosa probitas. Sed et cetera corporis exsecrabiliter ad [regulam qua diligenter aliquid affingunt] amussim7 congruentia: inenormis proceritas, suculenta gracilitas, rubor temperatus, flavum et inaffectatum capillitium, oculi caesii quidem, sed vigiles et in aspectu micantes, prorsus aquilini, os quoquoversum floridum, speciosus et immeditatus incessus.”

  • 2tamen in this position is strange: the text may be corrupt and the clause-division wrong. Frassinetti’s conjecture is attractive: circumibam. Tandem, dum . . .
  • 3F luxu. Editors, unnecessarily disturbed by the phrase in luxum nepotalem similis, have proposed numerous emendations and additions.
  • 4F cupidinis.
  • 5F has one letter erased between hercules and Lucius; some editors do not print a second est.
  • 6F reiecto.
  • 7F has only sim of amussim. The bracketed words are almost certainly a gloss explaining the meaning of amussim as “a ruler with which people fashion something carefully.”
