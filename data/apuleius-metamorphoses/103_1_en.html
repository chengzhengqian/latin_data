Metamorphoses IV

They came to the appointed crag of the steep mountain and placed the girl, as decreed, on its very summit, where they abandoned her one and all. The bridal torches, with which they had lighted the way, now extinguished by their tears, they left behind; and with heads bent low they began their homeward journey. Her unhappy parents, shattered by this great catastrophe, shut themselves up in the darkness of their house, resigning themselves to perpetual night.
