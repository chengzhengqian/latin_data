I beg you, therefore, not to spurn the meagreness of our little hovel. You can have that adjoining bedroom right over there, as a decent little retreat. I hope you will be pleased to stay with us. Not only will you make our house greater by the honour of your presence, but you will lay claim to a token of great repute if you are content with a tiny hearth, in emulation of the virtues of your father’s namesake Theseus, who did not disdain the meagre hospitality of old Hecale.”

Then he summoned the maid. “Photis,”29 he said, “pick up our guest’s bags and see that they are safely placed in that bedroom. Also bring some rubbing oil out of the storeroom and drying-towels and whatever else he needs, right away. Then take my guest to the nearest baths. He has had quite a difficult and extensive journey and he is tired.”

When I heard this I realised Milo’s character and stinginess,but since I wished to get further into his good graces, I said to him, “I do not need any of those supplies; they accompany me everywhere on my travels. And I can easily ask directions to the baths. What I am really most especially concerned about is my horse, who has conveyed me here so vigorously. Photis! Here, take these coins and buy him some hay and barley.”
