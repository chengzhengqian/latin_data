So she lamented. For my part, although I was a complete ass and a beast of burden instead of Lucius, I still retained my human intelligence; and so I held a long, earnest debate12 with myself concerning that utterly worthless and criminal woman. Should I kick her repeatedly with my hoofs, assault her with my teeth, and kill her? But that was a rash idea and better thinking brought me back to my senses, lest, by punishing Photis with death, I also destroy the assistance I needed for recovery. So, lowering and shaking my head, I silently swallowed my temporary humiliation, and accommodating myself to my harsh misfortune, I went off to the stable to join my horse, my most excellent mount. I discovered another ass stabled there too, belonging to Milo, who used to be my host. I assumed that, if any unspoken natural bond of allegiance existed among dumb animals, my horse would be moved by recognition and a feeling of pity to offer me hospitality and privileged treatment.13 But—O thou guestgod Jupiter14! O ye invisible deities of Loyalty!—that noble mount of mine and the ass put their heads together and immediately agreed on my destruction.
