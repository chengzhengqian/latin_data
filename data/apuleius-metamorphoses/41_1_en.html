“When I was still a minor I set out from Miletus23 to see the Olympic games.24 Since I also wanted to visit this area of the celebrated province, I travelled through the whole of Thessaly and, under dark omens, arrived at Larissa.25 Since my travel-allowance had worn quite thin, I wandered all over town trying to find some remedy for my poverty. I caught sight of a tall old man in the middle of the forum, who was standing up on a rock and proclaiming in a loud voice that anyone willing to guard a dead man should bid for the job. I said to a passerby, ‘What is this I hear? Do dead men usually run away around here?’

“‘Quiet!’ he answered. ‘You are terribly young and very much a stranger, and naturally you do not understand that you are in Thessaly, where witches are always taking bites out of corpses’ faces to get supplies for their magic art.’
