XII. Kal. 21st

721 He who would learn what the Agonia are, may turn back to January, though they have a place in the calendar at this season also.b

XI. Kal. 22nd

723 In the night that follows the day the dog of Erigone risesc: I have given the explanation of this constellation in another place.d

X. Kal. 23rd

725 The next day belongs to Vulcan; they call it Tubilustria.e The trumpets which he makes are then cleansed and purified.

IX. Kal. 24th

727 The next place is marked by four letters, which, read in order, signify either the custom of the sacred rites or the Flight of the King.f

  • cSirius: true morning rising was on July 19; apparent, August 2.
  • dSee iii. 849.
  • eAn account of this was to have been added later, but the poem was never finished.
