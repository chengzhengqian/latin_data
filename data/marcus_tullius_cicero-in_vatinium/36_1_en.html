The right of looking for auspicia impetrativa was known as spectio; and the exercise of that right as servare de caelo, “to look for something coming down from the sky.” Mommsen assumed that this righta was confined to magistrates. According to strict practice, a magistrate, or otherwise qualified person, who had observed an unfavourable omen was bound to announce it to the magistrate who was about to preside over an Assembly. This announcement was called obnuntiatio. But such obnuntiatio would only be served when a magistrate persisted in his intention to hold an Assembly.b In general, an announcement by a magistrate or tribune that he had “watched the sky,” or merely that he intended to do so, was in itself enough to deter a magistrate from holding an Assembly.
