Our knowledge of the lex Aelia Fufia is defective, for it is not possible to state the purpose of more than two of its sections. One section regulated the use of the auspicia with reference both to the comitia populi (centuriata, tributa) and the concilium plebis, and allowed not only curule magistrates to practise obnuntiatiob at Meetings of the concilium plebis but tribunes also at Assemblies of the comitia populi. Another section forbade, by a regulation de iure et de tempore legum rogandarum,c the holding of legislative Assemblies on certain dies fasti, among which was the period of three nundinad (probably twenty-four days) between the announcement of elections and the holding of the elections themselves.
