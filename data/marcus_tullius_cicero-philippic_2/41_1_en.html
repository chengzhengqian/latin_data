Ignorance compounded by impudence! He neither knows what an augur ought to know nor acts as a man of discretion ought to act.

Well, then, recall his behavior as consul from that day 82down to the fifteenth of March. Was there ever a magistrate’s assistant so abjectly subservient? He had no power of his own. He asked for everything. Putting his head into the back of his colleague’s litter, he would beg favors from him in order to sell them.

[33] So the day of Dolabella’s electoral assembly arrives. Lots are drawn for the right of first vote;95 Antonius makes no move. The result is declared; Antonius keeps silent. The first class is called to vote, then the six centuries of knights96 in the usual way, then the second class—all sooner done than said. After 83the business is finished, our adept augur—a real Gaius Laelius, you’d say!97—announced: “Meeting adjourned.” Matchless impudence! What had you seen, what had you perceived, what had you heard? For you did not say that you had watched the heavens, nor are you saying so today. So the flaw which you had already foreseen on the first of January and predicted so long beforehand duly appeared. Thus, by god, you lied about the auspices, to your own great mischief, I truly hope, and not to that of the Republic. You bound the Roman people in a religious liability.
