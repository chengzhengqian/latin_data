We are but ciphers, born to consume earth’s fruits, Penelope’s good-for-naught suitors, young courtiers of Alcinous, unduly busy in keeping their skins sleek, whose pride it was to sleep till midday and to lull care to rest to the sound of the cithern.

32 To cut men’s throats, robbers rise up by night; to save your own life, won’t you wake up? Nay, just as, if you won’t take up running in health, you’ll have to do it when dropsical; so, if you don’t call for a book and a light before daybreak, if you don’t devote your mind to honourable studies and pursuits, envy or passion will keep you awake in torment. Why indeed are you in a hurry to remove things which hurt the eye, while if aught is eating into your soul, you put off the time for cure till next year? Well begun is half done; dare to be wise; begin! He who puts off the hour of right living is like the bumpkin waiting for the river to run out: yet on it glides, and on it will glide, rolling its flood forever.
