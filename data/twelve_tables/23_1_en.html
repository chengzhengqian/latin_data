Disputes between owners of conterminous properties:

Cicero: I admire the excellence not only of the purport of the Law but also its diction—

5a If parties disagree,

it says. By ‘iurgium’e is meant a contest between friends, not a quarrel between enemies. . . . The Law then thinks of neighbours as disagreeing, not quarrelling.f

Arbitration in such disputes:

Cicero: 5b Since the Twelve Tables ordained (see above, pp. 466–7) that ownership within a five-foot strip should not be acquired by long usage, we will not allow the ancient estate of the Academy to be eaten away by this sharp persong; and the boundaries will be marked by us not as by one arbitrator apiece as by the Mamilian lawh but as by three arbitrators, as required by the Twelve Tables.
