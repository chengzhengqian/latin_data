In after years Severus himself continually avowed that he had found this journey very enjoyable, because he had taken part in the worship of the god Serapis, had learned something of antiquity, and had seen unfamiliar animals and strange places. For he visited Memphis, Memnon,2 the Pyramids, and the Labyrinth,3 and examined them all with great care.

But since it is tedious to mention in detail the less important matters, only the most noteworthy of his deeds are here related.4 He discharged the cohorts of the guard5 after Julianus was defeated and slain; he deified Pertinax against the wishes of the army;6 and he gave orders that the decisions of Salvius Julianus should be annulled,7 though this he did not succeed in accomplishing.
