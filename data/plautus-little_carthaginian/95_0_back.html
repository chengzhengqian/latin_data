YTMM is the third-person masculine singular imperfect pu‘al of TMM, “be good,” with the initial vowel lost by scribal error or merged with the preceding word. l. 942

iulecantheconaalonimbalumbar dechor

Variants: sic A, lula canti (cant CD) chona P

Discussion: Not much in this line is clear. The word alonim, “gods,” can be isolated, and canthe corresponds to caneth in l. 932, which in combination with the wish particle L at the beginning of the line (lu in P) means “may I acquire.” Sznycer analyzes cona/chona as KH, “here,” together with the adhortative particle N’. This is possible, but by no means certain.
