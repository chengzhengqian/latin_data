GG ‘is attested as the name of a profession in a Punic inscription; since the inscription leaves it unclear what that profession is, and since Hanno has clearly traveled, I have translated the word as “tradesman,” a term which Agorastocles may be familiar with despite not speaking Punic. Gratwick prefers to give the last sentence to Milphio and to regard gugga as the name of a bird. In Greek the gyges is the bittern, but Gratwick prefers to see in the bird the purple heron, which would introduce a rather complex pun: Phoenicians traded in purple, and hence Hanno can be seen as a purple heron; the heron was also called the “treacherous bird” by the Romans, and this association would introduce negative stereotyping of Carthaginians.
