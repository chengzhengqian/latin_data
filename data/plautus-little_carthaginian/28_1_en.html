Poenulus

Act Three

Enter agorastocles and his advocates from the right; in the ensuing dialogue, Agorastocles typically addresses all advocates, but only one of them replies as their spokesman.
ago

(to the audience) As truly as the gods may love me, nothing is more useless than a slow friend, especially for a505 lovesick man, who rushes everything he does. For instance, I’m bringing along these advocates, the most slow-moving of men, slower than freight ships are on a calm sea. And I deliberately avoided my elderly friends: I knew they’d be slower because of their age, and I feared delay for my love. But I’ve gained no advantage510 by choosing these attendants for myself, leaden-footed, very slow ones. (to the advocates) If you’re going today, go, or go and be hanged! Should friends walk like this in order to support a lovesick man? That step of yours has been sifted through a flour sieve;31 unless you learned to walk with shackles at that pace.
