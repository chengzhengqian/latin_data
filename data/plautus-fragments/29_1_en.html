Fragments

54 I never come to town except when the robe is brought in in the procession

Serv. Aen. 1.480: peplum refers to the embroidered robe dedicated to Minerva.1

55 I don’t blare about politics at table and I don’t bellow about the laws

Serv. Dan. Aen. 1.738: an exhortation is better understood in a loud voice.

56 and I’ll trample you underfoot like a sow does her litter

Serv. Dan. Aen. 2.357: catuli, “pups,” are not just young dogs, but also young snakes and other animals.1

57 to defile

Serv. Aen. 3.42: scelerare means “defile.”

58 I set about this talk

Serv. Dan. Aen. 4.533: insistere, “set about, proceed with,” can be used with the accusative.

59 the bird creates death for itself

Serv. Aen. 6.205: mistletoe, from which birdlime was made, grows out of the seeds that have passed through the digestive tract of thrushes.

  • 1This robe was displayed during the festival of the Panathenaea at Athens.
  • 1Perhaps this line is an imprecise quotation of Truc. 268.
