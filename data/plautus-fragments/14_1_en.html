Fragments

The Twin Pimps

This boy is distressed at being led away for sale.

Fest. 290–92: puer, “boy,” has a diminutive puellus.

The Little Garden

An auctioneer should be present; he should be sold with a garland for any price he fetches.

Fest. 400: a garland was worn by prisoners of war when they were auctioned; the garland thus came to indicate low value.

Lipargus1

I don’t care for a drone in the hive to eat up the bees’ food.

Prisc. gramm. 2.522: edere, “eat,” mostly has es and est as second and third persons, but edis and edit (with short initial vowel) occur as well.2

The Fetter Comedy

1 I forthwith fleeced that old man with a rasp.

Varro ling. 7.68: scobina, “rasp,” is derived from scobis, “sawdust.”

2 And other duties prevent me. From what means should I fulfill my own?

Fest. 128–30: moenia means “duties.”

  • 1The meaning of this title is obscure.
  • 2In our quotation, peredit is almost certainly a subjunctive rather than an indicative.
