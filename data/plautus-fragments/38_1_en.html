Fragments

27this is my stable

Sacerd. gramm. 6.472: praesepe, “stall for cattle,” is neuter, but has a feminine by-form, praesepes.

28I call in answer

Prob. cath. gramm. 4.38: in Latin verbs only the vowels a, e, and i occur before the first-person ending -o; reboo is not a real exception because the base verb boo (I call) is a Greek loanword.

29in this way I fled by the side roads and escaped my father

Char. gramm. 282: semitatim, “by the side roads,” is an unusual adverb.

30nonsense

Char. gramm. 315: the interjection butubatta means “nothing” or “nonsense.”

31to those people

Explan. in Don. gramm. 4.545: Plautus does not use illibus instead of illis.

32give it to my father

Cledon. gramm. 5.59: the imperative cedo, “give/say,” has a plural form, cette.

33but do it

Cledon. gramm. 5.74: in ancient writers autem, “but,” can occupy the first position as well as the second.
