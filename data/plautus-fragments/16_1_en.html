The Lazy Hanger-On

1 From there he ∗∗∗ at the first dusk, having had a good share of drink

Varro ling. 7.77: crepusculum, “dusk,” is a Sabellic word.1 It refers to the time when there is doubt whether it is night or day.

2 I began to go home on the footpath on the right-hand Street

Varro ling. 7.62: trames, “path,” is derived from transuersum, “crosswise.”1

  • 1Rods were used to make forms for pressing cheese and baskets for keeping it in. The origin of the word is actually unclear. The etymology contains a true part: trames is indeed derived from trans-, “through,” but the second element has nothing to do with uersum, “turned.”
  • 2Actually, caseus refers to a piece of cheese, while caseum refers to cheese as a substance.
