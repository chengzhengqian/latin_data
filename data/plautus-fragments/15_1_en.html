Fest. 254: prodigere (from which the adjective prodigus is derived) means “use up, waste.”

5 very weak wine

Fest. 378: sublestus means “weak” or “causing weakness.”

6 each of the two is crazily much in love with ∗∗∗

Non. 185: insanum can be an adverb like the regular insane, “crazily.”

7 wretched, bony, tottering, filthy women

Gell. 3.3.6: Favorinus assigned the Nervolaria to Plautus on the basis of this verse; Varro ling. 7.65 quotes the line as belonging to the Cistellaria; and Non. 248 quotes it as belonging to the Aulularia.
