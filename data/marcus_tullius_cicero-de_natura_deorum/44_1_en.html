“However, as to that, you may take your choice. What I want to know is, how did such a piece of good luck happen (for according to your school nothing in the universe was caused by design)—but be that 91 as it may, what accident was so potent, how did such a fortunate concourse of atoms come about, that suddenly men were born in the form of gods? Are we to think that divine seed fell from heaven to earth, and that thus men came into being resembling their sires? I wish that this were your story, for I should be glad to acknowledge my divine relations! But you do not say anything of the sort—you say that our likeness to the gods was caused by chance.

“And now is there any need to search for arguments to refute this? I only wish I could discover the truth as easily as I can expose falsehood.
