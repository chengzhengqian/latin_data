She is also called Diana Omnivaga (wide-wandering), not from her hunting, but because she is counted one 69 of the seven planets or ‘wanderers’ (vagari). She was called Diana because she made a sort of day in the night-time. She is invoked to assist at the birth of children, because the period of gestation is either occasionally seven, or more usually nine, lunar revolutions, and these are called menses (months), because they cover measured (mensa) spaces. Timaeus in his history with his usual aptness adds to his account of the burning of the temple of Diana of Ephesus on the night on which Alexander was born the remark that this need cause no surprise, since Diana was away from home, wishing to be present when Olympias was brought to bed. Venus was so named by our countrymen as the goddess who ‘comes’ (venire) to all things; her name is not derived from the word venustas (beauty) but rather venustas from it.
