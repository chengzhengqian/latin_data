XXI. And now for your colleague! He, after squandering the vast plunder which he had squeezed from the wealth of the tax-farmers and the lands and cities of our allies, plunder which had been swallowed up either in the gulf of his licentiousness or in novel and unheard-of extravagances, or had been spent upon purchases in the very scenes of his depredations, in order to raise that mountain of a villa at Tusculum; when he began to be in want, when work upon his intolerable structure had been suspended and finally had come to a full stop, he then sold to the king of Egypta his person, his fasces, the army of the Roman people, the name and the interdict of the immortal gods, the responses of the priests, the authority of the senate, the mandate of the Roman people, and the name and the dignity of this empire. Though the49 limits of his province were such as he had wished, had hoped for, had purchased at the cost of my outlawry, yet great as they were he could not contain himself within them; he led his army out of Syria. How could he legally have led them outside his province? He lent himself out as a hired assistant to the king of Alexandria.
