When he defended the Turoni, 2 who feared the conflict, thou wast not there; but a little later ye fought together where Cloio 3 the Frank had overrun the helpless lands of the Atrebates. There was a narrow passage at the junction of two ways, and a road crossed both the village of Helena, which was within bowshot, and the river, where that long but narrow path was supported by girders. The two strategic points were the crossroads and the bridge; there Aëtius and Majorian were respectively posted. The agger and the trames are the same thing.
