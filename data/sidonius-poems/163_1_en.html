II. Panegyric on Anthemius

and to your friend Felix, 1 O book of mine, and where their father’s library stands, a library such as not even the austere Philagrius 2 had, Probus, 3 having given you approbation, will cause you to be admitted. Here you will often be read by my kinswoman Eulalia, 4 of whose character, worthy of Athenian Minerva, strict greybeards and even her husband’s father 5 in the days when he wore the purple used to stand in awe.

But enough! Away with you, put out from the harbour, and, lest I weight you further with a load of sandy ballast, up with the anchor even while these verses sound!

  • 1See 9. 1 n.
  • 2See n. on 7. 156.
  • 3See 9. 6 and 333; Epist. IV. 1 is written to him.
  • 4Wife of Probus and cousin of Sidonius.
  • 5Magnus. The “purple” is that of the consulship.
