It was the same when we found ourselves in the hall of my own Marcellinus, 3 learned in the law, who being immeasurably truthful and strict is deemed harsh by the ignorant; but if anyone has proved him well, then he knows that our friend’s judgment is what he would like his own to be; for Marcellinus is never afraid to utter what is right—nor would he be were Sulla or savage Carbo or gloomy Marii or ferocious Cinnas threatening him, or if the swords of the triumvirs flashed about his head and side.
