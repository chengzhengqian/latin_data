3. And not long after, by the coming of a merchant vessel he had his wish.3 Now at that time Egbert, king of Kent, had sent from Britain a man named Wighard4 which had been chosen for the office of bishop, and had been well instructed in all ecclesiastical usage by the Roman scholars of the blessed pope Gregory in Kent; and Egbert desired to have him ordained bishop at Rome, so that having a prelate of his own nation and tongue,5 he and all the people under him might be the more perfectly instructed whether in the words or mysteries of the faith; insomuch as they would receive these things, not through an interpreter, but by the lips and hand withal of a man that was of their own kin and tribe.
