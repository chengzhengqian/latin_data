All this company, laid under the everlasting altarb and kept by a mother of purple-robed leaders, prays for pardon for our backslidings. Come, let us with pious tears wash the letters cut on the marble slabs under which lies my hope of unloosing the bonds which hold me fast. Cast thyself down along with me, noble city, on the holy graves, thou and all thy people; then when their souls and bodies rise again thou and all thy people will follow them.
