FRL II: ENNIUS

COMOEDIAE (F 1–6)

Ennius wrote comedies in the palliata tradition best known from the plays of Plautus and Terence, but little remains, and he was apparently not regarded as very successful in the genre (T 4). A plot cannot be determined for any play.

CAUPUNCULA (F 1)

The title of this play, corrupted in transmission, does not refer to a figure or an event from myth or history; the play is therefore generally regarded as a comedy.
