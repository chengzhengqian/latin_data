59 Paul the Deacon, Epitome of Festus

That daedalus [“skillful”], from the variety of things and works of art, is said of the earth in Lucretius [1.7; 1.228: daedala tellus], of Minerva in Ennius, of Circe in Virgil [Aen. 7.282; cf. Serv. ad Verg. G. 4.179] is easy to understand, since the Greeks have daidallein [“to embellish skillfully”] meaning “to give variety to.”
