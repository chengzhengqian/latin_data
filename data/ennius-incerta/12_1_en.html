28 Porphyrio, Commentary on Horace

“but a good part of men”: “good” [bona] is here said in place of “large” [magna], as Ennius and other ancients often did.1

29 Diomedes

Iterative or frequentative is a quality of verbs that has a regular force in the action, whence it takes on that appellation, since it repeats the effect of the action frequently, just as . . . likewise adeo [“I go to”], adis [“you go to”]: we repeat this when we say adito [“I go frequently”], aditas [“you go frequently”], as Ennius:

they frequently went up to him
