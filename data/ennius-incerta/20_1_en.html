52 Macrobius, Saturnalia

Antonius Gnipho1 [F 2 GRF], a learned man, whose school Cicero used to visit after the work of the forum, demonstrates that Salii2 were given to Hercules [cf. Verg. Aen. 8.285–304] in that volume in which he discusses what festra [“window”; unusual form of fenestra] is, which is a rather small opening in a shrine, a word that Ennius too used.

54 Grammatical fragment from Bobbio

55 Priscian

Ennius:

they all desire to be followed

adsectari [“to be followed”] passively [in form and meaning, as the Greek verb]: “to be followed.”

  • 1A teacher of grammar and rhetoric at Rome in the first half of the first century BC (cf. T 71, 111).
  • 2A particular type of priest.
  • 1From a list De dubiis nominibus, clarifying the gender of a number of words.
