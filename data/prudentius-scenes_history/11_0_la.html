Prudentius

perstat adhuc templumque gerit veneranda columna, nosque docet cunctis inmunes vivere flagris.

XLII. Passio Salvatoris

165Traiectus per utrumque latus laticem atque cruorem Christus agit: sanguis victoria, lympha lavacrum est. tunc duo discordant crucibus hinc inde latrones contiguis: negat ille Deum, fert iste coronam.

XLIII. Sepulcrum Christi

Christum non tenuit saxum, non claustra sepulcri; 170mors illi devicta iacet, calcavit abyssum. sanctorum populus superas simul ivit ad oras; seque dedit multis tactuque oculisque probandum.]1

XLIV. Mons Oliveti

Montis oliviferi Christus de vertice sursum ad Patrem rediit signans vestigia pacis. 175frondibus aeternis praepinguis liquitur umor, qui probat infusum terris de chrismate donum.

  • 1This quatrain lacks MS. authority. It was first printed by two editors of the sixteenth century, Giselinus (in his second edition) and Fabricius.
