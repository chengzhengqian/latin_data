The people of Halaesa were made independent in122 ecognition of the many valuable services and benefits rendered to Rome by themselves and their ancestors; but not long ago, in the consulship of Lucius Licinius and Quintus Mucius,a owing to an internal dispute regarding the way of filling vacancies in their Senate, they asked the Roman Senate to legislate for them. The Senate honoured them by passing a decree appointing the praetor Gaius Claudius Pulcher (son of Appius Claudius) to draw up for them regulations for filling the aforesaid vacancies.
