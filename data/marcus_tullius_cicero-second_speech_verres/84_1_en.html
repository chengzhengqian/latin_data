Need we wait to hear what the witnesses from Sicily shall tell us? Has anyone looked at that temple without becoming a witness to your rapacity and injustice and reckless wickedness? Has anyone walked along the road from the statue of Vertumnus to the Circus Maximus without being reminded of your rapacious greed at every step he took? LX. He conducted criminal as well as civil cases155 during his praetorship; and the following occurrence should not be passed unnoticed. A charge involving a fine was brought before him as praetor against Quintus Opimius, who was nominally prosecuted because, when tribune of the people, he had used his veto in violation of the Cornelian law, but really because, during his year of office, he had made a speech offensive to some man of high rank. If I chose to tell the full story of this trial, I should have to name, and cause pain to, a great many people.
