This man’s case offers no liberty for misconduct10 to anyone: no, gentlemen, none: the accused is such, the times are such, this court is such, and even—if it will not seem a presumptuous thing to say to an audience like this—the prosecutor is such, that neither stealth can effect without detection, nor force effect with impunity, the escape of a man so guilty, so abandoned, so clearly convicted of the charges brought against him. Before such a bench of judges as this, shall I fail to prove that Gaius Verres has acquired wealth illegally? Will such gentlemen as these succeed in disbelieving this multitude of Senators and Knights, of civic bodies, of respectable inhabitants of that noble province, of public and private records? Will they succeed in refusing what the people of Rome so strongly desires?
