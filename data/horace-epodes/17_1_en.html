The god perhaps will lay these disturbances to rest again, bringing round a welcome change.

Now is the time to soak our hair with balsam fit for Achaemenes and to lift the awful depression from our hearts with Mercury’s lyre strings; as the venerable Centaur sang when instructing his tall pupil:20 “Invincible lad, mortal son of the goddess Thetis, the land of Assaracus21 awaits you, which is divided by the cold streams of the yellow Scamander and the rolling Simois; from there the Fates with their unalterable thread have broken off your return, and your sea-green mother will not carry you home. While you’re there, lighten all your woes with wine and song,22 those sweet assuagers of horrid despair.”
