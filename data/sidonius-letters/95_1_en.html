VIII Sidonius To His Friend Eucherius,* Greeting

1. I reverence the ancients, but not so much as to underrate by comparison the qualities or the good services of my contemporaries. Even though the Roman commonwealth has sunk to such an extremity of helplessness that it no longer rewards those who are devoted to it, it does not follow that my times never give birth to a Brutus or a Torquatus.2 “What are you driving at?” you ask me. I am speaking to you about yourself, you marvel of efficiency, for the state owes you the recognition which history praises it for having paid to the above-named persons. 2.
