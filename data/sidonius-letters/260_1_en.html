Book VII

So much for that; I have already dwelt rather too long on it. 2. Meanwhile I introduce to you Petrus, a man of tribunician rank,1 the bearer of this letter, who earnestly desires the introduction, and who can indicate to you more succinctly by word of mouth the nature of his business. I pray you, from regard for this letter, to grant him all the help in your power, subject always to a proper respect for justice, for it is not my habit to oppose justice in urging the causes even of my more intimate friends. Deign to hold me in remembrance, my Lord Bishop.
