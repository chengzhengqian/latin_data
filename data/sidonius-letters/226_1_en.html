If the real story of these doings were unfolded by a worthy narrator it would make a pleasant tale; but as you yourself demand that my letter should bring you something both cheerful and lengthy, forgive me if, with all respect to your grave ears, I sketch this same reception accorded to our mail-carrier, a reception which will bear comparison with the jests of comedy. I hope thus to convince you that I do not want to look as if introducing a man for the second time and not now knowing him for the first.2 Besides, if the established rules3 allow a composition to start from any sort of material, why in the preparation of a casual screed should I go out of my way in seeking and searching for material instead of making the subject of my screed Amantius himself, whose luggage will be this screed of mine? 3.
