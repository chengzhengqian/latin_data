Gracious heaven! How proud our fathers were to see that with Christ’s help you had the power to teach and I to learn, and that you not only did what you could but took pleasure in what you did, so that you were declared to be no less kind-hearted than you were accomplished! 3. And truly you had found in Eusebius’s1 house a kind of mint of such studies; shaped on its philosophic anvil you used sometimes to expound to us the various principles involved in subjects and discourses in such a way that the very master who had taught you approved; at other times, just as Plato under Socrates was already almost superior to his teacher, so you under our teacher Eusebius already showed yourself a real Athenian, a past master of dialectic, versed in the Aristotelian categories, while Eusebius was still tempering our unstable, feeble, raw immaturity with stern rebuke or seasoning it with wholesome precepts. 4.
