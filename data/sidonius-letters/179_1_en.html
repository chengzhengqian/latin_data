In the generation between them and us our fathers, when they had scarcely emerged into full manhood, shared the same quarters, serving the Emperor Honorius5 as tribunes and secretaries; and in this sojourn abroad they lived on such affectionate terms that the least cause of their camaraderie was that they were talked6 of as the sons of friends. In the reign of Valentinian7 one of them governed part, the other the whole, of Gaul; but the honours of both were balanced in such a nicety of brotherly equipoise that the one who was second in status was first in the date of appointment. 3.
