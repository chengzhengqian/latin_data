IV Sidonius to the Lord Bishop Graecus,* Greeting

1. Our traveller and courier wears down the track of his customary journey in his frequent comings and goings along the stretch of road and through the extensive regions that divide our respective towns; therefore it becomes us also, having planned a diligent correspondence, to maintain these mutual attentions; indeed, we ought with a fixed purpose of mind to redouble them, using both other travellers who pass to and fro, and especially our messenger Amantius,1 lest perhaps he should think that he calls for our letters as a matter of habit rather than that we compose them as a labour of love, my Lord Bishop; and therefore I would have you more often remember us, your friends, among whom I presume to count myself, your friends who are uplifted by your good news and cast down by your bad. 2.
