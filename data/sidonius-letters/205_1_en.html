II Sidonius To The Lord Bishop Pragmatius,* Greeting

The venerable matron Eutropia is a lady whom I for my part regard2 as a shining example. She is one in whom abstemiousness and kindliness are equally matched; she feeds herself with fastings no less than she feeds the poor with food; she is un-slumbering in Christ’s service; sin is the only thing in her that is forced to sleep. The whole of this letter is so hyperbolically overwritten that I am sure Sidonius was more concerned with his style than with his sin.—W.H.S.
