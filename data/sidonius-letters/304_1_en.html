6. There you have the poem for you to read as you take your ease, watching me struggle in the dust and sweat, while like a charioteer who has already won his crown you view the contest from the grandstand. For the future, there is no reason for you to suspect that I intend to repeat this kind of offering, however pleased you may be with the present one—certainly not until I have ceased dreaming of misfortunes rather than poems. Farewell.
