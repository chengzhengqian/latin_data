But it may be that a minister of religion finds it offensive to be praised by means of Greek analogies derived from the idolatry of Eleusis,4 which he regards as inappropriate; therefore, leaving out of account the special sanctity of his mystic understanding,5 I would fain bring into comparison the solicitude (considered as a historical fact) of the venerable patriarch Joseph, who because he had prevision easily made provision against the dearth that was to follow the seven years of plenty: and indeed after all, from the moral point of view, a man seems in my judgment no whit inferior who, without the power to foresee, comes to the rescue in just such an emergency. 8.
