Book VI

XI Sidonius To The Lord Bishop Eleutherius,* Greeting

1. The present note commends to you a Jew,1 not because I favour a false belief through which all who are involved in it are lost, but because it is not seemly to pronounce any of those persons entirely beyond redemption, so long as life remains to him; for one who still has a chance of conversion still has a prospect of absolution. 2. It is best that he should tell you with his own lips in a personal interview the whole story of his trouble;2 for it is unwise to swell with discursive talk the trim compactness3 proper to the epistolary style: after all, from the standpoint of earthly business and jurisdiction people of this sort commonly have quite good cases; so even you4 may well defend this unfortunate man’s person while attacking his persuasion.5 Deign to hold me in remembrance, my Lord Bishop.
