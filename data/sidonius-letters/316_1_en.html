XII Sidonius to His Friend Trygetius,* Greeting

1. Does the town of Bazas1, built not on solid ground but on dust, Bazas with its Syrtes-like territory and shifting soil and its sands flying this way and that with the quarrelling winds, does Bazas hold you so firmly that neither the powers-that-be, nor friendships, nor oysters fattened in fishponds can lure you to Bordeaux, although you have been besought with earnest entreaties, and are only a short distance away, and your coming has been awaited for many days? Are you kept in two minds by the risks of the roads in the winter season, fearing a sort of landlubber’s shipwreck on an overland journey because the whirlwind of Bigorre is wont to obliterate the traces of the unstable roads? 2. Where, pray, has the memory of Calpis2 so quickly gone from your mind, Calpis lately conquered by your feet? Where has gone the memory of your camp pitched on the western bounds of Cadiz? Where the memory of those travels which my dear Trygetius ended at the same place3 as Hercules?
