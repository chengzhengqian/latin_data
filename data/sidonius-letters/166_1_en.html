You were delighted with the novelty of this work and stimulated by its ripe perfection: so you had no sooner seen the book than you asked for it that you might hurriedly go through it and copy it, and you had no sooner asked than you obtained it under a promise to return it speedily. It is not seemly that I should be deceived or that you should deceive me. It is time to restore the loan, because the book itself, if approved, ought by now to have afforded you ample satisfaction, and if not approved it ought to have aroused disgust. But however that may be, do discharge your promise quickly, for if you intend to wait longer before returning the wanted book, you are in danger of being thought to be fonder of parchment than of literature. Farewell.
