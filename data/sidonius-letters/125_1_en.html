The fourth1 hour was now well on its way, the sun was up and with increasing warmth had absorbed the dewy moisture of the night; heat and thirst were growing more intense, and in the boundless brightness the only cloud to give protection against the weather was one of dust. Moreover, the long road running through a green expanse of open plain and fully visible to the eye of the beholder made us groan, knowing as we did that it meant a late lunch for us; for although, with our journey still before us, it was not yet wearing us with toil, it was already worrying us with the prospect. 4. All this preamble, my honoured brother,2 is aimed at convincing you that I had scant freedom of mind or body and little time at my disposal when I complied with your demand. Well, then, to return to the substance of your letter, after the words which began by bidding me greeting, you asked me to send you a little poem, limited to twelve lines, suitable for inscribing on a capacious shell-shaped basin, in which the side where each handle is placed is traversed by six fluted groves, winding from the round base to the end of their sinuous course. 5. I suppose you intend to inscribe a line in each of their arched bellies or, better still, on each of their curving ridges, and you plan to offer the basin, thus embellished, to Queen Ragnahilda,3 in the hope, no doubt, of securing beforehand an invincible support alike for your ambitions and for your actions.
