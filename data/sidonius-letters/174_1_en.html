These are the men who (if I am to tell you even their smallest offences) are intoxicated with unaccustomed wealth and by their intemperate use of it reveal how small is their experience of possession; for they like to march in armour to feasts, in white to funerals, in furs to church, in mourning to weddings, in beaver fur to prayers. No man, order, or occasion of any kind pleases them. In the forum they are Scythians, in a room they are vipers, at a dinner party they are buffoons, in their exactions harpies, in conversations statues, in investigations brute beasts, in discussions snails, in contracts usurers; in intelligence they are like stones, in judgment wooden, fiery in anger, unforgiving as iron, in their friendship they are panthers, in their witticisms bears, in their deceitfulness foxes, in their arrogance bulls, in their voracity minotaurs. 5.
