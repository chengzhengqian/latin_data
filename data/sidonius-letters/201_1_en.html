XXI Sidonius To His Friends Sacerdos And Justinus,* Greeting

Victorius, your paternal uncle, a most distinguished man and learned in every respect, showed mastery in everything but particularly in poetical composition. I also from boyhood have constantly cultivated the Muses; but now you come forth as heirs of your kinsman’s house, by legal right and no less by merit. So then I am akin to a poet by profession, you by blood; accordingly, it is entirely right that each of us should succeed the departed according to the nature of his connection; so do you keep the property for yourselves, and hand over the verses to me.1 Farewell.
