And now it is not only in the country parishes that solitude reigns; to crown all, the congregation even in the city churches is becoming sparser. 9. What comfort is left to the faithful when not only the teaching of the clergy but even the memory of them perishes?1 If, when one of the clergy dies, he does not admit an heir to his office by handing on his benediction,2 then assuredly it is the priesthood that dies in that church, not the priest. And what hope would you say remains when the end of a man’s life implies the end of religion? Examine more deeply these losses of spiritual members, and you will certainly realise that with each removal of a bishop3 you will have the faith of a people4 put in jeopardy.
