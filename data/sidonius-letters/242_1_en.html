It was they who, out of love for the State, feared not to hand over Seronatus2 to the law when he was lavishing whole provinces on the barbarians; and the State in its turn scarcely had the courage to put him to death after his conviction. 3. Is this our due reward for enduring want and fire and sword and pestilence, for swords fed fat with gore and warriors emaciated with hunger?
