V Sidonius To The Lord Bishop Theoplastus,* Greeting

He who carries a letter from me to you pleads my cause without knowing it; for in becoming the opportune bearer of a homage I longed to pay, he is really conferring a kindness while imagining that he is receiving it. So it is now with the venerable Donidius,2 a man worthy to be numbered among the most sterling of mankind.3 I commend to you his client and slaves, who have left home on the urgent business of patron or master.4 Support the labour
