Now you ask what I think of my plunder. I should have liked not to tell you, in order to prolong your suspense; for by keeping silence about the opinion I have formed, I should gain an ample revenge. But already you yourself must be feeling a justifiable pride, knowing that you have in you such pre-eminent power of eloquence that the sheer force of enjoyment wrings from the reader, whether reluctantly or willingly, an overmastering need to praise. So here is what I think of your writings even after the bad treatment I received from you. 10. I read a work laborious and many-sided, vigorous and lofty, well divided by headings, well provided with examples, bipartite in the plan of its dialogue, quadripartite in the presentation of the subject-matter. Much you have written ardently, much more with grandeur; some parts are simple without being uncouth, others are subtle without being crafty; weighty matters are treated maturely, deep things with earnest care, doubtful points resolutely, debatable points controversially; some things are handled sternly, others winningly, all is morally exquisite and powerfully eloquent. 11.
