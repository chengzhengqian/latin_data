XXV Sidonius to his Friend Domnulus,* Greeting

1. I cannot refrain from hastening to make you a sharer in a great joy; for of course you want to know what Patiens,2 our father in Christ and our bishop, did with characteristic piety and firmness after setting out for Cabillonum.3 When he arrived at the aforesaid town he was partly preceded and partly escorted by the priests associated with him in the province.4 Their purpose, you must know, was the ordination of a man as chief priest of that community,

  • 1Is curator here in its proper legal sense (guardian of one between puberty and twenty-five years of age, etc.) or = tutor (cf. sub annis adhuc tutelaribus)?—A.
  • 2Saint Patiens was a rich and generous man, Archbishop of Lyon before a.d. 470. Cf. Ep. VI. 12; II. 10. 2; III. 12. 3. Gregory of Tours, Hist. Franc. II. 24; Duchesne, Fastes Épisc. II. 163; Sidonius, Ep. V. 17. 3, note.
  • 3Châlon-sur-Saône. For elections to bishoprics cf. Stevens Sid. Apoll., pp. 122–129; Nora Chadwick, Poetry and Letters in Early Christian Gaul, 290–292 (reading Châlon-sur-Saône for Châlons).
  • 4Is collegio here technical? Is provincialium here political or ecclesiastical?—A. Cf. Ep. VI. 1. 3.
