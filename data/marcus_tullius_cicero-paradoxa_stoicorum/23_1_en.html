But who can ever have correctly described a person who needs to make gain 47as a wealthy man? For the value of wealth consists in abundance, and abundance means a full and overflowing supply of goods; and as this will never be attained by you, you will never be a wealthy man at all.

But as my money meets with your scorn, and rightly (for to the ideas of the common public it is moderate, to yours, non-existent, to mine a modest sum), I will be silent about myself and speak about 48the subject of property.
