A further result is that even the candidate himself cannot devote all his attention, all his care and all his unremitting effort to the campaign. He is also working on the plans for his prosecution, and this is of course his main concern and no mere side line. It is a big undertaking for you to collect the evidence with which to drive a man into exile, especially a man who is not friendless and without support and one who is defended by his own efforts, by his friends and even by strangers. We all rush to repel danger and those of us who are not open enemies fulfil the obligations and good offices of the closest friendship even in the case of total strangers when their civil rights are in danger. Knowing from experience how much work 46 is involved in fighting an election or conducting a defence or a prosecution, I have observed that in an election the driving force is the spirit of partisanship, in a defence the sense of obligation and in a prosecution sheer hard work. It is, therefore, my view that it is quite impossible for a man simultaneously to prepare and organize with due care both a prosecution and a campaign for the consulship.
