34 Finally he said: “If someone must die for you, Clitus is the first choice; but when you award the prizes of a victory, those bear off the palm who most wantonly mock the memory of your father. 35 You assign to me the province of Sogdiana,a so often rebellious, and not only untamed but not even capable of being subdued. I am sent to wild beasts, to which 36 Nature has given incorrigible recklessness. But of what concerns me I have nothing to say. You scorn the soldiers of Philip, forgetting that if old Atarrhiasb here had not called back the younger men when they shrank from battle, we should still be lingering around 37 Halicarnassus. How then would you have subdued all Asia with those young men of yours? That is true, in my opinion, which your unclec is known to have said in Italy, that he had encountered men, you women.”d

Nothing among all the taunts which Clitus had ill advisedly and rashly uttered had more aroused the king than the honourable mention made of Parmenion.e Yet he restrained his resentment, content 39 with ordering Clitus to leave the banquet. And he added nothing else than that perhaps if Clitus had spoken at greater length, he would have taunted him with having saved his life; for of this he had often 40 arrogantly boasted. And when Clitus still delayed to rise, those who had reclined next to him laid hands upon him and with remonstrances and warning were 41 trying to lead him from the room.
