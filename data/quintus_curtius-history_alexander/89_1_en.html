11

Then he put Menona in place of Andromachus and executed those who had slain his general. Certain tyrants,b including Aristonicus and Stesilaüs of Methymnê, he handed over to their own subjects, who put them to death by torture because of their outrages.

12

Then Alexander gave audience to the envoys of the Athenians, the Rhodians, and the Chians; the Athenians congratulated him on his victory and begged that the Greek prisoners should be restored to their country; the Rhodians and the Chians made 13complaints of their garrisons. The requests of all seemed just, and were granted. To the people of Mitylenê also, because of their remarkable loyalty to his cause,c he not only repaid the money which they had spent on the war, but also added a great 14tract of neighbouring territory. To the kings of the Cypriotes besides, who had both gone over from Darius to him and had sent him a fleet when he was besieging Tyre, honour was paid according to their deserts.
