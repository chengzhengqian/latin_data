At the Granicus, we fought against the enemy with a very small part of our strengtha; in Cilicia, Syria could receive us in case we were defeated, and the Tigris and the Euphrates were mighty bulwarks of 11our realm. We have come to the place where, if we are worsted, there is no room even for flight. Everything behind us has been consumed by so long a warb; the cities do not have their inhabitants, the fields have no labourers. Our wives also and our children follow this army, an easy prey for the enemy unless we oppose our bodies in defence of our dearest pledges.

“So far as my duty is concerned, I have assembled an army which this almost boundless plain can hardly contain, I have distributed men and horses, I have seen to it that so vast a multitude may not lack supplies, I have chosen a place in which our army 13can deploy. The rest is in your power; only have courage to conquer, and scorn mere reputation, the weakest of weapons against brave men.c What you have heretofore feared as valour is rashness; and when this has spent its first attack, it becomes weak, like some insects when they have ejected their sting.d
