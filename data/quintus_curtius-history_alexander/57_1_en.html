The envoys replied that there was a temple of Hercules outside of the city, in the place which they call Old Tyreb; that there the king would properly offer sacrifice to 5the god. Alexander could not restrain his anger, which as a rule he was unable to control. Accordingly he said: “You indeed, relying on your situation, because you live on an island, despise this army of foot-soldiers, but I will soon show you that you are on the mainland. Therefore I want you to know that I will either enter your city or besiege it.” 6The envoys, dismissed with this response, began to warn their countrymen that a king whom Syria and Phoenicia had received they also should suffer to 7enter their city. But the Tyrians, having plenty of confidence in their situation, had decided to sustain a siege; for a strait of four stadia separates the city from the mainland and was especially exposed to the Africc wind, which rolls upon the shore wave on wave 8from the deep. And there was nothing which more than that wind stood in the way of receiving the work by which the Macedonians were preparing to join the island to the mainland.
