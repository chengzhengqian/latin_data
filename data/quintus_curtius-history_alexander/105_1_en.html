Then Ario-barzanes and Orontobates led the Persians with the 8Mardii and the Sogdiani. These men commanded divisions of the forces, in charge of the whole was Orsines, a descendant of the “seven Persians”a and tracing his genealogy also to Cyrus, that most re­nowned 9king. These were followed by other nations, not very well known even to their allies. Phradates came after these nations, leading fifty four-in-hand chariots, with a large army of Caspii.b The Indi and the rest of the dwellers on the Red Sea,c mere names rather than auxiliaries, were behind the chariots.

10

The rear of this part of the army was brought up by other scythe-bearing chariots, to which he had joined the foreign soldiers.d These were followed by those who are known as the Lesser Armenians,e the Armenians by the Babylonians, and both by the Belitae and those who dwelt in the mountains of the Cossaeif

11

After these marched the Gortuae, really a Euboean race,g who formerly followed the Medes, but were now degenerate and ignorant of their native customs. Next to these he put the Phrygians and the Cataon-ians. Then the Parthyaei, inhabiting the lands now held by the Parthians from Scythia, brought up the rear of the whole force.
