Then Nearchus said that no one could deny that only the blood and stock of Alexander was suited to 11 royal majesty, but that to wait for a king who was not yet born, and to pass over one who was already living, suited neither the desires of the Macedonians nor their exigencies; that the king had a son by Barsinêa; he ought to be presented with the crown. 12 His speech was approved by no one; and so, clashing their shields with their spears after their custom, they persisted in uproar.
