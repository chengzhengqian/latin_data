To wage war with captives and women is not my habit; he must be armed whom I hate. But if it 18 were at least in good faith that he asks for peace, I might perhaps consider whether I would grant it. But, in solemn truth, since he now with letters tempts my soldiers to betray me, and now with money bribes my friends to kill me, I must pursue him to destruction, not as a legitimate enemy, but as an assassin who resorts to poison.

“Furthermore, as to the conditions of peace which you propose, if I accept them, they make him the 19victor. He generously offers me what is beyond the Euphrates. You have forgotten then, where it is in fact that you now come to see me. Surely I am across the Euphrates ! Therefore my camp is beyond the most liberal boundary of the dowry which he promises. Drive me from here, in order that I 20may know that what you offer to cede is yours. With like generosity he gives me his daughter, who forsooth is the destined bride of one of his slaves. A high honour truly he confers upon me in preferring me to Mazaeus as a son-in-law !
