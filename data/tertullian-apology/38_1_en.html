XV. And all the other wanton men of genius to promote your pleasures do dishonour to the gods. Look at the pretty trifles of a Lentulus or a Hostiliusd—is it your actors or your gods that make you laugh with their jokes and tricks? Think of “Anubis the Adulterer,” “The Gentleman Moon,” “Diana Lashed,”e and the reading of “The Will of the late Jove, deceased,” and the mockery of “Three hungry Herculeses.” But the literature of the stage2 too attributes every foulness to the gods. The Sun laments his son hurled from the skyf—to your delight. Cybele sighs for her proud shepherd—and you don’t blush. You suffer a song to be sung of the illdeeds of Joveg; you let Juno, Venus, Minerva submit to a shepherd’s award.
