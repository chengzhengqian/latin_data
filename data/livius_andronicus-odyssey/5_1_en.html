VIII

Ulysses in Alcinous’ palace. During the pauses in Demodocus’ recital, Ulysses in grief wipes his eyes:

Festus: ‘Noegeum.’ Some say that this is a kind of cloak fringed with purple, while others say it is white, translucent, derived as it were from ‘naucum,’ which is the very thin and whitish peel of certain fruits. So Livius writes in The Odyssey—

as soon as he wiped away with the mantle the tears from his face,

‘noegeo,’ that is, with a whitea mantle.

  • aThis is wrong. Livius by ‘noegeum’ means simply the φᾶρος, or cloak; in Homer, Odysseus covers his head to hide his grief, wipes his eyes, and again uncovers his head. Homer does not make Odysseus dry his tears with the cloak.
