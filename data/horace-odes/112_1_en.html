Odes IV

9The poet will not allow Lollius to die

You may imagine that these words will perish which I, born by the far-echoing Aufidus, speak to be wedded to the strings of the lyre by an art never before displayed to the public. Yet if Maeonian Homer holds first place, it does not follow that the Muses of Pindar and of Ceos28 are forgotten, or those of Alcaeus and Stesichorus, respectively aggressive and august; nor has time eclipsed the fun that Anacreon once enjoyed; and that love still breathes and that passion still lives which the Aeolian girl29 confided to the strings of her lyre.
