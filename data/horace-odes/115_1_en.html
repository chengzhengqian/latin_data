Odes IV

Telephus, the young man whom you are after, belongs to a higher class; he is in the clutches of a girl who is both rich and voluptuous, and he likes the fetters that hold him so fast. Phaethon, who was shot down in flames, frightens off dreams of avarice, and the winged Pegasus, who disdained the weight of his earthly rider, Bellerophon,32 provides a weighty moral: you should always strive for what is appropriate to yourself; you should avoid an unequal partner, regarding it as wrong to direct your hopes beyond what is permissible.

So come now, the last of my loves (for after this I shall feel no ardour for any other woman); learn some tunes that you can sing to me with your lovely voice. Black thoughts will be lightened by song.
