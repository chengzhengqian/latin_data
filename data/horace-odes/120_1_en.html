Odes IV

With Caesar in charge of affairs, peace will not be driven out by civic madness or violence, or by the anger that beats out swords and makes cities wretched by turning them against one another.

Those who drink the deep Danube will not infringe the Julian Laws, nor will the Getae or the Chinese42 or the untrustworthy Parthians, or the people born by the river Don.

As for ourselves, on working days and holidays, surrounded by the merry God of Freedom’s43 gifts, along with our wives and children, we shall first offer due prayers to the gods; then in song accompanied by Lydian pipes we shall sing in our fathers’ fashion of leaders who lived their lives like true men, of Troy and Anchises and the offspring of kindly Venus.44

  • 42Chinese silk was imported by the Romans, but the idea of political submission is fanciful hyperbole.
  • 43Bacchus.
  • 44Augustus via Aeneas.
