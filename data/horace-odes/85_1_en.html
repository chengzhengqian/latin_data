Odes III

Quick, my boy, prepare a toast to the new month, to midnight, to Murena the augur!52 Cups are mixed appropriately with three or nine ladles.53 The inspired poet who loves the odd-numbered Muses will ask for three times three ladles; the Grace who links arms with her naked sisters does not allow more than three, for fear of brawls. I want to go mad. Why have the blasts of the Berecyntian pipe not begun? Why does the syrinx just hang beside the silent lyre?54 I detest close-fisted hands. Scatter roses! Let that killjoy Lycus hear the wild uproar, and the woman next door who is not well matched with old Lycus. You, Telephus, who, with your thick shiny hair, are like the clear Evening Star, receive the attentions of Rosy, who is just the right age for you. I burn with a smouldering passion for my Sweetheart.
