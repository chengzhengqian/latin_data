In the meantime, while you take out your swift arrows and she sharpens her fearsome teeth, the one who decides the contest, they say, has put his bare foot on the palm of victory, and cools his shoulders in the gentle breeze as they are brushed by his scented hair—like Nireus, or the one who was carried off from many-fountained Ida.56

21In praise of wine

O born with me in Manlius’ consulship, whether you bring with you reproaches or fun or quarrels and passionate love or ready sleep, o kindly jar, under whatever epithet57 you preserve the choice Massic, you deserve to be called forth on an auspicious day; so come down,58 for Corvinus urges me to bring out an especially mellow wine. Although he is steeped in the Socratic dialogues he will not neglect you like an uncouth ascetic; they say that even old Cato, with all his moral rigour, often thawed out with unmixed wine.
