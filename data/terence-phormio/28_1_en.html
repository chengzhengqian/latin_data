Phormio

get There, coming out of his private gymnasium. 39 (they stand aside)

Enter phaedria and dorio from Dorio’s house.

phae Dorio, listen to me, for goodness’ sake.

dor I’m not listening.

phae Just for a moment.

dor Can’t you leave me alone?

phae Hear what I have to say.

dor I’m tired of hearing the same thing a thousand times.

phae But I’m about to say something you’ll be glad to hear.

dor Speak. I’m listening.

phae Can’t I persuade you to wait these next three days? (Dorio makes to depart) Where are you off to?

dor I thought it was strange if you had anything new to offer.

ant (aside to Geta, ironically) Oh dear! I’m afraid the pimp is asking for trouble. 40

get (aside to Antipho) I fear so too.

phae You still don’t believe me?

dor (sarcastically) How did you guess?

phae What if I give my word?

dor Nonsense!

phae You’ll find your kindness was a splendid investment.

dor Fairy tales!

phae Believe me, you’ll be glad you did it. It’s true, for god’s sake.

dor Dreams!

  • 39Literally, “wrestling ground,” meaning the pimp’s house.
  • 40Literally, “is sewing something for his own head,” a colourful phrase which combines the metaphorical “stitch together trouble for someone” and the proverbial “bring something on one’s own head.” Some editors give these words to Geta, as a completion of the sentence begun by Antipho, though the MSS give the whole sentence to Antipho.
