No. 33 (Ep. CXXVI) (a.d. 411) To The Holy Lady And The Revered Handmaid Of God, Albina,a Augustine Sends Greeting In The Lord

It is right that I should assuage, and not augment,1 the grief of your spirit, which you describe in your letter as inexpressible; in this way I may, if possible, heal your suspicions and not add to the agitation of your heart, so venerable and so devoted to God, by indignantly repudiating them for my own sake. The people of Hippo did nothing to make our holy brother, your son Pinianus, apprehensive of death, even though he himself perhaps entertained some fear of it.   • aWhen Albina, Melania, and Pinianus came to Hippo, the people there, allured by Pinianus’s wealth, sought to have Pinianus forcibly ordained. Augustine was unable to restrain them from extracting from Pinianus an oath that he would not leave Hippo and would not receive ordination elsewhere, but the trio, despite the oath, returned next day to Tagaste. Letters cxxv.-cxxviii. then passed between Augustine, Alypius, and Albina, warmly discussing the validity of Pinianus’s oath, which Augustine held to be valid, and repudiating the charge of covetousness. When Pinianus lost his property, the Hipponiensians quietly let the matter drop.
