If these lips that address you have enough of horror and pollution; if I never chant these spells when fasting from human flesh; if I have often chopped up bosoms inhabited by the divinity,2 and washed them with warm brains; if any infant would have lived when his head and inner organs were laid upon your platters—then comply with my petition. I ask not for one who lurks in the depths of Tartarus and has long been accustomed to the darkness, but for some soul that is just going down and leaving the light behind him; he still lingers at the entrance of the chasm that leads to gloomy Orcus, and, though he obey my spells now, he will go down but once to the shades. Let the ghost of a Pompeian, who but lately was alive,3 foretell all the future to Pompey’s son, if ye owe gratitude to the civil war.”

When she had spoken thus, she raised her head and foaming mouth, and saw beside her the ghost of the unburied corpse. It feared the lifeless frame and the hateful confinement of its former prison; it shrank from entering the gaping bosom, the vital parts, and the flesh divided by a mortal wound. Hapless wretch! unjustly robbed of death’s last gift—the inability to die a second time. Erictho marvelled that fate had power to linger thus.
