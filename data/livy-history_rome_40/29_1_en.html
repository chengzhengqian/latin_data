The armies were then assigned, and the consuls were given four legions (each of which was to have 5,200 Roman infantry and 300 cavalry) and 15,000 infantry and 800 cavalry from the allies and those with Latin rights.51 In the Spains the old praetors had their imperium extended and retained command of their armies; and they were also assigned a supplementary force of 3,000 Roman citizens, together with 200 cavalry, as well as 6,000 infantry and 300 cavalry from the allies and those with Latin rights. Naval affairs were not neglected, either. The consuls were ordered to organize the election of duumvirs to supervise them, and the duumvirs were to see to the launching of twenty ships, to be manned by crews of Roman citizens who had been slaves but with command restricted to the freeborn. Defense of the coastline was so divided between the duumvirs (each of them commanding ten of the ships) that the promontory of Minerva52 would serve as a line between them; from there one was to stand guard over the area to the right as far as Massilia, and the other over the left as far as Barium.53
