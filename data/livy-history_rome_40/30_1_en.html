Disturbed by these prodigies and natural disasters, the senators issued a decree that the consuls should offer full-grown victims in sacrifice to whatever gods they thought fit and also that the decemvirs should consult the Books.58 Following a decree of the decemvirs, a one-day period of supplication at all the couches in Rome was enjoined. Also at the dictate of the decemvirs, the senate decreed—and the consuls issued a declaration to the effect—that a three day period of supplication and holiday should be held throughout Italy. Because of a revolt by the Corsicans and a war fomented in Sardinia by the Ilienses59 the senate had voted that 8,000 infantry and 300 cavalry be mobilized from the allies and holders of Latin rights, and that the praetor Marcus Pinarius should take them across to Sardinia with him. However, such was the virulence of the plague that the consuls reported that the dead and the sick were everywhere too numerous for such a total to be reached. The praetor was then ordered to make up the difference in strength from the troops of the proconsul Gnaeus Baebius, who was wintering at Pisae, and then cross to Sardinia.

The praetor Lucius Duronius, to whom Apulia had fallen as his province, was assigned the additional responsibility of investigating the Bacchanalia. During the previous year some residual outgrowths, as it were, from the earlier mischief had already come to light; but the inquiries conducted before the praetor Lucius Pupius60 were, in fact, only in their initial stages rather than concluded. The senators ordered the new praetor to exterminate this evil to prevent it spreading once more. In addition, on the authority of the senate, the consuls brought before the people a law against corruption.
