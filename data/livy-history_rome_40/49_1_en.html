This was because they were scattered and dispersed all over the plain and at no point did their enemy surround them as groups. Even so, as many as 12,000 were cut down, and more than 5,000 men captured, along with 400 horses and 62 military standards. The stragglers who reached home after the rout turned back a second army of Celtiberians coming to the fight by telling them of the surrender of Contrebia and their own defeat. They all then immediately slipped away to their villages and strongholds. Leaving Contrebia, Flaccus conducted his legions on a marauding expedition through Celtiberia, assaulting large numbers of strongholds, until most of the Celtiberians surrendered.

34. Such were that year’s operations in Hither Spain. In the farther province the praetor Publius Manlius97 fought a number of successful battles against the Lusitanians.

The Latin colony of Aquileia98 was founded that year in Gallic territory. Three thousand infantry each received 50 acres, and centurions each received 100 and cavalrymen 140.99 The founding triumvirs were Publius Cornelius Scipio Nasica, Gaius Flaminius and Lucius Manlius Acidinus. Two temples were dedicated in the course of the year. One was the temple of Venus Erycina100 at the Porta Collina; this was dedicated by the duumvir Lucius Porcius Licinus, son of Lucius, and it had been promised in a vow by Lucius Porcius as consul during the Ligurian War.101
