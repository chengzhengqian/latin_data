27. When no assistance appeared anywhere, Aemilius assumed his riders had been intercepted and felt he should no longer delay putting his own fortunes to the test. The enemy had been conducting the siege in a rather remiss and halfhearted manner, and before they arrived back on the scene he deployed his troops at the four camp gates84 so they could make a concerted sally on every side once given the signal.

To the four supplementary cohorts Aemilius attached a further two, putting his legate Marcus Valerius in command, and ordered them to make their charge from the praetorian gate. At the main gate on the right he deployed the hastati of the first legion, and placed the principes of that same legion in reserve; the military tribunes Marcus Servilius and Lucius Sulpicius were given command of these troops. The third legion was drawn up before the main gate on the left. The only change here was that the principes were set in front and the hastati in reserve; the military tribunes Sextus Iulius Caesar and Lucius Aurelius Cotta were given command of this legion.85 The legate Quintus Fulvius Flaccus was stationed at the quaestorian gate with the right auxiliary unit; and two cohorts plus the triarii of the two legions were instructed to stay back to protect the camp.
