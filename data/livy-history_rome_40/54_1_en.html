If their sentiments must be gauged from their conversations with each other, or from the tenor of their shouts before the commander when he was haranguing them, they had openly and loudly proclaimed that they would either keep the commander in the province or come to Italy with him.

The dispute between the praetor and the legate was interrupted by a motion from the consuls, who recommended that their provinces should be provided for113 before the praetor’s army was discussed. An entirely new army was formally assigned to the consuls: two Roman legions for each, with the appropriate cavalry, and the regular number of allies and holders of Latin rights, that is 15,000 infantry and 800 cavalry. With this army the consuls were ordered to make war on the Ligurian Apuani. Publius Cornelius and Marcus Baebius114 had their imperium prorogued and were instructed to govern their provinces until the arrival of the consuls; at that point they were to demobilize the armies under their command and return to Rome.

Discussion of Tiberius Sempronius’ army followed. The consuls were instructed to mobilize a new legion for him consisting of 5,200 infantry and 400 cavalry, with an additional force of 1,000 Roman infantry and fifty cavalry. They were also to conscript for him 7,000 infantry and 300 cavalry from the allies and holders of Latin rights. This was the army with which it was decided that Tiberius Sempronius was to proceed to Hither Spain.
