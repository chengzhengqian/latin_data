This led to violations on both sides and, as these increased every day, war broke out. Finally, since they were unable to cope with the violence and numbers of the enemy, the Thracians abandoned their villages on the plains and withdrew to a mountain of enormous height that they call Donuca.209 The Bastarnae wanted to climb it but as they vainly attempted to approach the peaks a storm overtook them like the one by which it is said the

Gauls were crushed when they were plundering Delphi.210 They were overwhelmed with a torrential downpour, followed by densely falling hail, while the sky roared deafeningly with thunderclaps and flickered with blinding lightening; and on top of that there were thunderbolts flashing on every side, making it look as if it were their bodies that were the targets; and not only the rank and file but their leaders, too, were struck and brought to the ground. The Thracians proceeded to pounce on the shattered Bastarnae while they were being struck down or were rushing to destruction as they blindly sought headlong flight over towering cliffs; but the Bastarnae themselves claimed that the gods were responsible for their rout and that the heavens were falling in on them.
