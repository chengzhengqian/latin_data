Then, to avoid the impression of having made prior inquiries himself or of now making slanderous allegations, he kept telling you to ask the men he named whether they had carried a sword so that—as if the matter were actually in question!—they could be regarded as guilty when you had interrogated them about what they

freely admit. Why do you not rather have them asked whether their purpose in taking up weapons was to kill you, and whether I authorized it and knew of it? This is what you want to appear to be so despite what they say and what is quite plainly the case. They say they took them up for self-protection. Whether they were right or wrong in so doing, they will themselves account for their action. Do not confuse my case with that—it has nothing to do with what they did. Otherwise explain whether we were going to attack you openly or covertly. If it was openly, why did we not all have weapons? Why did no one have them except those who manhandled your spy? If it was covertly, what was the program? When I had left on the carousing that followed the breakup of our banquet, would the four36 have stayed behind to attack you in your sleep? How would they have escaped detection? They were strangers, they were my men and they were particularly suspect because they had been involved in a fight shortly before. How were they going to get away themselves after killing you? Could your residence have been captured and taken by storm with four swords?
