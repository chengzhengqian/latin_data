Furthermore, periods of supplication116 were proclaimed in honor of Fulvius’ sterling service to the state. The other praetors were also sent off to their provinces, and Quintus Fabius Buteo had his imperium prorogued in Gaul. It was decided that there should be a total of eight legions that year, apart from the veteran army in service in Liguria, which anticipated discharge in the near future. And because of the epidemic that had already been ravaging the city of Rome and Italy for two years,117 it was only with difficulty that the complement even of this force was attained.

37. The praetor Tiberius Minucius now died, and not long after him so did the consul Gaius Calpurnius118 and many other distinguished men of all classes. Eventually the calamitous death toll became regarded as a portent. The pontifex maximus, Gaius Servilius, was ordered to look into ways of appeasing the anger of the gods, while the decemvirs were to consult the Books119 and the consul was to offer vows of gifts and present gilded statues to Apollo, Aesculapius and Salus120 (and he did indeed offer the vows and make the presentation).121 The decemvirs proclaimed a two-day period of supplication for the return of the country’s health, to be held in Rome but also
