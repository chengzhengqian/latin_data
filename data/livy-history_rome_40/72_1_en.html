He then commanded the military tribunes to order all the infantry and cavalry troops to take up their weapons and maneuver under arms. Dismissed after this display, the delegates discouraged their people from bringing help to the beleaguered city. The townspeople raised fires on their watchtowers at night—the agreed signal—but to no avail; and after losing their one hope of assistance they surrendered. The sum of 2,400,000 sesterces was taken from them, plus 400 knights from the best families. These were not termed hostages since they were ordered to serve in the army, but they were in fact to be a guarantee of loyalty.

48. From there Gracchus next marched to the city of Alce,177 the site of the Celtiberian camp from which the delegates had recently come to him. For several days he harassed the enemy with minor skirmishes, launching light-armed troops against their outposts; then, each day, he engaged them in more intensive fighting in order to draw them all from their fortifications. When he felt this aim had been adequately achieved, he ordered the commanders of the auxiliary forces to join battle and then to suddenly turn tail as if they were outnumbered, and run in disorder for the camp. He himself marshaled his troops at all the gates within the rampart.
