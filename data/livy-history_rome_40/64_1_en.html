The triumvir epulo144 Publius Manlius, who had recently returned from Farther Spain, was also a casualty, and Quintus Fulvius, son of Marcus, was co-opted as a triumvir in his place while still wearing the toga praetexta.145 On the question of the appointment of a rex sacrificulus146 to replace Gnaeus Cornelius Dolabella, there was a dispute between the pontifex maximus Gaius Servilius and the duumvir navalis147 Lucius Cornelius Dolabella. The pontiff ordered Dolabella to resign from his office so that he could install him in the priesthood. When the duumvir refused to do that,148 he was fined for it by the pontiff, and when Dolabella appealed the dispute came before the people.

When most of the tribes149 had already been called in to vote and were stating their decision that the duumvir should obey the pontiff and that the fine be waived if he resigned his office, an unfavorable sign came from the sky to interrupt the proceedings. After that the pontiffs felt there was a religious impediment to inaugurating Dolabella, and they instead installed Publius Cloelius Siculus, who had the second-place nomination.150
