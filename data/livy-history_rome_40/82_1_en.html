Even so, Antigonus could have been advanced to the throne if either he had been present himself or the death of the king had been immediately brought to public notice. The doctor Calligenes, who was in charge of Philip’s treatment, did not wait for the king’s death. At the first signs that the case was hopeless, he followed a prearranged plan: he sent messengers to Perseus by horses posted in relays and concealed the king’s death from all outside the palace until his arrival.

57. As a result, Perseus caught everyone off guard and ignorant of the situation, and he ascended a throne won by crime.

Philip’s death came at an opportune moment for delaying and withdrawing some forces from the war.203 For, a few days later, the Bastarnian people204 left their homes, as they had long been encouraged to do, and crossed the Danube with a large force of infantry and cavalry. Antigonus and Cotto then went ahead to make a report to the king. Cotto was a Bastarnian nobleman and Antigonus had often before been sent by the king as an envoy with this same Cotto to incite the Bastarnae to arms.205 Not far from Amphipolis they were first met by a vague rumor and then unambiguous reports that the king was dead, and this threw into confusion the entire scenario that they had planned.
