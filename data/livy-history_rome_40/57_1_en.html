38. As the Ligurians had not been expecting a war before the arrival of the consuls in the province they were taken by surprise and about 12,000 surrendered. Cornelius and Baebius, after first consulting the senate on the matter by letter, decided to bring them down from their hills into land on the plains, far from their home, so they would have no hope of returning—until this was done, they thought, there would be no end to the Ligurian War. There was in Samnite territory some public land that belonged to the Roman people and which had been the property of the Taurasini.126 As this was where the consuls wished to relocate127 the Ligurian Apuani, they issued an order for them to come down from the hills with their wives and children and bring with them all their possessions. The Ligurians made numerous appeals through spokesmen, begging not to be forced to leave their household gods, the place where they had been born and the tombs of their forefathers; and they promised to surrender their arms and give hostages. As their appeals were of no avail but they had not the strength to fight, they obeyed the order. Some 40,000 free men along with their wives and children were relocated at public expense. They were granted 150,000 sesterces for the purchase of necessary items for their new homes. Charged with the division of the land and distribution of the money were Cornelius and Baebius, the same men who had handled the relocation.
