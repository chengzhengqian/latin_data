They added that the senate considered it appropriate that if they were taken into his charge they be disarmed and sent to the consuls.

The praetors Publius Manlius and Quintus Fulvius Flaccus arrived in their provinces at this same time,41 Manlius in Farther Spain, which he had also received as his province in his previous praetorship,42 and Flaccus in Hither Spain, where he took over command of the army from Aulus Terentius. Farther Spain had been without imperium since the death of the proconsul Publius Sempronius.

As Fulvius Flaccus was laying siege to a Spanish town called Urbiaca,43 the Celtiberians launched an attack on him. A number of hard fought battles took place there and large numbers of Roman soldiers were either wounded or killed. Fulvius prevailed through his determination, as he could be diverted from the siege by no show of force; and the Celtiberians left exhausted after a number of battles of mixed success. Their support removed, the city was within a matter of days captured and pillaged, and the praetor left the plunder to his men.
