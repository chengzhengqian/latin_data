46. “Censors: We have not forgotten that a short time ago you were put in charge of our morals by the entire people of Rome and that it is we who should be admonished and directed by you, not you by us. Even so, we must point out what it is about you that irritates all loyal citizens, or at least what change they would prefer to see.

“Marcus Aemilius, Marcus Fulvius: When we look at you as individuals, we find no one in the state today whom we would wish to see put ahead of you if we were recalled to take the vote. But when we look at you together, we cannot but fear that you are a bad combination, and that the fact that we all like both of you enormously may do the state less good than your dislike for each other may do it harm. For many years you have continued a fierce and implacable vendetta against each other, and there is danger that from today this may prove more prejudicial to us and to the republic than to you. Many thoughts occur to me on the reasons for this fear of ours, which might be adduced unless perchance implacable rage has ensnared your minds.170

“We are united in requesting that you terminate this quarreling today, in this sacred place; and that you allow the men whom the Roman people have linked together by their votes also to be linked by us in a spirit of reconciliation and friendship. Be of one mind and one purpose in choosing the senate, reviewing the knights, conducting the census, and performing its purification.
