6. As it happened, the time now came round for the ceremonial purification of the army, the rite for which proceeds as follows.20 The head and fore section of a bitch that has been cut in two are placed on the right side of the road, the rear and intestines on the left. The troops are then led in arms between the members of the severed victim. Ahead of the front of the column are carried the distinctive arms of all the kings dating back to the earliest beginnings of Macedonia and then the king follows in person, along with his children. Next comes the royal company and the king’s bodyguards, and then the Macedonian rank and file bring up the rear of the army.

Flanking the king were his two young sons: Perseus, now in his thirtieth year, and Demetrius, five years his junior, the former at the acme of his youth, the latter at the flowering of his—grown children of a lucky father, had his mind been sound! Once the ritual purification was over, it was the custom for the army to hold military exercises; it would divide into two battle lines, which would clash in a mock engagement. The young princes were appointed leaders in this war game. But it was no simulated fight; they clashed as though the throne was at stake and there were numerous wounds inflicted by the practice swords. All that was lacking to give it the appearance of a real battle was the cold steel. The group that was under Demetrius’ command was by far the stronger.
