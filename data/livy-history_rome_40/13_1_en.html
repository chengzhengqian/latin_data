For if I had the standing and affection due to a son in my relations with you, your fury would be directed not against me when I complain about the conspiracy that has been uncovered, but against the one responsible for it; nor would my life mean so little to you as to leave you unmoved by the peril I have already faced and will face in future if the conspirators go unpunished. So if I must die in silence, let me remain silent, begging the gods only that the crime that started with me also ends with me and that a weapon is not being aimed at you through my body. However, when men are waylaid in deserted spots, nature herself prompts them to cry out for protection even from people they have never seen. So, if I too am allowed a cry for help when I see a sword drawn against me, then I beg you by your title as my father (and you have long understood which of the two of us reveres it more) to hear me now, just as if you had been woken by my cries and lamentations in the dark and had responded to my appeal and caught Demetrius and his armed men in my courtyard at dead of night. What I would have cried out in terror then in my hour of danger—this is now the essence of my complaint on the day after.
