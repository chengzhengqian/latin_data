In the midst of these prodigies there was also a report from Reate of the birth of a three-footed mule.165 Because of these events the decemvirs were ordered to consult the Books.166 The decemvirs then made a pronouncement on which gods should be given sacrifices and the number of victims to be offered, and also prescribed one day of supplication. After this, the votive games of the consul Quintus Fulvius were held over a ten-day period, with great ceremony.

Election of the censors followed; and those elected were the pontifex maximus Marcus Aemilius Lepidus and Marcus Fulvius Nobilior, who had triumphed over the Aetolians. Between these men there was a notorious feud,167 made public on many occasions in numerous bitter quarrels both in the senate and before the popular assembly.
