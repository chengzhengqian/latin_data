Titus Tatius and Romulus ruled in harmony in the city where they had as enemies clashed in battle in the midst of the Forum.171 Wars, too, and not just quarrels, come to an end; and from being bitter enemies men often become loyal allies and sometimes even fellow citizens. When Alba was destroyed the Albans were brought to Rome; and Latins and Sabines were welcomed into our state. That well-known saying that ‘immortal should friendships be, but feuds mortal’ became a proverb because it was true.”

A buzz of assent arose, following which the voices of the entire crowd, all making the same request, blended into unison and cut off his words. Aemilius then made a number of complaints, particularly about being twice rejected from the consulate by Marcus Fulvius when his election was assured. Fulvius for his part complained about relentless provocation from Aemilius and the judicial wager172 made to discredit him. Even so, each man indicated readiness to follow the bidding of so many of the state’s leading citizens if the other also agreed. At the urging of all present, they shook hands and pledged genuinely to renounce and terminate their antagonism. Then, amid general applause, they were escorted to the Capitol. Both the solicitude of the leading citizens over such an issue and the ready acquiescence of the censors were warmly commended and praised by the senate. The censors then requested that a sum of money be allocated to them to be used on state projects and for this they were decreed a year’s revenue.173
