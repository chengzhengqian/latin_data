BOOK XL

47. That same year, in Spain, the propraetors Lucius Postumius and Tiberius Sempronius174 came to an agreement between themselves that Albinus should go through Lusitania on an offensive against the Vaccaei,175 but would return to Celtiberia if the war there should escalate; and Gracchus was to march into the furthest reaches of Celtiberia. Gracchus first took the city of Munda176 by assault, launching a surprise attack at night. After accepting hostages and installing a garrison, he proceeded to attack strongholds and burn agricultural land until he reached another very powerful town (the Celtiberians call it Cartima). When he was already in the process of bringing up siege engines, delegates came to him from the town. Their language was blunt in the old fashioned manner and they made no secret of their readiness to fight if they had the strength. For the request they made of Gracchus was to be allowed to go to the camp of the Celtiberians to solicit assistance; if they failed to gain it, they would consider their position without taking account of the Celtiberians. Gracchus gave his permission and they left, and a few days later brought back with them ten other delegates.

It was midday. The very first thing these men asked of the praetor was that he issue orders for them to be given a drink. Their first cups drained, the delegates asked for others, provoking considerable mirth among the onlookers by their uncouth character and complete lack of manners.
