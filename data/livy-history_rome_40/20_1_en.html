13. “But no matter how trumped up it is, let us see how this charge develops. Perseus claims that he was the object of several forms of attack, and all my methods of entrapping him he has brought together into the space of a single day. I wanted to assassinate him in the daytime during our contest following the purification and, for god’s sake, on the actual day of the purification ceremonies! When I invited him to dinner, I wanted to kill him—with poison, presumably. When men wearing swords came with me for after-dinner drinks, I wanted to do away with him with the sword. You can see the sorts of occasion chosen for the murder: sports, a banquet, a drinking session. And what sort of day was it? One on which the army was purified, when the royal arms of all the kings of Macedon who ever lived were carried ahead of us and we two alone, father, rode each side of you between the severed victim, while the main body of the Macedonians followed us. Even had I been previously guilty of some deed that required atonement, I was purified and cleansed by that rite.
