In Corsica there was war with the Corsicans; and the praetor Marcus Pinarius killed some 2,000 in battle. By this defeat the Corsicans were obliged to give hostages and 100,000 lbs of wax.106 The army was then taken to Sardinia and there were successful engagements with the Ilienses,107 a tribe that is not even today completely pacified. The same year 100 hostages were returned to the Carthaginians and the Romans guaranteed them peace not only with the Romans themselves but also with Masinissa, who was occupying disputed territory with an armed force.108

35. The consuls had a province free of trouble. Marcus Baebius was recalled to Rome for the elections, and he returned as consuls Aulus Postumius Albinus Luscus and Gaius Calpurnius Piso.109 The following were then elected praetors: Tiberius Sempronius Gracchus,110 Lucius Postumius Albinus, Publius Cornelius Mammula,111 Tiberius Minucius Molliculus, Aulus Hostilius Mancinus and Gaius Maenius. All these took up their appointments on March 15th.
