FRL II: ENNIUS

SOTA (F 1–5)

Ennius’ Sota is named for the third-century Greek poet Sotades (using a short version of the name), who was credited with inventing a particular meter, the Sotadeus or ionicus quaternarius a maiore: ‒ ‒ ⏑ ⏑ ‒ ‒ ⏑ ⏑ ‒ ‒ ⏑ ⏑ ‒ × ||, with various resolutions permitted (Bettini 1982, esp. 71–75). The lines attributed to Sotades cover a wide variety of topics; some have philosophical content and a looser linguistic and metrical structure (remains in Powell 1925, 238–45).
