EPICHARMUS

​

5 Priscian

Nevertheless, one must note that they also said concordis masculine and feminine and concorde neuter [“like-minded”; unusual forms of nominative instead of concors], amentis masculine and feminine and amente neuter [“out of one’s mind”; unusual forms of nominative instead of amens]. Nor is it strange when Ennius put forward also its simple form, mentis [“mind”: nominative; cf. F 2], in Epicharmus:

the body is earth, but the mind is fire

instead of “mens” [usual nominative].
