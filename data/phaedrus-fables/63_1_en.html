8 The Serpent at the Blacksmith’s

He who attacks with wicked teeth one who can bite still harder should understand that he himself is described in the substance of this fable.

A viper came into a blacksmith’s shop and, while exploring for something to eat, set his teeth on a file. But that file was stubborn and unyielding. “Why, you fool,” it said, “do you try to wound me with your teeth? It’s my habit to gnaw through every kind of iron.”
