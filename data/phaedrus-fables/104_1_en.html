Suddenly, by the compassion of Venus, the sky is stirred by winds, the crash of thunder resounds throughout the heavens and brings on a night made rough by heavy rains. The light of day is snatched from their sight, and at the same time a storm of hail bursts forth, throwing the wedding guests into confusion everywhere, scattering them, and compelling each one to take refuge for himself in flight. The ass enters the familiar shelter close at hand and announces his arrival with a loud bray. The servants run out; the sight of a beautiful girl meets their startled eyes, and presently they report to their master. He, reclining at a table with a few friends, was consoling his disappointed love with cup after cup of wine. When the news was brought to him he made a joyful recovery; and, prompted by Bacchus as well as by Venus, he proceeded to consummate his own sweet nuptials amid the applause of his comrades. The bride’s parents advertised for their daughter by the town crier, and the new husband grieved for the loss of the wife that was to be his. When people came to know what had happened everybody hailed with approval the favour shown by the celestial gods.
