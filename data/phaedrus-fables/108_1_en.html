22 When The Bear Gets Hungry

Hunger sharpens the wits of living creatures

Whenever the bear’s food supply in the woods gives out, he runs to a rocky shore and, clinging to a rock, lets down his hairy legs little by little into the water; then as soon as some crabs have caught hold amid the shaggy hairs, he jumps back on to the land, shakes out his booty from the sea, and enjoys, clever fellow, the dainties he has collected from all sides.

So, you see, hunger sharpens the wits even of the dull-witted.
