9 Aesop and the Writer

On a bad author praising himself

A certain man had recited to Aesop some poor compositions, in the course of which he had inappropriately sounded his own praise at great length. Wishing, therefore, to know what the old man thought of it, he said: “I hope I have not appeared to you to be too proud of myself? The confidence that I feel in my own genius is no illusion.” Aesop, who was completely worn out by listening to the miserable volume, replied: “For my part, I emphatically endorse your bestowing praise on yourself; for it will never come to you from any other source.”
