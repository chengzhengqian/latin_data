3 What Aesop Said to a Certain Man Concerning the Success of the Wicked

When a certain man had been torn by the bite of a vicious dog he dipped a piece of bread in his own blood and tossed it out to the evildoer, because he had heard that this was a remedy for such a wound. Then said Aesop: “Don’t let any more dogs see you doing this, lest they devour us alive when they learn that guilt is rewarded in this way.”

The success of the wicked lures many others into evil ways.
