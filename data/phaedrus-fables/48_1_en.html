16 The Cicada and the Owl

He who shows no consideration for others usually meets with punishment for his arrogance.

A cicada was making a loud racket, much to the annoyance of an owl, whose habit was to forage in the dark and to enjoy her sleep in the hollow limb of a tree during the day. The cicada was asked to keep quiet, whereupon she began to clamour much more loudly than before. Another plea was addressed to her, and this redoubled her zeal.
