1 The Ape and The Fox

That the miserly man does not like to give away even what he doesn’t need

An ape asked a fox for a part of his tail, so that he might decently cover up his bare buttocks; but the spiteful creature said to him: “Even if my tail should become longer than it is now, still I would sooner trail it through mud and briars than share a part of it with you, however small.”

2 About Those Who Read This Book

That which my Muse here playfully creates, whatever its quality, is praised alike by worthless and by worthy people; but the latter are sincere about it, the former secretly angry.
