7 Prince, the Flute-Player

When an empty mind, carried away by a frivolous breeze of popularity, has assumed an upstart self-assurance, its feather-brained folly is easily brought to ridicule.

Prince, the flute-player, was rather well known, since he usually accompanied Bathyllus with his music on the stage. It happened at one of the shows, I don’t quite remember which, that, as the stage machine was being whirled through the air he accidentally had a bad fall and broke his left shin-bone, although he would rather have lost two bones on the right, had they been trombones. a He was raised up in the arms of friends and carried home groaning with pain. Several months passed by before treatment wrought a cure; and, since spectators as a class are soft-hearted and good natured, they began to long for the man whose breath inspired the dancers with energy.
