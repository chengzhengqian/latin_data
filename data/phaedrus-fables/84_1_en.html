4 The Ass and the Pig’s Barley

A certain man after sacrificing a pig to the deified Hercules, to whom he owed the fulfillment of a vow in return for his safety, ordered what was left of the pig’s barley to be set before the ass. But the ass spurned it, saying: “I should have a genuine appetite for that food, if he who was fed on it had not had his throat cut.”

Alarmed by consideration of this fable, I have always avoided profit that involved danger. But you say: “Those who have stolen riches still have them.” Come now, let’s reckon up the number of those who have been caught and have perished; you will find that those so punished are the greater throng. To a few men rashness brings fortune, to many ruin.
