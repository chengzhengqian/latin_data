Book II, Fable 2

1 The Bullock, the Lion and the Robber

A lion was standing over a bullock which he had brought down. A robber came up and demanded a share in the spoil. “I would give it to you,” said the lion, “were you not in the habit of taking things for yourself without leave,” and thus he rebuffed the rogue. By chance an innocent wayfarer came upon the same place and, on seeing the wild beast, began to retrace his steps. “You have nothing to fear,” the lion said to him benignly, “and you may boldly take the portion to which your modesty entitles you.” Then, having divided the carcass, he made off for the woods, in order to provide free access for the man.

This is a shining example altogether, and worthy of praise; but the truth is that greed is rich and modesty poor.
