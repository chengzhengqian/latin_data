18 On the Fortunes of Men

When a certain man was complaining about his ill fortune, Aesop invented the following story to comfort him.

A ship had been badly tossed about by fierce storms so that its passengers were in tears and fear of death, when suddenly the weather changed and took on a serene aspect; the ship began to ride safely, borne along by favourable winds, which raised the spirits of the sailors to an excessive pitch of joy. Hereupon the pilot, made wise by danger in the past, remarked: “One must be cautious in rejoicing and slow to complain, for the whole of life is a blend of grief and joy.”
