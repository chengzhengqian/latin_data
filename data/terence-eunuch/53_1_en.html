The Eunuch

pyth (to Chremes) Have you already shown the nurse the tokens?

chr All of them.

pyth What does she say, darling? Does she recognise them?

chr Yes; she remembers them well.

pyth Excellent news, by heaven! I’m fond of the girl. You two go inside. My mistress has been at home expecting you for a while. (looking down the street, as Chremes and Sophrona exit into Thais’ house) Look, here comes that fine fellow Parmeno, swaggering along. What a leisurely saunter, if you please! I think I’ve found my own way to torture him. I’ll go in and make sure of the recognition. Then I’ll come out and terrify the barbarian.

Enter parmeno left, looking very pleased with himself.

par (to himself) I’m returning to see how Chaerea is getting on. If he’s handled the situation cleverly, heaven help us, how much praise will Parmeno receive today and how deservedly! Not to mention that I’ve secured for him without trouble and without cost or outlay a very difficult and very expensive love affair, since the girl he loved belonged to a greedy courtesan; there’s the other point—and I reckon this to be my master stroke—that I’ve found a way for a young lad to discover the character and habits of courtesans in good time, so that having discovered them he will hate them forever. So long as they’re out in public, there’s nothing more refined, more composed, more elegant, as they pick daintily at their food while dining with a lover.
