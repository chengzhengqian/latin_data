thr True. He entrusted his whole army to me, and his plans.

gna Amazing!

thr Moreover, when he was weary of company and tired of work, when he wanted to rest, as if—you know?

gna I know: as if to cast out the troubles from his mind.

thr You’ve got it. Then he would take me aside as his sole companion.

gna Wow! You’re talking about a most discriminating king.

thr No, that’s how he is. He’s a man of very few friends.

gna (aside) None at all, if he spends his life with you.
