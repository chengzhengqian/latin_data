Antoninus Pius v

V. His father, as long as he lived, he obeyed most scrupulously, and when Hadrian passed away at Baiae1 he bore his remains to Rome with all piety and reverence, and buried him in the gardens of Domitia; moreover, though all opposed the measure, he had him placed among the deified.2 On his wife Faustina he permitted the senate to bestow the name of Augusta,3 and for himself accepted the surname Pius.4 The statues decreed for his father, mother, grandparents and brothers, then dead, he accepted readily; nor did he refuse the circus-games ordered for his birthday,5 though he did refuse other honours. In honour of Hadrian he set up a superb shield6 and established a college of priests.7

After his accession to the throne he removed none of the men whom Hadrian had appointed to office, and, indeed, was so steadfast and loyal that he retained good men in the government of provinces for terms of seven and even nine years. He waged a number of wars, but all of them through his legates. For Lollius Urbicus,8 his legate, overcame the Britons9 and built a second wall, one of turf,10 after driving back the barbarians.
