ERECTHEUS (F 49–51)

When besieged by an army led by the Thracian Eumolpus, a son of Neptune, Erectheus, king of Athens, sought an oracle in Delphi and was told to sacrifice one of his daughters. Erectheus sacrificed his daughter Chthonia, and according to some versions, his other daughters then killed themselves. Additionally, in other versions, Neptune demanded the sacrifice of a daughter as recompense after Erectheus’ victory (see, e.g., [Demosth.] Epitaph. 60.27; Lycurg. Orat. in Leocratem 24.98–100 [pp. 67.13–68.3 Conomis]; Apollod. Bibl. 3.15.4 = 3.201–4; Hyg. Fab. 46, 238.2).
