TRAGEDIES

​

67 Nonius

tumulti [“of tumult”: rare and ante-class. form of genitive, instead of tumultūs]. Ennius in Hectoris lytra:

what is this shouting here, what this tumult? who makes use of my name?1

​

68 Nonius

strepiti [“of noise”: rare and ante-class. form of genitive], instead of strepitūs [regular form of genitive]. Ennius in Hectoris lytra:

what noise is there in the camp?1

​

69 Nonius

sonit [“sounds”: third-conjugation form ante-class.], instead of sonat [first conjugation]. . . . Ennius in Hectoris lytra:

bronze arms sound, spears are broken, the ground sweats with blood

  • 1Sometimes connected with Enn. Trag. F 68.
  • 1Sometimes connected with Enn. Trag. F 67. The structure of Nonius’ dictionary suggests that these two pieces followed closely upon one another.
