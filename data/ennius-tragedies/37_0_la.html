HECUBA (F 72–81)

This tragedy deals with the fate of queen Hecuba, Priam’s wife, after the fall of Troy. Tragedies of the same title are known for Euripides and Accius (Trag. 481 R.2–3). The model of Ennius’ tragedy is probably Euripides’ Hecuba (F 73) as well as parts of Euripides’ Troades.

The fragments of Ennius’ tragedy apparently display Hecuba lamenting (F 74, 78, 80), a dialogue between her and Ulixes (F 73), statements about Hecuba’s children (F 74, 79), and an utterance by the herald Talthybius (F 81). The following unattributed fragments have been mentioned as possibly belonging to this tragedy: Enn. Trag. F 155, 166, 170, 200.
