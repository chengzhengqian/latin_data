58 Diomedes

There is a third one similar to these [the verbs “I hate” and “I remember”], as some believe (for people were not lacking who said that this verb was of the present tense): “I have learned = know,” “you have learned = know,” “he / she / it has learned = knows”; and this is similar with respect to present and perfect, like “I remember.” . . . Others conjugate both tenses similarly, as if “to know” <was> like “to remember.” Among the ancients the present of this verb is found in the plural, as they say nomus [“we know”: unusual contracted form in first person plural with present meaning] for this that is novimus [“we have learned = know”], as Ennius in Lytra:

it is right for us to be quiet; we both know Ulixes

59 Nonius

fuam [“I might be”]: “I may be” or “I may become” [present subjunctive of sum or fio]. . . . Ennius in Hectoris Lytra:

but you, almighty one, I ask that this plan be of benefit to the Achaeans
