a Varro, On the Latin Language

In creating this templum [“sacred area”] it is evident that trees are set as boundaries and inside those areas where the eyes are to see, that is, we are to watch, whence templum <is> developed and contemplare [“to look at”], as in Ennius in Medea: “look at and behold the temple of Ceres to the left.” That contempla [“look at!”] and conspicare [“see!”] are the same is evident, . . .
