70 Nonius

saeviter [“ferociously”: ante-class. form of adverb], instead of saeve [standard form]. . . . Ennius in Phoenix [Trag. F 114]: . . . Ennius in Hectoris lytra:

ferociously, with luck and steel, do they decide victory by fighting

71 Nonius

derepente [“suddenly”: mainly ante- and post-class.]: . . . Ennius in Phoenix [Trag. F 116]: . . . Ennius in Hectoris lytra:

but see, darkness arose: it took away the entire view suddenly; he took to his heels

HECUBA (F 72–81)

Della Casa (1962, 73) suggested that the play was first performed in about 188 BC; Fleckenstein (1953, 66–69) tentatively proposed a date of about 200 BC. Osann (1816, 126–49) and Fleckenstein (1953, 59–61) compare the tragedies by Ennius and Euripides.

Lit.: Osann 1816, 126–49; Ribbeck 1849, 5–6; 1875, 142–46; Fleckenstein 1953, 59–61; Della Casa 1962; Rosner 1970, 42; Paduano 1974, 38–39; Traina 1974, 125–44; Masiá 1999; Broccia 2000; Rosato 2003a; Stockert 2004, 278; Scafoglio 2007.
