110 Charisius

duriter [“austerely,” “uncouthly”]: Terence in Adelphoe [Ad. 45–46] “to spend his life in the countryside, always keeping himself thriftily and austerely”; where Acro says “according to the conventions of the ancients.” For Ennius too in Phoenix <says>:

what a speech he gave to you from his mouth with words uncouthly spoken

111 Nonius

cupienter [“eagerly”: ante-class. adverb]: “most desirously.” . . . Ennius in Phoenix:

he is stupid who . . . (?), desiring, desires most desirously1

  • 1Part of the fragment is corrupt, but the general sense and the wordplay are clear. To complete the clause an object seems to be required, perhaps denoting something that one should not wish for.
