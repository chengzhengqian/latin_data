TRAGEDIES

​

199 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

genis [“from the eyes”]: “from the eyelids.” Ennius about someone sleeping:

and s/he presses eyelid to eyelid

​

200 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“Cisseis”:1 queen Hecuba, daughter of Cisseus, according to Euripides [cf. Eur. Hec. 3], whom Ennius and Pacuvius and Virgil follow: for Homer [Il. 16.718] says <that she is the daughter> of Dymas. She saw that she was giving birth to a torch and brought forth Paris, who was the cause of a fire.

​

201 Paul the Deacon, Epitome of Festus

vitulans [“uttering a cry of joy”: rare and mainly ante-class.]: “rejoicing with joy,” as about birth. Ennius:1

he has the crown, uttering a cry of joy in victory

  • 1Some scholars assign the word Cisseis (“daughter of Cisseus”) to Ennius, but the passage does not imply that it is a quotation; this section is rather a testimonium on the genealogy of Hecuba followed by various poets (cf. Eur. Hec. 3–4; Apollod. Bibl. 3.12.5 = 3.148; Hyg. Fab. 91.1). Most scholars refer the reference to Ennius’ Hecuba.
  • 1This extract is often attributed to Ennius’ Alexander and referred to a description of Alexander’s (= Paris’) victory.
