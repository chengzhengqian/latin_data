TRAGEDIES

​

103 Nonius

superstitent [“they may keep alive”]: “they may save.” Ennius in Melanippa:

and so that they may preserve and keep alive our kingdom

​

104 Nonius

auscultare [“to listen to”] is “to oblige.” . . . Ennius in Melanippa:

oblige me, my son: order the boys to be burned

​

105 Nonius

auguro [“I foretell by augury, I predict”]. . . . Ennius in Melanippa:

there is no dispute that there is a portent: I tell you this and predict it from inference

​

106 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “the sea gleams under the quavering light”)

“quavering light” is an expression derived from the appearance of the phenomenon itself. But earlier Ennius <used it> in Melanippe:

the earth and the blue hollows <of the sky> shine thus with quavering light
