THYESTES (F 132–41)

In the confrontation between the two Mycenaean brothers Atreus and Thyestes (who had an affair with Atreus’ wife), Atreus killed Thyestes’ sons and served them to him for dinner. When Thyestes had realized the terrible crime, he fled to king Thesprotus in Epirus, where he, in line with an oracle, raped his own daughter Pelopia, and Aegisthus, the future avenger of his father, was conceived (see, e.g., Aesch. Ag. 1583–602; Schol. ad Eur. Or. 812 [vol. II, pp. 210–11 Dindorf]; Accius’ Atreus [Trag. 196–2342 R.2–3], Pelopidae [Trag. 512–19 R.2–3]; Hyg. Fab. 86, 87, 88).
