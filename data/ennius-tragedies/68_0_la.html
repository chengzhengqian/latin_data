FRL II: ENNIUS

Many scholars regard Thyestes’ dinner as the subject of the tragedy; Jocelyn (1967, ad loc.) suggests that Ennius’ tragedy covered events at the court of King Thesprotus in Epirus slightly later. At any rate, the surviving fragments include Thyestes lamenting about his fate (F 135, 140), cursing Atreus (F 132), and referring to Apollo’s authority (F 136).
