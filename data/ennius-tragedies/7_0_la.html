ALCMEO (F 12–14)

Alcmeo, the son of Amphiaraus and Eriphyla, killed his mother on his father’s orders, since it was due to her bribery that Amphiaraus had to participate in the fighting of the Seven against Thebes, where he died. Because of this deed Alcmeo was later pursued by Furies (see, e.g., Apollod. Bibl. 3.7.5 = 3.86–90; Hyg. Fab. 73).

Tragedies of the same title are known for the Greek tragedians Sophocles (F 108–10 TrGF), Euripides (F 65–87a TrGF [Ἀλκμέων α’ καὶ β’]), Achaeus I (20 F 12–15 TrGF [Ἀλκμέων σατυρικός]), Agathon (39 F 2 TrGF),
