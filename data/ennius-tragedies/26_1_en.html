But Catullus was not the only one to use this word [deprecor, “I try to avert by prayer”] in this sense. Books are full of <instances of> similar meaning for this word, out of which I have set down one or two that were at hand. [9] Ennius in Erectheus [Trag. F 49] said, not much different from Catullus [Catull. 92.3]: . . . ; it signifies “I drive away” and “I remove,” either by using a prayer or by any other means. [10] Likewise Ennius in Cresphontes:

while I spare my own life, should I try to avert death from the enemy?
