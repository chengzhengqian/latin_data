HECTORIS LYTRA (F 56–71)

Hectoris lytra (“Hector’s ransom”; the Greek words in the title often corrupted in the transmission) denotes Priam’s visit to Achilles toward the end of the fighting before Troy to reclaim the body of his dead son Hector. However, it is uncertain when exactly the plot of this tragedy begins, i.e., how much of the action preceding Priam’s visit is included
