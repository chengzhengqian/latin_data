Then is it we that are shameless, who do not wish to make a slip, or they presumptuous, who have persuaded themselves that they alone know everything? ‘I don’t say that I myself know,’ says he, ‘but that the wise man knows.’ Excellent! no doubt you mean ‘knows the doctrines that are in your system.’ To begin with, what are we to think of this—wisdom being unfolded by a man that is not wise? But let us leave ourselves and speak about the wise man, on whom all this inquiry turns, as I have often said already.
