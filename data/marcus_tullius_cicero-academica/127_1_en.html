Academica, II. (Lucullus)

it is an opinion and will know that there is nothing that can be comprehended and perceived; and therefore although agreeinga with their rule of epochē as to everything,b I assent emphatically to that second view, that nothing exists that can be perceived.” “I have your view,” said I, “and I do not think it quite negligible; but pray, Hortensius, what do you think?” “Away with it!”c he replied with a laugh. “I take you,” said I, “for that is the true Academic verdict.” The conversation thus concluded, Catulus stayed behind, while we went down to our boats.

  • aPossibly the Latin should be corrected to ‘disagreeing.’
  • bi.e., refusal to state any opinion, whether as certain or as probable: see § 104, and for the term, § 59.
  • cA double entente, (1) ‘make a clean sweep’ of assent, and (2) ‘weigh anchor.’
