The same argument it will be possible to employ at every section of the discussion. 44 For if they say that they can see through the matters that they are discussing with complete clearness, and are not hampered by any overlappingb of presentations, they will confess that they can ‘comprehend’ them. But if they maintain that true presentations cannot be distinguished from false ones, how will they be able to advance any further? for they will be met as they were met before; since valid inference is not possible unless you accept the propositions taken as premisses as so fully proved that there cannot possibly be any false propositions that resemble them: therefore if a process of reasoning that has carried through its procedure on the basis of things grasped and perceived arrives at the conclusion that nothing can be grasped, what more self destructive argument could be discovered? And when the very nature of accurate discourse professes the intention of revealing something that is not apparent, and of employing sensations and manifest presentations to facilitate the attainment of this result, what are we to make of the language of these thinkers who hold that everything does not so much exist as seem to exist?
