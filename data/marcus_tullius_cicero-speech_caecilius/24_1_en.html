You forget that it is usual for people to ask not merely who should, but also who can, avenge them. The man who has both qualities is better than any other; but if a man has only one of these things, it is usual to inquire rather what he can do than what he would like to do. If, however, you really think that the54 right to prosecute Verres should be conceded to the greatest sufferer from his wrongdoing, which fact, after all, do you think should excite the greater indignation in the minds of this court—that he has injured you, or that he has ravaged and ruined our province of Sicily? You will, I presume, allow that the latter is the more serious, and deserves to excite, in every mind, the more serious indignation. You must therefore also allow the province to have preference over you in the privilege of prosecuting—for the province it is that prosecutes, when her case is conducted by the man whom she has definitely chosen to maintain her rights, to avenge her wrongs, and to conduct her case throughout.

XVII. You may object that the wrong Verres55 has done you is serious enough to stir deeply the feelings of all others, even though it does not touch them directly. I deny this. It is not, I take it, beside the point to ask what sort of wrong is alleged as the reason for a personal enmity.
