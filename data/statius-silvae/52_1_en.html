THE VILLA OF POLLIUS FELIX AT SURRENTUM

Between the walls well known by the Sirens’ name1 and the cliffs burdened with Tyrrhene Minerva’s temple2 there is a lofty villa looking out upon the Dicarchean deep, where the land is dear to Bromius and the grapes ripen on the high hills nor envy Falernian presses. Hither I came gladly across my native bay after the quinquennial festival of my home,3 when a lazy lull had settled on the stadium and the dust lay white as the athletes turned to Ambracian laurels. I was drawn by the eloquence of gentle Pollius and elegant Polla’s youthful4 grace, though already eager to bend my steps where Appia, queen of long highways, takes the traveler along her familiar track.5

But the delay was worthwhile. Curving cliffs on either side pierce crescent waters,6 making a calm recess. Nature provides space. The watery7 beach interrupts the heights, running inland between overhanging crags.
