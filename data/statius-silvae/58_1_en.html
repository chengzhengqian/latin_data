And you, <most accomplished> by far <among Latian> brides, <with mind to match your man, no>38 cares have changed your breast, no threats your brow; bright joy is ever in your face and carefree pleasure. For you no sterile strongbox strangles hoarded riches, nor do ramifications of grasping usury39 torment your soul: your wealth is plain to view, and you enjoy it in educated temperance. No hearts unite more blessed of heaven, no other minds has Concord better taught <to love their bonds>. Learn untroubled <the joys of life as it slips by unnoticed>.40 Mingled flames from your breasts coalesced for long time to come and hallowed love keeps chaste friendship’s laws. Go through the years and centuries, outdoing the glories of ancient fame.
