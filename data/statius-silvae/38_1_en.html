Scarcely was dim night advancing upon the world when a flaming ball ascends18 from the center of the arena shining in the dense gloom, surpassing the flare of the Cretan crown. The sky brightens with flames, allowing no license to night’s obscurity. At the sight lazy Rest and Sleep must take off for other cities. Who should sing the shows, the unbridled jests, the banquets, the viands unbought,19 the rivers of lavish Lyaeus? Now, now my strength fails and your Bacchus draws me tipsy into tardy slumber.

Through how many years shall this day travel? Sacred shall it endure throughout all time. So long as Latium’s Hills and Father Tiber, so long as your Rome shall stand and the Capitol you restore to earth, it shall remain.
