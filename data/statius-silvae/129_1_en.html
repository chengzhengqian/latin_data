For potent eloquence is not your only strength. You have limbs well fitted for war and arms slow (?)13 to don a heavy corselet. If you make to march on the level, your crest will wave above the ranks; if you manage jingling reins, the mettlesome charger will be your slave. I drift into old age singing other men’s deeds, whereas you, handsome in your own arms, shall yourself perform acts deserving song and prepare great examples for little Geta,14 of whom his martial grandfather already demands worthy feats, acquainting him of domestic triumphs. Up then, boy, be doing, catch your young parent, fortunate in your mother’s lineage and your father’s valor! Already the happy Senate House fondly rears you for herself in Tyrian15 bosom and joys to promise you every curule chair.”
