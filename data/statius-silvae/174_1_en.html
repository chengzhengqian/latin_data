Under your direction grew up the Dardan inspector of the hidden fire,45 who conceals the sanctuary of that which Diomedes stole,46 and learned the ritual as a boy. You approved the Salii and showed them their arms, you showed the certain Augurs the sky that gives them foreknowledge, showed who is authorized to unroll the song of Chalcis47 and why the hair of the Phrygian Flamen48 is concealed; and greatly did the girt-up Luperci fear your stripes.49 And now one of that company perhaps gives laws to eastern nations, another holds down Iberians, another with Zeugma keeps off the Achaemenian Persian.50 These bridle the rich peoples of

  • 42Puteoli.
  • 43I.e., do not harm them, by contrast with Pompeii and Herculaneum, destroyed by the highly visible fires of Vesuvius.
  • 44She refused him after he had granted her what she asked.
  • 45The Pontifex Maximus, under whose supervision the vestals maintained the sacred flame brought by Aeneas from Troy. It is surely not incumbent to take this as a concrete reference to the existing Pontifex Maximus, Domitian.
  • 46The Palladium (statue of Pallas Athene = Minerva, stolen from her temple in Troy by Diomedes and Ulysses and supposedly kept in Vesta’s temple).
  • 47The Sibylline Books kept by the Quindecimviri.
  • 48The flamen (priest) of Cybele, like the other flamens, wore a special headgear (apex), but the implication of lateat is obscure.
  • 49The future Luperci—the whipped becoming the whippers. “Girt-up” applies to both.
  • 50The ancient counterpart of the contemporary Arsacid king of Parthia.
