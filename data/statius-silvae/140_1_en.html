POEM OF CONGRATULATION TO JULIUS MENECRATES

Fling wide, Parthenope, the doors of the High Ones and fill the garlanded temples with Sabaean clouds and the breathing entrails of beasts. A third scion now gives increase to renowned Menecrates’ line. Your noble crowd1 of grandees grows, solacing the losses of mad Vesuvius. And let not Naples only in isolation surround the festal altars; let fellow havens, land beloved of gentle Dicaearchus and the Surrentine region, dear to the madid god, gird their altars with chaplets, the shore where lives his maternal grandfather, surrounded by a throng of grandchildren vying to resemble his features.
