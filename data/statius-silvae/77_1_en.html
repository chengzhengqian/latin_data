Not long before his death the sentence had been lifted and he returned to Rome. His wife, Etrusca, of distinguished family, gave him two sons, but both died when they were very young. One of them, the recipient of the Consolation, bore her name. Claudius Etruscus’ wealth, including the celebrated Baths of 1.5 and Martial 6.42, derived from his father.

4

Following an ancient Greek custom, Domitian’s boy eunuch Flavius Earinus sent the first clippings of his hair along with a mirror set in gold and gems to the temple of Asclepius in Pergamum, his birthplace. Martial has three related epigrams (9.16, 17, 36) and three others on the boy’s unscannable name (from ἔαρ, spring): 9.11–13.
