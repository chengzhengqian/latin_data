Bless your heart that you favor things Greek and spend your days in Grecian country! Nor let Dicarchus’ city26 that gave you birth be jealous. We shall more fitly possess our poet foster child.27

Why now should I tell of rural wealth, plowlands cast upon the sea,28 cliffs awash in Bacchus’ nectar? Often in autumn, when Lyaeus is burgeoning, a Nereid has climbed the rocks and in night’s secret shade wiped her dripping eyes with a ripened vine shoot29 and snatched sweet grapes from the hills. Often the vintage is sprayed by the adjoining flood. Satyrs fell into the shallows and the mountain Pans lusted to catch Doris naked in the waves.

Be fruitful for your lord and lady, earth, unto the years of the Mygdonian ancient and the Pylian,30 nor change your noble bondage! Let not the Tirynthian hall31 be better appointed nor Dicarchus’ bay nor the seductive vineyards of Therapnaean Galaesus more often please them.
