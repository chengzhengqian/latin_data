PREFATORY NOTES 1

Date: late summer of 91. For Pollius see on 2.2.

2

The “Send-Off” has extant or partially extant precedents from Erinna to Ovid listed in Vollmer, pp. 394f. M. Maecius Celer is known only from Statius and the Acts of the Arval Brethren, which list him as consul suffect in 101.

3

The father of Claudius Etruscus was about ninety when he died, in 92. Originally a slave, born in Smyrna, he spent most of his life in the service of successive emperors starting with Tiberius, who gave him his freedom. Hence the name Claudius; his slave name, which would have become his cognomen, is unknown. (His entry in the index in the first printing of my Loeb edition of Martial must be corrected on this point.) Nero made him head of the imperial treasury, and Vespasian made him a knight. But under Domitian he fell into disgrace and exile (ca. 82), into which his son accompanied him. (Martial 6.83.8, esse quod
