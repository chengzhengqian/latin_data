BOOK V

Who could recount in worthy song the obsequies and funeral gifts of the sinister procession? There close-packed in lengthy abundance flows the whole springtime of Arabia and Cilicia, Sabaean flowers, Indian harvest for the flames, incense preempted from Palestinian temples, Hebrew essences too, Corycian strands and Cinyrean buds. She herself reclines on a lofty couch, shaded by cover of silk and Tyrian purple. But in the entire column the husband draws every eye. On him great Rome turns her gaze, as though he were bearing young sons to final burial; such grief is in his face, such night upon his hair and cheeks.21 Her they call free in a peaceful end and happy; they shed their tears for the husband.

Length of time will have no power to wither nor labors of years to harm; such care is taken for your body, so much wealth the venerable marble breathes out. Soon you are made anew, changed into various semblance: here shines Ceres in bronze, here the Cnosian maid,23 in that clay is Maia, Venus (no wanton) in this stone.
