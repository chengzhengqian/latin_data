Congratulating Pollius Felix’ son-in-law on the birth of a third child, a boy, written somewhere between the terminals of 93 and 95.

9

The Saturnalia of December 94 were the occasion. Grypus was a senator and Statius his client (vv. 48–52). The lively hendecasyllables “recall Catullus and are evidently considered appropriate to Saturnalian jocularity” (Coleman, comparing 1.6).
