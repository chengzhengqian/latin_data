But look! Through all the aisles comes another crowd no less in number than the seated throng, handsome and smartly dressed. Some carry bread baskets and white napkins and elegant eatables, others freely serve mellowing wine. One could think them so many cupbearers from Ida. At one and the same time you satisfy the Circle where it is reformed and sobered8 together with the peoples of the gown; and since you feed so many folk, wealthy lord, haughty Annona9 knows not this day. Come, Antiquity, compare now if you will the ages of ancient Jove10 and the golden time: not so freely did wine flow then, not thus would harvest forestall the tardy year. Every order eats at one table: children, women, populace, Knights, Senate. Freedom has relaxed reverence. Nay, you yourself (which of the gods could thus invite, which accept invitation?)11 entered the feast along with us. Now everyone, be he rich or poor, boasts of dining with the leader.
