Publilius Syrus

To ask a favour is slavery of a sort. To lose what is not your own is not to lose but to give back. Anger always thinks it has power beyond its power. When shame rebukes a man, there’s hope for his soul’s health. 645It’s the well-wisher’s way to advise before he corrects. The sage bears arms against the world when he thinks. ’Tis most just to remember to whom you owe yourself. Fools fear fortune, wise men bear it. Wisdom is found by sense, not years. 650Bounty holds herself ever rich. The wise man gives an injury room to settle down. In a hazard venturesomeness replaces deliberation. Counsel is ever lacking when most needed. It’s a curt refusal when the wise man meets a request with silence. 655The mind always fears the unknown evil more.
