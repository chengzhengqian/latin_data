Publilius Syrus

The height of misery is life at another’s will. The tame way is safer, but it’s the way of slaves. 415A dishonourable death is fate’s insolence. You’d go wrong less often if you knew your ignorance. Any evil-doer under punishment is a protection to the good. It’s an ill want that springs from plenty. Advice, not punishment, is what fits folly. 420He who is pardoned in his wrong-doing is far more shamefully condemned. a Make not another’s misfortune your joy. For the unlucky it’s always best to do nothing. The eyes commit no wrong, if the mind controls the eyes. Think nothing your own that can change. 426It’s long before the downfall overwhelms him who fears a crack.
  • a i.e. a man who has such a bad character that no one pays attention to his misdeed is, in fact, wholly out of court. To treat his misdeed so lightly shows what is thought of the offender.
