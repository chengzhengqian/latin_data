Publilius Syrus

30The old woman in skittish mood is Death’s darling toy. The one who causes also cures the wound of love. Hasty judgement means speedy repentance. The cleverer the gamester, the greater his knavery. Love causes worry in the leisure hour. 35None should be greedy, least of all the old, Modest women choose a man by mind, not eye. A lover’s oath involves no penalty. A lover is like a torch—blazes the more he’s moved. Love, like a tear, rises in the eye and falls on the breast. 40The sage will rule his feelings, the fool will be their slave. Misfortune reveals whether you have a friend or only one in name. ’Tis time, not the mind, that puts an end to love. Courage grows by daring, fear by delay. Help wounds the pride of those whose cause is lost. 45The man whose luck is fair enough gives ruin a wide berth.
