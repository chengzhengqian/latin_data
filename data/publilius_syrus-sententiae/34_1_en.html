Publilius Syrus

It is more than punishment to let one live in misery. Who steals another’s modesty loses his own. Patience of mind has secret wealth. 605Dumb grief thinks of much worse to come. Money alone is the ruling principle of the world. Modesty, once dismissed, never returns to favour. Only want sets a limit to waste. Punishment with creeping pace comes on the offender in the moment of his haste. 510’Tis more than punishment to yield to wrong. The pain that kills pain acts as medicine. The man who unites patience and courage secures his own happiness. In peace one must forecast the sinews of war. The parent enraged is most cruel to himself. 515A gift is lost, not presented, unless there be recollection of it. In overlooking even a good man’s offences, you would impair the laws.
