Publilius Syrus

Desire finds even quickness slow. From a neighbour’s fault a wise man corrects his own. The wretched have too little and too much of thought. Sometimes ’tis fitting even to forget what you are. 180The grumbling of men made Fortune a goddess. To shun desire is to conquer a kingdom. The exile with no home anywhere is a corpse without a grave. Even those who do an injustice hate it. Anger is rightly robbed of a weapon, not given one. 185He suffers exile who denies himself to his country. Even one hair has a shadow of its own. Alas, how wretched to be aged by fear! He who has confidence in his policy is fair even to an enemy. The exalted are much more readily hurt by misfortune. 190The end always passes judgement on what has preceded. After much strife reconciliation becomes more beautiful. It is often bad to grow used even to good things.
