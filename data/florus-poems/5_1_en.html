Florus

XIV

The Nine Musesa

Clio records past ages in her prose. Euterpe’s hollow reed makes double sound. Voice-famed Thalia revelling loves the sock. Melpomene’s notes in tragic iambs seethe. Terpsichore’s golden lyre thrills all the sky. Strings touched by Erato sweet love-songs make. Polymnia’s odes suit swift and varying moods.b Urania scans the stars of heaven in verse. Calliope crowns epic bards with bays.

  • aAscribed to Florus, Baehrens, P.L.M. IV. p. 279. Cf. the verses which have come down under the name of Cato, P.L.M. III. p. 243: see infra, p. 634. their functions and varying symbols in literature and art see “Musen” in Roscher’s Ausführliches Lexikon der gr. und röm. Mythologie and “Musai” in P. W. Realencyclopädie.
  • bMotua is here taken of the mind. But it is possible to take it of bodily movement (“P. sways her body in easy and in varied movements”); for a province assigned at a late period to Polymnia was that of pantomime: see p. 635, note b.
