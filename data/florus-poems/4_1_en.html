Florus

A fourth was bright with well-grown naked bloom, One rears its head, while one untwines its coil: So, while their maiden virtue’s chastely garbed, At dawn pull roses fresh: maids soon grow old.

XII

The Rose

The rose was Cupid’s smile, or from her comb Dawn drew it forth—Dawn of the lustrous hair, Or haply Venus was by briars caught And on the sharp thorns this her blood remained.

XIII

Venus’ Rose-Garden

Venus a garden had, rose-bushes round— Its lady’s darling plot; once seen, beloved. Her boy, in random haste to cull the blooms And crown his tresses, pricked with pointed thorn His marble fingers. Soon, as pain stabbed limbs And blood-stained hand, the tear-drop bathed his eye. In rage he seeks his mother with his plaints: “Whence comes it, mother, that the roses hurt? Whence fight thy flowers with hidden arms? They war On me: the flower’s hue is the same as blood!”
