Moreover, though this Deiotarus has repeatedly been adjudged by the senate to be worthy of the title of king, and though he has been distinguished by the recommendations of our most brilliant generals, you actually order that he shall share that title with Brogitarus. The former, however, owes his kingly title, through us, to the senatorial pronouncement, while Brogitarus owes his, through you, to the price which he paid you.a . . . I shall believe the other to be a king when he has found the means to pay you what you advanced him under a note of hand.
