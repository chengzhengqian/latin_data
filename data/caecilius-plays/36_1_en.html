Cicero: No one is so old that he does not believe he can live for a year. But these same men spend all their labour on things which they know have no application to them—

He plants trees that they may be a profit to another age,

as our Statius says in Comrades in Youth. Nor too does the husbandman, even though he be an old man, hesitate to answer the person who inquires for whose sake he is planting. . . .
