Plays

Wise in His Own Conceit a

34–5

Donatus, on ‘In person snatched from him in person’ in Terence: These words, added redundantly, reinforce the meaning . . . so Caecilius in Wise in his own Conceit—

This is plainly a crowd of gladiators, where each ally wounds his own ally.

The Exile b

36

Nonius: ‘Abscondit’ for ‘abscondidit.’ Caecilius in The Exile—

for this fellow hid himself away inside, in the darkness.

37

Nonius: ‘Putare,’ to dispute in the mind . . . Caecilius in The Exile—

Haven’t you a thought for all this? Don’t you turn it over and over in your heart?
