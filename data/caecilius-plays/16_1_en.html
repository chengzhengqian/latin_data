77

Nonius: ‘Rabere’ is a term derived from ‘rabies.’ . . . Caecilius in The Changeling, A Play of the Hoe—

He says he’s raving.

78

Nonius: ‘Iracunditer.’ Caecilius in The Changeling—

Please don’t dispatch this business heedlessly, no, nor angrily.

79

A slave is forced to speak out:

Nonius: ‘Coepere,’ to begin. Caecilius in The Changeling, A Play of the Hoe—

Oh, sir! Oh my! Please, stop it! Wait a —I’ll begin.

80

Nonius: ‘Lactare’ means to lead on or coax, fleece, cheat. . . . Caecilius in The Changeling, A Play of the Hoe—

What whim, what pleasure, what openhandedness is it that’s diddling you?

81–2

Gellius: Correct and elegant is the passage of Caecilius in The Changeling—

For the worst of foes are those that have bright faces, gloomy hearts, You don’t know how to hold’em and you cannot let’em go. b

  • aPossibly the master’s reply—‘Stay there! I’m only just going to begin!’
  • bGellius proceeds to comment on frons used in the masculine gender.
