Now I’m going. You’ll hear later on if you say ‘let the daughter come back.’ b

109

Nonius: ‘Infelicent’ . . .—

Well, may all the gods unbless you, ill-informed memory and all!

110

Nonius: ‘Suppilare’ means to fly at or seize; it is a word derived from the idea of pulling out ‘pĭli’(hairs). Whence to those who have suffered a theft the term ‘conpilati’ (plucked, fleeced) is applied . . .—

The clothes and all the ornaments he plucks away.

  • aFrom Menander’s Ναύκληρος?
  • bThe metre is anapaestic, apparently; but the words si dicis are doubtful.
