Ausonius

X.—Grammaticis Latinis Burdigalensibus Philologis[Macrino Sucuroni Concordio Phoebicio1]Ammonio Anastasio Grammatico Pictaviorum

Nunc ut quemque mihi flebilis officii relligiosus honor suggeret, expediam, 5qui, quamvis humili stirpe, loco ac merito, ingeniis hominum Burdigalae rudibus introtulere tamen 10grammatices studium. Sit macrinus in his: huic mea principio credita puerities; et libertina 15sucuro progenie, sobrius et puerorum utilis ingeniis. et tu concordi, qui profugus patria 20mutasti sterilem urbe alia cathedram nec reticebo senem nomine phoebicium, qui Beleni aedituus 25nil opis inde tulit; set tamen, ut placitum, stirpe satus Druidum gentis Aremoricae, Burdigalae cathedram 30nati opera obtinuit: 31permaneat series. 35ammonium [et recinam2—] 32relligiosum etenim commemorare meae 34grammaticum patriae— 36qui rudibus pueris prima elementa dabat, doctrina exiguus, moribus inplacidis: 40proinde, ut erat meritum, famam habuit tenuem.

  • 1Omitted in V: restored (but in a different order) by Scaliger, and (in the above order) by Schenkl. Peiper omits all but the name of Anastasius from the title.
  • 2In V the name of Ammonius is omitted from the text: it was replaced as the first half of 1. 35 by Schenkl and Peiper. The result is not good; and a full stop is here substituted at the end of 1. 31 for Peiper’s semicolon, and 1.35 is placed between 11. 31 and 32 (whence it was possibly omitted through homoeoteleuton with 1. 32). For the order of the verses (which is much confused) in the MS., see the editions of Peiper or Schenkl. The latter half of 1. 35 is supplied by the Translator.
