Letters to Atticus

Balbus too is here and I see a lot of him. He has had a letter from Vetus1 dated 31 December to the effect that when Caecilius was besieged by him and on the point of capture Pacorus of Parthia came up with a very large force. Thus Caecilius was snatched from his clutches with the loss of many of his own men. He blames Volcacius2 in the matter. So it looks to me as though war in that quarter is imminent. But let Dolabella and Nicias3 worry! Balbus also gives better news of Gaul. He has a letter three weeks old announcing that the Germans and the tribes there, hearing what had happened to Caesar, had sent envoys to Hirtius’ deputy Aurelius promising to obey orders. All redolent of peace in fact, contrary to what Baldie had said to me.

364 (XIV.10)

Cumae, 19 April 44

Cicero to Atticus
