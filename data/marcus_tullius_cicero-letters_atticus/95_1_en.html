A little while back we feared a massacre; brave old Considius’ words dispersed it.5 Suddenly the sort of pretext (?) we had constantly to fear has turned up. In short, to my way of thinking (?) Catulus is supremely to be envied, not only in the dignity of his life but now at this present. However, in all these tribulations I keep my heart up, am not in the least flustered, and have a careful eye, in the most honourable way, both to my security and my dignity. Pompey tells me not to worry about Clodius, and expresses the most cordial sentiments towards me in everything he says. I very much wish to have you with me to advise me on my tactics, to share my anxieties, to join in everything I have in mind. I have asked Numestius to speak to you about this; and myself ask you likewise, if possible more emphatically, to fly to my side. I shall breathe again once I see you.
