28 (II.8)

Antium, 16 (?) April 59

Cicero to Atticus

As usual, I was avidly expecting a letter from you towards evening, when along comes word that some boys have arrived from Rome. I call them in and ask whether they have any letters for me. They say not. ‘What,’ say I, ‘nothing from Pomponius?’ Frightened by my tone and look they confessed that they had been given one but had lost it on the way.
