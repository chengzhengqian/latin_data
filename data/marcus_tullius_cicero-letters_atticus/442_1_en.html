So I am beset on all sides. I can hardly stand it; or rather I just cannot stand it. Worse than all the rest of my afflictions put together is the thought that I shall leave that poor girl despoiled of father, inheritance, all that was hers. On that account I should indeed like to see you, as you promise. I have no one else to whom I can commend her since I perceive that the same fate4 is in store for her mother as for me. Brundisium, 19 January 47

Cicero to Atticus

Reports reaching me about the Quinti have dropped new bitterness into my overflowing cup. My friend P. Terentius had a job as managing director of the customs and pasture rents company in Asia. He saw Quintus junior at Ephesus on 8 December, and was at pains to be hospitable because of his friendship with me.
