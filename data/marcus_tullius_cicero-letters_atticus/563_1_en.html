338 (XIII.46)

Tusculum, 12 August 45

Cicero to Atticus

Punctual to his promise to return by the Ides of August Pollex presented himself to me at Lanuvium the day before, but a pollex indeed he proved, not an index.1 You will hear from himself. I met Balbus (Lepta took me to him, being exercised about his commissionership of entertainments); it was in the house near Lanuvium which he made over to Lepidus. His first words were: ‘I have just received a letter in which he2 confirms emphatically that he will be back before the Roman Games.’ I read the letter, which contained a good deal about my ‘Cato.’ He says that reading and rereading it has improved his powers of expression, whereas after reading Brutus’ ‘Cato’ he began to fancy himself as a writer.

Then I learned from him about the terms of acceptance in Cluvius’ will (careless Vestorius!)—free acceptance before witnesses within sixty days. I was afraid I might have to send for him.
