Later, if desirable, it won’t be difficult, and come when it may it will be acceptable to him. But I say this with the reservation that if Caesar continues as he has begun, with sincerity, moderation, and prudence, I shall think hard and look more attentively to our interests.’ On 9 March you write that my quiescence is also approved by our friend Peducaeus, whose judgement carries great weight with me.

I console myself with these passages in your letters, which make me think that I have done nothing amiss so far. But you must defend your own advice; there is no occasion to do so to me, but I want others in the picture. For my part, if I have done nothing wrong, I shall not fail in what remains. Encourage me in that, and in general help me with your deliberations. Nothing is heard here yet as to Caesar’s return. By this letter I have done myself this much good at least: I have read all yours through, and found comfort in the reading.
