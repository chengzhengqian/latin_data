218 (XI.7)

Brundisium, 17 December 48

Cicero to Atticus

Many thanks for your letter, in which you have set down in detail everything you thought concerned me. Very well, I shall do as you say they think best, i.e. that I should keep the same lictors I now have, a concession made, you say, to Sestius. I suppose he was not allowed to keep his own lictors but given them by Caesar. For I hear that Caesar does not recognize senatorial decrees passed after the departure of the Tribunes.1 Therefore if he wants to be consistent he will be able to authorize my lictors.

But what am I doing to be writing about lictors, when I have almost been ordered out of Italy? Antony has sent me a copy of a letter from Caesar to himself, in which Caesar says that he has heard that Cato and L. Metellus have returned to Italy intending to live openly in Rome, that he does not approve of this in view of the risk of disturbances resulting, and that all persons are barred from Italy except those whose cases he has personally reviewed.
