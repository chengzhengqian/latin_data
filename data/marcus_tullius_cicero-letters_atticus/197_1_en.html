Camp outside Cybistra, 20 September 51

Cicero to Atticus

How I wish you were in Rome, unless perhaps you are—I have no definite information except that I got your letter dispatched 19 July, in which you wrote you would be going to Epirus about the Kalends of August. However, whether you are in Rome or in Epirus, the Parthians have crossed the Euphrates under Pacorus, son of King Orodes of Parthia, with almost their entire force. There is no word so far of Bibulus being in Syria. Cassius1 is the town of Antioch with his entire army. I am in Cappadocia in the Taurus region with my army near Cybistra. The enemy is in Cyrrhestica, the part of Syria nearest my province. I am writing to the Senate2 on these matters.
