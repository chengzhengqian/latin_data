One thing he declared very firmly, that if condemned persons were restored he would go into exile. To that I said that this would certainly happen and that what was already going on was no less deplorable, giving many examples. 207 (X.15)

Cumae, 10 (?) May 49

Cicero to Atticus

While Servius was at my house Cephalio arrived with your letter, on the 10th. This brought us great hope of better things, in connexion with the eight cohorts. And those in this area too are said to be wavering. The same day Funisulanus brought me a letter from you in which the same report is repeated more positively. I made him a more than liberal answer about his own affair, giving you all credit. So far he has not been meeting his obligations—he owes me a tidy sum and is not considered well off. Now he says he will pay; that a debtor of his is behind time; that, if you have funds of his with which to meet the debt (?), you may hand the amount over to the couriers. Philotimus’ man Eros will tell you the figure.
