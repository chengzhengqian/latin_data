Letters to Atticus

Having answered your last page with my first, I shall now come back to your first.2 I took the statement that all the Peloponnesian communities adjoin the sea from the accounts3 of Dicaearchus, no scamp but a man approved by your own judgement. In the course of Chaeron’s story4 about Trophonius he takes the Greeks to task under many heads for clinging to the coast, and makes no exception of any place in the Peloponnese. Though I thought him a good authority (after all he was extremely well informed and had lived in the Peloponnese), I was surprised and almost incredulous, so I consulted Dionysius. He was taken aback at first, but afterwards, having as high an opinion of this Dicaearchian deity5 as you have of C. Vestorius or I of M. Cluvius,6 he was satisfied that we ought to credit him. He thought that Arcadia included a place on the coast called Lepreon, while Tenea, Aliphera, and Tritia7 were recent foundations in his opinion, which he supported by the Catalogue of Ships,8 where they are not mentioned. So I took the passage over from Dicaearchus just as it stood. As for ‘Phliasians,’ I know that this is the correct form, and please put it in your copies as I have done in mine.
