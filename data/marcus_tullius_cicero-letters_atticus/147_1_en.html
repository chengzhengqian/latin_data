Cumae, about 19 April 55

Cicero to Atticus

The news about Lentulus1 affects me as it should, naturally. We have lost a good and great man, who combined a really lofty spirit with much grace and kindliness of manner. My consolation, poor enough but still a consolation, is that I feel no sorrow at all on his account—I don’t say so after the fashion of Saufeius and your co-sectaries,2 but because upon my soul it seems to me a gift of providence that a man who loved his country as he did should be snatched away from its conflagration. After all what could be more ignominious than the life we lead, I especially? For you, though you are a political animal by nature, are not subject to any peculiar servitude, you have it in common
