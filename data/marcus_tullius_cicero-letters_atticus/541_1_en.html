Letters to Atticus

I am waiting to hear from you if there is any news about Brutus. Nicias to be sure thought the matter settled, but says the divorce4 is not popular. That makes me all the more anxious on the same score as yourself. If there is any adverse reaction this may put it right.

I must go to Arpinum. My little properties there need my attention, and I am afraid I may not be able to get away once Caesar comes home. Dolabella holds the same opinion about the date of his return as you deduced from Messalla’s letter. When I get there and see what there is to do I shall let you know approximately when I shall be coming back.

318 (XIII.10)

Tusculum, 18 (?) June 45

Cicero to Atticus

Little wonder you are shocked by the news about Marcellus1 and see new causes for alarm! Who could have feared anything so unprecedented, so apparently outside the course of nature? Clearly we are safe from nothing. But you make a historical slip, you of all people, when you speak of me as the last surviving Consular.2 What do you call Servius?
