For, think of me what you like (of course I should wish you to think as well as may be), if things take the course they seem to be taking (you must bear with what I am about to say), I find no satisfaction in the Ides of March. He would never have come back, fear would not have obliged us to confirm his measures; or again—here I join Saufeius and desert those ‘Tusculan Disputations’ which you are urging even Vestorius to read—I was so much in his good graces (may the gods confound him, dead as he is!) that at my time of life, since the killing of our master has not set us free, he was not a master to run away from. I am blushing, I do assure you; but it’s written now and I’m not disposed to cross it out.

I wish it had been true about Menedemus.5 I wish it may be true about the Queen. Other matters we shall discuss together, especially what our friends ought to do and what I myself ought to do if Antony is going to surround the Senate with soldiers. I was afraid that if I gave this letter to Fufius’ courier he would open it, so I am sending it by special messenger as yours needed an answer.
