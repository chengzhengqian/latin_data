As I think of it his behaviour seems to me the acme of gaucherie. And if you think we ought to carry on as we have begun as to the shrine, please encourage and stimulate Cluatius. Even if we decide in favour of a different locality I think we ought to make use of his services and advice. Perhaps you will be leaving for your country house tomorrow.

276 (XII.37)

Astura, 4 May 45

Cicero to Atticus

I received two letters from you yesterday, one dispatched the day before by Hilarus, the other by courier on the same day, and I also heard yesterday by my freedman Aegypta that Pilia and Attica are doing very nicely—the letter arrived on the third day from Cumae. Thank you for sending me Brutus’ letter. He wrote to me also. I am sending the letter itself to you and a copy of my answer.

As to the shrine, if you fail to find me anything in the way of a suburban property (but you should find one, if you are as fond of me as you assuredly are), I highly approve
