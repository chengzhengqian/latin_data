188 (VIII.9)

Between Formiae and Arpinum, 29 or 30 March 49

Cicero to Atticus

You write that my letter1 has been broadcast. I am not sorry to hear it, indeed I have myself let a number of people take copies. In view of what has already happened and of what is coming I want to have my sentiments about peace upon record. In urging this particular person in its favour I saw no readier means of influencing his mind than by representing that the course I was urging upon him befitted his wisdom. If I called the latter ‘admirable,’ in urging him to the salvation of our country, I had no fear of appearing to flatter; in such a cause I would gladly have thrown myself at his feet. In the passage where I ask him to ‘spare some time’ I was not referring to peace but requesting him to give some thought to myself and my obligations. As for my statement that I had taken no part in the war, the truth of it is sufficiently evident; but my object in so writing was to lend weight to my persuasions, and similarly with my approval of his cause.
