As for yourself, do come without fail, if you can bear to stick down here for a while, and bring Pilia,1 as is right and proper and as Tullia much wishes.

Well, upon my word you have bought a fine troop! I hear the gladiators are fighting magnificently. If you had cared to hire them out you would have cleared your outlay in these last two shows. But of this later. Do come, and please don’t forget about the clerks.

79 (IV.8)

Antium, shortly after 78 (IV.4a)

Cicero to Atticus

Many things in your letter delighted me, but the plate of fish au gratin1 was best of all. As for what you say about the bit of brass, ‘boast not before you see the end of all.’

I can’t find anything for you in the way of a house in the country. There is something in the town, which may or may not be for sale, and very near our house too.
