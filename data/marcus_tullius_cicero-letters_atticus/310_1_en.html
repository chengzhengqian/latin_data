159 (VIII.10)

Formiae, 24 February 49

Cicero to Atticus

Dionysius having come to me contrary to my expectation, I spoke to him in the most liberal way. I explained the circumstances and asked him to tell me what he had in mind, saying that I was not demanding anything of him against his own free will. He replied that he did not know what shape his investments were in. Some of his debtors were not paying, others were not yet due to pay. He added some stuff about his slaves as a reason why he could not keep us company. I let him have his way and dismissed him. As a teacher for the boys I was sorry to lose him, as an ingrate I let him go without reluctance. I wanted you to know, and to know my opinion of his behaviour.
