Thanks to me you are persona gratissima with Patro and the other dunderheads,4 and upon my word you deserve to be. He told me that you wrote to him saying I had taken care of that matter5 in accordance with his letter, for which he was very much obliged! Actually, after Patro requested me to ask your Areopagus to rescind the decree they passed when Polycharmus6 was General, it seemed best to Xeno (and later to Patro himself) for me to write to Memmius, who had left for Mytilene the day before my arrival in Athens, asking him to let his friends know that he had no objection. Xeno, you see, was certain that the Areopagites would not agree against Memmius’ wishes. Memmius had given up the idea of building, but he was annoyed with Patro. Accordingly I have written to him with some care, and I enclose a copy of my letter.

Would you please say a word of comfort to Pilia de ma part? For I’ll tell you, but you must not say anything to her: I received a parcel of letters in which there was one from Pilia to Quintus. I took it, opened it, and read it. It was very sympathetically written.
