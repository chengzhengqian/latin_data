I wish enough it were so, but I understand it to be far otherwise and I think you yourself are of that opinion, though you write otherwise to comfort (not deceive) me, especially now that Spain too is joining Africa.

You advise me to write to Antony, etc. If you think it needful, please do what you have often done before.1 I can’t think of anything I ought to write. You say you have heard that I am rather down in the mouth. What do you suppose, when you see how on top of my previous chagrins there are now my son-in-law’s distinguished activities?2 But do not stop writing to me as much as you can, even if you have nothing to write about. Your letters always bring me something.

I have declared my acceptance of Galeo’s estate. I suppose it is a simple acceptance, since no form was sent me.

8 March.
