143 (VII.19)

Formiae, 3 February 49

Cicero to Atticus

I have nothing to write to you about, indeed I have not dispatched a letter which I had already written to you by lamplight. It was full of optimism because I had heard of the feeling in the public meeting and thought Caesar would take the terms, particularly as they were of his own making. Then, at daybreak on 3 February, comes your letter along with others from Philotimus and Furnius and one from Curio to Furnius, in which he ridicules L. Caesar’s embassy. We seem to be quite crushed, and I don’t know what course to take. It’s really not myself I am worried about but the boys; I don’t know what to do about them. However I am leaving for Capua as I write this, hoping there to come by information about Pompey’s affairs more easily.
