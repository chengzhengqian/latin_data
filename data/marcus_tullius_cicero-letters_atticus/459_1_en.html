233 (XI.19)

Brundisium, 22 July 47

Cicero to Atticus

Since I have the chance of giving a letter to your people, I wonâ€™t neglect it, though I have nothing to write about. You write to us more rarely than you used and more briefly, which I take to be because you have nothing to say that you think could be agreeable for me to read or hear. But I should like you to write anything at all, no matter what. The only thing I have to pray for is some peace move, if that were possible. Not that I am in any hope of this, but you sometimes throw out faint hints, and so make me hope what is almost past praying for.

Philotimus is said to be coming on the Ides of August. I have no other news of him. I should be grateful for a reply to what I wrote to you earlier. I only need enough time to make some provision, the best I can in these deplorable circumstances, I who have never made provision for anything. Good-bye.

22 July.
