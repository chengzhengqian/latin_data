Letters to Atticus

I hope it augurs well. I am most grateful to you for your trouble in seeing that he gets plenty to live on in good style, and I beg you once more to do this. Your ideas about the Buthrotians are sound, and I am not forgetting this matter; in fact I shall take on myself the whole conduct of the case, which is evidently growing easier every day. As regards the Cluvius property, since your care for my interests exceeds my own, it’s getting up to the 100,000 mark. The collapse of the buildings has not lowered the returns, indeed I rather think it may actually have increased them.

Balbus, Hirtius, and Pansa are with me here. Octavius has just arrived, in fact he has gone to the house next to mine, Philippus’.1 He is totally devoted to me.2 Lentulus Spinther is staying the night with me. He is off early tomorrow.

366 (XIV.12)

Puteoli, 22 April 44

Cicero to Atticus

My dear Atticus, I fear the Ides of March have brought us nothing except joy and a satisfaction for our hatred and grief. The things I hear from Rome! And the things I see here! ‘’Twas a fine deed, but half done!’1 You know how warm a feeling I have for the Sicilians and what an honour I consider it to have them as my clients. Caesar was generous to them and I was not sorry that he should be—though the Latin franchise2 was intolerable, but let that pass.
