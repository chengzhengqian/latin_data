Meanwhile the tipsy wives laughed together, and gave each other drunken kisses, one prating of her merits as a housewife, the other of the favourites of her husband and his own demerits. While they were hobnobbing, Habinnas got up quietly, took Fortunata by the legs, and threw them up on the sofa. She shouted out, “Ow! Ow!” as her dress flew up over her knees. She took refuge in Scintilla’s arms, and buried her burning red face in a napkin.

After an interval, Trimalchio ordered a further dessert-course of food to be brought in. The slaves took away all the tables, brought in others, and sprinkled about sawdust coloured with saffron and vermilion, and, what I had never seen before, powdered mica. Trimalchio at once said, “I might really be satisfied with this course; for you have got your fresh relays. But if there is anything nice, put it on.”

Meanwhile a boy from Alexandria, who was handing hot water, proceeded to imitate a nightingale, and made Trimalchio shout “Oh! change the tune.” Then there was another joke. A slave, who was sitting at the feet of Habinnas, began, by his master’s orders I suppose, suddenly to declaim in a sing-song voice: “Now with his fleet Aeneas held the main”.2
