caught a Tartar:1 but his first vintage put his ribs right again: for he sold his wine for any price he then wanted. And what made him hold up his chin was that he came into an estate out of which he got more than had been left to him. And that blockhead, in a fit of passion with his brother, left the family property away to some son of the soil. He that flies from his own family has far to travel. But he had some eaves-dropping slaves who did for him. A man who is always ready to believe what is told him will never do well, especially a business man. Still no doubt he enjoyed himself every day of his life. † Blessed is he who gets the gift, not he for whom it is meant. He was a real Fortune’s darling, lead turned gold in his hands. Yes, it is easy when everything goes fair and square. And how many years do you think he had on his shoulders? Seventy and more. But he was a tough old thing, carried his age well, as black as a crow. I had known him world without end,2 and he was still lecherous. I really do not think he left even his dog unmolested in his house. No, he was still a boy-catcher, a man of every fine art. Well, I do not blame him: it is only his past pleasures he could take with him.”
