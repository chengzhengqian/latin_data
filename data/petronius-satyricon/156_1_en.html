who is it that fears my fame, who are the men that forbid me make war? Base hirelings bought at a price, to whom my native Rome is a step-mother. But I think that no coward shall bind my strong arm unhurt without a blow in return. Come, men, to victory while anger is hot, come, my comrades, and plead our cause with the sword. For we are all summoned under one charge, and the same doom hangs over us all. My thanks are your due, my victory is not mine alone. Wherefore, since punishment threatens our trophies, and disgrace is the meed of conquest, let Chance decide how our lot shall fall. Raise the standard and prove your strength. My pleading at least is accomplished; armed amid so many warriors I cannot know defeat..’ As he spoke these words aloud, the Delphic bird1 in the sky gave a happy omen, and beat the air as it flew about. And from the left quarter of a gloomy grove strange voices sounded and fire flashed thereafter. Even Phoebus2 glowed with orb brighter than his wont, and set a burning halo of gold about his face.

“Heartened by these omens, Caesar advanced the standards of war, and marched first to open this strange tale of daring. At first indeed the ice and the ground fettered with white frost did not fight against them, and lay quiet in the kindly cold.
