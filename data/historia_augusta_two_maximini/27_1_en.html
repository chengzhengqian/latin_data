“Like to the star of the morning when he, new-bathèd in Ocean, Raises his holy face and scatters the darkness from heaven,2 So did the young man seem, fair-famed in the name of his father.”

Latin grammar he studied under Philemon, jurisprudence under Modestinus,3 and oratory under Titianus, the son of that elder Titianus4 who wrote a very beautiful work on the provinces and was called the ape of his age because he imitated everything. He employed also the Greek rhetorician Eugamius, who was famous in his day.

Junia Fadilla,5 the great-granddaughter of Antoninus, was betrothed to him; but afterwards she was espoused by Toxotius, a senator of the same family, who died after serving his praetorship, certain poems of his being extant today.
