Inscriptions on Movable Articles

10–11

On scrapers.

(i) A small weasel, with base and handle. Praeneste.

Caress1 me. Property of Lucia Cornelia, daughter of Lucius.

(ii) Found near Corchiano, Faliscan territory.

Me Lucilius made.

12–16

On ware made before 220 b.c. at Cales2 and found in various parts of Italy (see C.I.L., I, 2, 405–417; and id, pp. 720–1, numbers 405a, 406(a), 2487 ff.).

(i) On a number of paterae.

Made by Lucius Canuleius of Cales, son of Lucius.

(ii) On paterae found at Tarquinii.

I, Retus Gabinius, slave3 of Gaius, made you at Cales.

(iii) On a patera bought at Naples.

Kaeso Serponius made this at Cales, in the Esquiline quarter. A slave of Gaius.

(iv) On a mould for paterae, found at Capua: now in the British Museum.

I am from the workshop4 of Lucius Canuleius.

(v) On a clay vessel. Rome?

Marcus? a household slave; at Cales.

  • 1So 1 take it; the owner meant stroke me softly when I use you. Cf. Buecheler, Rh. Mus., LII, 392; Fay, Studies in Honour of Gildersleeve, 194 thinks fove = fui. Read perhaps fave, ‘be favourable to Lucia Cornelia,’ since the weasel was believed to have magic powers. Or translate ‘fove,’ ‘cherish,’ with the same idea.
  • 2For the whole series, of. Pagenstecher, Die Calenische Reliefkeramik, Arch. Jahrb., Ergänz.-heft VIII; and Arch. Jahrb., XXVII, 146 ff.; Koerte, Götting. Gel. Anz., 1913, 253.
  • 3Here and in the next inscription slave means freedman.
  • 4Or:—‘I am the workmanship’; cf. Pliny, XI, 2.
