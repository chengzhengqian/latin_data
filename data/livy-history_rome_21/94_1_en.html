BOOK XXI

They voted unanimously to recall him, drag him back, and compel him to present himself for the performance of all his duties toward gods and men before leaving for his army and province. On the delegation sent to him—for it was decided that delegates should be sent—were Quintus Terentius and Marcus Antistius,257 who made no more impression on him than had the letter sent to him by the senate during his previous consulship.

A few days later he entered office, and as he was offering sacrifice the calf, which had already been dealt the blow, charged from the hands of those performing the sacrifice and spattered many of the bystanders with blood. There was even greater commotion and alarm among those further away, who were unaware of the excitement’s cause. By most people this was interpreted as a frightful omen.258

After that Flaminius took over command of the two legions from Sempronius and two others from the praetor Gaius Atilius, and the army proceeded on its march into Etruria along tracks over the Apennines.
