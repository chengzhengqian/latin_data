63. Flaminius, one of the consuls designate, to whom the legions wintering at Placentia had come by sortition, sent written instructions to the consul for these troops to be present in camp at Ariminum on March 15.252 It was Flaminius’ intention to commence his consulship right there in the province since he remembered his earlier disagreements with the senate as tribune of the plebs253 and later as consul, when there was first one over his consulship, which the senators tried to revoke, and then one over his triumph.254 But he was also unpopular with the senators because of a new bill, directed against the senate (which the plebeian tribune Quintus Claudius had proposed and Gaius Flaminius was the only senator to support), forbidding any senator or anyone whose father had been a senator to own a seagoing vessel with a capacity of more than three hundred amphorae.
