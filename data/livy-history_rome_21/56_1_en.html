The armies were now almost in sight of one another and two commanders had come to a confrontation in which each, though not yet knowing the other well, felt some sort of admiration for him. Hannibal’s name had been very well known to the Romans even before the destruction of Saguntum; and Hannibal believed Scipio was a remarkable man simply from his being chosen as the commander to face him. And each had also raised this mutual esteem in the other, Scipio because, left behind in Gaul, he had still come to face Hannibal after his crossing into Italy; and Hannibal because of such a daring attempt at crossing the Alps and its success.

However, it was Scipio who crossed the Po first, and after moving his camp to the River Ticinus161 he proceeded with the following speech to encourage the men before he led them into battle:162

40. “Men! Were I leading into battle the army I had with me in Gaul, I could have dispensed with an address before you.
