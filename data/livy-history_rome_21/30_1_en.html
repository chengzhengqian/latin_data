To compensate for that, he requested troops from Africa, mainly light-armed javelin throwers, so that Africans would be serving in Spain and Spaniards in Africa, both contingents likely to prove the better soldiers for serving far from home through being bound, as it were, by reciprocal obligations. He sent to Africa 13,850 caetra-bearing infantry,82 870 Balearic slingers,83 and 1,200 cavalry84 made up of many different peoples. He ordered some of these forces to serve as a garrison for Carthage and others to be deployed throughout Africa. At the same time he sent recruiting officers to the communities85 with orders for 4,000 handpicked men of military age to be conscripted and brought to Carthage to strengthen the garrison and also serve as hostages.

Feeling that Spain should not be neglected, either (the more so since he was not unaware that the Roman ambassadors had done the rounds of it to enlist the support of tribal chieftains), he assigned that responsibility to his brother Hasdrubal, a man of great energy, and equipped him mostly with African troops: 11,850 African infantry, 300 Ligurians and 500 Balearic islanders. To these auxiliary infantry forces the following cavalry was added: 450 Libyphoenicians (a race of mixed Punic and African descent), some 1,800 Numidians and Moors, who live by the Ocean, and a small force of 300 Ilergetan86
