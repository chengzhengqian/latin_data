The infantryman, unsteady on his feet and wary of the ford, could easily have been thrown off balance even by the random charges of an unarmed cavalryman, but the cavalryman could move freely, not having to carry the weight of his body and weapons, and fighting, at close quarters or at a distance, from a mount that remained stable even in the midst of swirling waters.

Most27 were swept away by the river; some were borne into their foes by the churning current and trampled under by the elephants. The rearmost could return more safely to their bank of the river, but as they tried to regroup after their various forms of confusion Hannibal entered the stream with a company in square formation28 before they could recover from their sheer panic and drove them in flight from the bank. He ravaged their fields and within a few days also accepted the surrender of the Carpetani. And now everything south of the Ebro was under Carthaginian control, apart from Saguntum.

6. To this point there had been no war with Saguntum, but already quarrels were being fomented between her and her neighbors, especially the Turdetani,29 in order to provoke a war. As the Turdetani were receiving assistance from the instigator of the conflict and it was clear that the goal was not a fair settlement but violent confrontation, legates were sent to Rome by the Saguntines begging for assistance for a war that was clearly imminent.
