They realized that they had never come to grips with a more ruthless or combative foe and that Rome had never been in such a shiftless and enervated condition. The Sardinians, Corsicans, Istrians and Illyrians had teased Roman military power but not really put it to the test, and with the Gauls there had been desultory rather than regular warfare.59 But the Carthaginians were their enemy of old, who had spent twenty-three years in the hardest kind of campaigning amid the Spanish tribes, campaigns from which they had always emerged victorious. They were accustomed to the most demanding leader and were crossing the Ebro fresh from their destruction of a prosperous city. They had called to arms and were bringing with them so many Spanish peoples, and would now rouse to action the ever-belligerent Gallic tribes. The Romans would have to fight the whole world in Italy and before the walls of Rome.

17. Their provinces had already been designated for the consuls, and they were now instructed to proceed to sortition.60 Spain fell to Cornelius and Africa together with Sicily to Sempronius. It was decided that six legions would be employed that year61 along with as many allied troops as the consuls should deem appropriate and as great a fleet as could be mustered.
