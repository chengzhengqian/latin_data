14. A crowd had gradually come together to hear this and the general assembly of the people had merged with the senate when the leading men suddenly split away, and before a reply could be given to Alorcus all the silver and gold from public and private buildings was brought together in the forum. This they threw into a fire hurriedly built for the purpose, and most of them flung themselves in as well. When the resulting fear and panic had swept through the city, another clamor was heard coming from the citadel. A tower that had taken a long pounding had collapsed and a company of Carthaginians had forced its way through the breach. When the company signaled back to its commander that the city was now deprived of its customary sentry posts and guards, Hannibal, thinking such an opportunity did not brook delay, attacked in full force and, after giving the sign for all of fighting age to be put to death, he took the city in no time.

That order was callous but in the event was found to have been almost necessary; for how could any be spared when they shut themselves away with their wives and children and burned their homes over their own heads, or took up weapons and allowed only death to end to their resistance?

15. The town was taken with an enormous amount of plunder. Most property had been deliberately destroyed by its owners; in the bloodbath exasperation had barely allowed any differentiation in ages; and captives had also become the booty of the rank and file. It is, however, well established that a considerable sum of money was realized from the sale of goods and that much expensive furniture and clothing was shipped to Carthage.
