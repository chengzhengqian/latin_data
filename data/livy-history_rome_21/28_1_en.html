On the contrary, they said, they were receiving reports of men of their race being driven by the Roman people from their land on the Italian borders,74 and of these men paying tribute and suffering all other such indignities.

Much the same sort of verbal exchanges occurred in the other tribal councils in Gaul and no hospitable or friendly word was heard until the envoys came to Massilia.75 There they learned the full story from their allies, who had made careful and precise inquiries: Hannibal, they were told, had anticipated them in enlisting the support of the Gauls.76 But, added the Massiliots, even Hannibal himself would not find them a very easy people to deal with, naturally aggressive and violent as they were, unless their chieftains were repeatedly wooed with gold, for which the race had a voracious appetite.77

So, their round of the peoples of Spain and Gaul complete, the envoys returned to Rome not long after the consuls had left for their provinces. They found the whole community tense with the anticipation of war as there was a persistent rumor that the Carthaginians had already crossed the Ebro.
