From the prisoners it was learned that apart from the fleet of twenty ships sent to Italy, to which the prisoners themselves belonged, a further thirty-five quinqueremes were making for Sicily with the aim of spreading disaffection among former Carthaginian allies. The principal mission of these vessels was to seize Lilybaeum,194 they said, but they thought that this fleet had also been driven off course toward the Aegates Islands by the same bad weather by which they had themselves been scattered.195 The king made a full report of this, just as it had been recounted to him, in a letter to Marcus Aemilius, whose province was Sicily, and advised him to secure Lilybaeum with a powerful garrison. Legates were immediately sent around the various city-states by the praetor, and tribunes also pressed their men to heighten their caution. Above all they kept Lilybaeum on a war footing, with a proclamation issued for crews to take ten daysâ€™ cooked rations to their ships and for no one to delay embarking once the signal was given; and men were sent all along the coast to keep watch from towers for the approach of the enemy fleet.
