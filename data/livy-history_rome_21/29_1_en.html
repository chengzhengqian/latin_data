“Allies!” he said “I think you, too, can see that, after winning over all the tribes of Spain,78 we must terminate our campaign and demobilize our armies or else move our operations into other lands. For the tribes here will enjoy the fruits not just of our victory but also of peace only if we search for plunder and glory among other races. And so, as a campaign far from home lies ahead and it is unclear when you are going to see your homes and the things there that are dear to each of you, I am granting furlough to any of you wishing to visit his family. I give you notice to be here at the start of spring so that with heaven’s help we may embark upon a war that will bring us immense glory and spoils.”

They were almost all delighted to be freely offered the opportunity to visit their homes; they had already been missing their loved ones and saw ahead an even more protracted longing for them. An entire winter of repose between hardships experienced or others soon to be faced revived them physically and psychologically to confront all new challenges ahead.79 At the start of spring they mustered as instructed.

After a review of his auxiliary troops from all the nations, Hannibal set off for Gades80 where he discharged his vows to Hercules81 and bound himself with further vows in the event of continued success. He then divided his attention between offensive and defensive measures.
