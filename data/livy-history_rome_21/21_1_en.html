If that is so, then Publius Cornelius and Tiberius Sempronius54 cannot have been the consuls to whom the Saguntine envoys were sent at the start of the siege and who also engaged with Hannibal during their term of office (one at the River Ticinus, and both somewhat later at the Trebia55). Either all these events took a considerably shorter time or else it was not the start of the siege but the capture of Saguntum that took place at the beginning of the year in which Publius Cornelius and Tiberius Sempronius were consuls. For the battle of the Trebia cannot have been as late as the consular year of Gnaeus Servilius and Gaius Flaminius because Gaius Flaminius entered his consulship at Ariminum,56 his election supervised by the consul Tiberius Sempronius. Sempronius had come to Rome after the battle of the Trebia to oversee the consular elections, and when the elections were over he returned to his army in its winter quarters.57
