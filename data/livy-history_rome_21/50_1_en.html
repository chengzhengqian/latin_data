Hannibal then spent a night without his cavalry and baggage.140

35. Since the barbarian assaults slackened the next day, the Carthaginian force reunited and the top of the pass was reached, not without losses but with more pack animals killed than men. From there on, the mountain men became fewer in number and made predatory raids rather than regular strikes, now at the head and now on the rear of the column, wherever the terrain offered an opportunity or where they could capitalize on the Carthaginians advancing too far or falling behind. As for the elephants, they could be driven only at a snail’s pace along the steep and narrow paths, but wherever they went they also kept the column safe from the enemy who, being unfamiliar with the beasts, feared to come too close.

On the ninth day they reached the crest of the Alps, mostly by detours over trackless terrain, either because of the duplicity of their guides or because, distrusting the guides, they would stray blindly into gorges while guessing their way. They encamped on the crest for two days, and the soldiers, exhausted from their exertions and fighting, were given a rest. A number of pack animals that had fallen along the rocky path reached the camp by following the tracks of the column.

The men were sick and tired of all their tribulations, when a fall of snow—it was now the setting of the constellation Pleiades141—added tremendous fear.
