Hasdrubal was also assigned a fleet to protect the coastline because it could be supposed that the Romans would then, too, operate with that mode of warfare in which they had earlier been victors.87 There were fifty quinqueremes, two quadriremes, and five triremes; but of these only thirty-two quinqueremes and the five triremes were fitted out and furnished with crews.88

From Gades Hannibal returned to the army’s winter quarters in New Carthage; and on leaving there he marched along the coast, past the city of Onussa,89 to the Ebro. There is a tale that he there saw in a dream a young man of godlike appearance, who said he had been sent by Jupiter as Hannibal’s guide into Italy and that he should therefore follow him and not take his eyes off him at any point. At first, they say, Hannibal, frightened, followed without letting his gaze wander around or back at any point; but then, with the curiosity of any human being, he began to wonder what it could be that he was forbidden to see behind him, and he could not help but look. He then saw a snake of amazing size sliding along, causing massive destruction to trees and undergrowth, with a deafening thunderstorm following in its wake. Then, asking the young man what the monstrous apparition was and what the portent meant, he was told that it was the destruction of Italy and that he should simply proceed on his journey, ask no further questions and leave destiny shrouded in darkness.90
