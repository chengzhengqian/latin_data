With that, panic struck the Romans, and that panic was increased when the consul was wounded, the threat to his life averted only by the intervention of his son (who was then just coming to maturity). This was to be the young man who would have the distinction of terminating this war and be called “Africanus” for his outstanding victory over Hannibal and the Carthaginians.181

The disordered flight, however, was mostly confined to the javelin throwers, whom the Numidians attacked first. The rest of the cavalry closed ranks, took the consul into their midst, giving him protection not only with their weapons but also with own bodies, and brought him back to camp with no hint of panic or disorder in their retreat. Coelius182 assigns the glory of the consul’s rescue to a slave of Ligurian nationality. I would prefer to accept as true the version about the son, an account that more authors have transmitted and popular tradition has also maintained.

47. This was the first engagement with Hannibal, and it was quite clear from it that the Carthaginians had cavalry superiority and that, for this reason, open plains like those between the Po and the Alps were not a suitable theater of operations for the Romans. Accordingly, the following night, after ordering his men to pack up quietly, Scipio struck camp from the Ticinus, and marched swiftly to the Po183 to take his army across on the pontoon bridge with which he had spanned the river (and which was not yet dismantled), thus avoiding confusion and enemy harassment.
