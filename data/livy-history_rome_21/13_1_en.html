It was evident that, refused a hearing, the ambassadors would carry straight on to Carthage. Hannibal accordingly sent letters and messengers ahead to the leaders of the Barca faction40 so they could prime his supporters to make it impossible for their opposition to concede anything to the Roman people.

10. Thus, apart from being received and given a hearing in Carthage, this delegation also proved ineffective and a waste of time. Hanno alone challenged the senate and defended the treaty,41 heard in hushed silence because of the respect he commanded but with no support from his audience as he implored the senate, in the name of the gods who oversee and witness treaties, not to provoke hostilities with Rome as well as Saguntum. He had advised them, he had warned them not to send the child of Hamilcar to the army, he said; Hamilcar’s ghost could not remain calm nor could any son of his, and never while any of the Barca bloodline or name survived would the treaty with Rome remain undisturbed.

“A young man burning with desire for supreme power,” he continued, “and seeing only one way to it, that is by living surrounded by weapons and legions and by following one war with another—that is what you have sent out to your armies, virtually adding fuel to the flames. So you have fed this fire in which you are now being consumed.
