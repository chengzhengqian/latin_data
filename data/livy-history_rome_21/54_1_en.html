With these included, the total brought into Italy was 80,000 foot and 10,000 horse, but it is likely, and some authors indeed affirm it, that it was within Italy rather that these peoples flocked to join him. Cincius also claims that he heard from Hannibal’s own lips that after crossing the Rhone he lost 36,000 men and a huge number of horses and other beasts of burden.

The Taurini Semigalli151 were the first people Hannibal encountered when he came down into Italy. As this is universally accepted, I am all the more surprised at the dispute over his route over the Alps, at the common belief that he crossed by the Poenine Alps (and that this Alpine range was given its name on that account), and at Coelius’152 statement that Hannibal crossed by way of the Cremo ridge,153 both of which would have brought Hannibal down not among the Taurini but among the Libuan Gauls, through the mountain-dwelling Salassi.154 Moreover,
