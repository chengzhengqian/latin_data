39. Very conveniently for the opening of Hannibal’s campaign, a war had been launched against the Insubres158 by the Taurini, the tribe closest to them. Hannibal, however, could not put his troops under arms to assist one side or the other since it was now, during recovery, that they were feeling the worst effects of their earlier afflictions. Rest after hardship, plenty after privation, care after filth and disease—all this was having various physical affects on physiques that had been reduced to squalor and near brutalization. That was why the consul Publius Scipio hastened toward the Po after reaching Pisa159 by sea with his ships, even though the army he had taken over from Manlius and Atilius160 was one of raw recruits demoralized by their recent shameful performance—his aim was to engage an enemy whose strength was not yet restored. When the consul reached Placentia, however, Hannibal had already moved from his camp and had taken by storm a single city of the Taurini—in fact, the tribal capital—because its people would not readily enter into an alliance with him.
