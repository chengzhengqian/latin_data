pack animals shriveled with cold; human figures shaggy and unkempt; everything, animate and inanimate, stiff with frost; and all else a grimmer sight than could possibly be described in words.135 As they brought the column up the nearest slopes, mountain dwellers came into view, occupying the heights overlooking them; had they instead positioned themselves in the gorges that provided more cover they could have suddenly sprung to the attack and caused a massive flight and slaughter. Hannibal ordered a halt, sent some Gauls forward to reconnoiter the area and, learning from them that there was no way through, pitched camp in the most level depression that could be found in the generally broken and rocky terrain. Then, through these same Gauls, who managed to insinuate themselves into conversations with the local mountain men, from whom they differed little in language and customs, he further discovered that the pass was guarded only by day and that at night the men all slipped away to their own homes. And so, at dawn, he approached the heights as if intending to force his way through openly in broad daylight.
