As they had to cut through rock, they felled some massive trees in the area,147 stripped them of their branches, made a huge pile of logs, and set them on fire when a strong breeze arose suitable for whipping up a blaze; and when the rocks were hot they made them disintegrate by pouring vinegar on them.148 After scorching the cliff face with fires in this way, they opened it up with picks and softened the gradient with short zigzag paths so that not only the pack animals but even the elephants could be led down.

Four days were spent at the cliff, during which the pack animals almost starved to death; for the mountain peaks are practically bare and the snows cover such pasture as there is. The lower regions have valleys and a number of sunny hillocks; and they have streams with woods close by them and more suitable areas for human habitation. There the pack animals were put out to pasture and the men, exhausted from their road building, were given rest. In three days they descended from there into the plains, and by now both the environment and the disposition of the inhabitants were more agreeable.
