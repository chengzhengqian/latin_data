He then took a few days’ repose after which, although his wound was not yet properly healed, he set off to launch an attack on Victumulae.219 This had been a storage depot for the Romans during the Gallic War. After that, as it had been fortified, it had become thickly settled by an assortment of inhabitants from neighboring tribes; and at this time fear of marauding had driven a number of people to it from the countryside. Such was the makeup of the band that hastily took up arms and advanced to face Hannibal, their spirits fired by word of the stout defense mounted by the garrison at Placentia. They encountered each other on the road as marching columns rather than battle lines; and since there was nothing but a disorganized mob on one side, and a commander with confidence in his soldiers and soldiers with confidence in their commander on the other, some thirty-five thousand men were routed by a few.

The next day the townspeople capitulated and received a garrison within their walls. They were ordered to surrender their weapons, and when they complied a signal was suddenly given to the conquerors to pillage the place like a captured city. Nor were the people spared any of the atrocities usually recounted by historians as occurring in such circumstances. Indeed, the wretched inhabitants were subjected to every form of sexual abuse, cruelty, and inhuman mistreatment. Such were Hannibal’s winter operations.
