Am I to compare myself with this six-month commander, with this deserter of his own army?174 Should someone today take away the standards and show him the Carthaginians and the Romans, I am sure the man would have no idea of which army he is the consul! And, personally, I do not consider it to be of little importance that there is not one of you before whose eyes I have not myself often performed some military exploit, or one whose courage I have not personally observed and witnessed, whose feats I could not recount, with details of time and place. I shall proceed to battle with men praised and decorated a thousand times by me, and I, a stepson to you all before becoming your commander, will be facing soldiers who do not know each other.

44. “Wherever I have turned my eyes all I see is courage and strength: a veteran infantry; cavalry, with and without bridles,175 drawn from peoples of truly noble spirit; you, our allies, most loyal and brave; and you Carthaginians, who will fight both for your country and also because of your most righteous indignation. We are on the offensive and descending to attack Italy, ready to fight all the more boldly and bravely—as the invader we have higher hopes and greater morale than the defender. In addition, our hearts are fired and stimulated by resentment, injustice, and humiliation.
