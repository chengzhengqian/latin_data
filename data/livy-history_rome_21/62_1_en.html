You have had enough of chasing sheep on the desolate mountains of Lusitania and Celtiberia172 and seeing no return for all your tribulation and danger. It is now time to make your service rich and profitable and to earn great rewards for all your efforts after so great a journey over so many mountains and rivers and through so many belligerent tribes. It is here that Fortune has granted an end to your labors; here she will give you the reward you deserve when your service is done.

“And do not think that victory will be as difficult as the fame of the war might suggest. Often an underrated enemy has put up a bloody fight, and famous peoples and kings have been defeated with very little effort. For set aside this one thing, the shining name of Rome, and how can they be compared with you? To say nothing of the twenty years you have served with your famed valor and success, you have arrived here as victors from the columns of Hercules and from the Ocean and the furthest bounds of the earth,173 passing through all the most violent tribes of Spain and Gaul. You will fight an inexperienced army that was massacred, beaten and blockaded by Gauls this very summer, an army that its commander still does not know and which does not know its commander.
