For all the other prodigies the decemviri were instructed to consult the books,243 but in the case of the shower of stones in Picenum a nine-day sacrifice was officially prescribed;244 and immediately after that almost the entire citizenry set about performing other ceremonies of expiation. First of all, the city was ritually purified and full-grown sacrificial animals killed for specified gods. A gold gift weighing forty pounds was transported to Lanuvium for Juno, and the married women dedicated a bronze statue to Juno on the Aventine. A lectisternium245 was prescribed at Caere, where the oracular lots had shrunk, and a supplication246 to Fortune on Mt. Algidus.247 At Rome, too, a lectisternium was prescribed, there for Juventas,248 and a supplication was to be offered at the temple of Hercules, who was specifically named; but then the whole population was also directed to offer one at all the couches of the gods.
