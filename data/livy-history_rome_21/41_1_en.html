When their men returned to their respective commanders after the engagement, Scipio felt he could not adhere to any plan other than that of reacting to the strategy and moves of the enemy. As for Hannibal, he was unsure whether to continue the march on Italy that he had begun or rather engage with this first Roman army that appeared before him; but he was kept from an immediate confrontation by the arrival of a delegation of Boii and their chieftain Magalus. They solemnly undertook to act as guides for the routes ahead and to share the dangers, and they recommended that he attack Italy before opening hostilities and weakening his forces anywhere else.119 Most of the men did, indeed, fear their enemy, the memory of the last war being not yet erased; but they dreaded more the endless march over the Alps, something rumor made terrifying, especially for those with no experience of it.

30. And so, his decision to continue his march and head for Italy unchanged, Hannibal called a meeting of his men and roused spirits with a mixture of criticism and encouragement.
