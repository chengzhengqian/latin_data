32. Some three days after Hannibal moved camp from the bank of the Rhone the consul Publius Cornelius had come to the enemy encampment with his army in battle formation, intending to engage without delay. However, when he saw that the fortifications were abandoned and that catching up with the enemy would not be easy when they had such a head start, he returned to his ships on the coast, thinking it would be safer and easier to confront Hannibal while he was coming down from the Alps. But not to leave Spain (which he had been allotted as his province) bereft of Roman support, he sent his brother Gnaeus Scipio132 with most of his troops against Hasdrubal, not just to defend old allies of Rome and enlist new ones but also to drive Hasdrubal out of Spain. He himself headed back to Genua133 with considerably diminished forces, intending to defend Italy with the army that was now in the vicinity of the Po.
