“Romans, are you not embarrassed to ask us to set friendship with you above friendship with Carthage, when you—their allies!—betrayed men who did just that, acting more callously than the Punic foe who destroyed them.72 I think you should be looking for allies where the disaster of Saguntum is not known. To the peoples of Spain the ruins of Saguntum will stand as an object lesson, as harrowing as it is striking, not to put one’s trust in Roman loyalty or a Roman alliance.”

With that, the ambassadors were ordered to leave the territory of the Volciani at once, and they received no more favorable a response thereafter from any other tribal council in Spain. Accordingly, after a fruitless journey through Spain, they crossed into Gaul.

20. There the embassy was presented with a strange and alarming spectacle: the Gauls, as was their tribal custom, came armed to the meeting. When, after heaping praise on the glory and valor of the Roman people and the greatness of their empire, the ambassadors asked the Gauls not to grant the Carthaginian passage through their lands and cities when he attempted to carry hostilities into Italy,73 it is said that such noisy guffaws arose that the younger warriors could barely be brought to order by the magistrates and older men. It seemed such a stupid and impudent request—to suggest that the Gauls, to prevent a war from passing into Italy, should bring it upon themselves and expose their own rather than someone else’s farmlands to devastation.
