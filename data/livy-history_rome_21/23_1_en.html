The question was then put before the people whether it was their wish and command62 that war be declared on the people of Carthage, and because of the war a supplication63 was held throughout the city, with entreaties made to the gods for a victorious and happy outcome in the war that the Roman People had authorized.

The division of troops between the consuls went as follows. Sempronius was given two legions, each comprising 4,000 infantry and 300 cavalry,64 together with 16,000 allied infantry and 1,800 allied cavalry. He was also assigned a hundred and sixty warships and twelve cutters. With such land and naval forces Tiberius Sempronius was dispatched to Sicily, from which he was to cross to Africa if the other consul sufficed to keep the Carthaginian out of Italy.

Cornelius was given fewer troops because the praetor Lucius Manlius was also being sent to Gaul with a force of not inconsiderable strength. In particular, the number of Cornelius’ vessels was curtailed: he was given just sixty quinqueremes, since the senate believed the enemy would not come by sea or employ that mode of warfare. He received in addition two Roman legions with their regular complement of cavalry, and 14,000 allied infantry together with 1,600 allied cavalry.
