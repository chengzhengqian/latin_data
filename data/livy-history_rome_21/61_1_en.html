43. When the performance of a number of pairs had had this effect, Hannibal dismissed the men, called an assembly and is said to have addressed them as follows:

“If in considering your own prospects you demonstrate the same spirit as you did when a little while ago you observed the fate of others, then we have won, Men. For that was not just a show but something virtually mirroring your own situation. I wonder, in fact, if fortune has not imposed on you stronger fetters and more pressing circumstances than on your prisoners. To the right and left two seas hem you in, and you have not a single ship even to make an escape. Around you is the River Po, bigger and more violent than the Rhone, and to your rear is the barrier of the Alps, which you could barely cross when your strength was unimpaired and robust. Here, men, you must either conquer or die as soon as you meet the enemy. And that very same fortune that has imposed on you the obligation to fight holds out for you, if you prevail, prizes greater than men usually ask for even in prayers to the immortal gods! If all that we were going to recover by our valor were Sicily and Sardinia, which were filched from our fathers, those would be prizes great enough in themselves.
