BOOK XXI

At that time there was internal strife: two brothers were locked in a struggle for the throne. The elder, whose name was Braneus, had already been the ruler but now faced being ousted by his younger brother and a clique of younger men with less justice but greater strength on their side. Very opportunely, adjudication of this dispute was referred to Hannibal who, having the disposal of the kingdom in his hands, restored authority to the elder brother because that had been the preference of the senate and leading citizens. For this service Hannibal was provided with support in the form of all manner of supplies and provisions, especially clothing, acquiring which the infamous cold of the Alps made necessary.

After settling the Allobrogian disputes Hannibal now headed for the Alps, but instead of taking the direct route he veered to the left into the lands of the Tricastini.128 He then advanced through the border territory of the Vocontii129 into the territory of the Trigorii,130 meeting no obstacle anywhere on his route until he reached the River Druentia. Also an Alpine river, this is, of all the waterways of Gaul, by far the most difficult to cross. The volume of water it carries is enormous but it is not navigable because it has no banks confining it.
