53. But in the eyes of no one at all was the victory greater and more just than in the consul’s. He was transported with joy at having carried the day with that branch of the forces with which the other consul had been defeated. His men’s morale had been restored and strengthened, he claimed, and there was no one apart from his colleague who wanted to delay an all-out battle; the man’s problem was more psychological than physical, he said—the thought of his wound made him shudder at the prospect of armed engagement. But they should not become decrepit along with the ailing man. Why should things be further delayed or time frittered away? What third consul, what other army was expected to come? The Carthaginians’ camp was in Italy and almost in sight of the city.
