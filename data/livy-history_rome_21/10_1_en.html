Hannibal entered their territory with an invading force, laid waste their agricultural lands and made a three-pronged attack on the city.35 There was a corner of the city wall overlooking a valley, where the ground became more level and open than the other land surrounding the town. It was toward this that Hannibal proceeded to advance his siege sheds,36 under which the battering ram could be brought up to the walls. But although the terrain at some remove from the wall was level enough for moving up the siege sheds, the plan met with no success at all once it came to the actual execution. There was a huge tower rising above the spot, and the wall there had also been built up higher than elsewhere, as one would expect at an insecure point; and an elite detachment was also posted there to provide stiffer resistance in an area recognized as particularly dangerous and suspect. At first, the detachment kept the enemy at bay with projectiles and left the siege workers with no security; and later on not only were their missiles flashing before the walls and the tower but they even found the courage to make forays against their enemy’s sentry posts and siege works; and in these spontaneous engagements Saguntine casualties were hardly greater
