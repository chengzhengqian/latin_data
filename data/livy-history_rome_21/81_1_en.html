Furthermore, as soon as the Roman line had unwittingly passed the Carthaginian hiding place, Mago and his Numidians rose up to their rear and caused enormous confusion and panic. But despite these setbacks all around, the Roman line held firm for a time, even in the face of the elephants, which none could have expected. Skirmishers,211 expressly posted for the job, would turn the beasts with showers of spears and, when they turned, would follow and stab them under the tail where, because of the softness of the hide, the animals are most easily wounded.

56. The beasts being panic-stricken and now almost roused to fury against their own troops, Hannibal ordered them to be driven from the center of the fight against the Gallic auxiliaries on the far left wing. There they immediately started what was indubitably a rout; and fresh panic also overtook the Romans when they saw their own auxiliaries put to flight.

They were now fighting bunched in a circle and, being unable to find any other way out, about ten thousand men broke through the African center, which was reinforced with Gallic auxiliaries, inflicting heavy losses on the enemy. Then since, being cut off by the river, they could neither return to camp nor, because of the rain, decide where to bring assistance to their comrades, they headed straight for Placentia.
