During that time he was whetting their spirit, at one moment by provoking them to anger against the enemy and at the next by promising them rewards. In fact, when he proclaimed in an assembly that the spoils from the captured city would go to them, they were all so fired up that it appeared that they would have been completely unstoppable had the signal been immediately given. As for the Saguntines, while they had had several days’ rest from fighting, neither launching nor facing attack, they had never halted work on the defenses by day or night in an effort to construct a new wall where the town had been left exposed by the collapse of the old one.

Then the assault on them recommenced with considerably more ferocity than before, and in the confused uproar and shouting everywhere they could not be sure where to bring help first or in the fullest measure. Hannibal was himself on hand to give encouragement where a moveable tower that surmounted all the city’s defenses was being pushed forward. When it was brought up, it swept the walls clean of defenders with its catapults and ballistas47 that were deployed on all its platforms; and then Hannibal, sensing an opportunity, sent some five hundred Africans with pickaxes to burrow under the wall.48 That was no difficult operation, either, because the stones were not set with mortar but merely lined with clay in the manner of the old masonry. As a result, the wall caved in beyond the section where it was being cut away, and packs of armed Carthaginians streamed into the city through openings left by the collapse.
