And there is nothing I fear more than this: that after you have fought, someone may think it was the Alps that actually defeated Hannibal.166 And yet perhaps it should be so, that the gods themselves, with no human aid, should start and virtually finish a war with a treaty-breaking commander and people while we, who are victims of the outrage next after the gods, should finish off what they began and almost completed.

41. “I have no fear that any of you may think this is bluster on my part just intended to encourage you, while my true feelings are different. I could have gone off with my own army to Spain, my province, for which I had already started out; there I would have my brother involved in my decisions and sharing the danger, and have Hasdrubal rather than Hannibal as my foe and a war of clearly lesser proportions. But no! On receiving the news about this enemy as I was skirting the coast of Gaul with my fleet, I disembarked and, sending my cavalry ahead, moved my camp up to the Rhone. In a cavalry battle, the only branch of my forces with which I was given the chance to engage, I scattered the enemy. Their infantry column was quickly marched off like an army in flight, and because I could not catch up with it on land I returned to my fleet, made a long, circuitous journey over sea and land with all possible speed,167 and have come to meet this formidable enemy almost at the foot of the Alps.
