Somewhat had she pushed them wavering, but that grim Tisiphone had marked her deceit and swifter than celestial fire was upon her, upbraiding: ‘Why do you oppose enterprises of war, sluggish deity, made over to peace? Begone, shameless! This is our battlefield, our day. Too late you now defend guilty Thebes. Where were you then, when Bacchus stirred up war and his orgies drove armed matrons mad? Or where were you idling while the Martian snake drank the unholy pool, while Cadmus ploughed and the Sphinx fell vanquished, while Oedipus was questioned by his father, 25 while Jocasta came to the marriage chamber by our torch’s light?’ So she urges, and as the other shrinks from her very aspect and draws her own modest countenance far back, presses her with hissing serpents and brandishes her torch. The goddess draws her cloak down over her eyes and flees to complain to the great Thunderer.

Then indeed anger is roused by sharper goads. They want battle; the troops are changed and fain to be spectators. Again they start the cruel atrocity. The impious king makes ready his darts and is first to take the chance of a deadly spear. The weapon makes to drive through the mid orb of the shield but fails to carry its impact through and is overcome by the deep-set gold.
