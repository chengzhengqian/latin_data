First the Tirynthian, crushing the gasping lion and breaking it into its own bones 25 by the harsh friction of his breast. Not without fear did the sons of Inachus see him, though in bronze and their own glory. Father Inachus is next beheld reclining leftward on the mound of his reedy bank and giving free course to the pouring urn. Behind him Io, already prone 26 and her father’s grief, watches Argus, starred with eyes that never set. But Jupiter in kinder mood had raised her up in the Pharian land and already Aurora was worshipping her guest. 27 Then father Tantalus is borne, not he who hangs over the delusive waters or catches at the barren air of retreating branches, but the good Tantalus, dinner guest of the great Thunderer.
