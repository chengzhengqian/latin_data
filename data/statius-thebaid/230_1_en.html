What crime, I pray, do we expiate unawares? Is it that we come here from Inachus’ land, a foreign folk? Every soil is natal to man and it would ill become you, noble one, to distinguish by so cruel and so base 35 a line between peoples who, no matter whence and where, are yours. Stay common to all and bear the arms of either side. Let it be granted to us, I pray, to gasp out our fighting lives in the order of war and return them to the sky. Snatch not breathing bodies to sepulchres so sudden, be not in haste. We shall come by the path all take, the permitted road. Only hear our prayer and stabilize the fickle plain for the Pelasgi; do not forestall the swift Parcae. But you, dear to the gods, whom no hand nor Sidonian swords but great Nature embraced in her opened bosom and came together, as though she were burying you in Cirrha’s cavern as you deserved, in gladness grant, I beg, to know your forms of prayer and commend me to heaven and the truth-telling altars; and teach me what you were about to reveal to the peoples. I shall bring you prophetic rites and as your deity’s ambassador invoke you in Phoebus’ absence.
