But the father with his own hand hurls his sceptre’s pride and the Thunderer’s emblems on the pyre and clips with steel the hair that falls down his back and breast, covering the tiny face of the dead babe with the severed tresses and mingling with parental tears such words as these: ‘Far otherwise, perfidious Jupiter, had I once consecrated these locks to you, due to discharge 17 the vow should you have granted me to offer my son’s youthful cheeks along with them at your temple. But your priest’s words were not ratified, his prayer was denied. Let this shade take them who deserves them more.’ The torch is put, the fire in the lowest branches cries aloud, it is a task to keep back the demented parents.

Danai stand as ordered with levelled shields barring vision afar from unlawful view. The flames are enriched. No ash was ever wealthier than they. 18 Gems crack, silver melts in mass, gold sweats from embroidered fabrics.
