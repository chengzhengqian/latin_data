The Brothers

making about the wedding ceremony. They’re taking the whole day to prepare.

dem

(approaching him) How are you doing, Aeschinus?

aes

Oh hello, my dear father! I didn’t realise you were here.

dem

Your father indeed in heart and in nature, who loves you more than his own eyes. 45 But why don’t you go and fetch your bride?

aes

I want to. But we’re waiting for the piper and the choir to sing the wedding hymn. 46

dem

Say, are you willing to take advice from an old man?

aes

What?

dem

Forget about all that, wedding hymns, congregations, torches, pipers. Get that garden wall knocked down as soon as you can. Fetch her across that way, unite the two families, bring the mother and the whole household over to us.

aes

Good idea! Father, you’re wonderful.

dem

(aside) Splendid! Now I’m called wonderful. My brother’s house will become a thoroughfare, he’ll have crowds of people in his home, it’ll cost him a lot. Why should I care? I’m wonderful and I’m becoming popular. Now let that spendthrift 47 pay out his twenty minas! (to Syrus) Syrus, what are you waiting for? Go and do it!

syr

Do what?

dem

Knock the wall down. (Syrus exits into Micio’s house) (to Geta) You go and bring the women over.

  • 45See note 37.
  • 46At both Athens and Rome the bride was conveyed to the bridegroom’s house by a torchlight procession, accompanied by the singing of the marriage hymn.
  • 47Literally, “that Babylonian”: the Babylonians were proverbial for high spending.
