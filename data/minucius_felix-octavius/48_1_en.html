The Romans, by way of sacrifice, buried alive a Greek man and woman, and a Gaulish man and woman; even to-day a human victim is offered to Jupiter Latiaris,a and, as becomes the son of Saturn, he battens on the blood of a criminal offender. It was he, I believe, who instructed Catilineb5 to cement conspiracy with a covenant of blood, and Bellonac to imbrue her sacrifice with draughts of human blood; and to heal the falling sickness with a man’s blood, a cure worse than the disease.d They6 are on a par with those who eat of wild beasts from the arena, fresh glutted with blood and gorged with the limbs and entrails of men.e For us it is not permissible either to see or to hear of human slaughter; we have such a shrinking from human blood that at our meals we avoid the blood of animals used for food.
