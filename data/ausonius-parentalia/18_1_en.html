For the Spanish province of Tarraco (Tarragona) offered you its corrector’s3 court, and anxiously sought to have you for its patron. You could not be regarded as a son-in-law—you who adored your wife’s mother as your own, and were treated as a son by her. A loyal friend, you lived among others of like heart, and died after a span of eighteen Olympiads.

XXV.—Aemilia Dryadia, my Aunt

To you also, Dryadia my aunt, in mournful strains I, whom your sister bare, almost your son,—do reverence with loving lips. Death, jealous of your happiness, hurried you from your bridal-chamber and the light of the nuptial torches; and funeral ceremonies changed your bridal-couch for a bier. You learned, my aunt, to be a mother to me; therefore now I, a son, offer you this sad token of my love.
