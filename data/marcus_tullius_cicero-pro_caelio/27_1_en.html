For many are the allurements to which nature of her own accord has given birth, such as can lull virtue to rest at times and cause her to relax her vigilance; she has put before the young many slippery paths, on which they can scarcely keep their footing or even enter without falling or stumbling; she has presented them with a variety of delightful things, adapted to charm not only youth, but even the settled strength of maturity. And so, if by chance42 you find anyone who despises the sight of beautiful things, whom neither scent nor touch nor taste seduces, whose ears are deaf to all sweet sounds—such a man I, perhaps, and some few will account heaven’s favourite, but most the object of its wrath.
