Cicero’s moral lecture in defence of Caelius as a steady and industrious young man who knew where to draw the line is continued till the end of the praemunitio in paragraph 50. Many upright and distinguished Romans had their fling in youth. Caelius is young but not vicious: his own speech in his defence, his previous career proclaim his good character. The wild charges against him, for which Clodia is responsible, are without foundation. Liaisons with ladies of easy virtue have long been countenanced. “Who can blame the lovers of a gay widow who behaves so immodestly? Do not imagine, Clodia, that anything I have said referred to you.
