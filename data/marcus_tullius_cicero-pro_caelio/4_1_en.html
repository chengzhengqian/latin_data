Now as to the reproaches cast on his morals, as to all the clamour made by his accusers, not criminal charges but abuse and slander, Marcus Caelius will never feel this so bitterly as to regret that he was born not unhandsome. For such slanders are commonly uttered against all who in their youth have been distinguished by a becoming figure and noble appearance. But abuse is one thing, accusation is another. Accusation requires ground for a charge, to define a fact, to mark a man, to prove by argument, to establish by testimony. The only object of slander, on the other hand, is to insult; if it has a strain of coarseness, it is called abuse; if one of wit, it is called elegance.
