Those who have by now calculated the movements of the stars, and reduced to fixed laws the yearly changes of winter and summer, men to whom no part of the universe is a mystery, are still in doubt as to the Ocean. Does it shut off the earth like a band, or does it go round in a circle of its own, seething into those gulfs that are navigable as into breathing holes serving its great size?1 Beyond it is there fire, which it itself goes to increase, or air? What are you about, fellow-soldiers? Are you letting the conqueror of the human race, great Alexander, enter something whose very nature is still in dispute?—Remember, Alexander: you are leaving your mother2 in a world that is still subdued rather than pacified.
