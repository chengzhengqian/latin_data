These strains, Muses divine, it will be enough for your bard to have sung, as he sits and weaves a basket of slender willow. These strains ye shall make of highest worth in Gallus’ eyes—Gallus, for whom my love grows from hour to hour as fast as the green alder shoots up when spring is young. Let us arise. The shade is oft perilous to the singer—perilous the juniper’s shade, hurtful the shade even to the crops. Get home, my full-fed goats, get home—the Evening Star draws on.
