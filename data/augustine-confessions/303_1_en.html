BOOK XI

2. (2) Ps 45:1With my pen acting like a tongue,3 when am I up to the task of proclaiming all your encouragements and all your terrors, the consolations and directions, by which you convinced me to preach your word and 1 Cor 4:1minister your sacrament4 to your people? Even if I am up to the task of proclaiming it all in a proper order, the drops of time are worth a lot to me.5 For a long time Ps 39:3I have been fired with passion for meditating on your law,6 and confessing to you how much I know of it and what ignorance I still possess,7 the beginning of your enlightening me, and the last traces of my darkness, until my weakness was consumed by strength. I am reluctant that the hours which I find free from such necessities as bodily refreshment, and mental concentration, and the services I owe to others (or I do not owe the service but I render it nonetheless), should trickle away.

(3) Ps 61:1Ps 10:17O Lord my God, listen to my prayer and let your mercy pay heed to my desire, for it is not aflame for my own benefit only, but wants to be at the service of brotherly love.8 You see in my heart that it is so. Let me offer as a sacrifice to you the obedient service of my mind and tongue, so first bestow what I am to offer to you. Ps 85:1I am weak and poor, Rom 10:12you are bounteous to all who call upon you; free from care yourself, you take care of our cares. Ex 6:12; Jer 6:10Vg; Gal 5:6Circumcise my physical lips and my spiritual from all presumption and all deceit.9 Your Scriptures are my pure delights,10 let me neither be deceived in them nor use them to deceive.

  • 3A. neatly reverses the metaphor, “my tongue is the pen of a fast-writing scribe” (lingua mea calamus scribae velociter scribentis).
  • 4Cf. Conf. 4.2.3 on sacramentum, a Latin translation of Greek mysterion (μυστήριον).
  • 5Metaphor from the drips of a water clock (clepsydra); GM parallel “the sands of time” from an hourglass.
  • 6A. alerts the reader to the shift in subject matter onto Scripture.
  • 7Cf. Conf. 10.4370.
  • 8OD points out that 11.2.3–4 is the only part of Conf. set out per cola et commata in the Bibliothèque Augustinienne series (Turnhout, Brepols, 1933–).
  • 9Closest in Scripture to the metaphor here is Dt 10:16.
  • 10castae deliciae verges on the oxymoronic.
