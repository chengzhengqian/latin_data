So I am wavering between the dangers of pleasure and my own experience of its wholesome properties. I am more inclined (obviously without giving an irrevocable opinion) to approve the practice of singing in church. Then the delight experienced by the ears will inspire wavering minds toward feelings of devotion. Yet if I have the experience of being moved more by the singing than by the subject matter, I admit that I am sinning and deserve punishment and then would prefer not to listen to the singer. Just look at the state I am in! Weep with me and weep for me, if you have within you any of the goodness from which actions spring. If you do not have this goodness, these things have no impact on you. But you, O Lord my God, listen! Ps 80:14Look and see and have mercy and heal me; Ps 103:3; Mt 4:23before your eyes I have become a puzzle to myself, and this itself is my weakness.
