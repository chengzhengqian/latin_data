BOOK XIII

(28) All these things are lovely with you as their Maker, and look!—you have made all things, and you are indescribably lovelier. If Adam had not fallen away from you, the bitter brine of the sea would not have poured forth from his womb;72 that is, the human race—so insistently inquisitive, so vehemently proud, so fickle and so lax. 1 Cor 4:1Thus there would be no need for your ministers to perform the actions and words of the sacrament Sg 8:7in many waters both physically and perceptibly. This is what I would take the reptiles and birds to stand for, because even human beings washed and initiated, and having submitted to the physical signs of the sacraments, would be unable to go further unless their souls began to grow in the Spirit on a different level after the words of initiation, Hb 6:1and turned their gaze toward their perfecting.

21. (29) By your Word, then, it was not the depth of the sea but the earth, separated from the bitterness of the waters, that cast forth not a Gn 1:24multiplicity of creatures alive and crawling or flying, but a single living soul. It no longer has the same need of baptism as the pagans do, and as it did itself when it was beneath the waters. For there is no other way in to the kingdom of heaven since the time when Jn 3:5you established baptism as the means of entry; and it seeks no mighty wonders to bring it to faith. 1 Cor 1:22Nor does this living soul fail to believe without seeing signs and marvels; it is already believing earth,73 separate from the waters of the sea, which are bitter faithlessness. 1 Cor 14:22Likewise tongues are for a sign to unbelievers, not believers.74

  • 72GM: “A remarkable example of catachresis”; OD (of GM): “Understatement.” Adam has a figurative womb in that he stands for all humanity. At the time of writing Conf., A. held that Adam and Eve had no sexual liaison or offspring before the “Fall.” See Gn. Litt. 9.4.8, 10.16, but compare also Barr, Garden of Eden, 66–73.
  • 73terra fidelis: a very striking image for the human being (’adam in Hebrew) created from earth.
  • 74“Tongues”: specifically speaking in tongues, or “glossolalia.”
