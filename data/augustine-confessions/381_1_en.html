8. (9) Down fell the angel, down fell18 the human soul: and showed the abyss there for the whole spiritual creation in that deep darkness—had you not said from the beginning, “let there be light,” and there was light, and then every obedient intelligence in your heavenly city cleaved to you, and found rest in your Spirit, who moves unchangingly above all that is subject to change. Otherwise even the actual heaven of heavens would be a dark abyss in itself; Eph 5:8whereas now it is light in the Lord. For in that wretched restlessness that is the downward flux of spirits revealing their own shadows, once they are stripped of your clothing of light, you prove how remarkable and rational a creation you have made; for it finds nothing less than you at all adequate to provide blessed rest, and so not even itself. But you, our God, will lighten our darkness: from you originates our clothing of light19 and Is 58:10even our darkness will be bright as the noonday. Give yourself to me, O my God, give yourself back to me. You see, I do love you, and if it is too little, let my love become stronger. I

  • 16Sursum corda, from the introductory formula from the liturgy of the eucharist; cf. Conf. 12.16.23, n 41.
  • 17Aquas . . . substantia: Vg has the equally puzzling “proud water” (aquam intolerabilem). The Hebrew (zēydoniym) means “raging.”
  • 18defluxit: the same verb is translated as “discharging downward” in Conf. 13.7.8 and “is the downward flux” later in Conf. 13.8.9. No English verb so combines downward direction, fluid composition, and moral negativity.
  • 19Picking up the “clothing of light” from the previous sentence. Cf. Mt 17:2, Rv 7:13, etc.
