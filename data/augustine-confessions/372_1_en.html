30. (41) Let Truth itself bring forth harmony amid such a variety of true opinions; Ps 67:1and let our God have mercy upon us, so that we make proper use of his law, of the objective of that 1 Tm 1:5commandment, namely pure love. Because of this, if anyone asks me which of these did Moses, that servant of yours, intend, these are no true reports of my confessions unless I confess to you, “I do not know.” And yet I do know that they are true opinions. I am setting aside those who are unspiritual, fleshly; on that subject I have said as much as I thought proper,79 and these words of your book, humble in its sublimity, generous in its brevity,80 1 Cor 3:1do not frighten those who are young in the faith81 but promising.82 But as for all of us who, I acknowledge, perceive and speak the truth in scripture, Mt 22:37-39let us love one another, and likewise let us love you, our God,83 the wellspring of truth—if we are thirsty for that truth, and not for vanities. And let us likewise so honor that same servant of yours,84 the good steward of your scriptures, filled with your Spirit, that we believe that when he wrote these things, as you revealed them to him, he intended the meaning which in the text most excels both in the light of truth and in the yield of profit.
