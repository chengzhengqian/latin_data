BOOK IX

13. (34) These days my heart has been healed of that wound (in which it could be held guilty of affection according to the flesh); so now I pour out to you, our God, a very different kind of tears for her, your servant. They flow from a spirit struck by the thought of the dangers threatening every soul that dies in Adam.94 Although she received new life in Christ, and so—even when she was not yet free from the flesh—was determined to live her life in such a way that her faith and conduct would win praise for your name, I do not presume to declare that, from the moment when you remade her in baptism, not a single one of the words which left her lips was against your teaching. Indeed, your Son spoke truly, Mt 5:22“if anyone says to a brother, ‘you fool!’ he will be guilty of hellfire.”95 Woe betide even people who live praiseworthy lives if you set your mercy aside Ps 143:2when scrutinizing them! For you are not zealous in investigating sins, and so we have confidence to hope for a place beside you. After all, if anyone recounts to you their genuine merits, what are they recounting to you but your own gifts? If only humanity would recognize that it is only human! 1 Cor 1:31; 2 Cor 10:17And let the one who boasts, boast in their Lord!

(35) Now, therefore, O God of my heart, my praise and my life, I am briefly setting aside her good deeds (for which I give you joyful thanks), and instead am praying that you will pardon my mother’s sins. Hear me through that medicine96 for our wounds which hung upon a tree and came to Rom 8:34; Gal 3:13sit at your right hand to make intercession for us. I know she practiced mercy, and from her heart forgave

  • 95Gehenna, from the Hebrew for the “valley of the sons of Hinnom” (gey ben-hinnom), was an archetype of evil; there the pre-Israelite inhabitants practiced child sacrifice. The association with burning comes from later tradition. Cf. 2 Kgs 23:10, 2 Chr 28:3, etc.
  • 96Medicina here is a metaphor for Christ; cf. Conf. 9.8.18, n. 59.
