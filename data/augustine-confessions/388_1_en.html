BOOK XIII

In the morning I shall stand before him, Ps 43:5and I shall behold the salvation of my countenance,43 that is, my God; Rom 8:11and he will bring life to our mortal bodies by the Spirit who dwells in us, because in its mercy it moved over the darksome flux which is our inner selves. As a result, 2 Cor 1:22, 5:5on our pilgrim journey we have received a pledge that we are now light, Rom 8:24while as yet we have been saved in hope, and are children of light, and children of the day, 1 Thes 5:5not children of night and shadow, as we were before. You alone distinguish between the children of darkness and us, since the state of human knowledge is still so unreliable; you delve into our hearts, and you call the light “day” and the shadows, “night.” After all, who can judge between us if not you? 1 Cor 4:7What do we possess which is not your gift—Rom 9:21vessels for honorable use from the same lump of clay from which other vessels are also formed, but for ignominious use?

15. (16) Again, who if not you, our God, Gn 1:7made for us an authoritative firmament44 above us in the form of your holy Scripture? Is 34:4For heaven will be rolled up like a book, though now it is Ps 104:2stretched out above us like a skin. Your holy Scripture has a higher authority, since those mortals you used as channels for bestowing it on us have submitted to death. Jn 21:15-17Gn 3:21And you know, Lord, you know45 how you have clothed humanity in skins when—by reason of their sin—they became subject to death.46 So, like a skin, you stretched out the firmament that is your book, your words to us that are in perfect concord, which you have set in authority over us through the ministry of mortal beings.

  • 43vultus: “face” stands for the person, or character.
  • 44Cf. Conf. 12.8.8, n. 14.
  • 45Cf. the iteration of “I do not know” in the next paragraph.
  • 46Thinking figuratively. The sky stands for Scripture; the stretched-out skins (as in the process of tanning animal hides) signify the extension of the Scriptures through time and space. Cf. En. Ps. 103.1.8, “Jeremiah is dead, and all the prophets are dead; and because the words of those dead prophets were not their own but were delivered through them, though they belonged to him who ‘stretched out the heaven like a skin,’ their words remain for generations yet to come.”
