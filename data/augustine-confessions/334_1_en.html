What holds for the whole hymn holds for the individual parts of it too, and the individual syllables, and for the longer performance (of which the hymn is only a part): and in a whole human life, the parts of which consist of all the actions of that human individual; and in the whole age of the children of men,78 the parts of which consist of the lives of every human being.

29. (39) Ps 63:3Since your79 mercy is better than life itself, look and see that my life is a kind of distraction.80 Pss 18:35, 63:8Your right hand has supported me in my Lord who as the Son of Man is 1 Tm 2:5mediator between you who are One, and us who are many, drawn in many directions by many factors, Phil 3:12-14so that through him I may take as my own the one who has made me his own. Leaving behind my former times, I recollect myself and follow the One. I forget what is past, and instead of being distracted81 I reach out, not for what is in the future and so transitory, but for those things which are before me: I press forward, going in the right direction, rather than being distracted, to the prize82 of my highest calling. There I shall hear the voice of praise and shall Ps 27:4contemplate your delight83 which neither comes into being nor passes away.

Now, though, Ps 31:10my years are spent in groaning, and you are my consolation, Lord, for you are my eternal Father. But I became alienated as I entered into time, not knowing the order in which it passes, and my thoughts, the inmost parts of my soul, are ripped apart by turbulent vicissitudes, until I flow back together toward you, purged, and shining with the fire of your love.
