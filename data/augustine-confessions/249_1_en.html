But their love for created things puts them in subjection—and those who are subjects cannot exercise the power of judgment. As for those created things, they only reply to the questioners who also exercise judgment. They do not silence their own voice (that is, their beauty)21 if one person only sees, while another sees and then questions, so that they look one way to one person, but another to the other person. No, their beauty appears in the same way to both, but to the one it is silent, while to the other it speaks. In fact,22 it speaks to all, but only those who compare the voice heard outwardly with the truth within actually understand it.23 For truth says to me, “your God is not earth and heaven nor every physical thing.” Their inherent nature declares this. Do you see?24 That nature has its own proper bulk, its parts are less than its whole.25 Now I tell you, my soul,26 you are better than this, for you animate the physical frame that is your body and provide it with life, which no one physical entity can provide for another. Your God is the life, however, of your life itself.27
