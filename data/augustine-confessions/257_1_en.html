12. (19) In a similar way, the memory includes countless principles and laws about number and dimension, none of them an impression from any bodily sense, because none has either color, or sound, or smell, or taste, or any tactile quality. I have heard the sounds of words that signified these concepts when they were under discussion, but the concepts are one thing, and the words used for them are something else. So the words sounds are different between Greek and Latin, but the concepts are not Greek, or Latin, or any other kind of language. I have seen designs drawn by craftsmen, as fine as can be, like spider silk, but the lines of a diagram43 are one thing: they are not the same as the impressions of lines that my physical sight has shown me. Anyone who recognizes them inwardly, without reflecting on any kind of physical object, truly knows them. I have perceived, too, with all my bodily senses, the numbers we use in calculating: but the ones we use for real calculation are something else,44 not mere impressions of the numbers we perceive by our senses, and therefore they have authentic existence.45 Anyone who does not perceive numbers of this kind may laugh at me for saying these things: and I will pity them while they laugh.
