So what am I to do, Jn 14:6O my true Life, my God? I shall go beyond this power of mine that is called memory, I shall go beyond it so that I can find my way to you, Eccl 11:7my sweetness and light. What are you saying to me? Look, I make my way upward56 through my mind toward you, and you remain high above me: and I shall go beyond that power of mine that is called memory. I want to reach you by whatever pathway allows you to be reached. I want to cleave to you, wherever cleaving to you is open to me. Herds and flocks have memories, or they could never find their way back to the fold or nest, and many other things they are familiar with; and but for memory, no creature would have the capacity to find things familiar. So I shall pass beyond even memory, to reach him who has set me apart from the beasts and made me wiser than the birds of the air. I shall pass even beyond memory, but where may I find you, O true goodness, my sure sweetness, where may I find you? If I find you beyond my memory, I cannot remember you. And how shall I find you, if I have no recollection of you?57
