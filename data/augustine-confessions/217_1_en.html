BOOK IX

(10) I then read, Ps 4:4Vg“Be angry, and sin no more,”35 and how I was moved, O my God! For by now I had learned to be angry with myself over my past deeds, and in such a way that I would not sin any longer in other respects36—and to be angry with good reason, because it was not some other nature, from a species of darkness, which was sinning through me, as is claimed by those people who fail to be angry with themselves, and so store up a hoard of anger for themselves on the Rom 2:5day of anger and of the revelation of your righteous judgment!

Ps 4:6Good things were no longer external to me, nor did I seek them with my physical sight in the light of day. For those who are willing to enjoy what is external easily fade away 2 Cor 4:18and are dispersed into things that are visible and transitory: and their hungry thoughts cause them to lick those empty images.37 If only they would grow weary of such starvation, and say, Ps 4:6“who will show us these good things?”! Then we would say—and they would hear—Ps 4:7“we have been sealed with the light of your countenance, O Lord.” For it is not we who are Jn 1:9the light which enlightens every person; instead you enlighten us, so that Eph 5:8we who were once shadows may become light in you. If only they could see the inner eternity, which I—Ps 34:8because I had tasted of it—was grinding my teeth in frustration that I could not make clear to them!
