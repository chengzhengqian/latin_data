Thus let them be loved in him, and take what souls you can to him with you, and say to them: “Let us love him: he made all these things, and he is not far off.49 He has not made all this to abandon it, but all that is from him is in him. Look, there he is!—wherever truth is distinguished: he is deep within the heart, though the heart has strayed from him. Return to your heart, you transgressorsIs 46:8,50 and cleave to him who made you. Stand with him, and you will stand fast indeed; take your rest in him and you will find peace. Why do you make your way toward what is difficult? Why are you going therecf. Ps 138:7? The good that you love is from him: insofar as it refers to him, it is good and sweet; but whatever it is that comes from him, it will justly become bitter, if he has been abandoned, because then it is loved unjustly.

“To what purpose do you go on and on walking these difficult and wearisome waysWs 5:7? There is no peace where you seek it. Seek what you seek, but it is not there where you seek it. You seek a life of blessing51 in the realm of death: it is not there. How can a life of blessing be there, where there is no life at all?
