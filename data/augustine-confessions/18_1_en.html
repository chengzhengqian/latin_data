Where did this aversion come from, if not the sin and vanity of mortal life, in which I was flesh and spirit, traveling and not returningPs 78:39? Certainly the basic language skills were better, because they were more definite. They gave me the capacity and then the ability that I still possess, of reading any piece of writing I come across, and of writing for myself if I want to compose something. They were better, that is, than the literature in which I was made to master the wanderings of some fellow called Aeneas (meanwhile neglecting my own wanderings),57 and to weep for Dido’s dying, just because she killed herself for love.58 And all the time—pitiable though I was—in such matters I endured my own dying away from you, O God, my life; and I shed not a single tear.

(21) What, after all, is more pitiful than a pitiable person who does not look with pity on their own pitifulness59—and who weeps for the death of Dido, which came about through her love for Aeneas; yet does not weep for their own death, which was coming about because they had no love for you, O God, who are the light of my heart and bread of the mouth of my soul60 deep within, and courage wedded to my mind, and the bosom of my thoughts?
