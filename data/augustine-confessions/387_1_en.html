14. (15) I too say, “Where are you, O my God?” but look—here you are. Jb 32:20Ps 42:4VLPs 42:4In you I recover myself a little, when I pour out my soul above myself 41 in a voice of rejoicing and confession, the sound of someone celebrating a festival. But still my soul is sad, because it relapses and becomes an abyss once more—or rather it feels that it still remains an abyss. My faith, which you kindled in the nighttime to light the way before my feet,42 says to it, Ps 42:5“Why are you sad, O soul, and why do you trouble me? Put your hope in the Lord. His Word is a lantern for your feet. Hope and endure, until the night passes (night is the mother of evildoers), Is 26:20Eph 2:3until the Lord’s anger passes; we are children of that anger, but once we were darkness, and we bear the marks of it on our bodies, Rom 8:10which are dead because of sin, Sg 2:17until the day dawns and the shadows are dispelled. Hope in the Lord; Ps 5:3in the morning I shall stand before him and gaze upon him, I shall make confession to him for ever.”
