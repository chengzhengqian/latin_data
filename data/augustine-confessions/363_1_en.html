He could have meant, “at the actual start of creating”61 when he said, “in the beginning”; he could also have wanted to understand heaven and earth at that point as nature not yet given shape or perfection either spiritual or physical, but both unfinished and still without shape. To be sure, I see that whichever of those views is stated, it could be stated truthfully. But which of the two Moses intended in what he wrote, that I cannot see. All the same, I do not doubt that he saw the truth and expressed it appropriately, whether it was one of the above-mentioned readings, or some other which I have not recorded, which this great man saw in his mind when he pronounced these words.
