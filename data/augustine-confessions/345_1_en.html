(12) Yes indeed, your voice has sounded strongly to my inward ear, saying that not even that created domain of yours22—of which you are the sole delight—is coeternal with you. With unswerving purity it drinks of you23 and nowhere vaunts itself, nowhere: you are always present to it, and it cleaves to you with utter devotion, having nothing in the future to await, and not consigning what it remembers to the past. It is always untouched by change, and never diverges24 into any space of time. O how blessed a creature, if there be such—blessed by its cleaving to your blessedness, blessed by your continual indwelling, and your enlightenment! I can find no better name to call the Ps 115:16heaven of heavens belonging to the Lord than Ps 27:4“your house,” which contemplates your loveliness without the blemish of abandoning it for something else: it is the pure intelligence of holy and spiritual beings, who are the citizens of your city25 that is in heaven (above this visible heaven), harmoniously at one upon a Eph 4:3foundation of peace.26

(13) How can the soul understand, at the end of its long journey, if it still thirsts for you, if its tears have become its bread, while day after day people say to it, Ps 42:2-3“Where is your God?”; if it is seeking one thing from you, and requiring it, Ps 27:4that it may dwell in your house all the days of its life?
