BOOK XIII

By their very death the firm foundation of authority in your scriptures—which they produced—was extended on high, above all that is below. This was because it was neither so sublime nor so all-embracing while they still lived. You had not yet stretched out the heavens like a skin; nor had you yet spread the fame of their deaths far and wide.

(17) Let us behold the heavens, Lord, Ps 8:3the works of your fingers.47 Clear away from our eyes the cloud under which you have hidden them. There is your evidence,Ps 18:8Vg which equips little ones with wisdom. Ps 8:2From the mouths of babes and sucklings, O my God, bring your praise to perfection. We know of no other books so capable of bringing down pride, of bringing down Ps 8:2the enemy, the defender who—by defending his own sins—refuses to give in and be reconciled to you. I do not know, Lord, I do not know any other writings of such purity that they could persuade me into confessing, and make my neck submit to your yoke, and appeal to me to worship you with no thought of reward. Let me understand these scriptures, good Father, grant this to me (even though I am far beneath them), because you have made them strong for the sake of those of us who dwell below.

(18) There are other waters above this firmament,48 I believe; they are immortal and set apart from earthly corruption. Ps 148:1-5Let them praise your name, let them praise you—your hosts of angels above the heavens, who have no need to look up to this firmament, and learn, from reading it, to recognize your Word. Mt 18:10For they always behold your face, and without spoken syllables taking time to pronounce,49 that is where they read what your eternal will decides. There they read, they make their choice, and they love: they read eternally, and what they read never passes away,
