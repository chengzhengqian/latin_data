At that time, he himself was living in perfect chastity in that respect. This was surprising, in particular because he had had sexual experience in early adolescence, but he had not persisted and instead regretted and rejected it, and from then on lived in strictest continence. I was opposing him by citing the examples of those who lived as married men yet venerated wisdom and pleased God, living faithfully and loving their friends. I was so far from their greatness of mind, so bound to that disease of the flesh with all its deadly desirability that I continued to drag my chain: I was afraid to be unfettered. It was as if I had a wound and someone had struck it: I went on rejecting the words of my wise adviser as if his hands were unbinding me. What is more, that serpent began to speak through me to Alypius too, contriving and, by my tongue, spreading sweet snares in his way. These snares were to entangle the feet of that decent man, who up till then had been free.
