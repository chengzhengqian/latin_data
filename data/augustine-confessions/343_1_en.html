9. (9) So when the Spirit, your servant’s17 teacher, records that in the beginning you made heaven and earth, he says nothing about time, mentions nothing about days. The heaven of heaven that you made in the beginning is surely some kind of intellectual creation.18 Although by no means coeternal with you, the Trinity, it nonetheless became a sharer in your eternity; it certainly holds its mutability in check for the sake of the sweet delight of that greatest joy, contemplating you. Without any backsliding from its moment of creation, Ps 73:28by cleaving to you it rises above all the whirling fluctuation of time. Not even that very shapelessness—the earth invisible and unstructured—is reckoned in the counting of days. Where there is no shape, no structure, there nothing comes or goes; and where this does not happen there are undoubtedly no days, and no progression in periods of time.
