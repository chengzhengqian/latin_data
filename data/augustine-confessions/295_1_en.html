(62) Look, O Truth! In you I see that I ought to be affected by praises I receive not for my own sake, but for the sake of my neighbor’s wellbeing.130 But I do not know whether I am or not. In this matter I know myself less well than I know you. I beg you, O my God, make me clear to myself as well (so that I can confess to my brothers, who will pray for me) what is the wound that I have discovered in myself. Once again I must question myself carefully. If it matters to me, when I am praised, that it does my neighbor some good, why does it matters less to me if some other person is criticized unfairly than if I am criticized myself? Why am I consumed more by an insult hurled at myself than one which is just as unfairly cast at someone else? Do I really not know the answer to this? Or is it in fact the case Gal 6:3; 1 Jn 1:8that I deceive myself, and I do not behave honestly in your presence, either in my thinking or speaking? Make this madness far from me, O Lord, to stop my own mouth from being a Ps 141:5sinner’s oil for anointing my head.131
