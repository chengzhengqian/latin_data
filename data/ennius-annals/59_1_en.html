*11 Priscian

The monosyllable Nar similarly has a long a in the genitive Naris and is specific to the river. For if we want to indicate the nose, we pronounce the nominative naris [nostril] like the genitive of Nar. . . . but Nar maintains the long a even in the oblique cases. Ennius in Annals 7:

he placed the blowholes of the Nar1 by its sulfurous waters

​

*12 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“box” means a wood, not a tree, although on Ennius’ example he could also have meant a tree in the neuter. So he [Ennius] says in his seventh book:

and slender cypresses stand with leaves straight and with bitter body the box

  • 1Presumably the Fury’s name, perhaps from paludamentum, “a commander’s cloak” (so Varro, guessing), or from palus, “swamp” (Skutsch, tentatively).
  • 1An Umbrian river (mod. Nera), a tributary of the Tiber known for its sulfurous springs. Ennius probably puns on the ambiguous form naris.
