1 Augustine, City of God

Just as even Cicero himself, at the beginning of the fifth book [of his Republic], speaking not in the words of Scipio or of anyone else but in his own, having previously recalled the verse of the poet Ennius, where he had said

on old-time ways the Roman state stands fast and on its men
