*14 Macrobius, Saturnalia

As for the other words that seemed novel to our friend Avienus, we must defend them with the testimony of the ancients. For Ennius—unless someone thinks he should be denied a place in the more refined elegance of our age—said noctu concubia in these lines:

at the time for bedding down at night [noctu conubia], the Gauls slipped stealthily over the citadel’s highest walls and suddenly bloodied the guard

Here we should note that he said not only noctu concubia but also qua noctu. He did this in the seventh book of the Annals, in the third of which he said the same thing more clearly . . . [3 Ann. F 5].
