ANNALS: BOOK I

*14 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“to swim through the clear summer air”: and elsewhere nare [to swim] for volare [to fly], as in Ennius, in the first book:

she1 floated swiftly through the soft waves of misty darkness

The connotation of the word nevertheless better suits those swimming than those flying.

​

*15 Festus

The ancients used to say sos for eos [them], as Ennius Book 1:

thereupon she stood, illustrious goddess, close by them

​

*16 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “there is a place Greeks call by the name Hesperia”)

Ennius in his first book:

there is a place men used to know as Hesperia1

  • 1The subject is probably Venus, perhaps on her way to counsel Aeneas.
  • 1That is, “The Western Land.”
