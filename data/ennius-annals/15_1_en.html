24 Priscian

Indeed, they used to say Laurentis instead of Laurens. Ennius in the Annals:

which men1 Laurentum’s land once received

25 Atilius Fortunatianus

The longest verse has seventeen syllables . . . the shortest has twelve, as the Ennian line:

to him replied the king of Alba Longa1

​

*26 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “accept and give in exchange this pledge; our hearts are primed for war”)

Ennius in his first book:

accept and give in exchange this pledge and strike a most firm treaty

  • 1The Trojan refugees under Aeneas are the probable reference. Most fragments cited by Priscian without book number belong to Book 1.
  • 1A wholly spondaic line made possible by the archaic genitive -āī. olli (classical illi) is also archaic.
