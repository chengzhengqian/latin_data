6 Varro, On the Latin Language

Pacuvius therefore is right when he says that “the ether adds breath,”1 and Ennius:

and earth, which gave the body, herself takes it back and sustains not the slightest loss

  • 1The meaning is problematic. Warmington, following Scaliger, prints destituunt rivos, “they leave the streams,” a lectio facilior. “Water” was the likely subject of both verbs.
  • 1Non., p. 75.8 M. = 105 L. quotes Pacuvius’ complete thought: Mater est terra: ea parit corpus, animam aether adiugat “Earth is the mother. She gives birth to the body; the ether adds breath” (Trag. 94 R.2–3). Varro converts to indirect statement what his subsequent citations of these lines show was in Ennius a direct statement: editors thus restore with confidence the direct form reproduced here.
