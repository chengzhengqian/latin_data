10 Orosius, Histories (on the battle of Heraclea)

But Pyrrhus bore witness before his gods and before men to the horrific slaughter he sustained in this battle, placing an inscription in the temple of Jupiter at Tarentum, in which he wrote these lines:1

men who previously had been unvanquished, best father of Olympus, those I vanquished by force in battle and was by the same men vanquished

and when he was rebuked by his allies, asking why he, who had vanquished, said he had been vanquished, he is said to have replied, “Well, if I were victorious a second time in that way, I would return to Epirus with not a single soldier.”
