The Trojan origins of Rome, an idea originally popularized by Greek historians of the West, first gained currency among Romans through versions of Aeneas’ story in the history of Fabius Pictor (F 3 FRHist) and the epic of Gnaeus Naevius (BP, F 5–25 Strzelecki), but Ennius’ account became the canonical one in the Republic. For the complexities of the developing foundation legend, see Gruen 1992, 6–51.
