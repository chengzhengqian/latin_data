ANNALS: BOOK XVI

​

*14 Macrobius, Saturnalia

And his expression “nor the vault of heaven alight with starry brilliance” [Verg. Aen. 3.585–86], Ennius previously said in his sixteenth [book]:

meanwhile the torch1 sets and a reddening brilliance slowly covers the Ocean

​

*15 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “then a clammy sweat trickled over his entire body”)

Ennius in his sixteenth [book]:

then in his fear sweat trickles from all his body

​

*16 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

favens [favoring] for volens [wishing] . . . for the ancients too used favere for velle. Ennius:

the matrons crowd the walls, wishing to observe

​

*17 Festus

obstipum [lit., “bent”]: obliquum [slanting]. Ennius, Book 16:

with slanting [i.e., steep] mountains standing in the way, from which night rises

  • 1That is, the sun. Cf. Medea (exul), Trag. F 98.
