15 Paul the Deacon, Epitome of Festus

there the chief meddix is captured, the other killed

​

16 Gellius, Attic Nights

Marcus Varro’s words in Book 5 of his Instructions are these: “It is one thing to be elected praetor quarto [fourth] and another to be praetor quartum [for the fourth time], because quarto indicates the order with three elected before, quartum indicates time and three previous elections. Therefore what Ennius wrote was correct:

Quintus his father is elected consul for the fourth time

and Pompey was timid when, to avoid writing either ‘consul for the third time’ or ‘third’ on his theater, he did not write the final letters.”1
