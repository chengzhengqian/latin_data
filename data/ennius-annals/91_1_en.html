ANNALS: BOOK XI

​

*3 Priscian

sono [I sound] yields sonas and sonis [i.e., either first or third conj.]. Ennius in Annals 11:

then the small shields resound and the sword’s edge whizzes

​

*4 Priscian

From strido [I shriek], too, some conjugated stridui [i.e., second conj. perf.], others stridi [i.e., third conj. perf.]. Ennius in Annals 11:

and the spear, sent through his chest, shrieked as it passed through

​

*5 Festus

The ancients used to say sos for eos [them], as Ennius Book 1 [F 15] . . . , Book 3 [F 4] . . . , Book 7 [F 16] . . . , and Book 11:

they claim [compare?] the Greeks, Grecians are men accustomed to call them1

  • 1Roman poetry prefers the form Graius to the prosaic Graecus: thus Vahlen’s transposition of Graios, Graecos. The topic is widely assumed to be Roman kinship with the Greeks and the speaker to be Flamininus at the Isthmian Games (cf. Inc. Ann. F 17), but the sense is in fact uncertain.
