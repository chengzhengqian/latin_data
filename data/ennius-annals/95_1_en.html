BOOK XIII

192/1 BC, but its content is otherwise uncertain. The reference to Xerxes in F 1 is often taken to reflect Roman anxiety in 192 BC over a possible invasion across the Hellespont (cf. Liv. 35.23, 41). The contexts suggested for other fragments are even more problematic.

​

1 Varro, On the Latin Language

“as it were the Hellespont and its barriers,” because Xerxes once barricaded this place, for as Ennius says:

and he stretched a bridge over the deep Hellespont

​

2 Servius Danielis, Commentary on Virgil

“and salty sweat ran down its limbs”: “salty sweat” is said to have been an expression of the divinity’s agitation. Probus,1 admittedly, disliked “salty sweat” and thought its use superfluous. Ennius, however, said this about marshes:

saltymarshes

  • 1For the great grammarian M. Valerius Probus, much respected by Servius (and Donatus before him), see Kaster 1995, 242–50.
