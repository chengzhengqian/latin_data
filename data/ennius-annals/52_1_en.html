15 Cicero, On Old Age

There was also added to the old age of Appius Claudius the fact that he was blind. Nevertheless, when opinion in the Senate was inclining toward peace with Pyrrhus and making a treaty, he did not hesitate to say what Ennius renders in verse:

down what road [?] have your senses, which before used to stand upright, wandered off senseless

and the rest most solemnly, for you know the poem. And anyway, the speech of Appius himself survives.
