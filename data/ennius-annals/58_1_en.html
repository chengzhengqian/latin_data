*7 Priscian

verbs ending in -geo preceded by l or r turn -geo to -si to form the perfect, as indulgeo indulsi, fulgeo fulsi, algeo alsi, urgeo ursi . . . So Ennius in Book 7 of the Annals:

the sea had driven the ship, buffeted by the waves, to this place

*8–91 Festus

Ennius uses tonsa for “oar” because it is as if cut by a knife, as he says in Book 7:

then lean back and drive your chests from the oars

similarly:

they reach back, and then pull the oars to their chests
