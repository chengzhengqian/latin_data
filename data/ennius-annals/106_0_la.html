FRL I: ENNIUS

BOOK XVI

What Pliny (t 1) knew about this book almost certainly derived from Ennius’ own writing; F 1 to 5 are thus plausibly imagined as part of a proem in which the aged poet announced the resumption of his labors. The Caecilii said to have inspired him, however, are unknown, and confusion somewhere along the line of transmission with the exploits of the brothers Titus and Gaius Aelius recorded by Livy (41.1.7, 3.6–8, 4.3) and/or the Aelius (codd. Caelius) of 15 Ann. F 3 is quite possible. F 10 and F 15 may derive from a single combat described in Homeric terms, but the larger military context for it is unknown.
