Verrius thinks sas means eas [“them,” fem.], citing Ennius as a witness, who says in Book 1 [F 49] “maidens . . . them,” although it seems rather to mean “their own.” So in the same poet’s Book 7, it must be admitted that eam is meant when he says:

nor did anyone else see the wisdom,

which is what learning is called in Latin

which is called knowledge, in his dreams before he began to acquire it
