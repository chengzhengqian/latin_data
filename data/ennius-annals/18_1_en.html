30 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“they sat together in his double-doored hall”: He said “double-doored” literally, since heaven lies open from rising to setting. It is, moreover, Ennian language, taken from doors that are opened from both sides. Thus even now we understand

double-doored

to mean simply “open.”1

​

31 Servius, Commentary on Virgil

“we follow you, holy divinity, whichever one you are”: we must either punctuate after “holy,” or he said “holy divinity,” following Ennius:1

Juno, Saturn’s daughter, holy goddess, replied

  • 1The doors of the Curia remained open during meetings of the Senate. Divine assemblies in Lucilius, Cicero, and Virgil all echo Roman senatorial procedure, a practice that may have originated with Ennius.
  • 1Servius is unsure whether Virgil’s deorum (of gods) is to be understood with the vocative “holy one” or with quisquis, “whichever one.”
