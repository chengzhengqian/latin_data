ANNALS: BOOK I

F 50–51: A Prayer to Mars and Quirinus

​

*50 Gellius, Attic Nights

[On the scansion of Nerio] Ennius, moreover, in his first book of Annals, in this verse:

<you, Mars, you,> Nerio, consort of Mars, and Herie1

if he has preserved the meter, which he is not always accustomed to do, has lengthened the first syllable and shortened the third.

​

*51 Nonius

Hora: the goddess of youth. Ennius, Book 1 of the Annals:

<and you,> Father Quirinus, I beseech, and Hora, consort of Quirinus

​

*52 Charisius

concorditer: Ennius, Book 1 of the Annals:

see that both of you join eternal days harmoniously together

  • 1Nerio and Herie are in origin attributes rather than proper names, “valor” and “will,” respectively. So too Hora in F 51, from the same (Oscan) root as Herie. Gellius identifies Nerio as Sabine. They are the objects here of the speaker’s prayer.
