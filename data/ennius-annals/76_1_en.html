5 Gellius, Attic Nights

Virgil too disparages tunics of this type [long-sleeved] as effeminate and shameful: “and sleeves on the tunic,” he says, “. . .” [Aen. 9.616]. [7] Ennius also seems to have spoken of the Carthaginians’

tunic-clad youth
