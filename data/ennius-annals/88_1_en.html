ANNALS: BOOK X

​

*13 Diomedes

For hortatur [urges], which we commonly say, some old writers said horitur, as Ennius in Book sixteen of the Annals . . . [F 22]. Likewise in Book ten:

. . . keeps cheering them on . . . the commander1

as a kind of iterative form.

​

*14 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “he spins heaven’s vault, studded with blazing stars, on his shoulder”)

Ennius in Book One [F 22] . . . and in Book Three [F 7], and in Book Ten:

then night continued on, studded with blazing stars

​

*15 Nonius

passum: “stretched out,” “spreading open,” from which we also say passus [steps] because the feet spread apart with each step. Ennius in Book 10 of the Annals:

sick at heart, with hair spread in disarray

​

*16 Diomedes

Among the ancients we even find the letter n added, as pinso, which is “I beat,” and pinsit following the third conjugation, as Ennius in Book 10 of the Annals:

they beat the ground with their knees

  • 1induperator [commander] normally ends a verse in Ennius, but here could possibly open the following one.
