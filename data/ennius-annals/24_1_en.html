F 44–46: A Quarrel Leads to the Death of Remus (cf. Inc. Ann. F 7)

​

*44 Festus

Many old writers attest that the ancients used quamde for quam [than], including Ennius in his first book:

Jupiter1 that I am supported by my wall more than by my arm

​

*45 Nonius

torviter [fiercely]: Pomponius . . . Ennius Book 1:

but this one, whom now you have so fiercely berated

​

*46 Macrobius, Saturnalia (on Virgil, “well then, you will pay me back with your life’s blood”)

Ennius in his first book:

no living man at all will do this with impunity, nor will you, for you will pay me the penalty with your life’s blood

  • 1“Jupiter” is probably the subject of a preceding verb, e.g., “ordains” or “forbids.” The uncertain context makes it impossible to know whether the speaker is Romulus or Remus.
