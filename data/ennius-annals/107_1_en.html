FRAGMENTS

*1 Nonius

pigret [to be reluctant]. Ennius in Book 16:

he [?] is reluctant to take up the task in advanced age

​

*2 Charisius

Caecilius used hebem [weak] in Hypobolimaeus [The Substitute Son],1 “The facts will quickly render him weak” [Com. 81 R.2–3]. Ennius, Book 16, where Flavius Caper2 comments “he used it not as an adjective but as a noun”:

weak

​

*3 Festus

That quippe [surely] means quidni [lit., “why not?”] is attested by Ennius, Book 11 [F 1] . . . and 16:

surely it is not enough that the ancient wars of men be told
