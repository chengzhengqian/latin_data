17 Varro, On the Latin Language

In Ennius:

the envoy returns without a peace and refers the matter to the king

orator [envoy] is derived from “oration,” for one who spoke publicly before the one to whom he was sent is called an “orator” because of his oration. When a quite serious matter required a speech, those most particularly were selected, who were able to plead the case most effectively.
