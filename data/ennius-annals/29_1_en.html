ANNALS: BOOK II

BOOK II

Tullus’ destruction of Alba Longa as described in this book may be echoed by Virgil in Aeneid 2: cf. Servius ad 2.313, 486, whose comment (“de Albano excidio . . . translatus”) led Vahlen to place Inc. Ann. F 9 here as well.

2 Varro, On the Latin Language

In Ennius:

he established the sacrificial tables and likewise the sacred shields1

the term is from ambecisus [an incision on both sides], because those arms were cut in at both edges like those of Thracians. [44]
