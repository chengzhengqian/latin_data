Giving the king the forthright answer that they would accept no fresh alliance without the authorization of the Roman generals, they shut their gates and placed armed guards on the walls. Very opportunely, Gnaeus Octavius arrived in Leucas to bolster the resolution of the Acarnanians, filling the allies with hopes that Manius Acilius had already crossed the sea with his legions and that there was a Roman camp established in Thessaly. (Octavius had been sent by Quinctius and had received a body of troops and a few ships from Aulus Postumius, who had been put in charge of Cephallenia60 by the legate Atilius.) Since this rumor was made plausible by the fact that it was now a seasonable time of year for sailing, the king installed a garrison at Medion and certain other towns of Acarnania, left Thyrreum and returned to Chalcis by way of the cities of Aetolia and Phocis.

13. Marcus Baebius and King Philip had already met in the wintertime in the land of the Dassareti (at the time when they had sent Appius Claudius into Thessaly to raise the siege of Larisa)61 but because it was still too early in the year for military operations they had returned to their winter quarters. Now, at the beginning of spring, they joined forces and came down into Thessaly (at that point Antiochus was in Acarnania). On their arrival, Philip assaulted Malloea in Perrhaebia and Baebius attacked Phacium. Capturing Phacium with his first onslaught, Baebius just as quickly took Phaustus.
