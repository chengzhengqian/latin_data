Letter Fragments

vi. to a. Hirtius

1. M. Tullius to Hirtius, Book II: If I made something of a slip in that matter, you will defend my venial error.43

2. M. Tullius to Hirtius, Book V: And since, as things are now, I have nothing to share with my country . . .44

3. M. Tullius to Hirtius, Book VII: For since nobility is nothing but worth recognized, who would feel antiquity of race to be lacking in one whom he saw aging as to glory?45
