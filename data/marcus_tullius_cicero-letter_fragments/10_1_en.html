7. Cicero himself writes to Brutus as follows: Afraid perhaps that I might transfer something from there49 to my Cato, though the theme was quite different.

8 For Cicero rightly writes in a letter to Brutus in these very words: Eloquence which does not startle I don’t consider eloquence.50

10 Cicero writes to Brutus: I have duped the public and am regarded as an orator.

11 Cicero writes to Brutus: Since I have been reconciled to Appius Claudius and reconciled with Cn. Pompeius as intermediary—since therefore I have been reconciled . . .51

12. Cicero to Brutus: Our talk was naturally all about you. What better subject? Then Flavius said ‘the couriers leave tomorrow,’ and I scribbled this on the spot at dinner.52
