Then night added to all their uncertainties; so they cast anchor, that the ships might not collide or drift onto the shore. When day dawned the same wind sprang up and by dispelling the fog revealed the whole African coast. Scipio inquired what the nearest promontory was, and upon being told it was called Cape of the Fair God,2 he said “A welcome omen! steer your ships this way!” There the fleet came into port and all the troops were disembarked.3

That the passage was successful and free from alarm and disorder I have accepted on the authority of many Greek and Latin writers. Coelius4 alone describes all the terrors of weather and waves—everything short of saying that the ships were overwhelmed by the seas.
