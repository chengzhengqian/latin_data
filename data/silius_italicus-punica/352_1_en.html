Next Paulus came, hard to recognize in the dim light, and drank of the blood, and spoke thus: “Bright star of Italy, whose martial feats, too great by far for a single arm, these eyes beheld,a who forces you to descend into darkness and to visit this realm where those who enter must dwell for ever?” Scipio spoke thus in answer: “O mighty captain, how long did all Rome mourn your death! How nearly you carried down the Roman city in your fall to Stygian darkness! Also the Carthaginian, our foe, built a tomb for your dead body and sought to gain glory by honouring you.”b While Paulus shed tears to hear of his burial by the enemy, Flaminius came in sight, and Gracchus, and the sad face of Servilius who fell at Cannae. Scipio was eager to call them by name and converse with them; but his strong desire to look on the heroes of the past carried him away.

He saw Brutus who gained eternal fame by the merciless axe,c and then Camillus,d peer of the gods in glory, and Curiuse next who never welcomed gold. The Sibyl revealed to him their faces and names as they came up. “This blind manf drove Pyrrhus from his door and spurned the king’s dishonest overtures for peace; that otherg withstood the king who attacked the Tiber banks, and, when the bridge was broken down behind him, kept out the returning tyrants by his valour, single-handed.
