Punica, VIII

The Carthaginian copies us and carries the consul’s axes, and his lictors bear blood-stained rods. The triumphal procession of the Roman passes from Rome to Libya. And, O grief!—do the gods force us to witness this also?—victorious Carthage measures the downfall of Rome by all the heap of gold that was torn from the left hands of the slain.”a

  • aTo prove the greatness of his victory, Hannibal sent home the gold rings taken from the corpses of the Roman nobles: these were poured out in the Carthaginian senate, and filled three peck-measures, according to some authorities (Livy xxiii. 12. 1). See xi. 532 foll.
