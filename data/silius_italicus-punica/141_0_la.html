Silius Italicus

Liber Sextus

argument

Scenes on thefield of the lost battle. Flight of the Romans (1–61). Serranus, a son of the famous Regulus, is one of the fugitives: he reaches the dwelling of Marus, who had been his fathers squire in Africa: Marus dresses his wounds (62–100), and tells the story of Regulus as conqueror and as captive (101–551). Mourning and consternation at Rome after the defeat. Serranus returns to his mother, Marcia
