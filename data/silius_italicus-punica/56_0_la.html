Silius Italicus

Liber Tertius

argument

After the taking of Saguntum, Bostar is sent to Africa to consult Jupiter Ammon (1–13). Hannibal goes to Gades, where he is shown the famous temple of Hercules and marvels at the tides of the Atlantic (14–60). He sends his wife, Himilce, and his infant son to Carthage (61–157). He dreams of the coming campaign (158–213). He sets off: a catalogue of his forces (214–405). He crosses the Pyrenees
