Silius Italicus

Liber Septimus

argument

Fabius determines to take no risks in the field (1–19). Cilnius, one of his prisoners, informs Hannibal concerning the family history and character of Fabius (20–73). Religious observances at Rome (74–89). Fabius restores discipline in the army. Hannibal cannot tempt him to fight (90–122). Hannibal moves to Apulia and tries to provoke Fabius by various devices. He returns to Campania and ravages the Falernian country (123–161). The visit of Bacchus to the aged peasant, Falernus (162–211). Fabius explains his policy of inaction to his discontented soldiers (212–259). A trick of Hannibal’s, to make the Dictator more unpopular
