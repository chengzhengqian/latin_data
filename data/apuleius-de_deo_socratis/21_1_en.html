Because of their excessive reverence for omens, they let the words of others guide them rather than their own intuition, and they creep down alleyways picking up advice from other people’s remarks, thinking with their ears, so to speak, not with their brains.

But however that may be, those who prophesy from omens certainly do hear a voice perceived through the ears, and have no doubt that it issues from a human mouth. 2Socrates, though, did not say that it was “a voice” that had come to him but “a certain voice,”41 from which qualification one can readily see that he did not mean an ordinary or human voice. Had that had been so, it would have been pointless to say “a certain,” but rather “a voice” or at any rate “someone’s voice,” as the prostitute says in Terence,

I thought I just heard the soldier’s voice.42
