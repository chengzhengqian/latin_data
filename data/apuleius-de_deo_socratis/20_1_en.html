But as to why the demon sometimes moved to check some of Socrates’ undertakings, but never to encourage them, I have in a way already given the reason. 2As a perfect human being, ready of his own accord to perform every duty appropriate for him, Socrates never needed prompting, 3and yet did sometimes did need restraining when he happened to be embarked on a path across some concealed danger, so that after consultation he might plan ahead, and briefly abandon his intention, either to resume it later at less risk or to go about it some other way.
