Julius Obsequens

Consulship of Gnaeus Cornelius and Quintus b.c. 176Petillius

9. After the consuls had offered sacrifice, the liver melted away. Cornelius suffered a stroke on his way back from the Alban Mount and died at the spa of Cumae, while Petillius was killed in battle against the Ligurians. (XLI. xiv. 7; xv. 1–4; xvi. 3–4 [other prodigies, xvi. 6]; xviii. 8–11, 14.)

Consulship of Marcus Lepidus and Quintus b.c. 175Mucius

10. During a serious plague of men and cattle, corpses lay exposed because Libitina was overwhelmed, but no vulture appeared. The Celtiberians were crushed. (XLI. xxi. 5–7; xxvi.1)

Consulship of Quintus Aelius Paetus and b.c. 167Marcus Junius

11. At Rome several places, both consecrated and common, were struck by lightning. At Anagnia there was a shower of earth. At Lanuvium a blazing meteor was seen in the sky. At Calatia on land owned by the state blood trickled for three days and two nights. King Gentius of Illyricum and King Perseus of Macedonia were conquered. (XLV. xvi. 5–7.)
