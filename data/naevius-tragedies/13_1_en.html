Tragedies

54

The palace of Lycurgus is set on fire:

Nonius: ‘Fervit.’ From this verb comes ‘fervĕre’ with the vowel shortened . . .—

Lycurgus

our transoms glowing far and wide.

55–6

Liber, a victorious, calls for Lycurgus:

Nonius: ‘Cette’ (hither with! bring hither!) means ‘tell’ or ‘give’ . . .—

Liber

Then bring me hither The King Lycurgus, son of his father Dryas.

57

Lycurgus’ bodyguard must submit:

Festus: ‘Obstinato,’ steadfast, persevering . . .—

All you who stubbornly stand by him.

58

Unplaced fragments:

Nonius: ‘Amnem’ . . . in the feminine . . .—

as a river swift Goes hurrying on, yet twists and turns withal.

59
