Equos Troianus

We have apparently one fragment from a Trojan Horse of Naevius, one from a Trojan Horse of Livius, and two from a Trojan Horse of which the author is not named (see pp. 10–11). There may be a confusion of names, and all the frs. may belong to one play by Livius or Naevius. The play dealt with the famous trick played by the Greeks on the Trojans.
