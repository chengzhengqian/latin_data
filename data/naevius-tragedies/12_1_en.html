Tragedies

48

Dispute between Lycurgus and Liber:

Nonius: ‘Contendere’ means to compare. . .

Liber

Beware, I pray you, setting up your wrath With Liber’s wrath, in opposition.

49

Nonius: ‘Acrimonia’ is a liveliness of the mind . . .—

Lycurgus Let him not a rouse my savage temper’s wrath, And my soul’s hearty hate. 50–51

Nonius: ‘Iniurie.’ A term used for ‘iniuriose’ . . .—

Liber

. . . The gods do hate unrighteous mortals.

Lycurgus

Do we—he or I—unrighteously?

52–3

Liber prays that he may do harm to Lycurgus:

Nonius: ‘Fimbriae’ (fringe). A term for the extremity of anything. Naevius b in Lycurgus* * * ‘Flora.’ Naevius in Lycurgus—

Liber . . . that I may see by Vulcan’s agency These buildings flaring in a flower of flame.
  • aBut ne may here be positive—‘Yes, he shall feel . . .’ And by acrimoniam Naevius meant something much stronger than Nonius implies.
  • bMercier was doubtless right in thinking that a quotation from Naevius illustrating the use of fimbriae has fallen out with a new lemma flora.
