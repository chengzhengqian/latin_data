erg

(loudly enough for Hegio to hear) I’m sad that this unfortunate129 old man has to do prison business because of his son’s unfortunate circumstances. (aside) But if the son can be got back here in any way, I could even bear it if the father were doing executions.

heg

(looking around) Who’s speaking here?

erg

(comes to Hegio and starts crying) I, a wretched man who is growing weary, thin, old, and weak because of my grief for you. (aside) I’m skin and bones from miserable . . .135 thinness. (to Hegio) And nothing I eat at home ever pleases me. (aside) But the tiniest morsel I taste outside makes me happy.
