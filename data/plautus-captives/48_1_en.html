erg

He’s left and he’s put me in charge of the food department. Immortal gods, how I will chop the necks off the backs in a moment! What havoc will fall on the ham, what loss on the lard, what utter consumption on the udder, what misfortune on the meat, what sleepiness on the905 slaughterers and pork-butchers! Well, if I were to mention the other things which help toward the stomach’s sustenance it would just cause delay. Now I’ll go in order to pass judgment on the lard by virtue of my office and to bring help to the ham hanging unsentenced.
