Once I was just as free as your son; the enemy’s armed force has taken freedom away from me just as it did from him; and he’s a slave at our place just as I’m now a slave at yours. There really is a god who hears and sees what we are doing. He will look after him there the way you treat me here. The man deserving well he will reward well, the315 man deserving badly he will treat in the same way. 13 My father’s missing me just as you’re missing your son.

I do admit that my father has great wealth at home and that I come from a great family. But I entreat you, Hegio, do not let my riches make you greedier; otherwise it320 might seem more appropriate to my father that even though I’m his only son, I should be a well-fed slave at your place, nourished and clothed at your expense, rather than live as a beggar back there, where it would be most disgraceful.
