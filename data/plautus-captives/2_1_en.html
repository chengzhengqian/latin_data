The Captives

Characters
  • Ergasilus a hanger-on; Philopolemus’ friend
  • Hegio an old man; a very dignified Aetolian
  • Slave-Overseer works for Hegio
  • Tyndarus a slave, prisoner of war; serves Philocrates, but turns out to be Hegio’s son
  • Philocrates a young prisoner of war; from Elis
  • Aristophontes a young man; Philocrates’ friend from Elis
  • Slave-Boy works in the kitchen
  • Philopolemus a young man; Hegio’s other son, prisoner of war in Elis
  • Stalagmus a slave; of bad character
Staging

We are in a city in Aetolia. The stage represents a street in it. On the street there is Hegio’s house. To the left, the street leads to the harbor; to the right, to the city center. The house of Hegio’s brother is off-stage, in the direction of the city center.
