The consul was attacked by a number of the senators in speeches no less severe than those delivered in his absence, and returned to his province without obtaining either petition.1

The other consul, Postumius, spent the summer surveying the public lands, and without seeing his province at all returned to Rome to hold the elections. He announced the choice of Gaius Popilius Laenas and Publius Aelius Ligur.2 Next the praetors were chosen: Gaius Licinius Crassus, Marcus Junius Pennus, Spurius Lucretius, Spurius Cluvius, Gnaeus Sicinius, Gaius Memmius (for the second time).3

X. In that year the lustrum was closed; the censors were Quintus Fulvius Flaccus and Aulus Postumius
