“Many Men” and “Many Mortals”

After 143 a.d.

Inasmuch as Quadrigarius2 uses the expression “with many mortals,” what and how much difference it would make if he had said “with many men.”

The words from the thirteenth book of the Annals of Claudius Quadrigarius are:

The assembly being dismissed, Metellus came into the Capitol with many mortals: on his return home from there he was escorted by the whole city.

  • 1Horn. II. xxiii. 382.
  • 2A historian at the beginning of the first century b.c. who wrote a history of Rome from its capture by the Gauls.
