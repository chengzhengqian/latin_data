On Rhetoricians

Fragments preserved in Jerome’s Chronicle

(31). Lucius Cestius Pius,19 〈rhetorician from Smyrna, taught in Latin at Rome [13 b.c.]〉.

(32). Marcus Porcius Latro,20 〈Latin declaimer, commits suicide, unable to endure a double quartan fever [4 b.c.]〉.

(33). Quintus Curtius Rufus21...

(36). Lucius Statius Ursulus,22 〈of Toulouse, acquires fame from teaching rhetoric in Gaul [a.d. 56]〉.

(37). Publius Clodius Quirinalis,22 〈rhetorician from Arelate, teaches with distinction at Rome [a.d. 44]〉.

(38). Marcus Antonius Liberalis,22 〈Latin rhetorician, engages in bitter feuding with Palaemon [a.d. 48]〉.

(39). Sextus Iulius Gabinianus,24 〈a celebrated rhetorician, taught in Gaul [a.d. 76]〉.

(40). Marcus Fabius Quintilianus25 〈is brought to Rome by Galba [a.d. 68] 〉... 〈Quintilian, from Calagurris in Spain, the first to receive a professorial appointment at Rome with a salary from the treasury, enjoyed fame [a.d. 88]〉.
