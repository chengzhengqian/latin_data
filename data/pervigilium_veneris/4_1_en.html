The Creatress herself in hidden might from within sways flesh and spirit with her enkindling life; and through sky, through land, and through the sea beneath she poured a pervasive tide of herself along the passage of the seed and bade the universe know the ways of generation.

Let him love tomorrow who has never loved, and let him who has tomorrow love!

69 It is Venus who engrafted her Trojan offspring on the Latins; Venus who gave the maid of Laurentuma to her sonb for bride, and gives thereafter to Mars from Vesta’s temple a chaste virginc; Venus who brought about the marriage of the sons of Romulus with the Sabine women, wherefrom she might produce the Ramnes and Quiritesd and, for the glory of the future race of Romulus, the Caesars, father and nephew.e

Let him love tomorrow who has never loved, and let him who has tomorrow love!

76 The country quickens with love’s delight, the country feels Venus’ touch; Love himself, the child of Dione, is deemed country-born. Him, when the fields were in travail, she herself took up into her bosom, herself nursed with the dainty kisses of flowers.

Let him love tomorrow who has never loved, and let him who has tomorrow love!

  • bAeneas, Venus’ son by Anchises.
  • cRhea Silvia.
  • dThe poet specifies two (rather than the traditional three) tribes of ancient Rome to emphasize the union of the Romans (Ramnes) with the Sabines (Quirites).
  • eJulius Caesar and his nephew Augustus.
  • aLavinia, who was betrothed to Turnus of Laurentum before becoming the wife of Aeneas.
