BOOK XXXV

Nabis, tyrant of the Lacedaemonians, abandoned the Roman people, incited to do so by the Aetolians, who were urging both Philip and Antiochus to open hostilities against the Roman people, but he was killed by the Aetolians in the war fought against Philolopoemen, praetor of the Achaeans. The Aetolians also abandoned their alliance with the Roman people. When, forming a pact with the Aetolians, Antiochus king of Syria launched a war against Greece, he seized numerous cities, including Chalcis and all of Euboea.
