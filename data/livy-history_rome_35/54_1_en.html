With their help he had primed the supporters of Euthymidas to betray the city to him. Euthymidas went from Athens (which he had chosen as his place of residence) first to Thebes and then to Salganeus, while Herodorus proceeded to Thronium.125 Not far from there, in the Malian Gulf, Thoas had 2,000 infantry, 200 cavalry and about 30 light cargo vessels. Herodorus was instructed to take the ships together with 600 infantry over to the island of Atalante so that he could cross from there to Chalcis when he found out that the land forces were approaching Aulis and Euripus.126 Thoas himself proceeded to take the rest of the troops as quickly as he could to Chalcis, marching mostly at night.
