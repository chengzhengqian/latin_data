Chapter VI

on the water screw

1. There is further an application of the screw which draws a large amount of water, but does not raise it as high as the wheel. The contrivance1 is as follows. A beam is taken as many inches thick as it is feet long. This is rounded to an exact circle. At the ends the circumference is to be divided by a compass into quadrants and eighths and so to eight parts; and the diagonals are to be so drawn that when the beam is horizontal the lines at either end correspond exactly. The whole length is to be marked off into spaces equal to one-eighth of the circumference. Again, the beam is to be laid level and lines are to be drawn from one end to the other guided by a level. Thus there will be equal divisions both round the beam and along it. Where the longitudinal lines are drawn, they will intersect the cross-lines, and these intersections are to be marked by points.
