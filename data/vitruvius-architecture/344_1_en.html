Again, when the ram tortoise came to demolish the wall, they let down a rope and caught the head of the ram. Then they wound the rope round a drum, using a windlass, and by keeping the ram raised, they prevented it from touching the wall. In the end they demolished the whole engine with fiery missiles1 and blows from the balista. Thus these victorious cities were liberated not by military engines, but, in face of their employment, by the skill of the architects.

In this book I have fully set forth the mechanical methods which I could furnish, and which I thought most useful in times of peace and war. Now in the previous nine books I have dealt with the other several topics and their subdivisions, so that the whole work, in the ten books, describes every department of architecture.
