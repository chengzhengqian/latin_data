For unless the soil were unlike and disparate in its juices, not only in Syria and Arabia would there be perfumes in the reeds, rushes, and all herbs, nor incense-bearing trees; nor would they yield pepper berries, nor would there grow flakes of myrrh; nor in Cyrene would the assafetida grow in the stalks of silphium. But in every land and region everything would be produced of the same kind. On the other hand, these varieties are produced in regions and localities by the climate and the nearer or more distant course of the sun, and are made such by the qualities of the juices of the soil.
