7. Subsequently the shipyards are to be built and with a northern aspect, as a rule. For southern aspects because of their warmth generate dry rot, wood worms and ship worms with other noxious creatures, and feed and maintain them. Further, such buildings should have very little wood in them because of fire. As to their dimensions no rule should be laid down. They are to be made to take the largest vessels; so that even if such vessels are drawn ashore, they may have a roomy berth.

In this book I have described how the works required for public purpose in cities are to be planned and carried out. The next book1 will consider the requirements of private buildings and their due proportions.
