Book VII

Chapter VIII

on vermilion and quicksilver

1. I will now go on to describe the treatment of minium1 or vermilion. It is said to have been discovered in the Cilbian Fields of Ephesus.2 The material and its treatment is sufficiently wonderful. For what is called the ore is first extracted. Then, using certain processes, they find minium. In the veins the ore is like iron, of a more carroty colour, with a red dust round it. When it is mined, and is worked with iron tools, it exudes many drops of quicksilver, and these are at once collected by the miners.

2. When the ore has been collected in the workshop, because of the large amount of moisture, it is put in the furnace to dry. The vapour which is produced by the heat of the fire, when it condenses on the floor of the oven, is found to be quicksilver. When the ore is taken away, the drops which settle because of their minuteness cannot be gathered up, but are swept into a vessel of water: there they gather together and unite. Four sextarii of quicksilver when they are weighed come to 100 lbs.3

3. When quicksilver is poured into a vessel, and a stone weight of 100 lbs. is placed upon it, the stone floats upon the surface.
