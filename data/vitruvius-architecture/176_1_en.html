10. Other halls in a foreign manner are those which the Greeks call Cyzicene. These are situated with a north aspect, and especially with an outlook upon gardens; they have folding windows in the middle. The halls themselves are broad and long enough to have two triclinia facing each other, with room to pass round; and these, on both hands, have garden windows with folding lights, so that the guests, under cover,2 may have a view of the garden.3 The height of the hall must be one and a half times its width.

11. In buildings of this kind, all the rules of symmetry must be followed, which are allowed by the site, and the windows will be easily arranged unless they are darkened by high walls opposite. But if they are obstructed by the narrowness of the street or by other inconveniences, skill and resource must alter the proportions by decreasing or adding, so that an elegance may be attained in harmony with the proper proportions.
