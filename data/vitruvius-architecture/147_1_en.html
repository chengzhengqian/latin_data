Chapter IX

on colonnades and passages behind the scenes

1. Behind the stage, colonnades are to be planned so that when the play is interrupted by sudden showers, the audience may have a place of refuge; the colonnades may also furnish room to set up the stage machinery.2 At Rome there are the Colonnades of Pompey3; at Athens there are the Colonnades of Eumenes,4 the Temple of Bacchus,5 and as you leave the theatre, on the left-hand side there is the Odeum.6 This7 Themistocles planned with stone columns and completed with masts and yards from the Persian spoils. It was burnt in the Mithridatic War8 and King Ariobarzanes9 restored it. At Smyrna is the Colonnade of Stratonice.10 At Tralles there are colonnades above the stadium on either side, like those of a theatre. In other cities also which have had skilful architects there are colonnades and walks adjoining the theatres. 2.
