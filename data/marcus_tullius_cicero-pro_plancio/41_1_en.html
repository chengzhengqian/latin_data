XXIV. But I now come to my friend Lucius Cassius,a and I have not yet called youb to account as regards the Juventius whom that excellent and cultivated young man mentioned in his speech as being the first plebeian to be elected curule aedile. As to that, Cassius, if, in replying to this statement of yours, I were to assert that the Roman people were not aware of this fact, and that there has been no one to tell us of it, especially now that Congusc is dead, you would not, I take it, be surprised; for indeed, though I am somewhat of an antiquarian myself, I must confess that I never heard of the fact until I heard it from you.
