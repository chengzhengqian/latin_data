XCIII. Proconsul Publius Servilius overcame the b.c. 75–75Isaurians in Cilicia and stormed several cities of the pirates.2 Nicomedes King of Bithynia made the Roman People his heir, and his kingdom was converted into a province.3 Mithridates made a treaty with Sertorius and attacked the Roman People. The muster of the King’s forces, infantry and naval; the seizure of Bithynia, and the defeat in battle near Chalcedon of Consul Marcus Aurelius Cotta by the King;4 the achievements of Pompey and Metellus against Sertorius . . . he was a match for them in all the arts of war and campaigning . . . he drove them away from the siege of the city of Calagurris and compelled them to make off in different directions, Metellus to Farther Spain, Pompey to Gaul.5

XCIV. Consul Lucius Licinius Lucullus fought successful b.c. 74–73cavalry battles against Mithridates, made several victorious marches, and, when his soldiers demanded battle, restrained them from mutiny.6 Deiotarus, Tetrarch of Galatia, crushed the officers of Mithridates when they stirred up war in Phrygia.7 The book also includes the successful campaign of Gnaeus Pompeius in Spain against Sertorius.8
