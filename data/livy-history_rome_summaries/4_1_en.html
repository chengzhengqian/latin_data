Summaries

In the consulship of Publius Lentulus and Marcus b.c. 181 Baebius, the books of Numa were found on the property of Lucius Neryllus,1 a clerk.

In the consulship of Aulus Postumius and Gaius b.c. 180 Calpurnius, successful campaigns were conducted against the Ligurians and Spaniards. Lucius Livius,2 tribune of the people, passed a law defining the age at which a man might stand for each office. He received the nickname Annalis.

In the consulship of Quintus Fulvius and Lucius b.c. 179 Manlius, the enmity of Marcus Lepidus and Fulvius Nobilior was brought to an end at the beginning of their censorship.

  • 1The name is given as Petilius in Book XL itself.
  • 2Correctly called Villius in XL. xliv. 1.
