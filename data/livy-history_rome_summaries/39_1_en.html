LXVI. Jugurtha was driven from Numidia by Gaius b.c. 106–105 Marius. When Jugurtha received the assistance of Bocchus king of the Moors, Bocchus’ forces were also slaughtered in battle, and Bocchus was unwilling longer to endure the war that he had so unfortunately undertaken. He therefore threw Jugurtha in chains and handed him over to Marius; in this operation the services of Lucius Cornelius Sulla, Marius’ quaestor, were outstanding.2

LXVII.
