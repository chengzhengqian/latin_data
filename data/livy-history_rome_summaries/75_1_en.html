Summaries

CXIX. Gaius Trebonius was killed in Asia by the b.c. 43treachery of Publius Dolabella. For this crime Dolabella was adjudged an enemy by the senate.1 When Consul Pansa suffered a reverse against Antony, Consul Aulus Hirtius came up with his army and by routing the troops of Marcus Antonius, brought into balance the fortunes of the two sides. Antony, being defeated thereafter by Hirtius and Caesar, fled into Gaul and acquired the support of Marcus Lepidus and the legions which he commanded; Antony was declared an enemy by the senate together with everyone who was within his lines. Aulus Hirtius, who after his victory had fallen in the very camp of the enemy, and Gaius Pansa, who died of the wound received in the battle he lost, were buried in the Campus Martius.2 The senate showed too little gratitude towards Gaius Caesar, the only survivor of the three commanders; for it voted the distinction of a triumph to Decimus Brutus who had been freed from siege at Mutina by Caesar, but made an insufficiently grateful reference to Caesar and his men. For this reason Gaius Caesar came to terms with Marcus Antonius through the mediation of Marcus Lepidus, came to Rome with his army, and stunning those who were ill-disposed towards him by the arrival of this force, was elected consul at the age of nineteen.3

CXX.
