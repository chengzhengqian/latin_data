34a. Note on Lucan, Pharsalia VII. 62 (Usener, 223): Titus Livius says that he (Cicero) was in Sicily, because of ill-health, at the time when the battle at Pharsalus took place, and that he received there a letter from the victorious Caesar, bidding him be of good cheer.

The reference to Sicily is a mistake, mechanical or otherwise, of the commentary on Lucan, cf. Summary CXI (“Cicero stayed in the camp”—also inexact); Cicero, On Divination I. 68.
