Gaius Gracchus on the other hand argued for the law, but Scipio carried the day.3

An account is given of the wars between King Antiochus of Syria and King Phraates of Parthia,4 and of the no less disturbed situation in Egypt. Ptolemy, surnamed the Benefactor, being hated by his people because of his excessive cruelty, fled secretly to Cyprus after his palace had been set on fire by the populace. When the crown was given by the people to his sister Cleopatra, whom he had divorced after violating and marrying her virgin daughter, Ptolemy in his anger killed in Cyprus the son he had had by Cleopatra and sent the head, hands, and feet to the child’s mother.5
