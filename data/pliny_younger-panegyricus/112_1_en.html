95. To you, Conscript Fathers, my debt is great, and this is published in the official records.2 You it was who paid me tribute according to the best traditions, for my orderly conduct as tribune,3 my moderation as praetor,4 my integrity and determination in carrying out the requests you made of my professional services for the protection of our allies.5 More recently, you hailed my designation as future consul with such acclamation that I am well aware that I must redouble my efforts if I am to receive your continued approval, and retain and increase it day by day; I do not forget that the truest judgement on whether a man merits an office or not is passed at the moment of his assuming it. All I ask is your support in my present undertaking and your belief in what I say.
