18–19

Priscianus, on ‘caepe.’ The most archaic writers are found to have used a feminine singular in a, rightly. Naevius in The Circumcised—

‘Well, God damn him—the kitchen-gardener who first produced an onion!’

  • aThis looks like an allusion to political foes.
  • bOn this play, cf. L. Alfonsi, in Dioniso, XIII, 184 ff.
  • cApparently: Gloss. Labb. apella λειπόδερμος, which apparently means circumcised. Cp. scholl. ad Hor., Sat., I, 5, 100 (Iudaeus Apella); V. Pisani, in Paideia, VIII, 18. Some take Appella to be Apula, Apulian (M. Berchem, De Naev. Poet, vita, 68–9).
