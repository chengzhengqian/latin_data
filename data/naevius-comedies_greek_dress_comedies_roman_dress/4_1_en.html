Macrobius

: ‘Praenestine nuts.’ c This term occurs in a passage of Naevius in the play called The Soothsayer—

(A) Who dined with you yesterday? (B) Guests from Praeneste and Lanuvium. (A) It would have been just the thing to have both parties entertained with their favourite fare; to the one d you should have given a little sow’s belly, drawn and boiled, while for the other you should have spilt out nuts at downhill speed!

  • aHardly modelled on Philemon’s Ἀγύρτης; cf. Ribb., Com. Fr., p. 9; for Leo, Gesch. d. Röm. Lit., I, 92, rightly thinks that Naevius’ play was a fabula togata, because the scene was laid in Italy and Italians were mentioned in it (see lines 22–6, given here).
  • bEspecially the Metelli. See pp. 154–5.
  • cHazel-nuts or filberts.
  • d sc. the Lanuvians.
