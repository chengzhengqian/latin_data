The Walnut-Tree

my leaves were trembling in the wind, but fear was the cause of my trembling. If I have deserved it, and am judged guilty, put me on the fire, and burn my limbs on smoky hearths: if I have deserved it, and am judged guilty, cut me down with the steel, and let my wretchedness once for all have an end. If ye have no cause for burning or for cutting down, spare me: so may ye finish the journey ye have begun.
