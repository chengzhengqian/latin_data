Tragedies

11

Agamemnon falls mortally wounded:

Nonius: ‘Fligi,’ the same as ‘affligi’ . . .— Himself fell hurt and dashed himself to earth.

12–13

Aegisthus demands that Electra be dragged from her refuge:

Nonius: ‘Procacitas’ is a term derived from ‘procare,’ that is, to demand. . . .—

Aegisthus You must endure the duty of obedience To what my majesty demands. Lead you This woman from the temple! 14

Unplaced fragment:

Nonius: ‘Laetare’ and ‘laetiscere,’ the same as ‘lactificare’. . .—

And have you done with gladdening your eyes Upon a sighta desirable?

Ajax Whip-Bearerb

15

The Greeks forget Ajax’s great deeds at Troy?:

Nonius: ‘Iamdiu’ for once upon a time. . . . Livius in Ajax—

Does that seem wonderful because ’twas done Now long ago?

  • aOf Agamemnon’s corpse.
  • bBased apparently on Sophocles’ Αἴας, to which name the MSS. of Sophocles add Μαστιγοφόρος—an epithet adopted probably by some Alexandrian scholar.
