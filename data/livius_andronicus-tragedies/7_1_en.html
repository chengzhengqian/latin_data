Tereus has discovered that he has eaten of his son:a

Nonius: ‘Praestolat’ for ‘praestolatur.’ . . .—

Tereus Oh, God! So all unknowingly it was That you stood waiting as a slave.

  • ehaurire suggests ‘draw out,’ ‘drain’ (not ‘drink dry’) in the sense in which exanclare was often used (cp. the Greek ἀντλεῖν). But anclare or anculare should here be taken in its other sense, which is the same as that of exanclare: ‘to serve out,’ to bring as a servant does (anculare dicebant pro ministrare—Paul., 15, 7). The Greek word meant by Paulus would be ἀγκυλοῦν, to crook the hand.
