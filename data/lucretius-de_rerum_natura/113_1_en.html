There is raving, because the strength of mind and spirit is set in a turmoil and, as I have shown,a divided apart and separated up and drawn asunder by that same poison. Next, when the cause of the disease has already turned back, and the corroding humour of the diseased body has returned to its secret haunts,b then first, staggering as it were, the man rises, and by degrees comes back to his full senses and receives back his spirit.
