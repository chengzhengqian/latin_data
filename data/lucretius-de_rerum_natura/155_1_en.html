Again, a colonnade may be of equal line froma colonnade seems to vanish a point; end to end and supported by columns of equal height throughout, yet, when its whole length is surveyed from one end, it gradually contracts into the point of a narrowing cone, completely joining roof to floor and right to left, until it has gathered all into the vanishing point of the cone.

At sea sailors seem to perceive the sun to risethe sun seems to rise and set in the sea; out of the water and to set in the water, and there to hide its light, naturally because they behold nothing but water and sky, that you may not lightly believe the credit of their senses to be utterly shaken.

Then those ignorant of the sea think that ships in harbour are maimed and struggling against the waves with stern-fittings broken. For whatever partoars seem to be broken back in water; of the oars is raised above the sea watera is straight, and the rudders above are straight; but whatever is submerged under water seems to be all broken back and wrenched and turned flat upwards and thus bent back to be almost floating upon the flood.

And when the winds carry scattered cloudsthe stars seem to move behind the clouds; across the sky in the night time, then the shining stars seem to glide against the clouds and to pass above them in a very different direction from their true one.
