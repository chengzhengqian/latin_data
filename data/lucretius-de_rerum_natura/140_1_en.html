De Rerum Natura

45

[Buta since I have shown of what kind are the beginnings of all things, and in how varying and different shapes they fly of their own accord driven in everlasting motion, and how all things can be produced from these, you shall now see me begin to deal with what is of high importance for this subject, and to show that there exist what we term images of things, which are to be called as it were their films or bark, because the image bears a look and shape like the object, whatever it is, from whose body it is shed to go on its way.]
