Cerberusb also and the Furies and the with-holdingCerberus, the Furies and Tartarus are the guilty conscience and the fear of retribution. of light, and Tartarus belching horrible fires from his throat—these neither exist anywhere nor in truth can exist. But in this life there is fear of punishment for evil deeds, fear as notorious as the deeds are notorious, and atonement for crime—prison, and the horrible casting down from the Rock, stripes, executioners, condemned cell,c pitch, red-hot plates, firebrands; and even if these are absent, yet the guilty conscience, terrified before anything can come to pass, applies the goad and scorches itself with whips, and meanwhile does not see where can be the end to its miseries or the final limit to its punishment, and fears that these same afflictions may become heavier after death. The fool’s life at length becomes a hell on earth.
