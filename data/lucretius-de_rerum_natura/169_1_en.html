Or will this rather be the reason?—because in one moment of time perceived by us, that is, while one word is being uttered, many times are lurkingA great veriety of images is ready at any given time; which reason understands to be there, that is why in any given moment all these various images are present ready in every placeb: so great is their velocity, so great the store of things. Therefore, when the first image perishes and a second is then produced in another position, the former seems to have altered its pose.
