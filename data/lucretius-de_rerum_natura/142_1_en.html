Lastly, whatever similitudes we see in mirrors,a(4) Mirrors and the like throw back these films unchanged. in water, in any bright surface, since they are possessed of the same appearance as the things, must consist of images thrown off from those things. There are therefore thin shapes and like semblances of things, which singly no one can perceive, yet being flung back by incessant and unremitting repulsion give back a vision from the surface of mirrors. Nor does there seem to be any other way in which they could be preserved so that figures so like each thing should be given back.
