Nor need one jot more of reasoning be added,III. Taste is caused because particles of food are squeezed out in the mouth and distributed through the pores of the palate: or any more trouble, to explain the tongue and palate, by which we perceive flavours.

617

In the first place, we perceive flavour in the mouth while we squeeze it out in munching the food, as if one by chance takes in hand a sponge full of water and begins to press it dry. Then that which we squeeze out is distributed abroad through all the pores of the palate and the tortuous passages of the spongy tongue.
