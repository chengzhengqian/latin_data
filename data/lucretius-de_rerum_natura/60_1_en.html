Mark now and learn in the next place of whatII. Shape (333–720) There are many varieties of shape int he atoms, kinds are the beginnings of all things, how far they differ in shape, how varied they are in their manifold figures: not that there are only a few endowed with similar shape, but because commonly they are not all like all. And no wonder: for since there is so great a store that there is no end to them, as I have taught, and no sum, they must assuredly not be all of like frame with all and marked by the same shape.
