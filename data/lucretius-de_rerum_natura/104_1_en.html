The first-beginnings of the elements so interpenetrate one another in their motionsb that no single element can be separated off nor can its power act divided from the rest by space, but they are, asno part acts separately, it were, the many forcesc of a single body. Just as in the flesh of any living creature there is a scent and a certain heat and flavour, and yet from all these is made one body grown complete: so heat and air and the unseen power of wind commingled form one nature along with that quickly moving force, which from itself distributes amongst themd the beginning of motion, whence first the sense-bringing motion arises spreading through the flesh. For this naturebut the fourth substance is spirit of the spirit; lies deep down, hidden in the most secret recess, and there is nothing in our body more deeply seated than thise; and it is itself furthermore the spirit of the whole spirit. Just as commingled in our frame and in all our body the force of mind and the power of spirit lies hidden, because it is composed of small and scanty elements: so, I tell you, this force without name composed of minute particles lies hid, and is furthermore itself as it were spirit of the whole spirit and lords it in all the body.
