And the moon, whether with bastard light sheSo is the moon, moves illumining the world, or whether she casts her own light from her own body, however that may be, her shape as she moves is no larger than that seems to be with which she is presented to our eyes. For all things that we see at a great distance through much air become dimmed in appearance before their size is diminished. Therefore the moon, since its outline is not blurred. since it offers a clear appearance and a firm outline, must be seen on high by us from the earth in exactly the shape that defines it and of the size it really is.
