And although bodies on bodies lay unburiedBirds and beasts would not touch the corpses; or they also died. upon the ground in heaps, yet the tribes of winged creatures and wild beasts would either leap away to escape the rank smell, or having tasted would faint in a speedy death. Yet it was not often in those days that any bird was to be seen at all, or the gloomy generations of wild beasts came out of the forests. Most of them grew faint with disease and died. Among the first were the dogs, faithful creatures, which, scattered about on all the roads, yielded their breath with reluctance; for the power of the disease wrenched the life out of their limbs.

Without mourners the lonely funerals competed with one another in being rushed through. Nor was any kind of remedy general and certain; for what had given one the power to draw the breath of life into his lips and to behold the regions of heaven, this to others was poison and brought them death.
