When wind has entered a cloud, and moving about within the same has made the cloud grow grows hot thick round the hollow, as I explained before,a it becomes hot by its own quick movement; just as you see everything become very hot and catch fire by movement, and indeed a leaden bullet even melts when it is whirled a long distance.b When therefore this burning wind has burst the black cloud, by violent pressure it seems suddenly to squeeze outand bursting the cloud scatters fire; and scatter abroad seeds of fire which cause the winking flashes of flame; then follows the sound, which is slower in striking the ears than what comes to the sight of our eyes. You may be sure that this is what happens, when clouds are thick and at the same time piled high one above another in a wonderful mass, that you may not be deceived because from below we see more readily how wide they are than how far they extend piled upwards.
