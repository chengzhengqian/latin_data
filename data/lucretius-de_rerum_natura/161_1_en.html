To proceed: there is no need to wonder how Sounds pass where eyes cannot see, (1) because they can pass through tortuous passages while images cannot, voices pass and assail the ears through places through which the eyes cannot see plain objects. We often witness a conversation going on behind closed doors, of course because the voice can pass unimpaired through tortuous passages in a substance, while images refuse: for they are split up, unless they have straight passages to swim through, such as those of glass through which every appearance can fly.
