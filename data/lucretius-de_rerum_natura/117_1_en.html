It is evident that no one in dying feels his soul(13) No one feels his soul issuing forth as a whole from his throat. go forth from the whole body intact, nor rise first to the throat and then pass up to the gullet; rather he feels it tail m the particular region where it is located, as he knows his other senses to be dispersing abroad each in its own part. But if our intelligence were immortal, in dying it would not so much complain of dispersing abroad, but rather of passing out and quitting its vesture, like a snake.b
