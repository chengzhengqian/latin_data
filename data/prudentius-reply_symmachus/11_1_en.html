Such are they whom Ancus, Numitor, Numa, and Tullus reckoned as gods, such the divinities that fled from the flames of Troy. So it is that we have Vesta and the Palladiumg and our imaginary household gods, and it was fear of such that kept safe the Refugeh of long ago. Once the vain superstition beset the fathers’ pagan hearts, it ran unchecked through a thousand generations one after another.
