  • aThe argument is put in a curious way, but seems to be that a majority was required, and the Christians now have it. Augustus (prisco sub tempore) fixed the number of senators at 600. On the strength of the Christian and pagan parties in the senate at the time of Symmachus’ petition, see Boissier, La Fin du Paganisme II, pp. 271–2; Dill, pp. 4, 29, 36–7.
  • bThe words refer to the procedure in taking a division in the senate (“discessio”).
