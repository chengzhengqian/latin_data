80 C. AURELIUS COTTA

​

T 5 Cicero, On the Orator

[Crassus:] In present company, consider these two, almost contemporaries, Sulpicius [P. Sulpicius Rufus (76), T7] and Cotta. What is so unlike each other? What is so eminent, each in their own way? The one [Cotta] polished and precise, unfolding the matter in appropriate and suitable words. He always sticks to the case, and, having discerned with supreme acumen the point that has to be proved to the judge, he leaves out all other arguments and fixes his thoughts and utterances on that.
