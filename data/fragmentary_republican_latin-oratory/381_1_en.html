F 13 Cicero, Pro Caelio

Accordingly, I am quite content that this part of the case has been fully argued by Crassus with weight and eloquence, that is, concerning the disturbances at Neapolis [modern Naples], the assault on the Alexandrians at Puteoli [modern Pozzuoli],1 the property of Palla.2 I would wish that he had also spoken about Dio.

  • 1An Alexandrian embassy was sent to Italy to make the case against the restoration of King Ptolemy XII. The leader of the embassy, Dio of Alexandria, an Academic philosopher, was murdered in Rome.
  • 2Cf. Quint. Inst. 4.2.27.—Palla was perhaps the mother or stepmother of L. Gellius Poplicola, the husband of Sempronia Atratina, the adoptive sister of Caelius’ prosecutor L. Sempronius Atratinus (171).
