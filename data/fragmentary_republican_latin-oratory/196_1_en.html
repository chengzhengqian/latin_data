T 10 Quintilian, The Orator’s Education

Some want rhetoric to be something innate and yet do not deny that it is supported by practice, as in Cicero’s books on the orator [cf. Cic. De or. 2.232] Antonius says that it is a kind of observation, not an art. T 11 Diomedes

If in the penultimate [position] there is a tribrach or a dactyl or a pyrrhic, and there follows a tribrach or a molossus or a pyrrhic or an anapest, there will be a structure that is called lame or loose or soft; Antonius the Elder is said to have used that.

T 12 Macrobius, Saturnalia

There are, moreover, two styles of speaking contrasting by their different character. One is mature and serious, like that associated with Crassus [L. Licinius Crassus (66)].... [17] The other is the opposite to this: fiery, confident, and aggressive, such as Antonius used.
