F 47 Cicero, On the Orator

[Caesar Strabo:] In facts there can equally be something funny; it is generally derived from some mimicry as caricature, as in this example of the same Crassus: “By your rank, by your lineage!” What else was there at which the assembly laughed, other than that mimicry of facial expression and intonation? “By your statues!” But when he said this and added even a touch of gesture with his arm stretched out, we laughed more heartily.1
