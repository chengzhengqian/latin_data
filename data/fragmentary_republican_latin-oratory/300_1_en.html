F 9 Cicero, On the Orator

[Antonius:] For though, as you know, that man [Rutilius] was a pattern of righteousness and though there was no one either more honorable or more blameless than he in the community, he not only did not wish to behave like a suppliant before the judges, but not even to be defended more eloquently or elaborately than the simple consideration of truth permitted. To Cotta here, a very eloquent young man and his sister’s son, he allotted only a small share of the role. Q. Mucius [Q. Mucius Scaevola (67), F7–8] too spoke in that case to a certain extent, in his own way, with no pomp, with unadorned and clear diction.
