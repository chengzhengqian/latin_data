Moreover, such conduct is not worthy of Romans, particularly since their aim is to assist the outraged country and defend it against oppressors; accordingly, they should not show themselves as greedy of gain as the wrongdoers. Caesar also points out that, by natural law, ruling and being ruled have been placed upon men: one discovers and commands what is required, and the other should obey without questioning. In conclusion, Caesar announces that he will never yield to these agitators under compulsion and even tells these men to quit the military service. After the speech Caesar executes the most audacious and dismisses the rest.

To Soldiers in Africa (F 42)

Suetonius reports a short speech given by Caesar to his soldiers when fighting in Africa (CCMR, App. C: 115). It is said to illustrate his approach to arousing the army’s courage (see Polyaenus, Strat. 8.23.19).
