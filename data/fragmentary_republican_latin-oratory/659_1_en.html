On Ptolemy in the Senate (F 4)

In 57 BC Favonius called the Roman Senate to action against Ptolemy XII, since violence had been used, and Romans had accepted bribes.

​

F 4 Cassius Dio, Roman History

The affair [Ptolemy’s activities], however, became so noised abroad that even the Senate was mightily displeased, while Marcus Favonius in particular urged them to action, on the two grounds that many envoys sent by the allies had perished by violence and that numerous Romans had again on this occasion taken bribes.

Against Measures of C. Trebonius (F 5)

In 55 BC Favonius spoke against measures of the Tribune of the People C. Trebonius, as did M. Porcius Cato (126), who also opposed a bill of Trebonius on the allocation of consular provinces (126 F 25).
