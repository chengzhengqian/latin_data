F 65a Seneca, Dialogues. De Consolatione ad Helviam

Fortune had reduced Cornelia [the mother of the Gracchi] from having twelve children to two; if you wished to count Cornelia’s funerals, she had lost ten, if to assess them, she had lost the Gracchi. Nevertheless, when her friends were weeping around her and cursing her fate, she forbade them to criticize Fortune, who had given her the Gracchi as sons. It was appropriate that he was born from such a woman, he who said at a meeting of the People: “Do you dare to revile my mother who gave birth to me?”1
