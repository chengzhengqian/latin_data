Against C. Rabirius (F 4)

C. Rabirius was prosecuted by T. Labienus (133 F 1–2) for the same reason, now charged with treason; he was again acquitted, on that occasion defended by Q. Hortensius Hortalus (92 F 34–35) and Cicero (Cic. Rab. perd.; TLRR 220, 221).

Or perhaps you [T.
