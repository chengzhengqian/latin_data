F 43 Quintilian, The Orator’s Education

Nor should I agree with Celsus [A. Cornelius Celsus], who imposed one particular form upon this part and declared the best type of composition for the prooemium to be what can be found in Asinius [Cels. Rhet., F 19 Marx]: “If, Caesar, an arbitrator for this case could have been chosen out of all the human beings who exist or have existed, nobody would have been more desirable for us than you.” [133] Not because I deny that this is well composed,1 but because I do not accept that this is the regulation for the composition of all beginnings.
