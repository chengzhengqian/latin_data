Likewise through allegory he [Cicero] said that M. Caelius, who was better at prosecuting than at defending, had a strong right hand and a weak left [cf. T 2].

Caelius had much talent and, especially in prosecuting, much wit, and he was a man who deserved that a wiser mind and a longer life had fallen to his lot.

T 11 Tacitus, Dialogue on Oratory

[Aper:] Now, out of the speeches of Caelius, surely those give satisfaction, either as a whole <or> parts of them, in which we find the polish and elevation of style of the present day. [4] But that filth of words, the disjointed arrangement, and the shapeless periods savor of antiquity, and I do not believe that there is anyone so devoted to antiquity as to praise Caelius insofar as he is antiquated.
