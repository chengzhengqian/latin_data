96 Q. LUTATIUS CATULUS MINOR

the 60s BC Catulus supported a clerk taken to court for fraud by M. Porcius Cato when quaestor (126 F 9A).

​

T 1 Cicero, Brutus

[Cicero:] For about the sound of his [Catulus’ father’s: Q. Lutatius Catulus (63), T 1] voice and the charm of his pronouncing the letters, do not expect me to say anything since you knew his son. Admittedly, the son was not in the ranks of orators, but, still, in voicing his opinion in the Senate, he lacked neither practical wisdom nor a certain refined and cultivated manner of speech.

​

T 2 Cicero, Brutus

[Cicero:] ... also Q. Catulus the son, let us withdraw them from the battle line, that is from the courts, and station them [the orators just listed] on the ramparts of the Republic, whose demands they are easily able to meet.

​

T 3 Cicero, Against Piso

Q. Catulus, leader of this order and a guiding voice in political deliberations, before a very well attended meeting of the Senate, named me “father of the fatherland.”
