T 6 Cicero, On the Orator

[Crassus:] Africanus [P. Cornelius Scipio Aemilianus Africanus minor (21)] had weight, Laelius [C. Laelius Sapiens (20)] smoothness, Galba harshness, Carbo [C. Papirius Carbo (35)] a kind of flow and melody. Which of these was not eminent in that period? And yet each [was] eminent in their own style.

T 7 Lactantius, Divine Institutions [Cicero, On the Republic]

As regards Carneades, a philosopher of the Academic school, what force there was in his arguing, what eloquence, what shrewdness, whoever does not know him personally will see it from Cicero’s commendation...when he was sent as an envoy by the Athenians to Rome [in 155 BC], he argued about justice copiously while Galba and Cato Censorius [M. Porcius Cato (8)], the greatest orators at the time, were listening.
