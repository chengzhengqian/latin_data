48 C. SEMPRONIUS GRACCHUS

Against Plautius (F 59)

​

F 59 Valerius Maximus, Memorable Doings and Sayings

There was a kind of competition in insolence between the Carthaginian and the Campanian Senate: for the former bathed in a bath separated from the common people, the latter used a different Forum. That this practice was retained in Capua for some time is evident also from C. Gracchus’ speech written against Plautius.

On the Bill of Cn. Marcius Censorinus (F 60)

The speech apparently concerns a Rogatio Marcia de tribunis militum? (LPPR, p.311), proposed by Cn. Marcius Censorinus, perhaps praetor in 123 BC (LPPR, p.311) or Tribune of the People in 122 BC (MRR I 517).
