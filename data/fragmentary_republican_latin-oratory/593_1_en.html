T 6 Cicero, Posterior Academics

At all events, our Brutus, eminent for every kind of distinction, sets out philosophy in Latin writing in such a way that you would not at all desire Greek writings on the same subjects. And, indeed, he is an adherent of the same doctrine as you [M. Terentius Varro]; for, for some time, he heard the lectures of Aristus [Academic philosopher] at Athens, whose brother Antiochus [of Ascalon; Academic philosopher] you [heard].
