But both were orators of the first rank. [104] And we say this with the recollection of our fathers as evidence; for we have orations of both Carbo and Gracchus not yet sufficiently brilliant in language, but acute and very full of sagacity. Gracchus, thanks to the careful attention of his mother Cornelia, had been trained from boyhood and thoroughly educated in Greek letters. For he always had carefully chosen teachers from Greece, including, when he was already a young man, Diophanes of Mytilene, the most eloquent speaker of Greece at that time. But he only had a brief period for developing and showing his talent.
