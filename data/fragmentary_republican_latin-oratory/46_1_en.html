F 14 Valerius Maximus, Memorable Doings and Sayings

When Ser. Galba was vehemently criticized by Libo [L. Scribonius Libo (29), F4], a Tribune of the People, from the front of the Rostra, because after faith had been pledged he had killed a large number of Lusitanians as praetor in Hispania and M. Cato [M. Porcius Cato (8) ORF4 F196–99], in extreme old age, supported the tribunician action by his speech that he has transferred into the Origines, the accused, no longer putting in any objections on his own behalf, in tears, began to commend his young sons and the son of Galus [C. Sulpicius Galus (14)], a blood relation of his, and once the meeting was softened by that deed, he, who was on the point of perishing by the consensus of all, suffered hardly any negative vote.
