F 22 Cicero, Brutus

“In all other offices indeed [Scaevola was Crassus’ colleague],” I [Cicero] said, “but he [Q. Mucius Scaevola (67)] was Tribune a year later [106 BC], and while he was sitting on the Rostra as presiding officer, Crassus argued in favor of the Lex Servilia ... But when this speech of Crassus, which I know for certain you have often read, was published, he was thirty-four years old and by as many years more advanced in age than me. For he argued in favor of that law under those consuls in whose consulship I was born [106 BC], while he himself was born in the consulship of Q. Caepio and C. Laelius [140 BC]; [he was] three years younger than Antonius....”
