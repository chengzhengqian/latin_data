21 P. CORN. SCIP. AEMIL. AFRIC. MINOR

In Front of the Temple of Castor (F 16)

The speech in front of the Temple of Castor (Astin: F 18) presumably also dates to Africanus’ censorship, since the censors typically set up the tribunal for the equestrian census in front of that temple.

On P. Sulpicius Galus (F 17)

The speech reproaching P. Sulpicius Galus (RE Sulpicius 68) with effeminate behavior might also be linked to Africanus’ censorial office (Astin: F 20).

  • 1According to TLG, this word for a type of garment is not attested in Greek texts, though χειριδωτός [χιτών], “sleeved [tunic],” exists.
