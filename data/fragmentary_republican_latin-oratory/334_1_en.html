[Cicero:] Hortensius, then, when as just a young man he had started to speak in the Forum, soon began to be called upon in cases of greater importance. <And> though his beginnings had fallen in the period of Cotta [C. Aurelius Cotta (80)] and Sulpicius [P. Sulpicius Rufus (76)], who <were> ten years older, when Crassus [L. Licinius Crassus (66)] and Antonius [M. Antonius (65)], then Philippus [L. Marcius Philippus (70)], and afterward Iulius [C. Iulius Caesar Strabo (73)], were preeminent, in renown as a speaker he was constantly compared with these very men.
