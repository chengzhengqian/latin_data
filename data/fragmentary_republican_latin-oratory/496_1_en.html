126 M. PORCIUS CATO MINOR

On Bribery to the People (F 10)

As Tribune designate in 63 BC, Cato spoke before the People about bribery in that year’s election campaign for the consulship (CCMR, App. A: 265).

​

F 10 Plutarch, Life of Cato the Younger

After he [Cato] was declared Tribune [for 62 BC] with others and with Metellus [Q. Caecilius Metellus Nepos (120)], seeing that the consular elections were affected by bribery, he rebuked the People; and, in concluding his speech, he swore that he would prosecute the briber, whoever he might be, making an exception only of Silanus [D. Iunius Silanus, cos. 62 BC] because of their familial relationship: for Silanus had Servilia, Cato’s sister, as his wife. [continued by F 11]
