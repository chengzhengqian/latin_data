Against Cn. Pompeius Magnus in the Senate (F 3–3A)

​

F 3 Cicero, Letters to Quintus

On the fifth day before the Ides of February [February 9, 56 BC] Senate in Temple of Apollo.... That day Cato inveighed against Pompey [Cn. Pompeius Magnus (111), F24] with great force and prosecuted him in a set speech like a defendant; he said many things about me with the greatest praise, against my will, while he denounced that man’s treachery toward me. He was heard in rapt silence by the ill-disposed.
