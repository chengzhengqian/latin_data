176 M. VALERIUS MESSALLA CORVINUS

​

F 21 Quintilian, The Orator’s Education

Our earlier orators thought the best exercise was translating Greek material into Latin.... Messalla liked this practice, and there are many speeches of this sort written by him, so much so that he even vied with the well-known delicacy of Hyperides’ [speech] on behalf of Phryne, a very difficult matter for Romans.
