The so-called Basilica Porcia had been dedicated by the elder Cato [M. Porcius Cato (8)] when he was censor [184 BC]. Here, then, the Tribunes of the People were accustomed to transact business; and as a pillar was thought to be in the way of their seats, they determined to take it down or move it to another place.
