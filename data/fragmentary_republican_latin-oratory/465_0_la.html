F 36A Sall. Cat. 50.5–52.1

Caesar stresses that the decision about the fate of the captured conspirators should be made objectively, with minds free from passion. To illustrate that, he recalls examples of ancestors who, in decreeing punishments, took into consideration what conduct would be consistent with their dignity rather than what action could be justified.
