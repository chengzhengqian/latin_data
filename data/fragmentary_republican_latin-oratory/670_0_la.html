169 Q. PILIUS CELER

Against M. Servilius (F1–2)

In autumn 51 BC Pilius prosecuted M. Servilius (RE Servilius 20) on a charge of extortion in a complex and protracted case (TLRR 338); a written version of the speech seems to have existed shortly afterward (F2).

The background to the case: C. Claudius Pulcher, provincial governor in Asia (55–53 BC), was charged with extortion upon his return in 53 BC (TLRR 336). He bribed M. Servilius, but was found guilty nevertheless.
