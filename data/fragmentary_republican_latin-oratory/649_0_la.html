Against P. Vatinius (F14–28)

While still a fairly young man, Calvus seems to have attempted to take P. Vatinius to court three times: in 58 BC he charged him under the Lex Licinia Iunia de legum latione of 62 BC (Cic. Vat. 33; LPPR, pp.383–84); the case seems to have been curtailed by the intercession of the Tribune of the People P. Clodius Pulcher (137) upon Vatinius’ pleading (TLRR 255; Cic. Vat. 33–34; on the political context see Gruen 1971, 62–67). In 56 BC there was a charge of ambitus under the Lex Tullia de ambitu of 63 BC (Cic.
