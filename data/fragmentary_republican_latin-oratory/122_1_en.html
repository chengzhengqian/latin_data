F 3 Cicero, Brutus

[Cicero:] When he [Rutilius], though perfectly innocent, had been brought to that trial by which, as we know, the Republic was profoundly rent asunder, while at that time L. Crassus [L. Licinius Crassus (66)] and M. Antonius [65], ex-consuls, most eloquent men, were around, he did not wish to invoke the aid of either of them. He spoke himself in his own defense, and C. Cotta [C. Aurelius Cotta (80), F 8–9], since he was the son of his sister, added a few words ...and Q. Mucius [Q. Mucius Scaevola (67), F 7–8] spoke, precisely indeed and in a carefully finished manner, as he was accustomed to, but in no way with that fire and fullness that this kind of trial and the significance of the case demanded.
