127 L. CALPURNIUS PISO CAESONINUS

Again Against M. Tullius Cicero (F 2)

​

F 2 Cicero, Letters to Quintus

The second point concerns Calventius Marius’1 speech: I am surprised that you write that in your view I should write a rejoinder to that, especially as nobody will read that [speech of Piso] if I write nothing in reply, whereas all the boys learn mine against him [Cic. Pis.] by heart as though it was part of their lessons.

Against Marc Antony in the Senate (F 2A)

In 44 BC Piso was among the first to oppose M. Antonius, (159) by delivering a courageous speech in the Senate on August 1, 44 BC (Cic. Phil. 1.28, 5.19: 159 F 15, 12.14; Att. 16.7.5, 16.7.7; Fam. 12.2.1).
