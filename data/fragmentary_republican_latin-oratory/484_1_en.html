125 C. MEMMIUS

​

T 1 Cicero, Brutus

[Cicero:] C. Memmius, Lucius’ son, highly educated in literature, albeit Greek [literature], disdainful indeed toward Latin [literature], an adroit orator with a pleasing diction, though averse to the labor not only of speaking, but even of thinking: he took away from his skill to the same degree that he reduced his effort.

As Tribune of the People in 66 BC, Memmius attacked (F 2, 3–6) the brothers L. and M. Licinius Lucullus (90 + 91) because of alleged political misbehavior (TLRR 204, 206; on the possible political context, see Gruen 1971, 56–58).
