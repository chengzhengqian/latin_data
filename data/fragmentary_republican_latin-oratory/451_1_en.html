121 C. IULIUS CAESAR

​

T 4 Quintilian, The Orator’s Education

But if C. Caesar had only been free to spend time in the Forum, no other of our countrymen could have been named as a rival to Cicero: so much force, such a shrewdness, such a drive is in him that it is obvious that he spoke with the same spirit as he waged war; yet he dressed all this up with a wonderful elegance of language, which he studied in particular.
