Against M. Tullius Cicero in the Senate (F 1)

​

F 1 Asconius on Cicero, Against Piso

For when Piso and Gabinius [cos. 58 BC] had been recalled from their provinces, in accordance with Cicero’s view that he had expressed [in the speech] on the consular provinces, in the consulship [56 BC] of Lentulus [Cn. Cornelius Lentulus Marcellinus (128)] and Philippus [L. Marcius Philippus], Piso, returning to the community, complained about Cicero’s hostile attack in the Senate and inveighed against him, trusting particularly in his son-in-law Caesar [C. Iulius Caesar (121), married to Piso’s daughter Calpurnia], who then held the Gallic provinces.
