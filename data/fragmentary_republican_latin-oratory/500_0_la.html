On the Catilinarian Conspirators (F13–16A)

In the discussion about the fate of the Catilinarian conspirators at the end of 63 BC, Cato supported severe punishment, in contrast to C. Iulius Caesar’s (121 F 32–36A) milder view; the Senate adopted Cato’s position (Suet. Iul. 14.2; App. B Civ. 2.6.21; Plut. Caes. 8.1–2; Cic. 21.4). A
