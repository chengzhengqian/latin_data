F 23 Gellius, Attic Nights

C. Caesar, too, a weighty authority on the Latin language, says in the Anticato: “for the arrogance, haughtiness, and tyranny of one man.” Likewise in the first book of the first action against Dolabella: “those in whose temples and shrines they had been placed for honor and adornment.” [9] In his books on analogy [F 26 GRF] as well he recommends that everything of that sort should be said without the letter <i>.1
