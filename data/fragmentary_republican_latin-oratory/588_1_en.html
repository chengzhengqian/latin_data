Against P. Clodius Pulcher (F 3–4)

In 61 BC Lentulus was the chief prosecutor of P. Clodius Pulcher (137) for sexual impurity in the wake of the Bona Dea scandal (cf. 137 F 9A), when Clodius was acquitted amid suspicion of bribery (TLRR 236).

And I see that this addition has been made to the response of the soothsayers: “Ancient and secret sacrifices have not been performed diligently enough and been desecrated.” ... “Ancient and secret.” I say that, when Lentulus, that grave and eloquent orator, was accusing you [P.
