F 3 Cicero, Letters to Atticus

F 6 Plutarch, Life of Pompey

While this [political unrest and decisions made by others] was going on, Bibulus shut himself up in his house and, for his last eight months as consul [59 BC], did not appear in public, but issued edicts full of accusations and slanders against both men [Cn. Pompeius Magnus (111) and C. Iulius Caesar (121)] ...

On Behalf of the People of Tenedos (F 7)

In 54 BC Bibulus was among the men who spoke on behalf of the liberty of the people of Tenedos (island in the Aegean Sea), who seem to have asked the Senate for the status of a free community and to have been refused.
