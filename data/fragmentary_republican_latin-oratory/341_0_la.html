On Behalf of Sex. Naevius (F16–18)

In 81 BC, again with L. Marcius Philippus (70 F 14), Hortensius supported Sex. Naevius against P. Quinctius, who was defended by Cicero (Cic. Quinct.) (TLRR 126;

​

F 16 Cic. Quinct. 1

quae res in civitate duae plurimum possunt, eae contra nos ambae faciunt in hoc tempore, summa gratia et eloquentia; quarum alteram, C. Aquilli, vereor, alteram metuo. eloquentia Q. Hortensi ne me in dicendo impediat, non nihil commoveor, gratia Sex. Naevi ne P. Quinctio noceat, id vero non mediocriter pertimesco.
