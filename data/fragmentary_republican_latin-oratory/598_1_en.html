F 20 Asconius on Cicero, Pro Milone

M. Cicero was the only one to reply to them [the prosecutors]: and while it would have pleased some to have the crime defended in such a way, namely that Clodius [P. Clodius Pulcher (137)] was killed for the sake of the Republic—a line of argument that M. Brutus followed in that speech that he composed on behalf of Milo and published, as if he had delivered it—this did not please Cicero, <so that>, whoever was condemned in relation to the public good, could also be killed without having been found guilty in court.
