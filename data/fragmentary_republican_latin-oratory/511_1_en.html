On Behalf of T. Annius Milo (F 26)

In 52 BC Cato was among the advocates defending T. Annius Milo (138) in one of the trials of that year (TLRR 306).

On Caesar’s Candidacy in the Senate (F 27)

In 52 BC Cato spoke against a bill, proposed by the Tribunes of the People, that would allow C. Iulius Caesar’s (121) candidacy for office in his absence (Plebiscitum de petitione Caesaris: LPPR, p.412; cf. Liv. Epit. 107).
