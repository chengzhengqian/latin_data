66 L. LICINIUS CRASSUS

On L. Marcius Philippus in the Senate (F 41)

Shortly before his death in 91 BC, Crassus delivered an impressive and passionate speech against the behavior and policies of consul L. Marcius Philippus (70) in the Senate.

[2] For when he had returned to Rome, on the concluding day of the dramatic festival, deeply moved by that speech that was reported to have been delivered at a meeting of the People by Philippus [L. There, after Drusus had complained much about Philippus [72 F 7], he [Crassus] raised for discussion in the Senate that very issue, namely that a consul had delivered such a violent attack upon this order at a meeting of the People.
