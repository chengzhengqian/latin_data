There were some impressive speeches of his given at public meetings and three merciless prosecutions, and all of these taken on as a result of strenuous effort for the Republic. His speeches in defense, although those points that I have mentioned were what he was better at, were still not negligible and indeed quite tolerable. When he had been made curule aedile [50 BC] with the greatest support of the loyal men, somehow, after my departure [to govern the province of Cilicia] he departed from himself and came to fall after he had begun to imitate those whom he himself had brought down.
