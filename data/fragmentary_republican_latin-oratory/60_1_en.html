T 4 Cicero, Pro Murena

The famous Scipio was a man like that: he was not ashamed to do what you [M. Porcius Cato (126), the prosecutor] are doing, to entertain a very learned man, Panaetius, in his house. By that man’s speech and doctrines, although they were the same as those that delight you, still, he [Scipio] did not become harsher, but, as I have heard from old men, extremely gentle.
