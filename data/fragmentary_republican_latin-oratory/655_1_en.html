165 C. LICINIUS MACER CALVUS

Against Asicius (F 31)

Alexandria to Rome in 57 BC. Asicius was defended by Cicero (Cic. Pro Asicio: Crawford 1984, 138–40) and acquitted (TLRR 267; Cic. Cael. 23–24).

​

F 31 Tacitus, Dialogue on Oratory

[Aper:] And I do not see that others dissent from this assessment of mine [i.e., that the works of orators of earlier periods are not worth engaging with]. For how very few read Calvus’ [speeches] against Asicius or against Drusus?

On Behalf of C. Messius (F 32)

C. Messius (tr. pl. 57, aed. 55 BC), prosecuted in summer 54 BC, was defended by Cicero (Cic. Pro C. Messio: Crawford 1984, 180–81) and Calvus; the outcome of the trial is uncertain (TLRR 289).
