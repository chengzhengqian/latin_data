F 30 Cicero, Letters to Atticus

I want to help Brutus in every way I can. I see that you feel the same as I do about his little speech. But I do not quite understand what you want me to write in a speech purporting to have been delivered by Brutus as he has published [his]. How on earth can this fit? Or so as, for example, “Against the tyrant lawfully assassinated”? Much will be said, much be written by us, but in another way and at another time.1
