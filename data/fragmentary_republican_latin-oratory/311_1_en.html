F 2 Rhetorica ad Herennium

When Lucius Saturninus [L. Appuleius Saturninus (64A), F 3] was about to put forward the grain law concerning the five-sixths as [i.e., 5/6ths of an as (Roman coin) as price per modius (Roman grain measure)], Q. Caepio, who was city quaestor during that time, explained to the Senate that the treasury could not endure so great a largesse [i.e., reducing the price].

On His Own Behalf Against M. Aemilius Scaurus (F 3)

In late 92 or early 91 BC, Caepio took M. Aemilius Scaurus (43 F 8) to court for extortion (TLRR 96). M. Aemilius Scaurus (43 F 8–10) then took Caepio to court on the same charge (TLRR 97).
