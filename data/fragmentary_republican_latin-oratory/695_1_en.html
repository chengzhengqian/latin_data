F 34 Charisius

clipeus in masculine gender is used in the sense of scutum [“shield”], as Labienus says; but in the neuter gender it signifies imago [“portrait”]. Yet Asinius, [in the speech] on behalf of Urbinia’s heirs, put clipeus imaginis as masculine: “a shield with a portrait in front put up.”
