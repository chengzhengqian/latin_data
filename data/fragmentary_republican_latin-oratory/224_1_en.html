F 28 Cicero, Pro Caecina

For Crassus himself did not plead the case before the centumviral court in such a way so as to speak against the legal experts, but so as to demonstrate that the point that Scaevola [Q. Mucius Scaevola (67), F 4–6] was maintaining was not law; and for that purpose he not only put forward arguments, but also employed the authority of Q. Mucius [Q. Mucius Scaevola (50)], his father-in-law, and many very learned men.
