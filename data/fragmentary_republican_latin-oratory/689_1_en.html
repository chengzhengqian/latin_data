[Words of] Asinius Pollio: “Therefore there was never any hesitation for Cicero to disown the speeches {as his} that he had poured out very passionately against Antony; and he promised to publish many times more speeches in the opposite sense to those and more carefully written, and even to recite them personally in public at a meeting of the People.” And he had added to this other things much shabbier: thus it was easily clear at once that the whole matter was false tosuch an extent that even Pollio himself did not dare to put it in his Histories. Indeed, those who were present at hisspeech on behalf of Lamia deny that he said these things—for he could not sustain lying within the knowledge of the triumviri—but composed them later.
