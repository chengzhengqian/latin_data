F 3 Cicero, Verrine Orations

You have heard Cn. Lentulus, a patron of Sicily, a very distinguished young man, say on that subject that, when the Sicilians were putting before him the issue on which he was to support their interests in the Senate, they complained about the misfortune of Sthenius [of Thermae], and that because of this wrong that had been done to Sthenius [by C. Verres; cf. Cic. Verr. 2.2.83–99] they had resolved to present the petition I speak of.
