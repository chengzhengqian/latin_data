85 Q. SERVILIUS CAEPIO

​

F 4 Cicero, Pro Scauro

By the same person [Caepio] also, under the Lex Varia, that guardian of the Republic [Scaurus] was taken to court for treason; he was attacked by the Tribune of the People Q. Varius [Q. Varius Hybrida (88)].

F 6 Charisius

cotidio [“daily”], wrongly instead of cotidie [usual form], Q. Caepio [in the speech] against M. Aemilius Scaurus under the Lex Varia: “when I saw the enemy helped daily by that man with pieces of advice.”

​

F 7 Charisius

vehementer [“strongly”], Caepio [in the speech] against M. Aemilius Scaurus under the Lex Varia: “Q. Albius [not in RE] is a good man and strongly of the right qualities.”

In Response to T. Betutius Barrus of Asculum (F 8)

Caepio was prosecuted by T. Betutius Barrus of Asculum (84 F 1) and replied with a speech allegedly written by L. Aelius Stilo Praeconinus (74 F 4) (TLRR 88, 106).
