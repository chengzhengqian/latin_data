XX. Mucius. What knowledge then, Laelius, do you think we ought to acquire, in order to be able to accomplish the result you demand of us?

Laelius. The knowledge of those arts which can make us useful to the State; for I consider this the noblest function of wisdom, and the highest duty of virtue as well as the best proof of its possession. Therefore, in order that these holidays may be devoted to discussions which, beyond any other object, are highly useful to the State, let us ask Scipio to tell us which form of government he considers the best.
