VII. . . . For I do not approve of the same nation being the ruler of the world and also its tax-gatherer; on the other hand, I consider frugality the best revenue both for private families and for States. . . .

. . . Credit seems to me to get its very name from the fact that what is promised is performed.4 . . .

. . . In a citizen of high rank or a man of high reputation, [I judge] flattery, ingratiation, and canvassing to be [indications] of shallowness. . . .
