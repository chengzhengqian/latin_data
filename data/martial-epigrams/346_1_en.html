Epigrams Book IX

A cheap little mat gives you carefree slumbers: there’s Gaius lying awake all night on feathers. From daybreak on Gaius in fear and trembling salutes so many masters: but you, Condylus, do not salute even your own. “Gaius, pay me back what you owe,” says Phoebus, and from yonder so says Cinnamus: nobody says that to you, Condylus. You fear the torturer? 152 Gaius is cut by gout in foot and hand and would rather take a thousand lashes. You don’t vomit of a morning or lick a cunt, Condylus; isn’t that better than being your Gaius three times over?

What are you waiting for, boy? Pour in the immortal 153 Falernian. Double three measures from the older jar. Tell me now, Catacissus, which of the gods will it be for whom I bid you pour six? “It will be Caesar.” Let stitched roses be ten times fitted to my hair, 154 making his number who founded the noble monument of the sacred family. 155 Now give me twice five kisses, adding up to the name which our god brought from the Odrysian world. 156

  • 152Slaves were often tortured to extract evidence.
  • 153I.e. it would keep indefinitely; cf. 11.36.5.
  • 154A variation of the practice illustrated in 1.71 and elsewhere.
  • 155Domitian(us), who founded the temple of the Gens Flavia.
  • 156If the name is Germanicus, Odrysio (properly “Thracian”) must = “northern,” as in 10.7.2. Some suppose it was “Sarmaticus,” referring to Domitian’s Thracian campaign, but he is not known to have taken this title. Cf. 9.101.20.
