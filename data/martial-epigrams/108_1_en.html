Epigrams Book III

55

Wherever you go, we think that Cosmus is moving shop and that cinnamon oil is streaming from a shaken phial. But don’t let exotic trash make you complacent, Gellia. You know, I suppose, that my dog can smell good in the same way.

56

I’d rather have a cistern at Ravenna than a vineyard, since I could sell water at a much better price.

57

A crafty innkeeper cheated me the other day at Ravenna. I asked for wine and water, he sold me wine neat.

58

Our friend Faustinus’ Baian villa, Bassus, does not hold down unprofitable expanses of broad acreage laid out in idle myrtle plantations, unwed 64 planes, and clipped boxwood, but rejoices in the true, rough countryside. Corn is tightly crammed in every corner and many a wine jar is fragrant with ancient vintages. Here, when Novembers are past and winter soon to come, the rugged pruner brings home the tardy grapes. Fierce bulls bellow in the deep valley and the calf with his harmless brow itches for combat.
