Epigrams Book I

9

Cotta, you would like to be seen as a pretty fellow 17 and as a big man, both. But a pretty fellow, Cotta, is a petty fellow.

10

Gemellus is a-wooing Maronilla. He is eager and insistent, begs her, gives her presents. Is she such a beauty? On the contrary, she couldn’t be uglier. So what is so desirable about her, so attractive? Her cough. 18

11

A knight is given ten tokens. 19 Why then do you take twenty drinks on your own, Sextilianus? The waiters carrying hot water would have run out of it by now, Sextilianus, if you were not drinking your wine neat.

12

On the road to the chilly heights of Herculean Tibur, where white Albula steams with her sulphurous waters, the fourth milestone from the neighboring city marks a farm and a sacred wood, acres dear to the Muses.
