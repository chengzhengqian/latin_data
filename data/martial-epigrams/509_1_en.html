Epigrams Book XIII

111. Falernian

Massic has come from the presses of Sinuessa. 115 Laid down under what consul, you ask? There wasn’t one. 116

112. Setine

Setia, that perched aloft looks down upon the Pontine flats, sent old jars from a tiny city.

113. Fundan

Opimius’ rich autumn bore this wine of Fundi. The consul pressed out the must and drank it himself. 117

114. Trifoline

A Trifoline 118 vine, I am not, I confess, of the first vintage, but even so I shall be seventh among wines. 119

  • 115In Campania, near Mons Massicus and Mons Falernus.
  • 116The wine was as old as the Roman kings!
  • 117As though Opimius, consul in 121 b.c., had been one of the ancient worthies who did their own farm work (cf. 6.64.2).
  • 118From the district of Trifolium near Naples, a favorite with the Emperor Augustus (Pliny N.H. 14.61). It had an earthy taste (Athen. 26e). Pliny (N.H. 14.69) makes it a plebeian wine, though better than most others in the area, nor is Juvenal’s reference (9.56) specially complimentary.
  • 119M. seems to imply the existence of a recognized classification, like that of comic poets by Volcatius Sedigitus (Morel, Fragm. poet. Lat., p. 46). Possibly though he should be translated: “my vine shall be among the seventh (category of) wines.”
