You seal your last testament thirty times a year, Charinus, and I sent you cakes wet with Hybla’s thyme. I give up. Have a heart now, Charinus. Seal less often, or do once for all what your mendacious cough continually promises. I have emptied my cashboxes and my purse. If I had been richer than Croesus, I should be poorer than Irus, Charinus, if you had eaten my beans 93 this often.

You painted Venus, but you worship Minerva, 94 Artemidorus; and are you surprised that your work found no favor?
