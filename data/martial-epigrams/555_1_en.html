Epigrams Book XIV

191. Sallust

Here will be Crispus, foremost of Roman historians, so the judgment of learned men declares.

192. Ovid’s “Metamorphoses” in parchment

This mass that has been built up for you with many a leaf contains the fifteen lays 170 of Naso.

193. Tibullus

Wanton Nemesis inflamed her lover Tibullus, who “was happy to be a cipher in all his house.” 171

194. Lucan

There are some who say I am no poet; but the bookseller who vends me thinks I am.

195. Catullus

Great Verona owes as much to her Catullus as does little Mantua to her Virgil.

  • 170I.e. books.
  • 171It was “Delia” of whom Tibullus (1.5.30) writes at iuvet in tota me nihil esse domo; “Nemesis” was his second love.
