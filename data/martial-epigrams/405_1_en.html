Epigrams Book XI

9

Memor, illustrious in Jupiter’s leaves, 25 fame of the Roman buskin, breathes, recalled by Apelles’ art. 26

10

>Turnus brought a mighty genius to writing satires. Why not to Memor’s kind of poetry? He was Memor’s brother.

11

Boy, remove the goblets, the chased chalices of warm Nile, and with nothing to fear hand me cups worn by our forebears’ lips, plain, with a close-cropped bearer to go with them; let their old-time dignity be restored to our boards. It is for you, Sardanapallus, 27 to drink from jewels, you who break a Mentor to make a chamber pot for your mistress.

12

You are welcome to the Right of Children, even seven, Zoilus, so long as nobody gives you a mother or a father.

13
