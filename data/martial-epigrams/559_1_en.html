Epigrams Book XIV

211. Ram’s head

You cut the soft throat of the Phrixian 185 husband of the flock. Did he deserve this, who gave you, cruel man, your tunic?

212. Dwarf

If you looked only at the man’s head, you would believe him Hector; if you saw him standing, you would think him Astyanax.

213. Buckler

Wont often to be defeated and to be victorious rarely, 186 this will be to you a buckler, but the shield of a dwarf.

214. Boy comic actors

No one will be “The Hated One” in this company; but any one of them can be “The Double Deceiver.” 187

215. Fibula

Tell me candidly, fibula, what is it you do for comic actors and singers? 188 “Get them a higher price for their fucking.”

  • 185Called “Phrixian” from the legendary ram with the golden fleece that carried Phrixus across the Hellespont.
  • 186Cf. 9.68.8n.
  • 187Two lost comedies of Menander.
  • 188Cf. 7.82.2; 11.75.3.
