Epigrams Book XIV

43. Corinthian 42 candelabrum

Candles gave me my ancient name. The oil lamp knew not our thrifty sires.

44. Wooden candelabrum

You see I am wood. Unless you watch the light, your candelabrum will turn into a great lamp. 43

45. Paganica (ball)

This paganica that swells with yielding feathers is not so soft as the follis and not so hard as the handball. 44

46. Triangle ball

If you know how to drive me off with nimble lefthanders, 45 I am yours. If you don’t, clod, return the ball. 46

  • 42Mingling gold, silver, and copper. According to Pliny (N.H. 34.8), there were three types of “Corinthian” bronze, silver predominating in the first, gold in the second, while in the third the three metals were in equal proportions.
  • 43The wood will catch fire.
  • 44As to the balls mentioned in this and the three following epigrams, cf. 4.19.5f and 7.32.7.
  • 45Cf. 7.72.11.
  • 46A technical term in the game? But here and in 163 with the sense “give up playing.”
