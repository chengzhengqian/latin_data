Epigrams Book IV

60

At the solstice let us make for Ardea and the Castran countryside and whatever fields are scorched by Cleonae’s constellation, 97 since Curiatius damns the breezes of Tibur, despatched to Styx amid her lauded waters. In no place can you shut out fate; when death comes, in the midst of Tibur is Sardinia. 98

61

Mancinus, you recently boasted proudly and joyfully that a friend had given you two hundred thousand. Three days ago, while we were chatting in the Poets’ Club, 99 you said that a cloak costing ten thousand was a present from Pompulla and swore that Bassa and Caelia had given you a genuine sardonyx100 girdled with three lines and two gems like sea waves. 101 Yesterday, when you abruptly left the theater while Pollio 102 was singing, 103 you were telling us as you fled that you had come into a legacy of three hundred thousand, and another hundred thousand this morning and another this afternoon. What great harm have we your friends done you? Take pity on us, cruel fellow, and at long last shut up. Or if that tongue of yours can’t be still, finally tell us something we would like to hear.
