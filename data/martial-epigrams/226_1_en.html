By chance I greeted you this morning by your real name, Caecilianus, instead of calling you “my lord.” Do you want to know how much such freedom costs me? It robbed me of a hundred farthings. 103

89

When tipsy Panaretus snapped his thumb for a late chamberpot (it was already midnight), he was handed a Spoletine flagon, one that he had drained himself and the whole of it had not been enough for him only. With complete accuracy he measured back to the jar its own wine, returning the full contents of his wine-holder. 104 Are you surprised that the flagon held all he had drunk? Stop being surprised, Rufus. He had drunk it neat.
