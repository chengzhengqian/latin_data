Epigrams Book X

74

Spare at length the weary congratulator, Rome, the weary client. How long shall I be a caller, earning a hundred coppers in a whole day, among escorts and petty clients, when Scorpus in a single hour carries off as winner fifteen heavy bags of gold hot from the mint? I would not want the plains of Apulia 132 as a reward for my little books—what do they deserve, after all? Hybla does not attract me, nor corn-bearing Nile, nor the dainty grape that surveys the Pomptine marshes from the top of Setia’s slope. Do you ask what I crave then? Sleep. 133

75

Once upon a time Galla asked me for twenty thousand, and I confess she was not overpriced. A year went by: “You will give ten thousand,” she said. I felt she was asking more than before. Six months later she asked two thousand sesterces. I offered a thousand, which she wouldn’t accept. Two Kalends perhaps or three passed, and without waiting for me herself requested four gold pieces. 134 I didn’t give them.
