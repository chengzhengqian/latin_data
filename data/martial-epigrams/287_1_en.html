Epigrams Book VIII

43

Fabius buries his wives, Chrestilla her husbands; each of them brandishes a funeral torch over the marriage bed. Venus, match the winners; the end awaiting them will be one bier to carry the pair.

44

Titullus, I warn you, live. It is always late for that. Though you start in your schooldays, 64 it’s late. But you, my poor Titullus, don’t live even in your old age. You polish every threshold with your calls and sweat in the early morning, damp with the kisses of the town; and scattering your presence over the three Forums, in front of all the horses 65 and Mars’ temple 66 and Augustus’ colossus, 67 you keep on the go through every third hour and every fifth. 68 Snatch, amass, seize, possess: it has to be left behind.   • 65I.e. equestrian statues.
  • 66Of Mars Ultor, Mars the Avenger (of Julius’ Caesar’s murder), in the Forum of Augustus.
  • 67Which colossal statue is meant is doubtful.
  • 68I.e. during the business hours of the day; cf. 4.8.2f.
  • 69Records of interest due on the first of the month.
