Epigrams Book XIV

201. Wrestler

I like him, not because he wins, but because he knows how to lie low and has better learned “lean-to” wrestling. 177

202. Monkey

A monkey, cunning at avoiding darts flung at me, I should be a long-tailed ape 178 if I had a tail.

203. Girl from Gades

Her waggles are so tremulous, her itch so seductive that she would make a masturbator out of Hippolytus 179 himself.

204. Cymbals

The hungry eunuch-priest is often wont to sell bronzes that mourn the Celaenaean darling 180 of the Mother.

205. Boy

Let me have a boy made smooth by youth, not pumice stone, on whose account no girl will please me.

  • 178The cercopithecus came from Aethiopia: Pliny N.H. 8.72. In Egypt it was a sacred animal: Juv. 15.4.
