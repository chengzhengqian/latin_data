You never write epigrams that are not bland and whiter than a white-leaded skin, without a grain of salt in them, not a drop of bitter gall: and yet, you crazy fellow, you want people to read them. There is no relish even in food deprived of vinegar’s bite, and a face without a dimple fails to please. Give honey apples and insipid figs to baby: my taste is for the Chian, 38 that knows how to sting.
