Epigrams Book VIII

Methymnean Arion. Let not just any one of the master’s troop of slaves, let your hand, Cestus, 79 inaugurate for me this noble gift with nectar worthy of it. Cestus, ornament of the feast, mix Setine; the boy himself, the goat himself seems to me athirst. Let the letters of Istantius Rufus’ name supply a number for our measures; for this precious gift comes to me from him. If Telethusa arrives bringing promised joys, I shall keep myself for my lady with your four, Rufus. If she’s doubtful, I’ll spin out the time with seven. 80 If she cheats her lover, to kill my sorrow I’ll drink both names.
