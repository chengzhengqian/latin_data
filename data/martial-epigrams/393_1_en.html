Epigrams Book X

Priapus’ pursuit. All this gentle godhead of my little plot you will propitiate with blood or with incense, and say: “Wherever your Martial is now, see, by my hand he along with me makes you acceptable offering, an absent priest. Think of him as present, and give to both whatever either shall pray for.”

93

Clemens, if before me you shall see Helicaon’s Euganian land and the fields decked with vine-hung trellises, 160 bring to Sabina of Atesta poems not yet published, only lately embellished with their purple gown. As a rose delights us freshly plucked 161 so pleases a new sheet unsoiled by the chin. 162

94

No Massylian snake guards my orchard, 163 nor does Alcinous’ royal domain serve me; my garden with its Nomentan trees germinates in security, and its low-grade apples fear no thief. Therefore I send you these yellow fruits of my autumn, born recently in the middle of Subura.

  • 160“Iugum is here the crosspiece along which vines were trained in a vinea iugata” (Housman, 730, with parallels).
  • 161Lit. “plucked by the first thumb,” apparently a careless expression.
  • 162Cf. 1.66.8.
  • 163I.e. unlike the garden of the Hesperides mine contains nothing of value.
