Epigrams Book XI

Book XI

1

Where, where are you going, holiday book, dressed in purple not of every day? Is it to see Parthenius? To be sure. You would go and return unrolled. He does not read books but petitions, 1 and has no time for the Muses, else he would have time for his own. Do you think yourself sufficiently happy if you fall into lesser hands? Then make for the colonnade of our neighbor Quirinus. 2 Not Pompey, nor Agenor’s girl, nor the fickle captain of the first ship 3 has an idler crowd. There are two or three there to unroll the bookworms breeding in my trifles, but only when the betting and gossiping about Scorpus and Incitatus is played out.

  • 1Addressed to the Emperor.
  • 2The temple of Quirinus near M.’s house; cf. 10.58.10.
  • 3The references are respectively to the Porticus Pompeii (cf. 2.14.10); the Porticus Europae (cf. 2.14.15); and the Porticus Argonautarum (cf. 2.14.6). Jason is called levis because of his conduct to Medea.
