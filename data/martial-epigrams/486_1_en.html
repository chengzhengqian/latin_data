Epigrams Book XIII

5. Pepper

When a shiny wax-colored beccafico with ample loins is given you by lot, if you are wise, add pepper.

6. Spelt water

I can send you spelt water, 10 a rich man will be able to send you mead. If the rich man won’t send it, then buy!

7. Beans

If pale beans froth for you in a red pot, you can often say no to the dinners of the high-livers.

8. Groats

Impregnate common jars with porridge from Clusium, so that after dinner you may drink sweet new wine from them empty. 11

9. Lentils

Receive Nile lentils, a present from Pelusium; 12 they’re cheaper than spelt, dearer than beans.

  • 10Cf. 12.81.3.
  • 11 Puls was seemingly supposed to ripen new wine.
  • 12Celebrated for its lentils: Virg. Georg. 1.228.
