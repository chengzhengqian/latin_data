Epigrams Book XIV

84. Book holder

Lest gown or overcoat make your books bearded, 75 this fir-wood will give long life to the paper.

85. Peacock couch

A bird, most beauteous with his painted plumage, gives his name to a couch. 76 Now he is Juno’s bird, but formerly he was Argus. 77

86. Saddlecloth

Take, huntsman, the cloth of a girt-up hunter; 78 for a fig is apt to arise from a bare-backed horse.

87. Semicircular couches

Accept a sigma 79 inlaid with crescent tortoiseshell. It takes eight. Let him come, whoever is a friend.

88. Tray for hors d’oeuvres

If you think a female land tortoise is part of me, you are deceived. I am male spoil of the sea. 80

  • 75I.e. fray the edges of the rolls.
  • 76From the veins in the wood (Pliny N.H. 13.96). Cf. 14.89n.
  • 77See index.
  • 78 Veredus, a fast breed of horse. The word is Celtic.
  • 79Cf. 10.48.6n.
  • 80Shell from the sea tortoise (? turtle) was held superior to that of the land tortoise, and the male shell was superior to the female. According to Pliny (N.H. 9.38) the land tortoises were called chersinae and were found in African deserts, where they subsisted on dew (K).
