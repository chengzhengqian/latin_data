Epigrams Book VII

99

So may you ever see the Thunderer in kindly mood, Crispinus, and Rome love you no less than your own Memphis: if my verses shall be read in the Parrhasian palace (for they are wont to enjoy Caesar’s sacred ear), venture, as a candid reader, to say of me: “He brings some credit to your times; he is not too much inferior to Marsus and elegant Catullus.” That is sufficient. I leave the rest to the god himself.
