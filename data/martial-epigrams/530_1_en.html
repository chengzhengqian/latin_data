Epigrams Book XIV

75. Nightingale

Philomela bewails the crime of foul Tereus, and she that was mute when a girl is called garrulous as a bird.

76. Magpie

A chattering magpie, I greet you as “lord” with a clear voice. If you did not see me, you would say I am no bird.

77. Ivory cage

If you have such a one as Lesbia, Catullus’ beloved, mourned, 67 it can live here.

78. Medicine chest

You see a medicine chest, 68 ivory appanage of the healing art. You will have a gift which Paccius 69 would wish his own.

79. Whips

Play, frisky slaves, do nought but play. 70 These I shall keep under seal for five days.

  • 68The narthecium was, as its name implies, made in the shape of a joint of the giant fennel (νάρθηξ) (K).
  • 69See index.
  • 70Slaves during the Saturnalia were allowed a degree of license.
  • 71Prometheus, according to the myth, brought fire from Heaven in the stem of the giant fennel (ferula or νάρθηξ), and gave it to men (K).
  • 72Used as a pillow. The Cynics, in imitation of beggars, equipped themselves with a staff and wallet: cf. 4.53.3 (K).
  • 73It seems that the analecta (cf. 7.20.17) used only his hands.
