Tell me, Muse, what is my friend Canius Rufus doing? Is he putting on paper the acts of Claudian times for posterity to read? Or does he emulate the compositions that a mendacious writer ascribed to Nero 22 or the fables23 of rascal Phaedrus? Is he wanton in elegy or solemn in epic or grim in Sophoclean buskins? Or idling in the Poets’ Club, 24 does he tell jests tinctured with Attic wit? Leaving that, does he tread the colonnade of *, 25 or saunter through the spaces of the Argonauts? 26 Or again, does he sit or stroll in dainty Europa’s 27 sunshine among the box trees warm in the afternoon, free of gnawing cares?
