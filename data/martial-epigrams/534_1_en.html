Epigrams Book XIV

94. Bold cups

We are plebeian cups of bold 84 glass, and our ware is not cracked by hot water.

95. A chased gold bowl

Although I am noble and ruddy with Galician metal, I glory more in my workmanship, for this is the work of Mys.

96. Vatinian cups

Accept a cup, a cheap memorial of cobbler Vatinius; 85 but that nose was longer.

97. Gold-inlaid dishes

Do not insult large gold-inlaid dishes with a small mullet. At the least he ought to weigh two pounds.

98. Arretine ware

I advise you not to be too scornful of Arretine ware. 86 Porsenna was luxurious with Tuscan crockery.
