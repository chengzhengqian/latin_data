Epigrams Book IX

37

You are at home yourself, Galla, but you are made up in the middle of Subura. Your hair is manufactured in your absence. You lay your teeth aside at night as you do your silks, and lie stored in a hundred caskets. Your face does not sleep with you. Yet you ogle with an eyebrow that is brought out for you every morning and have no respect for your hoary cunt, which you can now number among your ancestors. However, you promise the earth; 57 but my cock is deaf, and though it be one-eyed, it sees you. 58

38

Nimble Agathinus, 59 you play very dangerous games, but you will not manage to let your little shield fall. It follows you against your will, and returns through the air to sit on your foot or your back, your hair or your fingernail. Though the stage be slippery from a Corycian shower 60 and swift south winds tear away the awning denied us, the neglected shield roams over the boy’s heedless limbs; wind and water do not impair the artistry. 61 Though you want to make a mistake, whatever you do, you cannot slip; skill is needed to make your shield fall.

  • 58Cf. 6.23.
  • 59A juggler with a small shield. He cannot let it fall even if he tries.
