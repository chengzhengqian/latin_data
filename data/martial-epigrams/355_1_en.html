6

Happy they 15 to whom the urn has granted it to see our Leader gleaming with northern suns and stars. 16 When shall be the day on which ground and tree shall shine and every window, adorned by Latium’s daughters? 17 When shall be the sweet delays, the long trail of dust behind Caesar, and all Rome to be seen on the Flaminian Way? When shall the cavalry ride and the painted Moors in their tunics of Nile, and one voice of the people be heard: “He comes.”

7

Rhine, father of Nymphs and rivers, all that drink Odrysian 18 frosts, so may you ever enjoy clear waters and the barbarian wheel of the insolent cattle-driver never trample and fret you; so may you flow with your golden horns 19 recovered, Roman on either bank: send Trajan back to his peoples and his city—Tiber, your lord, bids you.

  • 14Tantalus.
  • 15Evidently a senatorial delegation to Trajan, chosen by lot.
  • 16The sun and stars are imagined as in attendance upon the Emperor (Trajan, expected from the Rhine in a.d. 98).
  • 17Seemingly with reference to gaily dressed people on the ground, lights or decorations in the trees, and women at the windows.
  • 18Properly = “Thracian,” here “northern”; cf. 9.93.8.
  • 19Previously shattered by defeat; cf. 7.7.3; 9.101.17.
