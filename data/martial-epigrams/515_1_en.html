Epigrams Book XIV

7. Parchment tablets

Suppose it wax, though it be called parchment. You will erase whenever you want to write afresh. 10

8. Vitellian tablets

Although she may not have read them yet, a girl knows what Vitellian tablets 11 want.

9. The same

Because you see we are very small, you think we are being sent to somebody’s mistress. You are wrong. This tablet asks for money also.

10. Bigger sheets

There’s no reason for you to think it a petty present when a poet gives you blank sheets. 12

11. Letter paper

Whether sent to a slight acquaintance or a dear friend, this paper calls everybody “dear.” 13

  • 10The parchment seems to have been specially prepared so as to admit of erasure, as on a wax tablet (K).
  • 11Of very small size, perhaps named after the maker. They were often used for billets-doux: cf. 2.6.6.
  • 12Instead of poems.
  • 13 Suus was commonly used in the heading to a letter, e.g. C. Plinius Maximo suo s. (salute).
