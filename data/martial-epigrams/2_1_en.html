Where the Claudian colonnade unfolds its wide-spread shade, was the outermost part of the palace’s 11 end. Rome has been restored to herself, and under your rule, Caesar, the pleasances that belonged to a master now belong to the people.

3

What race is so remote, so barbarous, Caesar, that no spectator from it is in your city? The farmer of Rhodope has come from Orphic Haemus, the Sarmatian fed on draughts of horses’ blood 12 has come, and he who drinks discovered Nile’s first stream, and he 13 on whom beats the wave of farthest Tethys. The Arab has sped hither, the Sabaeans too, and the Cilicians have here been sprayed with their own showers. 14 Sygambrians have come with hair curled in a knot and Ethiopians with hair curled otherwise. 15 Diverse sounds the speech of the peoples, and yet it is one, when you are called true father of the fatherland.
