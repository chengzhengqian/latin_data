51

If it irks you to buy my trifles, Urbicus, and nonetheless you have a fancy to know my wanton verses, you will seek out (and perhaps you know him) Pompeius Auctus. He sits at the entrance of Mars the Avenger’s temple. Steeped in law and practiced in the various employments of the gown, he is not my reader, Urbicus, he is my book. He remembers and recites my little books in their absence so that not a letter is lost to my pages. In fine, he could pass for their author if he wished, but he prefers to favor my fame. You may solicit him from the tenth hour onward (before that he will be too busy); a small dinner will accommodate the two of you. He will read, 77 you drink. Though you wish he wouldn’t, on he will boom. And when you say “That’s enough,” on he will read.

52

I am gratified, Auctus, that you read my books to Celer, that is, if Celer too likes what you read. He governed my peoples, Celts and Iberians, nor in our world was good faith ever more sure. So much the greater reverence confounds me, and I conceive his ears to be not a listener’s but a judge’s.
