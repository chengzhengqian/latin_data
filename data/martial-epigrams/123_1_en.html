Epigrams Book III

95

You never say good day, Naevolus, you always return it, though even a crow often says it first. Please tell me, Naevolus, why you expect this of me. As I suppose, you are neither a better man than I nor my superior, Naevolus. Both Caesars have praised and rewarded me, and given me the status of father of three children. 111 I have many readers and fame, without waiting for me to die, gives me a name well-known up and down the towns. This too counts for something: Rome has seen me tribune, and I sit where Oceanus 112 turns you out. I have made more citizens by Caesar’s bounty, I suspect, than you have slaves. But you are sodomized, Naevolus, and agitate your bottom to admiration. Ah, now you’re my superior, Naevolus, you win: good day!

96

You don’t fuck my girl, you lick her, and you talk like a lover and fucker. If I catch you, Gargilius, you’ll shut up. 113

97

I charge you, Rufus, don’t let Chione read this book. She has been hurt by my verse, 114 and she too can hurt. 115
