Epigrams Book XIV

183. Homer’s “Battle of Frogs and Mice”

Read through the frogs sung in Maeonian song and learn to relax your brow with my trifles. 162

184. Homer in parchment notebooks

The Iliad and Ulysses, foe to Priam’s realm, lie together, stored in many layers of skin. 163

185. Virgil’s “Gnat”

Accept, studious reader, the “Gnat” 164 of eloquent Maro; no need to read “Arms and the Man,” when you put away your nuts. 165

186. Virgil on parchment

How small a quantity of parchment has comprised vast Maro! The first leaf bears his own countenance. 166
