Epigrams Book XII

49

Linus, supervisor of the long-haired troop, 71 whom wealthy Postumilla calls master of her possessions and to whom she entrusts jewels, gold, wine, male concubines, so may your patroness prefer nobody to you, tried in perpetual loyalty: I beg you, aid my unhappy craze and for once keep negligent watch over these beings that sorely burn my heart, that night and day I eagerly long to see in my bosom, these beautiful, snow-white matching twins, these big 72 —not boys, but pearls.

50

You are sole proprietor of plantations—laurel, plane, and airy pine—and baths not made for one; for you stands a lofty portico with a hundred columns, and alabaster gleams trodden under your foot; the swift hoof strikes the dusty hippodrome and everywhere sounds the flow of water going to waste, 73 halls stretch at length. But there’s nowhere a place to dine or to sleep. How well you are—not lodged!

51

Does it surprise you, Aulus, that our friend Fabullinus is so often taken in? A good man is always a greenhorn.

  • 73Frontin. Aqu. 88 pereuntes aquae (of fountains) is compared.
