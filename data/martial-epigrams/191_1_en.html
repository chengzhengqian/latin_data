Epigrams Book V

81

You will always be poor if you are poor, Aemilianus. Nowadays wealth is given only to the rich.

82

Why did you promise me two hundred thousand, Gaurus, if you could not give me ten thousand, Gaurus? Or can you and don’t want to? I ask you, is that not more discreditable? Get along with you, Gaurus, to the devil with you! You are a paltry fellow.

83

You pursue, I fly; you fly, I pursue. This is the way I am. Your wishing, Dindymus, I wish not; your wishing not I wish.

84

Now the schoolboy sadly leaves his nuts, 155 recalled by the clamorous master, and the boozy gambler, betrayed by an all too alluring dice box and just hauled out of a secret tavern, is pleading with the aedile. 156 The Saturnalia are over and done with, and you sent me no small presents, Galla, not even smaller ones than you used to send.
