66

You bought a house for twice fifty thousand which you want to sell even for a lesser sum. But you seduce the buyer with a crafty trick, Amoenus; a cottage is concealed by ostentatious wealth. Jewelled couches gleam with firstclass tortoiseshell, and Moorish citrus wood, massive and choice. A three-legged table of complex design bears silver and gold. Boys stand about whom I would fain have as my “Masters.” 102 Then you boom about two hundred thousand and say it’s worth no less. You are selling a furnished house cheap, Amoenus. 103

67

The Ides of May created Mercury. Diana returns on the Ides of August. Maro made sacred the Ides of October. 104 May you often keep these Ides and those, you who celebrate great Maro’s Ides. 105

  • 102Cf. 5.57n.
  • 103M. ironically assumes (knowing better of course) that the price includes the furnishings.
  • 104May 15 was the dedication day of the temple of Mercury; Aug. 13 that of the temple of Diana on the Aventine; and Oct. 15 the birthday of Virgil.
  • 105The epigram need not be addressed to any particular person, though Silius was doubtless in mind; cf. 11.50.
