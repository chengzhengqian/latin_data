Epigrams Book XIV

33. Dagger

A dagger, which a narrow circle 31 marks with curving groove: this did Salo 32 dip hissing in his icy waters.

34. Sickle 33

Our Leader’s assured peace curved me for quiet employments. I am now the farmer’s, I used to be the soldier’s.

35. Small hatchet

When a dismal auction was held for payment of debts, this was bought for four hundred thousand. 34

36. Barber’s metal gear

This implement 35 is useful for cutting your hair, this 36 is apt for long nails, that 37 for your cheeks.

37. Bookcase 38

Unless you give me books packed tight, I shall admit moths and savage bookworms.

  • 31A stamp.
  • 32Cf. 1.49.12.
  • 33A converted sword.
  • 34The price is meant to be absurd. The securicula was a child’s ornament or toy: cf. Plaut. Rud. 1159. Such things were also hung round children’s necks as amulets, or as proofs of identity (K).
  • 35Scissors.
  • 36Little knife.
  • 37Razor.
  • 38The scrinium was a cylindrical case for holding books and papers.
