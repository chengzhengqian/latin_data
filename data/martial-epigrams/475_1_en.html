Polytimus is hurrying to the girls, 117 Hymnus doesn’t like admitting that he’s a boy, Secundus has buttocks acornsated, 118 Didymus is effeminate but doesn’t want to be, Amphion could have been born a girl. 119 Avitus, I would rather have their whims and haughtiness and querulous disdains than five times two hundred thousand sesterces of dowry.

  • 113 Audaces calices were cups not valuable enough to cause anxiety as to breakage: cf. 14.94. It is a “bold” thing to send such cups to a man that imports crystal.
  • 114 Gemma often means a jewelled vessel, sometimes one made of precious material such as murrine or even of glass (4.22.6); cf. 8.68.5. Here ironically of earthenware (cf. 14.94), unless geminus (Gruter) is read, as in older editions (“cheap goblets have two advantages”).
  • 115Lit. “chased cups.” But toreumata may be ironic, the cups in fact being plain, or used loosely; cf. 4.46.16n.
  • 116As having been defiled by impure lips: cf. 2.15 and Anth. Pal. 11.39.
  • 117He is impatient to become a man.
  • 118There is a play in the Latin on glans, acorn or the like, and glans penis. “Secundus”’ nates have had their fill.
  • 119So loath is he to oblige more puerili.
