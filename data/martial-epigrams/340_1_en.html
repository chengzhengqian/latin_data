Epigrams Book IX

Numidia exported, that Eurotas laved with his verdant stream. 129 But wood is lacking: put the first bath under the second.

76

The face you see belongs to my Camonius; these were his features as a child, his earliest form. His countenance had grown more manly in twice ten years 130 and a beard rejoiced to adorn its parent cheeks; the reddish down, shorn but once, had only lately besprinkled the razor’s tip. One sister of the three 131 begrudged, and as the wool hastened on, she cut the thread. An urn returned the absent dead to his father. 132 But not alone shall the painting tell of him in childhood; here in my pages there shall be a larger likeness.

77

Priscus’ fluent page discusses what is the best sort of dinner party. Much of what he writes is charming, much of it elevated, all of it accomplished. Do you want to know what is the best sort of dinner party? One that doesn’t have a flute player.
