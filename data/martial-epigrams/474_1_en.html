Epigrams Book XII

71

There is nothing you do not refuse me when I ask, Lygdus; but once, Lygdus, you refused me nothing. 108

72

Having bought the acres of a little farm that crouches near the tombs 109 and the propped-up roof of a jerry-built cottage, you forsake the city lawsuits that were your landed properties, Pannychus, and the small but sure rewards of your threadbare gown. As an attorney you used to sell wheat and millet and barley and beans; now as a farmer you buy them.

73
