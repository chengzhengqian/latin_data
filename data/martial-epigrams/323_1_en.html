Epigrams Book IX

39

This dawn was the first to rise for the Palatine Thunderer, 62 dawn on which Cybele would have wished to give birth to Jupiter. And on this day was born Caesonia, revered wife of my Rufus; 63 no girl owes her mother more. 64 The happy husband’s prayer is doubly granted; 65 good fortune has let him love this day twice over.

40

When Diodorus left Pharos and made for Rome to seek the Tarpeian wreath, 66 Philaenis made a vow for her man’s return, namely to lick, innocent girl, what even chaste Sabine women love. His ship was wrecked in a dreadful storm, but though Diodorus sank in the waves and the sea overwhelmed him, yet he swam out to land, to the vow. What a very tardy, lazy husband! If my girl had made that vow on the shore, I should have returned right away. 67

41

Ponticus, do you think it nothing that you never fuck but use your left hand as a mistress and make it a kindly servant

  • 62Domitian, born on 24 October.
  • 63Which Rufus is uncertain; probably not Canius, who presumably married his Theophila (7.69).
  • 64Her mother gave her the same birthday as Domitian’s.
  • 65Vows made on the previous birthday for the safety and welfare of Caesonia and the Emperor.
  • 66To compete in the Capitoline contest (cf. 4.1.6n).
  • 67Without embarking from Egypt at all.
