Epigrams Book XIII

29. Jar of damsons

Take plums wrinkled by shrivelling old age in foreign parts; 29 they are wont to dissolve the load of a constipated stomach.

30. Luna cheese

A cheese marked with the image of Etruscan Luna 30 will afford your boys a thousand lunches. 31

31. Vestine cheese

If you wish to take a frugal, meatless breakfast, this hunk comes to you from a Vestine flock.

32. Smoked cheese 32

The cheese that has imbibed not just any hearth, not every smoke, but Velabran, that cheese has savor.

33. Trebula cheeses

Trebula 33 gave us birth. A double attraction commends us, whether we are lightly toasted or soaked in water.

  • 29Plums of Damascus; cf. 5.18.3. Pliny (N.H. 15.43) says that those grown in Italy did not shrink “because their native suns are lacking.”
  • 30No doubt a crescent.
  • 31Cheeses were made very large at Luna. Pliny N.H. 11.241 says they were made of a thousand pounds’ weight.
  • 32Athenaeus refers (113c) to this cheese by its Latin name, τῷ φονμώσῳ φνρῷ.
  • 33One of several towns so called, probably on the borders of Samnium and Campania; cf. 5.71.1.
