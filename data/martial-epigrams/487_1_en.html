Epigrams Book XIII

10. Flour

Impossible to count the properties and uses of flour, since it so often comes in handy for the baker and the cook.

11. Barley

Receive something for your muleteer not to give his mules—they won’t talk. This gift I have given not you but the innkeeper. 13

12. Corn

Take three hundred pecks from the harvest of the Libyan farmer, lest your land near the city die. 14

13. Beet

To give some flavor to insipid beet, the workman’s lunch, how often will the cook ask for wine and pepper!

14. Lettuces

The lettuce that used to wind up our forebears’ dinners, tell me, why does it begin our meals? 15

  • 13The muleteer steals the barley and sells it to the innkeeper.
  • 14I.e. go barren by being over-cropped, and not allowed to lie fallow. The gift of Libyan corn will maintain the farmer for a time. But the quantity seems suspiciously large.
  • 15Cf. 11.52.5.
