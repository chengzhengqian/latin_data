On the Spectacles

34 (30; 28)

It had been Augustus’ labor to pit fleets against each other here 50 and rouse the waters with naval clarion. How small a part is this of our Caesar! Thetis and Galatea saw in the waves beasts they never knew. Triton saw chariots in hot career in the sea’s dust 51 and thought his master’s 52 horses had passed by. As Nereus prepared fierce battle for ferocious ships, he was startled to find himself walking on foot in the liquid expanse. 53 Whatever is viewed in the Circus and the Amphitheater, that, Caesar, the wealth of your water has afforded you. So no more of Fucinus 54 and the lake of direful Nero; 55 let this be the only sea fight known to posterity.

35 (31)

Pardon the hasty work. He deserves not to displease you, Caesar, who hurries to please you. 56

36 (32)

To yield to a superior is valor’s second glory; but grievous is the palm that a lesser enemy holds.

  • 51I.e. foam.
  • 52Neptune.
  • 53Just what is described here is doubtful.
  • 54The lake is central Italy on which Claudius staged his sea fight; see Tac. Ann. 12.56–57.
  • 55Who had also represented a sea fight: Suet. Nero 12.1. I translate diri (Heinsius).
  • 56This and the two following are not in α and probably (certainly in the case of 37) do not belong in this book.
