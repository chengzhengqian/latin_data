Epigrams Book XIV

114. Cuman dish

The chaste Sibyl 99 has sent you this platter red with Cuman earth, her fellow townsman.

115. Glass cups

You see the ingenuity of Nile. Desiring to add more to them, ah, how often has the artist spoiled his work! 100

116. Flagon for snow

You drink Spoletine or wine laid down in Marsian cellars. 101 What use to you is the noble chill of iced water? 102

117. Same

Ingenious thirst has invented a way of not drinking snow but drinking water fresh from snow. 103

118. Same

Forbear, boy, to mix Massilia’s smoke 104 with snow water, lest the water cost me 105 more than the wine.

  • 99Cf. 9.29.3.
  • 100I.e. the ornamented cups.
  • 101These wines were inferior; cf. 13.120 and 121.
  • 102 Decocta (as to which cf. 2.85.1) is wasted on them.
  • 103Cf. 5.64.2.
  • 104Cf. 10.36.1; 13.123.2.
  • 105“You” (tibi) in the manuscripts, but the slave would not be paying.
