So, if you trust your mirror and me, you will fear laughter as Spanius fears the wind 40 and Priscus a touch, as powdered Fabulla fears a rain shower, as painted Sabella fears the sun. Put on a face more solemn than Priam’s wife and chief daughter-in-law. 41 Avoid funny Philistion’s mimes and parties of the naughtier kind and anything sprightly and saucy that loosens the lips in revealing laughter. Your place is by a sorrowing mother or a woman mourning her husband or loving brother, you should have time only for the tragic Muses. No, take my advice and weep if you have any sense, girl, weep.
