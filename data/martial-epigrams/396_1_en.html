Epigrams Book X

Though you have one foot like Ladas, it’s no use, you silly man, trying to run with a wooden leg.

101

If Gabba of old, happy in his own Caesar, were perchance to return, sent back from the Elysian Fields, anybody who heard Capitolinus and Gabba jesting together would say: “Clownish Gabba, hold your tongue.”

102

You want to know how Philinus, who never fucks, became a father? Let Gaditanus answer that, Avitus, who writes nothing and yet is a poet. 169

103

You whom Augustan Bilbilis on her steep hill, that Salo girdles with his rapid waters, created my fellow townsmen, do you rejoice in the flourishing fame of your poet? For I am your ornament and renown and glory, nor does his Verona owe more to spare Catullus, or would wish me less to be called hers. A thirtieth summer has joined four harvests since you first gave Ceres your rustic cakes without me, while I have lived among the fair structures of imperial Rome.
