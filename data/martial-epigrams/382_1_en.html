Epigrams Book X

68

Although your home is not Ephesus or Rhodes or Mitylene but in Patrician Row, 119 Laelia, and although your mother, who used no make-up, was a daughter of the sunburnt Etruscans and your dour father came from the district of Aricia, you are always piling on the Greek—“my lord, my honey, my soul”—shame on you, a countrywoman of Hersilia and Egeria! Let the bed hear such expressions, and not every bed at that, but one made for a gamesome gentleman by his lady-friend. Do you wish to know how you talk, you, a respectable married woman? Could a waggle-bottom be more blandishing? You may learn all Corinth by heart and reproduce it, but, Laelia, you will not be altogether Lais. 120

69

You set men to watch your husband, 121 Polla, but you don’t accept them yourself. This, Polla, is taking your husband to wife. 122

70
