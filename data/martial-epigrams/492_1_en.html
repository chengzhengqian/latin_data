Epigrams Book XIII

34. Onions

Since your wife is an old woman and your member lifeless, all you can do is to stuff yourself with onions. 34

35. Lucanian sausages

I come, a Lucanian sausage, 35 daughter of a Picene sow; hence is given a welcome garnish to white porridge.

36. Small box of olives

These olives, that came taken from Picene presses, begin meals and also finish them.

37. Citrons

These fruits are either from the branches of Corcyra’s garden or they were the Massylian dragon’s. 36

38. Beestings

I give beestings, 37 abstracted by the shepherd from kids not yet able to stand, coming from the first milk of their dams.

  • 34Eaten as aphrodisiacs; cf. 3.75.3; Athen. 63e.
  • 35Cf. 4.46.8; 5.78.9. According to Apicius (2.4) the sausage was compounded of minced pork flavoured with pepper, cumin, savory, rue, parsley, and bay leaves. It was called in Low Latin salsicia, whence the word sausage (K).
  • 36They were either from Alcinous’ garden (cf. 10.94.2) or were golden fruits of the Hesperides.
  • 37The first milk given by the mother. It was considered harmful to lambs (Colum. 7.3.17), and presumably to kids.
