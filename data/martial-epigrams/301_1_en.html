Epigrams Book VIII

72

Not yet decked in purple and polished by the bite of dry pumice, you hasten, my little book, to follow Arcanus, whom fairest Narbo, Narbo Paterna 119 of lettered Votienus, bids now return to her laws and annual magistracy. You will find what you should pray for in equal measure: that place and this friend. How I would have liked to become my own little book!

73

Istantius, than whose heart none is accounted truer and of snowier sincerity, if you wish to give strength and courage to my Thalia and seek verses that will live, give me something to love.
