Epigrams Book III

24

A billy goat guilty of gnawing a vine stood sentenced to die at your altar, Bacchus, a welcome victim at your rites. When the Tuscan soothsayer was about to sacrifice him to the god, he told a rude country fellow, as it chanced, to be quick and sever the goat’s testicles with a sharp sickle, so as to get rid of the noisome smell of unclean flesh. While he himself, bent over the green altar, was cutting the throat of the struggling animal with a knife and pressing him down with his hand, an enormous hernea stood revealed to the indignant rites. The bumpkin seizes thereon and cuts it with his steel, in the belief that ancient ritual of sacrifice so required and that with such organs the deities of old are worshipped. So, soothsayer, recently Tuscan but now Gaul, 33 in slaughtering a goat you yourself became a gelding.
