Epigrams Book XIII

120. Spoletine

You would prefer crusted wines from Spoletine flagons to drinking Falernian must. 126

121. Paelignian

Paelignian growers send turbid Marsic. 127 Don’t drink it yourself, but let your freedman do so.

122. Vinegar

Don’t despise a jar of Nile vinegar. When it was of wine, it was cheaper. 128

123. Massilian

When your dole shall mark off a hundred citizens, 129 you can serve them smoky wines of Massilia. 130

  • 126Spoletine was a poor wine (cf. 14.116), and it is no great tribute to say that when aged it was better than new Falernian. Athenaeus (27b) says it was sweet and golden in color.
  • 127Very dry, and good for the stomach (Athen. 26f).
  • 128Egyptian vinegar was celebrated: Athen. 67c; Juv. 13.85. M. plays on two senses of vilis, “contemptible” and “cheap.”
  • 129I.e. when you wish to repay clients for their services.
  • 130Cf. 10.36.1.
