Epigrams Book IV

34

Soiled though it be, Attalus, whoever says you have a snowy 50 gown speaks truth.

35

We saw soft deer clash head on and lie dead in equal destiny. The dogs gazed at their quarry, and the haughty hunter stood amazed that nothing remained for his knife to do. How came light spirits by such fiery rage? So fight bulls, so fall brave men. 51

36

Your beard is white, your hair is black. You can’t dye your beard, 52 Olus (this is the reason), and you can dye your hair.

37

“Coranus owes a hundred thousand, Mancinus two hundred thousand, Titius three hundred thousand, Albinus twice that much, Sabellus a million, Serranus another million. My apartment houses and estates bring in a clear three million and my sheep farm at Parma six hundred thousand.” This you tell me, Afer, all day long and I know these figures better than my name. You must pay some­thing
