Read this, of which life can say: “It’s mine.” 8 You won’t find Centaurs here or Gorgons or Harpies: my page smacks of humanity. Whoever he be, despiser of stole or purple, 10 that has assailed with impious verses those whom he ought to respect, let him wander through the city, exiled from bridge and slope, 11 and, last among hoarse beggars, crave morsels of shameless bread, 12 fit only for dogs. May a long December and a wet winter and a closed archway drag out for him miserable cold. May he call them happy, acclaim them fortunate who are borne in an Orcivian litter. 13 But when the threads of his final hour have come and his tardy day of death, let him hear the wrangling of dogs and flap his rags to drive off noxious birds.
