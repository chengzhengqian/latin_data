51

Since you dine without me so often, Lupercus, I’ve found a way to spite you. I’m angry. You may invite me all you please, send, beg—“What will you do?” you say. What shall I do? I’ll come.

52

In this tomb lies Pantagathus, snatched away in his boyhood years, his master’s care and grief, skilled to cut straying locks and shave hairy cheeks with steel that barely touched them. Though you be kind and light, earth, as you should be, you cannot be lighter than the artist’s hand.

53

Andragoras bathed with us, ate a cheerful dinner; the same man was found dead in the morning. Do you enquire the cause of so sudden a demise, Faustinus? In his dreams he had seen Doctor Hermocrates. 64
