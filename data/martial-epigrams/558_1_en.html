Epigrams Book XIV

206. Cestus

Bind round your neck, boy, a cestus 181 warm from Venus’ bosom, love undiluted.

207. Same

Take a cestus, treated with Cytherean nectar. This girdle burned amorous Jupiter. 182

208. Stenographer

Though the words speed, the hand is faster than they. The right hand has finished its work, while the tongue has more to do. 183

209. Seashell

Let the Mareotic bark 184 be smoothed with the seashell; the reed will run its course without hindrance.

210. Idiot

His stupidity does not lie, is not feigned by wily art. He that is witless to excess has his wits.

  • 181Cf. 6.13.5.
  • 182Hera borrowed from Aphrodite her cestus to inflame the ardor of Zeus: Horn. Il. 14.214–221.
  • 183Not only does the stenographer keep pace with the speaker, he runs ahead anticipating words to come.
  • 184Papyrus. Pliny (N.H. 13.81) says that papyrus was smoothed by an ivory instrument or by a shell, but that the writing fades.
