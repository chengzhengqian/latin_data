Epigrams Book III

46

You demand client service from me, no end to it. I don’t go, but I send you my freedman. “It’s not the same,” you say. I shall show you that it’s far more. I shall barely keep up with your litter, he’ll bear it. If you get into a crowd, he’ll shove them all back with his elbows; my sides are weak and gentlemanly. You tell some tale pleading a case, and I shall keep quiet; but he will roar “bravo” three times over. You have a lawsuit, he will shout abuse in a voice of thunder; good manners have forbidden me strong language. “So,” you say, “is there nothing you will do for me as a friend?” All that a freedman cannot do, Candidus.

47

Where Porta Capena rains big drops, 53 where Almo washes the Phrygian Mother’s steel, 54 where the hallowed field of the Horatii 55 is green and the shrine of the petty Hercules is thronged, Faustinus, along came Bassus in a full four-wheeler, bringing with him all the produce of a prosperous farm. There you might see fine, leafy cabbages, both kinds of leek, 56 squat lettuces, beets not without use to a sluggish digestion; there you might see a hoop heavy with fat thrushes, a hare wounded by the teeth of a Gallic hound, 57 and a sucking pig that had yet to munch a bean.

  • 53From its aqueduct, a branch of the Aqua Marcia.
  • 54The priests of Cybele annually washed the statue of the goddess, and the sacred implements, in the Almo: Ov. Fast. 4.339.
  • 55They were buried there.
  • 56Cut (sectivum) and headed (capitatum); cf. 10.48.9n.
  • 57Plutarch (Quaest. conv. 2.9) mentions a notion that the flesh of sheep bitten by wolves became more tender.
