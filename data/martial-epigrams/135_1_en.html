Epigrams Book IV

27

Often, Augustus, you are wont to praise my little books. But here comes Jealousy and denies it. Is it any the less true for that? And besides, you did not honor me in words only, but gave me gifts 38 that no one else could give. Envy is gnawing his black nails again. Caesar, give all the more, to make him smart.

28

Chloe, you gave young Lupercus Spanish cloaks and Tyrian and scarlet, and a gown washed in warm Galaesus, Indian sardonyxes, Scythian emeralds, and a hundred sovereigns in newly minted coin; and whatever he asks for, you go on and on giving. Alas for you, prey to a smooth skin, 39 unhappy girl! Your Lupercus 40 will stand you up naked. 41

29
