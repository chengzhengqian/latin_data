42

If perchance somebody could give me what I ask, hear, Flaccus, what sort of boy I should like to ask for. First, let this boy be born in the land of Nile; no country knows better how to give naughty ways. Let him be whiter than snow; for in dusky Mareotis that complexion gains beauty in proportion to its rarity. Let his eyes rival stars and soft tresses float upon his neck; curly hair, Flaccus, is not to my liking. Let his forehead be low and his nostrils not too large and slightly aquiline, let his red lips vie with the roses of Paestum. Let him often force me when I am not in the mood and refuse me when I am, let him often make freer than his master. Let him have no fear of the boys and often shut out the girls; 57 let him be a man to all besides, a boy to me only. “I know now, you don’t fool me; it’s true and I too so judge. Such,” you will say, “was my Amazonicus.”
