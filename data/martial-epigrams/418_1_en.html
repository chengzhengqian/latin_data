Epigrams Book XI

42

You call for lively epigrams, but you propose lifeless themes. How is it to be done, Caecilianus? You demand that Hyblaean or Hymettian honey be produced for you, and you proffer Corsican thyme 66 to the Cecropian bee.

43

Catching me with a boy, wife, you upbraid me harshly and point out that you too have an arse. How often did Juno say the same to her wanton Thunderer! Nonetheless, he lies with strapping Ganymede. The Tirynthian used to lay aside his bow and bend Hylas over: do you think Megara had no buttocks? Fugitive Daphne tormented Phoebus: but the Oebalian boy 67 bade those flames vanish. Though Briseis often lay with her back to Aeacus’ son, his smooth friend was closer to him. So kindly don’t give masculine names to your belongings, wife, and think of yourself as having two cunts.
