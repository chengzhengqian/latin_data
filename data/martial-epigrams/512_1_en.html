Epigrams Book XIII

124. Caeretan

Let Nepos 131 serve Caeretan, 132 you will think it Setine. He does not serve it to a crowd, but drinks it with three guests. 133

125. Tarentine

Let Aulon, 134 renowned for wool and blest in vines, give costly fleeces to you and wine 135 to me.

126. Unguent

Never leave unguent or wine to your heir. Let him have the money, but give these all to yourself.

127. Garlands of roses

Winter gives you forced garlands, Caesar. The rose used to be spring’s, now it has become yours. 136

  • 131Cf. 6.27.1.
  • 132From Caere in Etruria.
  • 133He treats it with much respect.
  • 134A valley near Tarentum; cf. Hor. Od. 2.6.18.
  • 135Sweet, mild, and good for the stomach, but lacking “punch” (Athen. 27c).
  • 136Cf. 6.80.
