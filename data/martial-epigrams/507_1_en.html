Epigrams Book XIII

102. Garum of the allies 103

Receive lordly garum, an expensive present, from the first blood of a mackerel still breathing its last.

103. Jar of muria

I am the daughter, I admit it, of Antipolitan tunny. 104 Had I been of mackerel, I should not have been sent to you. 105

104. Attic honey

This noble nectar has been sent to you by the bee, ravager of Thesean Hymettus, from the woods of Pallas.

105. Sicilian honeycombs

When you give Sicilian combs from the heart of Hybla’s hills, 106 you may say they are Cecropian. 107

106. Raisin wine

A Gnosian vintage of Minoan Crete produced this for you. It is wont to be the poor man’s mead. 108

  • 103 Garum, made of the intestines and offal of mackerel. The finest was called garum sociorum, and came from a manufactory New Carthage in Spain: Pliny N.H. 31.93f (K).
  • 104I.e. I am the inferior fish sauce called muria, made of the entrails of other fish than mackerel, principally tunny (K).
  • 105But to somebody more important.
  • 106Sicilian honey was inferior to Attic, though Hyblan and Hymettian honey are constantly mentioned together: cf. 11.42.3 (K).
  • 107They are good enough to pass as such; cf. 13.117 and 1.105.4.
  • 108 Mulsum was wine and honey mixed: cf. Ep. 108. Passum was made from a grape called apiana (? muscatel) dried in the sun (K).
