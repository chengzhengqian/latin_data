62

Great king of ancient heaven and a bygone world, under whom was idle repose, no toil, no too regal thunderbolts, nor any that deserved them, nor was the ground cloven down to the underworld but kept its riches for itself: 93 come, happy and kind, to these customary joys of Priscus. 94 It is fitting that you be with your rites. You bring him back to his fatherland in the sixth midwinter, best of fathers, from pacific Numa’s Latin city. Do you see how an array 95 like an Ausonian market hangs aloft in your honor, how magnificent the luxurious revel, how unstinting the hand and the tokens 96 of the lavish board, what riches, Saturn, are counted out to you? And to add value and esteem to these benefactions, it is a father 97 and a man of thrift who thus celebrates your rites. But do you, holy one (so may you ever be loved in your own December), bid these days often return for him.

  • 93There was no mining for precious metals.
  • 94Priscus (see Appendix A) used to give a feast annually at the Saturnalia.
  • 95The “wealthy cord” of 8.78.7.
  • 96Representing presents to be taken away by guests (apophorêta). The fourteenth book is wholly concerned with such.
  • 97As a paterfamilias, Priscus would not ordinarily indulge in such extravagance. Pater also seems to pick up pater optime in v. 7.
