Epigrams Book XIV

216 (218). Fowler

The bird is deceived not only by rods but by song, as the cunning reed lengthens in the silent hand.

217 (216). Hawk

He was a pirate to birds; now as the fowler’s servant he seizes them and grieves that they are not caught for himself. 189

218 (217). Caterer

Say how many you want to dine and for how much, and don’t add another word; your dinner is ready.

219. Bullock’s heart

Since, a pauper barrister, you write poems that bring in no money, accept the heart 190 you have.

220. Cook

Art alone is not enough for a cook. I would not have his palate in slavery. A cook should have the taste of his master.
