Epigrams Book X

31

You sold a slave yesterday for twelve hundred sesterces, Calliodorus, in order to dine well just once. But well 56 you did not dine. The chief item of the dinner, the pièce de resistance, was a four-pound mullet you had bought. One feels like crying aloud: “This is no fish, greedy, no, it’s a man: Calliodorus, you are a cannibal.” 57

32

This picture which I decorate with violets and roses, do you ask whose face it recalls, Caedicianus? Such was Marcus Antonius Primus in middle life; in this countenance the old man sees his younger self. Would that art could represent his character and soul! No painting in the world would be more beautiful.
