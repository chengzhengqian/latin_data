You love to be sodomized, Papylus, but afterwards you weep. You want the doing, Papylus, so why are you sorry for the deed? Do you regret the lewd itch? Or is it rather that you cry, Papylus, because the sodomizing is over?

49

Anybody who calls them just frivolities and jests, Flaccus, doesn’t know what epigrams are, believe me. More frivolous is the poet who writes about the meal of savage Tereus or your dinner, dyspectic Thyestes, or Daedalus fitting his boy with liquid 71 wings, or Polyphemus feeding Sicilian sheep. All bombast is far from my little books, neither does my Muse swell with tragedy’s fantastic robe. “And yet all the world praises such things and admires and marvels.” I admit it: that they praise, but this they read.
