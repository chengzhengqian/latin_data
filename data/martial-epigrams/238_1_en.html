Epigrams Book VII

21

This is the day that, conscious of a mighty birth, gave Lucan to mankind and to you, Polla. Ah, cruel Nero, for no death more hated, this at least should not not have been permitted you.

22

The day returns made memorable by the mighty birth of Apollo’s poet. Aonian throng, to these rites be propitious. This day, when it gave you to earth, Lucan, deserved that Baetis be blended with Castalia’s water. 32

23

Come, Phoebus, but come in might, as when in person you gave the second quill of the Latin lyre 33 to him who thunders wars. What prayer should I make for so great a dawn? Polla, may you often commemorate your husband, and may he know it.

24
