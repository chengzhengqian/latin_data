Epigrams Book II

4

Oh, how affectionate you are to your mother, Ammianus, and how affectionate your mother is to you! She calls you “brother” and is called “sister.” Why do naughty names attract the two of you? Why aren’t you happy to be what you are? Do you imagine this is just innocent fun? Not so. A mother who wants to be a sister is not happy to be either mother or sister.

5

May I fall sick if I would not like to be with you all day and all night, Decianus. But there are two miles separating us; they become four when I go and have to get back. Often you are not at home. Even when you are, you are often said not to be. Often you have only time for your briefs or for yourself. All the same, I don’t mind going two miles to see you. I do mind going four not to see you.
