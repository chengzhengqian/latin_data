48

When you swore to me by all you hold sacred, by your head, Garricus, that I was heir to a quarter of your estate, I believed you—for who would willingly renounce his prayers?—and I kept my expectations warm with continual presents. Among them I sent a Laurentian boar of unusual weight—you would think he came from Aetolian Calydon. 79 You at once invited people and senate. Rome is still bilious, belching my boar. I myself (who would believe it?) was not even at the tail end of your guest list, not so much as a rib or a tail was sent to me. What am I to hope for from your quarter, Garricus? Not an ounce 80 of my own boar came my way.
