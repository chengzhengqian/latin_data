Epigrams Book IX

102

You return me my bond for four hundred thousand, Phoebus. Lend me a hundred instead, Phoebus. Find somebody else to hear you boast so empty a gift. What I can’t pay you, Phoebus, is mine.

103

What new Leda bore for you servitors so like? What naked Laconian girl was caught by another swan? Pollux gives his features to Hierus, Castor his to Asylus; 179 and in both faces shines their sister, Tyndareus’ daughter. If there had been such beauty in Therapnaean Amyclae 180 when lesser gifts defeated two goddesses, 181 you would have stayed behind, Helen, and Dardanian Paris would have gone back to Phrygian Ida with twin Ganymedes.

  • 179The unnamed owner of the two boys was one Ti. Claudius Livianus; see Heraeus’ index under Asylus.
  • 180Therapnae and Amyclae, towns near Sparta, were both associated with Helen and her brothers, but the combination is probably only a verse-filling periphrasis for Sparta or Laconia.
  • 181When Venus’ promise to Paris of Helen overweighed the promises of Juno and Minerva in the contest of beauty.
