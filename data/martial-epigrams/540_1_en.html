Epigrams Book XIV

124. Gown (toga)

He that gave the stars to his great father 109 makes “the Romans lords of the world and people of the gown.” 110

125. Same

If you don’t mind losing your morning sleep, you will wear out your gown and often get a dole.

126. Thick wrap

A poor man’s gift it is, but not a poor man’s wear. This wrap I send you in lieu of a cloak. 111

127. Brown Canusian cloak

This Canusian cloak, very like in color to turbid mead, 112 shall be your gift. Be happy; it will not soon grow old.

  • 109Domitian, who founded a temple to the Flavian family (cf. 9.1.8). He seems to have enjoined the use of the toga in certain public places; cf. Suet. Aug. 40.5.
  • 110From Virg. Aen. 1.282.
  • 111The endromis was not a garment, but a warm wrapper of rough texture used by richer men for warmth after gymnastic exercises: cf. 4.19.
  • 112Cf. 9.22.9n.
