Nothing is worn smoother than Hedylus’ cloak: not the handles of old Corinthian bronzes, not a shin polished by a ten-year shackle, not the skinned neck of a ruptured mule, not the ruts that cleave the Flaminian Way, not the pebbles shining on the beaches, not a hoe polished by a Tuscan vineyard, not the yellowing gown of a dead pauper, not the shaken wheel of a lazy 94 muleteer, not a bison’s flank shaven by the cage, not the aging tusk of a ferocious boar. One thing though (he won’t deny it himself), Hedylus’ arse, is worn smoother than his cloak.
