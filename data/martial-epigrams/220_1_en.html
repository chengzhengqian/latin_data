Epigrams Book VI

71

Skilled to match wanton gestures to Baetic airs and dance to the measures of Gades, able to stretch doddering Pelias and stir Hecuba’s husband 83 at Hector’s pyre, Telethusa burns and torments her former master. He sold a slave girl; now he buys her back as mistress of his house.

72

Cilix, 84 a thief of all too well-known rapacity, wanted to rob a garden, but in that huge garden, Fabullus, was nothing but a marble Priapus. Not choosing to return emptyhanded, Cilix made off with Priapus himself.

73

No rude farmer made me 85 with unschooled sickle; you see the steward’s noble work. For Hilarus, wealthiest cultivator of Caere’s land, owns these fields and fertile slopes. See how my face’s clear outline seems made not of wood, and in my groin I bear no weapon destined for the fireplace; my rigid cock, carved immortal from everlasting cypress, is worthy of Phidias’ hand. Neighbors, take heed: honor holy Priapus and spare the fourteen acres.

  • 83Priam.
  • 84A personal name (cf. Cic. Fam. 3.1.2) rather than “a Cilician.”
  • 85A statue of Priapus.
