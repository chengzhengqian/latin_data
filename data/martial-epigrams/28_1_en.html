Epigrams Book I

38

The little book you are reciting, Fidentinus, belongs to me. But when you recite it badly, it begins to belong to you.

39

If one there be to be numbered with such rare friends as old-time faith and ancient fame know of, one steeped in the arts of Cecropian and Latin Minerva, a good man, truly without guile; if one there be that guards the right, admires virtue, and asks nothing from the gods with secret lips; if one there be that rests on the strength of a noble heart: hang me if his name be not Decianus.
