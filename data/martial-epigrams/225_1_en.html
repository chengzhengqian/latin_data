Epigrams Book VI

84

Philippus, who is sound of body, is carried in a litter and eight, Avitus. If you think him sound of mind, 100 you are crazy, Avitus.

85

See, a sixth book of mine is published without you, Rufus Camonius, and does not hope, my friend, to have you as its reader. The unholy land of the Cappadocians, seen by you under an evil star, returns your ashes and bones to your father. Pour tears, Bononia, bereaved of your Rufus, and let lamentation sound all along the Aemilian Way. 101 Alas, what filial love, alas, how brief a life has perished! He had but lately seen Alpheus’ prizes for the fifth time. 102 You used to reel off my jests from memory, Rufus, and have by heart whole epigrams; so take a sorrowful friend’s brief poem along with his tears, and think of it as incense from the absent.
