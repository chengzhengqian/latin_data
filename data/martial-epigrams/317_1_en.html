Epigrams Book IX

26

He that dares send poems to eloquent Nerva 39 will give you pale glaucine, 40 Cosmus; he will give violets and white privet to a Paestan gardener and Corsican honey to Hybla’s bees. 41 And yet even a petty Muse is not altogether without charm; when bass is served, cheap olives are in request. Nor be surprised that my Thalia, aware of her bard’s humble quality, is afraid of your judgment. Nero himself is said to have feared your ears, when in youth he lightly turned out for you some playful piece. 42

27

You carry depilated testicles, Chrestus, and a cock like a vulture’s neck and a head smoother than prostituted arses, there is not a hair alive on your shins and the cruel tweezers purge your white jowls. But your talk is of Curius, Camillus, Quinctius, Numa, Ancus, and every hairy worthy we ever found in books; you are loud and threatening with big words, and you quarrel with the theaters and the times.
