Epigrams Book XIV

52. Horn oil flask

A steer bore me lately on his forehead. You will think me genuine rhinoceros. 50

53. Rhinoceros 51

This to you will be he that was lately in our Lord’s Ausonian arena, to whom a bull was as a straw dummy. 52

54. Small rattle

If a little home-born slave hang weeping from your neck, let him shake this chattering rattle with his tender hand.

55. Whip

You will make no headway with this whip, though you lay it on continually, if the horse that runs for you be of the purple persuasion. 53

  • 50Oil flasks of rhinoceros horn were used at the baths by the rich: Juv. 7.130.
  • 51Perhaps the title should be Idem (“The same”), in which case the flask is bull’s horn but taken by the recipient for rhinocerus horn, as in the previous epigram. Otherwise we may think of a toy rhinoceros. See SB1 .
  • 52Cf. Sp. 11.4; 10.86.4.
  • 53It, and the Gold, had been added by Domitian (Suet. Dom. 7.1). Friedländer suggests that they had somehow incurred his displeasure and been abolished.
