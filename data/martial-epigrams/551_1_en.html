Epigrams Book XIV

173. Painting of Hyacinthus 155

The Oebalian boy, Phoebus’ fault and sorrow, turns dying eyes from the hateful quoit.

174. Hermaphroditus in marble

He entered the fountain a male; he came out doublesexed. One part is his father’s; the rest he has of his mother.

175. Painting of Danae 156

Why, ruler of Olympus, did Danae receive a price from you, if Leda gave herself to you for nothing?

176. German mask

I am a jest of the potter, mask of a red-haired Batavian. The face you mock, a boy fears. 157

177. Hercules in Corinthian bronze

The infant strangles two snakes without looking at them. Already the Hydra might fear his tender hands.

  • 155See 14.164n. The picture alluded to may be a copy of the one by Nicias of Athens (fourth century b.c.), the original of which was transported to Rome by Augustus on the capture of Alexandria: Pliny N.H. 35.131.
  • 156Possibly a copy of Artemon’s picture of Danae mirantibus earn praedonibus: cf. Pliny N.H. 35.139 (K).
  • 157To him it is a bogey.
