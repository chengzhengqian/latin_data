Epigrams Book II

53

You want to become a free man? You lie, Maximus; you don’t want. But if you do, this is how you can. You will be a free man, Maximus, if you don’t want to dine out, if Veii’s grape quenches your thirst, if you can laugh at unhappy Cinna’s gold-inlaid dishes, if you can be content with a gown like mine, if you go with a vulgar tart for a couple of asses, if you can’t get into your quarters without stooping. If you have strength and willpower enough for that, you can live more free than the king of Parthia.

54

What your wife suspects about you, Linus, and in which part she would wish you more modest, she has sufficiently proved by sure signs: she has set a eunuch to watch you. The lady is as sharp and malicious as they come.

55

You want to be cultivated, Sextus. I wanted to love you. I must do as you say. Cultivated you shall be, as you demand. But if I cultivate you, Sextus, I shall not love you.
