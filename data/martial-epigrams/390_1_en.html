Nobody was ever so aflame with passion for a new mistress as Laurus was fired with love for the ball. While youth lasted, he was first player; but now that he has stopped playing, he is first ball. 147

87

Come, let Rome in duty notice the October Kalends 148 of eloquent Restitutus. 149 Honor the occasion with all your tongues and vows. We are celebrating a birthday; lawsuits, be silent. No shrivelled client’s wax taper, if you please; let idle three-leaved tablets and exiguous napkins 150 await the jollities of chill December. Let the richer sort vie with their gifts. Let Agrippa’s puffed-up tradesman bring cloaks, fellow townspeople of Cadmus. 151 Let one arraigned for fisti-cuffs and a drunken night send dinner suits to his advocate.
