Epigrams Book VII

31

Birds of the cackling poultry yard, eggs of mother fowl, Chian figs yellow with insufficient heat, 48 and the youthful offspring of a bleating nanny goat, and olives no longer able to withstand the cold, and vegetables blanched by chill frosts—do you think they are sent to you from my place in the country? How egregiously 49 you are mistaken, Regulus! My little fields bear 50 nothing but myself. Whatever your Umbrian bailiff or tenant sends you, or your farm marked by the third milestone, or your Tuscan or Tusculan properties, 51 is produced for me up and down Subura. 52

32

Atticus, you who renew the name of an eloquent line 53 and permit not a mighty house to fall silent, the pious worshippers of Cecropian Minerva attend you, quiet seclusion and every wise man loves you. Other young fellows are courted

  • 48Sometimes understood as “midsummer heat,” but M. seems to be playing down his gifts. For medius, “moderate” (and so insufficient), cf. Sen. Nat. quaest. 4b. 12 hoc medio frigore.
  • 49Apparently a colloquial use of diligenter, not found elsewhere.
  • 50In two senses: “yield” and “support the weight of.”
  • 51See SB 3. Tusci is regular because the property takes its name from a region (Tusci = Etruria in Cic. Att. 8.12 C.1). Tusculi is a freak. Tusculus = Tusculanus occurs now and then (as in Mart. 4.64.13 and 9.60.2), but Tusculum being a municipality a property in its district is properly called Tusculanum, neuter singular adjectival substantive. Prompted by the jingle, Martial commits a barbarism, intentionally, no doubt, and facetiously.
  • 52I.e. M. has to buy in the market; cf. 10.94.5.
  • 53Nothing is known of this family. Cicero’s friend Pomponius Atticus had no male children.
