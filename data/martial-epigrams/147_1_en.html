Epigrams Book IV

57

While the alluring pool of wanton Lucrine and grottos warmed by volcanic springs detain me, you, Faustinus, frequent the realm of the Argive settler, 88 whither the twentieth milestone brings you from the city. But the shaggy breast of the Nemean monster 89 glows, and ‘tis not enough that Baiae is hot with her own fire. 90 Therefore, sacred springs and beloved shores, home of Nymphs and Nereids 91 alike, fare you well. Beat the hills of Hercules 92 in winter’s cold, but now yield to Tibur’s chills.

58

You mourn your lost husband in the shadows, Galla. Methinks you are ashamed not to weep for your man. 93

59

While a viper crawled among the weeping branches of the Heliads 94 a drop of amber flowed onto the creature in its path. As it marveled to find itself stuck fast in the viscous liquid, it stiffened, bound of a sudden by congealed ice. 95 Be not proud, Cleopatra, of your royal sepulcher, if a viper 96 lies in a nobler tomb.

  • 88Tibur, founded by Catillus the Argive.
  • 89The constellation Leo, containing the brilliant star Regulus.
  • 90Allusion to the hot springs; cf. v. 2; Pliny N.H. 31.4–5; Prop.
  • 91Freshwater Nymphs (Naiads) and sea Nymphs.
  • 92Patron deity of Tibur.
  • 93Or “weep in public,” with Heinsius’ attractive conjecture palam for virum. Galla cannot muster any tears and does not want the world to know.
  • 94See index.
  • 95A stretch of language.
  • 96Like the asp that killed her. M.’s “viper” will have been smaller.
