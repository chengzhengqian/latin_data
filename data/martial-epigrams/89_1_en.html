Epigrams Book II

92

The Right of Three Children he gave me at my petition who alone had the power, as a reward for my poetry. Goodbye, wife. Our Lord’s gift should not be wasted.

93

“If this is the second book,” you say, “where is the first?” 91 How can I help it if the other is more bashful? However, if you prefer that this one become the first, Regulus, you can take one iota from the title. 92

  • 91Apparently Regulus had not had a copy of Book I and M. had none left to give him.
  • 92The Greek letter iota (I) is the Roman numeral I. Thus by dropping an iota II becomes I.
