Epigrams Book V

66

Greeted often, you are never the first to greet. Is that to be your way? Pontilianus, farewell for ever. 128

67

When the Attic birds 129 in their wonted fashion were seeking their winter retreats, one remained in the nest. Returning in the spring time, they discovered the crime, and the deserter was torn to pieces by her own kind. Too late came her punishment. The guilty mother had deserved to be rent apart, but sooner, when she mangled Itys. 130

68

I sent you hair from a northern people, Lesbia, to let you know how much yellower is yours. 131

69

Antony, you have no stone to throw at Pharian Pothinus. Cicero makes you more guilty than your list. 132 Why do you in your madness draw sword against the mouth of Rome? Not even Catiline would have committed this atrocity. An impious soldier is bribed with accursed gold, and for so great a sum you buy the silence of a single voice. What avails the costly stillness of the sacred tongue? All mankind will begin to speak in Cicero’s stead. 133

  • 128The last salutation to the dead.
  • 129Swallows, called Attic from Procne, daughter of the Athenian king Pandion.
  • 130See index under Tereus.
  • 131Taken as a compliment to “Lesbia,” this surprises. Perhaps it is meant to imply that she dyes her hair to excess.
  • 132The triumviral proscriptions of 43 b.c. Cf. 3.66.
  • 133Not “for Cicero.”
