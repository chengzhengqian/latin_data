Epigrams Book XIII

58. Goose liver

See how the liver swells larger than the big goose. You will say in astonishment: “Where, pray, did this grow?” 55

59. Dormice

I sleep all through the winter and am fatter in the season when only sleep gives me nourishment. 56

60. Rabbits

The rabbit rejoices to live in dug-out caverns. He showed hidden routes to enemies. 57

61. Heathcocks

Among flavors of fowl the taste of Ionian heathcocks is said to rank first. 58

62. Fatted hens

The accommodating hen is fed on sweetened meal and on darkness. 59 Ingenious is the palate!

  • 55Geese were fattened on figs: cf. Hor. Sat. 2.8.88. The practice is recalled by the word fegato, Italian for liver (K).
  • 56Cf. 3.58.36. Dormice were kept in pens (gliraria), and, for purposes of fattening, even in casks: Varro R.R. 3.15. They were fattened on beechnuts: Pliny N.H. 16.18 (K).
  • 57As though siege tunnels (actually called cuniculi) were devised in imitation of rabbit burrows.
  • 58So, according to Pliny, N.H. 10.133, who says the attagen was formerly a rare bird, but in his day was found in Gaul and Spain and in the Alps (K).
  • 59Seneca, Epist. 122.4, says that fowl are kept in the dark so that they stay still and fatten easily.
