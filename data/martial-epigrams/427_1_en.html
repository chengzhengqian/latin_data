Epigrams Book XI

63

You watch me in the bath, Philomusus, and ask from time to time why my smooth boys are so well endowed. I’ll answer your question frankly: they sodomize Nosy Parkers, Philomusus.

64

Faustus, I don’t know what you write to so many girls. This much I do know, that no girl writes to you. 91

65

A multitude, Justinus, dine at your invitation to celebrate the day your life began. Among them, I remember, I used not to be the last, and this place was not begrudged me . . . 92 But you are repeating the ceremony of the festive board tomorrow. For the multitude you will be born today, tomorrow for me. 93

  • 91Alternative meanings: “This I know which no girl writes to you,” i.e. your filthy habits; or “I know what it is that no girl writes to you,” i.e. that she will do as you ask.
  • 92A couplet must have dropped out, to the effect that M. had not been invited this year.
  • 93I.e. “today you will be ‘not born,’ so far as I am concerned”; cf. 4.83.4n.
