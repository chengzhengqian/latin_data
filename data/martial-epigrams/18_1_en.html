Epigrams Book I

6

As the eagle bore the boy 12 through the airs of heaven, the timid talons did not harm their clinging freight. Now Caesar’s lions are won over by their prey and the hare plays safely in the massive jaws. Which do you think the greater marvel? Behind both stands the Highest. The one is Caesar’s, the other Jove’s. 13

7

My Stella’s pet, Maximus, his Dove (I may say it, though Verona 14 hear), has surpassed Catullus’ Sparrow. 15 My Stella is greater than your Catullus by as much as a dove is greater than a sparrow. 16

8

Decianus, in following the tenets of great Thrasea and consummate Cato but at the same time wishing to survive and not rushing bare-breasted upon drawn swords, you do as I would have had you do. I am not for the hero who buys fame with easy blood, I am for him who can win glory without dying.

  • 12Ganymede. A representation by the fourth-century sculptor Leochares is mentioned by Pliny (N.H. 34.79).
  • 13The first of several variations on this theme in Book I.
  • 14Catullus’ birthplace.
  • 15Catull. 2 and 3.
  • 16Catullus wrote two poems (2 and 3) on Lesbia’s sparrow; cf. 1.109.1; 4.14.14; 7.14.4; 11.6.16. Stella had written one on a pet dove.
