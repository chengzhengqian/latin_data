If you know Caesius Sabinus well, little book, Caesius the ornament of hilly Umbria, fellow townsman of my friend Aulus Pudens, you will give him these, even if he be busy. Though a thousand cares press and beset him, yet he will have time for my verses. For he loves me and reads me next to Turnus’ famous little volumes. Oh, what renown is in the making for you! Oh, what glory! What a multitude of fans! The dinner tables, the Forum, the houses, the crossroads, the colonnades, the shops will utter you. You are sent to one, you will be read by all.

98

You buy up everything, Castor; so it will come to pass that you sell up everything.
