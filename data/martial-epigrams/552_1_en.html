Epigrams Book XIV

178. Hercules in clay 158

I am fragile, but I warn you, do not scorn the figurine. Alcides is not ashamed to bear my name. 159

179. Minerva in silver

Tell me, fierce virgin, since you have helm and spear, why you don’t have the aegis. “Caesar has it.” 160

180. Painting of Europa

You could better have been changed to a bull, most excellent Father of the Gods, when Io was your cow.

181. Leander in marble

Bold Leander cried amid the swelling waves: “Drown me, ye billows, when I am on my way back.”

182. Clay figurine of a hunchback

Methinks Prometheus was drunk when he made these Monsters 161 for the earth. He too jested with Saturnalian clay.

  • 158Pliny (N.H. 35.157) mentions such a statue done by one Vulca of Veii in the time of Tarquinius Priscus.
  • 159An odd way of saying “Alcides is not ashamed to have me bear his name.” Perhaps a deliberate touch of humor—the little statue feels that he is the real Alcides.
  • 160See the description of Domitian’s breastplate, 7.1, 2.
  • 161Real hunchbacks, made originally (like the statuettes given as presents at the Saturnalia) from clay, by Prometheus (cf. 10.39.4).
