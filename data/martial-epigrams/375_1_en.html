Now the Tyrian bull looks back at the stars of Phrixus’ lamb 92 and winter has fled alternate Castor. 93 The land is smiling, the soil is clothed and clothed the tree, the Attic adulteress mourns Ismarian Itys. 94 Faustinus, what days, what 95 * has Rome taken from you! Ah suns, ah tunic-clad repose! Ah wood and fountains and the firm shore of moist sand and Anxur gleaming in her sea waters 96 and the couch that gazes on double wave, seeing on one side river 97 craft, on the other marine!
