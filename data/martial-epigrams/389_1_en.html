Epigrams Book X

83

Marinus, you collect your scattered locks from this side and from that, and cover the broad expanse of your shining baldness with hair from your temples. But at the wind’s bidding they move and return and are restored to themselves, to surround your bare top with big curls on either side. You would think that Cydas’ Hermeros was standing between Spendophorus and Telesphorus. 145 Why not be straightforward and admit to being an old man, so that at last you look like one man? Nothing is uglier than a bald-head with a lot of hair.
