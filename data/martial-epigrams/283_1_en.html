Epigrams Book VIII

33

You send me a leaf, Paulus, from your praetor’s garland and you would have it called a bowl. With this film your platform was lately coated and the pallid stream of red saffron washed it away. 48 Or was it rather a flake scraped off by a cunning servant’s nail—I think it is from the leg of your couch. It can feel the flight of a gnat at a distance and be agitated by the wing of the smallest butterfly. It hovers suspended in the heat of a tiny lamp and breaks if lightly splashed when wine is poured. With such spittle is smeared the nut which a shabby client brings along with a frugal copper coin 49 on Janus’ Kalends. Less slender is the filament with which grow pliant Egyptian beans, lilies 50 are thicker when they fall in too hot a sun. Neither does the errant spider run to and fro in a web so tenuous or the hanging silkworm ply so light a task. Denser stands the chalk on old Fabulla’s face, denser swells a bubble in water when you strike it. Stronger the net that keeps braided hair in place and the Batavian foam that dyes Latin tresses. 51 With such a skin is clothed the chick in Leda’s egg, such are the patches sitting on a crescent-bedizened forehead. 52 Why bother with a bowl when you could send a spoon, when you could send me a snail pick even (I talk of things too large), when you could send a snail shell, when in fine, Paulus, you could send nothing?

  • 48Cf. 5.25.8n.
  • 49Symbolic gifts like Easter eggs: cf. 13.27: Ov. Fast. 1.189.
  • 50I.e. their petals.
  • 51A kind of soap giving the hair a bright hue; cf. 14.26.
  • 52Cf. 2.29.9.
