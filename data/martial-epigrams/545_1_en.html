Epigrams Book XIV

145. Frieze coat (paenula)

Such is my whiteness, such the beauty of my shag, that you would wish to put me on even in mid harvest. 127

147. Frieze blankets (cubicularia)

Your shaggy 128 blankets are bright with purple brocade. What is the good, if your aged wife freezes you up?

148. Small bedspreads (lodices)

Lest the blankets 129 on your bare bed show, we have come to you, sisters joined in one.

149. Wrap

I fear big-breasted women. Hand me over to a young girl, so that my linen may enjoy a snowy bosom.

  • 127Frieze garments were ordinarily worn in winter: cf. 6.59.
  • 129The lodix was a small shaggy blanket. Sometimes two were sewed together to form a coverlet. The Emperor Augustus used it as a wrap for warmth in the open air: Suet. Aug. 83. Lodices came from Verona: cf. 14.152 (K).
