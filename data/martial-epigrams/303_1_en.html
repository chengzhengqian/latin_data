Epigrams Book VIII

77

Liber, your friends’ sweetest care, Liber, worthy to live wreathed in everlasting roses, if you are wise, let your hair ever glisten with Assyrian unguent and garlands of flowers circle your head. Let the clear crystal grow dark with old Falernian and the soft couch be warm with a beguiling loved one. He who has so lived, though he end in mid span, has made his life longer than it was given to him. 127

78

Games such as Phlegra’s victory 128 would have wished its own, such as the Indian pageant, Lyaeus, 129 would have wished yours, did Stella exhibit in celebration of the Hyperborean triumph, 130 and (what modesty! what devotion!) he thinks them all too little. Hermus dark with turbid gold does not suffice him, nor Tagus, resounding in the Hesperian world. Each day has its gifts. The wealthy cord 131 takes no holiday and ample plunder falls into the crowd. Now come sportive tokens 132 in sudden showers, now the lavish coupon bestows the animals they have been watching, now birds are happy to fill safe laps and find masters in absence by lot, lest they be torn apart. 133 Why

  • 128The victory of the gods over the giants in the Phlegraean Plains in Campania: cf. 8.49.1.
  • 129Cf. 8.26.7.
  • 130Not to be taken literally. Domitian had declined a triumph.
  • 131A cord hung with gifts for the populace. Cf. Suet. Nero 11.2.
  • 132Entitling the holder to receive presents. Friedländer, however, thought that lasciva refers to tokens giving free access to brothels or to prostitutes in the theater (cf. Stat. Silv. 1.6.67).
  • 133Birds, instead of being scrambled for and so torn to pieces, are assigned by lot.
