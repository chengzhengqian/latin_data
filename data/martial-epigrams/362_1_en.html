Epigrams Book X

23

Antonius Primus, happy man, now counts fifteen Olympiads 35 gone by in his tranquil life. He looks back in security at past days and years, 36 nor fears the waters of Lethe, now less far away. No day, as he recalls it, is unwelcome and disagreeable; there was none he would rather not remember. A good man enlarges for himself his span of life. To be able to enjoy former life is to live twice over.

24

My natal Kalends of March, day fairer to me than all Kalends, on which even girls send me presents, 37 for the fifty-seventh time I give your altar cakes and this censer. To these years, but only if I ask what is good for me, add, I beg, twice nine, so that not yet slowed by excessive age, but with three arcs of life 38 complete, I may seek the groves of the Elysian girl. 39 Beyond this Nestor 40 I shall not ask for so much as a day.

  • 35I.e. seventy-five years; cf. 7.40.6n.
  • 36Lit. “back at past days and safe years”—nothing can happen to them; cf. Sen. Ben. 3.4.2.
  • 37Who ordinarily received gifts on that day (cf. 5.84.11).
  • 38The four segments into which the full circle of life (100 years) is divided: cf. Manil. 2.844–55. M., being fifty-seven, would in eighteen years have completed three arcs, and have reached the last arc of too protracted age.
  • 39Proserpine.
  • 40I.e. length of days.
