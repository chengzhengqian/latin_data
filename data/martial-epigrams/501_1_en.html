Epigrams Book XIII

77. Swans

The swan, chanter of its own death, modulates sweet songs with failing tongue.

78. Porphyrions

Has so small a bird 74 the name of a great giant? 75 It also has the name of Porphyrion of the Green. 76

79. Live mullets

The mullet breathes in the seawater brought along with him, 77 but already he is torpid and languishing. Give him the living sea, and he will be strong.

80. Murries

The big murry that swims in the Sicilian deep has not strength to submerge its sun-scorched skin. 78

  • 74The porphyrion is unknown. It was a bird with a long and narrow neck, and long legs. The beak and legs were red. So Pliny, N.H. 10.129 and 11.201. It is distinguished from the pelican in Arist. Av. 881. According to Athen. (388b-d) it came from Libya, and was also a domestic bird (K).
  • 75One of the giants who made war on the gods.
  • 76A charioteer.
  • 77Mullet were brought to table alive in glass jars so that the guests could watch their color changing as they died from lack of oxygen (Sen. N.Q. 3.17.2).
  • 78According to Aristotle (H.A. 8.3.4), turtles, when their shells were scorched by the sun, were unable to sink, and so were caught. M. says the same thing of the murry (K).
