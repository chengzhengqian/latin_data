Epigrams Book V

this one. Oh, how richly you deserve a cuff from Latinus, Marianus! I do believe you will be Panniculus’ successor. 117 Sees to your wife’s business, does he? Business, that curlylocks? It’s not your wife’s business he sees to, it’s yours.

62

You are fully authorized to spend the night as a guest at my place near Rome 118 if you can lay your limbs on the bare floor or if a large quantity of furniture is brought with you. For mine has already held up its finger 119 to guests. No cushion, not even an empty one, covers the broken couches, the rotten band lies limp with burst cord. But let us go shares in hospitality: I bought the place, that’s the larger part; you furnish it, that’s the smaller.

63

You say “what do you think of my little books, Marcus?” You often ask me that anxious question, Ponticus. I admire them, I’m dumbfounded, nothing is more perfect. Regulus himself will bow to your genius. “You mean it?” you say. “So may Caesar be good to you, so may Capitoline Jupiter.” No, to you. 120

  • 117Cf. 2.72.4.
  • 118 Horti (not “gardens”), perhaps M.’s Nomentan villa, which he sometimes calls “suburban” (e.g. 7.49).
  • 119I.e. asked for mercy, like a gladiator; cf. Sp. 31.5.
  • 120Ponticus’ blessing, being based on the sincerity of M.’s opinions, was an empty one.
