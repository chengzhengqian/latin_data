Epigrams Book VI

57

You fabricate false hair with ointment, Phoebus, and your dirty 68 bald pate is covered with painted locks. No need to call in a barber for your head. A sponge can shave you better, Phoebus.

58

While it is your pleasure, Aulus, 69 to gaze at the Parrhasian Bears from close at hand and to endure the lazy stars of a Getic sky, how nearly was I reft from you to the waters of Styx and beheld the dim mists of the Elysian plain! My eyes, though weary, sought your face, and Pudens’ name was constantly on my cold lips. If the spinning sisters draw me not dark threads and this voice finds the gods not deaf, I shall live to see you return safely to the cities of Latium and you will bring back the rank of Chief Centurion, 70 a notable knight.
