Epigrams Book XIV

25. Combs

This present of boxwood with multifissured tooth will find no hair here. What will it be good for?

26. Hair

Chattian foam lights up Teutonic locks. You can be smarter with captive hair. 26

27. Hair dye

If you wish to change your superannuated hair, white-headed lady, accept balls from the Mattiaci. 27 Why go bald?

28. Sunshade

Accept a sunshade to defeat the fierce sun. Should there be wind too, 28 your own awning will cover you.

  • 26Ladies wore false hair, much of which came from Germany, or from German captives. This hair was dyed with sapo, consisting of goats’ fat and beechwood ashes (Pliny N.H. 28.191) in the form of balls. See next epigram and cf. spuma Batava in 8.33.20 (K).
  • 27Tribe of the Chatti in the area of Wiesbaden. Or perhaps “from Mattium,” their settlement.
  • 28When the ordinary vela could not be spread or had to be furled: cf. 14.29.2.
