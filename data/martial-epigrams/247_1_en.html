Epigrams Book VII

43

Best is that you give me anything I ask, Cinna; next best, Cinna, is that you refuse promptly. I like a man who gives; I don’t hate a man who refuses, Cinna. But you, Cinna, neither give nor promptly refuse.

44

This, Ovidius, 67 is your 68 friend Caesonius Maximus, 69 whose features the living wax still preserves. Nero condemned him; but you dared to condemn Nero and follow the fate of a fugitive, which was not yours. You passed through Scylla’s sea as the great exile’s companion, you who but lately had declined to go as companion to a consul. If names consigned to my pages shall live and it is given me to survive my ashes, present and future generations shall hear that you were to him what he was to his Seneca.
