Epigrams Book XIII

63. Capons

Lest the cockerel grow thin by over-draining his loins, he has lost his testicles. Now I shall consider him a—cockerel. 60

64. The same

In vain the hen succumbs to her sterile husband. He should have been Mother Cybele’s bird. 61

65. Partridges

This bird is very rarely served on Ausonian tables. This you are wont often to play in the pool. 62

66. Doves

Harm not with perjured 63 tooth the tender doves, if the rites of the goddess of Cnidus have been entrusted to you. 64

  • 60Untranslatable play on two meanings of gallus: (a) a cockerel, (b) a Gaul or Galatian, hence a priest of Cybele, hence a eunuch.
  • 61See previous note.
  • 62Probably alluding to an indelicate water game (cf. πέρδομαι perdomai = “fart”).
  • 63Sense doubtful. Perhaps praeduro (“very hard”) should be read.
  • 64I.e. if you have been initiated into Venus’ mysteries. Doves were sacred to her.
