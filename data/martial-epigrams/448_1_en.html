Epigrams Book XII

9

Caesar most mild, Palma governs our 13 Iberians and Peace overseas enjoys his gentle sway. Therefore we happily thank you for so great a boon. You have sent your manners to our land.

10

Africanus has a hundred million, but still he fishes for legacies. Fortune gives too much to many, to none enough.

11

Give greetings, Muse, to your and my Parthenius. For who drinks more largely from the Aonian stream? Whose lyre comes forth from the Piplean grotto with a clearer tone? Which of the Pierian flock 14 does Phoebus love more? And if perchance (though this can scarce be hoped for) he has time to spare, ask him to hand my verses personally to the Leader and commend my timid, brief little book 15 with only four words: “This your Rome reads.”

12

You promise everything when you have drunk all night. In the morning you perform nothing. Drink in the morning, Pollio.

  • 13Trajan was born a.d. 52 at Italica, near Seville.
  • 14The Muses.
  • 15The short book was probably the abridgement of Books X and XI mentioned in 12.4.
