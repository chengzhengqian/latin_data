Epigrams Book XIV

20 (19). Case for writing materials

Having drawn the case in the lottery, remember to equip it with pens. We have furnished the rest, you must provide the lesser items.

21. Stilus case

These stilus cases you will arm with their steel. 23 If you give them to a boy, it will be no slight gift.

22. Toothpick

Mastic is better; but if a leafy point 24 is not available, a quill can relieve your teeth.

23. Earpick

If your ear is plagued by a capricious itch, I give you a weapon apt to such urges. 25

24. Gold pin

Lest damp hair harm bright silks, let a pin fix and hold up the twisted locks.

  • 23For writing on wax.
  • 24“A twig from the leafy mastic tree” (OLD).
  • 25 Libidinibus alludes to a different kind of prurigo, arma to the sense mentioned in TLL II.601.58 (SB1 ).
