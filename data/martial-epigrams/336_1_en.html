Epigrams Book IX

Children? 115 What you seek as a petitioner from our Lord and God, you will give yourself, if you can erect.

67

All night long I enjoyed a wanton girl, whose naughtinesses no man can exhaust. Tired by a thousand different modes, I asked for the boy routine; 116 before I begged or started to beg, she gave it in full. Laughing and blushing, I asked for something more indecent; the lustful hussy promised without hesitation. But so far as I am concerned, she was undefiled; she won’t be so far as you are concerned, Aeschylus, if you choose to accept this present on bad terms. 117

68

What have you to do with me, cursed schoolmaster, creature hateful to boys and girls? The crested cocks have not yet broken silence and already you make a din with your savage roaring and your thwacks. The bronze resounds as loudly from smitten anvils, when the smith is fitting a barrister 118 to the horse’s middle. The shouting rages less wildly in the great Amphitheater when the winning buckler 119 is applauded by its backers. We your neighbors
