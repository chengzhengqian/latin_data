As Phasis in the theater the other day (Phasis ruddy in resplendent cloak) was praising the edict of our Lord and God, 26 whereby the benches are more strictly assigned and the knights have regained their rows undefiled, 27 and proudly puffing made this vaunt: “At last we can sit more comfortably, knightly dignity is now restored, we are no longer pressed and soiled by the crowd”—while, lolling back, he delivered himself in these and similar terms, Leitus 28 ordered that resplendent and, arrogant cloak to get up.

  • 21Apparently with reference to the rebuilding by the Emperor of parts of Rome destroyed by fire.
  • 22Fire god and Venus’ husband; he is asked not to bear the Romans a grudge.
  • 23The Romans were supposed to descend from Mars, Venus’ paramour; Venus was the mother of Aeneas.
  • 24The chains with which Vulcan (Hephaestus) bound Mars and Venus (Ares and Aphrodite) as told in the Odyssey (8.266–366) are called Lemnian because of his connection with the island.
  • 25I.e. love Mars; see SB 3, adding for patienter Nemes. Ecl. 4.57 discatque diu patienter amare.
  • 26Domitian had adopted this style in official correspondence (Suet. Dom. 13.2).
  • 27The Roscian Law of 67 b.c. assigned fourteen rows in the theater to the knights. Domitian had brought it back into operation.
  • 28The attendant. Phasis was not a knight and could not claim a seat.
