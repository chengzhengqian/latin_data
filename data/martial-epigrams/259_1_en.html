73

You have a house on the Esquiline, and a house on Diana’s hill, 108 and Patrician Row 109 has a roof of yours. From one you view the shrine of bereaved 110 Cybele, from another that of Vesta, from this Jupiter’s new temple, from that the old one. 111 Tell me where I am to meet you, in what quarter to look for you. Who lives everywhere, Maximus, lives nowhere.

74

Ornament of Cyllene and of heaven, eloquent lackey, whose golden wand 112 is green with twisted snake, so may abundance of stolen wantonness not fail you, whether you desire the Paphian or are hot for Ganymede, and may your mother’s Ides 113 be decked with sacred fronds, and your old grandfather 114 be pressed with not too ponderous a mass: let Norbana with her husband Carpus ever joyfully celebrate this day on which they first joined in wedlock. A

  • 108The Aventine.
  • 109At the foot of the Esquiline.
  • 110By the death of Attis.
  • 111On the Capitol and on the Quirinal. Seven houses are involved.
  • 112The caduceus or herald’s wand, borne by Mercury as the messenger of the gods.
  • 113The Ides of May: cf. 12.67.1. Maia was the mother of Mercury.
  • 114Atlas, who sustained the weight of the sky.
