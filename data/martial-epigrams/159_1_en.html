Epigrams Book V

Book V

1

This I send you, Caesar, whether you tarry on Alba’s hills, viewing Trivia 1 on the one hand and Thetis on the other, or whether the truth-telling sisters 2 learn your responses, where on the town’s edge sleeps the level surface of the sea, or whether Aeneas’ nurse 3 please you, or the daughter of the Sun, 4 or gleaming 5 Anxur with her healthgiving waters, o blest protector and savior of the world, whose safety assures us of Jove’s gratitude. 6 Only accept it. I shall believe you have read it and proudly enjoy a Gallic credulity. 7

2

  • 1The temple of Diana at Aricia. Friedländer remarks that in Domitian’s villa it could only be seen from a high tower.
  • 2The two Fortunes worshipped at Antium, whose oracle Domitian would inspire if he were on the spot.
  • 3I.e. Caieta (see index).
  • 4I.e. Circeii, called after Circe.
  • 5Horace writes of Anxur (Tarracina) as “perched on her fargleaming rocks” (Sat. 1.5.26).
  • 6For Domitian’s rebuilding of his temple on the Capitoline; cf. 9.3.7.
  • 7For the credulity of the Gauls cf. Caes. B.G. 4.5.
