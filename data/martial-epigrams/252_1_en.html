Epigrams Book VII

55

Chrestus, if you return no man’s presents, then don’t give any to me or send any in return for mine; I shall think you sufficiently generous. But if you make return to Apicius and Lupus and Gallus and Titius and Caesius, you will lick a cock—not mine, which is well-behaved and diminutive, but one that comes from burned-out Jerusalem, 81 one lately doomed to pay taxes. 82

56

Rabirius, your pious mind has comprehended the stars and sky, builder of the Parrhasian dwelling with wondrous skill. 83 If Pisa shall prepare to give Phidias’ Jupiter 84 a worthy shrine, she will ask our Thunderer for these hands.

57

Gabinia has made Achillas a Castor out of a Pollux; he was pyx agathos, now he will be Hippodamus. 85

  • 81Captured by Titus and burned a.d. 70.
  • 82Cf. Suet. Dom. 12.2.
  • 83A reference to the domed roof of Domitian’s palace, built by Rabirius, his architect (cf. 10.71), and completed in a.d. 92.
  • 84The statue of Zeus at Olympia by Phidias.
  • 85I.e. she has made a pugilist a knight. The reference is to Hom. Il. 3.237, where π$$$ὺξ ἀγαθός (pyx agathos, “good with his fists”) is the epithet of Pollux the boxer and ἱππόδαμος (hippodamos, “horse-tamer”) that of Castor, the horseman. There is doubtless an obscene pun, since πυγή (pygê) or πύξ (pyx) = buttocks. Hippodamus is less easily explained, but see the note in my edition.
