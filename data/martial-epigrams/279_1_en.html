Epigrams Book VIII

22

You invite me to boar and you serve me pork, Gallicus. I am a hybrid, 36 Gallicus, if you deceive me. 37

23

You think me cruel and too fond of my stomach, Rusticus, because I beat my cook on account of a dinner. If that seems to you a trivial reason for lashes, for what reason then do you want a cook to be flogged?

24

Should I happen to make some petition in my slender, timid little book, if my page be not overbold, grant it. And if you do not grant it, Caesar, allow yourself to be asked. Incense and prayers never offend Jupiter. He does not make gods who sculpts sacred faces in gold or marble; he makes them who asks of them.

25

When I was very ill, Oppianus, you saw me only once. I shall see you often. 38

  • 36Offspring of dissimilar parents, as a sow and a wild boar.
  • 37I.e. I can tell the difference.
  • 38A sly assumption, wrapped up in what appears to be a promise to return good for evil (SB 1).
