Baetis, your hair wreathed with crown of olive, coloring golden fleeces with your shining waters, loved by Bromius, loved by Pallas; for whom Albula, 139 ruler of waters, opens a ship-bearing path through the seas: may Istantius enter the shores of Spain 140 with happy omens and may this year pass for its peoples like the last. He knows what a burden it is to succeed Macer. Who measures his load, can bear it.

  • 139Old name of the Tiber, called by Virgil (Aen. 8.77) “lord (regnator) of western waters.” He receives at Ostia the merchandise of Baetica after its journey overseas (SB3 ).
  • 140 Vestras = Baeticorum.
