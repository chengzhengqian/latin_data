You outdo lilies and privet still unfallen and the ivory that whitens on Tibur’s hill. 42 Sparta’s swans will yield to you and Paphian doves; the pearl shall yield, dug out from Erythraean shallows. But though this gift challenges fresh snow, it is no whiter than Parthenius, 43 its giver I would not rather have the painted fabrics of proud Babylon, embroidered by Semiramis’ needle. I would not rather admire myself in gold of Athamas, if Phrixus were to give me the Aeolian ram. 44 Oh, what merriment my cloak will raise, seen along with my Palatine gown! 45

He who writes distichs wishes, I suppose, to please by brevity. What use is brevity, tell me, if it’s a book?
