scruples, and sextules. 102 When the work is done and the same lies like a limp thong after its wrestlings, Marulla tells you how much lighter it has become. That hand of hers isn’t a hand, it’s a balance.

56

You tell me to be at your service all day, Gallus, and traverse your Aventine three times or four. Cascellius extracts or restores an ailing tooth; you, Hyginus, burn off hairs that trouble the eyes; Fannius doesn’t cut but removes a dripping uvula; 103 Eros effaces the repulsive brands of slaves; 104 Hermes is said to be the Podalirius of hernias; tell, me, Gallus, who is it that heals the broken-winded? 105

57

You used to send a pound of silver plate; it has become half pound, but of pepper. I don’t pay that much for pepper, Sextus.

  • 102The scripulum or scrupulum was 1/24 of an ounce (uncia), the sextula 1/6 of an ounce.
  • 103How he would do that without cutting is not clear; hence Alan Ker’s proposal insecat or consecat (“cuts”).
  • 104Inflicted as a punishment. I translate servorum.
  • 105Lit. simply “broken”; cf. 12.14.12.
