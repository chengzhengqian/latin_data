Epigrams Book XI

81

Eunuch Dindymus and an old man harass Aegle in common, and the girl lies dry in the middle of the bed. Lack of strength makes the one, length of years the other useless for the job; so each labors in fruitless desire. She begs in supplication for herself and the two unfortunates, Cytherea, that you make one of them young and the other a man.

82

As Philostratus was returning at night’s behest from a dinner party at the baths of Sinuessa to his rented lodging, he almost copied Elpenor and lost his life by a cruel fate, plunging down a long flight of steps. Nymphs, he would not have endured so great a peril if he had drunk your waters instead. 106

83

Nobody lives in your house free of charge unless he is rich and childless. Nobody, Sosibianus, lets his house at a higher rent. 107
