Epigrams Book XIII

81. Turbot

However wide the dish that bears the turbot, yet the turbot is wider than the dish.

82. Oysters

A shellfish, I have just arrived, drunk with Baian Lucrine. 79 Now in my extravagance I thirst for noble garum. 80

83. Prawns

Blue Liris, whom Marica’s wood protects, loves us. From hence are we prawns, a mighty throng.

84. Parrot wrasse

This parrot wrasse 81 that came eroded by sea waves is good as to the entrails, but for the rest it has a poor flavor.

85. Bolti

Leader in Nile’s market, bolti, 82 you are snatched up. No glory of Pella’s 83 palate outranks you.

  • 79Which produced the finest oysters: cf. 3.60.3; Macrob. Sat. 3.15.3.
  • 80Cf. 6.93.6n.
  • 81 Sparisoma cretense. It was a favorite fish, brought originally, according to Pliny (N.H. 9.62) by Tiberius from the Carpathian Sea, and planted by Optatus, praefectus classis, in the sea between Ostia and Campania. Athenaeus (319e–320c) gives a description (K).
  • 82This is Tilapia Nilotica; cf. Pliny 5.51; 9.68; 32.56. He says it was not caught in winter except on the same few days. See also Athen. 302b-309a. It was regarded as in every way superior to the mullus: Athenaeus 121c (K).
  • 83Pellaean = Macedonian = Alexandrian.
