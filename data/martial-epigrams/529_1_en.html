Epigrams Book XIV

70 (69). Priapus made of flour

If you want a full stomach, you can eat our Priapus; though you gnaw his very genitals, you will be clean.

71 (70). Pig

This pig will make you a good Saturnalia; he fed on acorns among the foaming boars.

72. Sausage

This sausage that has reached you at midwinter time had reached me before Saturn’s seven days. 65

73. Parrot

A parrot, I shall learn from you the names of others; this I have learned to say by myself: “Caesar, hail!”

74. Crow

Crow, who greet us, why are you thought to be a sucker? No cock has entered your head. 66

  • 65I.e. this is not a passed-on Saturnalian present. Elsewhere M. writes of the holiday as lasting five days; under the Republic it lasted seven.
  • 66Cf. Pliny N.H. 10.15 ore eos parere aut coire vulgus arbitratur . . . Aristoteles negat . . . sed illam osculationem quae saepe cernitur qualem in columbis esse; Arist. Gen. Anim. 3.6.
