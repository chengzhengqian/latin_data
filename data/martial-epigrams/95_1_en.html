Epigrams Book III

16

Cobbler, 18 princeling of stitchers, you’re giving a gladiator show, and what the awl has given you the dagger snatchers away. You’re drunk; for sober you would never have wanted to play with your own hide, 19 cobbler. Well, you have played with your hide. But now, mark my words, cobbler, keep yourself in your own little skin. 20

17

A tart, passed and passed around at dessert, cruelly burnt our hands with its excessive heat. But Sabidius’ greed was more fiery still; so forthwith he blew on it with his cheeks three or four times. The tart cooled to be sure, and seemed ready to admit our fingers, but nobody could touch it. It was filth.
