66

Muses, give pious incense and victims to Augustus for your Silius’ sake. See, his son is consul. Caesar, the world’s chief and only welfare, makes the twice six fasces 105 to return and the poet’s Castalian mansion to resound with the noble rod. 106 He rejoices, but there still remains something for him to pray for: happy purple and a third consul. 107 The senate gave to Pompey and Caesar to his son-in-law 108 sacred honors: Janus the peacemaker three times glorified their names. 109 But this is how Silius prefers to number a plurality of consulships.
