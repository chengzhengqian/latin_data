Epigrams Book XIII

24. Quinces

If quinces steeped in Cecropian honey are set in front of you, you can say: “These are honey apples.”

25. Pine cones

We are Cybele’s fruits; 24 go hence, traveller, last our fall come down upon your luckless head.

26. Sorb apples

We are sorb apples, tautening too loose bellies. You will do better to give this fruit to your boy 25 than to yourself.

27. Stem (?) 26 of dates

A golden date is offered on the Kalends of January; 27 and yet this is usually the gift of a poor man.

28. Jar of Syrian figs

These Syrians, that have come to you stored in a twisted cone, would be figs if they were larger. 28

  • 24Because she turned her favorite Attis into a fir, which thus became sacred to her.
  • 25I.e. cinaedo.
  • 26But see petalium in OLD.
  • 27By poor clients to their patrons: cf. 8.33.11f.
  • 28 Cottana were small figs from Syria: Pliny N.H. 13.51. Cf. 4.88.6 and 7.53.7.
