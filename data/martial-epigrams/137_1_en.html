thrive if it does not give me the greatest pleasure, and I want to put you in my pages. But you have a name laid on you when the Sisters’ fountain was averse; cruel was the mother 44 who gave it you. Melpomene could not utter it, nor Polyhymnia, nor dutiful 45 Calliope with Phoebus’ help. So adopt a name agreeable to the Muses. it is not pretty to be forever saying “Hippodame.” 46

32

Shut in Phaethon’s drop, 47 a bee both hides and shines, so that she seems imprisoned in her own nectar. She has a worthy reward for all her sufferings. 48 One might believe that she herself willed so to die.

33

You have bookcases full of laborious scripts, so why don’t you produce anything, Sosibianus? “My heirs,” you say, “will publish my poems.” When? It is already high time you were read, 49 Sosibianus.

  • 44Was the girl born a slave? Cf. 11.91.1n. Or did mothers decide for girl children?
  • 45Presumably toward her brother Phoebus.
  • 46“She who tames horses,” apparently substituted for a name which would not go into M.’s meters. Heraeus suggested a sexual innuendo, comparing 7.57.2.
  • 47I.e. a drop of amber. Cf. 4.59 and 6.15.
  • 48Not “toils.” Labor, like πόνος, can mean “pain.”
  • 49I.e. dead.
