Epigrams Book XII

41

It is not enough, Tucca, that you are a gourmand; you want to be called one and you want to appear one. 58

42

Bearded Callistratus married rugged Afer in the usual form in which a virgin marries a husband. The torches shone in front, the wedding veil covered his face, and, Thalassus, you did not lack your words. Even the dowry was declared. 59 Are you still not satisfied, Rome? Are you waiting for him to give birth?

43

You read me some all too well-turned verses about debauchees, Sabellus, such as neither Didymus’ girls 60 know of nor the voluptuous little books of Elephantis. Therein are novel erotic postures such as only a desperate fornicator would venture, what male prostitutes provide and keep quiet about, in what combinations five persons are linked, by what chain are held more than five, what can go on when the lamp is put out. You paid too high a price 61 for your poetic skill. 62

  • 58 Gulosus may mean “gourmand” or “gourmet.” M. uses it of “Tucca” in the first sense; “Tucca” would like it to be used of himself in the second.
  • 59Cf. the nuptials of Nero and Pythagoras described by Tac. Ann. 15.37.
  • 60 Nothing is known of them. One editor reads Didymae (γ), perhaps rightly.
  • 61“Wading through all this filth” (SB1 ). Or perhaps tanti is more general: “your poetic skill does not compensate (for the vile content).”
  • 62I.e. for displaying it.
