Epigrams Book VI

66

The other day Gellianus the auctioneer was selling a girl of none too good a reputation, such a one as those that sit in the middle of Subura. Wishing to prove to all that she was clean, since for a long time the bids were low, he drew her close to him against her will and kissed her twice and thrice and again. You ask what that kissing accomplished? Somebody who was bidding six hundred sesterces withdrew.
