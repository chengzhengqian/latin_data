Epigrams Book XI

66

You are an informer and a slanderer, a swindler and a dealer, a sucker and a trainer. 94 I wonder why you don’t have any money, Vacerra.

67

You give me nothing in your lifetime. You say you will give me something after your death. If you’re not a fool, you know what I want, Maro. 95

68

You ask small favors of big men; even these the big men refuse. To lighten your humiliation, Matho, ask big favors. 96

69

Reared among the trainers of the Amphitheater, a huntress, fierce in the woods, gentle in the house, I was called Lydia, most faithful to my master Dexter, who would not have preferred Erigone’s dog or him of Dicte’s breed 97 that followed Cephalus and came with him to the stars of the light-bringing goddess. It was not length of days nor useless age that carried me off, as was the fate of the Dulichian
