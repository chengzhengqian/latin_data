Epigrams Book X

63

The marble you are reading, traveller, is small indeed, but will not yield to the stones of Mausolus and the pyramids. My life was twice approved at Roman Tarentos 112 and lost nothing down to my dying day. Juno gave me five boys and as many girls; their hands all closed my eyes. Rare glory of wedlock was my lot, and my chastity knew but one cock. 113

64

Queen Polla, if you handle my little books, receive my jests with unfrowning brow. When your noble bard, 114 the glory of our Helicon, blew fierce warfare on Pierian trumpet, yet he did not blush to say in wanton verse: “If I’m not even sodomized, Cotta, what am I doing here?”
