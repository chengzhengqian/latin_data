Epigrams Book XII

26 (27)

You say you were fucked by bandits, Saenia; but the bandits deny it. 31

27 (28)

I drink two measures, you, Cinna, drink eleven. And you grumble, Cinna, because we don’t drink the same wine?

28 (29)

Hermogenes 32 is as mighty a thief of napkins, Ponticus(?), 33 as Massa 34 hardly was (I suppose) of money. You may watch his right hand and hold his left, 35 he will find a way to abstract a napkin. So a stag’s breath sucks up a chilly snake; 36 so Iris 37 snatches water that will later fall from aloft.   • 31Cf. 10.95n.
  • 32Hermes was the thief among the gods: cf. Hor. Od. 1.10.7; accordingly M. invents the name “Sprung of Hermes.”
  • 33A name, such as Pontice, has dropped out.
  • 34Baebius Massa was found guilty of peculation in his province of Baetica in a.d. 93. But the name may be “associative,” referring to an imaginary pickpocket.
  • 35The left was the thievish hand (Cat. 47.1; Ov. Met. 13.111); hence M.’s distinction between watching and holding.
  • 36According to Pliny (N.H. 8.118), stags with their breath draw serpents out of their holes; cf. also Lucr. 6.765.
  • 37The rainbow; cf. 4.19.1n.
  • 38Handkerchiefs were waved when a discharge or quarter was wished by the spectators for a gladiator: cf. Sp. 31.3.
  • 39As a signal for the starting of the races in the circus. The praetor presided.
