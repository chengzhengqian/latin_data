Epigrams Book XIII

39. Kid

Let the wanton animal, mischievous to the green vine, pay his penalty; young though he be, he has already harmed the god. 38

40. Eggs

If white fluid surrounds yellow yokes, let the Hesperian 39 liquid of the mackerel 40 season the eggs.

41. Sucking pig

Let a rich man set before me the nursling of a lazy mother, fed on pure milk, and eat of the Aetolian boar. 41

42. Soft-seeded pomegranates and jujubes

I give jujubes 42 and soft-seeded pomegranates, not from Libyan branches but from trees of Nomentum.

43. The same

Picked from suburban branches are sent soft-seeded pomegranates and home-grown jujubes. 43 What do you want with Libyan?

  • 38Cf. 3.24.1f; 8.50.12.
  • 39I.e. Spanish.
  • 40I.e. garum: cf. 13.102.
  • 41Like that slain by Meleager: cf. 7.27.2. If my host gives me sucking pig, he is welcome to the boar.
  • 42According to Pliny (N.H. 15.47) a kind of African fruit of two kinds, one white, the other red. Both were probably the jujube, Zizyphus vulgaris. At Verona grew a variety called lanata from having a down like a peach (K).
  • 43Pliny says (N.H. 15.47) the jujube was introduced into Italy by Sextus Papinius, quem consulem vidimus.
