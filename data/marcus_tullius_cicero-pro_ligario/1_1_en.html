On Behalf of Ligarius

The Speech on Behalf of Quintus Ligarius

(Delivered before Caesar in the Forum, 46)

I. Strange, indeed, Gaius Caesar, and hitherto unparalleled1 is the charge which has been submitted to you by my kinsmana Quintus Tubero—that Quintus Ligarius has been in Africa; and this charge Gaius Pansa,b with all his outstanding ability, has made bold to admit; fortified, possibly, by his intimacy with you. So what my course is to be I know not. For, hoping that you knew nothing of the matter at first hand and that you could have known nothing of it at second hand, I had come prepared to take advantage of your ignorance to save an unfortunate man. But now that his dark secret has been disclosed by an indefatigable opponent, there is nothing for it, I suppose, but to plead guilty to the charge, especially as, thanks to my friend Pansa, it is no longer a debatable question. So I must needs eschew controversy and convert my whole speech into an appeal for your compassion, to which so many have owed their safety, winning from you not indeed absolution from guilt but pardon for their errors.
