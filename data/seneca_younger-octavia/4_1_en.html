OCTAVIA

nurse

Ah! The voice of my sorrowing child strikes my ears. Must I be slow to enter her room through tardy old age?

(she enters the bedchamber)
octavia

Oh nurse, be receptive to my tears, you faithful witness to all my pain.

nurse

Pitiful one, what day will free you from such sorrows?

octavia

The day that sends me to the Stygian shades.

nurse

I pray that omen will be turned aside!

octavia

It is not your prayers any more, but doom that rules my lot.

nurse

A kindly god will grant better times after your hardships. You must just calmly win your husband by sweet submission.

octavia

I shall sooner win cruel lions over and brutal tigers than the fierce heart of this cruel tyrant!
