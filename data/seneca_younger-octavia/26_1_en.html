OCTAVIA

nero

God has brought together every distinction in this one woman; the Fates willed that such a paragon should be born for me.

seneca

Love will forsake you; do not have blind faith!

nero

You mean the tyrant of heaven, who cannot be banished by the lord of the thunderbolt, who penetrates the cruel seas and the realm of Dis, and draws down gods from the sky?34

seneca

It is human wrongheadedness that fancies winged Love as an implacable god, arms his holy hands with bow and arrows, equips him with a cruel torch, and believes him to be Venus’ son by Vulcan. A powerful impulse in the mind, a seductive warmth in the heart—that is what Love is. It is generated by youth, and fostered by leisure and high living amid Fortune’s genial bounty. If you stop cherishing and nurturing it, it fades, and is soon quenched and loses its strength.

nero

I think it the chief source of life, the fount of pleasure. The human race is preserved from extinction by being constantly engendered through enchanting Love, who charms even wild beasts. May this god carry torches to herald my marriage, and his fire bring Poppaea to share my bed!

  • 34I.e., to visit human loves; the “tyrant” is Amor/Cupid.
