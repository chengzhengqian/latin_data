OCTAVIA

seneca

It is idle and empty.

nero

Maybe, but it stigmatizes many.

seneca

It fears the great.

nero

But carps nonetheless.

seneca

It will easily be crushed. You should be swayed by your obligations to your deified father, by your wife’s youth, her probity and modesty.

nero

Enough, stop pressing the point! You are trying my patience now. Let me act in a way that Seneca disapproves. Indeed I have been delaying the people’s wishes for some time . . .35 since she is carrying in her womb a token and portion of myself. Come, let us set tomorrow as the day for the wedding. (exeunt)

(time: toward dawn of the next day)
