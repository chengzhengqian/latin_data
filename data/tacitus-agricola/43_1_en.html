Agricola

45. It was not his fate to see the Senate-house besieged, the Senate surrounded by armed men, and in the same reign of terror so many consulars butchered,1 the flight and exile of so many honourable women. Mettius Carus2 was still rated at one victory only; Messalinus’ rasping voice 3 was confined to the Alban citadel;4 and Baebius Massa 5 was still as before, on trial. A little while and our hands it was which dragged Helvidius 6 to his dungeon; it was we who were (put to shame) by the look which Mauricus 7 and Rusticus gave, we who were soaked by the innocent blood of Senecio. Nero after all withdrew his eyes, nor contemplated the crimes he authorised. Under Domitian it was no small part of our sufferings that we saw him and were seen of him; that our sighs were counted in his books; that not a pale cheek of all that company escaped those brutal eyes, that crimson face which flushed continually lest shame should unawares surprise it.

  • 1Besides Helvidius and Rusticus mentioned by Tacitus below, and Vettulenus Civica mentioned in 42.1, there were at least eight other exconsuls who fell victims to Domitian.
  • 2Mettius Carus was a well-known delator or informer who accused many people, including Herennius Senecio. But which successful accusation is meant here is unknown.
  • 3L. Valerius Catullus Messalinus, who had been consul with Domitian in a.d. 73, was a notorious blind accuser.
  • 4Domitian had built a villa at Alba Longa which incorporated the arx of the ancient settlement.
  • 5Massa Baebius who had been a procurator of Africa was officially accused in a.d. 93 by Pliny the Younger and Herennius Senecio for misdeeds as proconsul in Spain. Condemned, he yet was able to accuse successfully Herennius of impiety and became a formidable man.
  • 6Helvidius, son of C. Helvidius Priscus of Chapter 2, 1, was executed for an apparent allusion to Domitian in a play which he wrote.
  • 7Iunius Mauricus was brother of Q. Arulenus Iunius Rusticus of Chapter 2, 1. Involved in Arulenus’ troubles, he was exiled.
