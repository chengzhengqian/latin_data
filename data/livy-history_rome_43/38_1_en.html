Perseus also, setting out for Elimea,1 and purifying his army in that neighbourhood, led his force, at the invitation of the Epirotes,2 to Stratus. Stratus was at that time the strongest city of Aetolia; it is situated inland from the Ambracian Gulf near the River Inachus.3 Perseus set out thither with ten thousand infantry and three hundred cavalry, a smaller number of which4 he took with him because of the narrowness and roughness of the roads. When on the third day he had arrived at Mount Citium, the snow was so deep that he barely succeeded in crossing and had difficulty also in finding a place for his camp. Setting out thence, more because he could not stay than because either the way or the weather was bearable, with great suffering, especially of the baggage-animals, on the second day he pitched camp at the temple of Jupiter whom they call the Victorious.5 Thence having traversed a huge march to the Aratthus River, he halted. . . delayed by the depth of the river.
